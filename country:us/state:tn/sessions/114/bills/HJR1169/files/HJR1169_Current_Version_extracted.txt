HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1169
By Capley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Fred Hime Gillham of
Clifton.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Fred Hime Gillham of Clifton; and
WHEREAS, Fred Gillham was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Gillham was born on September 14, 1942, on Church Street in Clifton to
Delia Belle Gillham and Fred Elliot Gillham; he had one sister, Archie Dee Gillham, who passed
away at age eighteen; and
WHEREAS, during Fred Gillham's childhood, his father was a Seabee in the U.S. Navy
during World War II; thus, he was especially influenced by his mother and sister; and
WHEREAS, before moving to East Tennessee for work after high school, Mr. Gillham
promised his family in Clifton that after he made $100,000, he would move back to Clifton and
take care of the Gillham Family Farm; and
WHEREAS, in East Tennessee, he worked for the Preston Carroll Construction
Company digging trenches and repairing equipment; and
WHEREAS, in 1961, Fred Gillham married Mary Faye Brewer; they had three children,
Jennifer G. Morrison, Fred H. Gillham, Jr., and Holly G. Brown; and
WHEREAS, an industrious man of many trades, Mr. Gillham spent most of his working
years in Columbia buying and selling commercial real estate, trading equipment, restoring race
cars, and engaging in any other business venture he thought lucrative; and

WHEREAS, just as he promised, Fred Gillham was able to take care of his family farm
and also obtain his Uncle Fin's property in Hardin County, which he maintained until his death;
and
WHEREAS, in 1988, Mr. Gillham married Kelli C. Gillham; they renovated Elm Springs in
Columbia, which is now home to the National Confederate Museum, and Hamilton Place, which
they still owned until his death; and
WHEREAS, one of Fred Gillham's greatest passions was spending time at the
Tennessee River in Clifton at his cabin nestled between the banks of the Tennessee River and
Highway 128; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Gillham leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Fred Hime Gillham, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Gillham.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
