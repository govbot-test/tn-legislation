HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1308
By Hale
A RESOLUTION to recognize Commissioner Jeff Long of the
Tennessee Department of Safety and Homeland
Security on the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have dedicated their careers to the protection and well-being of the citizens
of this State; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable person is Commissioner Jeff Long, who has served with
distinction as Commissioner of the Tennessee Department of Safety and Homeland Security
since 2019 and will soon be taking a much-deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, Commissioner Long graduated from the University of Memphis with a
Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science and earned a Doctor of Jurisprudence degree from
the Nashville School of Law; and
WHEREAS, Jeff Long's career in criminal justice spans more than fifty-three years and
includes service as an Assistant District Attorney for the 21st Judicial District, Special Agent in
Charge with the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation, Investigator for the 21st Judicial District,
Arson Investigator with the Tennessee Fire Marshal's Office, Captain with the Williamson
County Sheriff's Office, and Federal Hospital Police Officer with the Veterans Administration
Hospital in Memphis; and
WHEREAS, in August 2008, he was elected Sheriff of Williamson County, in which
capacity he earned the respect and admiration of both his colleagues and the citizens he served
through his steadfast commitment to public safety and effective law enforcement; and
WHEREAS, Commissioner Long was appointed Commissioner of the Tennessee
Department of Safety and Homeland Security by Governor Bill Lee and was sworn in
immediately following the governor's inauguration in January 2019, and he was also appointed
to serve as the Governor's Homeland Security Advisor for the State of Tennessee; and

WHEREAS, additional responsibilities entrusted to Commissioner Long include service
on the Tennessee Criminal Justice Investment Task Force and leadership as chair of the Violent
Crime Task Force; and
WHEREAS, among his numerous honors and commendations are the Task Force
Against Domestic Violence Law Enforcement Award, recognition from the John Marshall
American Inns of Court, the Master Hall of Fame Morris Heithcock Lodge 41 Fraternal Order of
Police Award, an honorary doctorate from Williamson College, and a commendation from FBI
Director William S. Sessions; and
WHEREAS, Commissioner Long's many accolades also include the Williamson County
Impact Award, designation as the Law Enforcement Officer of the Year by the Williamson
County Bar Association, the Sons of the American Revolution Law Enforcement Commendation
Medal, and the Tennessee Sheriff of the Year Award; and
WHEREAS, he also had the honor of serving on the Dignitary Protection Detail for
presidents Ronald Reagan, Bill Clinton, George H. W. Bush, and George W. Bush, as well as
for Princess Anne, daughter of Queen Elizabeth II; and
WHEREAS, a respected leader in his profession, Jeff Long is a former president of the
Tennessee Sheriffs' Association and a current member of the National Sheriffs' Association and
the International Association of Chiefs of Police; in 2014, he was appointed by Governor Bill
Haslam to serve as a member of the Peace Officer Standards and Training Commission; and
WHEREAS, his many accomplishments aside, Commissioner Long is most grateful for
the love and support of his wife, Dora, and their children, Julie and Justin, and their five
grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Jeff Long exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community that
are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career, Commissioner Long has proven himself to
be wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service that have earned Tennessee
- 2 - 017296
recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially recognized for his many years of
honorable and astute service to the citizens of this State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Commissioner Jeff Long upon his retirement as
Commissioner of the Tennessee Department of Safety and Homeland Security, thank him for
his many years of dedicated service, and extend to him our best wishes for every continued
success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
