HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1010
By Barrett
A RESOLUTION to recognize Danny Herron on the occasion of
his retirement from Habitat for Humanity of
Greater Nashville.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and
enthusiasm throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Danny Herron is one such estimable individual who, having rendered over
sixteen years of sterling service to Habitat for Humanity of Greater Nashville as its president and
chief executive officer, will take his much-deserved retirement this year; and
WHEREAS, since its founding in 1985, the Nashville affiliate of Habitat for Humanity has
built more than 1,100 homes and served approximately 4,000 family members; and
WHEREAS, a native of Middle Tennessee, Mr. Herron has held a number of leadership
roles throughout his public service career and has positively impacted many within his
community; and
WHEREAS, Danny Herron's impact through his leadership with Habitat for Humanity is
immeasurable. Under his leadership, Habitat for Humanity of Greater Nashville has built more
than 500 homes, creating over $125 million in real estate value; engaged more than 75,000
volunteer experiences and expanded its service area into surrounding counties; established a
new office in a former Kmart building; and increased total organizational assets to more than
$90 million; and
WHEREAS, in 2019, Habitat for Humanity of Greater Nashville hosted the Jimmy and
Rosalynn Carter Work Project, attended by President and Mrs. Jimmy Carter, Trisha Yearwood,
and Garth Brooks. This project resulted in the construction of twenty-one homes in Habitat's
Park Preserve neighborhood, which was later completed with an additional twenty-six homes in

2025; the project also advanced plans for a $3 million renovation of the adjacent historic
Parkwood Community Club ballfields and supported the establishment of a fifty-nine acre Metro
Park; and
WHEREAS, in 2026, Habitat began constructing its newest single-family home
community, Bella Terra, in Antioch, a development of fifty-three homes that was made possible
through donor support, including a $3 million gift from The Frist Foundation and a $4.5 million
grant from the Barnes Affordable Housing Trust Fund; and
WHEREAS, Habitat for Humanity of Greater Nashville operates more than 75,000
square feet of retail space through its ReStore social enterprise, with stores located in
Davidson, Wilson, and Dickson counties; and
WHEREAS, ReStore generates approximately $5 million in annual sales to support
Habitat's mission and build more than thirty quality affordable homes each year; through the
stores, more than 1,500 tons of materials are diverted from local landfills annually and more
than forty people across three counties are employed; and
WHEREAS, Danny Herron is a graduate of Leadership Nashville and was named one of
Nashville's Most Admired CEOs by the Nashville Business Journal in 2020; he was included in
The Nashville Post's "In Charge" list in 2025, which recognizes business, political, and civic
leaders across Middle Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Herron previously held a variety of leadership roles in banking and real
estate prior to joining Habitat; from 1993 to 2007, he served as president and CEO of a local
community bank, and he currently serves on the board of directors of the Federal Home Loan
Bank of Cincinnati as an independent public interest director, a position he has held since 2022;
and

WHEREAS, Danny Herron has described leading Habitat for Humanity of Greater
Nashville as a privilege and has emphasized the organization's mission of putting God's love
into action by bringing people together to build homes, communities, and hope; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Herron exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his illustrious tenure, Danny Herron has carried out his
responsibilities with diligence and integrity, and his remarkable success as a public servant and
civic leader has set the mold for all who will follow; and
WHEREAS, a strong advocate for the citizens of Middle Tennessee, Mr. Herron has
compiled a singular record of success during his exemplary public service career, and he should
be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Danny Herron for his honorable and astute
service to the people of Tennessee as president and chief executive officer of Habitat for
Humanity of Greater Nashville and extend to him our best wishes for a happy and fulfilling
retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
