HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1255
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Eddie Neil Ferguson of
Macon County.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Eddie Neil Ferguson of
Macon County; and
WHEREAS, Eddie Ferguson was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Ferguson was born on April 5, 1952, in Red Boiling Springs to Lucian
Myers and Dessie Mae Copass Ferguson; and
WHEREAS, Eddie Ferguson dedicated his career to protecting and serving the people of
Tennessee as a master trooper with the Tennessee Highway Patrol for thirty-two years; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to his family, Mr. Ferguson always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Eddie Ferguson is survived by his wife, Melinda Smith Ferguson;
daughters, Krystal (Jason) Sallee and Kastonia (Steve) Herron; son, Shane Ferguson;
grandchildren, Adalyn Sallee, Corbin Sallee, Haleigh (Tyler) Eller, Hunter Herron, Jacquelyn
Ferguson, and Kathryn Ferguson; great-grandchildren, Keegan Eller, Stone Eller, and Aspen
Herron; sister, Nina Carter; brothers, Edwin Ether "Chigger" (Judy) Ferguson, Clarence
(Lorienda) Ferguson, Huey Randall (Mary Anna) Ferguson, and Dewey Joe Ferguson (Jean
Austin); sisters-in-law, Bessie Ferguson and Geneva Ferguson; brother-in-law, Tim (Diana)
Smith; and a host of nieces and nephews; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Mr. Ferguson was preceded in death by sisters,
Ruby Deckard, Blanche Hix, and Delpha Holland; brothers, Fred Ferguson, Kenneth Ferguson,

Jesse Ferguson, Harold Ferguson, and Elder Floyd Ferguson; and father-in-law and mother-in-

WHEREAS, Eddie Ferguson leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen

WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Eddie Neil Ferguson, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Ferguson.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
