SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 895
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Kimberly Black for her
many contributions to the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Kimberly Black, a highly respected member
of her community who has dedicated herself to improving the quality of life for her fellow citizens
in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, the CEO and President of Trusted Field Services and Volunteer Flooring,
Kimberly Black is a dynamic executive leader, community advocate, and strategic growth
expert, with a strong foundation in business development, operations, and marketing strategy;
and
WHEREAS, prior to her current position, Ms. Black served as Vice President and
Director of Marketing and Development at Abundant Capital Group; she received her Bachelor
of Science degree in International Agriculture and Business from Florida A&M University; and
WHEREAS, lending her expertise and business acumen to public service, she was
appointed to the Knoxville Board of Zoning and Appeals by Knoxville Mayor Indya Kincannon
and to the Tennessee Board of Judicial Conduct by Lieutenant Governor Randy McNally; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Kimberly Black was named a 2024
Business and Industry Honoree by YWCA Knoxville and the Tennessee Valley as part of its
annual Tribute to Women celebration; she was also the recipient of the 2023 Rising Star DBE
Award presented by the City of Knoxville, recognizing her leadership and impact as a
Disadvantaged Business Enterprise; and

WHEREAS, she has rendered sterling service to a number of community and charitable
organizations, including the executive board and the Advocacy and Public Awareness
Committee of the Junior League of Knoxville, the boards of the Boys & Girls Clubs of the
Tennessee Valley and Girl Scouts of the Southern Appalachians, Habitat for Humanity, the
Women in Construction Board of the Associated General Contractors of America, and the
Knoxville Chapter of The Links, Incorporated; and
WHEREAS, a woman of integrity, vision, and passion, Kimberly Clark is committed to
excellence in business, meaningful civic engagement, and fostering impact through service and
sisterhood; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Kimberly Black for her
many contributions to the State of Tennessee, salute her professional expertise and personal
excellence, and wish her all the best in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
