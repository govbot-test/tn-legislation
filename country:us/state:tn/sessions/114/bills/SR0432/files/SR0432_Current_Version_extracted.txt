SENATE RESOLUTION 432
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor Dr. Ollie Liddell on being named the
2026 Outstanding High School Music Educator.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body take great pleasure in paying tribute to
an outstanding educator who helps his students discover deeper meaning in life and develop
important skills through music instruction; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Ollie Liddell, Director of Bands at Memphis Central High School, has
been named the 2026 Outstanding High School Music Educator by the Tennessee Music
Education Association (TMEA) in recognition of his "dedication to excellence, student
achievement, and the lasting impact of music education"; and
WHEREAS, since joining Memphis Central High School in 2012, Dr. Liddell has
developed a nationally respected band program that is built on high expectations, strong
relationships, and a commitment to continuous growth; and
WHEREAS, under his leadership, students have earned numerous superior ratings and
national recognitions, reflecting both the strength of the program and the dedication of the
young musicians he serves; and
WHEREAS, on the same day that Dr. Liddell was honored with the TMEA Outstanding
High School Music Educator award, several Memphis Central students were participating in All-
State performances, continuing the school's strong tradition of musical excellence; and
WHEREAS, under Dr. Liddell's expert direction, Memphis Central participated in the
prestigious Essentially Ellington Jazz Competition, a defining moment for his students and their
confidence in themselves; and

WHEREAS, Dr. Liddell's great influence is demonstrated by his former students' career
choices; many of them have become music educators, while others continue to perform
professionally or maintain music as a lifelong passion; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Liddell encourages his students to strive for excellence without
limitations, urging them to measure their growth against the highest standards and pushing
them to continually refine their skills; and
WHEREAS, by maintaining rigorous expectations while celebrating progress, Dr. Liddell
helps students develop resilience, discipline, and confidence that support success in all areas of
life; and
WHEREAS, music has long been a central part of Dr. Liddell's life; his father was a band
director and former Director of Bands at Jackson State University, and he grew up immersed in
the discipline and creativity that music fosters; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Liddell spent nearly a decade touring as a professional musician before
returning to higher education to earn advanced degrees in music education, including a PhD;
and
WHEREAS, if student growth is the true measure of a teacher's success, then Dr. Liddell
has achieved an even greater milestone than this prestigious award, for he has inspired his
young musicians to reach for great heights and bright futures; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Ollie Liddell is most worthy of our approbation on this special occasion;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and congratulate Dr.
Ollie Liddell on being named the 2026 Outstanding High School Music Educator by the
Tennessee Music Education Association, recognize his exemplary service as Director of Bands
