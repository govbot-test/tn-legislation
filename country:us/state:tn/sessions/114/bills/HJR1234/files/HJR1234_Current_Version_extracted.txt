HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1234
By Bulso
A RESOLUTION to honor and recognize Luke Greeson, 2026
graduate.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
those students who, through their natural talents and unflagging commitment to academic
excellence, have distinguished themselves in the classroom; and
WHEREAS, Luke Greeson is one such exceptional student, who, after compiling a
record of academic excellence, will graduate from high school this year; and
WHEREAS, through diligent study, steady composure during examinations, and
steadfast commitment of time and talent, Mr. Greeson has achieved outstanding academic
success and joined the ranks of the best and brightest students in Tennessee and our country;
and
WHEREAS, in his remarkable high school career, Luke Greeson has been named a
National Merit Finalist, an AP Scholar with Distinction, and an Outstanding Senior for the Middle
Tennessee Home Education Association, and he has earned a perfect composite score of 36 on
the ACT, along with a perfect score and Gold Summa Cum Laude on the National Latin Exam;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Greeson's display of intellectual aptitude and ambition surely bodes well
for his future success; and
WHEREAS, his noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for his family
and his community; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Greeson's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of our respect,
admiration, and commendation; now, therefore,
HJR1234
016589
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend and honor the 2026 graduate Luke Greeson on his
impressive academic achievement and extend to him our best wishes for a happy, successful
future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 2 - 016589
