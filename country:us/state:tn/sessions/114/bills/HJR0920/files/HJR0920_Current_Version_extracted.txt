HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 920
By Glynn
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Clarksville Jeep
Crew 2026.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those citizens who perform with
uncommon devotion and enthusiasm in securing the safety of their community; and
WHEREAS, the all-volunteer Clarksville Jeep Crew was established in 2019 and has
now grown to 2,300 local members; and
WHEREAS, during the recent ice storm that paralyzed Middle Tennessee, the Clarksville
Jeep Crew played a critical role, helping hundreds of stranded drivers and transporting
emergency and essential personnel when road conditions became too dangerous for many
vehicles; and
WHEREAS, the Clarksville Jeep Crew used forty to fifty Jeeps and twenty-nine active
drivers to make 276 recoveries of stranded vehicles, including two ambulances and a police car;
and
WHEREAS, the Clarksville Jeep Crew members donated a combined total of 1,053
hours to recovery efforts during the ice storm with the assistance of seven dispatchers; and
WHEREAS, the Clarksville Jeep Crew is expertly led by eight administrators, including
Barry Sullivan, Heather Sullivan, Kenneth Klein, Crystal Liberatore, Mike Liberatore, Jennifer
Fialkowski, Christopher Bruno, and Chad Hunley; and
WHEREAS, the Clarksville Jeep Crew is also involved in tornado disaster relief work in
its community and recently sent a team to North Carolina to assist with flood relief; and
WHEREAS, the members of the Clarksville Jeep Crew have used their shared love for
Jeeps to organize one of Clarksville's most reliable volunteer response groups, an admirable
exercise in public service; and

WHEREAS, their collective goal is to give back to their community, and they have
certainly succeeded in that mission; and
WHEREAS, their heroism during the horrific ice storm demonstrates their dedication to
protecting and serving their community and their commitment to the welfare of others; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the leadership and members of the Clarksville
Jeep Crew for their lifesaving rescue and recovery work during the Middle Tennessee ice storm
of January 2026 and thank them for their ongoing commitment to the safety and welfare of the
Clarksville community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
