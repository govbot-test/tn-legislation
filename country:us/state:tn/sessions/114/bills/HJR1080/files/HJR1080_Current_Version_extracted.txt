HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1080
By Barrett
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Drew Holcomb for his
dedication to and love for the State of
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those talented individuals whose achievements bring pride to our State and whose work
celebrates the rich cultural heritage of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, one such notable Tennessean is Drew Holcomb, an accomplished singer-
songwriter whose music reflects a deep affection for the Volunteer State and whose career has
carried the spirit of Tennessee to audiences across the nation and around the world; and
WHEREAS, a native of Memphis, Drew Holcomb grew up in West Tennessee before
attending the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, where he further developed his passion for
music and storytelling; and
WHEREAS, after marrying fellow singer-songwriter Ellie Holcomb, Mr. Holcomb made
his home in Nashville, where the couple reside with their three children, Emmylou, Huck, and
Rivers, and together they continue to contribute to the vibrant musical life of the Volunteer State;
and
WHEREAS, throughout his career, Drew Holcomb has remained steadfast in his love for
Tennessee, often sharing the story of his life in this great State and the enduring pull it holds for
him even as his music has taken him to stages across the world; and
WHEREAS, through his songwriting and performances, Mr. Holcomb celebrates the
people, places, and traditions that make Tennessee unique, reminding audiences everywhere of
the profound sense of home and belonging that the Volunteer State inspires; and
WHEREAS, in particular, his music has captured the deep pride Tennesseans feel for
their home, as reflected in the heartfelt refrain of his song, "Tennessee, Tennessee, I was born

here and raised here and I'll make my grave here, it's home, Tennessee," words that resonate
with listeners far beyond this State's borders; and
WHEREAS, by sharing his love for Tennessee through his music and his public life,
Drew Holcomb has served as a worthy ambassador for our State and has helped to highlight
the beauty, history, and cultural richness that define the Volunteer State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Drew Holcomb for his dedication to and love for
the State of Tennessee and extend to him our best wishes for continued success and happiness
in all his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
