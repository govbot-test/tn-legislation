HOUSE RESOLUTION 188
By Boyd
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Tennessee
Scholastic Clay Target Program on the occasion
of its twenty-fifth anniversary.
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Wildlife Federation began managing the Scholastic Clay
Target Program in 2001; over the past twenty-five years, this program has provided thousands
of Tennessee youth with the opportunity to participate in trap, skeet, and sporting clays with
peers across the State, teaching the importance of safe firearm handling, responsibility,
leadership, and teamwork while promoting healthy competition; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Scholastic Clay Target Program has grown from a
grassroots program into one of the largest programs of its kind in the nation, while positively
impacting youth across Tennessee's rural, urban, and suburban communities; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Scholastic Clay Target Program has served approximately
214,000 athletes on 112 teams across the Volunteer State and engaged over 1,300 coaches
through its volunteer coaching network, which provides structured coaching certifications and
safety programs, creating long-term coach retention; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Scholastic Clay Target Program creates a life pathway, not
merely a season, with athletes remaining engaged far beyond graduation and returning to coach
and mentor teams for years to come; and
WHEREAS, these athletes have represented the State of Tennessee at the national
level, with hundreds competing annually at the Scholastic Clay Target Program National
Championship; and
WHEREAS, through their dedication and skill, Tennessee teams have produced
numerous top-five finishes and national championship titles and rank among the top-performing
states in the history of the Scholastic Clay Target Program; now, therefore,
HR0188
017421
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby
honor and commend the Tennessee Scholastic Clay Target Program on the occasion of its
twenty-fifth anniversary, applaud its many achievements, and extend our appreciation for its
lasting and positive impact on the young men and women of this State who have participated in
the program over the past twenty-five years.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
