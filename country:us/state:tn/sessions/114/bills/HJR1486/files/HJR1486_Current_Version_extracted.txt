HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1486
By Darby
A RESOLUTION to honor the students of Gleason School on
receiving the Anne Dallas Dudley Award.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those high school students who have evidenced a commitment to civic engagement and
responsibility at an early age; and
WHEREAS, the students of Gleason School in Weakley County have been honored with
the Anne Dallas Dudley Award for their success in registering young voters; and
WHEREAS, initiated in 2021 by the Secretary of State, the Anne Dallas Dudley Awards
focus on increasing voter registration in Tennessee among students who will be eighteen or
older on or before the next election, per State law, preparing them to become actively engaged
citizens; and
WHEREAS, this prestigious award is named in honor of renowned Tennessee suffragist
Anne Dallas Dudley, a central figure in the women's suffrage movement who worked tirelessly
to secure the ratification of the 19th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, which granted women
the right to vote; and
WHEREAS, Gleason School received the 2026 Gold Level Anne Dallas Dudley Award
for registering 100 percent of the eligible voters attending the school, an impressive
accomplishment; and
WHEREAS, the future of our nation depends upon our best and brightest being actively
involved in their communities, and the students at Gleason School are well on the way to
becoming the civic leaders of tomorrow; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
HJR1486
018167
- 1 -
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate the students of Gleason School on receiving
the Anne Dallas Dudley Gold Level Award for registering 100 percent of the eligible voters at
their school and extend to them our best wishes for continued success in their future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 2 - 018167
