HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 714
By Powers
A RESOLUTION to recognize Jo Bruce on the occasion of her
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those citizens who have performed
with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their years of industry; and
WHEREAS, Jo Bruce is one such estimable person, who has served with distinction for
thirty-one years as the Director of the Oak Ridge Schools Family Resource Center in Oak
Ridge. It is one of the original thirteen Family Resource Centers established by the State of
Tennessee in the fall of 1993, and Ms. Bruce has held the position since the inception of the
program; and
WHEREAS, in her role as director, Ms. Bruce has focused on and established an
extensive knowledge of the many resources available for families and children in order to help
them access as many resources as possible; and
WHEREAS, prior to her current position in Oak Ridge, she began her career with the
Tennessee Department of Human Services (now the Department of Children's Services) as a
case manager. Her work included investigations, court interactions, placements into foster care,
and reunification of children and families, as well as managing foster care home and adoption
services; and
WHEREAS, she also previously worked in education, both as a preschool teacher in
Mississippi and as a parent educator for Campbell County Schools in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Bruce has received extensive training in the areas of the effects of
poverty on development and the rewards of quality parent engagement and thus has been a
presenter at state and local levels on these topics; and
WHEREAS, school safety and trauma recovery are priorities for Ms. Bruce, and she has
used her experience to present on these topics in Tennessee and throughout the country,

working with dedication on the SAVE (Schools Against Violence in Education) Act to put policies
and procedures in place to help keep children and school staff safe; and
WHEREAS, her commitment to her community extends to countless years of service on
a number of area nonprofit boards, including Aid to Distressed Families of Appalachian
Counties (ADFAC), United Way, Children's Advocacy Network, Emory Valley Center, TORCH,
Blossom Center, DCS CAB, and the YWCA; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, she has received the Bill Wilcox Box
Tie Award from the ADFAC, the Patriotic Civilian Service Award, the United Way Starfish
Award, the ADFAC Silent Hero Award, and numerous school-related awards and
acknowledgments at both local and state levels; and
WHEREAS, a native daughter of Tennessee, Jo Bruce was born on January 14, 1956,
to Ray and Maxine Bramlett and grew up in Jasper alongside her older brother, Larry; and
WHEREAS, after graduating fourth in her class from Marion County High School in
1974, Ms. Bruce enrolled in Middle Tennessee State University (MTSU) on a full scholarship,
where she pursued a dual major of social work and psychology with a minor in criminal justice,
receiving her degree in 1978; and
WHEREAS, during college, she met the love of her life, Ken Bruce, at a Delta Tau Delta
party. He was the president of the MTSU chapter of Delta Tau Delta, and she was honored to
become the Princess of the fraternity's Little Sisters; and
WHEREAS, Jo and Ken Bruce were married on June 23, 1979, at Falling Waters Baptist
Church in Hixson; their marriage and Mr. Bruce's career in the United States Army carried them
to many "homes" around the world, including Tracy City, Tennessee; Fort Sill, Oklahoma;
Bremerhaven, Germany; Fort Riley, Kansas; Osterholz-Scharmbeck, West Germany; Fort
Leavenworth, Kansas; and Ole Miss ROTC, Oxford, Mississippi, with some short tours to West
Point, New York; San Antonio, Texas; and Charleston, South Carolina; and
WHEREAS, the Bruces enjoyed traveling throughout Europe, including West Germany,
East Germany, Italy, Austria, Switzerland, Belgium, Denmark, France, and the Netherlands; and

WHEREAS, the happy couple welcomed two sons: Christopher Scott in 1980 and
Patrick Alan in 1984. Chris is married to Tiffany Johnson, and Patrick is married to Sheryl Hunt.
Jo Bruce dotes on and enjoys her four grandchildren, Ainsley, Brynn, William, and Kenneth; and
WHEREAS, today, Jo Bruce, widowed in 2005, lives in LaFollette and is an active
member of Our Lady of Perpetual Help Catholic Church, where she has served on the Parish
Council and the Social Justice Committee, taught confirmation classes, and shared her beautiful
voice as a soloist in the choir; and
WHEREAS, Jo Bruce epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of a
true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout her estimable career, Jo Bruce has demonstrated the utmost
professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unbridled respect and admiration of her
colleagues, her community, and the State of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we extend to Jo Bruce our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling
retirement and every continued success in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
