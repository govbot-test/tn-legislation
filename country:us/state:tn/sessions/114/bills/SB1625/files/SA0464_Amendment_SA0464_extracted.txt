Section 2:
SECTION 3. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 55-8-209(e), is amended by deleting
the first sentence, which presently reads:
Personal delivery devices may be prohibited by local resolutions or ordinances if
the local government determines that the prohibition is necessary, in the interest of
public safety.
and substituting instead:
Personal delivery devices may be regulated or prohibited by local resolutions or
ordinances if the local government determines that the regulation or prohibition is
necessary, in the interest of public safety. A regulation adopted by a local government
must not be inconsistent with this section.
AND FURTHER AMEND by deleting the amendatory language of Section 4 and substituting
instead the following:
(a)
(1) Notwithstanding another law to the contrary and except as provided in
subdivision (a)(2) and subsection (e), a person or entity may operate a personal delivery
device on a sidewalk, crosswalk, bicycle lane or path, shoulder, the area adjacent to the
right-hand edge or curb of the roadway, parking lot, or a similar area in this state.
(2) A personal delivery device must not be operated:
(A) In a bicycle lane of any road or highway where the speed limit is
greater than forty-five miles per hour (45 mph);

(B) On the shoulder or the area adjacent to the right-hand edge or curb
of the roadway of any road or highway where the speed limit is greater than forty-
five miles per hour (45 mph) or the road or highway is a part of the interstate
highway system or a controlled access highway; or
(C) In a crosswalk, whether marked or unmarked, if the device is not
programmed to properly identify and safely use a crosswalk, including being able
to:
(i) Properly react to crosswalk signage or signals;
(ii) Identify and distinguish crosswalks, pedestrians, and vehicular
traffic;
(iii) Yield or not obstruct the right-of-way to pedestrians; and
(iv) Yield or not obstruct the right-of-way of traffic turning onto the
road or highway.
(3) A personal delivery device must be regulated as, and is subject to all of the
requirements under this chapter and chapter 10, parts 1-5 of this title that are applicable
to, a pedestrian when operated in a pedestrian area or a bicyclist when operated in all
other areas, except as to those provisions that by their nature can have no application or
that would place an unreasonable burden on the operation of the personal delivery
device.
