SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 617
By Hatcher
A RESOLUTION urging the passage of H.R. 2350 (S. 1137) of the
119th Congress to permit states to deploy cell-
phone jamming systems in prisons.
WHEREAS, federal law currently bans states from using cell-phone jamming technology,
leaving correctional facilities defenseless against the escalating threat of contraband cell
phones within their walls; and
WHEREAS, cell phones are not merely communication devices in prison; they are also
tools that enable convicts to conspire and commit crimes that grievously wound our citizens and
our communities; and
WHEREAS, H.R. 2350 (S. 1137) of the 119th Congress (sponsored by Tennessee
Congressman David Kustoff and Arkansas Senator Tom Cotton) seeks to disrupt inmates' ability
to orchestrate crimes from behind bars using smuggled cell phones; and
WHEREAS, this timely legislation would allow the states to deploy cell-phone jamming
systems in prisons but use only equipment that would not disrupt emergency signals such as 9-
1-1; and
WHEREAS, there can be no doubt that the proliferation of contraband cell phones is a
problem in state correctional facilities; a 2020 survey of twenty state corrections departments
uncovered 25,840 contraband cell phones in a single year, a stark reminder of the scale of
illegal activity by inmates; and
WHEREAS, in Tennessee and across the nation, investigations consistently reveal the
use of contraband phones by inmates to coordinate with external criminal networks in the
commission of felonies; and
WHEREAS, inmates typically leverage these contraband devices to:
1. Direct drug-trafficking operations with unprecedented efficiency;

2. Orchestrate violent crimes both inside and outside correctional facilities;
3. Execute complex fraud schemes targeting vulnerable citizens;
4. Intimidate witnesses and threaten victims' families; and
5. Coordinate escape attempts that put law enforcement officers at risk; and
WHEREAS, jamming systems represent the most effective technology available to
combat the use of contraband cell phones in prison and prevent inmates from directing criminal
enterprises while behind bars; and
WHEREAS, H.R. 2350 (S. 1137) of the 119th Congress offers a narrowly tailored solution
to this critical threat to our state correctional systems, while ensuring appropriate oversight and
safeguards; and
WHEREAS, a bipartisan coalition of thirty state attorneys general recently endorsed this
landmark legislation as an effective tool in the management of our prisons and the protection of
our citizens; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we urge the U.S. Congress to act decisively and
enact H.R. 2350 (S. 1137) of the 119th Congress, bipartisan legislation that permits states to
deploy cell-phone jamming systems in prisons, as a vital public safety measure.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that a certified copy of this resolution be transmitted to
the President and the Secretary of the U.S. Senate, the Speaker and the Clerk of the U.S.
House of Representatives, and each member of Tennessee's delegation to the U.S. Congress.
