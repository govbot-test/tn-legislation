HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 870
By Jones J
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Larry Bushart of
Henderson County.
WHEREAS, on September 21, 2025, in the middle of the night, Larry Bushart, a retired
police officer with more than three decades of experience, was arrested on false charges due to
having posted a pre-existing political meme on social media; and
WHEREAS, according to news reports, Perry County issued a warrant for his arrest and
charged him with "threatening mass violence at a school"; and
WHEREAS, handcuffed and put behind bars, Mr. Bushart could not afford his bond,
which was set at $2 million, and he spent the next thirty-seven days in the Perry County jail; as
a result of his arrest, he lost his job and missed both his wedding anniversary and the birth of
his grandchild; and
WHEREAS, after widespread media coverage of his arrest and incarceration, Larry
Bushart was finally released from jail, and all charges were dismissed; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bushart has said, "I spent over three decades in law enforcement, and
have the utmost respect for the law. But I also know my rights, and I was arrested for nothing
more than refusing to be bullied into censorship"; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend retired law enforcement officer Larry Bushart of
Henderson County for his defense of the First Amendment.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
