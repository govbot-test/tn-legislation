SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1142
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to recognize Julie Gail Adkission on the occasion
of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
careers in public service; and
WHEREAS, Julie Gail Adkission, Register of Deeds for Hardin County, is one such
estimable individual, who has distinguished herself as a professional of the greatest dedication,
ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, after seven terms and nearly twenty-eight years as Hardin County Register
of Deeds, Ms. Adkission is taking her much-deserved retirement from that office at the end of
her current term on August 31, 2026; and
WHEREAS, first elected in August 1998 and assuming office on September 1 of that
year, Julie Gail Adkission has provided steady and faithful leadership in maintaining the public
records of Hardin County through a period of significant technological transition and growth; and
WHEREAS, during her tenure, she oversaw the transition from handwritten records to
digital recordkeeping, ensuring the preservation, accessibility, and accuracy of vital property
records for the citizens of Hardin County; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Adkission's commitment to professional excellence is reflected in her
extensive involvement in statewide and regional organizations, having served as president of
the Tennessee Registers Association in 2010 and having been named Tennessee Registers
Association Register of the Year for 2006-2007; and
WHEREAS, she has further distinguished herself through service as secretary and
president of the Tennessee Registers of Deeds Association, as well as secretary, treasurer, and
president of the West Tennessee Registers Association, and through her participation on the

Tennessee County Officials Association as secretary and on numerous committees over the

WHEREAS, Julie Gail Adkission began her career in public service at the Hardin County
Courthouse in 1985, working for Judge Rayburn Tyler and later for Judge Max Seaton, thereby
contributing more than four decades of dedicated service to the citizens of Hardin County; and
WHEREAS, Julie Adkission was recognized as Hardin County's Woman of the Year in
2003; and
WHEREAS, throughout her distinguished career, Ms. Adkission has demonstrated the
utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unqualified respect and admiration of
her colleagues and the citizens she has so ably served; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Julie Gail Adkission upon
her retirement as Hardin County Register of Deeds, thank her for her many years of dedicated
service, and extend to her our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every
continued success in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
