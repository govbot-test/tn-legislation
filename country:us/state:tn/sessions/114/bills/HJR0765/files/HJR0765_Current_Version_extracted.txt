HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 765
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor the service of the Ingle family of
Hamilton County.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute the
valiant individuals who devote themselves to providing essential services to those throughout
their community; and
WHEREAS, the Ingle family of Hamilton County has a distinguished legacy of
generational service to those throughout the region, selflessly giving of their time and placing
their own lives in peril to ensure the safety of others; and
WHEREAS, the Ingle family's story has been intertwined with the Hamilton County
Special Tactics and Rescue Services team for decades, with four members of the family
currently serving on the volunteer team, which supplements police, fire, and emergency medical
services on some of the county's most demanding incidents; and
WHEREAS, headquartered in Harrison, the Hamilton County Special Tactics and
Rescue team is currently led by Chief Clay Ingle, whose father, Clyde Morris "Shot" Ingle,
previously led the team; the elder Ingle gave his life in the line of duty, succumbing to a heart
attack he had while on call; and
WHEREAS, Hamilton County Special Tactics and Rescue was founded on February 3,
1983, as a state-chartered, volunteer rescue agency to respond to a wide range of calls—from
missing children and wandering elders to swift-water incidents and natural disaster response;
and
WHEREAS, Chief Clay Ingle recalls the day the agency was founded, which was the
same day he filled out his application to join the team; he currently serves alongside his sister,
son, and daughter; and

WHEREAS, the Ingles were there from the hardscrabble early days of the agency,
when, before a headquarters existed, equipment was stored at members' houses, and monthly
meetings were held at a local restaurant; when a headquarters was established in 2012,
members would regularly cut the utilities off to save power whenever possible; and
WHEREAS, over the ensuing decades, the Ingle family has helped Hamilton County
Special Tactics and Rescue Services grow and thrive to a team of approximately fifty members;
today, the agency operates two stations and has received various grants and funding initiatives
that have helped purchase necessities, such as a command assistance van and a water rescue
and firefighting boat; and
WHEREAS, members of the Ingle family have devoted long hours and often sacrificed
time together on holidays to ensure the safety or safe recovery of individuals in peril; they have
volunteered their time while maintaining full-time employment, answering an average of
between seventy-five and ninety calls annually, which can take as long as a few hours to a few
days; and
WHEREAS, beyond the rescue agency, Ingle family members have served their
community through careers in law enforcement, firefighting, and nursing; and
WHEREAS, throughout the rescue agency's history, from its longtime leaders to the
team's first death on call, Ingles have made volunteerism and search-and-rescue part of their
family's identity; and
WHEREAS, the Ingle family has exhibited a singular devotion to serving others; they are
truly dedicated to the well-being of their fellow Tennesseans, and for their prodigious efforts on
behalf of the people of this State, they should be recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the members of the Ingle family, salute their
