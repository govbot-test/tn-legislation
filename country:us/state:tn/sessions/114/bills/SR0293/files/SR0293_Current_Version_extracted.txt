SENATE RESOLUTION 293
By Harshbarger
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Bobby Lynn Jarnagin of
Surgoinsville.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Bobby Lynn Jarnagin
of Surgoinsville; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jarnagin was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Jarnagin was born on February 27, 1947, to Dewey and Fannie
Jarnagin; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jarnagin retired from Holliston Mills after forty years of devoted service,
and he served his community as a member of the Hawkins County Volunteer Rescue Squad;
and
WHEREAS, Bobby Jarnagin owned and operated New Dimensions Hair Salon with his
wife, and after her passing, he returned to work with the Hawkins County Highway Department;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jarnagin served the town of Surgoinsville as an alderman for many
years and was also an active member of the Surgoinsville Ruritan Club; and
WHEREAS, an avid Tennessee Vols and Atlanta Braves fan, Bobby Jarnagin enjoyed
classic cars, fishing, and time spent with his friends and family, especially his grandson; and
WHEREAS, a faithful believer in Christ, Mr. Jarnagin was a member of First Free Will
Baptist Church in Surgoinsville; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and

WHEREAS, Bobby Jarnagin will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his
daughter, Nikki (Cliff) Cristy; son, Brad (Jennifer) Jarnagin; grandson, Braylon Jarnagin; sister-
in-law, Barbara Bamman; cousins, Joyce Sizemore and Harold Swatsell; dear friends, Rhonda
Salyer and Megan Gentry; and a host of extended family and friends; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his beloved wife of
forty-eight years, Shirley Jarnagin, and brother, Jimmy Don Jarnagin; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jarnagin leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor the memory of Bobby
Lynn Jarnagin, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to
living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Jarnagin.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
