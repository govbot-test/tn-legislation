HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1342
By Cochran
A RESOLUTION to honor the 250th anniversary of the United
States Postal Service.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should join with the
citizens of this great State in celebrating historic occasions; and
WHEREAS, the United States Postal Service celebrated its 250th anniversary in 2025,
commemorating its establishment in 1775, one year earlier than the founding of the country
itself; and
WHEREAS, for 250 years, the United States Postal Service has bound this nation
together, serving Americans across the country and providing reliable mail services to rural and
urban communities, while also opposing efforts to dismantle, privatize, or weaken the vitality of
the institution; and
WHEREAS, the United States Postal Service is owned by the people and is a wonderful
national treasure encapsulating the best of government "of, by, and for the people," and it is vital
that it remains public, affordable, and accessible to all; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the United States Postal Service on the occasion of its 250th
anniversary and salute the hard-working postal workers of the nation.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
