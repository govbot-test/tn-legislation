SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1045
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to commend D.T. McCall & Sons of Middle
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should applaud those
Tennessee businesses that have a longstanding commitment to providing exceptional service to
their customers; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable entity is D.T. McCall & Sons, which has ably served the
citizens of Middle Tennessee for more than 100 years; and
WHEREAS, D.T. McCall & Sons traces its beginnings back to 1896, when Albert and
Icie McCall opened their general store in Flat Rock; their son, D.T. McCall, was a salesman, and
their grandson, Albert Burr McCall, Sr., helped in the store during the summer; and
WHEREAS, after time as a salesman at his parent's store, D.T. McCall began his own
business using a horse and buggy to go to homes throughout Smith County, buying and selling
produce and other goods; he brought the first electric ranges to Carthage, and in the late 1930s,
he bought a corner lot on the town square, where he opened a furniture and appliance store;
and
WHEREAS, his son, Albert McCall, earned a bachelor's degree in agriculture from
Tennessee Technological University in 1950 and a master's degree in animal nutrition from the
University of Missouri in 1951; he taught agriculture for one year at Winterset High School in
Iowa before serving as a Second Lieutenant and forward observer in the Korean War, ultimately
being discharged at the rank of First Lieutenant; and
WHEREAS, he returned to Iowa following his military service, and it was there he met
his future wife, Virginia; the couple moved to Albert McCall's native Carthage, where he bought

into his father's furniture and appliance business, joined by his brothers, Dave McCall and John
McCall, and his nephew, Tom McCall; and
WHEREAS, Albert McCall proved himself motivated and industrious; he was an expert
at finding and creating opportunities to source products at low prices, which led the business's
growth and allowed him to pass savings on to customers; and
WHEREAS, in 1958, he learned that a local cheese company was changing its
manufacturing process, which meant that 1,700 dairy farmers would need new industrial milk
coolers to continue to supply the company with milk; further research showed that North
Carolina had recently outlawed the exact type of cooler that the Tennessee manufacturer
required, so after several trips East, he bought 1,000 coolers and had them shipped by truck to
Tennessee. On the way back, they stopped in South Carolina to fill the coolers with peaches to
sell back in Carthage; and
WHEREAS, in 1959, Albert McCall discovered that 500 commercial size Korean-War-
surplus electric ranges were available from a company in Akron, Ohio, a part of Ohio that used
primarily natural gas for heating and cooling, while TVA provided electricity for Middle
Tennessee; he bought the ranges for a great price and passed the savings along to various
schools in the State; and
WHEREAS, when oil prices fell in 1984, large inventories of household furnishings that
were originally intended for condos in Saudi Arabia were sitting in a warehouse in Houston,
Texas, and up for auction; Mr. McCall traveled to Houston to buy the full inventory, moving
thirty-seven truckloads in seven days, ultimately passing along the savings to his customers
while also making a good profit; and
WHEREAS, for twenty years, Mr. McCall advertised on The Ralph Emery Show on
Channel Four between 5:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m.; his live commercials were so popular that
Channel Four used them to promote the Emery show, resulting in business booming and Albert

McCall becoming a popular television personality in Channel Four's Middle
Tennessee/Southern Kentucky/Northern Alabama viewing audience; and
WHEREAS, The Ralph Emery Show had the highest lead-in audience to The Today
Show in the entire country, and D.T. McCall & Sons became the forty-ninth largest furniture and
appliance store in the United States, according to Home Furnishings Daily, a magazine that also
named D.T. McCall & Sons number one in the United States in profit per dollars' worth of sales
at that time; and
WHEREAS, D.T. McCall & Sons currently serves customers at five locations throughout
Middle Tennessee and is led by the fourth generation of the McCall family: Albert "AJ" McCall,
Jr., Cris McCall, JJ McCall, and Frank McCall; and
WHEREAS, for more than a century, D.T. McCall & Sons has been providing superior
products and customer service, and it is fitting that the business be commended; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commend D.T. McCall & Sons for its many years
of devoted service to the citizens of this State, extending our best wishes for much continued
success in the future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
