HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 790
By Atchley
A RESOLUTION to recognize fire protection services as an
essential service.
WHEREAS, the safety, health, and welfare of the citizens of Tennessee are the primary
responsibility of the State and local governments; and
WHEREAS, fire protection services constitute the first line of defense for Tennessee
communities, not only against the ravages of fire but also as primary responders to medical
emergencies, hazardous materials incidents, natural disasters, and technical rescues; and
WHEREAS, the dedicated men and women of Tennessee's fire services, encompassing
career, part-time, and volunteer professionals across the Volunteer State, place their lives in
jeopardy daily to protect the lives and property of their neighbors; and
WHEREAS, the availability of robust fire protection services is directly linked to the
economic vitality of the State, influencing homeowner and business insurance rates through ISO
ratings and ensuring the continuity of business operations; and
WHEREAS, historically, while law enforcement and emergency medical services are
frequently codified as essential services, fire protection has often been viewed as a
discretionary service in certain jurisdictions, leading to disparities in funding, resources, and
staffing stability; and
WHEREAS, the recent increase in call volumes, the complexity of modern emergency
response, and the challenges regarding recruitment and retention of qualified personnel require
that fire protection be elevated to a status that reflects its critical nature; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that the State of Tennessee recognizes fire protection services as an essential
