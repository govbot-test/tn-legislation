HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1037
By Jones J
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the eightieth anniversary of
Philippine-American Friendship Day.
WHEREAS, on July 4, 1946, following Japan's defeat in World War II, the United States
formally recognized the independence of the Philippines, marking the birth of the Republic of the
Philippines after years of colonial rule and the trials of World War II; and
WHEREAS, the 1946 Treaty of Manila officially relinquished United States sovereignty
over the Philippines, and the date of July 4 was celebrated as Independence Day in the
Philippines from 1946 to 1961; and
WHEREAS, today, July 4 is observed in the Philippines as Philippine-American
Friendship Day, a day that pays tribute to the long partnership between the two nations; and
WHEREAS, this partnership was strengthened during World War II, when President
Franklin D. Roosevelt ordered to duty the Philippine Scouts, the Philippine Commonwealth
Army, and the Philippine Constabulary to defend the Philippines and the United States against
the Imperial Japanese military forces; and
WHEREAS, more than 260,000 Filipinos fought during the four-year campaign, resulting
in some 57,000 killed and thousands more wounded; the Japanese invasion resulted in the
capture of approximately 76,000 American and Filipino prisoners of war who endured the
Bataan Death March; and
WHEREAS, among those who distinguished themselves throughout the war was
Magdalena E. Leones, who was the only Asian woman awarded the Silver Star during World
War II, the third-highest honor for valor in the United States Army, in recognition of her gallantry
and service; and

WHEREAS, similarly demonstrating courage and devotion to duty was Master Sergeant
Aaron Kliatchko, known as the "Rabbi of Cabanatuan," who volunteered as an American
intelligence agent when the Japanese invaded the Philippines in December 1941 and fought the
invading forces on the Bataan peninsula; in 1948, he was posthumously awarded the Medal of
Freedom, the highest honor the United States can bestow upon a civilian; and
WHEREAS, Philippine-American Friendship Day provides an opportunity to remember
the sacrifices and solidarity that have shaped the diplomatic relationship between the two
nations and to look forward to continued cooperation and friendship; and
WHEREAS, the Filipino population in Tennessee exceeds 30,000, making it the third-
largest Asian ethnic group in the State, and this vibrant community enriches the civic, cultural,
and economic life of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commemorate the eightieth anniversary of Philippine-American
Friendship Day, recognize the enduring partnership between the Republic of the Philippines and
the United States of America, and extend our appreciation to the Filipino community in
Tennessee for its many contributions to this State.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
