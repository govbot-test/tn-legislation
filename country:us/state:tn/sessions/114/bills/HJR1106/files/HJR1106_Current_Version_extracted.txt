HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1106
By Moon
A RESOLUTION to honor Senior Master Sergeant Chad E.
Wallace, Air National Guard, on the occasion of
his retirement.
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State is proud to be the home of so many valiant men and
women who have performed above and beyond the call of duty in the United States Armed
Forces, and their distinguished military service should be properly honored; and
WHEREAS, Senior Master Sergeant (SMSgt) Chad E. Wallace currently serves as
Cyber Systems Operations Superintendent for the 119th Cyberspace Operations Squadron at
McGhee Tyson Air National Guard Base in Louisville, Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, as the Senior Enlisted Advisor to the Division Commander, SMSgt Wallace
provides leadership and mentorship to ensure the operational readiness and professional
development of more than thirty Airmen who are responsible for providing tactical and
emergency communications support for State and national-level operations; and
WHEREAS, SMSgt Wallace is a prior-service U.S. Army veteran, having served from
1989 to 1996 as a Test, Measurement, and Diagnostic Equipment Specialist and an Imagery
Analyst before joining the Air National Guard in 2007; and
WHEREAS, during his initial Air Guard service as a Communications Computer Systems
Controller in the 228th Combat Communications Squadron, he supported domestic emergency
response missions, including providing critical communications for first responders during
Hurricane Gustav in 2008; and
WHEREAS, SMSgt Wallace later transferred to the 119th Cyber Operations Squadron,
where he served in various leadership roles before being promoted to Senior Enlisted Leader
over the entire Systems Communication Operations Division; and

WHEREAS, throughout his career, SMSgt Wallace has been instrumental in providing
communications support for numerous high-visibility and zero-fail missions; and
WHEREAS, he has supported three presidential inaugurations, ensuring resilient
command and control for joint taskforce security operations; and
WHEREAS, SMSgt Wallace has also been a key communications expert during multiple
natural disasters, including the 2016 Gatlinburg wildfires and Hurricane Helene in 2024; and
WHEREAS, his expertise has been crucial in over a dozen joint and interagency
exercises, including the Patriot and Vigilant Guard series; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of Clinton High School, SMSgt Wallace has completed the
Airman Leadership School, Non-Commissioned Officer Academy, and Senior Non-
Commissioned Officer Academy and holds an Associate of Applied Science degree in
Information Systems Technology from the Community College of the Air Force; and
WHEREAS, he has received numerous decorations during his peerless military career,
including the Meritorious Service Medal, Air Force Commendation Medal with oak leaf cluster,
Army Commendation Medal with oak leaf cluster, Air Force Achievement Medal, and Army
Achievement Medal; and
WHEREAS, after serving his country with pride, acumen, and integrity for more than
thirty-five years, SMSgt Wallace is taking a much-deserved retirement from the Air National
Guard; and
WHEREAS, while no offering can properly relay our gratitude, it is most appropriate that
we should honor SMSgt Chad E. Wallace to the full extent of our ability; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor Senior Master Sergeant Chad E. Wallace, Air National Guard, on
the occasion of his retirement, thank him for his long and distinguished service to his country,
- 2 - 015056
salute his essential work in tactical and emergency communications during emergency
response, security, and training operations, and extend to him our best wishes for much
continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
