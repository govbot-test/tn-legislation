SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 591
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Foguie Junior McKinnie
of Memphis.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Foguie Junior McKinnie of Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Foguie McKinnie was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on May 17, 1955, in Benton County, Mr. McKinnie was the son of Earl
and Lillie Brown McKinnie; and
WHEREAS, Foguie McKinnie attended school in Hardeman County and finished his
education in Memphis; and
WHEREAS, after finishing his schooling, Mr. McKinnie proudly served as a veteran of
the United States Army; and
WHEREAS, having confessed the Lord as his Savior at a young age, Mr. McKinnie was
a devout member of Pleasant Grove Church, now known as Rivers of Life International
Ministries; and
WHEREAS, Foguie McKinnie was deeply devoted to his faith and his family, and he
always endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Foguie McKinnie is survived by his loving wife, Rosie Lee Patterson
McKinnie; his children, Tammy Camper, Tomeka McKinnie, Brenda Macon, Revin McKinnie,
and Rhyan Moore; his sisters, Mary McKinnie and Visie McKinnie; his brother, Minister John
Albert (Rosie) McKinnie; his sister-in-law, Linda (Larry) Hoyle; his brother-in-law, Woody
(Beatrice) Hunt; thirteen grandchildren; three great-grandchildren; and a host of nieces,
nephews, and other relatives; and

WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Mr. McKinnie was preceded in death by his
brothers, Earl B. McKinnie, Jr., and Robert (Thelma) McKinnie; and his sisters, Emily (Lee
Chester) Woods, Lillian Neely, Idella Murden Brown, Lucille Chism, Vandella Hill, and Flossie
Geanes; and
WHEREAS, the epitome of excellence, Foguie McKinnie was a faithful soul who
impacted the lives of those who were blessed to know him, and he leaves behind an indelible
legacy of integrity and probity in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence
and dedication in all his chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Foguie Junior McKinnie,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. McKinnie.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
