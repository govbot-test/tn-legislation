HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 749
By Jones R
A RESOLUTION to recognize and address the importance of
substance abuse prevention education.
WHEREAS, the State of Tennessee is in a crisis, overtaken by substance abuse,
including alcohol abuse, and mental health disorders; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Together Student Survey of 2022-2023 reported that one in
fifteen students had misused prescription drugs, with 8.5 percent of students reporting that at
least one of their closest friends had used prescription drugs not prescribed to them in the past
school year; and
WHEREAS, the same survey reported that 29.8 percent of Tennessee students had
vaped nicotine, 13 percent had vaped marijuana, and 14.4 percent had vaped flavoring only,
with the average age of initiation to vaping to be 13.8 years of age; and
WHEREAS, the survey further showed that approximately one in three students in
Tennessee had reported drinking alcohol, with an average age of initiation to be 13.4 years of
age; and
WHEREAS, in 2022-2023, 1,138,000 Tennesseans over the age of twelve were
classified as needing substance use treatment, with only 318,000 receiving treatment, including
31,000 between twelve and seventeen years of age; and
WHEREAS, prevention education should begin in kindergarten, educating and
empowering our children to make healthy choices, as seventy-four percent of substance abuse
begins by age seventeen, with ten percent beginning by age eleven; and
WHEREAS, when we spend time building positive childhood experiences, it reduces
negative health outcomes later in life for our children; and

- 1 -
WHEREAS, effective preventions that work in schools include long-term, evidence-
based programs that start as early as kindergarten and continue through grade twelve; a social-
emotional learning curriculum that includes decision-making, problem solving, and emotional
regulations skills; mental health integration that provides access to professionals for one-on-one
student support, specifically for issues like domestic violence, bullying, and suicidal thoughts,
etc.; accessibility that is embedded in existing health and physical education curricula, with
resources available to parents and communities; and community connections that link students
and families to coalitions and local support services; and
WHEREAS, prevention education reduces the economic burden on the State of
Tennessee while fostering healthier communities; every $1.00 spent on prevention saves $4.60-
$18.00 in healthcare and criminal justice costs; and
WHEREAS, prevention saves lives, breaks generational cycles, and secures a healthier
future for Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that it is vital to the future of the State of Tennessee and its citizens that we
commit to do whatever it takes to reverse the alcohol and drug addiction rate that has been
growing for the last seventy years in Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that due to the information gathered via the Tennessean
Youth Survey and reviewed by the Tennessee Prevention Coalition, we fully support and
recommend that a long-term early intervention program that follows the student through
graduation with an evidence-based drug prevention, life skills training, and violence prevention
program be implemented into the required curricula in our schools.
- 2 - 010418
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that appropriate copies of this resolution be transmitted
to the commissioner of the Tennessee Department of Education and the executive director of
the State Board of Education.
