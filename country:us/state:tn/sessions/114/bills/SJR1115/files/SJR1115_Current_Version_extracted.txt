SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1115
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Almae Ray Chappell
on the celebration of her ninety-fourth birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to pay
tribute to those citizens who are celebrating special occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, Almae Ray Chappell celebrates her ninety-fourth birthday this year, a
milestone commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of life's rich pageant; and
WHEREAS, born in Acton on May 7, 1932, Mrs. Chappell has lived a remarkable life,
having experienced firsthand the most eventful and productive years of American history; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Chappell is deeply devoted to her family and treasures every moment
spent with them; she was joined in holy matrimony with the love of her life, Ecil Chappell, and
together they have been blessed with ten children, twenty-one grandchildren, and a host of
great-grandchildren and great-great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Almae Chappell is a woman of many talents and passions who enjoys
cooking and relaxing on her front porch—especially on Sunday evenings—and loves baking
both large and small pecan pies and sharing her renowned grape jelly with neighbors; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Chappell is an inspiration to all who have the good fortune of knowing
her, and her guidance and dedication to family and her community are immeasurable; and
WHEREAS, Almae Chappell will observe her birthday on May 2, 2026, surrounded by
her loved ones, and celebrate ninety-four years of love, laughter, family, friendship, and
cherished memories; and
WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
SJR1115
017805
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Almae Ray Chappell on
the celebration of her ninety-fourth birthday and extend to her our best wishes for many happy
returns of the day.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
