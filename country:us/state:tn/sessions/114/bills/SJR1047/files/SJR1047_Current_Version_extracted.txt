SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1047
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to honor the management and employees of
Clayton Home's Rutledge frame shop on forty-
two years of service to homebuyers.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in paying tribute
to those outstanding Tennesseans who have helped make the American dream more
accessible by building high-quality, yet affordable, housing; and
WHEREAS, March 26, 2026, truly marked the end of an era, as Clayton Home's
Rutledge facility turned out its last house frame after forty-two years in operation; and
WHEREAS, this fateful day also saw the beginning of a new era, as Clayton Homes will
now obtain frames from a nearby sister facility, and many Rutledge employees are transitioning
to the Clayton Bean Station frame shop as part of the company's new venture; and
WHEREAS, during his nearly twenty-five years of employment at the Rutledge frame
shop, Tracy Cunningham, the facility's team leader and most tenured member, established his
legacy by helping build more than 20,000 frames; and
WHEREAS, Chris Williams, Daniel Starnes, Bobby Taft, Rick Sexson, Tracy Ayers, and
Dalton Vail were the other vital employees who thrived at the Rutledge frame shop; and
WHEREAS, the State of Tennessee treasures its hard-working men and women who,
through the fruits of their labor, help meet the basic needs of their fellow citizens, and nothing is
more essential than a home to call your own; and
WHEREAS, these dedicated professionals are worthy of our approbation, today and
every day; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the management and employees of
