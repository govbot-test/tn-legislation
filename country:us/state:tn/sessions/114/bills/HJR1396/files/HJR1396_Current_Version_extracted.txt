HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1396
By Todd
A RESOLUTION commending trapping as an effective wildlife
management tool.
WHEREAS, regulated trapping has long served as a vital and scientifically grounded tool
for wildlife conservation and management, enabling state, federal, and tribal agencies to
maintain balanced ecosystems, control predator populations, and protect critical habitats
essential for biodiversity and species recovery; and
WHEREAS, trapping plays a pivotal role in furbearer management by protecting
endangered and vulnerable species during key life-cycle stages, such as ground-nesting birds
and sea turtles, while facilitating the successful reintroduction of populations such as river otter
and beaver, thereby promoting healthy wildlife communities across diverse landscapes; and
WHEREAS, through targeted predator management, trapping significantly enhances
waterfowl production in high-density breeding areas, where it reduces predation on duck eggs
by species such as raccoons and skunks, boosting nest success rates from as low as 5 percent
to 10 percent to the 15 percent to 20 percent threshold needed for stable populations and
ultimately increasing duck numbers by tens of thousands and restoring natural breeding
potential; and
WHEREAS, the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service recognizes trapping as an integral
component of integrated pest management on national wildlife refuges, which is regulated under
federal laws to sustainably manage wildlife populations, eradicate invasive species, control
pests, and safeguard refuge infrastructure while ensuring compliance with state licensing and
humane standards, including the trapping of feral wild pig populations that inflict $2.5 billion in
damage across thirty-five states; and

017806
- 1 -
WHEREAS, according to the 2024 Association of Fish and Wildlife Agencies trapping
survey, regulated private trapping subsidizes nuisance control, preventing escalated damage
and avoiding higher expenses for taxpayers by saving, on average, $16.5 million dollars; and
WHEREAS, trapping provides essential benefits, including disease control, economic
value through fur harvesting, prevention of costly damage such as beaver-induced flooding, and
effective population regulation of overabundant species to prevent the degradation of habitats
supporting native wildlife recovery; and
WHEREAS, only abundant wildlife species are subject to regulated trapping seasons set
by the Tennessee Fish and Wildlife Commission under scientifically based laws that monitor
populations, while the Tennessee Wildlife Resources Agency provides education and licensing
programs to foster ethical practices among participants; this regulatory structure reflects
widespread public support for trapping as a responsible stewardship of natural resources; and
WHEREAS, according to the most recent data from the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service,
on the national level, trappers contribute $884 million in Gross Domestic Product from related
spending; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commend regulated trapping as an essential, humane, and effective
component of modern wildlife management and conservation in Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we honor the education and outreach efforts that
inform the public about the role of regulated trapping in balanced wildlife management and
sustainable resource use.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we appreciate the contributions made by trappers to
state wildlife management.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
