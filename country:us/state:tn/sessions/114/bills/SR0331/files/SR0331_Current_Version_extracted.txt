SENATE RESOLUTION 331
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor Reverend Shavon Arline-Bradley,
keynote speaker for the 113th Founders Day
Observance of the Memphis Alumnae Chapter
of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those individuals who, through their
extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as leaders of whom we can all be proud;
and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy individual is Reverend Shavon Arline-Bradley, who is
the keynote speaker this year at Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., Memphis Alumnae Chapter's
113th Founders Day Observance; and
WHEREAS, a truly inspirational leader, Reverend Shavon Arline-Bradley is the president
and chief executive officer of the National Council of Negro Women and has more than two
decades of experience in health care, diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI), government affairs,
and executive leadership; and
WHEREAS, Reverend Shavon Arline-Bradley is also the founding principal and CEO of
R.E.A.C.H. Beyond Solutions, a public health, advocacy, and executive leadership firm that
promotes DEI, political and organizational strategy, risk management, government affairs, and
technical assistance; and
WHEREAS, prior to establishing R.E.A.C.H., she served as a senior advisor and the
director of external engagement with the United States Department of Health and Human
Services under the 19th U.S. Surgeon General, Dr. Vice Admiral Vivek H. Murthy, and enjoyed a
tenure as the executive vice president of strategic planning and partnerships and chief of staff
with the NAACP; and

WHEREAS, Reverend Arline-Bradley has served in a leadership capacity with a number
of groups and organizations, including Delta Women for Action, NAACP Board of Directors
Health Committee, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., the National Social Action Commission,
and the Oprah Winfrey Network "OWN Your Health" initiative; and
WHEREAS, lending her time and talents to a number of worthy endeavors, she is an
active member of the American Public Health Association, Links, Inc., and Jack & Jill of
America, Inc.; and
WHEREAS, a native of New Jersey, Reverend Arline-Bradley holds an undergraduate
degree from Tulane University in New Orleans, Louisiana, and a Master of Divinity degree from
the Samuel Dewitt Proctor School of Theology at Virginia Union University; she has completed
an Executive Certificate of Business Management from Howard University and an Executive
Certificate in Diversity and Inclusion from Cornell University; and
WHEREAS, throughout all of her professional endeavors, Reverend Arline-Bradley has
enjoyed the love and support of her husband, Andrew Bradley, and her two children; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we honor this inspirational leader on this special
occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we welcome Reverend Shavon
Arline-Bradley to Tennessee, commend her many contributions to public service and the greater
good, and extend to her our best wishes for much continued success in all her endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
