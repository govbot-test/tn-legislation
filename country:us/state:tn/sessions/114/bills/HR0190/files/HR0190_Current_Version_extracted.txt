HOUSE RESOLUTION 190
By Lamberth
A RESOLUTION to honor Chase Ingle upon his exemplary service
to House Majority Leader William Lamberth and
the Tennessee General Assembly.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those invaluable legislative employees who have performed with unwavering devotion and
enthusiasm throughout their tenure with the General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, Chase Ingle, former Chief Adviser to House Majority Leader William
Lamberth, is one such estimable professional, who rendered sterling service from the
commencement of his employment with the House Republican Caucus in January 2023; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Ingle was an integral member of Leader Lamberth's team, providing
counsel on key legislation and government issues and serving as a primary communication
channel between the House Republican Caucus, the Senate Republican Caucus, and the
administration of Governor Bill Lee; and
WHEREAS, in this capacity, he worked closely with members of the General Assembly
to advance legislative priorities, demonstrating sound judgment, discretion, and a keen
understanding of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Chase Ingle brought to his role a wealth of prior experience in public
service, including his work as Director of External Affairs for the Tennessee Public Charter
School Commission and as a legislative assistant to members of the General Assembly, as well
as his early service as a legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, a native of Sevier County, Mr. Ingle is a graduate of the University of
Tennessee, Knoxville, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts degree; and
WHEREAS, during his distinguished tenure with the House Republican Caucus, Chase
Ingle not only excelled in his official duties, but he also contributed to the smooth operation of
