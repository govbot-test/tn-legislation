SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 657
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to approve, pursuant to Tennessee Code
Annotated, Section 7-56-102 and subject to the
oversight and approval of the department of
transportation, the Nolensville Pike, Gallatin
Pike, and Downtown corridor projects as
included in the Metropolitan Government of
Nashville and Davidson County's Choose How
You Move transportation improvement program,
as adopted by the voters on November 5, 2024.
WHEREAS, Tennessee is a leader in transportation innovation; and
WHEREAS, pursuant to the IMPROVE Act and following all required procedural steps
outlined in Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 67-4-3206(b)-(f), on November 5, 2024, voters
of the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and Davidson County overwhelmingly approved a
sales tax surcharge to be used as a dedicated funding source for transportation improvements
included in the Choose How You Move program; and
WHEREAS, the General Assembly recognizes that, pursuant to the IMPROVE Act, local
government investment in transportation programs is in the public interest; and
WHEREAS, the establishment of a dedicated local funding source for transportation
improvements enables the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and Davidson County to
increase the competitiveness and readiness of Choose How You Move federal grant
applications by providing required local match dollars; and
WHEREAS, included in the Choose How You Move program are pedestrian safety,
signal improvement, service, and corridor projects to improve a public transit system, as defined
in Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 67-4-3201, and to help manage congestion for safe,
fast, reliable, efficient, and convenient service; and

WHEREAS, Choose How You Move corridor projects present the opportunity to drive
the local economy, which will provide benefits for all Tennessee communities, rural, suburban,
and urban, through increased tax revenue; and
WHEREAS, the voter-approved Choose How You Move transportation improvement
program includes proposed projects to improve some of Nashville's most heavily traveled roads:
the Nolensville Pike, Gallatin Pike, and Downtown corridors; and
WHEREAS, less congested Nolensville Pike, Gallatin Pike, and Downtown corridors
would provide more efficient routes and additional options for drivers traveling from surrounding
counties to downtown Nashville; and
WHEREAS, to address congestion and improve safety, the proposed Nolensville Pike,
Gallatin Pike, and Downtown corridor projects include incorporating bus rapid transit systems;
and
WHEREAS, a bus rapid transit system may establish, as needed, dedicated, clearly
marked roadway spaces that improve basic street functions by organizing traffic, adding safer
turn access, modernizing traffic signals, strengthening pavement for heavy vehicles, and
providing predictable, reliable movement for all, whether they drive, deliver goods, or use transit,
without changing the road's fundamental purpose; and
WHEREAS, pursuant to Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 7-56-102, certain bus
rapid transit systems using a separate lane or right-of-way dedicated to the use of a bus rapid
transit system on any state highway or state highway right-of-way is subject to the approval of
the General Assembly as evidenced by the passage of a joint resolution originating in either
house; and
WHEREAS, the proposed Nolensville Pike, Gallatin Pike, and Downtown corridor
projects will be designed to help address Nashville's congestion and traffic; and

WHEREAS, a recent survey by Vanderbilt University found nearly half of all commuters
in Nashville spend between thirty and ninety minutes in traffic each day; and
WHEREAS, another recent study by the transportation analytics company INRIX found
that each year, the average commuter spends ninety-nine hours and $1,400 on gas stopped in
heavy traffic congestion; and
WHEREAS, our State's population growth and increasing vehicular travel cause serious
traffic congestion near certain urban areas of Tennessee, and the inadequacy of roadway
capacity and mobility has contributed to this congestion; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee is delivering other innovative methods to address congestion,
including the Choice Lanes project overseen by the Department of Transportation (TDOT), of
which the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and Davidson County serves as a key partner;
and
WHEREAS, the Choice Lanes project will offer enhanced transit options as transit buses
may use the Choice Lanes to benefit from reliable travel speed and travel time; and
WHEREAS, the Choice Lanes project and the Nolensville Pike, Gallatin Pike, and
Downtown corridor projects will complement each other, providing additional and alternate
routes to continue to reduce traffic and improve safety for both commuters from surrounding
counties and residents of Davidson County; and
WHEREAS, pursuant to Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 7-56-102, TDOT has the
authority to approve or disapprove projects on any state highway or state highway right-of-way,
such as corridors with rights-of-way with state or federal highway designations; and
WHEREAS, prior to the approval of these types of projects by TDOT, pursuant to
Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 7-56-102, "The commissioner of transportation shall
provide written notice of any such proposed project to the speakers of the senate and the house
of representatives, the chairs of the finance, ways and means committees of the senate and the
- 3 - 012485

house of representatives, the chair of the transportation and safety committee of the senate,
and the chair of the transportation committee of the house of representatives"; and
WHEREAS, the General Assembly recognizes that TDOT will not approve any bus rapid
transit lane projects that impair future highway improvements or the safety of highway users and
that the department will provide notice to the General Assembly when approving projects
pursuant to Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 7-56-102; and
WHEREAS, because the General Assembly supports efforts to manage congestion and
improve safety, the General Assembly approves the bus rapid transit system proposed in the
Nolensville Pike, Gallatin Pike, and Downtown corridor projects, subject to the oversight of
TDOT and approval of the governing body of the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and
Davidson County and the requirements of Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 7-56-102; and
WHEREAS, because the General Assembly supports the responsible use of federal
funding for infrastructure, the General Assembly provides its approval through this resolution to
allow the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and Davidson County to be competitive for
federal grants, benefitting all Middle Tennesseans; and
WHEREAS, Choose How You Move corridor projects will be competing for federal
funding on behalf of Tennessee, and supportive alignment across the General Assembly,
TDOT, and the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and Davidson County is a fundamental
requirement for grant readiness and competitiveness; and
WHEREAS, the General Assembly recognizes that the Nolensville Pike, Gallatin Pike,
and Downtown corridor projects may serve as examples for future corridor improvement
projects in Nashville, and if the projects are satisfactory, then the General Assembly may
approve the additional voter-approved corridor projects included in the transportation
improvement plan adopted on November 5, 2024, subject to the oversight of TDOT and

approval of the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and Davidson County and TDOT; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that because we support efforts to manage traffic
congestion, improve safety, and create opportunities for Tennessee to receive federal
infrastructure funding, pursuant to the requirements of Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 7-
56-102, and subject to the oversight of TDOT and approval of the Metropolitan Government of
Nashville and Davidson County and TDOT, we approve bus rapid transit systems using a
separate lane, or separate right-of-way, dedicated to the use of a bus rapid transit system on a
state highway or state highway right-of-way as proposed in the Nolensville Pike, Gallatin Pike,
and Downtown corridor projects included in the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and
Davidson County's Choose How You Move transportation improvement program, adopted by
the voters of the Metropolitan Government of Nashville and Davidson County on November 5,
2024.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
