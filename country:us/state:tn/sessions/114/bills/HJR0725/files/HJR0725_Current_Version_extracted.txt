HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 725
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Strategic Hospitality on
the occasion of three of its Nashville restaurants
receiving Michelin stars.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly specially recognize
those outstanding Tennessee enterprises whose leadership and innovation have contributed
immeasurably to the cultural, economic, and civic vitality of our State; and
WHEREAS, in 2024, three Nashville restaurants earned one of the culinary world's
highest distinctions when the Michelin Guide awarded a coveted Michelin star to Bastion,
Locust, and The Catbird Seat, each operated by Nashville's own Strategic Hospitality Group;
and
WHEREAS, these selections mark a historic milestone, as they represent the first
Michelin stars ever awarded in Nashville and the first Michelin selections in the American South,
a testament to the extraordinary quality and innovation of Tennessee's contemporary dining
landscape; and
WHEREAS, the Michelin Guide, long recognized as a global standard of excellence,
evaluates restaurants with anonymous inspectors according to five rigorous criteria: product
quality, mastery of cooking techniques, harmony of flavors, personality of the chef as reflected
in the cuisine, and consistency over time and across the entire menu, ensuring an impartial and
internationally consistent measure of culinary achievement; and
WHEREAS, the Michelin inspectors praised Bastion, located in Nashville's Wedgewood-
Houston neighborhood, for its "approachable yet playful" contemporary Southern cooking, its
striking dishes that showcase exceptional ingredients, and its imaginative tasting menu that
showcases both mastery of technique and a spirit of culinary creativity; and

WHEREAS, the inspectors found Locust, in the 12South neighborhood, to embody
"simple elegance" and a clear attention to detail, with its Japanese-influenced seafood
preparations, refined shareable dishes, and signature offerings that have made the restaurant a
destination for diners seeking precision and artistry; and
WHEREAS, The Catbird Seat, the oldest of the three restaurants and now located atop
the Bill Voorhees Building on 8th Avenue South, earned praise for its "youthful, maximalist vibe"
and for the innovative, locality-focused cuisine crafted by its husband-and-wife chef duo, Andy
Doubrava and Tiffani Ortiz, whose tasting experience celebrates seasonality, preservation, and
bold culinary imagination; and
WHEREAS, these achievements reflect the vision and leadership of Strategic
Hospitality, a Nashville-based hospitality group founded in 2006 by brothers Benjamin and Max
Goldberg, whose work has shaped and elevated Nashville's dining culture for nearly two
decades; the company also includes partner and acclaimed chef Josh Habiger; and
WHEREAS, Strategic Hospitality's portfolio now spans an array of celebrated
restaurants, bars, and hospitality concepts, including The Patterson House, Henrietta Red,
Kisser, The Band Box, The Country Club at First Horizon Park, Kitty Hawk Eat + Drink, Voodoo
Doughnut, The Titans Press Box, The Music City Shop, and Friends In Low Places Bar &
Honky-Tonk, owned by Garth Brooks and Trisha Yearwood, each of which contributes to the
dynamic and welcoming character of Nashville's culinary and entertainment landscape; and
WHEREAS, Strategic Hospitality remains committed to innovation and growth,
exemplified by its 2025 opening of Babychan, a Japanese-inspired bakery and café created by
Brian Lea and Leina Horii, the duo behind the James Beard-nominated Kisser; and
WHEREAS, the consistent excellence of Strategic Hospitality and its team has earned
them multiple nominations from the James Beard Foundation for Outstanding Restaurateur,

underscoring their national reputation for creativity, hospitality, and community-focused
leadership; and
WHEREAS, the Michelin recognition of Bastion, Locust, and The Catbird Seat stands as
a landmark achievement not only for Strategic Hospitality but for the entire State of Tennessee,
elevating Nashville's reputation as an emerging center of culinary innovation and excellence;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Strategic Hospitality on the occasion of three of
its Nashville restaurants receiving Michelin stars, congratulate the company and its leadership
on this historic achievement, and extend to them our best wishes for every continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
