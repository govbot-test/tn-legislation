SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 706
By Gardenhire
A RESOLUTION to honor Junior Lee Hankins, the longest-serving
county commissioner in Bledsoe County history.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing a
gentleman who has distinguished himself as a vital and vibrant public servant for more than four
decades; and
WHEREAS, Junior Lee Hankins's forty-four-year legislative tenure ranks him as the
longest-serving county commissioner in Bledsoe County history and one of the longest-serving
county commissioners in the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hankins has always supported projects that not only benefit his district
and community but also Bledsoe County as a whole, and he has worked to keep taxes low for
the community's farmers and families by expanding the tax base through growth; and
WHEREAS, he has earned the respect of his colleagues on both sides of the aisle
through his spirit of cooperation and his willingness to listen to other viewpoints; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hankins was named 2022 Bledsoe County Republican of the Year in
appreciation of his many contributions to the prosperity of his community; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hankins has ever been a diligent worker, from an early age and into his
retirement years; he is a lifelong farmer, having maintained beef and dairy herds for most of his
career; and
WHEREAS, he has also worked as a coal miner, logger, sawmill operator, service
station operator, school bus driver, and a heavy equipment operator of singular ability and
versatility; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hankins is also a problem solver who can repair almost any device, and
he has earned his fellow citizens' admiration as "the best hillbilly engineer in the state"; and

WHEREAS, a lifelong resident of Bledsoe County, Junior Lee Hankins is the son of
Johnnie and Laura Swafford Hankins and a loving and supportive family man who treasures his
wife of sixty-eight years, Shirley Johnson Hankins; their children, Gary Hankins, Stanley
Hankins, Colleen Hankins Spears, and Dale Hankins; and his many grandchildren, great-
grandchildren, and great-great-grandchildren, who adore him as "the best Pa" ever; and
WHEREAS, he has been an outstanding role model for his family members, teaching
them the importance of a good work ethic, patience, and service to others; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hankins's friends and neighbors regard him as a wise and
knowledgeable man, but he is humble and unassuming in his daily conduct, always willing to
give help or advice when asked; and
WHEREAS, together, he and Mrs. Hankins have built a home at which every child,
neighbor, and friend feels welcome, and they often set aside their responsibilities to talk and
have coffee with their visitors; and
WHEREAS, Junior Lee Hankins became a Christian at the age of sixteen and now
serves as a deacon at his place of worship, Winesap Free Will Baptist Church, where he is
legendary for his listening ear, serving heart, unending patience, and hard work and
determination; and
WHEREAS, a member of First Families of Bledsoe County and First Families of
Tennessee, Mr. Hankins is an avid fisherman who once caught a 100-pound halibut in Alaska;
and
WHEREAS, Junior Lee Hankins is a man of rare ability and dedication, and his life of
purpose and commitment should be appropriately commemorated; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Junior Lee Hankins, the longest-serving
