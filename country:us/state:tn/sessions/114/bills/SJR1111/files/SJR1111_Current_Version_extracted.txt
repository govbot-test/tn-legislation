SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1111
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor the Child Advocacy Center of the 3rd
Judicial on its twenty-fifth anniversary.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those institutions that are dedicated to improving the lives of Tennessee's children, our most
precious natural resource, in a safe and welcoming environment; and
WHEREAS, the Child Advocacy Center (CAC) of the 3rd Judicial District celebrated its
twenty-fifth anniversary in 2025, marking a quarter century of serving children and families in
Greene, Hamblen, Hancock, and Hawkins counties; and
WHEREAS, located in Mosheim, the CAC has worked diligently since its inception to
carry out its mission: to validate voices, support families, and empower children; and
WHEREAS, the center provides a safe and welcoming place where children can feel
supported and protected during some of the most difficult times in their lives; and
WHEREAS, the CAC serves children and families during an active child abuse
investigation, ensuring both the child's safety and the family's needs; children are able to share
their stories in a safe environment through forensic interviews, which are designed to be
sensitive, respectful, and focused on the child's well-being; and
WHEREAS, families also have access to therapy that helps children heal from trauma
and begin to move forward; family advocacy services ensure that parents and caregivers have
the guidance, resources, and encouragement they need during challenging times; and
WHEREAS, for twenty-five years, the CAC of the 3rd Judicial District has worked to
assure the safety and overall well-being of children who have been abused or neglected by their
parents or guardians, a noble calling that serves not only those children but the community at
large by helping to rebuild their future; and

WHEREAS, children who have been subjected to harm deserve our compassion and
understanding, but they also need the best available treatment to help them lead their best
possible lives; and
WHEREAS, understanding the importance of this mission, the administration and staff of
the Child Advocacy Center work hard every day to protect and serve child victims of abuse and
neglect, and they are most worthy of our approbation on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we join in commemorating the twenty-fifth
anniversary of the Child Advocacy Center of the 3rd Judicial District and thank the center's
administration and staff for their ongoing commitment to Tennessee's most vulnerable children.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
