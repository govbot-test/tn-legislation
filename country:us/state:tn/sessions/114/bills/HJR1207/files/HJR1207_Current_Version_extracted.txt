HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1207
By Maberry
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Nathan Dwain Clark of
Clarksville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Nathan Dwain Clark of Clarksville; and
WHEREAS, Nathan Clark was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clark was born on January 18, 1965, in Searcy, Arkansas; from an early
age, he loved baseball and played throughout high school with the American Legion, where he
earned the nickname "Duck"; later in life, he developed a passion for golf and played on courses
around the world; and
WHEREAS, Nathan Clark spent his entire career in retail management, beginning with
Walmart, then spending the last twenty-two years as a store manager at Lowe's; he earned
many awards as a manager and held a deep respect for the team members at Lowe's; and
WHEREAS, recognized as a community partner of various military organizations, Mr.
Clark loyally supported the 5th Special Forces Group, 160th SOAR, and the 101st Airborne
Division; his dedication to these groups exemplified his commitment to the men and women who
serve our nation; and
WHEREAS, Nathan Clark was honored with the titles of both Tennessee Colonel Aide
De Camp and Kentucky Colonel, reflecting his impact and service to the area; most recently, he
achieved the distinction of being named the Champion of Fort Campbell, a testament to his
unwavering support, contributions, and tireless efforts within his community; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Clark made such a lasting impact as a supporter of the military and his
community that he is being formally recognized in the Congressional Record, ensuring that his
dedication, generosity, and spirit will be remembered for generations to come; and
WHEREAS, a man of deep faith, Nathan Clark was a member of the Sango Real Life
Church; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clark was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his loving wife, Marcia Winn Clark; children, Madison
Clark, Montgomery (Bailey) Davie, and Callie Ann Davie (Patrick) Cook; grandchildren, Ryder
Cook, Case Cook, Clayton Ann Cook, Tripper Cook, and Decatur Davie; and siblings, Renee
Clark (Steve) Hand and Elizabeth (Matt) Stone; and
WHEREAS, Nathan Clark was preceded in death by his father, Jimmy Dale Clark, and
his mother and stepfather, Barbara and Donald Hill; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clark leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public
life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Nathan Dwain Clark, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Clark.
