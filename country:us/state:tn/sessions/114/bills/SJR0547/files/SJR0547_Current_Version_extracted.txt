SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 547
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Jeffery Alan Hartline of
Lebanon.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Jeffery Alan Hartline of Lebanon; and
WHEREAS, Jeffery Hartline was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Rome, Georgia, on October 14, 1955, Mr. Hartline was the son of
John William Hartline and JoAnne Stewart Hartline, and he attended the Greater Atlanta
Christian School; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hartline earned a bachelor's degree in biblical studies from Freed-
Hardeman University, and before earning his master's degree in religious studies from
Lipscomb University in 1998, he attended the Harding Graduate School of Theology in Memphis
and worked at Floyd Avenue Church of Christ; and
WHEREAS, a lifelong student of the Bible and culture, Jeffery Hartline was proud to
have served as a worship leader, teacher, elder, summer camp director, and Room in the Inn
coordinator, among several other roles, at the Ashwood/Woodmont Hills Church of Christ; he
later served Antioch Church of Christ as a teacher and volunteer; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hartline and his family moved to Lebanon in 2021 and joined College
Hills Church of Christ, where he assisted in leading worship and teaching adult classes;
additionally, he taught high school history at the Whitver Academy and Bible classes at
Lipscomb University for several semesters; and

WHEREAS, a leader in his community, Jeffery Hartline devoted his time and resources
as a baseball coach for ten years and played a key role in creating the first-ever Homeschool
World Series in 2000; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hartline was a gentleman of many talents and interests and devoted the
last twenty years of his life to engaging in both State and local politics, running for elected office,
managing successful campaigns for others, and serving within political organizations such as
the Wilson County GOP and the Tennessee Firearms Association; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hartline was also deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Jeffery Hartline was joined in holy matrimony with Melodie Lynn Hopkins in
December 1978 at Floyd Avenue Church in Memphis; their blessed union resulted in three
children, Megan Leigh (Hayes Holland), Wesley Benjamin (Miles), and Logan Andrew (Jill), and
eleven grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hartline leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Jeffery Alan Hartline,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Hartline.
- 2 - 010009
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
