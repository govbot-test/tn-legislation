HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 781
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to welcome the Trust for Public Land's national
board of directors to the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, Trust for Public Land is a national nonprofit organization founded in 1972
and dedicated to ensuring that everyone, regardless of geography, income, or background, has
access to the joys and benefits of the outdoors, and, for more than five decades, has helped
protect over four million acres of land in all fifty states; and
WHEREAS, nationally, Trust for Public Land has established a distinguished legacy of
conservation and community-centered planning, having created more than 5,500 parks, trails,
and schoolyards; protected culturally and environmentally significant lands; and connected
people to nature in ways that strengthen community health, economic opportunity, and civic life;
and
WHEREAS, Trust for Public Land has been an active and trusted partner in the State of
Tennessee since 1987, working collaboratively with State and local governments, community
leaders, landowners, and residents to conserve land, expand public access to the outdoors, and
ensure that parks and trails reflect the unique history, culture, and values of Tennessee's
communities; and
WHEREAS, Trust for Public Land's impact across Tennessee includes the protection of
McLean Rock, the birthplace of the Cumberland Trail, thereby preserving a critical natural and
cultural landmark that anchors one of the State's most significant trail systems and contributes
to Tennessee's outdoor recreation economy; and
WHEREAS, since 1994, Trust for Public Land has played a pivotal role in securing land
for and the planning and funding of the Tennessee Riverpark and Riverwalk in Chattanooga, a
signature public space that helped catalyze the city's outdoor and economic renaissance by

Tennessee River; and
WHEREAS, Trust for Public Land has also helped protect ecologically and economically
important landscapes along the Mississippi River, including the Chickasaw National Wildlife
Refuge, thereby safeguarding one of the largest remaining bottomland hardwood forests in the
region for wildlife habitat, outdoor recreation, and long-term public benefit; and
WHEREAS, through its work across urban neighborhoods, rural communities, and

than eighty outdoor projects that strengthen local economies, improve public health, and

WHEREAS, the Trust for Public Land's national board of directors provides strategic
leadership and stewardship in advancing the organization's mission, guiding its national

WHEREAS, the visit of the Trust for Public Land's national board to Tennessee marks

opportunity to recognize past accomplishments, strengthen partnerships, and explore future
opportunities aligned with Tennessee's growth, conservation priorities, and community
aspirations; and

WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to recognize and
commend Trust for Public Land for its national legacy, its transformative impact across
Tennessee, and its shared vision with the State of Tennessee to connect everyone to the joys
and benefits of the outdoors, now and for generations to come; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we extend a warm welcome to the Trust for Public Land's national board of
directors and staff leadership as they convene in the State of Tennessee, expressing our
sincere appreciation for their decades of bipartisan leadership, partnership, and investment in
the people, places, and landscapes of the Volunteer State.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
