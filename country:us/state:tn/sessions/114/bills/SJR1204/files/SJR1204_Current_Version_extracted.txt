SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1204
By Bailey
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Ricky Anderson upon
the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those estimable citizens who have distinguished themselves as dedicated
professionals and public-spirited members of their communities; and
WHEREAS, Ricky Anderson is one such estimable person, who, after many years of
distinguished service in the funeral profession, retired on March 12, 2026, from Hunter Funeral
Home in Sparta; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Anderson has devoted himself to the field of funeral service since 1987,
providing comfort, care, and guidance to families during some of the most difficult moments of
their lives; and
WHEREAS, throughout his distinguished career, he has served families in Davidson,
Overton, Putnam, Loudon, and White counties, earning the respect and gratitude of those he
has assisted; and
WHEREAS, through his compassionate service and steady presence, Ricky Anderson
has demonstrated a commitment to professionalism and a deep sense of responsibility to the
communities he has served; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Anderson is a loyal husband, father, grandfather, brother, and friend
and is held in high esteem by all who know him; and
WHEREAS, in his leisure time, he enjoys following NASCAR and piddling in his shop,
pursuits that reflect his appreciation for both craftsmanship and recreation; and
WHEREAS, a public-spirited citizen of the highest order, Ricky Anderson has compiled a
singular record of service, and he should be specially recognized; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Ricky Anderson upon the
occasion of his retirement, thank him for his many years of dedicated service to the people of
Tennessee, and extend to him our best wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
