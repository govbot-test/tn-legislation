SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1020
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to commend Cason Orr for exemplary service as
a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Cason Orr is one such individual, who has distinguished himself in his role
as a legislative intern for Lieutenant Governor Randy McNally and Senator Adam Lowe; Mr.
Orr's work has resulted in innumerable benefits for the legislators he assisted and the General
Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Orr was born and raised in Estill Springs and graduated from Franklin
County High School as salutatorian of his class; he endeavored to follow in the steps of his
great-grandmother, Judge Dorothy E. Orr, whose actions inspired him to pursue a similar path
in politics; and
WHEREAS, a student at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville (UTK), where he is
highly regarded by his peers, Cason Orr has achieved academic success while pursuing a
Bachelor of Science degree in Political Science with a concentration in Public Policy and Public
Administration and minors in History and American Civics; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Orr served as a research intern at the UTK Center for the Study of
Tennesseans and War and as a student office assistant at the College of Communication and
Information and the Tombras School of Advertising and Public Relations; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his undergraduate studies, Mr. Orr has worked with Remote
Area Medical (RAM USA) to set up a pop-up clinic for underserved citizens in Franklin County,

further demonstrating his commitment to public service and the well-being of his community;

WHEREAS, Mr. Orr is a gentleman of many talents and interests; his creative projects
include drawing, writing, documentaries, and photography; he was previously a photographer
for the Knox County Republican Party and currently serves as a photographer for the legislative
internship program's newsletter; and
WHEREAS, Cason Orr showcased his creative side during the intern talent show,

WHEREAS, he is writing a book that profiles his great-grandmother's life and legacy and
partnering with his brother to film and produce a documentary about his mother's side of the
family, titled Perry: Family Through the Years; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Cason Orr brought his many abilities
and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth
General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Mr. Orr has surpassed all criteria
that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Cason Orr has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight and
rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Cason Orr for outstanding
service as a legislative intern for Lieutenant Governor Randy McNally and Senator Adam Lowe
and extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
