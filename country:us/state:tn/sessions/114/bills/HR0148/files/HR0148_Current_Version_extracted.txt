HOUSE RESOLUTION 148
By Hill
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Chesna Jeane Hulsey of
Church Hill.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body were greatly saddened to learn of the
passing of Chesna Jeane Hulsey, beloved mother of our valued colleague Representative Bud
Hulsey; and
WHEREAS, born on December 21, 1928, Jeane Hulsey grew up in central New Mexico
and on the Navajo Reservation, places she often spoke of with pride and with gratitude for the
opportunities she was afforded to witness and share the Gospel; and
WHEREAS, from a young age, she dedicated her life to bringing others to Christ,
whether it was picking up neighborhood children to take them to church or meeting parents
along her school bus route to invite their children to the Good News Clubs she helped start; and
WHEREAS, Jeane Hulsey was relentless in her mission to see souls saved; even in her
later years as her health declined she continued to witness to those around her, never ceasing
to speak of the hope she found in Jesus; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Hulsey was a faithful student of Scripture, able to recall and recite
passages and entire books with ease, and she carried herself as a true prayer warrior; her heart
was one of service, and her life was lived as a servant to her King, the Messiah; and
WHEREAS, she met the love of her life and her husband of nearly seventy-five years at
an Army Depot between Williams and Flagstaff, Arizona; together, they built a life centered on
faith, family, and service; although recently preceded in death by him, she continued to cherish
his memory deeply; and

WHEREAS, in addition to Representative Bud Hulsey, left to carry on her legacy of faith
are her granddaughter, Chesna Quillen, and her husband, James, as well as her great-
grandchildren, Aiden and Alysse; and
WHEREAS, throughout her many endeavors, Jeane Hulsey distinguished herself as a
devoted woman of God who was a blessing to all who had the good fortune of knowing her; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to her bountiful life of purpose and
commitment; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor the
memory of Chesna Jeane Hulsey, reflecting fondly upon her enduring legacy of faith and
compassion.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and condolences to the
family and friends of Mrs. Hulsey.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
