HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1580
By Scarbrough
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Juanita Webb Smith
on the celebration of her 100th birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
those citizens who are celebrating special occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, Juanita Webb Smith will celebrate her 100th birthday on May 1, 2026, a
milestone that will be commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of life's rich pageant;
and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Smith has lived a remarkable life, having experienced firsthand the
most eventful and productive years in American history; and
WHEREAS, born on May 1, 1926, in Coal Creek, Juanita Webb Smith was joined in holy
matrimony with Bill Smith at the age of sixteen, and they enjoyed sixty years of marriage before
his passing; and
WHEREAS, the Smiths were blessed with three sons, and Mrs. Smith still resides in the
home they built together seventy-three years ago; and
WHEREAS, during her years of industry, Juanita Smith was a highly valued employee of
CUB for thirty-five years before taking her well-deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, a woman of deep and abiding faith, she is known for her love of the Lord,
her family, and friends, and her town of Clinton; and
WHEREAS, as she gracefully enters her 100th year, Juanita Smith remains a blessing to
all who know her and is highly valued for her grace, compassion, and kindness; and
WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
HJR1580
018542
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and congratulate Juanita Webb Smith on the celebration
of her 100th birthday and extend to her our best wishes for much continued success and
happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
