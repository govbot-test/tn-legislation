HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1003
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Alyssa Clements upon
winning second place in the 2026 American
Farm Bureau Federation Convention national
agricultural competition.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to specially recognize
those outstanding citizens of our State who, through their extraordinary efforts, have
distinguished themselves as agricultural leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such outstanding individual is Alyssa Clements of Clay County, who
won second place in the 2026 American Farm Bureau Federation Convention national
agricultural competition held in Anaheim, California; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Clements was recently named the Tennessee Young Farmers and
Ranchers Excellence in Agriculture winner in 2025 and went on to represent Tennessee and
compete at the national level at the American Farm Bureau Federation Convention; and
WHEREAS, the Excellence in Agriculture Award recognizes young farmers who not only
actively contribute to agriculture and participate in Farm Bureau and other organizations but
derive the majority of their income from an off-farm occupation; and
WHEREAS, a resident of Clay County, Ms. Clements grew up with grandparents who
operated a tobacco and cattle farm and parents in the rural healthcare industry; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Clements earned a Bachelor's degree in Animal Science from the
University of Tennessee, Knoxville, a Master's degree in Ruminant Nutrition from the University
of Illinois, and a Master of Business Administration degree from Tennessee Technological
University; and
WHEREAS, Alyssa Clements has been involved with Young Farmers and Ranchers for
a number of years and most recently participated in the American Farm Bureau Federation

advocacy fly-in in Washington, D.C.; additionally, she has served as a member of the Clay
County Young Farmers and Ranchers committee and, for the past two years, as a member of
the Clay County Farm Bureau Board of Directors; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Clements began her sales career in animal science in 2015 and
currently serves as a sales specialist at Purina Mills, LLC, and Protrition, a partnership with
Tennessee Farmers Cooperative; and
WHEREAS, in her current role, Ms. Clements assists producers throughout the middle
Tennessee and south-central Kentucky areas in developing nutrition plans involving forages and
farm-grown feedstuffs; and
WHEREAS, her professional accomplishments aside, Ms. Clements is most grateful for
the love and support of her husband, Adam Boucher, and their son, Amos; and
WHEREAS, Alyssa Clements exemplifies the spirit and dedication that are characteristic
of a true Tennessean, and we find it appropriate to honor and commend such an outstanding
and remarkable individual; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Alyssa Clements of Clay County upon winning
second place in the 2026 American Farm Bureau Federation Convention national agricultural
competition, extending to her our best wishes for every continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
