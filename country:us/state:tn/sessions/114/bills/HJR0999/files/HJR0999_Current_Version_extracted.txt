HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 999
By Jones R
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Mabel Chandler
Edwards on the occasion of her 106th birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to those citizens who are celebrating
special occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, born on Leap Day, February 29, 1920, Mabel Chandler Edwards is
celebrating her 106th birthday, a milestone commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of
life's rich pageant; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Edwards has lived an amazing life and has experienced firsthand the
breadth and span of American history; and
WHEREAS, the youngest daughter of John Frank Chandler and Vina Elizabeth Willis
Chandler, Mabel Edwards grew up alongside her siblings Ishma, Bertha, and Mary; and
WHEREAS, at the age of eleven, she embraced Christianity and became a member of
Clear Branch Baptist Church, where she has maintained her membership faithfully throughout
her life; and
WHEREAS, while attending Milligan College to study home economics, Mrs. Edwards
began writing to Walter Clinton Edwards, a neighbor and United States Army serviceman who
would become her husband in 1942; and
WHEREAS, Walter and Mabel Edwards welcomed a son, David C. Edwards, in 1943;
and
WHEREAS, over the course of her husband's twenty-year military career, Mabel
Edwards and her family lived at various Army bases in Florida, Illinois, Germany, and other
locations before returning to Tennessee, where they eventually settled in Erwin and built their
home; and

WHEREAS, Mabel Edwards enjoyed sixty-seven years of marriage with her husband
until his passing in 2009; and
WHEREAS, throughout her life, Mrs. Edwards has enjoyed gardening and raising
flowers, especially her vibrant geraniums, which she carefully overwintered and replanted each
spring; she also continued to grow tomato and bell pepper plants behind her home until the age
of 103; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Edwards remains a beloved member of her community who is admired
for her faith, integrity, and enthusiasm for life; and
WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Mabel Chandler Edwards on the celebration of
her 106th birthday and extend to her our best wishes for many happy returns of the day.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
