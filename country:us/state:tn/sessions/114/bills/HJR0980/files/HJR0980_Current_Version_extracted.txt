HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 980
By Faison
A RESOLUTION to commend Representative Bud Hulsey for his
honorable and astute service to the people of
Tennessee as a member of the House of
Representatives.
WHEREAS, it is most appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should
honor those fellow legislators who have performed their duties with the utmost integrity and
whose efforts during their time in office have been dedicated to providing thoughtful and
exemplary service to their districts and to this great State; and
WHEREAS, Representative Bud Hulsey of Kingsport is one such distinguished public
servant, who has served both his constituency and this legislative body with honor and ability;
and
WHEREAS, during his tenure, Representative Hulsey has been a highly influential figure
in the political, social, and civic life of Sullivan County, and he has served his constituents in
numerous capacities, always working diligently at his elected duties and expending the
necessary effort to understand the opposing sides of complex issues; and
WHEREAS, Bud Hulsey has distinguished himself as a public-spirited citizen of the
highest order and as an exceptional asset to the good people of the 2nd House District, whom he
has served as a member of the 109th, 110th, 111th, 112th, 113th, and 114th General Assemblies;
and
WHEREAS, throughout his service, Bud Hulsey has been prominent as a dedicated and
well-informed legislator who always votes his conscience; and
WHEREAS, Representative Hulsey is a graduate of Bob Jones University and has
completed postgraduate work through the University of Virginia via the FBI National Academy;
and

WHEREAS, he is a retired police lieutenant who was employed by the Kingsport Police
Department for thirty-six years, serving as a watch commander for the last twenty-five years of
his tenure; and
WHEREAS, an active and dynamic participant in the legislative process during his time
in the General Assembly, Representative Bud Hulsey has rendered sterling service as Chair of
the Appropriations and Oversight Subcommittee, Co-Chair of the Joint Ways and Means
Committee, Chair of the Corrections Subcommittee, Chair of the Criminal Justice Committee,
Vice Chair of the State Government Committee, and Co-Chair of the Joint Ad Hoc Committee to
Review the Adequacy of the Supervision, Investigation, and Release of Criminal Defendants;
and
WHEREAS, he has also served with distinction as a member of the Agriculture and
Natural Resources Committee, Finance, Ways and Means Committee, and Private Acts
Committee; and
WHEREAS, he further served as the House Speaker's appointee to the Tennessee
Peace Officer Standards & Training Commission from 2016 to 2024; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to honors and accolades, Representative Bud Hulsey has
received the Tennessee Sheriffs' Association's Legislative Champion award, the National Coin
& Bullion Association's Diane Piret Memorial Outstanding Service Award, the Tennessee District
Public Defenders Conference's Mike Carter Award, and the U.S. Term Limits organization's
Champion of Term Limits Award; and
WHEREAS, throughout his outstanding career as a public servant, Representative
Hulsey has worked tirelessly on behalf of his constituents, bringing to his office an honorable
bearing and the traditional values of hard work and common sense; and
- 2 - 012804
WHEREAS, Representative Bud Hulsey is wholly committed to the noble precepts of
public service that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should
be specially recognized for his exemplary tenure in the General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor Representative Bud Hulsey for his meritorious service to
Tennessee as a member of the House of Representatives, commend him for his countless
contributions and many good works in service of the citizens of the 2nd House District, and
extend to him our best wishes for much continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
