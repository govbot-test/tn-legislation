SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 574
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Dr. Barbara Alfreda
Duncan-Cody of Millington.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Dr. Barbara Alfreda Duncan-Cody of Millington; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Barbara Alfreda Duncan-Cody was an exemplary public servant and
consummate professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow
citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on April 25, 1946, Dr. Duncan-Cody was a proud member of the
Manassas High School Class of 1964; she completed her undergraduate studies at Knoxville
College and went on to earn her Doctor of Medicine degree from Meharry Medical College
School of Medicine in 1980; and
WHEREAS, with more than forty-five years of professional experience, Dr. Duncan-Cody
served as a physician, specializing in obstetrics and gynecology, in which position she earned
the respect and admiration of her peers and community alike for the exemplary quality of her
work; and
WHEREAS, a leader in her profession, Dr. Barbara Duncan-Cody was the sole
proprietor of her practice, Duncan-Cody Medical Group, and the first Black female OB/GYN to
open a private practice in Memphis; she took her much-deserved retirement from the medical
profession in June 2025; and
WHEREAS, she was affiliated with Methodist Hospitals of Memphis, the Medical
Auxiliary, and the Bluff City Medical Society, working alongside several doctors and specialists
to ensure comprehensive care of her patients; and

WHEREAS, Dr. Duncan-Cody served her community in numerous capacities, including
as a member of Delta Theta Sigma Sorority, Inc., and the Memphis Chapter of Links, Inc., and
as a charter member of Jack & Jill of America, Inc., and the Memphis Chapter of The Society,
Inc.; and
WHEREAS, additionally, she served as a member of the Shelby County 911 Board and
the Methodist Healthcare Foundation Board of Directors, and she was featured in The
Commercial Appeal and Grace magazines; and
WHEREAS, a beloved wife, mother, grandmother, and friend, Dr. Duncan-Cody was
wholly devoted to her family, and she always endeavored to remain true to family values of the
highest order; and
WHEREAS, left to cherish her memory are her husband of fifty-seven years, Attorney
Homer Lee Cody; her children, Justin Ryan Cody and Rachel Joann Cody (Darrell) Alexander;
and her grandchildren, Laila, Rome, and Darrell; and
WHEREAS, committed to her faith, Dr. Duncan-Cody was a lifelong member of Trinity
Christian Methodist Episcopal Church in Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Duncan-Cody leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity
in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her
chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Barbara Duncan-Cody served her profession and her community with
kindness and distinction, touching the lives of countless individuals and families, and it is fitting
that the members of this General Assembly should remember the bountiful life of this
exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Dr. Barbara Alfreda

living the examined life with courage and conviction.
