SENATE RESOLUTION 367
By Rose
A RESOLUTION to commend Joel Cline for exemplary service as
a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this body is pleased to recognize certain persons
who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the legislative
process; and
WHEREAS, Joel Cline is one such individual, who has distinguished himself in his role
as a legislative intern for Senator Paul Rose; Mr. Cline's work has resulted in innumerable
benefits for the legislators he assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, born in Cortez, Colorado, Mr. Cline is the son of Casey Cline and was
raised with the support of his grandparents, Debbie and Donald Cline; his family moved to
Tennessee in 2004, establishing roots in Greene County; and
WHEREAS, a student at King University, where he is highly regarded by his peers, Joel
Cline has achieved academic success while pursuing a Bachelor of Arts degree in History and
is slated to graduate summa cum laude in May 2026; he has served the history, political
science, and faith and culture departments with acumen and alacrity as a teaching assistant;
and
WHEREAS, during his time at King University, Mr. Cline became the first student to
attend the Tennessee Legislative Internship Program twice and the second to win a game of
Diplomacy in Dr. Martin Dotterweich's European Age of Revolutions class; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Cline previously earned an Associate of Science degree in History from
Walters State Community College, graduating magna cum laude; and
WHEREAS, a gentleman of many talents and interests, Joel Cline has a passion for
storytelling in all forms. He took an interest in learning about history at a young age, and since

then, he has developed a fascination for Appalachian culture and history and exploring
alternate-history scenarios; his passion for history has led him to assist with events dedicated to
Appalachian heritage, and he intends to continue learning, even after he completes his
education; and
WHEREAS, he further enjoys exploring the differences between denominations and
deepening his faith in God through his studies; and
WHEREAS, a resident of Afton, Mr. Cline has brought his creativity, interest in history,
and passion for public service to his time with the legislature once more, having previously
served the Tennessee General Assembly as a legislative intern for Chairman Dennis Powers in
2025, in which role he gained experience with constituent relations and conducted bill tracking,
recordkeeping, and office duties; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Joel Cline brought his many abilities
and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth
General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Mr. Cline has surpassed all criteria
that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Joel Cline has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight and
rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby commend Joel Cline
for outstanding service as a legislative intern for Senator Paul Rose and extend to him our best
wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
