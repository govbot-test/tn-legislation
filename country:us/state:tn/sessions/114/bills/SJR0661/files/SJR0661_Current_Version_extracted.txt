SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 661
By Crowe
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Kenneth Harlan Green
of Johnson City.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Kenneth Harlan Green of Johnson City; and
WHEREAS, Ken Green was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on December 3, 1942, in Pathfork, Kentucky, Mr. Green was the son
of William Carl Green and Tursie Lee Green; and
WHEREAS, he began his football career as a player for Blackstar High School in
Eastern Kentucky; feeling led to mentor young men, Mr. Green answered the calling to become
a football coach; and
WHEREAS, Coach Green took his first coaching position at New Richmond High School
in Ohio, but he later returned to his Appalachian roots when he accepted a coaching position at
Cumberland High School in Kentucky; and
WHEREAS, in 1972, Coach Green moved to Tennessee and became the head football
coach at Daniel Boone High School, promising to beat the school's county rival, David Crockett
High School; he accomplished his goal, finishing his first season with a 30-8 victory and holding
a 21-7 game winning advantage in the Musket Bowl; he was later named Coach of the Decade
for the Upper Lakes Conference; and
WHEREAS, in addition to serving as the football coach, Mr. Green taught history at
Daniel Boone High School and served as assistant principal for a time, eventually retiring in
2001 at the culmination of an illustrious twenty-nine-year career; and

WHEREAS, in 2008, to honor Coach Green's success, dedication, and hard work,
Daniel Boone High School named its football field the "Ken Green Field," and in 2023, Coach
Green received the honor of being inducted into the National High School Football Coaches
Association Hall of Fame; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his love for football, Coach Green enjoyed fishing, spending
time on the lake with the "Gus Crew," traveling to his beach house in Mississippi, watching the
Kentucky Wildcats win, and witnessing to others about the love and grace of Jesus; and
WHEREAS, a strong man of faith, Ken Green was a devout member of Marvin's Chapel
Global Methodist Church, which he served for many years in a number of capacities; and
WHEREAS, Coach Green was deeply devoted to his faith and his family, and he always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Ken Green is survived by his only son, Kent Julian Green; daughter-in-law,
whom he thought of as his own daughter, Dana Laura Mullins Green; granddaughters, Macie
Ellyn Green and Kenley Danielle Green, who is his namesake; niece, Susan Beth Green (Mark)
Collett; sister-in-law, Dee (Ken) Julian; nephew, Zack (Faith) Julian; great-nephew, Zachary
Austin Julian; Danny and Donna Mullins, whom he saw as a brother and sister; and a host of
friends and church family; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Coach Green was preceded in death by his wife
of sixty-one years, Lynn Ellyn Julian Green; and his brothers, William "Carl" Green and James
Marlin Green; and
WHEREAS, the epitome of excellence, Ken Green was a faithful soul who impacted the
lives of those who were blessed to know him, and he leaves behind an indelible legacy of
integrity and probity in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and
dedication in all his chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
- 2 - 011699
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Kenneth Harlan Green,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Coach Green.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
