HOUSE RESOLUTION 164
By Faison
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Karen D. Williford
Rogers of Mohawk.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body were greatly saddened to learn of the
passing of Karen D. Williford Rogers of Mohawk; and
WHEREAS, born on March 17, 1961, in Alexandria, Virginia, Karen Rogers graduated
from East High School while simultaneously earning an associate degree in education from
Walters State Community College in Morristown, Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Rogers went on to earn a Bachelor of Science degree from East
Tennessee State University and later a master's degree and an Education Specialist degree
from Lincoln Memorial University; and
WHEREAS, from 1989 to 2003, Karen Rogers served as a project manager and grant
writer with Douglas Cherokee, where she completed multimillion-dollar projects; she then
transitioned to education from 2003 to 2019, teaching seventh-grade at East Ridge Middle
School and then teaching at East High School; she devoted the last six years of her career to
working with the First CORE office as a CTE consultant; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Rogers received many awards and recognitions throughout her career,
not only within her community but across the State; and
WHEREAS, Karen Rogers had a profound impact on her students and colleagues; she
was an advocate for all students and ensured access to equitable opportunities to build a better
future; her belief in others helped give her students the permission to think bigger; and
WHEREAS, along with her career in education, Mrs. Rogers had a passion for crafting
and enjoyed serving in sales for seven years with Thirty-One; through this, she met many

memory of Karen D. Williford Rogers, reflecting fondly upon her impeccable character and her
stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction. We express our
sympathy and offer our condolences to the family and friends of Mrs. Rogers.
