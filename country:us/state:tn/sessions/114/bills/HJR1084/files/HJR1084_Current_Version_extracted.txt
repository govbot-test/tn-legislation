HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1084
By Todd
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Agricultural Crime
Unit of the Tennessee Department of Agriculture
upon the celebration of its twenty-fifth
anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those governmental entities and public servants whose dedication and
professionalism safeguard the welfare, property, and livelihoods of the citizens of this State; and
WHEREAS, the Agricultural Crime Unit of the Tennessee Department of Agriculture
proudly celebrated twenty-five years of exemplary service to the people of Tennessee in 2025,
marking a quarter century of steadfast commitment to protecting the State's agricultural
resources; and
WHEREAS, established in 2000, the Agricultural Crime Unit operates statewide with ten
highly trained officers who specialize in the investigation of crimes uniquely affecting the
agriculture and forestry sectors, including livestock theft, farm equipment theft, timber theft,
wildland fire arson, animal cruelty, and agricultural fraud; and
WHEREAS, under the capable leadership of Special Agent in Charge Greg Whitehead,
the members of the Agricultural Crime Unit have demonstrated exceptional professionalism,
integrity, and dedication in the performance of their duties; and
WHEREAS, the Agricultural Crime Unit works in close partnership with local, State, and
federal law enforcement agencies, prosecutors, and agricultural organizations to ensure that
those who prey upon Tennessee's farming and forest communities are brought to justice; and
WHEREAS, the Agricultural Crime Unit has played a critical role in disaster response
efforts across the State, including its significant involvement following Hurricane Helene in East

Tennessee in 2024, when its officers worked diligently to assist impacted farmers during a time
of great need; and
WHEREAS, through its vigilance and service, the Agricultural Crime Unit of the
Tennessee Department of Agriculture has strengthened rural safety, preserved valuable
agricultural resources, and upheld the proud farming and forestry tradition that remains a
cornerstone of Tennessee's identity; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Agricultural Crime Unit of the Tennessee
Department of Agriculture upon the celebration of its twenty-fifth anniversary and extend to its
officers our sincere appreciation for their years of dedicated service to the people of this State.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
