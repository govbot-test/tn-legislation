SENATE RESOLUTION 363
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Calleigh Phillips upon
being selected to attend the Tennessee 4-H
Congress.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body are pleased to recognize those
outstanding young Tennesseans who, through their remarkable achievements and leadership,
bring great honor to their schools and communities; and
WHEREAS, one such impressive young woman is Calleigh Phillips, who was selected to
attend the Tennessee 4-H Congress, held in Nashville from March 6 to March 11, 2026; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee 4-H Congress is one of the State's premier 4-H events,
bringing together high school students to learn about government, citizenship, and leadership
and providing participants with the opportunity to engage in legislative simulations, including
debating bills, holding elections, and gaining firsthand experience in the legislative process at
the State Capitol; and
WHEREAS, during her participation in this prestigious program, Ms. Phillips was a
Public Speaking finalist for the Western Region and a Level II Leadership Portfolio finalist,
demonstrating exceptional skill in communication, leadership, and civic engagement; and
WHEREAS, in addition, she was selected to serve as a Know Your Government
facilitator and as chair of the Agriculture Committee, roles through which she helped guide her
fellow delegates in legislative simulations, including the drafting, debating, and passage of mock
legislation; and
WHEREAS, her selection as a top-six finalist statewide in both public speaking and
leadership portfolio competitions is a testament to her diligence, intellect, and commitment to
excellence; and

WHEREAS, a junior at Adamsville High School, Calleigh Phillips is an active and
engaged student who has distinguished herself through her involvement in agricultural
education programs, including her leadership in FFA; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Phillips serves as president of her school's FFA chapter and plays an
integral role in managing the school greenhouse, where she assists in the cultivation and sale of
plants, helps oversee daily operations, and provides instruction and mentorship to fellow
students; and
WHEREAS, through these experiences, Calleigh Phillips has demonstrated not only
academic ability but also a strong work ethic, leadership capacity, and a commitment to serving
others, qualities that will undoubtedly contribute to her future success; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Phillips has expressed an interest in pursuing a career in agricultural
engineering, with the goal of supporting farmers and strengthening agricultural communities;
and
WHEREAS, Calleigh Phillips's success has been aided by the encouragement and
support of dedicated teachers, advisors, and mentors who have guided her development; and
WHEREAS, her noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for her family,
her school, and her community; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Phillips's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of our respect,
admiration, and commendation; now therefore
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and commend
Calleigh Phillips for her exemplary achievement in being selected to attend the Tennessee 4-H
Congress and extend to her our best wishes for continued success in all her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
