SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 993
By Haile
A RESOLUTION to recognize Jeremiah James on becoming an
Eagle Scout.
WHEREAS, at a time when this nation's youth face difficult challenges, the Boy Scouts
of America is one of our finest assets, providing young people with an educational program that
contributes significantly to their character development, citizenship training, and improved
mental and physical fitness; and
WHEREAS, the highest rank a Scout can attain in the Boy Scouts of America Scouts
BSA program is that of Eagle Scout, and Jeremiah James, after considerable effort and diligent
preparation, has joined the prestigious Eagle Court of Honor; and
WHEREAS, Mr. James began his Scouting career as a first-grade student, when Scout
leaders visited his elementary school; he has advanced through all six ranks and earned thirty-
nine merit badges, including first aid, emergency preparedness, cooking, camping, swimming,
electronics, engineering, game design, sports, personal management, and family life, to help
him gain the achievement of Eagle Scout; and
WHEREAS, a member of Troop 425 in the Middle Tennessee Council, Jeremiah James
served as Senior Patrol Leader for two terms and demonstrated character, leadership, and a
strong commitment to serving his community; and
WHEREAS, for his Eagle project, he planned, financed, and supervised the construction
of a gaga ball pit for the students of Shafer Middle School; after designing the pit and
determining the materials needed, he fundraised over $1,000 to build the ball pit; the endeavor,
which required 109 hours of volunteer service, greatly contributed to the betterment of his
community and reflected the true spirit of volunteerism; and

WHEREAS, a freshman at Gallatin High School, Mr. James saw a need within his
community and built his service project around providing a safe space for students to be active
during recess; this addition to Shafer Middle School will serve the school community for years to
come; and
WHEREAS, his achievement and service bring great pride to his mother, Jolene James,
his family, and his community; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should pause to recognize Jeremiah James as one
of the most outstanding young people in our State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and congratulate Jeremiah James
on becoming an Eagle Scout and extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
