SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1238
By Bailey
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Edgar "Ed" Royce
McCoin of Cookeville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Edgar "Ed" Royce McCoin of Cookeville; and
WHEREAS, Mr. McCoin was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on December 18, 1947, in Livingston, Ed McCoin was the second of
three children born to his parents, Herbert Donald and Thelma Frances Roberts McCoin; he
grew up all across Middle Tennessee and spent his formative years in Jackson County, where
hard work was simply the way of life; and
WHEREAS, Mr. McCoin served in the United States Army, earning the rank of Second
Lieutenant; he was a veteran of Vietnam and served his country valiantly; and
WHEREAS, Ed McCoin married the love of his life, Catherine Tye, on April 20, 1974;
they settled down in Cookeville, where he built a long career at Fleetguard; and
WHEREAS, during his career, Mr. McCoin held positions in industrial engineering and
employee relations for over thirty years before his retirement in February of 2003; and
WHEREAS, an active leader in his community, Ed McCoin taught courses in the
Industrial Engineering and Manufacturing Technology departments at TTU for more than two
decades, mentoring students with the same practical, no-nonsense approach that shaped his
own career; and

WHEREAS, in 2012, he was honored by the Tennessee Technological University (TTU)
ROTC Battalion with induction into the unit's Hall of Fame, showcasing his contributions to the
Army and the TTU Battalion; and
WHEREAS, in Mr. McCoin's later years, he faced additional trials with his health, and he
met his illness with the same composure that had defined him throughout his life; and
WHEREAS, Ed McCoin was deeply devoted to family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his wife of fifty-two years, Catherine T. McCoin; his sister,
Mary McCoin; four children, Ann Marie McCoin, Lauren and Jon Rucker, Carrie and Nathan
Davis, and Clay and Amanda McCoin; six grandchildren, Alexander and Grace Vinson, JW and
Brenna Rucker, Luke Vinson, Mazie Davis, Zoie Davis, and Edgar Royce McCoin II (EJ); and
one great-grandchild, Bo Rucker; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Mr. McCoin was preceded in death by his brother,
Donnie McCoin; and
WHEREAS, Ed McCoin leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Edgar "Ed" Royce McCoin,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. McCoin.
