SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 807
By Hatcher
A RESOLUTION to recognize Gaye Hasty on the occasion of her
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm
throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Gaye Hasty is one such estimable person, who, having rendered forty-two
years of sterling service to Blount County government, will take her much-deserved retirement
on August 31, 2026; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Hasty began her fruitful tenure with Blount County in 1983 as a clerical
assistant for Blount County Property Assessor Ralph Lindsey, in which position she remained
for eleven years; she went on to serve as a receptionist and accounting assistant for Blount
County Executive Bill Crisp from 1995 to 2001 and as an office administrator and planning
assistant in the Blount County Planning Department from 2001 to 2008; and
WHEREAS, she served as a clerical assistant and property sales analyst for Blount
County Property Assessor Mike Morton from 2008 to 2012 and as an office manager for Blount
County Property Assessor Tim Helton from 2012 to 2016; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Hasty was appointed as Blount County Clerk on September 1, 2016,
and since then, she has carried out her responsibilities with diligence, patience, and integrity;
and
WHEREAS, her remarkable accomplishments as a public servant are directly
attributable to her reliance on the time-honored values of hard work and common sense and her
uncommon ability to work well with people from all walks of life; and

WHEREAS, Gaye Hasty is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service that
have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and she should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Gaye Hasty for her honorable
and astute service to the people of Tennessee as Blount County Clerk and extend to her our
heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in her future
endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
