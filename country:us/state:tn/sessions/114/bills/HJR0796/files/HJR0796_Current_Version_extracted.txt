HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 796
By Gant
A RESOLUTION to recognize the Somerville Rotary Club on the
occasion of its fiftieth anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly recognize those
exemplary organizations and their remarkable members who, through their unwavering
commitment to excellence, are a credit to this State; and
WHEREAS, one such notable organization is the Somerville Rotary Club, which is
celebrating its fiftieth anniversary this year; and
WHEREAS, Rotary International is a global service organization founded on the
principles of fellowship, leadership, and a shared commitment to improving communities around
the world; and
WHEREAS, the Rotary Club of Somerville was chartered by Rotary International on
January 20, 1976, and has, since its founding under its first president, James S. Wilder III,
remained steadfast in its mission of "Service Above Self"; and
WHEREAS, the Somerville Rotary Club has, for fifty years, faithfully served the residents
of Fayette County and beyond through countless acts of leadership, fellowship, and community
service; and
WHEREAS, the members of the Somerville Rotary Club have consistently demonstrated
the Rotary motto by dedicating their time, resources, and talents to humanitarian efforts that
improve quality of life locally and globally; and
WHEREAS, in keeping with Rotary's international commitment to humanitarian service,
the Somerville Rotary Club has partnered with other Rotary clubs to provide clean drinking
water to villages in Mexico, offering both financial and hands-on support to improve health and
living conditions abroad; and

WHEREAS, the Somerville Rotary Club has supported initiatives including youth
development, literacy, education, public health, senior support, community improvement
projects, scholarships, and international goodwill, thereby enriching the lives of individuals and
strengthening the civic fabric of Somerville and surrounding communities; and
WHEREAS, education has been a central focus of the Somerville Rotary Club's service,
including the provision of scholarships to graduating seniors, the sponsorship of Interact Clubs
and a Junior Rotarian program at local high schools, and support for leadership development
through the Rotary Youth Leadership Awards and the Tennessee Literacy Program; and
WHEREAS, the Somerville Rotary Club has provided a forum for business, professional,
faith, and civic leaders to collaborate in service, foster fellowship, and model ethical leadership
guided by the Rotary Four-Way Test; and
WHEREAS, through local partnerships with organizations such as Fayette Cares and
the Carl Perkins Center, as well as through park cleanups and community improvement
projects, the Somerville Rotary Club has continually demonstrated its hands-on commitment to
the well-being of Fayette County; and
WHEREAS, for five decades, the Somerville Rotary Club has upheld the highest
traditions of Rotary service, honoring the dedication of its charter members while inspiring new
generations to lead with compassion and purpose; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the members, both past and present, of the
Somerville Rotary Club for their unwavering commitment to service, fellowship, and leadership,
congratulate them on the club's fiftieth anniversary, and extend to them our best wishes for
every continued success in all their future endeavors.
