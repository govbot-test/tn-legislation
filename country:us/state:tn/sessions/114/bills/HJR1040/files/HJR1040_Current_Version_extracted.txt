HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1040
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Byrd Douglas Cain, Jr.,
of Williamsport.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Byrd Douglas Cain, Jr., of Williamsport; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Cain was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, Byrd Cain was born on August 14, 1936, to Margaret and Byrd Douglas
Cain, Sr.; from an early age, he was known for his curious mind, steady determination, and
willingness to lend a helping hand; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Cain worked in the automotive industry for fifty years, owning and
operating Cain Autoplex in Franklin until 2001; and
WHEREAS, Byrd Cain was a co-founder of Harpeth Academy in Franklin and served on
the board of directors of The Boys & Girls Club; he also served on the board of directors at the
Brentwood YMCA and as chairman of the Pontiac National Dealer Council; and
WHEREAS, he found joy in simple pleasures: time spent outdoors, stories shared
around the table, laughter and his legendary jokes, and quiet evenings on the farm enjoying
sunsets surrounded by his family; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Cain was an avid golfer, and he enjoyed his memberships at the
Hillwood Country Club, Nashville Golf and Athletic Club, and Graymere Country Club; and
WHEREAS, Byrd Cain will be especially remembered for his extraordinary kindness and
welcoming spirit; he had a way of making everyone feel at home, be it a lifelong friend or
someone he had just met; and

WHEREAS, his warmth was genuine and contagious, and his humor—quick, subtle, and
often perfectly timed—brought lightness to any room and left lasting smiles in its wake; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Cain was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his beloved wife of
thirty-five years, Anne; children, Doug (Terry) Cain, Leslie Cain, Scott (Susan) Cain, Austin
Pennington, and Trevor (Haverly) Pennington; granddaughters, Dr. Lauren Cain, Ashleigh Cain,
Dr. Virginia (Ethan) Cain, Margo (Mario) Cantu, and Hattie Anne Pennington; and grandson,
Beau Douglas Pennington; and
WHEREAS, Byrd Cain leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public
life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Byrd Douglas Cain, Jr., reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Cain.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
