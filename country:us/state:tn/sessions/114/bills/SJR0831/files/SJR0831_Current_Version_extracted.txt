SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 831
By Lamar
A RESOLUTION to honor Tracey Morant Adams, Ph.D., Alpha
Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc., South Eastern
Region Director.
WHEREAS, we are pleased to recognize the leaders of those estimable organizations
who, through their unflagging commitment and unwavering dedication to the highest tenets of
community service, enrich the lives of the citizens of this State; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Dr. Tracey Morant Adams, who currently serves the
South Eastern Region of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc., as Regional Director; and
WHEREAS, the South Eastern Region of Alpha Kappa Alpha includes the states of
Tennessee, Mississippi, and Alabama, with more than 14,000 active members who are
dedicated to the mission of their illustrious organization; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Cluster of the region has continued to grow and thrive over
the years; its current membership totals thirty-three chapters across the Volunteer State's three
Grand Divisions; and
WHEREAS, motivated by an altruistic concern for human welfare, Dr. Morant Adams
was initiated in the Mu Mu Chapter in 1985 and has remained an active member since that time;
she has served at all levels of the sorority, most recently as Chairman of the International
Membership Committee, spearheading a campaign to reactivate more than 30,000 members;
and
WHEREAS, her impressive tenure of service also includes being a Representative to the
Membership, Standards Committees, and the I/E Investigations Team; at the regional level, she
has served as Operations Chairman, Logistics Chairman, Secretary, and General Chairman of
the 79th South Eastern Regional Conference; and

WHEREAS, additionally, she is a Charter Member of the Upsilon Eta Omega Chapter
and a certified Parliamentarian, Graduate Advisor, and Financial Officer; and
WHEREAS, apart from her involvement with Alpha Kappa Alpha, Inc., Dr. Tracey Morant
Adams is Senior Executive Vice President and Chief Community Development and Corporate
Social Responsibility Officer for Renasant Bank and serves as Executive Strategist for the
bank's DEI framework; and
WHEREAS, her humanitarian efforts have been celebrated by many, including the
NAACP and Savoy magazine, which named her among the Most Influential Executives in
Corporate America; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Morant Adams's civic affiliations include serving on the Talladega
College Board of Trustees, the Birmingham Kiwanis Board, The Women's Foundation of
Alabama, the Children's Hospital of Alabama, and The Links, Incorporated; she also serves on
national community development advocacy boards and was appointed to the Alabama
Women's Tribute Statue Commission by the governor of Alabama in 2021; and
WHEREAS, her many accomplishments aside, she is most grateful for the love of her
husband, Jeffery J. Adams, Sr., a Life Member of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., and proud to
be the bonus mother of Jeffery Adams, Jr., and the daughter of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority,
Inc., Life Member Callie W. Morant; and
WHEREAS, Director Tracey Morant Adams has ably steered the South Eastern Region
toward continued, unparalleled success, ever furthering the mission of Alpha Kappa Alpha along
with her sorors from Tennessee, Mississippi, and Alabama; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc., South
