HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1074
By Littleton
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate the citizens of
Cheatham County upon the 170th anniversary of
the county's founding.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should join with the counties that comprise this great
State in celebrating joyous and notable occasions in their histories; and
WHEREAS, one such entity is Cheatham County, which this year celebrates the 170th
anniversary of its founding; and
WHEREAS, Cheatham County was officially established by the Tennessee General
Assembly on February 28, 1856, and was formed from portions of Davidson, Dickson,
Montgomery, and Robertson counties; and
WHEREAS, the county was named in honor of Edward Saunders Cheatham, a
distinguished Tennessee statesman who served in the Tennessee House of Representatives
and the Tennessee Senate and was highly regarded for his service to the citizens of this State;
and
WHEREAS, the county seat, Ashland City, was established along the banks of the
Cumberland River and became a center of commerce, transportation, and community life; and
WHEREAS, the Cumberland River played a vital role in the county's early economic
development, supporting trade, agriculture, timber, and transportation throughout the nineteenth
century; and
WHEREAS, throughout its history, Cheatham County has maintained strong agricultural
traditions, expanding into industry, small business development, and residential growth while
preserving its rural character and natural beauty; and

WHEREAS, the citizens of Cheatham County have demonstrated resilience and civic
pride throughout the county's history, contributing to the cultural, educational, and economic
advancement of Middle Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, generations of families have built churches, schools, farms, and businesses
that have shaped the identity and heritage of the county; and
WHEREAS, for 170 years, the citizens of Cheatham County have added and continue to
add immeasurably to the strength and character of the State of Tennessee; their history and
accomplishments have helped to build the cultural richness for which this State is known; and
WHEREAS, it is most appropriate that we should honor this fine community and its
citizenry as they commemorate this significant milestone in their historic existence; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate the citizens of Cheatham County upon the
170th anniversary of the county's founding and extend to them our best wishes for continued
success, growth, and prosperity in their vibrant, historic community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
