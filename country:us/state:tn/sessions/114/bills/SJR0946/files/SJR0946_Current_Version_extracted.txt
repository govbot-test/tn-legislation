SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 946
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to recognize Father Michael Woods, the 2026
Gala of Lights honoree, for his service to his
community and the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those citizens who, through their
extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community leaders of whom we can all
be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Father Michael Woods, the honoree at the
2026 Gala of Lights on Saturday, April 25, 2026, in Oak Ridge, who has served the Diocese of
Knoxville since 1994 with humility, warmth, and a deep love of community; and
WHEREAS, the Gala of Lights is St. Mary's School's signature fundraising event,
bringing together the community to celebrate, support, and shine, while supplying the much-
needed revenue necessary for the school to continue to provide exceptional education, rooted
in faith, tradition, and academic excellence; and
WHEREAS, ordained in Ireland, Father Michael Woods has been a priest for more than
six decades and is known for his distinctive Irish brogue, his heartfelt preaching, and his
unwavering commitment to bringing people together in faith; and
WHEREAS, this special event will bring together friends, parishioners, family, and
supporters to honor Father Woods's remarkable legacy while celebrating the light he has shared
so generously; and
WHEREAS, through his estimable service to his community, Father Michael Woods
epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Father Michael Woods
for his dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we specially recognize and congratulate Father
Michael Woods of the Diocese of Knoxville on being the honoree at the 2026 Gala of Lights, as
we commend his meritorious service to his community and this State.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
