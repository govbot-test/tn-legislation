HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1155
By Boyd
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Helen McPeak upon the
occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should honor those
esteemed professionals who have dedicated their time, talent, and energy to the betterment of
their communities and to the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Helen McPeak, CFE, IFM, is one such estimable individual, who has played
a vital role in the success and growth of the Wilson County–Tennessee State Fair, the largest
fair in the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, having served the organization with the utmost dedication, ability, and
integrity for more than four decades, Helen McPeak will be taking her well-deserved retirement
as Executive Director of the Wilson County–Tennessee State Fair on March 31, 2026; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. McPeak began her involvement with the Fair more than forty years ago
as a volunteer, and through her diligence and commitment, she advanced to serve as Volunteer
Director, interim Fair Coordinator, and Executive Director for more than fifteen years; and
WHEREAS, during her tenure, Helen McPeak has played a pivotal role in the continued
growth and success of the Wilson County–Tennessee State Fair, working alongside thousands
of dedicated volunteers and staff members each year to ensure a high-quality experience that
celebrates Tennessee's agricultural heritage and community spirit; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her leadership at the local level, Mrs. McPeak has rendered
outstanding service to the Tennessee Association of Fairs, serving on its board of directors as
well as on numerous committees, and she has also contributed her time and expertise to the
International Association of Fairs and Expositions and the Mid-West Fairs Association; and

WHEREAS, a lifelong resident of Wilson County, Helen McPeak and her family maintain
deep ties to the community, including the operation of a Century Farm that has been in her
family for more than two hundred years; and
WHEREAS, her many accomplishments aside, Mrs. McPeak is most grateful for the love
and support she shares with her husband, Terry; their sons, Justin, Luke, and Travis; and their
five grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, throughout her estimable career, Helen McPeak has won the respect and
admiration of her colleagues and community alike, and she should be recognized on this special
occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Helen McPeak upon her retirement as Executive
Director of the Wilson County–Tennessee State Fair, thank her for her many years of
outstanding service, and extend to her our best wishes for much happiness and fulfillment in her
future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
