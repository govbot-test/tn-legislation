SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 948
By Reeves
A RESOLUTION to recognize and celebrate the twentieth
anniversary of the Tennessee Agricultural
Enhancement Program (TAEP) and to honor its
enduring contributions to Tennessee agriculture.
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Agricultural Enhancement Program (TAEP) was established
in 2005 by the Tennessee General Assembly and is administered by the Tennessee
Department of Agriculture to strengthen farm operations and promote long-term agricultural
sustainability across the State; and
WHEREAS, originally created during a period of transition in Tennessee agriculture
following the conclusion of the federal tobacco program, TAEP was designed to provide
strategic cost-share assistance that would help producers adapt, diversify, and invest
confidently in their operations; and
WHEREAS, over the past twenty years, TAEP has grown far beyond its initial purpose
and has become a cornerstone program for Tennessee's agricultural community, widely
recognized and relied upon by farm families in every region of the State; and
WHEREAS, the program reimburses approved applicants for eligible expenses at a fifty
percent cost-share rate, subject to program-specific maximums, encouraging a strong financial
partnership between the State of Tennessee and agricultural producers, while enabling
producers to make meaningful capital improvements; and
WHEREAS, TAEP encompasses a broad array of program areas, including dairy
solutions, genetics, hay storage, hay equipment, herd health, livestock equipment, livestock
solutions, poultry grower, row crop solutions, swine producer, working structures and fence-line
systems, and producer diversification, supporting agritourism, fruits and vegetables, honey
bees, horticulture, organic production, and value-added enterprises; and

WHEREAS, through its annual application process and continuous refinement, TAEP
has supported more than 97,000 agricultural projects and invested more than $309 million in
cost-share funding since its inception, generating an estimated economic impact of more than
$2 billion for Tennessee's rural communities; and
WHEREAS, under the leadership of Commissioner Andy Holt and the dedicated
professionals of the Tennessee Department of Agriculture, the program continues to
demonstrate the State's unwavering commitment to agricultural advancement, rural prosperity,
and the preservation of Tennessee's farming heritage; and
WHEREAS, as TAEP celebrated its twentieth year in 2025, it stands as a testament to
thoughtful public policy, strong producer participation, and the enduring strength and resilience
of Tennessee agriculture; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Tennessee Agricultural
Enhancement Program upon the occasion of its twentieth anniversary and express our gratitude
for its lasting impact on the agricultural economy and farm families of this great State.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
