SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1253
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to recognize Lily D. Barnes on the occasion of
her retirement as Register of Deeds for
Hardeman County.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
careers in public service; and
WHEREAS, Lily D. Barnes, Register of Deeds for Hardeman County, is one such
estimable individual, who has distinguished herself as a professional of the greatest dedication,
ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, after twenty-eight years as Hardeman County Register of Deeds, Mrs.
Barnes is taking her much-deserved retirement from that office at the end of her current term;
and
WHEREAS, born on March 10, 1961, Lily D. Barnes is the daughter of Guy and Bobbie
Jean Dorris and was taught the importance of hard work and honesty at an early age alongside
her siblings, Guy, Scarlett, Amber, and David; and
WHEREAS, a 1979 graduate of Bolivar Central High School, Lily Barnes began her
public service career with Hardeman County in 1987 as an employee of the Hardeman County
Clerk's Office before taking two years off to stay home with her children; she returned to work in
the Hardeman County Trustees office for two years; and
WHEREAS, first elected to the post of Register of Deeds in August 1998 and unopposed
for six consecutive terms, Lily Barnes has provided steady and faithful leadership in maintaining
the public records of Hardeman County through a period of significant technological transition
and growth; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Barnes's commitment to professional excellence is reflected in her
extensive involvement in statewide and regional organizations, having served tenures as

president of the Tennessee Register Association and the County Officials Association of
Tennessee and as a member of committees of both organizations, including the Legislative,
Audit, Membership, and Awards committees; and
WHEREAS, a Certified Public Administrator, Lily Barnes completed the Local
Government Leadership Program in 2015 and was honored as the Outstanding Register of the
Year in 2001 and 2018 and named the Register of the Year in 2007; and
WHEREAS, an active part of her community, she is a longtime member of the Bolivar
Rotary Club, which she has served as president and treasurer, and is a faithful congregant of
Hornsby Baptist Church, where she teaches pre-school for Project 4:13; and
WHEREAS, a devoted wife, mother, and grandmother, Lily Barnes enjoys the love and
support of her husband, Greg Barnes; their children, Ginia (Ben), Clint, and Katiellen (Josh);
and their grandchildren, Ryder, Ridge, Brooks, and Landry; and
WHEREAS, throughout her distinguished career, Mrs. Barnes has demonstrated the
utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unqualified respect and admiration of
her colleagues and the citizens she has so ably served; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Lily D. Barnes upon her
retirement as Hardeman County Register of Deeds, thank her for her many years of dedicated
service, and extend to her our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every
continued success in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
