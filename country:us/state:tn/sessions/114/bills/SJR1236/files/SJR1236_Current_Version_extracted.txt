SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1236
By Hensley
A RESOLUTION to honor Reverend Jeff Kane of Westminster
Presbyterian Church in Columbia on the
occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those individuals who have answered
a divine call to the ministry and who have served with dedication, vigor, and enthusiasm to
perform God's work for their congregations and their communities; and
WHEREAS, Reverend Jeff Kane is one such venerable individual, who has inspired the
faithful as the pastor of Westminster Presbyterian Church in Columbia for more than twelve
years; and
WHEREAS, Reverend Kane will soon retire to Arkansas, having served with an
unwavering commitment of service to the people whose spiritual lives he guided throughout his
vibrant ministry; and
WHEREAS, originally from Michigan, Reverend Kane has poured himself into the
community—not only preaching the Gospel with clarity and conviction, but living it out daily
through compassion, discipleship, and love for both God and people; he has been recognized
for his work with at-risk youth, which showcases his commitment to social issues; and
WHEREAS, under Reverend Kane's leadership, Westminster Presbyterian Church has
expanded its outreach programs, including partnerships aimed at supporting vulnerable
populations, helping the un-housed; he founded the church's Room In The Inn program and its
successor, Grace House; and
WHEREAS, instead of fully retiring from the ministry, Reverend Kane will serve as the
senior minister at the Presbyterian church located in the retirement community that he and his
wife, Carol, are moving to in Arkansas; and

WHEREAS, a true shepherd of the flock, Reverend Jeff Kane has an abiding love for the
ministry; his many positive contributions to his church, his community, and this State are deeply
appreciated, and it is wholly fitting that he be recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Reverend Jeff Kane on
the occasion of his retirement and extend to him our best wishes for much continued success
and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
