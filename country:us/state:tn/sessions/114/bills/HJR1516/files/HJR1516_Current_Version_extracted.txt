HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1516
By Littleton
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend former United States
Congressman Robert Nelson Clement for his
distinguished service to the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those distinguished citizens whose lives of public service and leadership have profoundly
enriched the State of Tennessee and the nation; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Robert Nelson Clement, known to all as Bob
Clement, who has devoted his life to public service with a steadfast commitment to the people of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, born September 23, 1943, in Nashville, Congressman Clement is the eldest
son of former Governor Frank Goad Clement and First Lady Lucille Christianson Clement, from
whom he inherited a deep appreciation for civic duty, public leadership, and the enduring values
of service; and
WHEREAS, in 1972, at the remarkable age of twenty-nine, Bob Clement was elected to
the Tennessee Public Service Commission, becoming the youngest statewide elected official in
the history of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, in 1979, he was appointed by President Jimmy Carter to serve on the Board
of Directors of the Tennessee Valley Authority, where he contributed to the advancement of one
of the nation's most significant public utilities and economic development engines; and
WHEREAS, from 1983 to 1987, Congressman Clement served as President of
Cumberland University in Lebanon, where he provided visionary leadership, guiding the
institution's transformation into a four-year, fully accredited university and strengthening its role
as a center of higher education in the region; and

WHEREAS, in 1988, he was elected to the United States House of Representatives from
Tennessee's Fifth Congressional District and went on to serve eight consecutive terms, from
January 19, 1989, to January 3, 2003, earning widespread respect for his bipartisan approach,
dedication to his constituents, and advocacy for infrastructure, education, veterans, and
economic development; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure in Congress, Congressman Clement served with
distinction on key committees and was widely regarded as a thoughtful and effective legislator
who placed the interests of Tennessee above partisan considerations; and
WHEREAS, following his retirement from Congress in 2003, Bob Clement continued his
lifelong commitment to public service and historic preservation, particularly in his beloved home
of Davidson County and nearby Dickson County; and
WHEREAS, he was instrumental in the creation and development of the Clement
Railroad Hotel Museum at the Hotel Halbrook State Historic Site in Dickson, helping to preserve
the historic birthplace of Governor Frank G. Clement and ensuring that future generations may
learn from and be inspired by Tennessee's rich history; and
WHEREAS, Congressman Clement served as the Museum's first Board Chair, providing
critical leadership during its formative years, and continues to serve the institution with
distinction as President Emeritus and Chair of Development, demonstrating his unwavering
dedication to historical preservation, education, and community engagement; and
WHEREAS, throughout his life, Bob Clement has exemplified integrity, leadership, and a
deep love for the people of Tennessee, securing an enduring legacy that will continue to inspire
generations to come; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly acknowledge
this esteemed gentleman; now, therefore,
- 2 - 018247
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and commend former United States Congressman
Robert Nelson Clement for his extraordinary career in public service, his lasting contributions to
the State of Tennessee, and his continued dedication to preserving our shared history. We
extend to him our best wishes for much continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 3 - 018247
