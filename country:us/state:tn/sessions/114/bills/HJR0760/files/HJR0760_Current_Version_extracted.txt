HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 760
By Hale
A RESOLUTION to honor Coach Kevin Thomas on his 500th
career victory.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
those excellent coaches who have attained career milestones while leading their teams to
unprecedented success in athletic competition; and
WHEREAS, Coach Kevin Thomas of Jackson County High School recently notched his
500th career victory, a praiseworthy achievement and a testament to his devotion to his student
athletes and the game of basketball; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of Jackson County High School, Coach Thomas began his
successful coaching tenure at his alma mater, serving as assistant coach for two years before
being named head boys' basketball coach at Shelbyville Central High School in 1999; and
WHEREAS, Coach Kevin Thomas led the Shelbyville Central boys' basketball program
for thirteen seasons (1999-2012), after which time he transitioned to serve as head coach of the
Shelbyville Central girls' basketball program; and
WHEREAS, in the summer of 2016, he returned home to Jackson County to take over
the boys' basketball program as head coach; under his leadership, the program has continued
to grow, highlighted by a historic run to the state Final Four last season, which marked the
deepest state tournament run in program history; and
WHEREAS, Kevin Thomas has distinguished himself as a great basketball coach,
tactician, and motivator of young men and women, and he should be appropriately recognized
on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
HJR0760
011231
- 1 -
CONCURRING, that we honor Kevin Thomas of Jackson County High School on his 500th
career victory as a basketball coach and extend to him our best wishes for much continued
success on the court and in life.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
