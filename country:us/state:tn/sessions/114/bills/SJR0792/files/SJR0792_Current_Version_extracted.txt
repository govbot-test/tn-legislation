SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 792
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Caroline Brady of
Morristown-Hamblen West High School on being
named to the Tennessee Music Education
Association All-State choir.
WHEREAS, we are pleased to recognize and commend those successful high school
artists who, through their natural talents and extraordinary efforts, bring the joys of creative
expression and culture to the citizens of this State while promoting the importance of the
performing arts; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Caroline Brady of Morristown-Hamblen
West High School, a performer of extraordinary talent, skill, and dedication, who has proven
herself an exceptional vocalist; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Brady was recently named to the Tennessee Music Education
Association All-State choir in recognition of her excellence as a vocalist at Morristown-Hamblen
West High School; and
WHEREAS, she will be participating in All-State events in Nashville at the Gaylord
Opryland Resort in April 2026; and
WHEREAS, Caroline Brady epitomizes all that is good in today's student artist, as she
has achieved a high level of success in music while evincing great character both on and off the
stage; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Brady's notable achievement is a source of great pride for her family,
her community, and her school, and it is appropriate that we recognize this accomplished artist;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
SJR0792
011483
- 1 -
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Caroline Brady of
Morristown-Hamblen West High School on being named to the Tennessee Music Education
Association All-State choir, wishing her much success and happiness in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
