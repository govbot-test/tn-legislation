HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1660
By Behn
A RESOLUTION to honor the workers killed at the explosion at
the Accurate Energetic Systems facility on
October 10, 2025.
WHEREAS, on October 10, 2025, a catastrophic explosion at the Accurate Energetic
Systems (AES) facility in McEwen killed sixteen workers and injured seven others, making it one
of the deadliest industrial disasters in the United States in recent years; and
WHEREAS, the sixteen lives lost were Jason Adams, Erick Anderson, Billy Baker, Adam
Boatman, Christopher Clark, Mindy Clifton, James Cook, Reyna Gillahan, LeTeisha Mays,
Jeremy Moore, Melinda Rainey, Melissa Stanford, Trenton Stewart, Rachel Woodall, Steven
Wright, and Donald Yowell, neighbors and community members from Humphreys and Hickman
counties who went to work and did not come home; and
WHEREAS, behind every fatality number is a worker: someone who has family, friends,
community, and a life, and these sixteen are remembered not as statistics but as beloved
members of their families and their communities; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Occupational Safety and Health Administration found fifty-
nine willful violations and thirty-two serious violations at the AES facility and assessed the
highest workplace safety penalties in State history at $3,133,900, confirming that these deaths
were preventable; and
WHEREAS, observed each year on April 28th, Workers Memorial Day was established to
recognize workers who died or suffered from exposures to hazards at work and to encourage us
to think of ways in which we all can help achieve the goal of safer and healthier workplaces;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
HJR1660
018693
- 1 -
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of the sixteen workers lost at Accurate Energetic
Systems and extend our deepest condolences to their families and the communities of
Humphreys and Hickman counties.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
