HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 748
By Reeves
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Allan Donald Williams of
Franklin.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the untimely passing of Allan Donald Williams; and
WHEREAS, Allan Williams was an outstanding and heroic public servant who lost his life
in the line of service, a solemn reminder of the risks bravely undertaken by emergency
responders every day; and
WHEREAS, a devoted husband and father, Mr. Williams served with distinction as a
flight nurse and paramedic for Vanderbilt LifeFlight 1, where he was a valued member of a
critical care air transport team dedicated to saving lives; and
WHEREAS, born on March 5, 1970, in Alberta, Canada, Mr. Williams immigrated to the
United States in his twenties to pursue a nursing career and proudly became a dual citizen; and
WHEREAS, he held a bachelor's degree in nursing from the University of Alberta and a
master's degree in nursing education from Walden University, and his professional credentials
reflected both his deep knowledge of and commitment to patient care; and
WHEREAS, over the course of his remarkable thirty-one-year career in nursing, Allan
Williams earned the respect of his colleagues and patients alike for his clinical excellence, his
mentorship, and his unwavering kindness; and
WHEREAS, he recently celebrated fifteen years with Vanderbilt University Medical
Center, serving not only as a flight nurse and paramedic for LifeFlight 1, but also as a key
member of LifeFlight Event Medicine and a dedicated nurse educator whose impact reached
well beyond Tennessee; and

WHEREAS, a passionate teacher and mentor, Mr. Williams served as an adjunct faculty
member at Columbia State Community College and as a member of the Education Committee
of the Air and Surface Transport Nurses Association; and
WHEREAS, Allan Williams exemplified the highest ideals of service, compassion, and
integrity; his colleagues remember him as a steady and joyful presence, known for his
generosity, encouragement, and ready smile; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to his family, Mr. Williams always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his beloved wife, Jennifer Williams, and their sons,
Emerson Williams and Sebastian Williams, as well as children from his first marriage, Alex
Williams and Nathan Williams; father, Roy (Linda) Williams; sisters, Sandra Williams and
Amanda Williams; brother, Curtis Williams; mother-in-law, Mamie Moore; sister-in-law, Elizabeth
(David) Vaught; and many other loving family members and friends; and
WHEREAS, Allan Williams was preceded in death by his mother, Colleen Fay
Magnuson, and father-in-law, Dr. Walton Louis Moore; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Williams leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity, probity, and valor
in his public life, compassion and loyalty in his private life, and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Allan Donald Williams, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Williams.
