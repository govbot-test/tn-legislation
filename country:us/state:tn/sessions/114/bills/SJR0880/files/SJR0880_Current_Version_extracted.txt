SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 880
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Black Dog Fitness and
owner Zach Guza, recipient of the Business
Award at the 24th Annual John J. Duncan Sr.
Awards for Senior Advocacy.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
exemplary businesses and their owners, who, through their extraordinary efforts, have
distinguished themselves as community leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such exceptional business is Black Dog Fitness, owned by Zach Guza,
which has been named the winner of the Business Award at the 24th Annual John J. Duncan Sr.
Awards for Senior Advocacy; and
WHEREAS, the John J. Duncan Sr. Awards for Senior Advocacy were established to
honor those individuals and businesses who embody the legacy of the late U.S. Representative
John J. Duncan, Sr., who was an advocate for older adults in the Second Congressional District
of the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, a local boxing gym just for people with Parkinson's disease, Black Dog
Fitness was founded by Zach Guza, a personal trainer, who launched the business from his
garage as a private personal training program to help his clients fight anxiety and depression
with exercise; and
WHEREAS, after learning about the benefits of exercise for people with Parkinson's
disease, Mr. Guza started Rock Steady Boxing-Knoxville in 2016 in honor of his father, Sam
Guza, who died with Parkinson's disease; and
WHEREAS, ten years later, Black Dog Fitness has its own location with over 170
members, six coaches, seven different exercise programs, a YouTube channel with over 4,000
subscribers, and twenty-two weekly classes; and

WHEREAS, Zach Guza is a graduate of the United States Air Force Academy and
served as a helicopter pilot in the U.S. Air Force for nine years; after the military, he worked in
civilian manufacturing before deciding to make a career for himself in the fitness industry,
having realized that exercise as his self-therapy had probably saved his life after being
diagnosed with depression; and
WHEREAS, his many professional accomplishments aside, Mr. Guza is most thankful
for the love and support of his devoted wife, Jenny, and their wonderful sons, Brice and Blair;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Guza, through his efforts at Black Dog Fitness, has worked tirelessly to
serve seniors in his community, and it is most fitting that he be honored; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Black Dog Fitness and
owner Zach Guza upon receiving the Business Award at the 24th Annual John J. Duncan Sr.
Awards for Senior Advocacy, extending our best wishes for much continued success in the
years to come.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
