SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 583
By Roberts
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Humphreys County
Sheriff Chris Davis upon being named 2025
Tennessean of the Year by The Tennessean.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those estimable public servants who have dedicated themselves to the safety and wellbeing of
their fellow citizens and who, in times of crisis, exemplify courage, compassion, and steady
leadership; and
WHEREAS, Humphreys County Sheriff Chris Davis is one such outstanding
Tennessean, having been named the 2025 Tennessean of the Year by the editorial board of
The Tennessean in recognition of his empathy, grace, and leadership in the face of profound
tragedy; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Davis is a proud son of Waverly and a graduate of Waverly Central
High School, where he was a standout on the football team; he pursued higher education at
Murray State University, studying public safety and health and playing on the 1986 Ohio Valley
Conference Championship football team under legendary coach Frank Beamer, and at Middle
Tennessee State University, where he studied criminal justice and law enforcement
administration; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Chris Davis began his law enforcement career in 1986 as a
dispatcher and jailer with the Humphreys County Sheriff's Office under Sheriff Ronnie
Toungette; and
WHEREAS, in 1989, he joined the Dickson County Sheriff's Office, and in 1990, he
became a patrolman for the City of Dickson Police Department, where he taught the D.A.R.E.
curriculum in Dickson and Humphreys County schools, earned the rank of corporal, and served
on the Special Response Team; and

WHEREAS, in 1993, he joined the 23rd Judicial District Drug Task Force in the Criminal
Investigation Unit and was promoted to chief agent in 1995; three years later, he continued his
service to the State as an officer with the Tennessee Alcoholic Beverage Commission; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Davis returned to the Dickson County Sheriff's Office in 1999 under
Sheriff Tom Wall, where he was assigned to the vice narcotics unit and became a member of
the hostage negotiation team; and
WHEREAS, in 2006, at the age of thirty-seven, Chris Davis was elected Sheriff of
Humphreys County, a position in which he has served with distinction ever since; and
WHEREAS, throughout his thirty-plus years in law enforcement, Sheriff Davis has been
present at many of his county's most difficult moments; in August 2021, when unprecedented
floods devastated Waverly and surrounding areas, destroying homes and businesses and
claiming twenty lives, he led with resolve and compassion, bearing witness to heartbreaking
loss while helping guide his community through response, recovery, and grief; and
WHEREAS, on October 10, 2025, when an explosion at the Accurate Energetic Systems
munitions facility on the Humphreys–Hickman County line claimed sixteen lives, Sheriff Davis
again rushed to the scene, coordinating with first responders, plant officials, and neighboring
agencies, even as he learned that longtime friends and neighbors were among the missing; and
WHEREAS, in the days that followed, Sheriff Chris Davis worked closely with Hickman
County Sheriff Jason Craft and other officials, helped direct ongoing emergency operations, and
undertook the duty of delivering death notifications to families; during press briefings and
community vigils, his visible emotion and honest acknowledgment of shared pain became a
source of strength and connection for a grieving community; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Davis has been quoted locally, nationally, and internationally for his
remarks in the wake of these tragedies, and he has used his platform to speak candidly about
the emotional toll such events take on first responders and survivors; he has encouraged his

deputies and colleagues to seek counseling and debriefings and has helped to lessen the
stigma surrounding mental health care, particularly for men in rural communities; and
WHEREAS, beyond his official duties, Sheriff Davis has organized and supported
numerous fish fry fundraisers for residents battling life-threatening illnesses, helped restore
utilities for families whose power had been cut off, and, for some thirty years, has played Santa
Claus and helped organize Angel Tree efforts so that children in need receive gifts at Christmas;
and
WHEREAS, a devoted family man, Sheriff Chris Davis is the son of Dickie Davis and
Tonia Wiles and the grandson of Daniel D. and Lois Long Davis and Clint "Jack" and Vivian
Woods; he and his wife, Daphne Peek Davis, are the proud parents of three adult children,
David, Daniel, and Eran, who carries on the family legacy of service as an employee of the
Humphreys County Sheriff's Office; and
WHEREAS, in naming Sheriff Davis its 2025 Tennessean of the Year, The Tennessean
editorial board recognized his long record of public service, his steady leadership during the
2021 flood and the 2025 plant explosion, and his ongoing service to the people of Humphreys
County; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Chris Davis epitomizes the spirit of compassion and courage that are
characteristic of a true Tennessean, and it is wholly appropriate that we honor him for his
exemplary record of service to the people of Humphreys County and this State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Humphreys County Sheriff
Chris Davis upon being named the 2025 Tennessean of the Year by The Tennessean and
extend to him our deepest gratitude for his decades of faithful service to the people of
Humphreys County and the State of Tennessee.
