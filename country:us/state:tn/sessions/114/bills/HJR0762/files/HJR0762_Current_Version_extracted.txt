HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 762
By Reneau
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Kay Lee Arthur of
Chattanooga.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Kay Lee Arthur; and
WHEREAS, a highly respected author, teacher, and cofounder of the nonprofit ministry
Precept, Mrs. Arthur worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Jackson, Michigan, on November 11, 1933, Kay Arthur spent her
early years moving frequently before settling in Cleveland, Ohio, where she attended nursing
school at Case Western Reserve University; after earning a degree as a registered nurse, she
married Tom Goetz, and the couple had two sons, Tom and Mark, before their marriage ended;
and
WHEREAS, following a period of significant personal hardship, which she later
described with the observation that "All suffering has an end," Mrs. Arthur experienced a
deepening of the Christian faith that would shape the remainder of her professional and
personal life; and
WHEREAS, Kay Arthur later studied at Tennessee Temple University in Chattanooga,
where she met Tennessee Temple graduate and missionary Jack Arthur; the two were married
in 1965 and, over the course of their fifty-one-year marriage, worked together in Christian
service and education until Mr. Arthur's passing in 2017; and
WHEREAS, Kay and Jack Arthur served as independent missionaries in Mexico, where
their son, David, was born; after leaving the mission field, they returned to Chattanooga, where
Mrs. Arthur began leading Bible studies for teenagers in their living room; and

WHEREAS, what started as a small, local study group grew into Precept, a nonprofit
ministry based in Chattanooga that now serves nearly 190 countries in more than 110
languages, helping people around the world engage with Scripture in a structured way; and
WHEREAS, from 1999 to 2019, Mrs. Arthur hosted Precepts for Life™, a syndicated
Bible study teaching program that was broadcast to more than thirty countries and reached over
seventy-five million households; the program was twice recognized as Best Television Teaching
Program by the National Religious Broadcasters; and
WHEREAS, Kay Arthur's work earned national and international recognition; she was a
four-time Evangelical Christian Publishers Association (ECPA) Gold Medallion Award-winning
author, a recipient of ECPA's Pinnacle Award, and a recipient of both the National Religious
Broadcasters Hall of Fame Award and Lifetime Achievement Award; and
WHEREAS, in acknowledgment of her contributions to Christian education and
publishing, she received an honorary Doctor of Humane Letters degree from Tennessee
Temple University and an honorary Doctor of Divinity degree from Piedmont International
University; and
WHEREAS, even after being diagnosed with Parkinson's disease in 2020, Kay Arthur
continued to be involved with Precept for as long as her health permitted, demonstrating
perseverance and a sustained commitment to the mission she helped to establish; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Arthur was deeply devoted to her family, and she always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Kay Arthur is survived by her three sons, Tom Goetz, Mark Goetz, and
David (Margaret) Arthur; nine grandchildren, Meg (John) Day, Joseph Goetz, Daniel Goetz,
John (Whitney) Goetz, Ryan Goetz, Alexandra Goetz, Jesse (Zach) Callahan, Abigail (Robert)
Edens, and Ann Arthur; and seven great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pause to remember the bountiful life of this
exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
- 2 - 010873
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Kay Lee Arthur, reflecting fondly upon her
impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mrs. Arthur.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
