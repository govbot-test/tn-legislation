SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 692
By White
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Norman Brown,
Rutherford County Chamber of Commerce 2025
Business Legend of the Year.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those individuals whose vision, ambition, and work ethic are driving forces behind the success
of local economies and the economy of this State; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Norman Brown, who was recently named the 2025
Business Legend of the Year by the Rutherford County Chamber of Commerce, a most befitting
honor that recognizes his important contributions to his community; and
WHEREAS, owner and president of Roscoe Brown, Inc., Norman Brown is a third-
generation business owner, community leader, and servant-minded executive whose influence
has shaped Rutherford County for decades; and
WHEREAS, under Mr. Brown's leadership, Roscoe Brown has grown from a trusted
local HVAC company into one of Middle Tennessee's most respected HVAC, plumbing, and
insulation organizations, expanding its footprint to Nashville, Tullahoma, and McMinnville all
while preserving the company's family-owned culture and reputation for excellence; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Brown is a pillar of the Rutherford County community, generously
investing his time, resources, and leadership in organizations that strengthen education,
workforce development, and quality of life; and
WHEREAS, he is a former chairman of the Rutherford County Chamber of Commerce
and a long-time supporter of Destination Rutherford; he has also served as co-chair of the
Workforce Development Construction Council; and
WHEREAS, following in the footsteps of his grandfather and the company's founder,
Roscoe Brown, and his father, Gary Brown, who was previously honored as a Business Legend,

Norman Brown welcomed his son, Will Brown, to the family business in 2025 during the
company's eighty-fifth anniversary; and
WHEREAS, throughout his many worthy endeavors, Mr. Brown has enjoyed the love
and support of his wife, Beth, and their two children, Will and Courtney, and two grandchildren,
Waylon and Leighton; and
WHEREAS, Norman Brown has devoted his career to improving the well-being of the
citizens of Rutherford County, and it is most fitting that he be recognized at this time; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Norman Brown, Rutherford
County Chamber of Commerce 2025 Business Legend of the Year, extending our best wishes
for every continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
