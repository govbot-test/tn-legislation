HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1532
By Stinnett
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Charles Dwight Bailey of
East Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Charles Dwight Bailey; and
WHEREAS, a man of faith and esteemed community leader, Charles Bailey was a
consummate gentleman who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow
citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Maryville on February 10, 1947, to Ralph and Johnnie Mae Bailey,
Mr. Bailey was a graduate of Friendsville High School and Carson-Newman University, from
which he earned his degree to teach in the public school system; he later earned his master's
degree from the University of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Charles Bailey enjoyed a long career in education at Greenback High
School, where he served as a coach, teacher, athletics director, vice principal, and principal
during his tenure; he was an essential and beloved fixture of the school, and he treasured the
community he found there; and
WHEREAS, he was called into a preaching ministry and was voted to be the bi-
vocational pastor of Oak View Baptist Church in 1977, and shortly thereafter, he was called to
be the full-time pastor; during that time, he felt like an advanced degree was necessary, so he
pursued and earned his doctorate in Biblical Studies while he was at Oak View; and
WHEREAS, in 1989, Mr. Bailey left Oak View to become the pastor of Laurel Bank
Baptist Church, where he enjoyed a long, fruitful ministry that spanned twenty-six years until his
"first" retirement; because preaching the gospel and caring for his congregants were such an

important part of his life, he came out of retirement to pastor the loving congregation of Central
Baptist Church of Alcoa; and
WHEREAS, Charles Bailey was an honest, funny, creative, loving, genuine man who
followed Christ and served his Lord his entire life; he was proud of his association with the
Tennessee Baptist Convention, his participation in the Chilhowee Baptist Association, and his
role as the home plate umpire in the championship game of the very first Smoky Mountain
Classic softball tournament; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bailey is survived by his wife of fifty years, JoAnn Cargile Bailey; his
brother, Ronda (Lou) Bailey; his sons, Lee (JoAnne Everett) Bailey and Jackson (Kimberly
Whitman) Bailey; and his six grandchildren, Maxwell Bailey, Levi Bailey, Ella Bailey, Emma
Bailey, Dean Bailey, and Audrey Bailey; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his first wife, Jane
Davenport Bailey, as well as his brother, Keith Bailey; and
WHEREAS, Charles Bailey leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Charles Dwight Bailey, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
