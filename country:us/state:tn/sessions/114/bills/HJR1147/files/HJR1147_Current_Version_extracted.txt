HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1147
By Sherrell
A RESOLUTION opposing the closure of the McMinnville VA clinic
in Warren County.
WHEREAS, Warren County and the surrounding area are home to a United States
military veteran population of thousands of citizens who have served in the defense and
protection of our country; and
WHEREAS, the United States government has put in place a mechanism to care for the
medical needs of United States military veterans, and that mechanism is the Department of
Veterans Affairs (VA), which operates medical facilities throughout this nation; and
WHEREAS, one of those medical facilities, the McMinnville VA Clinic, is operated within
the jurisdictional boundaries of Warren County to serve the physical and mental health of the
military veterans who live in Warren County and surrounding counties; and
WHEREAS, the Department of Veterans Affairs has officially announced the closure of
the McMinnville VA clinic by May 31, 2026, putting an undue burden on our United States
military veterans in obtaining quality health care in their community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we urge the Tennessee Congressional delegation to take all necessary
measures to ensure that the McMinnville VA Clinic in Warren County remain in operation to
serve the healthcare needs of our military veterans who have sacrificed so much in service to
their country.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that a certified copy of this resolution be transmitted to
each member of the Tennessee Congressional delegation.
HJR1147
