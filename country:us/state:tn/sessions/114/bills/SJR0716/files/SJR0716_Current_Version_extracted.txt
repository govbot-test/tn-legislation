SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 716
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Thomas Vandiver of
Bolivar.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Thomas Vandiver of Bolivar; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Vandiver was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on February 12, 1954, Mr. Vandiver devoted his life to family,
community, career, and interests that brought him joy; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Vandiver graduated from the University of Memphis with a Master
of Education degree and served as a dedicated teacher for many years at Bolivar Central High
School and Middleton High School; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his career as an educator, Mr. Vandiver worked as a
contractor, sharing his skills and knowledge with those around him; and
WHEREAS, dedicated to serving others, Thomas Vandiver supported his community
with enthusiasm and commitment as an active member of the Bolivar Civitan Club and a
member of the Tennessee National Guard, which he served for six years; and
WHEREAS, known for his love of lighthouses, Mr. Vandiver spent his leisure time
outdoors fishing, engaging in woodcraft and music, and, above all, spending time with his
beloved grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Vandiver was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his loving wife, Peggy Ann Vandiver; his daughter, Stacy
(Eric) Seaton; his son, Stephan (Rebekah) Vandiver; his brothers, Donald (Caroline) Vandiver

and Ronald (Renee) Vandiver; his grandchildren, Riley Seaton, Katie (Bradley) Booth, Owen
Vandiver, Sara Vandiver, Rachel Vandiver, Phoebe Vandiver, and Thomas Vandiver; and his
dog, Holly; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Vandiver was preceded in death by his father, James Thomas
Vandiver, and his mother, Georgia Lucille Grantham Vandiver; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Vandiver leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Thomas Vandiver,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Vandiver.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
