SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 936
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor and recognize Samuel Milton Leslie on
the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
honor those individuals who have answered a divine call to the ministry and have served with
dedication, vigor, and enthusiasm to perform God's work for their congregations and
communities; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Samuel Milton Leslie, Minister of Music at South
Harriman Baptist Church, who is taking his much-deserved retirement following thirty years of
devout service in that role; and
WHEREAS, born on December 27, 1956, in Jackson, Mr. Leslie was raised in a
Christian home with two older sisters; the family attended worship services led by his
grandfather at a south Georgia Baptist church; and
WHEREAS, his mother was involved in music and youth activities at their church, led a
puppet team, and enjoyed participating in national and international missions; his father, a
World War II Army veteran, worked for South Central Bell; and
WHEREAS, Sam Leslie was saved at the age of seventeen following a mission trip to
North Jackson Baptist Church; during his first year at college, he felt called into full-time
Christian service, and he was ordained in January 1986 at Mignon Baptist Church in Alabama;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Leslie earned his Associate of Science degree in Music from Jackson
State Community College, his Bachelor of Music degree in Sacred Music with a minor in Music
Theory from Union University, and his Master of Church Music degree with a major in Voice and
a minor in Youth Ministry from The Southern Baptist Theological Seminary; and

WHEREAS, in his mission to help others in developing their relationship with God, Sam
Leslie has served in music ministry, youth ministry, senior adult ministry, and mission trips
throughout his life; he has enjoyed leading Vacation Bible Schools, the Judgment House, and
youth conferences with the Big Emory Baptist Association; and
WHEREAS, his professional ministry experience began in 1976, when he served as
Minister of Youth at Highland Park Baptist Church in Jackson, concluding that tenure in 1978,
the year he went on a summer mission trip to New Hampshire and accepted the position of
Minister of Youth at North Jackson Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Leslie became interim Minister of Music at Hillcrest Baptist Church in
Jackson in 1982 and served as Minister of Music and Youth at Fordsville Baptist Church in
Kentucky from 1983 to 1985, at which time he relocated to Mignon Baptist Church in Sylacauga,
Alabama, to serve as Minister of Music and Education from 1985 to 1995; and
WHEREAS, returning home to Tennessee, he began his tenure as Minister of Music at
South Harriman Baptist Church in 1996, and since that time, he has served the people of his
congregation with enthusiasm and dedication, inspiring many through the power of worship
music; and
WHEREAS, a vocalist himself, Mr. Leslie has lent his vocal talents to the Alabama
Singing Men of the Alabama Baptist State Board of Missions and currently enjoys his ministry
with the Tennessee Men's Chorale of the Tennessee Baptist Mission Board; and
WHEREAS, his professional accomplishments aside, he has been blessed with the love
and support of his wife, Leigh Ann Leslie, and their two sons, Aaron and Daniel, who serve full-
time in ministry; and
WHEREAS, Samuel Leslie has continued to demonstrate excellence in his calling as a
man of God; the members of his congregation have been proud to call him their minister and
friend; and

WHEREAS, a true shepherd of the flock, Mr. Leslie has an abiding love for the ministry;
his many contributions to his church, his community, and this State are deeply appreciated, and
it is wholly fitting that he be recognized on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Samuel Milton Leslie on
the occasion of his retirement, thank him for his meritorious service as Minister of Music at
South Harriman Baptist Church, and extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
