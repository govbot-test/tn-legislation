HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1565
By Warner
A RESOLUTION to honor James H. Bingham on the occasion of
his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of public service; and
WHEREAS, James H. Bingham is one such estimable person who has served for three
terms as Mayor of Lewisburg, from which position he will take his much-deserved retirement this
year; and
WHEREAS, throughout his exemplary tenure as mayor, Mr. Bingham has distinguished
himself as a public-spirited citizen of the highest order and as an exceptional asset to his
community; and
WHEREAS, born on October 21, 1942, in Hardin County, Mr. Bingham is the son of
James Howard and Dorothy Newman Bingham and the brother of William and Catherine; and
WHEREAS, in 1944, the Bingham family moved to Warren, Michigan, where Mr.
Bingham's father worked for the Packard Company, and his younger brother, Ronald, was born;
James Bingham attended elementary and junior high school there and was an active member of
the Boy Scouts; and
WHEREAS, the family moved to Nashville in 1958, when Mr. Bingham's father secured a
job with the United States Postal Service, and James Bingham attended Cohn, Central, and
David Lipscomb high schools; he graduated from David Lipscomb High School in 1960 and
earned a bachelor's degree in engineering from Vanderbilt University in 1964; and
WHEREAS, following his undergraduate studies, Mr. Bingham served the Tennessee
Department of Transportation (TDOT) as a highway design engineer and was later promoted to

assistant regional design engineer; he further served TDOT as an assistant state rural roads
engineer and as chief of local programs; and
WHEREAS, in 1968, he married, Carolyn Newlon, an educator with Metro Nashville
school system; and
WHEREAS, together, James and Carolyn Bingham made their home in the Madison
community, and their first daughter, Jamie Carol, was born in 1972; Elizabeth Ann "Betsy"
arrived in 1975, and their family became complete with the birth of their son, James Andrew
"Drew" in 1980; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bingham left TDOT and relocated to Marshall County in 1978, where he
established Bingham Engineering in Lewisburg; his firm provided professional engineering
services and land surveying; and
WHEREAS, throughout the years, James Bingham has been actively involved in his
community, attending Church Street Church of Christ, where he has served as a co-teacher in a
Sunday School class, delivered meals through the Saturday Meals program, and participated in
mission trips to Montana, Mexico, and Ghana; he also served as a deacon and elder; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bingham is a highly respected member of his community, having served
in numerous capacities over the past forty-eight years, including as President of the Lewisburg
Lions Club; President of the Marshall County Community Theatre; Chairman of the Tennessee
State Service Council of the American Red Cross; Chairman of the Tennessee Children's Home
Board of Directors; Tennessee State Representative of the Southeast U.S./Japan Association;
and as a member of the Marshall County Commission, which he served for two terms; and
WHEREAS, in addition to sponsoring a local Little League team, Mr. Bingham
contributed his engineering expertise to designing the Richard Cashion Little League Complex,
and he continues to serve his community as a dedicated member of the Duck River Advisory
Board; and
- 2 - 018105
WHEREAS, Mr. Bingham has demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and
integrity throughout his professional and public service career, winning the unbridled respect
and admiration of his community; and
WHEREAS, his remarkable success as a public servant and civic leader is directly
attributable to his reliance on the time-honored values of hard work and common sense and his
uncommon ability to work well with people from all walks of life; and
WHEREAS, a strong advocate for the citizens of this State, Mr. Bingham is wholly
committed to the noble precepts of public service that have earned Tennessee recognition as
the Volunteer State, and he should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor James H. Bingham for his honorable and astute service to the
citizens of this State and extend to him our best wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
