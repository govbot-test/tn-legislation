HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1134
By Martin G
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Tonya Craft on her
distinguished service to the legal profession and
the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those citizens who, through their
extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community leaders of whom we can all
be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Tonya Craft, an attorney whose personal
experience inspired her to obtain her legal degree and help others pursue truth and justice; and
WHEREAS, after a false accusation of sexual abuse ended her twenty-year career as a
kindergarten teacher in 2007, Tonya Craft had to advocate for herself by assembling a team of
attorneys and expert witnesses to pursue justice in her case; and
WHEREAS, fighting for the truth for more than two years in the legal system, Ms. Craft
was acquitted of all charges in 2010 and subsequently decided to earn a law degree,
completing law school and being sworn in as an attorney in 2019; and
WHEREAS, today, Ms. Craft is the head of Tonya Craft Legal Attorneys at Law in
Chattanooga, leading her team on their collective mission to practice law differently and obtain
justice for their clients; and
WHEREAS, through her estimable service to her community and the legal profession,
Ms. Craft epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of a true Tennessean;
and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Tonya Craft for her
dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
