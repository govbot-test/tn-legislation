HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1097
By Warner
A RESOLUTION to recognize Sheriff Billy Lamb on the occasion
of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of public service; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Marshall County Sheriff Billy Lamb, a
longtime veteran of law enforcement who is taking his much-deserved retirement after forty-four
years of service; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Lamb began his career in September 1982 as the deputy sheriff of
Marshall County, a position he held for nine years; and
WHEREAS, he was then appointed to chief deputy, in which capacity he served for
twenty-three years, before he was ultimately elected sheriff; and
WHEREAS, during his twelve-year tenure as sheriff, Billy Lamb fulfilled his promise to
the voters of Marshall County that he was devoted to the protection and safety of the citizens of
his beloved communities; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Lamb is a 1978 graduate of Forrest School, where he met his
sweetheart, Pam Bryant, whom he later married; and
WHEREAS, together, the Lambs have two sons and two daughters-in-law, Jed, Jeremy,
Emily, and Alyssa, and two grandchildren, Rivy and Ridge; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Lamb plans to spend his retirement with his grandchildren and also
hunting–a hobby he has long enjoyed; and
WHEREAS, Billy Lamb exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and

WHEREAS, throughout his illustrious tenure, Sheriff Lamb has distinguished himself as
a public-spirited citizen of the highest order, winning the respect and admiration of his
colleagues and the people he has so ably served; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor Marshall County Sheriff Billy Lamb for his meritorious
service in law enforcement, commend him for his countless contributions and many good works
in service of the citizens of this State, and extend to him our best wishes for much continued
success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
