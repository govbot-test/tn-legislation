HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1086
By Hale
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Tennessee Highway
Patrol officers and Sergeants at Arms who
served at the State Capitol and Cordell Hull
Building during the 114th General Assembly.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those dedicated public servants who safeguard the lives and property of our citizens and ensure
the orderly conduct of government in the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Highway Patrol (THP) officers assigned to the State Capitol
complex and the Sergeants at Arms of the Senate and House of Representatives faithfully
perform essential duties that allow the legislative branch to carry out its constitutional
responsibilities in a safe and secure environment; and
WHEREAS, these men and women maintain a constant and vigilant presence in the
State Capitol and the Cordell Hull Building, providing security for legislators, staff, and visitors
while preserving the decorum and dignity that characterize the proceedings of this General
Assembly; and
WHEREAS, through their professionalism, courtesy, and steadfast dedication to duty,
these THP officers and Sergeants at Arms ensure that the business of the people may be
conducted with efficiency, order, and respect for the democratic process; and
WHEREAS, throughout the sessions of the 114th General Assembly, the Tennessee
Highway Patrol officers and Sergeants at Arms serving the Capitol complex have demonstrated
the highest standards of public service, and they are most deserving of our sincere appreciation
and commendation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
HJR1086
014923
- 1 -
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Tennessee Highway Patrol officers and
Sergeants at Arms who served at the State Capitol and the Cordell Hull Building during the
114th General Assembly and extend to them our gratitude for their professionalism, vigilance,
and dedicated service to the State of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
