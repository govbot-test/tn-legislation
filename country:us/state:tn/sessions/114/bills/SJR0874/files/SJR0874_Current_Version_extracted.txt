SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 874
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Bo Carey upon being
named the NFIB's Small Business Champion
Award winner.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
salute those citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as
community leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable person is Bo Carey, who was named the Small
Business Champion Award winner by the National Federation of Independent Business (NFIB);
and
WHEREAS, the Small Business Champion Award honors NFIB members who go above
and beyond to help the association promote and protect the right of its members to own,
operate, and grow their businesses; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Carey currently serves the Loudon community with acumen and alacrity
as a fourth-generation owner and president of Greer's Home Furnishings and as owner of The
Shoppes on the Square, an outlet shop in the downtown district; and
WHEREAS, a leader in his profession, Bo Carey has served the NFIB as a member
since 1992 and NFIB Tennessee PAC as vice chair; he has enjoyed being a member of the
Tennessee Leadership Council for many years, advocating at NFIB's Small Business Days and
Washington, D.C., Fly-ins, and attending or hosting Guardian of Small Business Award
presentations to Tennessee legislators; and
WHEREAS, additionally, Mr. Carey operates Bo Carey History Tours, which includes
several important tours of Old Loudon. He is president of Loudon ALIVE 2023-2026; a fiddler
and vocalist for The Early Morning String Dusters, a Knoxville bluegrass and comedy band in its
forty-eighth year; and vice president of Loudon Legacy Foundation; and
SJR0874
014110
- 1 -
WHEREAS, throughout his fruitful career, Bo Carey has been an outstanding advocate
for small businesses and issues at the State and federal levels, working assiduously to connect
with elected officials and provide input on key issues and policies that impact small businesses;
and
WHEREAS, his professional accomplishments aside, Mr. Carey is most grateful for the
love and support of his wife, Lisette, and his daughter, Rachel; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Carey epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of a
true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Bo Carey for his many
contributions to the good people of Tennessee, his unwavering commitment to others, and his
astute leadership in Loudon; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and commend Bo Carey on being
named the Small Business Champion Award winner by the National Federation of Independent
Business and extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
