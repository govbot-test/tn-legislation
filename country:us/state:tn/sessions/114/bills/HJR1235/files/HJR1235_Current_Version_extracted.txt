HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1235
By McKenzie
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Beverly Renée Holland
on the occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those estimable Tennesseans who have dedicated themselves to improving
the quality of life for their fellow citizens during their years of industry; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Beverly Renée Holland, the founder and operator of
God's Creative Enrichment Center in Knoxville, who will soon take her much deserved
retirement; and
WHEREAS, an exemplary childcare professional, Mrs. Holland takes great pride in the
Center's twenty-four-year legacy of service and commitment to enriching the lives of children
and families within the community; and
WHEREAS, Beverly Holland holds a child development associate (CDA) credential from
Roane State Community College, further demonstrating her expertise and commitment to early
childhood education; and
WHEREAS, her professional and civic involvement includes service on the Tennessee
State Board of Reviews, reflecting her dedication to leadership and community impact; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to honors and accolades, Mrs. Holland has received the
prestigious Martin Luther King Jr. Business of the Year Award, recognizing her outstanding
contributions and service; and
WHEREAS, she is a devoted member of Community Evangelistic Church in Knoxville,
where she is a faithful member of the Deacon Board, and maintains strong ties to her alma
mater, Austin-East High School; and
HJR1235
016652
- 1 -
WHEREAS, devoted to her family, Beverly Renée Holland enjoys the love and support
of her husband of thirty-five years, Charles Anthony Holland, Sr., and their two biological
children, their blended family of six, and their thirteen treasured grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Beverly Holland exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and
community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout her estimable career, Mrs. Holland has demonstrated the utmost
professionalism, ability, and integrity; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we extend to Beverly Renée Holland our heartfelt wishes for a happy and
fulfilling retirement and every continued success in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
