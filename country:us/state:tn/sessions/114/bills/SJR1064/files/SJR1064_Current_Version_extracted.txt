SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1064
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Dr. Alan Corley upon his
receipt of the 2026 Good Scout Award.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Dr. Alan Corley, who is being honored with
the 2026 Good Scout Award by Scouting America, Sequoyah Council, for his outstanding
leadership in Greeneville and his longstanding support and promotion of Scouting and youth
development; and
WHEREAS, the award is presented by local councils to community leaders who
exemplify the values of the Scout Oath and Law in their daily lives; it recognizes individuals for
outstanding community service, leadership, and integrity, in addition to Scouting involvement;
and
WHEREAS, in 1986, Dr. Corley organized the regional Scout Conservation Camporee,
which brought over 650 Scouts together to complete conservation projects for state and national
parks; and
WHEREAS, he also made a generous donation that made possible a computerized
racetrack, dubbed the "Alan Corley Speedway" in his honor, where Greene County Cub Scouts
can race their Pinewood Derby cars; and
WHEREAS, through his estimable service to Greeneville, Dr. Alan Corley epitomizes the
spirit of leadership, generosity, and commitment that are characteristic of a true Tennessean;
and

WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Dr. Corley for his
dedication to his community, the State of Tennessee, and the ideals of Scouting; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Dr. Alan Corley upon his
receipt of the 2026 Good Scout Award, thank him for his meritorious service to the city of
Greeneville and this State, and extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
