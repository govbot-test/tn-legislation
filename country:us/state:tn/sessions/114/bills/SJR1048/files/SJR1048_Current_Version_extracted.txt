SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1048
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Carmon Thomas Hooper
III of Brownsville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Carmon Thomas Hooper III; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Hooper was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hooper was born on January 8, 1931, the first of four sons of Carmon
Thomas Hooper, Jr., and Annie Mann Hooper; and
WHEREAS, he attended Haywood County schools before completing his secondary
education at Kentucky Military Institute in Lyndon, Kentucky; in 1953, he graduated from the
University of Alabama, where he was a member of Delta Kappa Epsilon; and
WHEREAS, following his graduation, Tommy Hooper served his country with honor in
the United States Air Force, attaining the rank of First Lieutenant and flying the F-86D
interceptor while stationed in Chitose, Japan, during the early years of the Cold War; and
WHEREAS, after completing his military service, Mr. Hooper attended Vanderbilt
University School of Law on the G.I. Bill, graduating in 1959, and was admitted to the
Tennessee Bar that same year; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Hooper began his legal career with a Brownsville firm founded by
Larry Morgan and Alex Gray and, following their deaths in the 1960s, maintained a solo practice
for decades, serving generations of Haywood County residents as a trusted and respected
"country lawyer"; and

WHEREAS, a dedicated public servant, Mr. Hooper served six years as a member of the
Tennessee House of Representatives, fifteen years on the Haywood County Commission, and
eighteen years as County Attorney; and
WHEREAS, he was also a leader in the business and civic life of his community, co-
founding First Federal Savings and Loan in 1965 and later serving as Chairman of the Board of
First State Bank, and he contributed to local development through residential projects in
Brownsville; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hooper was a man of deep faith and enduring commitment to his
community, a longtime member of the Rotary Club of Brownsville and a Paul Harris Fellow, and
he gave generously of his time as an Eagle Scout and Scoutmaster of Troop 70; and
WHEREAS, in 1962, he married the love of his life, Martha Graham Myatt, and together
they shared sixty-three years of marriage and were blessed with five children; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Hooper will be remembered for his daily devotional, his steady
habits, his love of family, and his enduring presence in the life of Brownsville, where he
practiced law for sixty-seven years and became a familiar and respected figure on the Court
Square; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his beloved wife, Martha Myatt Hooper, Mr. Hooper is survived
by his children, Ashley (Mark) McClure, Allyson (Pat) Proctor, Anne Sanford French, Tom
(Collins) Hooper, and Sandy Hooper; his grandchildren, Mary Sanford (Henry) Shepherd, Myatt
McClure, McKay Proctor, Anna Proctor, Carmon Proctor, Grace Elizabeth Hawks, Holland
Hawks, Graham Hawks, Eads Hooper, Maggie Hooper, and Myatt Hooper; one great-
granddaughter, Martha Mae Shepherd; and his brothers, David (Eleanor) Hooper, Sr., and Jeff
(Marjorie) Hooper; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his parents and his brother, Jere Hooper; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hooper leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and

WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Carmon Thomas Hooper
III, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Hooper.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
