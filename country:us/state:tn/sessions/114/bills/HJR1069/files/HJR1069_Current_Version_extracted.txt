HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1069
By Moon
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Ronald S. Coffin for his
contributions to the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Blount County native Ronald S. Coffin, a
highly respected member of his community who has dedicated himself to improving the quality
of life for his fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, born on August 23, 1947, Mr. Coffin grew up in Maryville and attended W.J.
Hale School; on September 3, 1963, six days after the "March on Washington," led by Dr.
Martin Luther King, Jr., he became one of the first students to integrate Maryville High School;
and
WHEREAS, a Vietnam War veteran, he served his country valiantly as a member of the
United States Army from March 1968 to November 1971; his overseas tours also included
South Korea and Taiwan; and
WHEREAS, Ronald Coffin was duly recognized for his military service with the Army
Commendation Medal, National Defense Medal, Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal, Vietnam
Service Medal, Vietnam Campaign Medal, and Vietnam Cross of Gallantry with Palm; and
WHEREAS, in the years after his service, Mr. Coffin helped establish a post-traumatic
stress disorder (PTSD) group for veterans in the Maryville area, allowing these individuals to
meet with a Veterans Affairs therapist in Maryville instead of having to drive to Knoxville for
assistance; and

WHEREAS, through his efforts to establish this local PTSD group, he has greatly
improved the lives of his fellow veterans and has had a lasting impact on his community; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Coffin holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology with a minor in
Black Studies from the University of Tennessee, as well as a master's degree in social work; he
retired from his post of federal administrator after thirty-four years of dedicated service; and
WHEREAS, a man of abiding faith, Mr. Coffin is a member of St. Paul A.M.E. Zion
Church in Maryville, where he is active in several church ministries; and
WHEREAS, Ronald Coffin is also wholly devoted to his family; he enjoys the love and
support of his wife, Glenda Smith Coffin, and their five children, eighteen grandchildren, and
eight great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Coffin epitomizes the spirit and commitment to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly acknowledge
and applaud Ronald Coffin for his dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Ronald S. Coffin of Maryville for his
compassionate, devoted service to his fellow citizens, extending our best wishes for his every
future success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
