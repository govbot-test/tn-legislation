SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 890
By Reeves
A RESOLUTION to honor T. Wayne Williams on being elected
vice president of the National FFA Southern
Region.
WHEREAS, organized in 1928 to promote leadership, personal growth, and career
success among high school agriculture students, FFA continues to play an important role in
ensuring the growth and prosperity of agriculture in Tennessee and the nation by expertly
molding our future leaders; and
WHEREAS, we take great pleasure in recognizing those outstanding young citizens who
have distinguished themselves as emerging leaders in Tennessee's agriculture industry; and
WHEREAS, T. Wayne Williams of the Woodbury FFA Chapter was elected vice
president of the National FFA Southern Region for 2025-2026 during the ninety-eighth National
FFA Convention and Expo in Indianapolis, Indiana, making him the fifteenth National FFA
Officer from Tennessee in the organization's ninety-eight-year history; and
WHEREAS, the son of Martin and Kim Williams, T. Wayne Williams is a 2025 graduate
of Cannon County High School and a current agricultural communications student at Tennessee
Technological University; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Williams's journey as a member of FFA is one of passion,
perseverance, and purpose, and he has commenced his term as vice president with two words:
"Just One," reflecting his objective as vice president to make a positive difference in the life of at
least one member and be remembered for spreading kindness and joy; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Williams hails from a fourth-generation beef cattle operation farm in
Woodbury; his father and grandfather encouraged him to enroll in an agriculture class his
freshman year of high school, and the teacher of the class, Emily Haley, ultimately ignited his
passion for and career in FFA; and

WHEREAS, most recently, T. Wayne Williams served as the 2024-2025 Tennessee
State FFA President, leading thousands of student members across the State and representing
Tennessee FFA at various events; and
WHEREAS, an outstanding young man, Mr. Williams has proven himself a devoted and
worthy leader in FFA, and he is most deserving of our approbation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and congratulate T. Wayne
Williams on being elected vice president of the National FFA Southern Region, salute his many
contributions and achievements in agriculture, and extend to him our best wishes for success
during his term as a national FFA officer.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
