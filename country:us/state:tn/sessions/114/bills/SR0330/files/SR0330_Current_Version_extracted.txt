SENATE RESOLUTION 330
By White
A RESOLUTION to commend Griffin Hancock for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this legislative body is pleased to recognize certain
persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the
legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Griffin Hancock is one such individual, who has distinguished himself in his
role as a legislative intern for Senator Dawn White; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hancock's work, his exceptionally personable demeanor, and his
professionalism have resulted in innumerable benefits for the legislators he assisted, the
constituents of Senate District 13, and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, Griffin Hancock was born in Sikeston, Missouri, to Larry Hancock and
Chelsey Lowe; he was raised with the support of his grandparents, Don and Sue Moore, and his
stepfather, the late Christopher Lowe; a resident of Missouri until graduating from high school,
he currently resides in Murfreesboro surrounded by family, including his sister, Caroline Brown;
brother-in-law, Kyle Brown; and nephews, Rhett Alan and Easton Ray; and
WHEREAS, a student at Freed-Hardeman University, where he is highly regarded by his
peers, Mr. Hancock has achieved academic success while pursuing a degree in law and politics
and a minor in ministry; and
WHEREAS, Griffin Hancock is a compassionate volunteer with a background in church
community and camp service; he has served as a counselor and head counselor at Mid-South
Youth Camp in Henderson, where he helped campers grow in friendship and faith; and

WHEREAS, while working for the youth camp, Mr. Hancock met the love of his life, Mary
Grace Bolton, whom he will marry on October 10, 2026; they hope to build a family by laying a
foundation built upon service to their community, the Lord, and each other; and
WHEREAS, Griffin Hancock has brought leadership and organization to his time with the
legislature by assisting with the Senate Education Committee and collecting and organizing
weekly calendars; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Mr. Hancock brought his many abilities
and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth
General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, Griffin Hancock finds that the supportive office and kindness of all members
and staff of the Tennessee General Assembly have provided him with an experience that has
been beneficial beyond comprehension; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hancock has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight and
rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby commend Griffin
Hancock for outstanding service as a legislative intern for Senator Dawn White and extend to
him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
