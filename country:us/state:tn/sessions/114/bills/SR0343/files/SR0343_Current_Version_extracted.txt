SENATE RESOLUTION 343
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor University of Memphis basketball
legend Nathaniel Root.
WHEREAS, on January 14, 2026, the University of Memphis (UM) welcomed home one
of the most beloved and inspiring players in the history of Tigers' men's basketball, Nathaniel
Root; and
WHEREAS, UM prevailed over Temple on that special day, and McNairy County native
Nathaniel Root was honored by the university, a gesture that symbolized the unbreakable bond
between the men's basketball program and one of its own; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Root was a fan favorite during his playing days, and when he was
introduced in the FedEx Forum, the crowd erupted, with many fans chanting his name; and
WHEREAS, this touching moment served as a reminder that the impact of college
athletics is not measured solely by points scored but also by character shown and inspiration
left behind as a legacy; and
WHEREAS, this special night also reflected Mr. Root's lasting relationship with the UM
community and his deep connection with the program; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Root graduated from high school without a single athletic scholarship
offer; he enrolled at the University of Memphis in the fall of 1999 to pursue his lifelong dream of
playing college basketball; and
WHEREAS, he walked on at UM, pursuing a roster spot with grit and persistence and
finally earning his place on the team through relentless work and sacrifice; and
WHEREAS, Nathaniel Root's unrelenting effort and huge heart not only earned him the
respect of Coach John Calipari and his teammates in the locker room but also the admiration of
the Memphis faithful over time; and

WHEREAS, now an author, motivational speaker, and entrepreneur, Mr. Root continues
to give back to the community through speaking engagements across the region, always
encouraging students and young athletes to pursue their dreams with courage and resilience;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Root recently authored Root: A Memphis Hoops Dream, which features
a foreword by Coach Calipari; the book chronicles his unlikely path from small-town McNairy
County to the bright lights of Memphis and a major college basketball program; and
WHEREAS, resonating with athletes, coaches, and readers far beyond Memphis,
Nathaniel Root's memoir captures behind-the-scenes moments from his playing career and
recounts the emotional highs and lows that shaped him both on and off the court; and
WHEREAS, Nathaniel Root may no longer wear the Tiger Blue, but his legacy, heart,
and story continue to echo throughout the UM men's basketball program, inspiring the next
generation to dream big and work hard; and
WHEREAS, an icon whose past, present, and future is deeply woven into the fabric of
Tiger basketball, Mr. Root is most worthy of our approbation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor Nathaniel Root,
author, motivational speaker, entrepreneur, and University of Memphis basketball legend, and
extend to him our best wishes for much continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
