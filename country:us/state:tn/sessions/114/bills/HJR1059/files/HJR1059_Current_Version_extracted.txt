HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1059
By Jones J
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the eightieth anniversary of
Philippine-American Friendship Day.
WHEREAS, this year marks the eightieth anniversary of the United States-Philippines
diplomatic relationship, beginning in 1946 with the formal recognition of the Philippines'
independence through the Treaty of Manila; and
WHEREAS, that recognition of independence came after World War II, when the
Philippines and the United States worked together to expel the Japanese occupation of the
island through joint efforts by American forces, the Philippine Commonwealth Army, the
Philippine Scouts, the Philippine Constabulary, and everyday citizens fighting back; and
WHEREAS, beginning in 1946, the two sovereign nations began a new chapter built on
shared ideals of freedom, dignity, and democracy; and
WHEREAS, this eightieth anniversary occasion will include year-long programming
across both countries to celebrate and showcase the unwavering ties between the Philippines
and the United States as friends, partners, and allies; and
WHEREAS, our two nations have grown this partnership into one of the most enduring in
the modern world, whose foundation is rooted in opportunity, growth, and mutual prosperity; and
WHEREAS, the Philippines is the United States' second oldest treaty ally in Asia and the
longest, oldest, and strongest United States security alliance in the Indo-Pacific region; and
WHEREAS, the United States and the Philippines routinely work together to advance
strategic initiatives related to security, trade, energy, sciences, public health, regional
cooperation, and technological innovation; and
WHEREAS, through challenge and change, the United States and the Philippines have
stood together, bound by trust and strengthened through sacrifice; and

WHEREAS, Philippine-American Friendship Day is a joyous occasion that celebrates the
strong bond and enduring camaraderie between the Philippines and the United States; and
WHEREAS, at first, July 4, 1946, was designated as the Philippines Independence Day,
but later, then-President Diosdado Macapagal moved the Philippines Independence Day
celebration to June 12, renaming July 4 as the Philippine Republic Day, or Philippine-American
Friendship Day; and
WHEREAS, our partnership across the Pacific showcases the importance of collective
effort, cooperation, and helping one another; and
WHEREAS, Philippine-American Friendship Day provides an opportunity to remember
the sacrifices and solidarity that have shaped the diplomatic relationship between the two
nations and to look forward to continued friendship, peace, steadfast partnership, common
purpose, and shared prosperity; and
WHEREAS, the historic people-to-people ties are the bedrock of the United States-
Philippines relationship, with around 375,000 Americans calling the Philippines home, while
more than 4.6 million Filipinos and Filipino-Americans live, work, and study across the United
States; and
WHEREAS, these individuals compose living bridges connecting our communities to
each other and have been sustained and strengthened across generations; and
WHEREAS, the Filipino population in Tennessee exceeds 30,000, making it the third-
largest Asian ethnic group in the State, and this vibrant community enriches the civic, cultural,
and economic life of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commemorate the eightieth anniversary of Philippine-American
Friendship Day, recognize the enduring partnership between the Republic of the Philippines and
