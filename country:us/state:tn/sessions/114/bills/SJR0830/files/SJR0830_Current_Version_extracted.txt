SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 830
By Lamar
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Alpha Kappa Alpha Day
at the Capitol.
WHEREAS, members of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc., from across the State of
Tennessee convene for Alpha Kappa Alpha Day at the Capitol on February 18, 2026, bringing
together sorority members to engage in focused legislative advocacy and civic participation; and
WHEREAS, Alpha Kappa Alpha Day at the Capitol includes work sessions beginning at
8:30 a.m. in the Cordell Hull Building in Nashville, providing members the opportunity to engage
with public policy matters at the state level; and
WHEREAS, the work sessions and discussions focus on maternal health, Black youth
mental health, suicide prevention, and the Alpha Kappa Alpha legislative agenda, reflecting
issues of significant importance to communities across Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, the legislative agenda for the 2026 session includes advocacy for federal
and state funding for suicide prevention initiatives, maternal access to mental and physical
health treatment, and voter education; and
WHEREAS, Alpha Kappa Alpha Day at the Capitol serves as the primary vehicle
through which the sorority demonstrates its membership strength and provides members with
an opportunity to engage elected officials at the state level on matters of public concern; and
WHEREAS, the 2026 Alpha Kappa Alpha Day at the Capitol is conducted under the
theme "Transforming Midterms and Advocating for Justice," aligning the day's activities with the
sorority's advocacy priorities; and
WHEREAS, the event is organized under the leadership of Dr. Tracey Morant Adams,
South Eastern Regional Director; Leah Wiggins, International Connection and Social Action
Committee, South Eastern Representative; and Lashell Vaughn, Tennessee Connection and

Social Action Chair, whose guidance and coordination have been instrumental in organizing this
event; and
WHEREAS, this event marks the fourth in-person Alpha Kappa Alpha Day at the Capitol
in the South Eastern Region under the sorority's international theme, "Soaring to Greater
Heights of Service and Sisterhood," and introduced by International President Danette Anthony
Reed; and
WHEREAS, the members of Alpha Kappa Alpha are wholly committed to the noble
precepts of public service that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and
they should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commend the members of Alpha Kappa Alpha
Sorority, Inc., for their exemplary public service and welcome the organization's members to
Nashville on February 18, 2026, as they celebrate and commemorate Alpha Kappa Alpha Day
at the Capitol.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
