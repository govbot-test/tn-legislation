SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 590
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to propose an amendment to Article XI, Section
5 of the Constitution of Tennessee, to allow for
bingo events to benefit certain organizations.
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that a majority of all the members of each house
concurring, as shown by the yeas and nays entered on their journals, that it is proposed that
Article XI, Section 5 of the Constitution of the State of Tennessee be amended by adding the
following language at the end of the section:
This Section does not prohibit bingo games, as defined below, when conducted
by a tax-exempt 501(c)(3) or 501(c)(19) organization, as defined by the 2000 United
States Tax Code, to meet charitable, nonprofit, or community service purposes. Bingo
games authorized in this section shall be implemented and administered uniformly
throughout the state in such manner as the legislature, by general law, deems
appropriate.
"Bingo" means a specific game of chance in which participants use an electronic
or manual playing surface divided into horizontal and vertical spaces, each of which is
designated by a letter and a number, and prizes are awarded on the basis of the letters
and numbers on the playing surface conforming to a predetermined and preannounced
configuration of letters and numbers selected at random.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the foregoing amendment be referred to the One
Hundred Fifteenth General Assembly and that this resolution proposing such amendment be
published in compliance with Article XI, Section 3 of the Constitution of Tennessee by posting
