SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 652
By Hensley
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Wayne Brewer for his
contributions to the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Wayne Brewer, a highly respected member
of his community who has dedicated himself to improving the quality of life for his fellow citizens;
and
WHEREAS, for more than five years, Mr. Brewer has utilized his professional expertise
in carpentry and plumbing to provide essential home repairs for the disabled and those in
profound financial need; and
WHEREAS, Wayne Brewer operates entirely without charging for his service,
demonstrating his remarkable generosity by using his valuable skills to ensure that the most
vulnerable among us have safe, functional living environments; and
WHEREAS, a humble servant, Mr. Brewer never seeks recognition, praise, or monetary
compensation; his dedication, passion, and unwavering desire to help are driven by a deep love
for people, community, country, and God; and
WHEREAS, Wayne Brewer epitomizes the spirit and commitment to service and
community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly acknowledge
and applaud Mr. Brewer for his dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now,
therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Wayne Brewer for his
service to his community and the State of Tennessee and extend to him our best wishes for
much continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
