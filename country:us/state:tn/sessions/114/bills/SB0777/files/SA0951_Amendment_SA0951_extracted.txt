AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 777* House Bill No. 886
by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 2-2-116, is amended by adding the
following to the permanent registration record form:
(13) Political Party Affiliation (Select one):
{Name of majority party}
{Name of minority party}
Unaffiliated
Recognized minor party:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 2-2-116, is amended by designating
the existing language as subsection (a) and adding the following as a new subsection:
(b) The political party affiliation section of the permanent registration record must
include options to register with a statewide political party or recognized minor party, or to
be designated as unaffiliated. Statewide political parties must be listed in the same
order as required by § 2-5-208. The form shall include a blank line to designate a
recognized minor party affiliation. The form shall contain instructions that, if a voter fails
or refuses to make a designation or designates a political party that is not a statewide
political party or recognized minor party, the county election commission will enter on the
permanent registration records that the voter is unaffiliated.
SECTION 3. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 2-2-118, is amended by adding the
following as a new subsection:

(1) At the time of initial registration, each qualified voter may designate
on the registration application an affiliation with a statewide political party or
recognized minor party or may choose to be designated as "Unaffiliated." The
county election commission shall record the party affiliation or unaffiliated
designation as part of the voter's permanent registration record. If a voter fails or
refuses to make a designation or designates a political party that is not a
statewide political party or recognized minor party, then the county election
commission shall enter on the permanent registration records that the voter is
unaffiliated.
(2) A person who was registered to vote prior to February 1, 2027, may
designate the statewide political party or recognized minor party with which the
person intends to affiliate during the registration period under § 2-2-109. Any
change to such affiliation after the initial designation must be accomplished
pursuant to Section 7.
SECTION 4. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 2-1-104(a)(19), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(19) "Primary election" means an election held for a political party for the
purpose of allowing registered voters entitled to vote pursuant to § 2-7-115(b) to select a
nominee or nominees to appear on the general election ballot;
SECTION 5. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 2-1-104(a), is amended by adding
the following as a new subdivision:
( ) "Unaffiliated voter" means a voter who is not registered as being affiliated
with a statewide political party or recognized minor party;
SECTION 6. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 2-7-115(b), is amended by deleting
the subsection and substituting instead:
(b) A registered voter is entitled to vote in a primary election for offices for which
the voter is qualified to vote at the polling place where the voter is registered if:

(1) It is the first primary election the voter has voted in on or after
February 1, 2027, and at the time the voter seeks to vote, the voter declares
allegiance to the political party in whose primary the voter seeks to vote. The
county election commission shall record the voter's declaration or affirmation
made at the time the voter seeks to vote as part of the voter's permanent
registration record; or
(2) The voter has registered as being affiliated with a party pursuant to §
2-2-118.
SECTION 7. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 2, Chapter 13, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) An individual registering to vote on or after February 1, 2027, may register as
a member of a statewide political party or a recognized minor party.
(b) An individual who is registered to vote before February 1, 2027, or a voter
designated as unaffiliated may register as a member of a statewide political party or a
recognized minor party at the first primary election in which the individual participates on
or after February 1, 2027, or during the registration period under § 2-2-109. The party
with whom the individual registers must match the party for which the individual requests
a primary ballot.
(c) An individual who has registered with a party under subsection (a) or (b) may
change party affiliation during the registration period under § 2-2-109. Any such change
received after the last day of the registration period shall not be processed until after the
election.
SECTION 8. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 2-13-107, is amended by adding the

SECTION 9. If any provision of this act or its application to any person or circumstance
is held invalid, then the invalidity does not affect other provisions or applications of the act that
can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to that end, the provisions of
this act are severable.
SECTION 10. This act takes effect February 1, 2027, the public welfare requiring it.
