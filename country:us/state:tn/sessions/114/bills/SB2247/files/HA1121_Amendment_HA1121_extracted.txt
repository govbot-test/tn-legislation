AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 2247* House Bill No. 2532
by deleting Section 5 and substituting instead the following:
SECTION 5. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 49, Chapter 6, Part 35, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) The department shall annually collect and report to the general assembly, the
following information about the program for the school year designated in the report:
(1) The number of eligible students who applied for a scholarship,
disaggregated by the number of applications received from each county;
(2) The number of scholarship recipients from each county;
(3) The number of eligible students who applied for a scholarship,
disaggregated by the number who, at the time of submitting the scholarship
application, were enrolled in a private school, public charter school, or a school
managed and controlled by a local board of education;
(4) The number of eligible students who, at the time of submitting a
completed scholarship application, were not yet enrolled in school, but who were
applying for a scholarship for kindergarten enrollment;
(5) The number of scholarship recipients who were enrolled in a private
school in the school year immediately preceding the first school year for which
the recipient received a scholarship;
(6) Each private school that enrolls or enrolled a scholarship recipient
and the percentage of the private school's student enrollment for the reporting
year that received a scholarship pursuant to this part;

(7) The number of recipients disaggregated by:
(A) Annual household income in each of the following categories:
(i) Less than fifty thousand dollars ($50,000);
(ii) Between fifty thousand dollars ($50,000) and ninety
thousand dollars ($90,000), both inclusive;
(iii) Between ninety thousand one dollars ($90,001) and
one hundred twenty-five thousand dollars ($125,000), both
inclusive;
(iv) Between one hundred twenty-five thousand one
dollars ($125,001) and one hundred seventy-five thousand dollars
($175,000), both inclusive; and
(v) More than one hundred seventy-five thousand dollars
($175,000);
(B) The private schools at which the recipients used scholarship
funds in accordance with § 49-6-3505; and
(C) The LEAs and public charter schools in which recipients were
enrolled in the school year immediately preceding the first school year for
which the recipients received a scholarship, if applicable; and
(8) The total state expenditures for the program, including, but not limited
to:
(A) Third-party contracts;
(B) Administrative costs incurred by the department;
(C) Advertising and marketing expenses; and
(D) Scholarships awarded to recipients.
(b)
(1) Each private school that enrolls recipients shall annually report to the
department the number of student enrollment spaces that were available and that
