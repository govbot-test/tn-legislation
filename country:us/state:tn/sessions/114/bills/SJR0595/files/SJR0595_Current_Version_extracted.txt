SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 595
By Briggs
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory and legacy of Dr. John
Hayne McLeod, former Dean of Agriculture at
the University of Tennessee, and to commend
the University of Tennessee Institute of
Agriculture for naming its Food Science Building
in his honor.
WHEREAS, Dr. John Hayne McLeod was born on January 26, 1887, in Athens, Texas,
and devoted his life to advancing agriculture, education, and public service; and
WHEREAS, Dr. McLeod earned his Bachelor of Science degree in Animal Husbandry
from Texas A&M University in 1908 and his Master of Science degree from the University of
Wisconsin in 1912, establishing a strong academic foundation for a distinguished career in
agricultural leadership; and
WHEREAS, after serving as Head of the Extension Livestock Department at the
University of Arkansas, Dr. McLeod joined the University of Tennessee Agricultural Extension
Service in 1921 as a swine specialist, beginning a remarkable thirty-six-year career of service to
the people of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure at the University of Tennessee, Dr. McLeod held
numerous key leadership roles, including Vice Director, Acting Director, and Director of both the
Agricultural Experiment Station and the Agricultural Extension Service, and served with
distinction as Dean of Agriculture from 1948 to 1957; and
WHEREAS, Dr. McLeod played a pivotal role in establishing the cooperative Extension–
TVA Test Demonstration Program in the 1930s, and his expertise in agricultural economics and
program planning earned him widespread recognition and respect throughout the State and
beyond; and

WHEREAS, because of Dr. McLeod's extraordinary service and lasting impact, the
University of Tennessee bestowed upon him the honor of Dean Emeritus upon his retirement;
and
WHEREAS, following his retirement, Dr. McLeod continued his lifelong mission of
agricultural advancement by serving four years in the University of Tennessee's agricultural
program in India, where he successfully helped farmers increase rice production and improve
food security; and
WHEREAS, Dr. McLeod passed away on August 18, 1977, in Knoxville, leaving behind
a legacy of leadership, innovation, and unwavering commitment to the agricultural community of
this State; and
WHEREAS, in recognition of his enduring influence on the University of Tennessee and
agriculture across Tennessee, the University of Tennessee Institute of Agriculture has officially
named its Food Science Building the "John H. McLeod Food Science Building," ensuring that
future generations will remember and be inspired by his contributions; and
WHEREAS, Dr. McLeod's life exemplified the highest ideals of public service,
educational leadership, and dedication to improving the lives of Tennesseans through sound
agricultural policy, research, education, and outreach; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory and legacy of Dr. John Hayne
McLeod, commend his extraordinary service to the University of Tennessee and the State of
Tennessee, and congratulate the University of Tennessee Institute of Agriculture on naming its
Food Science Building in his honor.
