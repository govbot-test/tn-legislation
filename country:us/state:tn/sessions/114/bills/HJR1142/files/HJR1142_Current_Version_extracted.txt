HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1142
By Salinas
A RESOLUTION to honor native Tennessean Valerie June for her
many and diverse talents and her exemplary
career in music.
WHEREAS, music has long been an integral part of Tennessee's rich cultural heritage,
inspiring audiences around the world; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those gifted musical artists whose dedication to their craft brings distinction to themselves and
to this State; and
WHEREAS, one such exceptional artist is Valerie June Hockett, a self-described
ambassador for joy via her music; and
WHEREAS, Valerie June is a Grammy-nominated songwriter, singer, multi-
instrumentalist, recording artist, and author; her music is influenced by many genres, including
psychedelic folk, indie rock, Appalachian, bluegrass, country soul, symphonic pop, and blues,
and reflects both her Southern roots and East Coast experiences; and
WHEREAS, a native Tennessean, Valerie June Hockett was born in Jackson and grew
up in Humboldt, where she was exposed to gospel music at her local church and rhythm and
blues and soul music by her father, Emerson Hockett; and
WHEREAS, as a teenager, Valerie June first worked for her father at his construction
company and later served as a part-time promoter for gospel singers and pop artists such as
Prince, K-Ci & JoJo, and Bobby Womack; and
WHEREAS, at the age of nineteen, Valerie June began recording and performing as a
member of the duo Bella Sun; and
WHEREAS, relocating to Memphis in 2000, Valerie June began her career as a solo
artist, combining blues, gospel, and Appalachian folk music into a style that she describes as

"organic moonshine roots music"; she also learned to play guitar, banjo, and lap-steel guitar and
became associated with the Memphis-based Broken String Collective; and
WHEREAS, Valerie June self-released three albums before recording her debut album
as a signed artist, 2013's Pushin' Against a Stone, which was nominated in the Best New Artist
Debut category at the Blues Music Awards; the album's musical quality and popular appeal led
to her making an appearance on Austin City Limits; and
WHEREAS, following the success of her debut studio album, Valerie June released The
Order of Time in 2017 and The Moon and the Stars: Prescriptions for Dreamers in 2021, both of
which earned critical acclaim; and
WHEREAS, Valerie June's "Call Me a Fool" was nominated as the Grammy's Best
American Roots Song in 2021; and
WHEREAS, in 2025, Valerie June released her most recent album, Owls, Omens and
Oracles, which has been praised as an "album, full of melody, soul, and inspired songwriting";
and
WHEREAS, for more than a decade, Valerie June has played live shows worldwide,
notably opening for The Rolling Stones in London and sharing the stage and performing with
artists such as Willie Nelson, Neil Young, Jack Johnson, Brandi Carlile, Sturgill Simpson, Gary
Clark, Jr., John Prine, and Tyler Childers; and
WHEREAS, this multi-talented creator is also the author of three published books: Maps
for the Modern World, Somebody to Love: The Story of Valerie June's Sweet Little Baby
Banjolele, and Light Beams: A Workbook for Being Your Badass Self; and
WHEREAS, Valerie June's remarkable success as a singer, songwriter, musician, and
author stands as a testament to her perseverance, talent, and unwavering dedication to her art,
and she is most worthy of our approbation; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor native Tennessean Valerie June for her many and diverse
talents and her exemplary career in music, expressing the State of Tennessee's pride in her
accomplishments and wishing her all the best in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
