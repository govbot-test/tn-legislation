SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1075
By Bowling
A RESOLUTION to commend Kermina Wadeh for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Kermina Wadeh is one such individual, who has distinguished herself in her
role as a legislative intern for Senator Janice Bowling; Ms. Wadeh's work has resulted in
innumerable benefits for the legislators she assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at Vanderbilt University, where she is highly regarded by her
peers, Kermina Wadeh has achieved academic success while pursuing a Bachelor of Arts
degree in Sociology and Medicine, Health, and Society, with a concentration in Inequality,
Intersectionality, and Health Justice; and
WHEREAS, during her collegiate career, Ms. Wadeh has served the Department of
Sociology as an undergraduate teaching assistant, the LeaDores Certificate Program as an
inaugural cohort member, and the Vanderbilt Pre-Law Society as internship chair; her academic
work focuses on understanding structural inequities and health outcomes, particularly for
marginalized communities; and
WHEREAS, Kermina Wadeh is passionate about nonprofit work, and she is actively
involved in several initiatives that center community engagement, advocacy, and service;
through her experiences in both local and global organizations, she has supported efforts in
donor engagement, public health initiatives, and outreach programs; and
WHEREAS, prior to her time with the General Assembly, Ms. Wadeh devoted her time
and talents to a number of worthy organizations and institutions, including the Nashville Mayor's

Office, the Tennessee Justice Center, and the Tennessee Immigrant and Refugee Rights
Coalition; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies, Kermina Wadeh brought her many
abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, a resident of Nashville, Ms. Wadeh has shown her compassion, grace, and
careful attention to detail during her time with the legislature; in addition to assisting with daily
operations, she has proven to be kind and professional in every interaction with constituents,
and her initiative, dependability, and thoughtful approach to each task have made her a trusted
and highly valued member of Senator Bowling's office; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Ms. Wadeh has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Kermina Wadeh has performed her many duties with extraordinary insight
and rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Kermina Wadeh for
outstanding service as a legislative intern for Senator Janice Bowling and extend to her our best
wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
