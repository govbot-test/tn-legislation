Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 53, Chapter 1, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) The purpose of this section is to develop a voluntary program for the
promotion of meat and byproducts from livestock raised in this state.
(b) For purposes of this section, "livestock" means animals that are born, raised,
and harvested in this state for use as food for human utilization or consumption
including, but not limited to, cattle, sheep, swine, goats, and poultry.
(c) The department shall promulgate rules to implement and administer a
program for the promotion of meat and byproducts from livestock, which may be labeled
with an appropriate statement that indicates the meat or byproduct is a Tennessee meat
or byproduct. Rules must be promulgated in accordance with the Uniform Administrative
Procedures Act, compiled in title 4, chapter 5.
(d) The department may develop various labels for different types of livestock
and their byproducts.
(e) The department shall utilize existing staff and resources to implement and
administer the program.
(f) All labels for meat and byproducts developed by the department must be
compliant with the requirements of the United States department of agriculture's food
safety and inspection service.

Section 3:
SECTION 2. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
