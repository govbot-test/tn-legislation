HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1198
By Reneau
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Officer Kurtis Bacha
upon his receipt of the 2025 Patrol Officer of the
Year award.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal concerns to ensure the public safety and welfare, and the Volunteer State is especially
proud to be the home of so many valiant individuals who have performed above and beyond the
call of duty in service of public safety; and
WHEREAS, Officer Kurtis Bacha, an officer who serves his community selflessly, was
recently honored with the 2025 Patrol Officer of the Year Award by the Chattanooga Police
Department, in recognition of his proactive policing, which has significantly enhanced
community safety and trust; and
WHEREAS, in 2025, Officer Bacha's dedication resulted in over eighty-five arrests. His
work, which has focused on removing violent criminals and illegal weapons, led to the recovery
of nine firearms and the apprehension of multiple prohibited possessors, including quickly
capturing a multi-convicted criminal with a stolen firearm during a confrontation; his attention to
detail was key in the clearing and arrest of suspects involved in narcotics sales; and
WHEREAS, Officer Kurtis Bacha's contagious "can-do" attitude and exceptional ethos
make him an exemplary street officer, department leader, and model for all; and
WHEREAS, the selfless service and commitment provided by Officer Bacha have made
a lasting impact on the Chattanooga Police Department; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Officer Kurtis Bacha upon his receipt of the Patrol
Officer of the Year award from the Chattanooga Police Department.
HJR1198
016035
- 1 -
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
