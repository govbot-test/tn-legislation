SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 581
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of music legend Steve
Cropper.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Rock & Roll Hall of Fame guitarist Steve Cropper, who helped form the "Memphis
Soul" sound on Stax Records recordings throughout the 1960s and early 1970s; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Cropper was known for his spare, soulful playing and was the secret
ingredient to some of the greatest rock and soul songs ever recorded; he is ranked No. 45 on
Rolling Stone's list of the 250 Greatest Guitarists of All Time; and
WHEREAS, as the founding guitarist in Stax's house band during the Memphis label's
hit-making prime, Steve Cropper played on classics such as Sam & Dave's "Soul Man," Booker
T. and The M.G.'s "Green Onions," and Wilson Pickett's "In the Midnight Hour," the last two of
which he co-wrote; and
WHEREAS, he also helped pen Otis Redding's platinum-selling No. 1 hit, "(Sittin' On)
The Dock of the Bay," which is ranked No. 26 on Rolling Stone's list of the 500 Greatest Songs
of All Time; Mr. Cropper contributed the track's guitar chords and rhythm and finished work on
the song, including mixing, in the aftermath of Mr. Redding's death in December 1967; and
WHEREAS, Steve Cropper was born in Missouri and, as a child, moved to Memphis,
where he was exposed to gospel music; as a teenaged guitarist, he co-founded the band the
Mar-Keys, the group that recorded the classic instrumental "Last Night" for the newly founded
Stax label in 1961, making it one of the first tracks released by the label after it changed its
name from Satellite Records to Stax; and
WHEREAS, the Mar-Keys soon became the in-house band for Stax; in addition to
backing the artists who recorded at Stax's studio, members of the Mar-Keys themselves were

rebranded as Booker T. & The M.G.'s (fronted by Mar-Keys keyboardist Booker T. Jones) for
their own releases; and
WHEREAS, following his legendary, nearly decade-long stint at Stax, Steve Cropper
moved to Los Angeles, California, and became a go-to session musician, playing on tracks by
artists such as John Lennon (1975's Rock 'n' Roll), Ringo Starr, John Prine, Leon Russell, Rod
Stewart, and the Blues Brothers, with Mr. Cropper also appearing in the 1980 comedy based on
the Saturday Night Live duo, in which he revisited his work on Sam & Dave's "Soul Man"; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Cropper was inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in 1992 as a
member of Booker T. & The M.G.'s; he was also a two-time Grammy Award winner and
received the Grammys' lifetime achievement award in 2007; and
WHEREAS, Steve Cropper's impact has been far-reaching, and his legacy will live on
through his countless contributions to some of the most important songs of his or any
generation—songs that will forever remain part of the American music canon; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional music artist and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Steve Cropper, reflecting
on his indelible contributions to American music and in helping form the "Memphis Soul" sound.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
