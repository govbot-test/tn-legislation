HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1229
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to honor the City of Oak Hill's Fourth of July
celebration during America's
semiquincentennial.
WHEREAS, July 4, 2026, marks the 250th anniversary, or semiquincentennial, of the
adoption of the Declaration of Independence by the Second Continental Congress in
Philadelphia in 1776, signifying the founding of the United States of America; and
WHEREAS, throughout two and a half centuries, the American people have continued
striving to form "a more perfect Union," advancing the promise of liberty through sacrifice,
service, civic participation, and commitment to constitutional principles; and
WHEREAS, the State of Tennessee and its citizens have contributed immeasurably to
the strength, character, defense, and prosperity of the United States through their leadership,
innovation, military service, and dedication to community and country; and
WHEREAS, the citizens of Oak Hill will commemorate this landmark anniversary of our
country's founding with its thirty-seventh annual Fourth of July Neighborhood Celebration,
hosted by Robert Diehl; and
WHEREAS, the celebration will include a parade, a picnic, and various family fun and
games for the entire community; and
WHEREAS, the semiquincentennial offers a historic opportunity for reflection, gratitude,
education, and renewed commitment to the ideals upon which this nation was founded; and
WHEREAS, commemorative activities across this State can foster civic understanding,
strengthen community bonds, inspire patriotism, and encourage thoughtful engagement with our
shared history and future; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the City of Oak Hill's Fourth of July celebration during America's
semiquincentennial, extending our best wishes to host Robert Diehl and the good people of Oak
Hill for a festive, memorable event.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
