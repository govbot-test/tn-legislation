SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1187
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Isaiah Nathaniel
Peddicord of Harriman.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the untimely passing of Isaiah Nathaniel Peddicord; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Peddicord was an exemplary young man who worked assiduously to
improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Peddicord was born on May 28, 2002; he was a man of deep Christian
faith; and
WHEREAS, a jack of all trades, Isaiah Peddicord worked in construction, in food service,
at an Amazon warehouse, and in retail, and he farmed with his grandfather; and
WHEREAS, his favorite pastimes were reading, pontificating, challenging others in wit,
and exploring his path in life; and
WHEREAS, Isaiah Peddicord was deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Peddicord is survived by Brian (Nicole) Peddicord and Jenny (Michael)
Potter; his brother, Brian Hensley; his sisters, Kimberly Hensley, Jasmine Peddicord, and
Whitley Potter; Aidan and Kaitlyn Carroll; his grandparents, Michael (Mary) Peddicord, Lisa
Fritts-Peddicord, Wendy (Tom) Potter, and Tim (Debbie) Sweat; and
WHEREAS, he is further survived by his uncles, Mitchell Peddicord and Robert
(Jennifer) Harris; his aunts, Angel Hart, Heather (Mark) Carroll, Jessie (Shawn) Taylor, Allison
Sweat (Allison Karst); Olivia Carroll and nephew Elijah Carroll; his special friends, Larry Garrett
and Tyler Gosnell; and a host of cousins; and

WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by an aunt, Jennifer Peddicord, and a cousin,
Jerry Fritts; and
WHEREAS, Isaiah Peddicord leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
his public life, compassion and loyalty in his private life, and diligence and dedication in all his
chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Peddicord will be sorely missed by all those who were fortunate to know
and love him, and it is fitting that we should remember the effervescent life of this beloved
young man; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Isaiah Peddicord, reflecting
fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life
with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. Peddicord.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
