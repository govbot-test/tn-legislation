SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1104
By Bailey
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Dr. Chelsea T. Dowell
for her service to the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
acknowledge and applaud those community leaders who are wholly devoted to enhancing the
quality of life for their fellow citizens and improving and enriching the place they call home; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Dr. Chelsea T. Dowell, who has
distinguished herself as an exceptional business economist, educator, and community partner,
and is dedicated to expanding economic understanding and empowering the next generation of
leaders; and
WHEREAS, a native of Louisville, Kentucky, Dr. Dowell earned a bachelor's degree in
business economics and mathematics from Wofford College, a master's degree in economics
from the University of Kentucky, and a doctorate in business administration from Indiana
Wesleyan University; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Dowell's applied doctoral research earned institutional recognition, and
she was later named a Distinguished Alumnus of the National and Global DBA Program at
Indiana Wesleyan University; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Chelsea Dowell's scholarship has focused on economic education and
applied consulting, and she has published in peer-reviewed journals and presented at national
conferences, including the American Economic Association; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Dowell currently serves the College of Business at Tennessee
Technological University (TTU) with acumen and alacrity as an economics lecturer, in which
position she has earned the respect and admiration of her colleagues and students alike; and

directly with real-world challenges, partnering with nonprofits, public institutions, and community
organizations to deliver applied research, consulting, and financial literacy initiatives; and
WHEREAS, through experiential projects, professional credentialing opportunities, and
future-focused skill development in data literacy and artificial intelligence, Dr. Dowell prepares
her students to contribute meaningfully in complex, evolving economic environments by
emphasizing ethical leadership, service, and the belief that education is most powerful when it
uplifts others; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Dowell is a leader in her community and her profession; her astute
guidance reflects a sustained commitment to student success, institutional stewardship, and
public impact; and
WHEREAS, she serves as a member of the Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland's
Economic Scholars Program Board, advises the Omicron Delta Epsilon International Economics
Honor Society, and leads workshops for the Federal Reserve Economic Scholars Program; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Dowell is also the founder of the College of Business Journal for
Societal Impact, a platform that elevates student scholarship while strengthening university
community partnerships; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Dr. Chelsea Dowell is the recipient of
numerous honors that have duly recognized her excellence in teaching, innovation,
collaboration, and service; she was named Educator of the Year by the Tennessee Jump$tart
Coalition for Financial Literacy and received the Award for Excellence in Overall Performance
from the TTU College of Business, as well as several additional university-level awards for
outstanding teaching and community engagement; and
WHEREAS, a dedicated educator, researcher, and advisor, Dr. Chelsea Dowell is wholly
committed to building collaborative spaces where inquiry, service, and leadership converge,
