HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1546
By Faison
A RESOLUTION to recognize Trooper William Bennett on the
occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal concerns to ensure the public safety and welfare, and the Volunteer State is especially
proud to be the home of so many valiant individuals; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Trooper William Bennett of the Tennessee Highway
Patrol, who has rendered sterling service to the people of the State of Tennessee for thirty years
and will be taking his much-deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, a 1986 graduate of Mt. Juliet High School, Trooper Bennett is a United
States Army veteran who served with the 82nd Airborne Division at Fort Bragg during the mid-
1980s; and
WHEREAS, following his honorable discharge from active duty, he worked as a
commercial diver in the offshore oilfield industry while continuing his military service with the
Tennessee and Texas National Guard; and
WHEREAS, Trooper Bennett attended Middle Tennessee State University, where he
earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice with minors in Psychology and Political
Science; and
WHEREAS, Trooper William Bennett began his law enforcement career in 1997 with the
Wilson County Sheriff's Department as a patrol officer and was selected in 2002 to attend the
Tennessee Highway Patrol Academy; upon graduation from the Academy, he served as a road
trooper in Sumner and Wilson counties before transferring to the Capitol Protection Unit in
Davidson County; and
WHEREAS, Trooper Bennett, working with the Tennessee Highway Patrol, has been
indispensable in the fight for a safe and just society; during his tenure, he has received

numerous accolades for his dedicated service while becoming a friend to many members of the
Tennessee General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, on multiple occasions, he has run into harm's way to protect members and
staff of the Cordell Hull Building, including one notable instance in which he assisted in
disarming an individual who had pulled a firearm; and
WHEREAS, outside of protecting and serving his fellow citizens, Trooper William
Bennett commits his time to his family life with his beloved wife and two children at their home in
Lebanon; in addition, he has proudly owned and maintained his father's Tennessee Orange
1979 CJ5 Jeep; and
WHEREAS, Trooper Bennett epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are
characteristic of a true Tennessean, and his professionalism and dedication to serving his
community are evident to all who know him; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to recognize and applaud Trooper William Bennett for
his dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Trooper William Bennett on the occasion of his
retirement, thank him for his meritorious service, and extend to him our best wishes for every
future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
