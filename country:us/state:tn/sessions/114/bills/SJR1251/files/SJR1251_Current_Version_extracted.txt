SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1251
By Campbell
A RESOLUTION to honor Margot Café & Bar for twenty-five years
of operation.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
those exceptional establishments that not only offer unforgettable culinary experiences but also
cultivate community and enrich the lives of all who pass their thresholds; and
WHEREAS, one of East Nashville's most beloved restaurants, Margot Café & Bar, has
announced that it will close its doors on June 5, 2026, twenty-five years from the day of its
opening; and
WHEREAS, located at 1017 Woodland Street, Margot is a cozy, French-inspired
restaurant with southern influences featuring a menu that changes daily and focuses on locally
sourced products, seasonal ingredients, freshness, and simplicity; and
WHEREAS, Margot Café & Bar was founded by chef, owner, and namesake Margot
McCormack, who opened her restaurant in a former 1930s-era gas station in the historic Five
Points area in 2001, at a time when East Nashville was primarily a quiet, residential
neighborhood; and
WHEREAS, Ms. McCormack is a four-time James Beard Foundation Best Chef
Southeast nominee, a Nashville native, and a graduate of the University of Tennessee; she
began her career working at Nashville restaurants such as Faison's and F. Scott's Restaurant
and Jazz Bar before attending the Culinary Institute of America in New York, New York. After
several years of cooking in the city, she returned home in 1995; and
WHEREAS, many successful chefs spent time at Margot early in their careers, including
now-executive chef Hadley Long and City House's Tandy Wilson; and

WHEREAS, Margot Café & Bar has been a culinary staple of East Nashville, a
pioneering venture in the neighborhood that helped inspire Nashville's evolution as a premier
"foodie" destination; and
WHEREAS, the outstanding success and stellar reputation that Margot has attained
would not have been possible without the hard work and dedication of Margot McCormack; her
wife, Heather Parsons; their son, Jacob; and countless employees, both past and present; and
WHEREAS, it is our privilege to pay homage to this singular culinary establishment on
this bittersweet occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Margot McCormack and
the chefs and employees, past and present, of Margot Café & Bar on twenty-five years of
operation, extending our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
