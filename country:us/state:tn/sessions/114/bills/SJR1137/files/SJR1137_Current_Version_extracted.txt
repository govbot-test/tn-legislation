SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1137
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Chief Freddie Joe
Devotie of Jefferson City.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
recognize those exemplary citizens whose lives of service and sacrifice have profoundly
impacted their communities and the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Freddie Joe Devotie, born on October 16, 1944, was one such individual,
whose distinguished career in the fire service spanned thirty-eight years, a tenure marked by
unwavering dedication, leadership, and a deep commitment to the protection of life and
property; and
WHEREAS, in 1986, Chief Devotie reorganized and founded the Lakeway Central
Volunteer Fire Department; he faithfully served the Lakeway Central Volunteer Fire Department,
including more than three decades as Fire Chief, during which time he helped shape the
department into a respected and capable organization that has served Jefferson County and the
surrounding region with honor; and
WHEREAS, throughout his tenure, Chief Devotie exemplified the highest standards of
the fire service, mentoring countless firefighters, instilling discipline, and fostering a culture of
professionalism, integrity, and brotherhood that will endure for generations; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his service as a firefighter, Chief Devotie was the devoted
pastor of House of Prayer in Morristown for twenty-five years, faithfully ministering to his
congregation and serving as a spiritual leader within the community; and
WHEREAS, known as a man of conviction, strong character, and unwavering faith, Chief
Devotie led both in word and deed, always speaking truth and guiding others with wisdom and
purpose; and

WHEREAS, Joe Devotie put God first in everything he did, and his primary goal in death
was to kiss and wash the feet of Jesus; and
WHEREAS, his legacy is not only reflected in the department he helped build but in the
lives of those he mentored, the community he served, and the family he loved; and
WHEREAS, Joe Devotie is survived by his children, Tim (Kim) Devotie, Steve (Pam)
Devotie, Lisa Devotie, and Josh (Misty) Devotie; grandchildren, Rusty, T.J., Tanner (Chalea), Eli
(Lauren), Trinity (Jonathan), Derrick (Amber), Jacob, Layla, and Weston; great-grandchildren,
Samuel, Trenton, Ella, Emmy, and Walker; and a host of nieces, nephews, and other special
family and friends; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his wife, Judy Kay Devotie; granddaughter,
Taylor Devotie; parents, George and Ethel Devotie; and siblings, James, Charlie, Floyd, Paul,
David, Scottie "Ed," Ruby, Rosie, Ruth, Gladys, Dolly, Lula-Belle, Dorothy, and two infant
siblings; and
WHEREAS, Chief Devotie's passing leaves a profound void in the Jefferson County
community, the fire service, and the State of Tennessee, but his legacy of service, sacrifice, and
leadership will continue to inspire all who follow in his footsteps; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Chief Freddie Joe Devotie
for his extraordinary life of service and extend our deepest sympathy to his family, friends, and
the members of the Lakeway Central Volunteer Fire Department.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
