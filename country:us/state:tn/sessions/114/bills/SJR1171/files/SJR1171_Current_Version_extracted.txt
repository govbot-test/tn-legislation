SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1171
By Bailey
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of J.T. Bennett, Jr.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of J.T. Bennett, Jr.; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bennett was a highly respected member of his community and a
consummate professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow
citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Oak Ridge on October 21, 1945, Mr. Bennett was the son of J.T.
Bennett, Sr., and Pearl Wilhite Bennett and a 1964 graduate of Cookeville Central High School;
and
WHEREAS, J.T. Bennett honorably served his country as a member of the United States
Army; he completed three years of active duty service, including service during the Vietnam
War, fulfilling his role as an electronics equipment repairman with honor and distinction; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bennett was duly recognized for his service and earned several
commendations, including the National Defense Service Medal, the Vietnam Service Medal, the
Vietnam Campaign Medal with 60 Device, and the Army Commendation Medal; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bennett's dedication to civic engagement and community service was
reflected in his lifetime memberships with the Veterans of Foreign Wars and the American
Legion, both enduring expressions of his bond with his fellow servicemen; and
WHEREAS, following his military service, J.T. Bennett embarked on a notable career
with the Cookeville Police Department, beginning in July 1968; he served his community as a
law enforcement officer with unwavering devotion for nearly forty years, culminating in his
service as captain. He took his much-deserved retirement in 2007; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Bennett continued his service to others by working as a funeral
assistant at Hooper, Huddleston & Horner Funeral Home for several years, in which role he
extended comfort and offered support to families during their most difficult times; and
WHEREAS, J.T. Bennett was also devoted to his family and always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; he will be sadly missed but fondly remembered
by his wife of sixty years, Ann Stockton Bennett, whom he married on April 2, 1966; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bennett leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pause to remember the bountiful life of this
exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of J.T. Bennett, Jr., reflecting
fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life
with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Bennett.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
