HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1099
By Jones J
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Pam Weston for her
lifetime of devoted service to the citizens of
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that the members of this General Assembly should
acknowledge and applaud those community leaders who are wholly devoted to enhancing the
quality of life for their fellow citizens and improving and enriching the place they all call home;
and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Pam Weston of Sweetwater, who has
distinguished herself as a citizen of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Weston has lived a full, rich life devoted to serving others through
various causes she believes in; she has been so much to so many—a beloved teacher and
mentor; a justice-minded political, arts, environmental, and healthcare advocate; and a dear
friend; and
WHEREAS, a 1974 graduate of Sweetwater High School, Ms. Weston attended
Hiwassee College and earned a Bachelor of Science degree and Master of Science degree in
Mathematics from East Tennessee State University (ETSU); she was inducted into Phi Kappa
Phi honor society as an undergraduate in the top five percent of her class; and
WHEREAS, Pam Weston has worked as a full-time contract faculty member at ETSU,
the University of West Georgia (UWG), Pellissippi State Community College, and Tennessee
Wesleyan University (TWU); during her distinguished tenure, she has reviewed texts, conducted
seminars, and coauthored a number of original research articles; and
WHEREAS, while at ETSU, she worked with the political science department to conduct
a case study on three Tennessee Superfund sites: Bumpas Cove, Red Ridge Landfill, and
Fowler Mine; this work was inspired by her having grown up near a Superfund site and

witnessing the resultant harm to her working class, rural community—an experience that helped
shape her advocacy work; and
WHEREAS, in support of the well-being of her fellow citizens, Pam Weston served as a
member of the Monroe Health Council, Tennessee Health Care Coalition, and District 9
Coalition for Insure Tennessee; a mentor with Tennessee Promise and New Leaders Council;
and treasurer of 3M Indivisible McMinn Meigs Monroe; and
WHEREAS, her deep love for the arts led her to coordinate art workshops and events,
leading to the opportunity for her to serve as the lead artist on a children's international art
exchange; she also served as membership coordinator and advisory board member for the
Artists Association of Monroe County and an advisory board member for the Marcella Center for
the Arts and Education; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Weston is passionate about governance and has worked for many
years to foster local connections and collaborations; she has held various leadership roles
within the Tennessee Democratic Party, Monroe County Democratic Women, Progressive Rural
Overhill Women for Democracy, and the Tennessee Federation of Democratic Women, in
addition to running for local office and supporting the campaigns of fellow Democrats; and
WHEREAS, Pam Weston has been duly honored for her many contributions as an
educator, receiving the Distinguished Faculty Service Award from UWG Disability Services and
being nominated at TWU for the Thompson Award for Teaching Excellence; and
WHEREAS, she has also been recognized in her community, earning the Hometown
Hero Award from the Tennessee Justice Center and the Health Care Hero Award from the
Tennessee Health Care Campaign and being named Woman of the Year by the Monroe County
Democratic Women's Club; and

WHEREAS, Pam Weston's devotion to the arts has been recognized by the Johnson
City Division of Parks and Recreation, with the Arts & Humanities Award, and the Kennedy
Center, with the National Award for Excellence in Leadership in the Arts; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Weston has built a legacy of kindness, compassion, and tireless
service, and her indelible contributions to her students, her community, and this State will
continue to inspire others for years to come; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Pam Weston for her lifetime of devoted service to
the citizens of Tennessee, extending our sincerest gratitude for her compassionate mentorship,
leadership, and advocacy.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
