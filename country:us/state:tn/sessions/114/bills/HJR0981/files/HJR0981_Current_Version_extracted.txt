HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 981
By Faison
A RESOLUTION to commend Representative Ron Travis for his
honorable and astute service to the people of
Tennessee as a member of the House of
Representatives.
WHEREAS, it is most appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should
honor those fellow legislators who have performed their duties with the utmost integrity and
whose efforts during their time in office have been dedicated to providing thoughtful and
exemplary service to their districts and to this great State; and
WHEREAS, Representative Ron Travis of Dayton is one such distinguished public
servant, who has served both his constituency and this legislative body with honor and ability;
and
WHEREAS, during his tenure, Representative Travis has been a highly influential figure
in the political, social, and civic life of Bledsoe, Rhea, Sequatchie, and Van Buren counties, and
he has served his constituents in numerous capacities, always working diligently at his elected
duties and expending the necessary effort to understand the opposing sides of complex issues;
and
WHEREAS, Ron Travis has distinguished himself as a public-spirited citizen of the
highest order and as an exceptional asset to the good people of the 31st House District, whom
he has served as a member of the 108th, 109th, 110th, 111th, and 112th, 113th, and 114th General
Assemblies; and
WHEREAS, throughout his service, Ron Travis has been prominent as a dedicated and
well-informed legislator who always votes his conscience; and

WHEREAS, born on November 3, 1954, Representative Travis is a graduate of
Tennessee Technological University; he is a member of the Methodist faith and is highly
regarded as a professional insurance agent, owner, and CEO; and
WHEREAS, an active and dynamic participant in the legislative process during his time
in the General Assembly, Representative Ron Travis has rendered sterling service as a member
of the House Agriculture and Natural Resources Committee, House Disaster Relief Committee
of the 1st Extraordinary Session of the 114th General Assembly, House Health Committee,
House Judiciary Committee, House Transportation Committee of the 1st Extraordinary Session
of the 114th General Assembly, House Civil Justice Subcommittee, and House Population
Health Subcommittee; and
WHEREAS, he has also served with distinction as vice chair and chair of the House
Insurance and Banking Committee, chair of the House Business Subcommittee, chair of the
Joint Fiscal Review Committee, and member of the House Agriculture and Natural Resources
Subcommittee, House Consumer and Human Resources Committee, House Insurance and
Banking Subcommittee, House Transportation Committee, House Calendar and Rules
Committee, House Commerce Committee, House Consumer Subcommittee, House Local
Committee, and the House Cities and Counties Subcommittee; and
WHEREAS, he further served as a member of the House Naming and Designating
Committee, House Commerce Committee of the Second Extraordinary Session of the 112th
General Assembly, House Judiciary Committee of the Third Extraordinary Session of the 112th
General Assembly, House Population Health Subcommittee, and, during the 1st Extraordinary
Session of the 113th General Assembly, a member of the House Agriculture and Natural
Resources Committee, House Health Committee, House Agriculture and Natural Resources
Subcommittee, and House Population Health Subcommittee; and

WHEREAS, Ron Travis has proven that citizens can make a difference by being
involved in their community, which he has demonstrated through his participation as a member
of the Pikeville, Dayton, Dunlap, and Roane County Chambers of Commerce; Civitan Club;
Emmaus Community; Southeast Tennessee Rural Planning Organization; Board of Directors of
the National Association of Christian Athletes; Southeast Tennessee Development District
Executive Committee; Southeast Tennessee Human Resource Agency Governing Board;
Health Care Modernization Task Force (2019-2020), and Healthcare Balance Billing Task Force
(2016-2017); and
WHEREAS, his many accomplishments aside, Representative Travis is most grateful for
the love and support of his wife, Laura; daughters, Rachel Travis and Jessica Shannon; and
grandchildren, Alley Jo Shannon, Beckham Colby Shannon, Finley Scout Shannon, and Hadley
Jane Travis; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to honors and accolades, Representative Ron Travis was given
the 2022 U.S. Term Limits Champion of Term Limits Award, and in 2014, he was honored with
the Tennessee Men's Health Outstanding Legislative Leadership Award, Tennessee
Development District Association Legislator of the Year Award, and Tennessee Chamber of
Commerce Champion of Commerce Award; and
WHEREAS, in addition to being named Legislator of the Year in 2021 by the Tennessee
Renewable Energy and Economic Development Council, he was named Legislator of the Year
in 2014, 2017, and 2018 by the Tennessee Development District Association, as well as a
Nationwide Insurance President's Conference and Champion's Conference Qualifier and a Life
Underwriter's Training Council Fellow; and
WHEREAS, throughout his outstanding career as a public servant, Representative
Travis has worked tirelessly on behalf of his constituents, bringing to his office an honorable
bearing and the traditional values of hard work and common sense; and

WHEREAS, Representative Ron Travis is wholly committed to the noble precepts of
public service that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should
be specially recognized for his exemplary tenure in the General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor Representative Ron Travis for his meritorious service to
Tennessee as a member of the House of Representatives, commend him for his countless
contributions and many good works in service of the citizens of the 31st House District, and
extend to him our best wishes for much continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
