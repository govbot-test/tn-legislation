Section 1:
Amendment No. 1 to HJR1514
Russell
Signature of Sponsor
AMEND < S B> House Joint Resolution No. 1514*
WHEREAS, we are honored to recognize those selfless individuals whose quick thinking
and decisive actions have rescued those in peril; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Graham Tudor, the Legislative Director for the
Tennessee Department of Safety and Homeland Security, who recently become a hero of the
highest order when he bravely stepped in to aid another in a dangerous situation; and
WHEREAS, on April 5, 2026, Mr. Tudor was driving home with his family after a Sunday
dinner when they arrived on the scene of a two-car crash involving a rolled-over vehicle on
White Bridge Pike; and
WHEREAS, while others called 911, Graham Tudor and another Good Samaritan began
trying to free the trapped individual from the rolled-over automobile; after realizing that none of
the doors would open, he and the Good Samaritan agreed that the only option was to break the
window to free the individual; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Tudor proceeded to use a car jack to shatter the car window and free
the trapped individual inside before first responders arrived on the scene to treat all parties
involved in the wreck; and
WHEREAS, shortly after the arrival of the first responders, Graham Tudor left the scene
to get his young son home for bedtime; unfortunately, Mr. Tudor received injuries from the
incident that required medical treatment at St. Thomas West; and

WHEREAS, although he is modest and humble in relating the event to others, Mr. Tudor
exhibited quick thinking under pressure, and his compassionate action is a testament to his
character and bravery; and
WHEREAS, Graham Tudor's actions were sure and decisive in the midst of a serious
situation, and he is most deserving of recognition for his heroism; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Graham Tudor for his outstanding demonstration
of courage, resourcefulness, and presence of mind as we join with the citizens of Tennessee in
expressing our admiration and appreciation for his heroic actions.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
