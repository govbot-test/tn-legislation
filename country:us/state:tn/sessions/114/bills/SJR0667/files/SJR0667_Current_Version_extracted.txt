SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 667
By Bowling
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Colonel Thomas B.
Vaughn of Warren County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were saddened to learn of the
passing of Colonel Thomas B. Vaughn, a distinguished soldier, educator, writer, and
philanthropist whose life of service left an enduring mark on Warren County and the State of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Colonel Vaughn, a proud native of Warren County, began his military career
as a private in the Tennessee Army National Guard before entering the United States Army,
where he rose quickly through the ranks, earning promotion to Staff Sergeant at the age of
twenty-two and graduating with distinction from Infantry Officer Candidate School in 1962; and
WHEREAS, over the course of a remarkable military career spanning service on five
continents, Colonel Vaughn served as a two-term combat veteran of the Vietnam War and
earned numerous commendations, including the Legion of Merit with oak leaf cluster, Bronze
Star Medal with "V" device and four oak leaf clusters, Purple Heart, Defense Meritorious Service
Medal, Army Meritorious Service Medal with four oak leaf clusters, Air Medal with "V" device,
Combat Infantry Badge, Expert Infantry Badge, Parachutist Badge, Ranger Tab, and General
Staff Identification Badge; and
WHEREAS, Colonel Vaughn further distinguished himself academically, earning a
bachelor's degree from Omaha University in 1967 and a master's degree in political science
from the University of Kansas in 1972 and graduating from the prestigious Army War College in

Section 2:
1982. In 1984, he was placed on the Honor Roll of the Infantry Officer Candidate Hall of Fame
at Fort Benning, Georgia, in recognition of his exemplary leadership and service; and

WHEREAS, following his retirement from the Army, Colonel Vaughn continued his
commitment to public service by teaching American history, Tennessee history, state and local
government, and public speaking at the college level and by serving as a radio host on WBMC
and WCPI, where he informed and engaged the community; and
WHEREAS, a gifted writer and lifelong student of literature, Colonel Vaughn contributed
numerous columns to the Southern Standard, including his long-running My Turn series, and
was known for his thoughtful commentary, poetry, and speechwriting, even assisting leaders at
the Pentagon; and
WHEREAS, Colonel Vaughn was also a respected figure in the music industry, serving
as President and CEO of Bob White Music, Inc., where his leadership helped sustain the
company and support the careers of numerous artists; his friendship and collaboration with
Jacky Jack White spanned decades and reflected his loyalty, humor, and dedication to
excellence; and
WHEREAS, deeply committed to charitable work, Colonel Vaughn supported the Warren
County Animal Control and Adoption Center, the Tennessee Fisher House Foundation, and
other community causes, demonstrating a lifelong devotion to helping others and improving the
lives of those around him; and
WHEREAS, Colonel Vaughn was a devoted family man, a member of New Union
Church, and a beloved husband, father, and grandfather who cherished time with his family,
especially his "bonus daughters" and grandchildren, whose activities he faithfully supported
from the sidelines; and
WHEREAS, in his later years, Colonel Vaughn enjoyed traveling, beach vacations,
Scrabble tournaments, and the simple joys of life shared with those he loved, embodying
gratitude, generosity, and a spirit of service until his final days; and
- 2 - 012498
WHEREAS, Colonel Thomas B. Vaughn leaves behind a legacy of courage, leadership,
scholarship, and kindness, and it is fitting that we pause to honor his extraordinary life and
service to his community, his State, and his nation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Colonel Thomas B.
Vaughn, extend our sympathy to his family and friends, and celebrate the life of a Tennessean
whose service and character will long be remembered.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
