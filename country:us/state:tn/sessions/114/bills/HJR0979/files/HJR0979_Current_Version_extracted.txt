HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 979
By Cepicky
A RESOLUTION to honor Lieutenant Colonel Brian E. Celatka for
his service on the occasion of his military
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
extraordinary Tennesseans who, through their remarkable and exceptional efforts, have gone
above and beyond the call of duty by dedicating their lives to protecting the citizens of this State
and nation; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Lieutenant Colonel Brian E. Celatka, who began his
exemplary military career with the United States Army in January 1985; and
WHEREAS, Lieutenant Colonel Celatka served as an Infantryman with the 101st
Airborne Division (Air Assault), deploying in support of Operations Desert Shield and Desert
Storm, and with the 2nd Infantry Division, where he served within the Korean Demilitarized Zone;
and
WHEREAS, in 1997, Lieutenant Colonel Celatka continued his commitment to service by
joining the Tennessee National Guard, and in 2006, he was commissioned as an officer as a
graduate of the United States Army Officer Candidate School, branching into the Military Police
Corps; and
WHEREAS, from 2008 to 2009, Lieutenant Colonel Celatka deployed with the
Tennessee National Guard and served with distinction in a joint combat assignment as the
Officer in Charge of all Police Transition Team operations for United States Central Command
in Baghdad, Iraq, during Operation Iraqi Freedom; and
WHEREAS, in 2010, Lieutenant Colonel Celatka transitioned to the Tennessee Air
National Guard, where he held command of both a Security Forces Squadron and a Force
Support Squadron, and from 2018 to 2019, he deployed to Al Udeid Air Base, Qatar, as the

Headquarters Section and Installation Security Protection Commander for the United States Air
Force Central Command, Combined Air Operations Center, in support of Operations Inherent

WHEREAS, in fitting recognition of Lieutenant Colonel Celatka's commitment to
excellence throughout his career, his major awards include the Master Combat Infantryman's
Badge, the Imjin Scout Badge, the Air Assault Badge, the Bronze Star Medal, the Meritorious
Service Medal with three oak leaf clusters, and the Tennessee National Guard Distinguished
Service Medal; and
WHEREAS, Lieutenant Colonel Celatka earned a Master of Military Operational Art and
Science degree from the United States Air Force Air Command and Staff College and continues
his commitment to public service and lifelong learning through his pursuit of a Master of Public
Administration degree from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville; and
WHEREAS, in 2023, Lieutenant Colonel Celatka was selected to serve as a legislative
liaison for Governor Bill Lee and as the legislative director for the Tennessee Military
Department, allowing him to continue to serve the citizens of this State in a civilian capacity
following his military retirement; and
WHEREAS, throughout his distinguished career, Lieutenant Colonel Brian E. Celatka
has served the United States and the State of Tennessee with honor, integrity, and distinction,
exemplifying the highest traditions of the Tennessee National Guard and public service; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and recognize Lieutenant Colonel Brian E. Celatka of the
Tennessee National Guard upon his retirement, extend our gratitude for his many years of
