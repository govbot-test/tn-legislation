SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 565
By Crowe
A RESOLUTION to recognize March 2026 as "Endometriosis
Awareness Month."
WHEREAS, endometriosis affects one in ten women of childbearing age in the United
States; and
WHEREAS, endometriosis is a chronic gynecological disease of the female reproductive
system and is a leading cause of pelvic pain and infertility; and
WHEREAS, while endometriosis is known to cause pelvic pain and infertility, some
women have other symptoms depending on the location of the endometriosis, and some women
have no symptoms; and
WHEREAS, there is an average delay of ten years from the onset of symptoms to the
final diagnosis of endometriosis; and
WHEREAS, endometriosis and its treatment result in tens of thousands of
hospitalizations, lost productivity, and absenteeism at work due to chronic pain; and
WHEREAS, there is no cure for endometriosis, and more research is necessary to
develop additional treatment options to manage this potentially debilitating disease; and
WHEREAS, evidence-based standards are based on the severity of the disease, and
treatment may include oral contraception, progestins, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, and
surgery, although access to surgical treatment may vary by region, insurance coverage, and
availability of clinicians; and
WHEREAS, education about endometriosis and its symptoms is important in promoting
awareness among the public and healthcare providers alike and enhancing the early detection
and diagnosis of the disease; now, therefore,
