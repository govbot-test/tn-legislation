SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 569
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to celebrate George Washington's birthday.
WHEREAS, George Washington's birthday on February 22 is celebrated annually to
acknowledge the many sacrifices and contributions President Washington made for our young
country and for his service as "Father of our Country"; and
WHEREAS, in 1775, George Washington was appointed Commander in Chief of the
Continental Army, serving with great character and leadership for eight and one-half years
without pay, leading the American Army to victory against the British; and
WHEREAS, George Washington established the primacy of civilian rule when, in 1783,
he stood down a proposed mutiny against Congress by soldiers who had not been paid and
voluntarily relinquished his military commission, returning to his beloved farm, Mount Vernon;
and
WHEREAS, George Washington advanced the rule of law by chairing the Constitutional
Convention, signing the U.S. Constitution, and thereafter dedicating himself and his fellow
countrymen to creating "a more perfect union"; and
WHEREAS, George Washington was elected unanimously to be the first President of
the United States; and
WHEREAS, during his presidency, George Washington established many enduring
values, including the principle of religious freedom set out in his Letter to the Touro Synagogue;
and
WHEREAS, George Washington modeled the peaceful transfer of power when,
voluntarily, he chose to step down after eight years as President; and

WHEREAS, George Washington freed his slaves upon his death, thereby publicly
acknowledging that the institution of slavery was antithetical to the principles of the American
republic; and
WHEREAS, George Washington died on December 14, 1799, at his home, Mount
Vernon, and upon Washington's death, John Adams said, "His example is now complete, and it
will teach wisdom and virtue to magistrates, citizens and men, not only in the present age, but in
future generations, as long as our history shall be read"; and
WHEREAS, George Washington's Mount Vernon welcomes Tennesseans throughout
the year and sponsors Teachers' Institutes, which give Tennessee teachers the unique
opportunity to study and learn about George Washington; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we join with all American citizens across the nation
in the annual celebration of George Washington's birthday on Sunday, February 22, 2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
