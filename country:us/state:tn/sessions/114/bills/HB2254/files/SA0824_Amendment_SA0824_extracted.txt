SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 47, Chapter 18, is amended by adding
the following as a new part:
47-18-3501. Short title.
This part is known and may be cited as the "App Store Accountability Act."
47-18-3502. Part definitions.
As used in this part:
(1) ''Account holder" means the individual who is associated with the
mobile device;
(2) ''Age category" means one (1) of the following categories of
individuals based on age:
(A) "Child" means an individual who is less than thirteen (13)
years of age;
(B) "Younger teenager" means an individual who is at least
thirteen (13) years of age and less than sixteen (16) years of age;
(C) "Older teenager" means an individual who is at least sixteen
(16) years of age and less than eighteen (18) years of age; or
(D) "Adult" means an individual who is at least eighteen (18)
years of age;

(3) ''Age category data" means information about an account holder's
age category that is collected by an app store provider and is shared with a
developer;
(4) "Age rating" means one (1) or more classifications that assess the
suitability of an app's content and functions for different age categories;
(5) "App" means a software application or electronic service that a user
may run or direct on a mobile device, including a pre-installed app;
(6) ''App store" means a publicly available website, software application,

(7) ''App store provider" means a person that owns, operates, or controls

(8) "Content description" means a description of the specific content
elements or functions that informed an app's age rating;
(9) "Developer" means a person that owns or controls an app made

device;
(10) "Knowingly" means to act with actual knowledge or to act with
knowledge fairly inferred based on objective circumstances;
(11) "Minor":

years of age; and
(B) Does not include an individual who has not yet attained
eighteen (18) years of age but who is married or legally emancipated;
(12) "Minor account" means an account with an app store provider that is
established by an individual who is a minor;

47-18-3503. App store provider requirements.
(a) An app store provider shall:
(1) At the time an individual who is located in this state creates an
account with the app store provider, or, for existing accounts, within twelve (12)
months of the effective date of this part:
(A) Request age category information from the individual or
account holder; and
(B) Verify the individual's age category using commercially
available methods that are reasonably designed to ensure accuracy. For
an individual under eighteen (18) years of age, a method is commercially
available if the method includes:
- 5 - 014428

(i) Affirmative age attestation by an individual who is
reasonably believed to be the minor's parent or legal guardian;
and
(ii) Other account information collected in the ordinary
course of account creation or use;
(2) If the app store provider determines that the individual is a minor:
(A) Require the account to be affiliated with a parent account; and
(B) Obtain verifiable parental consent from the holder of the
affiliated parent account each time before allowing the minor to:
(i) Download an app;
(ii) Purchase an app; or
(iii) Make an in-app purchase;
(3) After receiving notice of a significant change from a developer:
(A) Notify the account holder of the significant change; and
(B) For a minor account:
(i) Notify the affiliated parent account; and
(ii) Obtain renewed verifiable parental consent before
providing access to the significantly changed version of the app;
(4) If requested by a developer, provide to the developer:
(A) Age category data for an account holder located in this state;
and
(B) The status of verifiable parental consent for a minor user
located in this state;
(5) Provide a mechanism for a parent account to withdraw consent and
notify a developer when a parent account revokes verifiable parental consent;
(6) Protect age category data and any associated verification data by:
(A) Limiting collection and processing to data necessary for:
- 6 - 014428

(i) Verifying an account holder's age category;
(ii) Obtaining verifiable parental consent; or
(iii) Maintaining compliance records; and
(B) Transmitting age category data using industry-standard
encryption protocols that ensure data integrity and data confidentiality;
and
(7) For a pre-installed app:
(A) Provide available age category information in response to a
request from a developer; and
(B) Take reasonable measures to facilitate verifiable parental
consent for use of the app in response to a request from a developer.
(b) An app store provider shall not:
(1) Enforce a contract or terms of service against a minor unless the app
store provider has obtained verifiable parental consent;
(2) Knowingly misrepresent the information in the parental consent
disclosure; or
(3) Share age category data and any associated data except as required
by this part or another law.
47-18-3504. Developer requirements.
(a) A developer shall:
(1) Request through the app store's data sharing methods verified age-
category data of an account holder located in this state, and for a minor's
account, whether verifiable parental consent has been obtained;
(2) Request age category data or verifiable parental consent:
(A) At the time an account holder:
(i) Downloads an app;
(ii) Purchases an app;
- 7 - 014428

(iii) Launches a pre-installed app for the first time; or
(iv) Creates a new account with the developer;
(B) When implementing a significant change to the app; and
(C) To ensure compliance with applicable laws;
(3) Notify the app store provider of a significant change to an app; and
(4) Use age category data received through the app store's data sharing
methods to:
(A) Ensure compliance with any laws or regulations; and
(B) Enforce any developer-created, age-related restrictions,
safety features, or defaults.
(b) When implementing a developer-created, age-related restriction, safety
feature, or default, a developer shall utilize the lowest category indicated by:
(1) Age category data received through the app store's data sharing
methods; or
(2) Age data independently collected by the developer.
(c)
(1) A developer shall not request age category data more than once
during each twelve-month period to verify:
(A) The accuracy of the age category associated with an account
holder; or
(B) Continued account use within the age category.
(2) Notwithstanding subdivision (c)(1), a developer may more frequently
request through the app store's data sharing methods verified age category data
of an account holder located in this state when there is reasonable suspicion of:
(A) Account transfer; or
(B) Misuse outside of the age category.

(3) In addition to the prohibition described under subdivision (c)(1), a
developer shall not:
(A) Enforce a contract or terms of service against a minor unless
the developer has verified through an app store's data sharing methods
that verifiable parental consent has been obtained;
(B) Knowingly misrepresent information in the parental consent
disclosure; or
(C) Share age category data and any associated data except as
required by this part or another law.
47-18-3505. Enforcement.
(a)
(1) A minor, or the parent or other legal guardian of a minor, who has
been aggrieved by a violation of this part may bring a civil action against an app
store provider or developer.
(2) In such an action, the court shall award a prevailing plaintiff:
(A) The greater of actual damages or one thousand dollars
($1,000) per violation;
(B) Punitive damages, if the court finds that the violation was
egregious; and
(C) Reasonable attorney fees and court costs.
(b)
(1) A violation of this part constitutes a violation of the Tennessee
Consumer Protection Act of 1977, compiled in part 1 of this chapter. A violation
of this part constitutes an unfair or deceptive act or practice affecting trade or
commerce and is subject to the penalties and remedies as provided in the
Tennessee Consumer Protection Act of 1977, in addition to the penalties and
remedies in this part.
- 9 - 014428

(2) In addition to the remedies described under subsection (a) and
subdivision (b)(1), the attorney general and reporter may bring an action against
an app store provider or developer to:
(A) Recover a civil penalty not to exceed seven thousand five
hundred dollars ($7,500) per violation;
(B) Restrain or enjoin the app store provider or developer from
violating this part;
(C) Seek injunctive relief; and
(D) Recover reasonable attorney fees and court costs.
47-18-3506. Safe harbor.
(a) A developer is not liable for a violation of § 47-18-3504 if the developer
demonstrates that the developer:
(1) Relied in good faith on applicable age category data received through
an app store's data sharing methods;
(2) Relied in good faith on notification from an app store provider that
verifiable parental consent was obtained if the account holder was a minor; and
(3) Complied with the requirements of § 47-18-3504.
(b) In determining an app's age rating and content description, a developer is not
liable for a violation of § 47-18-3504 if the developer:
(1) Uses widely adopted industry standards to determine the app's age
category and content description; and
(2) Applies such standards consistently and in good faith.
(c) The safe harbor described in this section:
(1) Applies only to actions brought pursuant to this part; and
(2) Does not limit an app store provider's or developer's liability under
any other law.

(d) This part does not affect or otherwise limit any other available remedies or
rights authorized under federal law or the law of this state.
47-18-3507. Application.
This part does not:
(1) Prevent an app store provider or developer from taking reasonable
measures to:
(A) Block, detect, or prevent distribution to minors of:
(i) Unlawful material;
(ii) Obscene material; or
(iii) Other material harmful to minors;
(B) Block or filter spam;
(C) Prevent criminal activity; or
(D) Protect app store or app security;
(2) Require an app store provider to disclose user information to a
developer beyond age category data or status of parental consent;
(3) Allow an app store provider or developer to implement measures
required by this part in a manner that is arbitrary, capricious, anticompetitive, or
unlawful;
(4) Require a developer to collect, retain, reidentify, or link any
information beyond what is:
(A) Necessary to verify age category data as required by this part;
and
(B) Collected, retained, reidentified, or linked in the developer's
ordinary course of business; or
(5) Require an app store provider to block access to an application that
an account holder has downloaded or installed onto a mobile device prior to this
part's effect date, except to the extent that:

(A) A parent account revokes verifiable consent for an affiliated
minor account; or
(B) There has been a significant change to the affected app.
