SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 722
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to affirm the State of Tennessee's support for the
State of Israel, our greatest friend and ally in the
Middle East, and to express our solidarity with
Israel's right to defend itself against terror
attacks, and commemorate the final return of all
hostages from the Hamas October 7,2023, terror
attacks.
WHEREAS, the State of Israel, the only democracy in the Middle East, is the greatest
friend, ally, and strategic partner of the United States in the region; and
WHEREAS, there is a strong historic fellowship of religious, cultural, economic,
business, social, governmental, and educational relationships between the United States of
America, the State of Tennessee, and the State of Israel; and
WHEREAS, Israel is a valued economic partner of the State of Tennessee. In 2025,
Tennessee exported $95.7 million worth of manufactured goods to Israel. Since 2021,
Tennessee exports to Israel have totaled more than $442 million, and Israel now ranks as
Tennessee's fortieth leading trading partner; and
WHEREAS, the continued cooperation between the State of Tennessee and Israel is
mutually beneficial and of significant importance; and
WHEREAS, 2026 marks seventy-eight years of robust United States and Israel relations,
reflected in the relations between the State of Tennessee and Israel, a special relationship
based on mutually shared democratic values, common strategic interests, and moral bonds of
friendship and mutual respect; and
WHEREAS, once again, we recognize the importance of expressing our unabridged
support for the Jewish people and the State of Israel's right to exist and its right to free trade;
and

WHEREAS, the State of Tennessee is a supporter of the State of Israel and its right to
defend itself against terror and any threat to its security; and
WHEREAS, on October 7, 2023, the terrorist group Hamas launched a surprise attack
from Gaza on multiple population centers in southern Israel; and
WHEREAS, at least 1,400 Israelis—mostly civilians—were massacred by terrorists,
including civilians brutally tortured and murdered in their homes and young people gunned down
at a music festival; and
WHEREAS, Hamas terrorists kidnapped and dragged into Gaza 250 men, women,
children, toddlers, and infants; and
WHEREAS, the U.S. State Department has confirmed that dozens of Americans were
killed or taken hostage in these attacks; and
WHEREAS, the State of Israel has conducted a just war of self-defense on behalf of the
free world in the face of evil and terror; and
WHEREAS, after more than two years of continuous resilience, sacrifice, and courage
on behalf of the Israeli Defense Forces and the Israeli people and after 843 days, all hostages
brutally held in captivity by Hamas have finally been returned; and
WHEREAS, 2026 also marks the first year in which there are no Israeli hostages held by
Hamas since 2013; and
WHEREAS, it is our hope that these conditions will pave the way for greater regional
cooperation; and
WHEREAS, the United States played a key role in establishing the historic Abraham
Accords, normalizing relations between Israel, the United Arab Emirates, Bahrain, and Morocco;
and
- 2 - 012850
WHEREAS, the Abraham Accords will continue to usher in a new era of peace and
economic prosperity between Israel and Arab countries working together for a better future for
their peoples; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby affirm the State of Tennessee's support
for the State of Israel and express our solidarity with Israel's right to defend itself against terror
attacks as we commemorate the final return of all hostages from the Hamas October 7, 2023,
terror attacks.
