SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 785
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to honor Todd Loveday on being named Region
1-5A Coach of the Year.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
an excellent coach who has received a prestigious honor for leading his team to unprecedented
success in athletic competition; and
WHEREAS, Todd Loveday, Head Coach of the Sevier County High School football
team, has been named the 2025 Region 1-5A Coach of the Year, a most befitting honor that
recognizes his essential contributions to the Smoky Bears' incredible success during the past
two seasons; and
WHEREAS, during the 2025 campaign, Sevier County compiled an impressive 9-1
regular-season record under Coach Loveday's leadership and then won four playoff games to
reach the title game of the TSSAA Class 5A State Championship tourney, finishing the year 13-
2; and
WHEREAS, in 2024, Coach Loveday led his charges to the promised land, as the
Smoky Bears posted five consecutive playoff victories to win the Class 5A State Championship;
and
WHEREAS, Coach Loveday has praised not only his players for their selfless play but
lauded the contributions of his assistant coaches, who helped keep the team's expectations
high; and
WHEREAS, Todd Loveday has distinguished himself as a great football coach, tactician,
and motivator of young men, and he should be appropriately recognized on this special
occasion; now, therefore,
SJR0785
011747
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Todd Loveday of Sevier County High
School on being named Region 1-5A Coach of the Year, salute his outstanding career on the
gridiron, and extend to him our best wishes for much continued success on the field of play and
in life.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
