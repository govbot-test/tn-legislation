SENATE RESOLUTION 335
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Officer Thomas Wills
upon being named Haywood County
Correctional Officer of the Year.
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State is especially proud to be the home of so many valiant
men and women who continue the time-honored tradition of public service in law enforcement,
dedicating their time and efforts to aiding those in emergency situations and apprehending
those who have broken the law; and
WHEREAS, Officer Thomas Wills of the Haywood County Sheriff's Office is one such
courageous public servant, who was recently named Haywood County Correctional Officer of
the Year by Sheriff Billy Garrett, Jr.; and
WHEREAS, Officer Wills has contributed greatly to the safety of the citizens of Haywood
County, serving with tremendous courage, fortitude, and professionalism and making a
significant impact for the betterment of his community; and
WHEREAS, Officer Wills epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of
a true Tennessean, and his professionalism and dedication to Haywood County are evident to
all who know him; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Officer Thomas Wills for
his dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and congratulate
Thomas Wills upon being named Haywood County Correctional Officer of the Year, thank him
for his meritorious service to the citizens of Haywood County and the State of Tennessee, and
extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
SR0335
015321
- 1 -
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
