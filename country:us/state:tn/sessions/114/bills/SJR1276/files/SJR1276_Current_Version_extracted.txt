SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1276
By Crowe
A RESOLUTION to commend Randall Franks's A Zippedy Doodle
Day: American Folk Songs for preserving
Appalachian musical heritage and supporting
Appalachian Music Scholarships.
WHEREAS, actor and fiddler Randall Franks, known around the world as "Officer Randy
Goode" from television's In the Heat of the Night, is a descendant of several of East
Tennessee's first families, with continuing roots, as his late Bruce grandparents' farm makes up
the Head of Sequatchie portion of the Cumberland Trail State Park; and
WHEREAS, Randall Franks is a respected bluegrass and Americana artist, producer,
and founder of the Share America Foundation, Inc., and is developing A Zippedy Doodle Day:
American Folk Songs, a historic string band album and documentary that honors the rich legacy
of folk artists John "Doodle" Thrower and the Golden River Grass, considered the last Georgia
fiddle band of country music's string band era; and
WHEREAS, this effort preserves unreleased 1980s tracks while reintroducing their
music to new generations through collaborations with stars of today; proceeds will benefit
Appalachian music scholarships administered by the Share America Foundation, Inc.; and
WHEREAS, among the first Tennessee stars stepping forward to play a vital role in the
project are Tim Atwood, Bruce Boxleitner, Gena Britt, Wyatt Ellis, Jimmy Fortune, Daniel
Grindstaff, Jim Lauderdale, Kathy Lee, Alex Miller, Kody Norris, Mary Rachel Nalley-Norris,
Ronnie and Justin Reno, Eb Saunders, Mike Scott, Margie Singleton, Larry Stephenson, Ketch
Secor of the Old Crowe Medicine Show, Derek Stone, Leroy Troy, and Conner Vlietstra; and
WHEREAS, the project's recording engineers include Buck Jarrell, Jim Price, and
Morgan Jahnig; additional artists from across the country who have contributed to the project

include The Skillet Lickers, Dom Flemons, Dante Flores, Trevor Holder, Dale Houston, Anthony

WHEREAS, the Share America Foundation's Pearl and Floyd Franks Scholarship
supports graduating seniors talented in traditional Southern and Appalachian music from
several Tennessee counties, empowering young musicians to pursue higher education and
carrying forward the State's rich musical heritage; and
WHEREAS, this initiative not only safeguards a unique chapter of American folk music
history celebrated by historians such as Alan Lomax and Loyal Jones, it also fosters the next
generation of Appalachian artists, strengthening cultural preservation and educational
opportunities across Tennessee and the region; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commend Randall Franks and A Zippedy Doodle
Day: American Folk Songs for their outstanding contributions to Appalachian music preservation
and scholarship support, and we express our gratitude to all Tennessee participants who have
helped and will help advance this important project.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
