Briggs

by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:

Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 41, Chapter 21, Part 10, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) As used in this section, "office" means the office of investigations and
conduct created in subsection (b).
(b) There is created the office of investigations and conduct that is
administratively attached to the board.
(c) The executive director shall appoint a director of investigations, who shall
serve as the chief administrative officer of the office.
(d)
(1) The director of investigations shall appoint:
(A) A special agent in charge for each grand division;
(B) Special agents to serve under the special agents in charge;
(C) For each correctional facility managed by the department:
(i) An institutional investigator;
(ii) A correctional sergeant;
(iii) A correctional corporal; and
(iv) A disciplinary board sergeant; and
(D) An administrative assistant for the office.
(2) It is the intent of the general assembly that the director of
investigations and the employees of the office appointed pursuant to this

subsection (d) are employees transferred from the office of investigations and
conduct, and prison operations, of the department of correction.
(e) The director of investigations, special agents in charge, and all special
agents of the office must be fully commissioned law enforcement officers in this state
with authority to carry firearms on or off duty, with full arrest authority for any criminal
violation, and have full authority to investigate any incident, violation of department
policy, or crime inside any correctional facility in this state, wherever inmates are housed
or incarcerated, or wherever department employees are employed, including department
regional offices, the department training academy, and offices of probation and parole.
The director of investigations, special agents in charge, and special agents of the office
have unlimited access to all department records of any kind, physical or digital, including
personnel, business, and all inmate records, including medical records of inmates.
(f) All employees of the department shall comply with a request from the office
during an investigation by the director of investigations, a special agent in charge, a
special agent, or an institutional investigator.
(g) The director of investigations, special agents in charge, and special agents of
the office shall participate in annual law enforcement training courses each calendar
year appropriate to the person's rank and responsibility and maintain POST certification.
(h) Institutional investigators have full investigative authority at the correctional
facility in which they are assigned and shall maintain an office in the correctional facility.
An institutional investigator has unlimited access to any area inside or around the
curtilage of all properties designated for usage by the department, and unlimited access
to all department physical or digital records of any kind, including personnel, business,
and all inmate records, including medical records.
(i) Correctional sergeants and corporals of the office shall serve as internal
affairs staff for the institutional investigator at each correctional facility and have the
same investigative authority as the institutional investigator.

(j) Each disciplinary board sergeant shall oversee the inmate disciplinary
process at each prison, conduct disciplinary hearings, and issue a monthly report of all
inmate disciplinary activities to the board. There must be a fully equipped office at each
prison for the disciplinary board sergeant.
(k) The office shall prepare an annual report on the activities of the office. The
board shall publish the report on the board's website and shall submit the report to the
governor, the speakers of the senate and the house of representatives, and the chairs of
the committees of the senate and the house of representatives having jurisdiction over
correction matters.
SECTON 2. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
