SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1245
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Rebecca Tipton upon
her induction into the Tennessee Boys & Girls
Clubs Hall of Fame.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly pause in their
deliberations to salute those citizens who, through their remarkable efforts, have distinguished
themselves as community and state leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, Rebecca Tipton is one such estimable individual, who was recently inducted
into the Tennessee Boys & Girls Clubs Hall of Fame, the highest honor bestowed by the
organization's Tennessee Area Council; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Tipton was one of five individuals selected for this prestigious
recognition, honoring a lifetime of achievement and service to the Boys & Girls Clubs; and
WHEREAS, a lifelong resident of Greeneville and Greene County, Rebecca Tipton has
demonstrated a deep and abiding commitment to service, guided by her personal motto, "We
are here for the kids," and has devoted nearly two decades to advancing the mission of the
Boys & Girls Club of Greeneville & Greene County; and
WHEREAS, through her leadership, dedication, and compassion, she has made a
lasting impact not only on her local club but also across the broader community and the State of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Tipton has been instrumental in strengthening programs, supporting
fundraising efforts, and enhancing opportunities for young people, contributing to the continued
success of one of the strongest Boys & Girls Clubs in the State; and
WHEREAS, her exceptional service has been recognized at both the State and national
levels; in 2020, she was named Tennessee Boys & Girls Club Board Member of the Year, and
she received the Boys & Girls Clubs of America's National Service to Youth Award in 2014; and

018477
- 1 -
WHEREAS, beyond her work with the Boys & Girls Club, Rebecca Tipton has been an
active and engaged member of her community, supporting numerous civic and charitable
organizations and serving in various leadership roles; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of South Greene High School, Ms. Tipton earned a Bachelor of
Business Administration degree in Accounting from East Tennessee State University before
becoming a certified public accountant; and
WHEREAS, she currently serves as executive vice president and chief financial officer of
Greeneville Federal Bank, where she has demonstrated excellence in her profession and
leadership in her field; and
WHEREAS, Rebecca Tipton is most grateful for the love and support she shares with
her husband, Rick Tipton; the Tiptons are the proud parents of Jordan Tipton and daughter-in-
law Ashley; and
WHEREAS, the success of this State, the strength of our communities, and the overall
vitality of American society depend, in great measure, upon the dedication of individuals such as
Mrs. Tipton, who use their considerable talents and resources to serve others and who should
be specially recognized for their public and community service; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Rebecca Tipton upon her
induction into the Tennessee Boys & Girls Clubs Hall of Fame and extend to her our best
wishes for continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
