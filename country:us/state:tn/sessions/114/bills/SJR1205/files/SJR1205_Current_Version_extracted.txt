SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1205
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to honor Knox County Sheriff Tom Spangler on
his outstanding career in law enforcement.
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State is proud to be home to many brave men and women
who go above and beyond the call of duty in service to public safety; and
WHEREAS, thousands of Tennesseans uphold the noble tradition of service in law
enforcement, devoting their time and efforts to protecting citizens and upholding justice; and
WHEREAS, Tom Spangler, Sheriff of Knox County, is one such distinguished law
enforcement professional, who has exemplified the highest ideals of public service and made
significant contributions to the safety and security of the citizens of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Spangler began his storied career with the Knox County Sheriff's
Office as a corrections officer; he was promoted to patrolman, then patrol supervisor, and then
Metro narcotics detective; and
WHEREAS, as Chief Deputy, he was instrumental in starting the Regional Training
Academy and the Knox County Sheriff's Office Aviation Unit; and
WHEREAS, Tom Spangler served astutely under two different sheriffs as Chief Deputy
before retiring from Knox County in 2009 to pursue a dream of professional fishing; and
WHEREAS, after a short time away from law enforcement, Sheriff Spangler returned to
the fold as the Assistant Chief of Training for the Blount County Sheriff's Office (BCSO); and
WHEREAS, in December 2016, Tom Spangler resigned from BCSO to run for Sheriff of
Knox County; and
WHEREAS, since September 1, 2018, he has served with the utmost dedication,
professionalism, and commitment as Sheriff of Knox County; and

WHEREAS, he is also a member of the High Intensity Drug Trafficking Areas Board of
Directors and the Police Officer Standards and Training Commission; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of Tusculum College, Sheriff Spangler proudly served his
country in the United States Air Force before being honorably discharged in 1980; and
WHEREAS, he is a helicopter pilot and has flown many disaster relief missions,
including during the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina; and
WHEREAS, Sheriff Spangler practices his faith as an active member of Clear Springs
Baptist Church in Corryton; and
WHEREAS, his many professional and personal accomplishments aside, Tom Spangler
is most grateful for the love, respect, and companionship he shares with his wife, Linda; his
daughters, Mellony and Mallory; and his five grandchildren; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Sheriff Tom Spangler on his outstanding
career in law enforcement and thank him for more than forty-five years of exemplary service to
the people of Knox County and the State of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
