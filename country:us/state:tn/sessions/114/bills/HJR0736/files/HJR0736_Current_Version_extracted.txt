HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 736
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to recognize and commend Steve Hiatt on the
occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those esteemed professionals who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm
throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Steve Hiatt is one such estimable person, who has taken his well-deserved
retirement after more than three decades of distinguished service in public and economic
development, having most recently served with dedication, ability, and integrity as director of
existing business development for the Chattanooga Area Chamber of Commerce; and
WHEREAS, raised in Florida, Mr. Hiatt moved to Tennessee in 1975; he attended
Chattanooga State Community College and the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga, where
he earned a bachelor's degree in political science in 1978; and
WHEREAS, prior to his work in economic development, Steve Hiatt served in a variety of
public and governmental roles, including service as assistant city manager of Red Bank and as
district chief of staff for former Congresswoman Marilyn Lloyd, gaining valuable experience in
problem-solving, constituent service, and collaborative leadership across multiple levels of
government; and
WHEREAS, in 1989, Mr. Hiatt entered the field of economic development, serving with
the Tennessee Department of Economic and Community Development and later with Partners
for Economic Progress and RiverValley Partners; and
WHEREAS, in 1998, when Chattanooga's public economic development functions
moved to the Chattanooga Chamber of Commerce, Steve Hiatt officially became a core part of
the team where he would serve with leadership and dedication for many years; and

WHEREAS, from his early days serving as founding president of the JFK Club and later
as president and officer of the Hamilton County Young Democrats, Mr. Hiatt built early
experience in community leadership; he served on the Tennessee Small Business Development
Center's Advisory Board and the Southeast Tennessee Workforce Development Board, helping
shape some of the policies and programs that are still in place today; and
WHEREAS, Steve Hiatt played a key role in the recruitment, retention, and expansion of
businesses across Hamilton County and the surrounding region, contributing to the creation of
tens of thousands of jobs, the generation of billions of dollars in capital investment, and the
strengthening of workforce and infrastructure resources that continue to benefit the region
today; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hiatt worked closely with his counterparts at the city and county level,
and made significant contributions to the expansions and locations of employers like McKee
Foods, Volkswagen, Gestamp, and many more companies of every size across the
Chattanooga region; he also had a hand in recruiting nearly every major employer in Enterprise
South and Centre South industrial parks, stepping in along the way to solve problems big and
small, from hiring issues to the occasional pothole; and
WHEREAS, in recognition of his leadership and outstanding achievements in economic
development, Steve Hiatt received the Fred H. Harris Professional of the Year Award from the
Tennessee Economic Development Council, an honor presented annually to individuals whose
work has made significant contributions to both their local communities and the State of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career, Mr. Hiatt earned a reputation as a
thoughtful, reliable, and effective advocate for the businesses and communities he served, and
he should be recognized on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we recognize and commend Steve Hiatt for his exemplary service to the
