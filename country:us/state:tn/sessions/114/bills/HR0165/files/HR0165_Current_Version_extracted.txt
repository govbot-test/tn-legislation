HOUSE RESOLUTION 165
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to recognize and commend the musical Suffs
and its performances at the Tennessee
Performing Arts Center.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this legislative body should honor those
artistic works that illuminate significant chapters of American history and contribute to the civic
and cultural life of this State; and
WHEREAS, Suffs is a Tony Award-winning musical with the music, lyrics, and book by
Shaina Taub, based on the suffragists of the American women's suffrage movement and
focusing on the events leading up to the ratification of the Nineteenth Amendment to the United
States Constitution, which afforded women the right to vote; and
WHEREAS, the musical Suffs brings to life the stories of the women who fought for
voting rights, exploring the strategic differences and shared goals of key figures such as Alice
Paul, Carrie Chapman Catt, Ida B. Wells, Lucy Burns, Inez Milholland, Doris Stevens, and Mary
Church Terrell as they organized marches, protests, and advocacy campaigns in pursuit of
equal suffrage; and
WHEREAS, Suffs traces pivotal moments in the suffrage movement, from the 1913
march on Washington, through which suffragists directly challenged national leaders to support
a federal amendment, to the internal debates and tensions within the movement regarding
tactics and inclusion, giving voice to both celebrated and historically overlooked activists; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee holds a distinguished place in the history of women's suffrage,
as the deciding vote cast in the Tennessee General Assembly in 1920 was crucial to the
ratification of the Nineteenth Amendment, helping to secure enfranchisement for women
nationwide; and

WHEREAS, the Tennessee Performing Arts Center will host Suffs at Jackson Hall in
Nashville from March 3 through March 8, 2026, offering Tennessee audiences the opportunity to
experience this compelling portrayal of civic engagement, democratic ideals, and the collective
struggle for equality; and
WHEREAS, Suffs premiered off-Broadway in 2022 and opened on Broadway in 2024,
earning critical acclaim as a bold and inspiring theatrical work that educates, moves, and
entertains through its rich score and dramatic storytelling; and
WHEREAS, through its vivid depiction of historical figures and events, Suffs highlights
the perseverance, courage, and complexity of the women who advanced the cause of suffrage
and underscores the importance of civic participation, social justice, and shared democratic
values; and
WHEREAS, we are pleased to welcome Suffs to Tennessee and to applaud its
presentation at the Tennessee Performing Arts Center; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we recognize
and commend the musical Suffs and its performances at the Tennessee Performing Arts
Center, celebrating its artistic excellence and its powerful portrayal of an essential chapter in
American history.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
