HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 793
By Carringer
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Thomas Hayes "Tommy"
Schumpert of Knox County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Thomas Hayes "Tommy" Schumpert; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Schumpert's life was one of service to his Lord and Savior Jesus
Christ, his family, and Knox County, particularly its youth; and
WHEREAS, born on December 5, 1938, in Dayton, Mr. Schumpert was the son of T.O.
Schumpert and Lucy Schumpert; shortly after his birth, his family moved to Knoxville, and he
spent more than eighty years of his life living in Fountain City; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Schumpert graduated from Knoxville Central High School in 1956;
after lettering in football and earning the title of County Most Valuable Player in the 1955
Knoxville City-County All-Star Game, he attended Tennessee Technological University on a
football scholarship before transferring to the University of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, he graduated from the University of Tennessee in 1960 and began teaching
at Knoxville Central High School that same year; he was the head baseball coach from 1968-
1973 and led the Bobcats to the 1973 State Championship; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Schumpert became the head football coach at Central in 1973 and led
the Bobcats to the State quarterfinals in two of his four years as head coach; he was named
The Knoxville Journal East Tennessee Football Coach of the Year in 1973; and
WHEREAS, for his service and success at Knoxville Central High School, Tommy
Schumpert was included on the Central High School Wall of Fame and was inducted as a
member of the high school's Sports Hall of Fame and the Greater Knoxville Sports Hall of Fame;
and
HJR0793
011499
- 1 -
WHEREAS, Knoxville Central High School's hitting facility is named after Tommy
Schumpert and his longtime friend Bud Bales, and in honor of Mr. Schumpert's service to the
city and its youth, the county created Tommy Schumpert Park in Fountain City in 2005; and
WHEREAS, despite leaving Knoxville Central in 1977, he remained in education, serving
as the Knox County School Business Manager and then Knox County School Assistant
Superintendent before being elected as Knox County Trustee in 1990 and then Knox County
Executive, a position now called mayor, in 1994; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Schumpert served two terms as the county mayor from 1994-2002;
he was the last Democrat to hold the position, and his tenure was marked by his ability to reach
across the aisle for Knoxville's betterment. In his capacity as mayor, he oversaw the creation of
eight new parks, pay increases for county employees, and infrastructure improvements to the
county's roads; and
WHEREAS, after his time as county mayor, he spent sixteen years as the program
director of Edscholar (Edsouth), a nonprofit corporation organized to acquire postsecondary
education loans under Title IV of the federal Higher Education Act of 1965; during his decade-
plus at Edsouth, the organization provided over $16 million in scholarships to over 870 students;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Schumpert was a dedicated Christian and servant of the Lord; he was a
member of Fountain City United Methodist Church for more than eighty years, teaching Sunday
School for over twenty years and taking on countless leadership roles in his decades at the
church; and
WHEREAS, above all, he was wholly devoted to his family, making time for his children
and grandchildren and frequently traveling for visits and to watch his grandchildren's sporting
events; and
- 2 - 011499
WHEREAS, Tommy Schumpert will be sadly mourned yet fondly remembered by his
high school sweetheart and wife of sixty-eight years, Charlotte Truan Schumpert; children, Scott
(Sally) Schumpert and Laura Schumpert (Mick) Raney; grandsons, Thomas Grant (McKenzie)
Schumpert, Andy Schumpert and fiancée Kate Linn, and Ryan Schumpert; great-grandchildren,
Kate Schumpert and Adrian Thomas Schumpert; brother, R.B. (Susan) Schumpert; brother-in-
law, Bill (Mila) Truan; and nieces and nephews, David (Nancy) Graves, Debbie (Mitch) Parks,
Kim Schumpert, and Amy Schumpert; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Mr. Schumpert was preceded in death by his
sister, Billie Ruth Graves, and her husband, Fred Graves, Jr.; and
WHEREAS, Tommy Schumpert will be remembered for his dignified leadership, his
lifelong service to Knox County, his devotion to faith and family, and his enduring connection to
his community; his legacy lives on in the thousands of lives he touched; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Thomas Hayes "Tommy" Schumpert, reflecting
fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life
with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Schumpert.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
