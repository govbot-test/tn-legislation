HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 915
By Hicks T
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Hobart L. "Coach"
Powell, Sr., of Jonesborough.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Hobart L. "Coach"
Powell, Sr., of Jonesborough; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Powell was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, Hobart Powell was born on December 22, 1933, to James and Agnes
Walker Powell; he attended Sulphur Springs High School, where he excelled in athletics and
academics; and
WHEREAS, he then attended Lincoln Memorial University, where he played basketball
and baseball; after graduating in 1955, Hobart Powell married the love of his life, Phyllis Henry
Bowman, and was drafted by the New York Yankees; and
WHEREAS, his professional baseball career was interrupted after two years when he
was drafted into the U.S. Army; and
WHEREAS, following his military service, Hobart Powell coached men's basketball in
Branford, Florida, for two years, leading the Branford High School Buccaneers to a 27-4 record
and a trip to the state semifinals; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Powell and his wife then returned to Tennessee, settling in
Jonesborough to raise their family; he coached men's basketball and baseball at Jonesboro
High School for eight years, men's basketball at Sulphur Springs High School for two years, and
varsity men's basketball at David Crockett High School for three years; and

WHEREAS, he then acquired a master's degree in education from East Tennessee
State University and transitioned to working in administration in the Washington County School
System; he first served as principal at Asbury Elementary School and then assistant principal
and principal at David Crockett High School, before becoming principal of Fall Branch
Elementary School and, ultimately, Jonesborough Elementary School; and
WHEREAS, in retirement, Hobart Powell spent twenty-two years working part time with
his friends in the Pro Shop at Pine Oaks Golf Course; and
WHEREAS, Coach Powell's career ambition was to be a coach; he cherished teaching
young men how to work hard, prepare, and compete to the best of their abilities; and
WHEREAS, Hobart Powell was a member of the Rhea Lodge No. 47, where he was
raised to the sublime degree of Master Mason in 1956; he is also a member of the Lincoln
Memorial University Sports Hall of Fame and the David Crockett High School Sports Hall of
Fame; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Powell was an avid golfer, lifetime New York Yankees fan, and
dedicated supporter of East Tennessee State University athletics and David Crockett High
School; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to his family, Hobart Powell always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his three children, Lori
Powell (Fritz) Thumler, Hobie Powell, and Jim Powell; four grandchildren, Abbie (Cliff) Thumler-
Robins, Alexander Thumler, Walker (Olivia) Powell, and Caroline Powell; great-grandson,
Shiloh Thumler-Robins; and a host of nieces, nephews, cousins, friends and family; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his beloved wife of
sixty-three years, Phyllis Bowman Powell, and his brothers, John Powell and Jean Powell; and

WHEREAS, Hobart Powell leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Hobart L. "Coach" Powell, Sr., reflecting fondly
upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with
courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Coach Powell.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
