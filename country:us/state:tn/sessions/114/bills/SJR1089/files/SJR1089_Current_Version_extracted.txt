SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1089
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Julie Slagle upon
winning second place at the National Sweet
Sorghum Producers and Processors
Association's annual Culinary Contest.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those Tennesseans who, through dedication to their craft and preservation of traditional
practices, bring distinction to this State; and
WHEREAS, Julie Slagle of Unicoi County has distinguished herself through her skill in
the time-honored art of sorghum syrup production; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Slagle was awarded second place in the culinary category at the
National Sweet Sorghum Producers and Processors Association's fortieth annual conference
and national contest held March 4-6 in Pigeon Forge, earning national recognition for her
standout sorghum ice cream; and
WHEREAS, the daughter of first-place winner Marlin Hensley, Julie Slagle is part of a
four-generation family farm deeply rooted in the community, where traditional methods of
sorghum production have been preserved and practiced across generations; and
WHEREAS, representing the next generation of this proud family tradition, Ms. Slagle
carries forward the Hensley Hills Farm legacy while maintaining its strong and enduring roots;
and
WHEREAS, Julie Slagle's achievement is a testament to her commitment to excellence
and her role in sustaining Tennessee's rich agricultural heritage; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Slagle's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of our respect,
admiration, and commendation; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Julie Slagle upon
winning second place at the National Sweet Sorghum Producers and Processors Association's
annual Culinary Contest and extend to her our best wishes for continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
