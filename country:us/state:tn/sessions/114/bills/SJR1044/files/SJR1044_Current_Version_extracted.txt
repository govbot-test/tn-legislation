SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1044
By Haile
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Westmoreland High
School robotics team.
WHEREAS, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize those outstanding and gifted
students of our State who bring honor to Tennessee as members of academic teams and who
act as ambassadors for our State through successful academic competition; and
WHEREAS, students Andrew Fowler and Hayden Eakle and advisor Joey Garrison of
the Westmoreland High School robotics team are especially deserving of acclaim, having
recently met with great success and qualifying for the VEX Robotics World Championship in St.
Louis, Missouri, on April 21-24, 2026; and
WHEREAS, the Westmoreland team has the distinction of being the only Sumner
County high school team to qualify for world competition this year; and
WHEREAS, the hard work and dedication of the team have been evident throughout the
year at every level of competition and surely bode well for their future competitive endeavors
later this year; and
WHEREAS, the noteworthy achievement of the members of the Westmoreland High
School robotics team is a tremendous source of pride for their community and the State of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, the robotics team members' dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of
the respect, admiration, and commendation of this General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend students Andrew Fowler
and Hayden Eakle and advisor Joey Garrison of the Westmoreland High School robotics team
