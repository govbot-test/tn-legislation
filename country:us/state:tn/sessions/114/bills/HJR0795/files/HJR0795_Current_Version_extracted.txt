HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 795
By Terry
A RESOLUTION to celebrate "National Blood Donor Month" in the
State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, more than fifty-five years ago, the month of January was designated as
"National Blood Donor Month," as an annual national observance to honor voluntary blood
donors and encourage more people to donate blood at a time when blood supplies are
historically low; and
WHEREAS, blood donations are the backbone of Tennessee health care, as every two
seconds, someone needs a blood transfusion. Blood cannot be manufactured and is only made
possible by generous donors who roll up their sleeves to help save lives; and
WHEREAS, the population of the State of Tennessee is aging, and not enough new
donors are stepping up to take their place. Only about three percent of the age-eligible
population donates blood, but patient needs are constant; and
WHEREAS, type O blood is often used in trauma situations, making it the most needed
blood type and most likely to be in short supply during a blood emergency; and
WHEREAS, patients requiring blood transfusions include cancer patients, accident,
burn, and trauma victims, newborn babies and their mothers, transplant recipients, surgery
patients, chronically transfused patients suffering from sickle cell disease or thalassemia, and
many more; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we join in the celebration of the month of January 2026 as National Blood
Donor Month in the State of Tennessee, as we recognize the lifesaving contributions of
volunteer blood donors.
