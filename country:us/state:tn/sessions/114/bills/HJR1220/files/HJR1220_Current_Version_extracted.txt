HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1220
By McKenzie
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Juanita Usher Cannon of
Knoxville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Juanita Usher Cannon of Knoxville; and
WHEREAS, a dedicated educator and community leader, Juanita Cannon was an
exemplary public servant and consummate professional who worked assiduously to improve the
quality of life for her fellow citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, a 1958 graduate of Austin High School, Ms. Cannon earned degrees from
Knoxville College and the University of Tennessee, Knoxville; she was a member of Alpha
Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc., and in 2011, she was named a Golden Soror in honor of fifty years
of active membership within the organization; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cannon was a professional educator of nearly forty years and always
sought the best for her students and her community; she cared deeply for her students and was
known for expressing her compassion, showing respect, and building relationships with her
colleagues and students; and
WHEREAS, Juanita Cannon was proud to teach at Austin-East Magnet High School
from 1969 to 1989; during that time, she taught health, physical education, and tap dancing, and
she coached volleyball and cheer; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cannon became principal of Vine Middle Magnet School in 1994 and
further served her community as principal of the Transition School and vice principal of Spring
Hill and Holston middle schools; and
HJR1220
016005
- 1 -
WHEREAS, a renowned leader in her community and her profession, Juanita Cannon
was the first African-American president of the Knoxville Education Association and served as a
member of the Tennessee Education Association board of directors; and
WHEREAS, she was a member of Knoxville's Community Development Corporation for
eighteen years; her work with the board, which ended in 2012, included service as chair and
redeveloping Mechanicsville through the Hope VI project; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and recognition, Ms. Cannon was the recipient of the
Dewey W. Roberts human relations award; in 2013, a street near Paul Hogue Park was named
in her honor, a most befitting recognition that celebrates her civic service and years of
community involvement; and
WHEREAS, Juanita Cannon was also wholly devoted to her faith and was an active
member of Mount Olive Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cannon lived her life filled with love and selfless service, and she
greatly impacted the lives of those who were blessed to know her with her guidance and
mentorship; and
WHEREAS, Juanita Cannon leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Juanita Usher Cannon, reflecting fondly upon her
impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
- 2 - 016005
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
