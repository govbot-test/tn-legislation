HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1560
By Salinas
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate the Overton Park
Shell on the celebration of its ninetieth
anniversary.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those legendary Tennessee landmarks that serve as bastions of culture and history; and
WHEREAS, built in 1936 through a partnership between the City of Memphis and the
Works Progress Administration (WPA) as part of the New Deal, the Overton Park Shell has long
served the Memphis community as a beloved open-air amphitheater and museum within
Overton Park; and
WHEREAS, designed by architect Max Furbringer, the Overton Park Shell is one of only
a few of the original twenty-seven WPA band shells, making it a rare and enduring piece of
Memphis and Tennessee history; and
WHEREAS, during the 1930s and 1940s, the Overton Park Shell became a vibrant
cultural hub, hosting Memphis Open Air Theater orchestral concerts, as well as various light
opera performances and musicals; and
WHEREAS, the most celebrated moment in the Shell's history came on July 30, 1954,
when a young Elvis Presley opened for headliner Slim Whitman in his first paid concert
performance, an event regarded by music historians as the first-ever rock and roll show; and
WHEREAS, in the 1960s, the Shell was transferred to the Memphis Arts Center with
plans for demolition; a preservation campaign led by Noel Gilbert, longtime conductor of the
Memphis Concert Orchestra, gained public support and ultimately saved the Overton Park Shell
from destruction; and
WHEREAS, in 1986, a group of dedicated private citizens founded Save Our Shell, Inc.,
an organization whose efforts brought hundreds of free public programs to the venue; and

WHEREAS, in 2007, the Shell was renamed the Levitt Shell following a major renovation
made possible through the generous support of the Levitt Foundation; on March 3, 2022, the
venue was restored to its historic name, the Overton Park Shell, reaffirming its mission of
bringing free music under the stars to the entire Memphis community; and
WHEREAS, the Overton Park Shell continues to welcome audiences of all backgrounds,
shaping the city's cultural fabric and demonstrating the enduring power of live performances;
and
WHEREAS, the Overton Park Shell has remained a true cultural treasure of this State
for nine decades, and it is most fitting that we specially recognize its dedicated staff and
invaluable supporters on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate the Overton Park Shell on the occasion of its
ninetieth anniversary, extending our gratitude for its many contributions to Memphis and this
State and our best wishes for much continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
