HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 804
By Faison
A RESOLUTION urging the Secretary of State to include The Old
Men: The conversations that help boys become
men by T.L. Jones in the Tennessee Blue Book.
WHEREAS, one of the foundational principles of the Appalachian people is a fierce
respect for their elders, who are the patriarchs and matriarchs of their families and whose words
carry the weight of generations; and
WHEREAS, the importance of sharing and receiving generational wisdom remains
paramount to the people of Appalachia today; and
WHEREAS, sadly, the longstanding ways of Appalachia are quickly fading in these
contemporary times; however, an insider's view of traditional Appalachian culture and values
can be gleaned from The Old Men: The conversations that help boys become men by T.L.
Jones (2024); and
WHEREAS, The Old Men transports the reader into the early life of an Appalachian boy,
born in 1970, who was influenced by the thinking and the words of the old men of Appalachia
and a few others, as he later wandered outside "the land of the blue smoke"; and
WHEREAS, the eighty-four essays contained in this book, as its title suggests, represent
the conversations that help boys become men; although the book is firmly rooted in Appalachia,
the principle of passing wisdom from old men to boys is universal and timeless; and
WHEREAS, in The Old Men, Mr. Jones relates many exciting adventures and provides
keen insight into the mountain wisdom that he learned at the knees of his elders, including
essays about Faith in God, Equipment Maintenance, Bar Fights, Murder, Church, Respecting
Elders, Bear Hunting, Trout Fishing, Poverty, Hard Work, Grace, and Salvation; and

WHEREAS, a native son of East Tennessee, T.L. Jones spent much of his boyhood in
the company of old men, farming, hunting, fishing, and "loafering" with them in the mountains of
Appalachia; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jones became a Christian at the age of eighteen and was called to the
ministry in 1990; while spreading the good news of the Gospel, he planted and pastored two
churches, one in Whitehall, Montana, and the other in Greeneville; and
WHEREAS, T.L. Jones has expertly crafted a work that brings to the life the everyday
experiences, unique culture, and time-honored values of the people of Appalachia, the high
esteem in which they hold their elders, and the great importance they place upon generational
wisdom; and
WHEREAS, the Secretary of State publishes the Tennessee Blue Book biennially for the
edification and enjoyment of all Tennesseans; Section VI, which focuses on the State of
Tennessee's history and heritage, includes a chapter on "State Arts and Crafts"; and
WHEREAS, given the book's cultural and historical significance and its impact on the
lives of so many Tennesseans, it is fitting that the Secretary of State should recognize The Old
Men in the Tennessee Blue Book; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby urge the Secretary of State to include reference to The Old
Men: The conversations that help boys become men by T.L. Jones in the Tennessee Blue
Book, along with an appropriate explanation of the book's subject matter and its significance in
illustrating and illuminating the history and culture of Appalachia.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an enrolled copy of this resolution be transmitted to
the Secretary of State.
