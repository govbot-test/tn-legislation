HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1290
By Martin B
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Barry and Connie
Martin on the celebration of their fiftieth wedding
anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those outstanding citizens who have demonstrated uncommon devotion to
family, faith, and community; and
WHEREAS, Barry and Connie Martin will celebrate their golden wedding anniversary on
August 27, 2026, a milestone that stands as a testament to enduring love, commitment, and
steadfast partnership; and
WHEREAS, Barry Martin and the former Connie Cummings were united in holy
matrimony on August 27, 1976, after first meeting as students at their alma mater, Bethel
College; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Martin, a native of Jeffersontown, Kentucky, and Mr. Martin, who grew
up in Nashville, have made their home in Camden for more than forty years, where they have
been pillars of their community; and
WHEREAS, together, Barry and Connie Martin devoted forty years of their lives to the
field of education, faithfully serving as teachers at Trezevant High School, Big Sandy High
School, and Camden Central High School, positively impacting countless students and families
along the way; and
WHEREAS, their greatest joy has been their family, beginning with the birth of their
children: Tony Eugene Martin, born March 10, 1981; William Brock Martin, born October 4,
1984; Anna Marie Martin, born July 31, 1987; and Paige Nicole Martin, born November 8, 1990;

WHEREAS, Barry and Connie Martin instilled in their children the values of faith,
discipline, and togetherness, ensuring that family remained a top priority through shared meals,
active involvement in sports, and faithful attendance at church; and
WHEREAS, these strong family traditions have been carried on by their children, all of
whom are now married, and by their eleven beloved grandchildren: Macee, Lane, Kamden,
Steele, Adalyn, Cannon, Callaway, Palmer, Storie, Camryn, and Stockton; and
WHEREAS, through their unwavering commitment to one another, their faith, and their
family, Barry and Connie Martin have established a lasting legacy and a model for future
generations to follow; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Barry and Connie Martin on the occasion of
their fiftieth wedding anniversary, commend them for their many years of service to education
and their community, and wish them continued happiness and fulfillment in the years to come.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
