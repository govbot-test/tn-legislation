SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 809
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to recognize March 16-17, 2026, as AARP
Tennessee Day on the Hill.
WHEREAS, in furtherance of its mission to empower individuals fifty-plus-years-old to
choose how they live as they age, AARP advocates for its members on critical priorities, such
as strengthening caregiving, protecting seniors from fraud, and ensuring all Americans have
independence and choice as they age; and
WHEREAS, AARP Tennessee serves as a one-stop resource for information on the
State's fifty-plus population and offers programs and tools that help Tennesseans make the best
decisions about their health and financial security; and
WHEREAS, in addition to providing information for its members, AARP Tennessee has
fought for the passage of legislation protecting its 670,000 members, including the creation of
the Tennessee Department of Disability and Aging; and
WHEREAS, most of AARP's members want to stay in their own homes and communities
for as long as possible, and to that end, AARP helps Tennessee cities, towns, and counties
become great places to live for those of all ages through its Network of Age Friendly States and
Communities; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that March 16-17, 2026, is recognized as AARP
Tennessee Day on the Hill. We extend our best wishes for continued success in AARP's very
important mission of improving the lives of fifty-plus-years-old citizens and their families
throughout Tennessee.
