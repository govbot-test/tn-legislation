SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1103
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Victoria Niederhauser,
Dean of the University of Tennessee, Knoxville,
College of Nursing on the occasion of her
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pay tribute to
those medical professionals, educators, and administrators who have wholly devoted
themselves to serving others and thereby contribute significantly to the well-being of their fellow
citizens; and
WHEREAS, Victoria Niederhauser, Dean of the College of Nursing at the University of
Tennessee, Knoxville (UT), is taking her well-deserved retirement at the end of the 2025-2026
academic year after fifteen years of visionary leadership that transformed the college into a
nationally recognized leader in nursing education, research, and practice; and
WHEREAS, Dean Niederhauser, DrPH, RN, PPCNP-BC, FAAN, was appointed to serve
as the fourth dean of UT's College of Nursing in 2011, and since that time, she has led the
college through a period of extraordinary growth and innovation; and
WHEREAS, during her tenure, enrollment has increased by more than 160 percent, and
licensure pass rates have outpaced state and national averages, currently standing at a first-
time NCLEX pass rate of 97.3 percent; UT College of Nursing's rankings have improved, with
the Bachelor of Science in Nursing program and the Doctor of Nursing Practice program landing
at No. 19 and No. 22, respectively, according the 2024/2025 U.S. News & World Report
rankings; and
WHEREAS, Dean Niederhauser's advocacy for a new home of Vol Nursing, including
leadership through appropriations process, design, and philanthropy, resulted in the August
2025 dedication of the UT Medical Nursing Building; on the whole, under her leadership, the

WHEREAS, over the last fifteen years, she has helped grow a team of top nursing

research that advances healthcare delivery (Precision Health, Telehealth, Simulation), bio

WHEREAS, Dean Niederhauser has also built strong relationships with regional
healthcare institutions that have not only resulted in unparalleled clinical learning opportunities
for students but have also transformed into scholarship-generating ventures through the BSN
Scholars Program (UT Medical Center) and Pediatric Registered Nurse Program (Dolly Parton
Children's Hospital); these innovative academic-practice partnerships add more than 100 BSN
graduates to Knoxville's hospital workforce, creating a pipeline that addresses a critical nurse
shortage; and
WHEREAS, a respected leader among her peers in academia and health care, her
professional tenure includes service with various national organizations, including the Beryl
Institute, American Association of Colleges of Nursing (Board Director-at-Large, 2020-2024),
Robert Wood Johnson Nurse Executive Fellows Alumni Association, Friends of the National
Institute of Nursing Research (President, 2023-2025), and American Academy of Nursing; and
WHEREAS, Dean Niederhauser has also devoted herself to serving the Knoxville and
University communities, serving with the Dolly Parton Children's Hospital Board of Directors
(current Vice Chair), Leadership Knoxville (2014), Leadership Tennessee (2019), and University
of Tennessee Leadership Institute (2017); and

WHEREAS, in 2015, she was named a Fellow of the American Academy of Nursing, an
organization representing the most accomplished nursing leaders in policy, research, practice,
and education; and
WHEREAS, Victoria Niederhauser is a board-certified pediatric nurse practitioner and
the author of more than sixty-five articles and book chapters on child health and educational
issues, as well as the book Core Review for Pediatric Primary Care Nurse Practitioners; and
WHEREAS, her career has been defined by innovation, compassion, and her
unwavering commitment to advancing the nursing profession, and the focus of her scholarly
work has been child and adolescent Health Promotion and Disease Prevention, with an
emphasis on immunizations; her funding for nursing research and training grants totals over $16
million; and
WHEREAS, throughout her many worthy endeavors, Dean Niederhauser has enjoyed
the love and support of her husband, Stewart McCorkle; children, Travis (Cory) Niederhauser,
Matt (Tracey) Niederhauser, Emily (Myles) Niederhauser Lund; stepchildren, Parker McCorkle
and Taylor McCorkle; and grandchildren, Grayson, Griffin, Kea, Noa, Lachlan, and Maren; and
WHEREAS, Dean Niederhauser has been a tireless advocate for students, faculty, and
the nursing profession; her legacy will continue to inspire others as they build upon the solid
foundation she helped create; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we recognize Victoria Niederhauser for her countless
and indelible contributions to UT's College of Nursing on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we extend to Dean Victoria Niederhauser our
heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement, applaud her dedication to the nursing
