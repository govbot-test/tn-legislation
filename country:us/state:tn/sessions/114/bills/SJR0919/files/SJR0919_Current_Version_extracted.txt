SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 919
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Richard "Pierce" Dodson
of Wilson County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Richard "Pierce" Dodson of Wilson County; and
WHEREAS, Pierce Dodson was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Lebanon on July 30, 1948, Mr. Dodson was the son of Jewel Davis
Dodson and Pierce William Dodson; he graduated as Salutatorian from Castle Heights Military
Academy in 1966 and earned his Master of Divinity degree from Southern Baptist Theological
Seminary in 1978; and
WHEREAS, he met and married his beloved wife, Barbara Ann Gallman, in 1969, and
the two enjoyed fifty-six years of love and companionship; and
WHEREAS, Pierce Dodson preached the Gospel of Jesus Christ for forty years, faithfully
serving as a pastor throughout Kentucky and Tennessee; his ministry was marked with a deep
passion for missions; and
WHEREAS, in addition to traveling to many countries to preach and teach the Gospel,
he served as an adult education teacher at the Wilson County Adult Learning Center, where he
dedicated himself to helping others continue their education; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Dodson is survived by his siblings, Patsy Dodson Plumlee and Bill
(Carol) Dodson; children, Jonathan (Heather) Dodson and Rachel (Josh) McWaters; and
grandchildren, Kaden, Kolton, Chayton, Jaxon, and Kynlee; and

WHEREAS, Pierce Dodson dedicated his life to ministry, his family, and serving others,
and he was a blessing to all who were fortunate to know him; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Richard "Pierce" Dodson,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family, friends, and colleagues of Mr. Dodson.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
