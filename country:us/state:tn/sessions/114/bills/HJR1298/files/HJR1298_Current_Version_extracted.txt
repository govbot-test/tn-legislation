HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1298
By Crawford
A RESOLUTION to commend Seth Koenes for exemplary service
as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Seth Koenes is one such individual, who has distinguished himself in his
role as a legislative intern for Representative John Crawford; Mr. Koenes's work has resulted in
innumerable benefits for the legislators he assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at Austin Peay State University, where he is highly regarded by
his peers, Seth Koenes has achieved remarkable academic success; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the myriad aspects and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Mr. Koenes brought his many abilities
and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth
General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Seth Koenes has played an
instrumental role in supporting operations for the House State and Local Committee, has
provided exceptional constituent services, and has distinguished himself as a professional and a
joy to have in the office; he is a bright-shining star and a great sign of hope for future
generations; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Koenes has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight and
rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
HJR1298
017371
- 1 -
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Seth Koenes for outstanding service as a legislative
intern for Representative John Crawford and extend to him our best wishes for every future
success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 2 - 017371
