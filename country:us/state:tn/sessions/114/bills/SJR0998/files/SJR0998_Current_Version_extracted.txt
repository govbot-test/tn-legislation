SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 998
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to honor Jan McNally of Oak Ridge for her many
contributions to the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are honored to recognize an
individual who has dedicated herself to serving the needs of this State and its citizens,
committing her time and energies to improving the quality of life for Tennesseans; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Jan McNally, a sterling and accomplished
professional, a respected community leader, and the wife of our colleague Lieutenant Governor
Randy McNally; and
WHEREAS, a highly respected healthcare professional, Jan McNally was director of the
Journey to Excellence, Leadership Development at Covenant Health before taking her much-
deserved retirement; she previously served as the president and chief administrative officer for
Methodist Medical Center of Oak Ridge; and
WHEREAS, a fellow of the American College of Healthcare Executives, Mrs. McNally
serves on the board of directors for Ridgeview Behavioral Services and is a former member of
the University of Tennessee College of Nursing Advisory Board; and
WHEREAS, Jan McNally has made significant contributions to arts and culture in
Tennessee through her dedicated service and visionary leadership as a member of the
Tennessee Arts Commission; and
WHEREAS, first appointed by Governor Bill Haslam in 2017 and reappointed by
Governor Bill Lee for a five-year term in 2024, Mrs. McNally played a pivotal role in supporting
arts and culture in the State during her tenure as two-time chair of the Tennessee Arts
Commission from 2020 to 2022; and

WHEREAS, she spearheaded an innovative $1 million initiative, partnering with nursing
homes across Tennessee, to bring the transformative power of the arts to seniors statewide.
Under her guidance, the agency achieved record funding levels, amplifying the economic impact
of Tennessee's arts and culture sector in all ninety-five counties and ensuring broad support for
creative communities throughout the State; and
WHEREAS, she is a graduate of the J&J Wharton Fellows Program for Nurse
Executives at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania and holds a Bachelor of
Science degree in Biology and Psychology from Mississippi University for Women, a Bachelor
of Science degree in Nursing from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, and a Master of
Health Service Administration degree from the University of St. Francis; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Jan McNally has received the
University of Tennessee College of Nursing Outstanding Alumni Award, the University of
Tennessee College of Nursing Distinguished Alumni Award, the Ned R. McWherter Excellence
Award from the Tennessee Center for Performance Excellence, the Muddy Boot Award (2007)
from ETEC, and the Knoxville Healthcare Heroes Award; and
WHEREAS, a beloved family matriarch, Jan McNally enjoys the love and support of her
devoted husband, Randy, and their two daughters and sons-in-law, Melissa and Rob Stubbs
and Maggie and Doug Kimball; their grandchildren, Haley Stubbs, Trent Stubbs, and Morgan
McNally; and their two great-grandchildren, Malachi Johnson and Jeremiah McNally; and
WHEREAS, Jan McNally is an inspiration to all who know her, and her dedication to her
family and to improving the lives of Tennesseans is worthy of recognition by this General
Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby recognize Jan McNally for her many

contributions, applaud her tireless service to the State of Tennessee and its citizens, and extend
our best wishes for her every future success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
