HOUSE RESOLUTION 195
By Grills
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Stanley Gordon Jones II.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those citizens who, through their
extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community leaders of whom we can all
be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Stanley Gordon Jones II, an exemplary
public servant and consummate professional who works assiduously to improve the quality of
life for his fellow citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jones has been a faithful husband to his wife, Jeanie Jones, for forty-
one years, and an outstanding father to Stanley III, Jeanie Elise, Annie Mae, and Joseph
"Tommy" Presley Thomas, who was named after Gordon's younger brother, who died of
lymphatic cancer at nine years old; and
WHEREAS, a mechanical engineer, Gordon Jones is a graduate of the University of
Tennessee, Knoxville, and has held licenses as a professional engineer in more than ten states;
he has also been licensed to do business in thirty-eight states, in addition to Mexico, Venezuela,
the Cayman Islands, and Canada;
WHEREAS, Mr. Jones has completed projects around the word, including the
construction of a radar unit in Taiwan for the Taiwanese Air Force and a new airport terminal in
New Orleans, and served as president and CEO of the Stanley Jones Corporation, located in
South Fulton; and
WHEREAS, during his professional years, he also worked at the Denver, Colorado,
airport and the Austin, Texas, airport as a mechanical, plumbing, and fire-suppression expert
and SME; and

WHEREAS, a true jack of all trades, Mr. Jones is an avid reader, and he has also been a
licensed single-engine pilot, a NASCAR pit crew member, a Formula Ford race car driver, and
an IHRA drag racer; and
WHEREAS, Gordon Jones was an avid member of the Boy Scouts of America, having
earned his Eagle Scout Award and served as a Cubmaster on the local level; he also served as
president of the Shawnee Trails Executive Council and received the Order of the Arrow and
Silver Beaver Award for his performance at the council level and the Silver Antelope Award for
his performance at the national level; and
WHEREAS, a man of deep faith, Gordon Jones was a member of The Gideons
International for over thirty years and regularly spoke in churches on behalf of the organization;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jones is a devoted member of The People Place Assembly of God in
South Fulton; he has performed with various church orchestras and praise bands for over forty
years, playing the trumpet in the church orchestra in Union City and the drums in the Ministerial
Alliance of the Twin Cities; and
WHEREAS, Gordon Jones also played in the famous Park Terrace Blue Room Supper
Club with The Rhythmaires and was the drummer for The Memory Makers Stage Band, which
performed in assisted living facilities and nursing homes, at private and community events, and
in churches; and
WHEREAS, Gordon Jones epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic
of a true Tennessean, and we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud this extraordinary
human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and
commend Stanley Gordon Jones II on his many contributions to the citizens of this State.
