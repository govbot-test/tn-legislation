HOUSE RESOLUTION 173
By Todd
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Tennessee Century
Farms Program on the occasion of its fiftieth
anniversary.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body take great pride in honoring those
programs and institutions that preserve and promote the rich history and heritage of our State;
and
WHEREAS, agriculture has long stood at the heart of Tennessee's economy, culture,
and identity, shaping communities in every county and sustaining generations of families; and
WHEREAS, established in 1975, the Tennessee Century Farms Program was created to
recognize farms that have remained in continuous operation by the same family for 100 years or
more, thereby honoring the perseverance, stewardship, and dedications of Tennessee farm
families; and
WHEREAS, the program has certified more than 2,300 farms, with at least one Century
Farm located in every Tennessee county, reflecting the enduring strength and widespread
legacy of family farming across the Volunteer State; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Century Farms Program works in partnership with the
Department of Agriculture to ensure that these farm families receive formal recognition for their
longstanding contributions to Tennessee agriculture; and
WHEREAS, under the distinguished leadership of Dr. Carroll Van West at the Center for
Historic Preservation at Middle Tennessee State University (MTSU), the Tennessee Century
Farms Program has become a nationally respected model for rural preservation; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Century Farms Program has strengthened rural
preservation efforts, encouraged further historic recognition through National Register listings,

Dr. Carroll Van West and the many farm families whose dedication has preserved our State's
proud agricultural legacy.
