HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 738
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Victor Joseph Ankar of
Chattanooga.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Victor Joseph Ankar; and
WHEREAS, Victor Ankar was a beloved friend and neighbor who did his utmost to
improve the well-being of all who were blessed to know him; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Ankar was born on December 29, 1938, in Ramallah, Palestine, and
was one of eight children; and
WHEREAS, in 1961, Victor Ankar made the decision to immigrate to the United States
with nothing to his name but the determination to create a life full of opportunity; and
WHEREAS, with very little, Mr. Ankar started selling merchandise out of the trunk of his
car before opening his first convenience store in 1967; and
WHEREAS, in 1973, Victor Ankar founded Ankar's Billiards, which recently celebrated its
fifty-second year in business; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Ankar was kind and served many friends, neighbors, and even
strangers, never asking for anything in return; and
WHEREAS, a man with many passions, Victor Ankar found great happiness in
gardening and cultivating his prized fig trees; he often hand delivered a fresh harvest of figs, a
simple, heartfelt gesture that reflected his generosity; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Ankar's family was the center of his life, and his every action was rooted
in his desire to provide for them; and

WHEREAS, Victor Ankar is survived by his four children, Charley (Misa) Ankar, Ronny
(Jenna) Ankar, Bobby (Kailey) Ankar, and Christina Ankar; six grandchildren, Arianna, Charley,
Sloane, Reese, Benjamin, and Luke; as well as countless friends and his community; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his beloved wife, Angela Ankar; and
WHEREAS, throughout his many worthy endeavors, Victor Ankar distinguished himself
as a beacon of kindness, humility, and wisdom; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to the bountiful life of this exceptional
Tennessean; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Victor Joseph Ankar, reflecting fondly upon his
ability to live the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and condolences to the
family and friends of Mr. Ankar.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
