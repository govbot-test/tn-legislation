HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1649
By Butler
A RESOLUTION to honor Coach Lamar Rogers on the occasion
of his retirement.
WHEREAS, the members of the General Assembly take great pleasure in paying tribute
to one of the greatest high school basketball coaches of all time, who has coached his teams to
more than a thousand victories over his fifty-year career at Clarkrange High School; and
WHEREAS, for five decades, Coach Lamar Rogers has been the heart and soul of the
Clarkrange High School girls' basketball program and guided the Lady Buffaloes to
unprecedented heights of achievement; and
WHEREAS, the winningest girls' basketball coach in TSSAA history, Coach Rogers
recently announced his retirement after amassing an unrivaled record of 1,364-350 for his
career; and
WHEREAS, during his illustrious career as head coach of the Lady Buffaloes, Lamar
Rogers has led Clarkrange to eight state championships, three state runner-up finishes, twenty-
six state tournament appearances, and forty-nine consecutive winning seasons; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Coach Rogers has been inducted
into the TSSAA Hall of Fame, the NFHS Hall of Fame, the BCAT Hall of Fame, and the
Fentress County Boys & Girls Club Hall of Fame and has been the recipient of the John
Wooden Legacy Award, the Fred Russell Lifetime Achievement Award, and the Jim and Nancy
Cartwright Legacy Award; and
WHEREAS, Coach Rogers has had the privilege of coaching fifty-two scholarship
athletes, thirty-six All-State players, four Miss Basketball finalists, two Miss Basketball award
winners, and four honorable mention All-Americans; and
WHEREAS, over the course of fifty years, Lamar Rogers has earned the respect and
admiration of his colleagues and sports fans across the State as a great basketball coach,
