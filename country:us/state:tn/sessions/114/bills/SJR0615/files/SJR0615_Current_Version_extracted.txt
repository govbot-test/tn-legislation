SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 615
By Yarbro
A RESOLUTION to honor Nashville SC, 2025 Lamar Hunt U.S.
Open Cup champions.
WHEREAS, Nashville SC, one of the most beloved and dynamic soccer teams in this
country, claimed its first-ever tournament trophy by winning the 2025 Lamar Hunt U.S. Open
Cup championship, the nation's oldest and longest-running soccer tournament; and
WHEREAS, by defeating Austin FC 2-1 on October 1, 2025, Nashville SC not only won
its first trophy but also became the first professional sports team in the State of Tennessee to
win a national championship; and
WHEREAS, this victory marked a historic triumph for Nashville SC and the city itself. A
dozen years after a scrappy, supporter-owned amateur side first took the field at Vanderbilt's
lacrosse venue, Nashville SC—a different iteration of that same club—claimed American
soccer's most storied trophy, crowning more than a decade of achievements; and
WHEREAS, as the 2025 Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup champions, Nashville SC has now
qualified to compete in the prestigious 2026 CONCACAF Champions Cup and will face
championship teams from across CONCACAF in the tournament; and
WHEREAS, Nashville's path to glory saw the team defeat Chattanooga Red Wolves SC
(1-0), Orlando (3-2), D.C. United (5-2), Philadelphia (3-1), before downing Austin FC (2-1) in
Texas behind an early goal from 2022 MLS MVP Hany Mukhtar and a tremendous penalty kick
by the team's leading scorer, Sam Surridge; and
WHEREAS, Hany Mukhtar and Sam Surridge once again delivered when it mattered
most, each finding the net for Nashville; the match belonged less to individual brilliance than to
collective determination—a squad-wide refusal to be denied. From the first whistle to the final

the 2025 Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup, thank the team's players, coaches, and staff for their
tremendous performance on the pitch, and extend to them our best wishes for many more years
of continued success and tournament victories.
