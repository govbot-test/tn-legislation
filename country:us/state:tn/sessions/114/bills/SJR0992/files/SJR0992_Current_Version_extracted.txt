SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 992
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to commemorate May 1, 2026, as "Do
Something Tennessee Day" in Tennessee.
WHEREAS, Do Something Tennessee (DSTN) Day is a statewide day of action
designed to mobilize youth, families, schools, and community organizations to participate in
meaningful volunteer service; and
WHEREAS, rooted in the social impact initiative "It Takes a Child to Raise a Village,"
DSTN reframes volunteerism as both a youth-led and community-wide responsibility; and
WHEREAS, by aligning Tennessee's inaugural Do Something Day with national service
movements, DSTN will position the State as a leader in youth civic engagement and service-
culture engagement; and
WHEREAS, DSTN is being spearheaded by Miss Knoxville Meghan Gadsden; through
her initiative, she will activate service opportunities across the State, including the major
Tennessee cities of Memphis, Nashville, Knoxville, and Chattanooga, and will engage schools
and youth-serving organizations in coordinated service efforts; and
WHEREAS, DSTN is part of a broader vision to strengthen youth volunteerism
infrastructure across the State and nation, including a visit to Washington, D.C., to begin policy
advocacy conversations regarding incentivizing volunteerism in K-12 education; and
WHEREAS, it is most appropriate that we applaud those citizens and entities across the
State who are focused on increasing youth engagement in volunteerism and community service;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commemorate May 1, 2026, as "Do Something
