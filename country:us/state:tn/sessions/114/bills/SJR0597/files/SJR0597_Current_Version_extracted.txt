SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 597
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to recognize and commend Beverly Bell.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should honor those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have devoted themselves to improving the lives
of others; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy individual is Beverly Bell of Lebanon, who is the
founder and director of Optimum Hope!, a nonprofit wellness recovery program specifically
designed for individuals living with Parkinson's disease; and
WHEREAS, Beverly Bell graduated from Pensacola Junior College in 1991 with an
Associate of Arts degree as a physical therapy assistant; and
WHEREAS, moving to Tennessee soon thereafter, Mrs. Bell embarked on a
distinguished thirty-one-year career as a physical therapist assistant, working in home health,
rehabilitation, and clinical settings and specializing in the treatment and care of patients with
Parkinson's disease; and
WHEREAS, upon nearing retirement, she recognized a serious gap in continued therapy
options for Parkinson's patients in Wilson County; refusing to let that need go unmet, she
founded Optimum Hope! in 2016 with colleague Michele Lineberry under the auspices of The
Therapy Gym; and
WHEREAS, beginning her endeavor with only five members and no salary, Beverly Bell
poured her time, talent, and passion into building Optimum Hope! into a thriving nonprofit
organization that currently serves more than sixty-five participants; and
WHEREAS, she has taught up to six classes each Monday, Wednesday, and Friday at
the Mount Juliet location, conducted additional classes at the Jimmy Floyd Center in Lebanon

and Del Webb in Mount Juliet, and met privately with clients throughout the week—all with
minimal or no compensation during most of the past eight years; and
WHEREAS, Optimum Hope! has grown to include professional staff, including a
personal trainer and an occupational therapist, and is supported through grants from the
Parkinson's Foundation, scholarships from Peterson's Parkinson's in Nashville, and the
dedication of its members; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Bell holds numerous specialized certifications, including as a PWR!
(Parkinson's Wellness Recovery) Move Instructor and in Lee Silverman Voice Treatment, Rock
Steady Boxing, lymphedema, and neuro-progressive therapy, demonstrating her exceptional
expertise and steadfast dedication to evidence-based care; and
WHEREAS, she also supports and teaches at the Mount Juliet Parkinson's Support
Group and participates annually in Moving Day Nashville, the statewide Parkinson's Foundation
event that raises awareness and celebrates the power of movement in managing the disease;
and
WHEREAS, through Optimum Hope! and her tireless volunteerism, Beverly Bell has
inspired countless Tennesseans affected by Parkinson's disease to persevere, rebuild
confidence, and celebrate every victory, large or small; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to pause in our deliberations to acknowledge and
applaud Mrs. Bell for her dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Beverly Bell, Founder and
Director of Optimum Hope!, for her dedicated service to Tennesseans living with Parkinson's
disease and extend to her our best wishes for continued success and happiness in all her future
endeavors.
