SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1184
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Marshall McKamey of
LaFollette.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Marshall McKamey of LaFollette; and
WHEREAS, Marshall McKamey was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. McKamey was born on August 20, 1939, in Clinchmore to Virgil
McKamey and Annie Lou Hutson McKamey; and
WHEREAS, after more than thirty years of service, Marshall McKamey retired from Cain
Ford; he also served on the Campbell County Election Commission for thirty years; and
WHEREAS, Mr. McKamey was devoted to his family and always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Marshall McKamey is survived by his wife of sixty-three years, Linda
McCarty McKamey; daughters, Bridget (Ken) Sloane and Bettina McKamey; grandchildren,
Catherine "Callie" (Zach) Marshall, Connor (Jordan) Worden, and Bria McKamey; and sister,
Evelyn (Charles) Foutch; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his son, Marshall
Brian McKamey, and sister, Barbara Hatmaker; and
WHEREAS, Marshall McKamey leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity
in his public life, compassion and loyalty in his private life, and diligence and dedication in all his
chosen endeavors; and

WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Marshall McKamey,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. McKamey.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
