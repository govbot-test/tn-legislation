SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 579
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Marzie Thomas of
Memphis.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Marzie Thomas of
Memphis, the former publisher of The Tri-State Defender and public servant, a community
leader who was greatly admired and respected by all; and
WHEREAS, Marzie Thomas spent more than sixteen years at The Tri-State Defender,
where she rose from advertising manager to general manager, editor, and eventually publisher,
becoming one of the few Black women in that role at the time; and
WHEREAS, her leadership helped sustain the historic Black newspaper through
turbulent years of transition, financial hardship, and evolving media landscapes; she had a
rapport with the community, the advertisers, and the churches; and
WHEREAS, after leaving The Tri-State Defender, she worked briefly with Art Gilliam's
WLOK Radio before she joined the office of U.S. Representative Steve Cohen (D-Tenn.) as a
special assistant for veterans' affairs and community outreach in 2007; and
WHEREAS, an extremely talented administrator and outstanding public servant, Marzie
Thomas understood the district and worked tirelessly to meet the needs of Congressman
Cohen's constituents; and
WHEREAS, her outstanding efforts and dedication to the people of Memphis and Shelby
County led to her being named Congressman Cohen's deputy district director in 2009 and
promoted to district director in 2013, rightfully serving as his top aide in the Congressional
district; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of Booker T. Washington High School and LeMoyne-Owen
College, Marzie Thomas was a lifelong member of East Trigg Avenue Baptist Church where she

sang as a soloist in the choir and grew up under the spiritual mentorship of the legendary
Reverend Dr. Herbert Brewster, a noted gospel composer; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Thomas was deeply devoted to her family, and she always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, a beloved family matriarch, Marzie Thomas is survived by her two
daughters, Marthel Ellison and Richara Thomas; her sister, Aimee Gates; and her
granddaughter, Madison Ellison; and
WHEREAS, she was preceded in death by her husband, Alonzo Thomas, Jr., and her
brother, the Reverend Richard Gates; and
WHEREAS, Marzie Thomas leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Marzie Thomas of
Memphis, reflecting fondly upon her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living
the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and extend our
condolences to the family of Mrs. Thomas.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
