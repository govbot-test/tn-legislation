HOUSE RESOLUTION 144
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to urge the United States Department of Justice
and the federal government to immediately
comply with the law and release the remaining
Jeffrey Epstein files in the interest of justice,
transparency, and the protection of women and
children.
WHEREAS, the sexual exploitation and abuse of minors perpetrated by Jeffrey Epstein
represents one of the most disturbing failures of accountability in modern American history; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee has a long and proud tradition of demanding accountability from
government, insisting on the rule of law, and standing with victims of crime rather than powerful
abusers; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee General Assembly has repeatedly taken action to
strengthen penalties for human trafficking, sexual exploitation, and abuse of children, and has
affirmed that protecting vulnerable Tennesseans is a core responsibility of government; and
WHEREAS, survivors, their families, and the American public have called for full
transparency regarding individuals who participated in, enabled, or benefited from Epstein's
criminal enterprise; and
WHEREAS, federal law and court orders require the timely release of records related to
the Epstein investigation, including flight logs, communications, financial records, and the
identities of individuals connected to these crimes; and
WHEREAS, the deadline for the release of these files has passed, yet the United States
Department of Justice has failed to fully comply, placing the Department in violation of the law
and undermining public confidence in our justice system; and
HR0144
012121

WHEREAS, legislation in Congress to require disclosure of the Epstein files has
bipartisan sponsors and bipartisan support, demonstrating that justice, transparency, and
accountability transcend party affiliation; and
WHEREAS, Tennesseans expect equal justice under the law and reject a system in
which wealth, power, or political connections provide protection from accountability; and
WHEREAS, members of the Tennessee General Assembly—particularly those in the
Republican supermajority—frequently emphasize their commitment to protecting women and
children, and that commitment must be reflected not only in rhetoric but in action, including
demanding transparency in cases of sexual exploitation; and
WHEREAS, continued secrecy surrounding these records deepens harm to survivors,
erodes trust in government, and contradicts the values of fairness, decency, and accountability
that Tennesseans expect from public institutions; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby urge
the United States Department of Justice and the federal government to immediately release all
remaining Jeffrey Epstein files, as required by law.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we condemn the failure of the Department of Justice
to meet the legal deadline for disclosure and calls for immediate compliance without further
delay.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we affirm that transparency and accountability in
cases involving sexual exploitation are essential to protecting women and children and to
honoring the values long upheld by the State of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we urge Tennessee's congressional delegation to
support bipartisan efforts in Congress to compel the release of these records and to ensure
justice for survivors.
- 2 - 012121
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that a certified copy of this resolution be transmitted to
the President of the United States, the Attorney General of the United States, the Speaker and
the Clerk of the United States House of Representatives, the President and the Secretary of the
United States Senate, and each member of Tennessee's Congressional delegation.
