HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 939
By Littleton
A RESOLUTION commending Taiwan for its deepening relations
with the United States and the State of
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the United States of America and Taiwan are long-standing partners that
share and cherish the values of democracy, peace, and prosperity, and the State of Tennessee
commends Taiwan for pursuing "Integrated Diplomacy" and advancing its "Three Chains"
Strategy, grounded in shared values, strong partnerships, and economic vitality, which, through
the strategic frameworks of the Global Democratic Value Chain, the First Island Chain in the
Indo-Pacific, and the Non-Red Supply Chain, strengthen ties with like-minded nations,
safeguard regional security, and leverage Taiwan's technological and economic achievements
to promote mutually beneficial partnerships in the Indo-Pacific region; and
WHEREAS, the year 2026 marks both the 250th anniversary of the independence of the
United States and the thirtieth anniversary of Taiwan's first direct presidential election—
milestones that honor liberty and the principle that sovereignty resides in the people; and
WHEREAS, this shared celebration reaffirms Taiwan-U.S. commitment to the rule of law,
human rights, and a rules-based international order; through close cooperation, Taiwan and the
United States strengthen democratic values and contribute to Indo-Pacific security, economic
resilience, and global peace and stability for future generations; and
WHEREAS, the People's Republic of China has repeatedly engaged in economic
coercion, conducted military exercises directed toward Taiwan, and misinterpreted United
Nations General Assembly Resolution 2758 (XXVI) to obstruct Taiwan's contributions to the
international community, thereby contributing to global challenges to the rules-based
international order, enabling aggressive authoritarian expansionism, and undermining the
progress of free and open societies; and

WHEREAS, Taiwan is the United States' seventh-largest trading partner, and the United
States is Taiwan's second-largest, with bilateral trade-in-goods value estimated at $158.8 billion
in 2024; Taiwan ranked as Tennessee's sixth-largest market and eighteenth-largest export
market in the world in 2024; and
WHEREAS, the first Agreement implemented under the U.S.-Taiwan Initiative on 21st
Century Trade entered into force on December 10, 2024, strengthening the institutional
foundation for deepening the United States-Taiwan economic and trade partnership; and
WHEREAS, building on this framework, the State of Tennessee and Taiwan intend to
work closely to expand mutually beneficial trade, investment, and commercial cooperation, and
to identify and pursue new economic opportunities; and
WHEREAS, in October 2025, Governor Bill Lee led a trade delegation to Taiwan, signed
an Economic Memorandum of Understanding, and announced plans to establish a Tennessee
presence in Taiwan in 2026, thereby promoting two-way trade, investment, and industrial
cooperation through information sharing, business visits, local support, connections to
appropriate contacts and available incentives, briefings, and collaboration in R&D and
technology applications; and
WHEREAS, the State of Tennessee and Taiwan are entering into negotiations on
Memoranda of Understanding concerning K-12 education, higher education, and high-tech
talent education, intending to cultivate professionals with a global vision and strengthen
scientific, educational, economic, social, cultural, and other learning-related programs and
linkages between the Volunteer State and Taiwan in the field of educational cooperation; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commend Taiwan for its deepening relations with the United States and

success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
