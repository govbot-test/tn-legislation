HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 813
By Powers
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Greg Marlow of
LaFollette for his contributions to the State of
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Greg Marlow, a highly respected member of
his community who has dedicated himself to his art and to improving the quality of life for his
fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, born in the mountains of East Tennessee, Mr. Marlow has called LaFollette
home for over two decades; his music ministry began in 1988 with the formation of the
bluegrass gospel band New Harvest, of which he was the lead singer; he also played several
instruments during his thirty-year tenure with the band and traveled throughout the eastern
United States to play at churches, benefits, and major bluegrass festivals; and
WHEREAS, he went on to begin a new musical journey as a solo artist with a new sound
and energy, mixing country, bluegrass, southern rock, and blues into his own unique sound,
coupled with a songwriting style reflective of his personal journey of experiencing God's
miracles; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Marlow's first single, "Second Chances," topped the Christian Country
charts and was the No. 4 song of the year in 2024; the video that accompanied the song was
nominated for Social Impact Video of the Year in 2025; and
WHEREAS, his second release, "The Fire Burns," also topped the Christian Country
Countdown charts, while his third single made it to No. 2; and

WHEREAS, Greg Marlow's music brings a message of hope and personal testimony of
God's love; having overcome a terminal cancer diagnosis and a drug addiction as a result of
various surgeries, he has channeled his message through not only music ministry, but also
through his work as a public speaker and author; and
WHEREAS, wholly devoted to his family, Greg Marlow has been blessed with the love of
his wife of thirty-two years, Angela; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Marlow epitomizes the spirit and commitment to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly acknowledge
and applaud Greg Marlow for his dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Greg Marlow for his many contributions as a
musician, public speaker, and author and extend to him our best wishes for much continued
success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
