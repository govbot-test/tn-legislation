SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1086
By Lowe
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Captain Phillip Shelton.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Captain Phillip Shelton; and
WHEREAS, Captain Phillip Shelton was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Captain Shelton, a longtime Bradley County firefighter, devoted his life to
serving his community and ensuring the safety and well-being of those around him; and
WHEREAS, beginning in 1975, Captain Phillip Shelton began his career in fire service
with the Charleston Volunteer Fire Department; he became a member of the Calhoun Fire
Department before ultimately joining the Bradley County Fire and Rescue team, earning the
respect and admiration of his peers and the community he so ably served; and
WHEREAS, a gentleman of many talents, Captain Shelton was mechanically gifted and
could fix almost anything; as a trusted advisor and mentor in the industry, he brought joy and
laughter into the lives of those who were blessed to know him, and his wisdom will be carried
forward by future generations of firefighters and community leaders alike; and
WHEREAS, for more than four decades, Captain Phillip Shelton embodied strength,
determination, perseverance, and leadership, dedicating himself fully to protecting and serving
his community throughout his fire service career; and
WHEREAS, Captain Shelton was also devoted to his family; he was perhaps most proud
of his role as a father and always endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest
order; and

WHEREAS, Captain Phillip Shelton leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity,
probity, and valor in his public life as a firefighter; compassion and loyalty in private life; and
diligence and dedication in all his chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the life of Captain Phillip Shelton,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to public service.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Captain Shelton.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
