HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 962
By Powers
A RESOLUTION to honor Caryville Police Chief Freddie Stagnolia
on the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
honor those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout
their careers in law enforcement; and
WHEREAS, Chief Freddie Stagnolia of the Caryville Police Department is one such
estimable individual, who has devoted his life to the preservation of law and order and the
protection of his fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, after numerous years of distinguished service to the citizens of Caryville,
Chief Stagnolia took his much-deserved retirement this year, having dedicated himself to the
highest tenets of public service and law enforcement throughout his career; and
WHEREAS, a 1988 graduate of Campbell County High School, Chief Stagnolia has
proudly served the Caryville Police Department as Chief since 2022, responding to countless
emergencies and aiding the residents of his community in their greatest time of need; and
WHEREAS, Chief Freddie Stagnolia began his esteemed career in law enforcement as
an auxiliary officer under Bill Rutherford, Chief of the Jacksboro Police Department; he went on
to complete the police academy and begin work as a full-time officer, in which role he excelled
for more than seventeen years; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his work with the Caryville and Jacksboro police departments,
Chief Stagnolia served the sheriff's office for twelve years; and
WHEREAS, for more than thirty years, Chief Freddie Stagnolia has performed his duties
with probity and expertise, helping his fellow citizens in any way that he could and proving
himself time and again to be a tremendous asset to his community; and

WHEREAS, Chief Stagnolia exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and
community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his years in public service, Chief Freddie Stagnolia
demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the sincere respect and
admiration of his colleagues and the citizens of Caryville; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and commend Police Chief Freddie Stagnolia on his
exemplary career with the Caryville Police Department and extend to him our best wishes for
much continued success and happiness during his retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
