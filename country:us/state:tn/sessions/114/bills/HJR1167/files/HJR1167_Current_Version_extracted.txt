HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1167
By Capley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of William Samuel "W.S."
Howard, Jr., and Mary Elizabeth "Tootsie"
Howard of Clifton.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should honor the
memory of those esteemed individuals whose lives of service and dedication left an indelible
mark upon their communities and the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, two such individuals are William Samuel "W.S." Howard, Jr., and Mary
Elizabeth "Tootsie" Howard, who distinguished themselves as citizens of the greatest
dedication, ability, and integrity throughout their lives; and
WHEREAS, W.S. Howard was born on June 17, 1914, in Clifton, to William Samuel
Howard, Sr., and Mary Pauline Tucker Howard; from an early age, he learned the value of
diligence and perseverance while working in his family's grocery store and on Tucker Farms in
Decatur County; and
WHEREAS, a 1932 graduate of Frank Hughes School, Mr. Howard played basketball in
school and second base on Clifton's independent baseball team; he later worked at Milky Way
Farm in Giles County before returning to Clifton to devote himself to the family business and to
the community he loved; and
WHEREAS, in the spring of 1934, W.S. Howard met "Tootsie" Crotts of Savannah, the
daughter of Gaines and Amy Porter Crotts; a talented and accomplished young woman who had
been crowned Miss Savannah, she shared his deep appreciation for family, community, and
civic life; and
WHEREAS, W.S. Howard and Tootsie Crotts were united in marriage on June 17, 1935,
beginning a partnership that would endure for decades and contribute greatly to the civic and
social life of the Clifton community; and

WHEREAS, together, Mr. and Mrs. Howard raised three sons, Bill, Gene, and Don, and
remained active members of the Methodist Church in Clifton while participating in many civic
and community activities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Howard carried forward the family tradition of service and enterprise
when he and his wife purchased Howard's Grocery and continued to operate the longtime Main
Street establishment until his retirement in 1972; he further served the community as a member
of the Masonic Lodge, a director of Peoples Bank, and a city commissioner for Clifton; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Howard was actively involved in a number of civic and community
organizations, including the Eastern Star and the Shiloh Chapter of the United Daughters of the
Confederacy, and she was also known for her artistic talent, sharing many beautiful paintings
with family and friends; and
WHEREAS, W.S. Howard passed away in October 1973 at the age of fifty-nine, and
Tootsie Howard continued to embody the values they shared until her passing in November
2014 at the age of ninety-four, leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire those who knew
them; and
WHEREAS, the lives of W.S. and Tootsie Howard were marked by dedication, integrity,
and steadfast commitment to the principles they held dear; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of William Samuel "W.S." Howard, Jr., and Mary
Elizabeth "Tootsie" Howard of Clifton, reflecting with gratitude upon their lives of service and
extending our deepest respect to their family and all those who continue to cherish their
memory.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
