HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1643
By Salinas
A RESOLUTION to honor the Vollintine and Evergreen Greenline
on the occasion of its thirtieth anniversary.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to recognize those
parks and natural areas that are celebrating historic milestones of preserving natural and
cultural resources for both the Tennesseans of today and generations to come; and
WHEREAS, one such public space is the Vollintine and Evergreen Greenline in
Memphis, which is celebrating its thirtieth anniversary this year; and
WHEREAS, the Vollintine and Evergreen (V&E) Greenline was established in 1996 by
community volunteers in Memphis's Vollintine Evergreen district, making it one of the State's
first rail-to-trail projects; and
WHEREAS, in 1995, the Vollintine Evergreen Community Association secured grant
funding for a major community revitalization effort and, on November 11, 1996, purchased the
abandoned right-of-way, officially renaming it the V&E Greenline; and
WHEREAS, today, the V&E Greenline is an integral part of a culturally rich and diverse
community, and its vibrant, unpaved walking and biking trail stretches from Crosstown
Concourse to Springdale Street; and
WHEREAS, the trail features two bridges, a stationhouse, an extensive canopy of oak
trees, several art sculptures, shade and sun gardens, and diverse wildlife, particularly near Lick
Creek; and
WHEREAS, the V&E Greenline is supported by residents and trail users alike, with a
dedicated group of volunteers that manage and maintain the trail; and
WHEREAS, over the years, the trail has received numerous awards and recognitions,
including Trail of the Month from the Rails-to-Trails Conservancy, Outstanding Grassroots
