Amendment No. 1 to SB1247

AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 1247* House Bill No. 1289
by deleting all language after the caption and substituting:
WHEREAS, people enjoy parks and other outdoor recreational areas because they
provide contact with the natural environment and a social environment that offers opportunities
to meet with others, all of which help to establish a feeling of community and security; and
WHEREAS, well-designed and well-used parks and recreation areas are a major asset
for local communities; however, that asset can become a liability when parks become unsafe
and lose their value and benefit to the community; and
WHEREAS, keeping parks and recreation facilities safe is key to community wellness
and has a direct relationship to their usage rates; and
WHEREAS, there is a direct relationship between the level of park use and the
perception of security. The larger the number of visitors involved in positive activities, the more
likely that crime and other types of antisocial behavior are deterred; and
WHEREAS, an emphasis on expanded recreation initiatives will encourage greater use
and ultimately create a safer environment in parks and other recreational areas; now, therefore,
BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE:
SECTION 1. This act is known and may be cited as the "Alyssa Lokits Safety Act."
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 11, Chapter 24, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
11-24-114.
(a) As used in this section:

(1) "Local governmental entity" means a governing body, board,
commission, committee, or department of a municipality, county, or county
having a metropolitan form of government; and
(2) "Park":
(A) Has the same meaning as defined in § 11-3-101; and
(B) Means all areas of land acquired by a local governmental
entity that are dedicated to and forever reserved and administered by the
local governmental entity for the recreational and cultural use and
enjoyment of the people.
(b) The division of parks and recreation in the department of environment and
conservation, in conjunction with the department of environment and conservation, the
department of safety, and representatives from local governmental entities, shall develop
a uniform safety plan composed of an integrative strategy involving design,
programming, maintenance, and citizen involvement to create and maintain safer spaces
in parks.
(c) In developing the safety plan under subsection (b), the entities and
representatives shall consider the following factors:
(1) Locating programmed activities near the park perimeter, beside an
entrance, or along a main pedestrian path;
(2) Locating food concessions at the park edge that serve both the
nearby streets and the park;
(3) Ensuring that park activities include a human presence from early
morning to evening;
(4) Creating programming and the physical design of the park to
encourage use of the park during the evenings;
(5) Developing activities beyond those for organized sports facilities and
playgrounds;

(6) Offering a variety of tours or events that will encourage more
widespread use of parks and increase positive uses;
(7) Involving park users in the design or redesign of park spaces;
(8) Creating clear and understandable signage to enhance the feeling of
safety;
(9) Using formal and informal surveillance to reduce vandalism,
inappropriate activities, and feelings of isolation;
(10) Using lighting to enhance perceptions of safety, as improved lighting
and increased legitimate activity allow for greater nighttime surveillance;
(11) Establishing clear sightlines to give the perception of actual safety
so that people can see their surroundings and the presence of others;
(12) Maximizing physical access by providing users with a choice of
legible routes to and from park areas;
(13) Creating diversity in the form, color, and texture of landscape
elements to create an environment that attracts higher usage;
(14) Creating signage in the form of maps and descriptive text to promote
a greater sense of safety;
(15) Providing access to security telephones and park staff to provide a
greater sense of wellbeing and safety;
(16) Fostering a sense of ownership and pride through citizen
involvement to build a constituency of users with an interest in keeping parks
safe;
(17) Enforcing rules for inappropriate activities to prevent cycles of
withdrawal and reductions in positive use; and
(18) Evaluating the availability of emergency communication systems,
including call boxes or other rapid-reporting mechanisms for park users.

(d) Upon request, all appropriate state and local governmental agencies shall
provide assistance to the entities and representatives listed in subsection (a) in the
development of the safety plan.
(e) The entities and representatives shall submit the complete safety plan,
including any proposed legislation and reports, to the governor, the general assembly,
the legislative librarian, the municipal technical advisory service (MTAS), and the county
technical advisory service (CTAS) no later than February 1, 2027.
(f) This section is repealed July 1, 2028.
SECTION 3. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
