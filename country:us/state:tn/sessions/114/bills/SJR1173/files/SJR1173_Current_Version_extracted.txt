SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1173
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor Ellen Irlene Mandrell as an entertainer
and a public servant.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing a
multi-talented artist whose love for Tennessee is evident in all her endeavors; and
WHEREAS, at the tender age of eleven, Irlene Mandrell began playing drums in the
family band that included her sisters and her father, a former traveling preacher and musician;
and
WHEREAS, from these humble beginnings, Irlene Mandrell achieved national stardom
on the long-running Barbara Mandrell and the Mandrell Sisters Variety Show that attracted more
than forty million worldwide viewers per week; and
WHEREAS, sharing the spotlight with her sisters, Barbara and Louise, on the program,
Irlene Mandrell wowed audiences with her singing talent and her comedic wit; and
WHEREAS, she later earned renown for her comedic performances on Hee Haw during
the show's final eight years of production; and
WHEREAS, Irlene Mandrell has appeared on numerous other television programs,
including The Tonight Show, The Oprah Winfrey Show, and The Love Boat; and
WHEREAS, Irlene Mandrell co-hosted Bright Lights and Country Nights and This Week
in Country Music on television; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to accolades, she has won two Comedy Act of the Year awards
and a North American Country Music Association International Award and has been honored
with her own star on the Country Music Walkway of the Stars; and
WHEREAS, music runs deeply in Irlene Mandrell's blood, a trait that is demonstrated in
her single "We Will Stand," which reached No. 1 on the Independent Country Charts in 2017;

this success was followed by "You Ain't Nothin' But a Heartache," which reached the top ten

WHEREAS, a lady of abiding faith, Irene Mandrell authored God Rains Miracles, which

Rains Miracles radio show and podcast; and
WHEREAS, Irlene Mandrell can trace her roots back to 1819 Sumner County, and after
touring and experiencing the world as an entertainer for many years, she has returned to these
roots; and
WHEREAS, Irlene Mandrell gives back to her community through her work with Holiday
Fest and Christmas 4 Kids in Sumner County; her involvement with the Nashville Humane
Society; her performances at numerous benefits for local heroes, veterans, and first responders;
and her generous support of numerous civic and charitable organizations and events that serve
her community; and
WHEREAS, Irlene Mandrell possesses an innate authenticity and affability that inspires
a connection with her fans, and these same qualities, in addition to her lifetime of hard work and
dedication, make her a valuable citizen of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Ellen Irlene Mandrell as a gifted
entertainer and a devoted public servant and thank her for her many contributions to Sumner
County and the State of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
