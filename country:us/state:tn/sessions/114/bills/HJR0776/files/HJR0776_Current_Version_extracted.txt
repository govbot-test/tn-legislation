HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 776
By Lynn
A RESOLUTION to commend Green Hill Church on the occasion
of its 125th anniversary.
WHEREAS, the churches of our nation have been an integral part of our history as a
people and have had an unparalleled impact in forming and maintaining a stable and just
society; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should recognize those venerable religious
institutions and their congregations who, through their unflagging capacity for love, dedication of
spirit, and faith in God, strive to enrich the lives of people in their communities; and
WHEREAS, one such dynamic community of faith is that of Green Hill Church in Mt.
Juliet, whose members will celebrate the church's 125th anniversary this year; and
WHEREAS, Green Hill Church traces its inception to September 22, 1900, when
seventeen individuals congregated as the first members of what was then called Green Hill
Baptist Church; the organization of the church came fifteen years after members began a
nondenominational Sunday School that met in a one-room schoolhouse in the Green Hill
community that included eight members of Little Cedar Lick Church; and
WHEREAS, the initial meeting was at Brother J.C. Lanius's home and opened with the
song "Come Thou Fount of Every Blessing"; in addition to Little Cedar Lick Church, the twelve
charter members also came from congregations in Mt. Olivet and Shop Springs; and
WHEREAS, the church continued to meet in the schoolhouse without a pastor until
1903, when the first church building was constructed on land donated by the Lanius family and
through the efforts and largesse of various community members; and
WHEREAS, by 1961, the church began to see substantial growth, and on April 26, 1964,
congregants voted to buy approximately ten acres of land for the location of new facilities; the

first unit of the church's current facilities had a groundbreaking ceremony on November 21,
1965; and
WHEREAS, expansion took place once more in 1976, with education and fellowship
space constructed, and in 1980, with the acquisition of fifteen additional acres; the present-day
sanctuary was completed and first occupied on Easter Sunday, March 31, 1991, with an
estimated 868 individuals attending worship services and 535 in Sunday School; and
WHEREAS, the past decade of ministry at Green Hill Church has been marked by both
internal impact and increased health within the congregation and external impact in the
surrounding community; and
WHEREAS, in 2016, the church launched a partnership with nearby W.A. Wright
Elementary School, and in March 2018, Pastor Daryl Crouch began the Everyone's Wilson
initiative to facilitate partnerships among churches, businesses, and schools within Wilson
County as Green Hill Church committed to pursuing the welfare of the community; and
WHEREAS, in 2019 and 2020, the Hospitality Project allowed the church to make
renovations and help create welcoming spaces where Gospel growth could occur and respond
to opportunities to expand its missional imprint; and
WHEREAS, in the fall of 2019, Green Hill Church partnered with Glenwood Baptist
Church in an effort to revitalize their congregation in Nashville; at the same time, Pastor Derrick
DeLain, along with approximately fifty other members of The Summit Church in North Carolina,
began preparing to move to Nashville to plant Proclamation Church, ultimately incorporating
members of Glenwood Baptist, with Green Hill Church continuing to offer support; and
WHEREAS, in October 2022, Green Hill Church committed to a vision to raise funds to
renovate the house on the corner of the church's property along Lebanon Road to serve as a
ministry house, positioning the church to expand its ministry in the coming years, reach more
people with the Gospel, and raise future ministry leaders for the church; and

WHEREAS, the church launched the Generations campaign this year, a thirty-two-month
long generosity initiative designed to accelerate and expand Green Hill Church's mission and
ministries, creating a transformative legacy for future generations; and
WHEREAS, as the church celebrates 125 years of service, it continues to be a light in
the community, and the devout members of the congregation have faithfully striven to carry out
God's work in the world; and
WHEREAS, Green Hill Church is truly one of the great religious institutions and catalysts
for positive spiritual change in our State and nation, and the members of this exceptional
congregation should be appropriately honored for their exemplary service to their fellow
Christians and the good people of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we join in the celebration of the 125th anniversary of Green Hill Church,
thank the good people of this congregation and their ministers for their notable service to their
Lord and the State of Tennessee, and wish them much continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
