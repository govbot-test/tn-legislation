HOUSE RESOLUTION 202
By Lamberth
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the 105th Attack
Squadron upon the occasion of its 105th
anniversary.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal safety and concerns to ensure our individual and collective freedom, and the Volunteer
State is especially proud to be the home of so many valiant men and women who have
performed above and beyond the call of duty; and
WHEREAS, the citizens of the Volunteer State have, throughout the years, willingly
forsaken the comforts and securities of inaction by risking their lives and leaving their loved
ones behind to honorably safeguard the freedoms and liberties guaranteed to all citizens of
these United States and vanquishing those who threaten our way of life; and
WHEREAS, for 105 years, the members of the 105th Attack Squadron, Tennessee Air
National Guard, operating from Berry Field in Nashville, have remained ever vigilant and
prepared for duty, proving time and again their ability to protect fellow Tennesseans and
Americans and those around the world in need of aid; and
WHEREAS, the roots of the 105th Attack Squadron reach back to World War I, when the
Aero Squadron of the American Expeditionary Force was formed at Kelly Field, Texas, in 1917;
and
WHEREAS, after the war, in 1919, veterans of the 105th Aero Squadron residing in the
Nashville area gathered for the purpose of organizing an air element of the Tennessee National
Guard; and
WHEREAS, the unit received federal recognition on December 4, 1921, and was
assigned to the U.S. Army's 30th "Old Hickory" Division; the squadron's insignia still includes a
figure of Andrew Jackson, "Old Hickory," on horseback; and

WHEREAS, with the drums of war approaching, the 105th was called to active duty in
1940 and became a ready source of trained personnel and seasoned pilots when our nation
entered World War II; and
WHEREAS, during the war, members of the 105th flew a variety of missions around the
globe, including observation, submarine patrol, reconnaissance, and bombardment, switching
assignments and aircraft as necessary. From 1943 to 1945, the 105th flew over 100 combat
missions, utilizing the B-25G "Mitchell" bomber against Japanese targets in the Pacific Theatre;
and
WHEREAS, since the 1950s, the unit has been known by several designations and has
flown many types of aircraft, but it is perhaps best and most prominently known for its tenure as
part of the 118th Airlift Wing when it flew the C-130H; and
WHEREAS, under its assigned transport mission, the unit flew more than 200,000 hours
and millions of miles in international and stateside missions in direct support of United States
military missions; the 105th supported the Berlin and Cuban missile crises, national and state
civil disturbances, the Vietnam War, and Operations DESERT SHIELD, DESERT STORM,
VOLANT OAK, NOBLE EAGLE, ENDURING FREEDOM, AND IRAQI FREEDOM, to name only
a few of the better-known operations; and
WHEREAS, in support of those military operations and assignments, the members of the
105th have seen duty at locations around the globe, including Iraq, Kuwait, Oman, Saudi Arabia,
Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Pakistan, and Afghanistan, in addition to serving as an Air
Education and Training Command to teach C-130 pilots and crew from more than seven
countries; and
WHEREAS, called on to support humanitarian efforts at home and abroad, the 105th
rendered sterling support in Texas, Louisiana, and Mississippi to provide aid in the wake of
- 2 - 018291
hurricanes Rita and Katrina, as well as in Haiti as part of Operation UNIFIED RESPONSE after
the devastating earthquake there in 2011; and
WHEREAS, in 2012, the mission of the 105th was reassigned to Air Combat Command
and was formally renamed the 105th Attack Squadron as the last C-130 left Berry Field on
September 26, 2012, to be replaced by the unit's new aircraft, the MQ-9 Reaper; and
WHEREAS, since transitioning to Air Combat Command, the MQ-9, a persistent
overhead intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance, and strike platform, the 105th has flown
over 90,000 combat hours, conducted 288 kinetic strikes, operated throughout numerous global
Area of Responsibilities in five Combatant Commands in direct support of Operations
ENDURING FREEDOM, ODYSSEY LIGHTNING, FREEDOM'S SENTINEL, RESOLUTE
SUPPORT, JUNIPER SHIELD, INHERENT RESOLVE, and EPIC FURY among many others;
and
WHEREAS, the members of the 105th made history in 2023 by flying the first ever MQ-9
in the Bilateral BALIKITAN exercise, bolstering international relations between the United States
and the Armed Forces of the Philippines, impacting global deterrence efforts and key strategic
initiatives; and
WHEREAS, additionally, the 105th Attack Squadron revolutionized the MQ-9 community
in 2024 by implementing the first Satellite Launch and Recovery exercise in the State of
Tennessee, requiring skilled and seamless integration between civilian aircraft and unmanned
assets, demonstrating Agile Combat Employment capability; and
WHEREAS, the members of the "Old Hickory Squadron" have proudly upheld the ideal
of the citizen soldier, an essential tradition of American society, and for 100 years, their
courageous service has stood as an enduring example of all the characteristics that have
ensured the continued freedom of our nation and the preservation of its ideals over the course
of our history; and
- 3 - 018291
WHEREAS, it is most appropriate that the members of this General Assembly honor the
brave and noble men and women of the 105th Attack Squadron as they have made untold and
innumerable personal sacrifices to preserve the liberties we enjoy today and will continue to
cherish for generations to come; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and
congratulate the members of the 105th Attack Squadron, Tennessee Air National Guard, on the
occasion of the unit's 105th anniversary and express our deepest gratitude for the sacrifices
these valiant men and women have so selflessly made to secure the blessings of liberty for the
people of this great nation.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
- 4 - 018291
