HOUSE RESOLUTION 294
By Pearson
A RESOLUTION to honor Memphis Community Against Pollution
(MCAP) and its president, KeShaun Pearson, on
the fifth anniversary of the cancellation of the
Byhalia Connection Pipeline project in
Southwest Memphis.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body take great pride in recognizing
individuals and organizations whose courage, advocacy, and commitment to justice have
strengthened communities across the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Memphis Community Against Pollution (MCAP) has emerged as a leading
grassroots organization dedicated to protecting the health, environment, and civil rights of
residents in Southwest Memphis and beyond; and
WHEREAS, under the leadership of its president, KeShaun Pearson, MCAP has
championed environmental justice by organizing, educating, and mobilizing community
members to stand against environmental hazards disproportionately impacting historically Black
and economically disadvantaged neighborhoods; and
WHEREAS, the proposed Byhalia Connection Pipeline project threatened to run a crude
oil pipeline through Southwest Memphis, placing drinking water, air quality, and the overall well-
being of thousands of residents at risk; and
WHEREAS, through steadfast advocacy, coalition building, and unwavering community
engagement, MCAP and its allies successfully opposed the pipeline, culminating in the
cancellation of the Byhalia Connection Pipeline project, a landmark victory for environmental
justice; and
WHEREAS, the cancellation of the pipeline stands as a powerful testament to the
strength of grassroots organizing and the fundamental principle that all people, regardless of
race, income, or geography, deserve clean air, safe water, and a healthy environment; and

WHEREAS, on the fifth anniversary of this historic achievement, it is fitting to honor
MCAP and Mr. Pearson for their leadership, resilience, and enduring commitment to justice,
equity, and the protection of future generations; and
WHEREAS, their work continues to inspire movements across Tennessee and the
nation, serving as a beacon of hope and a model for community-driven change; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby
honor Memphis Community Against Pollution (MCAP) and its president, KeShaun Pearson, on
the fifth anniversary of the cancellation of the Byhalia Connection Pipeline project and commend
their extraordinary contributions to environmental justice and community empowerment.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
