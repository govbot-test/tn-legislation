HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1060
By Faison
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Ken Tarkington, Jr., for
fifty-five years of service with the State of
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it the privilege of the members of this General Assembly to specially
recognize those estimable public servants who have dedicated themselves to improving the
quality of life for their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Ken Tarkington, Jr., who marks fifty-five years of
service this year with the State of Tennessee, a milestone that serves as a testament to his
competency and his devotion to serving others; and
WHEREAS, a resident of Forest Hills, Mr. Tarkington came on board with the State on
June 24, 1971, as a Business Manager 1 with the Department of Correction; he soon ascended
to the position of Business Manager 2, then Associate Warden 3 and Associate Warden 4; and
WHEREAS, he then transitioned to serve with the Tennessee Wildlife Resources
Agency (TWRA) as Fiscal Director 1; his intermediate posts included Fiscal Director, Wildlife
Manager 5, and Fiscal Director 3, which led to his current position as TWRA's Chief Budget
Officer; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure with TWRA, he has served as a commissioned officer for
the organization and given his personal time on weekends and holidays to complete boating
officer work and work in deer check stations; he also attended Tennessee Law Enforcement
Training Academy and took part in law enforcement in-service training, all in addition to his
fiscal role at TWRA; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Tarkington is a 1966 graduate of Battle Ground Academy and holds a
bachelor's degree in business from Middle Tennessee State University; he is a licensed pilot
and a veteran of the Tennessee Army National Guard and remains involved with the Employer

Support of the Guard and Reserve as the Communications Representative/Public Affairs

WHEREAS, he served with the National Guard and Army Reserve for thirty-seven years,

WHEREAS, in addition to his many vocational pursuits, Ken Tarkington is a talented

WHEREAS, throughout his many worthy endeavors, he has enjoyed the love and

Kennard (Jennifer) Tarkington III and Emily Tarkington, as well as four grandchildren, Hunter,
Hannah, Hope, and Eva; and
WHEREAS, Ken Tarkington has distinguished himself as a public-spirited citizen of the
highest order and as an exceptional asset to his community; and
WHEREAS, his remarkable success as a public servant and civic leader is directly
attributable to his reliance on the time-honored values of hard work and common sense and his
uncommon ability to work well with people from all walks of life; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Tarkington is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service
that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
