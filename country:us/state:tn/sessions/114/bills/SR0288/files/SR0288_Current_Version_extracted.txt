SENATE RESOLUTION 288
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to confirm the appointment of Miles Burdine of
Kingsport to the Tennessee board of regents.
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-8-101, provides for the creation of
the Tennessee board of regents; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-8-201, provides that the governor
shall appoint ten members to the Tennessee board of regents; and
WHEREAS, Governor Bill Lee has appointed Miles Burdine of Kingsport to the
Tennessee board of regents as the member representing the 1st Congressional District to a term
that will expire on June 30, 2031; and
WHEREAS, an accomplished individual, Miles Burdine is the President and CEO of the
Kingsport Chamber of Commerce; under Mr. Burdine's leadership, education and workforce
development have been major areas of focus for the Chamber; and
WHEREAS, a military veteran, he retired at the rank of Colonel after an exemplary thirty
years of service with the United States Marine Corps and the United States Marine Corps
Reserve; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to public service, Mr. Burdine has been appointed to a number
of boards and commissions, including the Board of Judicial Conduct, the Governor's First to the
Top Advisory Council, the Governor's Commission on Judicial Nominations, and the Governor's
Energy Policy Task Force; and
WHEREAS, he has lent his time and talents as a member of the State Collaborative on
Reforming Education, the BlueCross BlueShield of Tennessee Board of Directors, Tennesseans
for Quality Early Education, and the Tennessee Economic Development Partnership Board; and

WHEREAS, a native of Kingsport, Mr. Burdine holds a Bachelor of Science degree in
Business Administration from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, and is a graduate of the
U.S. Chamber of Commerce national management program for chamber of commerce
executives; and
WHEREAS, Miles Burdine is a person of integrity and an active citizen whose
benevolence, acumen, and strong commitment to public service have helped to enhance the
well-being of this State and its citizens; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-8-201, provides that each member
appointed by the governor to the Tennessee board of regents shall be confirmed by the senate;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that this body does hereby confirm
the appointment of Miles Burdine to the Tennessee board of regents for a term that will expire
on June 30, 2031.
