HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1203
By Reneau
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Adam Williams upon his
receipt of the Field Training Officer of the Year
Award from the Chattanooga Police Department.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal concerns to ensure the public safety and welfare, and the Volunteer State is especially
proud to be the home of so many valiant individuals who have performed above and beyond the
call of duty in service of public safety; and
WHEREAS, Adam Williams was recently honored with the Chattanooga Police
Department's (CPD's) Field Training Officer of the Year Award in recognition of his dedication
and expertise in shaping the next generation of law enforcement; and
WHEREAS, during the past year, Officer Williams's proactive training led to numerous
arrests of violent individuals during traffic stops and consensual encounters, including three
specific weapons violations; and
WHEREAS, he takes ownership of probationary officer training, exhibiting
professionalism integral to the department's success; and
WHEREAS, Officer Adam Williams builds community trust through narcotic
apprehension training; his detailed reports with four separate probationary officers resulted in
over twelve arrests of narcotics sellers, providing foundational investigative techniques; and
WHEREAS, his work assures the community that the department seriously addresses
violent crime; and
WHEREAS, Officer Williams's selfless service, willingness to train, and eagerness to
learn are contagious; he is a role model for the entire department and is highly deserving of this
honor; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
