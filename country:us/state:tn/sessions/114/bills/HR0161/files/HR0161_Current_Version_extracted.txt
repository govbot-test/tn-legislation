HOUSE RESOLUTION 161
By Chism
A RESOLUTION to recognize and congratulate the Tennessee
Black Caucus of State Legislators Living Legend
Award honorees, Reverend Ann Jones-Pierre,
Dr. Carolyn Baldwin Tucker, and Representative
Johnny Shaw.
WHEREAS, it is most appropriate that the members of this legislative body should honor
those estimable individuals who have dedicated their time and energies to providing thoughtful
and exemplary service to their fellow citizens during their lifetimes; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Black Caucus of State Legislators has honored three
individuals this year with its Living Legend Award in recognition of their outstanding
contributions to their communities and this State; and
WHEREAS, this year's honorees are Reverend Ann Jones-Pierre, Dr. Carolyn Baldwin
Tucker, and Representative Johnny Shaw; and
WHEREAS, these individuals are most deserving of recognition as "Living Legends," as
they have all made important, indelible contributions during their decades of public service; and
WHEREAS, Reverend Ann Jones-Pierre has been active in ministry for four decades,
during which time she has held various positions of leadership and influence; she is also retired
from the Tennessee Valley Authority, where she served as a senior manager in finance; and
WHEREAS, Reverend Jones-Pierre served as CEO of Church Koinonia Federal Credit
Union for thirteen years and currently continues the fight for justice and economic equality as
president of the Chattanooga Branch of the NAACP, of which she is a Diamond Life member;
and
WHEREAS, Dr. Carolyn Baldwin Tucker is a longtime educator and administrator who
was employed with Metro Nashville Public Schools for thirty years—as a classroom teacher,
principal, and, ultimately, elementary schools director; and

WHEREAS, Dr. Tucker's influence extended to her service as a visiting professor at
Lipscomb University and as Lipscomb's Director of Graduate Studies in Education, as well as
her roles as an Exemplary Educator for the Department of Education and a member of
Nashville's Metro Council; and
WHEREAS, Representative Johnny Shaw has served the people of Hardeman and
Madison counties as the State Representative of House District 80 since January 2001; he
previously served as a Madison County Commissioner and, alongside his wife, founded the
Shaw Broadcasting Company, LLC, and established WOJG-FM in Bolivar in 1992; and
WHEREAS, Representative Shaw was called to preach the gospel and later became the
Under Shepherd for Saint John Missionary Baptist Church, where he continues to serve the
congregation; he is a lifetime member of the NAACP and has served as a board member of the
National Civil Rights Museum; and
WHEREAS, the unwavering dedication to serving others exhibited by these exceptional
individuals will continue to inspire others for generations to come; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we recognize these exemplary Tennesseans on this
special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we recognize
and congratulate the Tennessee Black Caucus of State Legislators Living Legend Award
honorees, Reverend Ann Jones-Pierre, Dr. Carolyn Baldwin Tucker, and Representative Johnny
Shaw, salute their continuing legacies as community leaders, and extend to them our best
wishes for much continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
