HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 726
By Warner
A RESOLUTION supporting reconstruction of the Columbia Dam.
WHEREAS, in 1969, the Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA) started planning and
eventually constructed to 90% completion the Columbia Dam located on the Duck River just
east of Columbia, Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, nearing the completion of the Columbia Dam, environmental groups brought
a case in federal court against the TVA in an attempt to halt dam construction; and
WHEREAS, in 1981, the Sixth Circuit Court of Appeals ruled against the TVA Columbia
Dam project which eventually led to a halt of construction of the dam (Pacific Legal Foundation
v. Andrus, 657 F.2d 829); and
WHEREAS, after spending approximately $83 million for the initial construction, the dam
was demolished between 1999 and 2000, costing another $2.4 million; and
WHEREAS, the original Columbia Dam was built after the TVA Normandy Dam was
completed on the Duck River in order to work in tandem with each other to provide flood control,
generation, and navigation, which are TVA's congressional obligations; and
WHEREAS, with the dismantling of the Columbia Dam, the concept of the two dams
working together in tandem was lost, presumably forever, and with no control, the area
experiences the loss of farmland and flooding after heavy rains; and
WHEREAS, with the unexpected rapid growth of industry and residential housing in the
Middle Tennessee area, the Duck River levels run very low during our hot summers and
potentially cannot supply adequate water for current and future generations; and
WHEREAS, the Normandy Reservoir no longer has the capacity to release enough
water to properly dilute gray water discharged into the Duck River; and

WHEREAS, the reconstruction of the Columbia Dam aligns with southern middle
Tennessee's long-term strategic goals for water resource management, economic development,
environmental stewardship, and recreational use; and
WHEREAS, with the population growth in all of Middle Tennessee, water resources are
becoming a real concern; and
WHEREAS, this State recognizes the need to comply with all applicable federal, state,
and local laws and regulations governing dam reconstruction, including the National
Environmental Policy Act (42 U.S.C. § 4321, et seq.), the Clean Water Act (33 U.S.C. § 1251, et
seq.), and the Safe Dams Act of 1973 (Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 69, Chapter 11); now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that the State of Tennessee expresses its strong support for the reconstruction
of the Columbia Dam Project and recognizes it as a critical priority for the State to establish an
adequate water supply system for sustained growth, economic development, and recreational
use.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
