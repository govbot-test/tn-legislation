HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 782
By Capley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Charles W. "Charlie"
Holt, Jr.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Charles W. "Charlie" Holt, Jr., of Lawrenceburg; and
WHEREAS, Charlie Holt was born on January 4, 1951, to Charles W. Holt and Nell Holt;
he diligently served the people of Lawrence County and Middle Tennessee by practicing law for
nearly five decades, building a reputation as a steady and principled counselor who guided
generations of families and businesses through some of their most important and difficult
decisions; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Holt graduated from Middle Tennessee State University (MTSU) in 1973
and went on to briefly teach and coach football; he earned his law degree from the University of
Tennessee College of Law in 1976 and was admitted to the Tennessee Bar in 1977; and
WHEREAS, while attending MTSU, Charlie Holt was a four-year starter for the Blue
Raiders football team and was consistently singled out for his toughness and leadership; he
earned All Ohio Valley Conference (OVC) honors for three seasons and was chosen as one of
six of MTSU's Most Outstanding Seniors; and
WHEREAS, throughout his life, he always carried with him the traits that he refined
during his football career: steadiness, discipline, and team-orientation; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Holt began his legal career in Smyrna but soon moved back to
Lawrenceburg, where he became a central figure in the firm now known as Boston, Holt &
Durham; and

WHEREAS, Charlie Holt also served as County Attorney for Lawrence County for more
than thirty years, in which position he helped shape local government by providing quiet, steady
advice through countless meetings, crises, and transitions; and
WHEREAS, an active member of the statewide legal community, he was a Tennessee
Bar Foundation Fellow and a member of the Lawrence County Bar Association and the
Tennessee Bar Association; and
WHEREAS, he was a mentor to many young attorneys, a careful listener, and someone
who led by example and believed law was, at its core, a profession to serve others; and
WHEREAS, Charlie Holt was a member of the Downtown Church of Christ and later the
Pulaski Street Church of Christ; he also served as President of Christian Homes, Inc., which
provides resources for vulnerable members of the community; and
WHEREAS, members of these communities remember him for his calm judgment, sense
of fairness, and habit of putting others first; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Holt's favorite pastimes were spending time with his family and friends,
engaging in deep conversations, cheering on the MTSU Blue Raiders, spending time at the river
with his wife, taking boat rides, and going on drives; and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to his family, Mr. Holt enjoyed the loving companionship of
his wife of fifty-two years, Martha, and was the proud father of Catherine "Cassie" Holt and
Charles W. "Trey" Holt III; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his wife and children;
his sisters, Nelda Graham, Laura Haynes, and Sara Jane (Bill) Margraf; his nephews, Kenneth
(Brenda) Smith, William Smith, and Will Haynes; his nieces, Natalee (Marc) Estrin and Rachel
Haynes (Austin Cruse); his eight great-nieces and -nephews, Jack Haynes, Charlee Haynes,
Lynden Estrin, Griffin Estrin, Kori Outland, Brian Smith, Jennifer (Craig) Fox, and Caley Smith;
- 2 - 011440
his family at Boston, Holt & Durham; and a host of extended family members and dear friends;
and
WHEREAS, Charlie Holt leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Charles W. "Charlie" Holt, Jr., reflecting fondly
upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with
courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Holt.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
