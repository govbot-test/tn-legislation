SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 929
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor Herbert "Buddy" Mayes on being
inducted into the Boys & Girls Clubs of
Tennessee Hall of Fame.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those stalwart citizens who are dedicated to improving the quality of life for the youth
in their communities; and
WHEREAS, Herbert "Buddy" Mayes, longtime board member of the Boys & Girls Club of
Morristown, is one such outstanding servant-leader; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Mayes has been inducted into the Boys & Girls Clubs of Tennessee Hall
of Fame in recognition of his hard work and generosity in support of this worthy organization;
and
WHEREAS, Buddy Mayes has proudly served as a board member of the Boys & Girls
Club of Morristown for forty-eight years, and he continues to go above and beyond for young
people and their families; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Mayes has provided valuable leadership to the Boys & Girls Club of
Morristown in numerous roles, including as chief volunteer officer, vice chair of the board, and
chair of the president's council/executive committee; and
WHEREAS, he has also been a leading fundraiser, board recruiter, and Club
ambassador for the organization throughout his tenure, and he was instrumental in establishing
the Club's major fundraising event, the Super Auction, which now raises more than $125,000
each year for the Club; and
WHEREAS, for more than twenty years, Mr. Mayes and his family provided Christmas
gifts for Club members and their families, as well as clothes for children, first cars for families,
and resources for students to attend school events and trips; and

WHEREAS, in addition to chaperoning Club events, Mr. Mayes provided jobs for
teenagers, helped former Club members start businesses, and assisted numerous Club families
with funeral expenses; and
WHEREAS, perhaps one of his proudest accomplishments during his career with the
Boys & Girls Club was recruiting Bill Seals as Executive Director in 1980; and
WHEREAS, Buddy Mayes and Bill Seals were able to reach incredible heights, including
securing numerous State and National Boys & Girls Club of America program awards and
celebrating a National Youth of the Year winner and runner-up; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Buddy Mayes was a 1982 recipient of
the President's Gavel, the Boys & Girls Club's highest honor; he also received the Man of the
Year Award, the Service to Youth Award, and the prestigious Lifetime Achievement Award,
most befitting honors that recognize his generosity, his exceptional leadership, and the positive
impact he has made on so many young lives; and
WHEREAS, Buddy Mayes earned a degree from the Kentucky School of Mortuary
Science in 1969; in 1972, Mr. Mayes and his father purchased Mayes Mortuary, continuing a
legacy of compassionate care that has served generations of families; and
WHEREAS, a leader in his profession, Mr. Mayes served as President of the State
Board of Funeral Directors and Embalmers, President of the Tennessee Funeral Directors
Association, and later as Vice President of Operations for one of the largest providers of funeral,
cemetery, and cremation services in North America; and
WHEREAS, Buddy Mayes currently works part-time alongside his daughter at
Stubblefield Funeral Home, continuing his service to families in his community; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Mayes has served the broader local community with the same
dedication and distinction as a member of the Youth Emergency Shelter Board, the Kingswood
Home for Children Board, the Morristown Jaycees, and the Rotary Club of Morristown; and
- 2 - 015446
WHEREAS, his many professional accomplishments aside, Mr. Mayes enjoyed many
years of love and companionship with his cherished wife, Donna Tinnell Mayes, until her
passing; and
WHEREAS, together, Mr. and Mrs. Mayes formed a lifelong partnership rooted in faith,
family, and service and raised three children: Terri, Tracy, and Tyler; Mr. Mayes is the proud
grandfather of several grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Buddy Mayes is wholly committed to the noble precepts of youth
empowerment that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and it is
appropriate that he should be specially recognized on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Herbert "Buddy" Mayes
on being inducted into the Boys & Girls Clubs of Tennessee Hall of Fame and thank him for his
ongoing commitment to his community and the youth of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
