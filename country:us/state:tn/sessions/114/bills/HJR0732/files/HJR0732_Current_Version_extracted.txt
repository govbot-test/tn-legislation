HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 732
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Donald Leon Rush, Sr.,
of Harrison.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Donald Leon Rush, Sr.; and
WHEREAS, Don Rush was a beloved friend and neighbor who did his utmost to improve
the well-being of all who were blessed to know him; and
WHEREAS, the son of Jack and Grace Rush, Mr. Rush was a lifelong resident of
Chattanooga, and he bravely served his country as a member of the United States Air Force;
and
WHEREAS, Don Rush took his much-deserved retirement after many years of devoted
service to the Chattanooga Fire Department; and
WHEREAS, a man with many passions, Mr. Rush loved to cook, hunt, and fish, and he
enjoyed spending time with his family in the outdoors; he was an avid and loyal sports fan and
enjoyed cheering on the Atlanta Braves and the Tennessee Volunteers; and
WHEREAS, Don Rush was known as a man of faith who loved God and was a servant
to all; he was a devoted member of Maple Grove Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Rush is survived by his wife of sixteen years, Sherlene Rush; son,
Donald Leon (Brenda) Rush, Jr.; daughter, Rhonda (Charles) Clark; son-in-law, Doug Curtis;
daughter-in-law, Bev Rush; grandchildren, Donald (Bethany) Rush, Zachary (Kelly) Rush,
Amber (Tanner) Hafley, Daniel (Brandy) Curtis, Doug (Jennifer) Curtis, John (Jesi) Shahan,
Josh (Amber) Shahan, Ricky (Melissa) Shahan, Michael (Melissa) Rush, and Matthew Rush;

nineteen great-grandchildren; stepchildren, Jeff (Sandy) Howell and Angie (Dewayne) Everett;
step-grandchildren, Kyle Howell, Colby (Francesca) Howell, Lindsey Everett, and Landon
Everett; a step-granddaughter; and nieces, nephews, friends, and church family; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his wife of fifty-four
years, Patricia; son, Mike Rush; daughter, Reni Curtis; brother, Jack Rush; and sisters, Glenda
Campbell and Betty Robinson; and
WHEREAS, throughout his many worthy endeavors, Don Rush distinguished himself as
a beacon of kindness and wisdom; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to the bountiful life of this exceptional
Tennessean; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Donald Leon Rush, Sr., reflecting fondly upon his
ability to live the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and condolences to the
family and friends of Mr. Rush.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
