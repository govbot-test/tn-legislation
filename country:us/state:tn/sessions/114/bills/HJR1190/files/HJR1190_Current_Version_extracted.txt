HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1190
By Reneau
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Ed Caram on his receipt
of the Chattanooga Police Department Chaplain
of the Year award.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal concerns to ensure the public safety and welfare, and the Volunteer State is especially
proud to be the home of so many valiant individuals who have performed above and beyond the
call of duty in service of public safety; and
WHEREAS, Ed Caram is one such individual, who was recently honored with the
Chattanooga Police Department (CPD) Chaplain of the Year award, in recognition of his
outstanding service in the CPD Chaplain Corps to officers, staff, and community members
affected by crime and trauma; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Caram has shown unwavering commitment to the CPD, known for his
intentional ride-alongs, quick, compassionate crisis response, and continuous efforts to improve
officer well-being; and
WHEREAS, although often unaware of his significant impact, he always provides quiet,
consistent, non-judgmental support; his ability to foster deep relationships enables reserved
officers to feel comfortable reaching out in their hardest moments; and
WHEREAS, Ed Caram has worked with purpose and conviction to better the lives of the
good people of the Chattanooga Police Department and the Chattanooga community at large;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Ed Caram upon his receipt of the Chaplain of the
Year award from the Chattanooga Police Department.
HJR1190
016017
- 1 -
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
