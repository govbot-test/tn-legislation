HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1561
By Salinas
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the fifteenth anniversary of the
Overton Park Conservancy.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize and
commend those organizations that have made significant contributions to the preservation and
enhancement of our State's natural and cultural resources; and
WHEREAS, the Overton Park Conservancy was founded in December 2011 as a
nonprofit organization entrusted with the care, protection, and improvement of Overton Park in
Memphis; and
WHEREAS, operating under a management agreement with the City of Memphis, the
conservancy provides essential services, including daily maintenance and landscaping, capital
improvements and renovations, trail upkeep and forest management, and community
programming and environmental education; and
WHEREAS, through its mission to protect, preserve, and improve Overton Park, the
Overton Park Conservancy has worked to foster a beautiful, welcoming, and safe environment
that encourages members of the community to explore, learn, create, socialize, and play; and
WHEREAS, since its founding, the conservancy has completed numerous projects that
have enhanced the park's accessibility and appeal, including improvements to the Old Forest
Trail, Rainbow Lake Playground, pedestrian plazas and pathways, wayfinding and trail markers,
welcome gateways, and the Overton Bark dog park; and
WHEREAS, the efforts of the Overton Park Conservancy have contributed significantly
to the beautification of Memphis and the continued vitality of one of its most treasured public
spaces; and

WHEREAS, the fifteenth anniversary of the conservancy's founding provides a fitting
opportunity to reflect upon its many accomplishments and its ongoing commitment to serving
the citizens of Memphis and visitors alike; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commemorate the fifteenth anniversary of Overton Park Conservancy,
honoring its dedication to the preservation and improvement of Overton Park and its meaningful
contributions to the community, and applaud the conservancy's efforts to beautify Memphis and
maintain Overton Park as a valued and vibrant public resource.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
