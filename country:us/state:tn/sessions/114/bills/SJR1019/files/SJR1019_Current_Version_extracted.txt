SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1019
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Thomas Farmer of the
Tennessee Bureau of Investigation on the
occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, our nation was built by individuals who dedicated themselves to ensuring
the safety and welfare of the public, and the Volunteer State is proud to be home to many brave
men and women who have gone above and beyond the call of duty in service to public safety;
and
WHEREAS, thousands of Tennesseans have upheld the noble tradition of service in law
enforcement, devoting their time and efforts to protecting citizens, responding to emergencies,
and upholding justice; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Farmer of the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation (TBI) is one such
distinguished law enforcement professional, who has exemplified the highest ideals of public
service and made significant contributions to the safety and security of the citizens of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Farmer has demonstrated unwavering dedication, professionalism,
and commitment to the people of Tennessee throughout his thirty-seven-year career in law
enforcement; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Farmer began his storied career with the Hamilton County Sheriff's
Office in 1989 as a Corrections Deputy and then advanced to serve as a Sheriff's Deputy before
being promoted to Detective in narcotics investigation; in this position, he continued to
distinguish himself, eventually rising to the rank of Lieutenant Detective; and
WHEREAS, he joined the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation in 2007 and has served
the Bureau and Tennessee with distinction over the past two decades; and

WHEREAS, he has been an instrumental part of the TBI's Dangerous Drug Task Force
and led the task force as Special Agent in Charge (SAC) for numerous years; and
WHEREAS, SAC Farmer has served as an advisor to the Tennessee Governor's
Methamphetamine Task Force and as the Director of the Governor's Task Force over Marijuana
Eradication; and
WHEREAS, his leadership, expertise, and tireless efforts have been instrumental in the
continued growth and success of the TBI's Drug Division; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Farmer's invaluable expertise and knowledge have been
repeatedly utilized by the General Assembly, and his passionate testimony in numerous
legislative committees has helped to shape and guide Tennessee's drug policy; and
WHEREAS, his work has struck fear in drug dealers across Tennessee and the nation;
and
WHEREAS, Thomas Farmer's retirement marks the conclusion of a remarkable career in
law enforcement, and it is fitting that he be recognized for his outstanding service and
dedication to the citizens of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Thomas Farmer of the
Tennessee Bureau of Investigation on the occasion of his retirement, praise his thirty-seven
years of exemplary service in law enforcement, and extend to him our best wishes for every
future success as he enjoys the fruits of his labor.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
