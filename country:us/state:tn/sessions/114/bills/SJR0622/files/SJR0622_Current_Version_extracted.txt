SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 622
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to recognize Andrea Bailey Holland on the
occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and
enthusiasm throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Andrea Bailey Holland is one such estimable person, who, having rendered
thirty-two years of sterling service to Chester County, is taking her much-deserved retirement;
and
WHEREAS, a lifetime resident of Chester County, Andrea Holland was born and raised
in the Jacks Creek community, and she is the daughter of Lance Bailey and the late Ann
Wallace Bailey; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Holland began her estimable career with Chester County in 1993 as an
administrative assistant for West Tennessee Community Services and the Chester County
Juvenile Court with City Mayor Terry Bell; and
WHEREAS, from 2000 to 2010, Mrs. Holland served with the Chester County Sheriff's
Department alongside Sheriff Paul Hodges, Sheriff Mark Davidson, and Sheriff Blair Weaver;
and
WHEREAS, for the last fifteen years, Andrea Holland has worked for Chester County
trustee Lance Beshires as chief deputy trustee; she has carried out her responsibilities with
diligence and patience, earning the respect and admiration of her colleagues and community
alike; and
SJR0622
012198

WHEREAS, in addition to working alongside County Mayor Barry Hutcherson, Mrs.
Holland serves as a county commissioner, in which role she has taken immense pride in leading
the citizens of her community with both integrity and humility; and
WHEREAS, her many professional accomplishments aside, Mrs. Holland enjoys the love
and support of her husband, Tony Holland; her daughters, Ashley (Joel) Barnes and Heather
(Jeremy) Durham; and her grandchildren, Weston Barnes, Coleman Durham, and Hazel
Durham; and
WHEREAS, Andrea Holland is currently a devout member of Christ Community Church
in Henderson; and
WHEREAS, her remarkable success as a public servant is directly attributable to her
reliance on the time-honored values of hard work and common sense and her uncommon ability
to work well with people from all walks of life; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Holland is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service that
have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and she should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Andrea Bailey Holland on her
honorable and astute service to the people of Chester County and Tennessee and extend to her
our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in her
future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
