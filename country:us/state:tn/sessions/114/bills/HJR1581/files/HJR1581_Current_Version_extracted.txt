HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1581
By Scarbrough
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Arnold Justice
Wallace on the celebration of his 100th birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to pay
tribute to those citizens who are celebrating special occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, Arnold Justice Wallace celebrates his 100th birthday on May 9, 2026, a
milestone commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of life's rich pageant; and
WHEREAS, born on May 9, 1926, Mr. Wallace spent most of his life in Clinton, Oak
Ridge, and Lenoir City, ultimately returning to Clinton in 1976; and
WHEREAS, as a child, Arnold Wallace picked green beans at Carden Farm for six cents
a basket and planted cabbage for seventy-five cents a day; in his teenage years, he assisted his
uncle, George Hall, a plumber and electrician, from whom he learned the trade; and
WHEREAS, eager to serve his fellow citizens and his nation, Mr. Wallace persuaded his
mother to sign his enlistment papers a month before his eighteenth birthday in 1944, following in
the footsteps of his father, a World War I veteran; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Wallace joined the Navy during World War II, serving as a gunner on a
landing craft support ship stationed in Leyte Gulf Bay, Philippines; these gunboats provided
close-in fire and were armed with rockets, guns, and smoke screens to protect troops; and
WHEREAS, as Mr. Wallace and his fellow crewmates prepared for an invasion of Japan,
two bombs were dropped, and when the war ended, his unit celebrated by firing every flare and
rocket on board; and
WHEREAS, in November 1947, Arnold Wallace began his distinguished forty-year
career at X-10 in Oak Ridge, taking his much-deserved retirement after decades of astute
service; and

WHEREAS, a highly respected leader in his community, Arnold Wallace has served as a
Freemason for sixty-five years, a tradition continued by his son, Kenneth; and
WHEREAS, a gentleman of many talents and interests, Mr. Wallace has been an avid
beekeeper since 1980, sharing his knowledge with anyone who is curious enough to ask; he
enjoys woodworking and has crafted several oak pie safes, which he donated to Relay for Life
fundraisers supporting cancer research; and
WHEREAS, he was also a cattle farmer, carefully tending to his herd and spending
countless hours cutting, raking, baling, and stacking hay for winter feed; his commitment
extended to meticulously maintaining his farm equipment to keep the fields in prime condition;
and
WHEREAS, deeply devoted to his family, Mr. Wallace was joined in holy matrimony with
his beloved wife, Ila Wier Hillon Wallace, on April 30, 1948, with whom he shared more than
sixty-five years of marriage until her passing in 2013; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Wallace and his wife were active members of the Order of the Eastern
Star; Mr. Wallace remains a member of Sinking Springs United Methodist Church; and
WHEREAS, Arnold Wallace continues to be an inspiration to all who have the good
fortune of knowing him; he is ever supportive and steadfast in his faith, and his guidance and
dedication to family, church, and community are immeasurable; and
WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Arnold Justice Wallace on the celebration of his
100th birthday and extend to him our best wishes for much continued success and happiness.
