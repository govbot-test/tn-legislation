SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 881
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Charles Goodson of
Lancing.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Charles Goodson of Lancing; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Goodson was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Charles Goodson was born on September 14, 1938, in Fleming, Kentucky,
to Charles E. and Bethel Honeycutt Goodson; he grew up on Rome Road in Morgan County
and graduated from Sunbright High School; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Goodson began his career as a textile graphist at Kellwood Corporation
in Sunbright and also worked as a farmer; he owned Acme Silver Company and Goodson's
Grocery in Lancing, and in his later years, he raised cattle and gardened for everyone in his
family; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Goodson was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, in 1960, Charles Goodson married Myrtle Poe; together, they raised four
children who survive them: Peggy (John) Rochelle, Charlie (Sheila) Goodson, Joe Goodson,
and David (Jeri) Goodson; and
WHEREAS, they were also blessed with three grandchildren, Allison (Jeff) Goulder, Alex
Goodson, and K.W. (Brittany) Goodson, and one great-grandchild, Marcus Goodson; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Goodson's favorite activities were raising cattle, farming hay, gardening,
and watching New York Yankees baseball; after his wife's passing, he took up painting and

created amazing paint-by-number scenes of trains, farms, and The Last Supper and also
discovered YouTube, which enabled him to experience the world from his home; and
WHEREAS, Charles Goodson was actively involved in the Morgan County Cattlemen's
Association and was a man of deep faith who shared the Gospel of Jesus Christ with everyone
he met; his faith journey came later in life, as he was baptized in his sixties and later became a
deacon at Potter's Chapel Missionary Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Goodson leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Charles Goodson,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Goodson.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
