SENATE RESOLUTION 354
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to recognize and commend Malcolm Louis
Wallace on the occasion of his seventy-ninth
birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to those citizens who are celebrating
special occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, Malcolm Louis Wallace will celebrate his seventy-ninth birthday on July 25,
2026, a milestone to be commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of life's rich pageant;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Wallace has lived an amazing life and has experienced firsthand the
breadth and span of American history; he also contributed to that history as a veteran, having
served in the United States Army during the Vietnam era and the Army Reserve for more than
ten years; and
WHEREAS, wholly devoted to service above self, Mr. Wallace has held the post of
Senior Partner and CEO of CLW & Associates, LLC (Memphis CUEST) for more than forty-five
years and has extensive experience managing anti-poverty programs; and
WHEREAS, for more than half a century, Malcolm Wallace has worked throughout
Tennessee and the Mississippi Delta region, providing technical and management assistance to
numerous organizations, agencies, and municipalities in his areas of expertise—housing,
economic and community development, workforce development, job training and placement,
welfare reform, entrepreneurial training and business creation, grant development, and program
operation; and
WHEREAS, a trusted leader in his profession, Mr. Wallace is a former vice chair for the
Shelby County Families First Advisory Council and served as the western at-large
representative to the Tennessee Department of Human Services Advisory Council and as a

consultant to the Mississippi Department of Human Services, and he provides pro-bono
technical assistance to several FBOs in the Delta region; and
WHEREAS, he served as a Specialist Assistant for Economic and Minority Business
Development for Governor Lamar Alexander and Governor Don Sundquist, and he currently
serves as the Chief Operating Officer and Director of Workforce Development for the South
Memphis Renewal Community Development Corporation. He is also the Volunteer Regional

WHEREAS, Malcolm Wallace is a Life Member of Alpha Phi Alpha, Inc., and a charter
member of the Sigma Rho Lambda Chapter in Bartlett, Mississippi; in 2009, he became the First
National President of the Tennessee State Athletic Association, the national supporter group for
the Tennessee State University Athletic Department; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Wallace holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in Business Administration and
Pre-Law from Tennessee State University, a Juris Doctorate from the University of Illinois
College of Law, and an MBA from Southern New Hampshire University, as well as several
certificates from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development; and
WHEREAS, throughout his many worthy endeavors, he has enjoyed the love and
support of his wife of twenty-eight years, Delores Chester Wallace; stepchildren, Johnsie
Wallace Gault and Jon-Michael Wallace; and granddaughters, Cheyenne Ford Wallace and
Kendall Elaine Wallace; and
WHEREAS, Malcolm Wallace is a joy to be around and a blessing to all who have the
good fortune of knowing him; and
WHEREAS, as Mr. Wallace marks his seventy-ninth birthday, he remains a beloved
member of his community who is admired for his faith, integrity, and enthusiasm for life; and
WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
