HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 768
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Eugene M. Pike, Jr., of
Chattanooga.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Eugene M. Pike, Jr., of Chattanooga; and
WHEREAS, Eugene Pike was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Pike graduated from Lafayette High School and furthered his education
at the John A. Gupton Mortuary School in Nashville; and
WHEREAS, Gene Pike was hardworking from a young age and took an interest in his
father's work as a funeral director at just twelve years old; he began his career at the
Chattanooga Funeral Home in 1977 and proudly served as president for forty-eight years; and
WHEREAS, during his distinguished tenure, Mr. Pike conducted the funeral services of a
number of Chattanooga citizens and prominent local figures, including Mayors Rudi Olgiati,
Robert Kirk Walker, and Gene Roberts; in 1977, he assisted with the funeral service of Elvis
Presley at the Ellis Auditorium in Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Chattanooga Funeral Home was acquired by Service Corporation
International in 1970, and under Gene Pike's leadership, the funeral home grew to include four
chapels, an expanded flower shop, a care center, and a crematory; and
WHEREAS, at one point in his career, Mr. Pike oversaw sixty-five funeral homes
throughout the Eastern United States, a testament to his profound ability as a compassionate
and diligent professional; and

WHEREAS, Gene Pike was a man of devout faith and devoted his life to Christ at an
early age; he was baptized at the age of twelve and joined the First Baptist Church of Lafayette
before transferring to Central Baptist Church at Woodmore. He later became a member of
Ridgedale Baptist Church, where he served as president of the Friendship Sunday School
class, outreach director, and as a deacon, deacon emeritus, and chairman of church finance;
and
WHEREAS, a leader in his community, Mr. Pike supported the Vietnam Veterans of
America Chapter 203 and assisted with bringing the Vietnam Traveling Memorial Wall to
Chattanooga on several occasions; he was proud to have received the Hometown Patriot
Award, an honor that recognized his decades of service to area veterans from the Chief John
Ross Chapter of the National Society Daughters of the American Revolution; and
WHEREAS, he was also a member of the Chattanooga Civitan Club since 1965, which
he served as president; chairman of the Walter Boehm Birth Defect Center; financial chairman
of Contact Telephone Ministries; board member of Big Brothers Big Sisters; member of the
Freedom Foundation Valley Forge Chattanooga Chapter; president of the Half Century Club of
Chattanooga; and chairman of the John A. Gupton Mortuary School, in which capacity he
served since 1979; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Pike was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, left to cherish his memory are his wife of sixty-seven years, Patricia Gail
Pedigo Pike; his son, Stephen (Lea) Pike; his granddaughter, Mary (Keshun) Gentry; his great-
grandchild, Kingston James Gentry; his brother, Bill (Ellen) Pike; his nephew, Ernie (Linda)
Brown; and his niece, Cindy (John) Cagle; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his parents, Eugene M. Pike, Sr., and Bonnie
Pike; and

WHEREAS, for more than six decades, Mr. Pike rendered sterling service to the good
people of his community and offered steadfast support to those who had lost family, friends, and
loved ones; and
WHEREAS, Gene Pike leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Eugene M. Pike, Jr., reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Pike.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
