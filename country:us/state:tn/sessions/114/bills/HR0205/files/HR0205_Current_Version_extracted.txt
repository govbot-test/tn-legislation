HOUSE RESOLUTION 205
By Freeman
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Coach Josh Clark and
the Montgomery Bell Academy debate team
upon winning the 2026 Policy Debate
Tournament of Champions.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body take great pleasure in recognizing
those outstanding Tennesseans whose achievements bring honor to our State and serve as a
source of pride for their communities; and
WHEREAS, Montgomery Bell Academy, an all-boys college preparatory school in
Nashville, is widely respected for its commitment to developing gentlemen, scholars, and
athletes, a guiding ideal that reflects the school's dedication to cultivating character, intellectual
rigor, and leadership among its students; and
WHEREAS, the Montgomery Bell Academy debate program has once again
distinguished itself at the highest level of national academic competition, demonstrating
exceptional intellectual rigor, discipline, and teamwork; and
WHEREAS, under the leadership of Director of Debate Josh Clark, the Montgomery Bell
Academy debate team captured the 2026 Policy Debate Tournament of Champions, the most
prestigious national championship in high school policy debate, widely regarded as the pinnacle
of the activity; and
WHEREAS, seniors Russell Howard and Jimmy Li earned the national title after
advancing through one of the most competitive fields in the country, defeating top-ranked teams
from across the United States and internationally; and
WHEREAS, seniors Res Seibels and Oliver Johnston advanced to the semifinals of the
Tournament of Champions, while senior Fox Travis and junior Enno Brandes also advanced to
the quarterfinals; and

WHEREAS, further demonstrating the program's exceptional depth and national
competitiveness, the Montgomery Bell Academy debate program placed three teams in the
national quarterfinals, with an additional team advancing to the semifinals and finishing third in
the nation—just one round shy of an all-MBA final; and
WHEREAS, this achievement marks the third Tournament of Champions victory in
Montgomery Bell Academy's history, with previous championships in 1999 and 2020, and
reflects a program that has reached the final round five times in the past seven years; and
WHEREAS, this victory caps a historic season in which the debate team established
itself as the number-one-ranked policy debate team in the nation and also earned The David P.
Baker Award, the premier, season-long honor in United States high school policy debate,
presented by the National Debate Coaches' Association; and
WHEREAS, over the course of the season, the championship team won five of the
seven major tournaments it entered, an extraordinary accomplishment reflecting sustained
excellence; and
WHEREAS, Josh Clark's leadership, mentorship, and commitment to academic
excellence have played an instrumental role in cultivating one of the nation's premier debate
programs and the most decorated high school policy debate team in history, inspiring students
to excel in critical thinking, research, and civic engagement; and
WHEREAS, the accomplishments of these students and their coach exemplify the very
best of Tennessee's commitment to educational excellence and serve as an inspiration to
students across the State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and
commend Coach Josh Clark and the Montgomery Bell Academy debate team for winning the
