SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1002
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to honor Leadership Franklin on the occasion of
the organization's thirtieth anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pause to specially recognize those estimable
organizations that encourage success and effective leadership by their participants; and
WHEREAS, one such notable organization is Leadership Franklin, which is celebrating
its thirtieth anniversary in 2026; and
WHEREAS, Leadership Franklin was established in 1996 with an initial board of
directors consisting of Julian Bibb III, Caroline Cross, Jim Cross, Gayle Moyer-Harris, and
Richard Herrington; and
WHEREAS, Leadership Franklin was created for the purpose of bringing together
existing leaders within the Franklin community who represent a wide range of disciplines and
diverse backgrounds for a ten-month program that equips those leaders with an understanding
of the many issues facing Franklin; and
WHEREAS, over the course of each ten-month program, participants are exposed to the
challenges and opportunities present in Franklin across a wide variety of areas, including
history, health care, business, government relations, education, criminal justice, quality of life,
tourism, economic development, entertainment, and land resources; and
WHEREAS, one of the central goals of Leadership Franklin is to enable participants to
work together to make decisions that benefit the Franklin community; and
WHEREAS, more than 500 alumni have benefited from the program and alumni
engagement opportunities offered by Leadership Franklin; and
WHEREAS, Leadership Franklin has contributed to more than 100 class projects
benefiting the Franklin community, including the Jimmy Gentry statue erected in downtown

Franklin, ideas that led to the establishment of Bicentennial Park, the creation of both the

on the occasion of Leadership Franklin's thirtieth anniversary, and applaud their determination
to improve the quality of life for their fellow citizens.
