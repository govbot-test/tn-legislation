SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 963
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Sally Yoho Swaney.
WHEREAS, it was with great sadness that the members of this General Assembly
learned of the passing of our former colleague Sally Yoho Swaney, who we will fondly
remember as an accomplished attorney, trusted advisor, and treasured friend; and
WHEREAS, a mainstay of the Office of Legal Services for the General Assembly from
1978 to 2013, Sally Swaney served as legal counsel for numerous legislative committees, most
notably and memorably the House State and Local Government Committee; and
WHEREAS, Sally Swaney was renowned throughout the Capitol complex for her
benevolence in helping members, lobbyists, staff, and citizens alike navigate the legislative
process; even though her workload was usually heavy, she was never too busy to help others,
and this kindness earned her a special place in the hearts of her work family; and
WHEREAS, among her Legal Services colleagues, she was a valuable resource and a
sagacious mentor; not only was her legal knowledge extensive, she always thought like a
lawyer, and she expected other attorneys to apply the same careful attention to the devilish
details of the law; and
WHEREAS, most of all, Sally Swaney was a person of integrity, and she earned the
respect of legislators from both poles of the political spectrum and all stops in between; she was
always fair and never biased in presenting her legal opinions; and
WHEREAS, a native of Energy, Pennsylvania, Sara "Sally" Yoho was the second-
youngest of the seven children in her family; and
WHEREAS, growing up in post-World War II America, Sally Yoho was forced to walk a
mile to attend Castlewood Elementary School, regardless of the weather, be it snow sleet, rain,

or sunshine; in later life, she enjoyed sharing this legendary anecdote with her friends, family,
and the entire world on a frequent basis; and
WHEREAS, by the time Sally reached the sixth grade, she and her siblings were able to
ride the bus to Shenango High School, and they were extremely grateful for this landmark
improvement in transportation that swapped heels for wheels; and
WHEREAS, Sally Yoho and her siblings spent the halcyon days of their childhood in the
traditional pursuits of playing ball, playing hide-and-seek in the woods, climbing trees, and ice
skating on a nearby pond, and they even learned to walk on stilts; and
WHEREAS, after graduating as salutatorian of her high school class, Sally Yoho worked
her way through college, earning her bachelor's degree from Geneva College; and
WHEREAS, she then attended law school at Ohio Northern University while working at
the local bank; it was there that she met Donald "Don" A. Swaney, who she later married; and
WHEREAS, leaving Pennsylvania, Don and Sally Swaney first relocated to Washington,
D.C., before moving to Nashville, where they made their forever home and had their only child,
David A. Swaney; and
WHEREAS, Sally Swaney then sat for and passed the Tennessee bar exam; soon
thereafter, she was hired as an attorney for the Office of Legal Services for the General
Assembly, thus beginning her thirty-four-year journey with this legislative body; and
WHEREAS, Sally's whole world revolved around her family and those fortunate ones
she treasured as family; she especially loved being "Mimi" to her grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Sally Swaney enjoyed playing golf, taking road trips with her sisters, and
attending Barbershop Quartet competitions; at home, she loved cross-stitching and spent more
than five years completing a five-panel Nativity scene; and
- 2 - 015977
WHEREAS, Sally Swaney was respected by all as a person of deep and abiding faith,
and she found great joy singing in the choir at Cook's United Methodist Church for more than
twenty years; and
WHEREAS, Sally Swaney's influence will live on in each life she touched; this
remarkable lady is survived by her son, David A. Swaney; her daughter-in-law, Tricia; her
grandchildren, Logan and Caitlyn; her younger sister, Susan Colella; her sister-in-law, Norma
Yoho; her brother-in-law, John M. Roth; and dozens of nieces, nephews, grandnieces,
grandnephews, and great-grandnieces and great-grandnephews; and
WHEREAS, she was preceded in death by her parents, John E. and Catharine Irene
Yoho; her husband, Donald "Don" A. Swaney; and her siblings Neil, Jean, John, Ray, and
Leann; and
WHEREAS, Sally Swaney is mourned by a community of friends and colleagues and a
large and loving family, and we wish to pay our respects to a life spent in devoted service to
God and family; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we celebrate the life of Sally Yoho Swaney, even as
we honor her memory and mourn her passing, remembering the purposeful life of a humble
Christian who accomplished much and was beloved by many.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our condolences to Mrs. Swaney's family
and many friends.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
