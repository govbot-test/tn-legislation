SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 62, Chapter 76, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) As used in this section:
(1) "Biometric data" means data generated by automatic measurements
of an individual's biological characteristics, including, but not limited to, a palm
print or scan, voiceprint, eye retina or iris scan, physical or digital photograph,
video or audio recording, physical or digital fingerprints, or other unique biological
pattern or characteristic, that is used to identify a specific individual;
(2) "Board" means a board, commission, agency, or other governmental
entity created pursuant to this title or title 4 that issues a license, certificate, or
registration to engage in a profession or occupation authorized under this title or
title 4;
(3) "Contractor" means a contractor of a board that conducts testing for
the issuance or renewal of a license, certificate, or registration by the board, or
that facilitates the completion of continuing education requirements by the holder
of such license, certificate, or registration;
(4) "Qualifying objection" means a religious or sincerely held belief or
objection to the collection of biometric data; and
(5) "Third-party administrator" means a nonprofit or for-profit private
entity that is not a contractor of a board, and that conducts an examination or

testing, the result of which is used for the issuance or renewal of a license,
certificate, or registration by a board, or that facilitates the completion of
continuing education requirements by the holder of such license, certificate, or

(b) A board, contractor, or third-party administrator shall provide a reasonable
accommodation to an individual with a qualifying objection from a requirement to provide
biometric data as a prerequisite to:
(1) Taking an exam or test, the result of which is used for licensure,
certification, or registration; or
(2) Completing continuing education requirements, the proof of which is
used for the purpose of obtaining, maintaining, or renewing a license, certificate,
or registration issued under this title.
(c) This section does not prohibit a board, contractor, or third-party administrator
from obtaining:
(1) A physical or digital copy of an individual's fingerprints or government-
issued photo identification card for the purpose of ensuring that the individual is
qualified to take a test or exam or complete continuing education requirements;
or
(2) A physical or digital copy of an individual's fingerprints or other
identifying information as required by another law for the purpose of conducting a
criminal background check.
(d) This section does not apply to an audio recording to demonstrate language
proficiency during foreign language testing or necessary recordings or monitoring for
proctoring a remote test.
