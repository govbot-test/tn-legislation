SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1185
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Larry Richard West of
Huntsville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Larry Richard West of Huntsville; and
WHEREAS, Larry West was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on January 19, 1947, Mr. West was the son of Harold and Ida Bell
Coffey West; and
WHEREAS, Larry West was a longtime teacher and businessman; following his college
graduation, he taught in the Scott County School System and at the Tennessee Technology
Center; and
WHEREAS, as the former manager of the Scott County Vocational Rehabilitation Board,
Mr. West recognized the need for services and employment opportunities for citizens with
developmental disabilities; in response to this need, in 1984, he established Scott Appalachian
Industries (SAI) and served as executive director until his retirement; and
WHEREAS, SAI began small, with only two staff members and a $42,000 budget, and
provided habilitation services to only seven local citizens; now, this organization is one of Scott
County's best-loved enterprises, largest employers, and cornerstone among local businesses,
providing jobs, ongoing services, and human dignity to hundreds of individuals and families; and
WHEREAS, along with his successful career, Larry West was an active member of his
community. In 1997, he founded Housing Opportunities and People Enterprises (HOPE), and
he also developed an alternative fuel station using compressed natural gas within his
community; he was very involved with youth athletics and helped start Scott County Junior Pro

leagues, coaching not only his children's youth teams but several Amateur Athletic Union (AAU)
teams; and
WHEREAS, he was recently awarded Person of the Year by the Independent Herald
and the Scott County Chamber of Commerce; and
WHEREAS, Larry West always remained committed to improving the lives of citizens in
his community; to further this goal, he served on many State and special interest committees to
improve both local and special interest policies; and
WHEREAS, Mr. West was deeply devoted to his faith and his family, and he always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Larry West was an active member of White Rock Baptist Church, where he
served in many roles and always strived to live in a manner that was pleasing to God; and
WHEREAS, Mr. West is survived by the love of his life and wife of fifty-eight years,
Bonnie Keeton West; his children, Terina Cantrell, Kaprecia (Donnie) Babb, Toya (Ashley) Ellis,
Bo Mandy (Chris) Yancey, and Miata (Michael) Richardson; his grandchildren, Taylon (Cole)
West, Chevan (Heather) Brewster, Malaki (Haley) Ellis, Journey (Steadman) Rudd, Shaelee
(Eli) Hembree, Sadie (Austin) West, Skye Babb, Stone Yancey, Hannah Ellis, Brody Yancey,
Rygel Yancey, and Jep Yancey; his great-grandchildren, Hailyn West, Ellie West, Emrie West,
Colden West, Gentry West, Hudson Ellis, Adley Rudd, Iva Hembree, McCoy Hembree, Auzie
West, and Landry West; his sister-in-law, Mary Helen West; and a host of friends and church
family; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Larry West was preceded in death by his beloved
grandson, Kadon Tristin Babb; his great-granddaughter, Myla Rose Brewster; his father-in-law
and mother-in-law, Seamon and Mable Buttram Keeton; his brothers, Arnold (Kathleen) West
and Donald (Wanda) West; and his sister, Francine (Greg) Wilson; and
WHEREAS, Mr. West leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public
life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
- 2 - 017931
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Larry Richard West,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. West.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
