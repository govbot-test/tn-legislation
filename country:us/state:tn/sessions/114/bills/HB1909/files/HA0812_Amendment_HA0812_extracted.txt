SECTION 16. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-22-103, is amended by deleting
the section and substituting:
(a) The board shall license without examination an applicant for licensure as a
marriage and family therapist who is licensed to practice independently and is in good
standing in another state or territory if the applicant's qualifications meet the
requirements for licensure under this part.
(b) The board may enter into a reciprocal agreement with another state that
licenses marriage and family therapists.
(c) The board shall license by endorsement an applicant for licensure as a
marriage and family therapist who is licensed to practice independently in another state
or territory upon receipt of the following:
(1) A completed application for licensure by endorsement;
(2) Payment of all application fees;
- 2 - 016297
(3) Documentation of each license to practice marriage and family
therapy that the applicant has held and the disciplinary status of each license;
(4) Successful completion of the Tennessee jurisprudence examination;
(5) Proof that the applicant is at least eighteen (18) years of age; and
(6) Evidence satisfactory to the board of the applicant's good moral
character and professional ethics.

Section 21:
SECTION 20. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-22-113, is amended by deleting
the section and substituting:
(a) This part does not apply to:
(1) Activities and services of a student intern or trainee in marriage and
family therapy who is pursuing a program of studies in marriage and family
therapy at an accredited institution of higher learning, if such activities are
performed under supervision and are part of a supervised program of study.
Such person may use the titles "marriage and family therapy intern", "family
therapy intern", or "student therapist"; or
(2) A person who is under a supervisory arrangement with an approved
supervisor working toward independent licensure, and who is working under a
community mental health center, as defined in § 33-1-101, residential or inpatient
facility, intensive outpatient facility, or federally qualified health center. Such
person may use the title "prelicensed marriage and family therapist" or a
postgraduate degree designation.
- 3 - 016297
(b) This part does not prohibit qualified members of other professional groups as
defined by the board, including licensed clinical social workers, licensed psychologists,
licensed psychological examiners, licensed senior psychological examiners, certified
psychological assistants, psychiatric nurses, physicians, attorneys at law, or members of
the clergy from performing or advertising that they perform the work of a marriage and
family therapy nature consistent with the accepted standards of their respective
professions, or prohibit alcohol and drug abuse counselors licensed pursuant to title 68,
chapter 24, part 6, or operating under qualified supervision while seeking such
certification from performing counseling consistent with the accepted standards of that
profession.

Section 23:
SECTION 22. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-22-115, is amended by deleting
subdivisions (a)(2) and (a)(3) and substituting:
(2) "Approved supervisor" means:
(A) A licensed marriage and family therapist, or other licensed mental
health professional, who is independently licensed and an American Association
for Marriage and Family Therapy-approved supervisor;
(B) A supervisor-in-training who is under the supervision of, and
receiving mentoring from, a current American Association for Marriage and
Family Therapy-approved supervisor; or
(C) A board-approved supervisor.
(3) "Board-approved supervisor" means a person who demonstrates to the
board that the person has:
(A) At least five (5) years of full-time experience as an independently
licensed marriage and family therapist with an unencumbered license, with at
least the last two (2) years of experience completed in this state;
- 4 - 016297
(B) Thirty (30) hours of instruction specific to the practice of marriage and
family therapy supervision through a board-approved continuing education
provider; and
(C) Completed six (6) hours of continuing education in supervision every
two (2) years. Of the six (6) hours, at least three (3) hours must be relevant to
ethics and the laws of this state governing the practice of marriage and family
therapy. The hours required by this subdivision (a)(3)(C) are in addition to any
other continuing education requirements for maintaining licensure;
