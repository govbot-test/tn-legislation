HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 875
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Michael Aaron Butler on
thirty years of service to the Tennessee Wildlife
Federation.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Michael Aaron Butler of Jackson, who is
celebrating thirty years of service to the Tennessee Wildlife Federation this year; and
WHEREAS, a lifelong outdoorsman who grew up hunting and fishing in West
Tennessee, Michael Butler studied wildlife biology at the University of Tennessee and received
his master's degree in wildlife biology from Montana State University; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Butler began his career at the Tennessee Conservation League in 1996
as a Technical Advisor and was promoted to Director of Conservation Programs in 1998; and
WHEREAS, Michael Butler became Chief Executive Officer of the newly renamed
Tennessee Wildlife Federation in 2002; since that time, he has worked diligently and with
uncommon resolve to grow the organization in depth and impact; and
WHEREAS, during his time at the Federation, the organization has grown to become a
model for policy and program success for state conservation organizations; and
WHEREAS, he has been appointed to numerous boards and advisory groups
throughout his tenure, including an appointment by President George W. Bush to serve on the
Board of Trustees for the Morris K. and Stewart L. Udall Foundation for Scholarship and
Excellence in Environmental Policy; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Butler is currently proud to chair the advisory board of the School of
Natural Resources at his alma mater, the University of Tennessee; and

WHEREAS, under his leadership, the Federation has grown to include programs such
as the Scholastic Clay Target Program, Hunters for the Hungry, and the Hunting and Fishing
Academy – which have served to introduce tens of thousands of Tennesseans to the outdoors
and the shooting sports; and
WHEREAS, these programs have received national recognition for their success in the
recruitment, retention and reactivation movement; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Butler has led the conservation of thousands of acres of wetland,
grassland, and forest habitats through the Federation's programs; he played a key role in the
reintroduction of elk into Tennessee by working with landowners, sportsmen, and State and
local government officials; and
WHEREAS, he has also worked alongside numerous lawmakers and staff throughout
his career to conserve Tennessee's outdoors and sporting heritage, including spearheading the
efforts to pass Tennessee's Constitutional Right to Hunt and Fish, securing this right for
sportsmen for generations to come; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Butler epitomizes the spirit and commitment to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly acknowledge
and applaud Mr. Butler for his dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Michael Aaron Butler for thirty years of
outstanding service with the Tennessee Wildlife Federation and extend to him our best wishes
for much continued success.
- 2 - 012772
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
