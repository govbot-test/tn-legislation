Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 67-4-2009, is amended by adding
the following as a new, appropriately designated subdivision:
( )
(A) There is credited upon the tax imposed by this part an amount equal
to eight cents (8¢) per pound dry weight of spent grain byproduct donated during
a taxpayer's tax year by a taxpayer that holds a manufacturer license to brew
high alcohol content beer, pursuant to § 57-3-202, a taxpayer that holds a permit
to brew or manufacture beer, pursuant to § 57-5-103, or a taxpayer that holds a
license or permit to brew or manufacture beer, high alcohol content beer, or
substantially similar products in another state;
(B) To receive the credit in subdivision ( )(A), the donated grain must be
used exclusively for agricultural use, including for use in composting or as animal
feed, and be donated to a recipient located in this state that is not more than one
hundred miles (100 mi.) from the location where the spent grain byproduct was
used to produce the beer;
(C) The credit taken pursuant to this subdivision ( ) must not exceed fifty
percent (50%) of a taxpayer's liability under this part in any taxpayer's tax year;
(D) Any unused credit may be carried forward in any tax period until the
credit is taken; however, the credit must not be carried forward for more than
twenty-five (25) years; and

(E) In order to receive the credit, the taxpayer must submit a claim for the
credit, along with documentation as required by the department to determine the

Section 3:
SECTION 2. For purposes of promulgating rules, this act takes effect upon becoming a
law, the public welfare requiring it. For all other purposes, this act takes effect January 1, 2027,
the public welfare requiring it, and applies to tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2027.
