SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 4, Chapter 8, is amended by adding the
following as a new part:
4-8-501.
There is designated a capitol hill protection zone around the state capitol grounds
that includes the state capitol, capitol annexes, World War II Memorial Building that
formerly housed the state library and archives, Supreme Court Building, William R.
Snodgrass Tennessee Tower, War Memorial Building, Legislative Plaza, James K. Polk
State Office Building, Rachel Jackson Building, Andrew Jackson Building, John Sevier
State Office Building, Cordell Hull Building, and the grounds, roads, and sidewalks along
either side of the roads immediately surrounding such buildings.
4-8-502.
(a) Law enforcement officers assigned to the capitol hill protection zone by the
commissioner of safety have concurrent law enforcement authority with local law
enforcement agencies, including the power to arrest, carry a firearm, and investigate
crime.
(b) The assigned law enforcement officers must be certified by the peace
officers standards and training commission (POST). Any law enforcement officer
assigned to the capitol hill protection zone must have completed, in addition to the
training required for POST-certification, training specifically on the laws regulating
