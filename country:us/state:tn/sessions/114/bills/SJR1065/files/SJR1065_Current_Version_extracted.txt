SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1065
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Freed-Hardeman
University women's basketball team upon
winning the 2026 Mid-South Conference
Championship.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly should salute those student athletes
who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as true champions of
whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, the Freed-Hardeman University Lady Lions have proven themselves to be
the fiercest of competitors and truly worthy of admiration by winning the Mid-South Conference
Championship and advancing to the Round of 16 in the 2026 NAIA National Tournament; and
WHEREAS, Freed-Hardeman was perfect in non-conference play and then posted a 9-3
record in the Mid-South Conference to finish the regular season as co-champions; and
WHEREAS, in the Mid-South Conference Tournament, the Lady Lions vanquished
Cumberland in the quarterfinals, defeated (RV) Cumberlands in the semifinal round, and bested
Bethel (Tennessee) in the championship game to claim the conference's automatic bid to the
NAIA National Tournament; and
WHEREAS, playing host for the first two rounds of the Big Dance, Freed-Hardeman
blasted Rust and then topped Bethel (Indiana) to advance to the Round of 16 in Sioux City,
Iowa; and
WHEREAS, the following players are proud to roar like Lady Lions: JB Clifton, Brooklyn
Crouch, Julie Hampton, Rachel Satterly (Second Team All-Mid-South Conference honoree),
Carissa Curtis, Jenna Satterly (First Team All-American and First Team All-Mid-South
Conference honoree), Jocy May, Julieth Rivera Morales, Ally Weathers (two-time Mid-South

Conference Player of the Week), Lilly Kee (First Team All-American and First Team All-Mid-
South Conference honoree), Elli Chumley, and Alaina Stiles; and
WHEREAS, the Lady Lions' superb performance would not have been possible without
the expert guidance of Head Coach Abby Stutts, her assistant coach, Darby Phillips, and
student assistants, Angelina Barr and Aubrey Merritt; and
WHEREAS, the members of the Freed-Hardeman University women's basketball team
epitomize all that is good in today's student athletes; they have not only achieved a premier
level of success in competition but have also distinguished themselves as scholars, with twelve
players earning Academic All-Mid-South Conference honors; and
WHEREAS, the perseverance, determination, work ethic, and talent of this group of
young women not only made possible a championship season but also surely bode well for their
future success; and
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly find it appropriate to acknowledge
and applaud the Freed-Hardeman Lady Lions for their outstanding season on the hardwood;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the players and coaches of
the Freed-Hardeman University Lady Lions women's basketball team upon winning the Mid-
South Conference Championship and advancing to the Round of 16 in the 2026 NAIA National
Tournament and extend to them our best wishes for every continued success in all their future
endeavors.
