SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 672
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Melissa Batson on her
selection as the Greeneville City Schools
Supervisor of the Year.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those outstanding professionals who have devoted their careers to providing
the highest quality education for Tennessee's students; and
WHEREAS, Melissa Batson is one such estimable administrator, who has distinguished
herself as a professional of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Batson has been selected as the Greeneville City Schools Supervisor
of the Year, a most befitting honor that recognizes the positive influence she has on so many
young lives; and
WHEREAS, in her capacity as chief human resources officer, Melissa Batson has
proven herself to be an extremely talented and compassionate administrator who works
assiduously to motivate students, teachers, and staff to reach their full potential as scholars,
professionals, and citizens; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Batson serves as an inspiration to the faculty, staff, and students of
Greeneville City Schools, and her friendship and counsel are highly valued by those who are
fortunate to know her; and
WHEREAS, Melissa Batson truly epitomizes the dedicated professional who is wholly
committed to the highest ideals of public service and quality education for all, and she should be
specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and congratulate Melissa Batson

on being chosen as the Greeneville City Schools Supervisor of the Year, commend her
exceptional service to the children of Tennessee, salute her professional expertise and personal
excellence, and wish her all the best in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
