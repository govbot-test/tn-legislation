Senate Health and Welfare 1
Amendment No. 1 to SB2198
Crowe
Signature of Sponsor
by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:

Section 2:
SECTION 1. This act is known and may be cited as the "Subsidy Accountability and
Fraud Evaluation (SAFE) for Kids Act."

Section 3:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 71, Chapter 1, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
71-1-128. Child care payment integrity pilot program.
(a) As used in this section:
(1) "Artificial intelligence" means a machine-based system that can, for a
given set of human-defined objectives, make predictions, recommendations, or
decisions influencing real or virtual environments and that is capable of using
machine and human-based inputs to perceive real and virtual environments,
abstract such perceptions into models through analysis in an automated manner,
and use model inference to formulate options for information or action;
(2) "Child care agency" has the same meaning as defined in § 71-3-501;
and
(3) "Participating child care provider" means a child care agency that
participates in the child care payment assistance program administered by the
department.
(b) The commissioner shall establish a pilot program to strengthen payment
integrity, fraud detection, and oversight of publicly funded child care payment assistance
payments.

(c) The commissioner, or the commissioner's designee within the department,
shall administer the pilot program. The director of TennCare shall provide pilot program
assistance to the commissioner upon request.
(d) The commissioner shall strive to ensure that the pilot program utilizes:
(1) Fraud detection analytics that are augmented with child care-specific
signals, including attendance pattern data from child care management solutions
used by participating child care providers, state licensing data, and child care
consumer demand data compiled through child care search tools and third-party
relationships;
(2) Software tools and methodologies commonly employed to provide
oversight on fraud and abuse by identifying anomalous payment patterns, billing
irregularities, or other indicators;
(3) Other available technology that enables the tracking of attendance,
matching attendance records with payment records; and
(4) Artificial intelligence to identify payment abnormalities.
(e) The pilot program begins July 1, 2026, and ends June 30, 2028.
(f)
(1) It is the intent of the general assembly that the pilot program be
funded using available reserve funds held by the bureau of TennCare that are
designated for actuarial stabilization, payment integrity, or program oversight
purposes.
(2) This section does not require a new or recurring state appropriation or
the use of federal medicaid matching funds.
(g)
(1) The commissioner shall enter an agreement with a qualified third-
party analytics or oversight vendor in accordance with state procurement law to
support payment integrity functions under this section.
- 2 - 014130
(2) The vendor selected must demonstrate:
(A) Experience in operating a child care management solution
that allows for attendance tracking in child care programs, administration
of state child care subsidy systems, and development of child care search
tools; and
(B) The ability to implement a fraud detection solution on or
before the pilot program start date.
(h) The commissioner shall ensure that any analytics or decision-making support
tools used with the pilot program under this section are advisory in nature and do not
independently determine eligibility, provider participation, or enforcement actions.
(i)
(1) No later than July 31, 2027, the commissioner shall compile a
progress report on the first twelve (12) months of the pilot program which
contains such data and information as the commissioner determines relevant to
include, and submit a copy of the report to the chief clerk of the senate, the chief
clerk of the house of representatives, and the legislative librarian.
(2) No later than July 31, 2028, the commissioner shall compile a final
report on the pilot program which contains the commissioner's findings regarding
program effectiveness and cost savings and any recommendations for the
general assembly, and submit a copy of the report to the chief clerk of the
senate, the chief clerk of the house of representatives, and the legislative
librarian.

Section 4:
SECTION 3. The commissioner of human services is authorized to promulgate rules to
effectuate this act. The rules must be promulgated in accordance with the Uniform
Administrative Procedures Act, compiled in Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 4, Chapter 5.
- 3 - 014130

Section 5:
SECTION 4. The heading in this act is for reference purposes only and does not
constitute a part of the law enacted by this act. However, the Tennessee Code Commission is
requested to include the heading in any compilation or publication containing this act.

Section 6:
SECTION 5. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
