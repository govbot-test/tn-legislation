SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 582
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Minister Michaele Anne
Byers of Shelby County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Minister Michaele Anne Byers; and
WHEREAS, Minister Michaele Byers was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, a longtime resident of Shelby County, Minister Byers was a highly
respected member of the Eads and Memphis communities; and
WHEREAS, born on November 30, 1949, she was one of sixteen children born to Rance
T. and Lorse L. Johnson and raised in a home deeply rooted in faith, family, and service; and
WHEREAS, under the guidance of the late Bishop Gary Hunt, Minister Byers accepted
Jesus Christ as her Lord and Savior, was baptized, and guided to ministry through her service
at Apostolic Faith Temple; and
WHEREAS, in 1982, Minister Michaele Byers was ordained by the Pentecostal
Assemblies of the World, Inc., and she later became an active and devout member of the Faith
Christian Church, which she served faithfully for many years, working in the youth ministry and
teaching Sunday School and Bible Study; and
WHEREAS, when delivering the Word on Sundays, Minister Byers allowed God to speak
through her with joy and creativity, and she often used visual aids, props, and expressive
demonstrations to bring Scripture to life to ensure everyone, young and old, could clearly
understand the message; and

WHEREAS, Minister Michaele Byers was a 1967 graduate of Carver High School; she
continued her education by becoming a licensed cosmetologist through Suzanne's Business
College in Los Angeles, California, and attending Southwest Tennessee Community College;
and
WHEREAS, Minister Byers served with acumen and alacrity for thirty-two years with the
Shelby County Sheriff's Department and retired as Captain in 2022, in which position she
earned the respect of her colleagues and community alike for the superior quality of her work;
and
WHEREAS, known for her elegance, unmistakable sense of style, and signature
brooches, Michaele Byers was a deeply generous woman who gave her time, resources, and
love without hesitation, and she shared her love of God with a radiant spirit that inspired all who
encountered her; and
WHEREAS, she was also deeply devoted to her family and always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Minister Byers is survived by her children, L. Angela (Amber) Webb, Robert
L. Coles, Jr., Micah J. (Marshae) Lyons, Royale K. Lyons, and Darrold Lyons; eight
grandchildren, Micheale Currin, Jamie Currin, Pirsia Coles, Macai Lyons, Illias Lyons, Sabirah
Lyons, Carlyn Lyons, and Darrold Lyons, Jr.; siblings, Michael Andrew Johnson, Royale
Johnson, Bishop Eugene (Carolyn) Johnson, Mary Wilson, E'thel Tillis, and Hester Moore; and a
host of nieces, nephews, church family, and friends; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her parents, she was preceded in death by her siblings, Alfred
Johnson, Jerry Johnson, Sr., Ollie Johnson, Tommie Butler, Pinkie "Sharp" Johnson, and Peggy
Copeland; and

WHEREAS, Minister Michaele Byers leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and
probity in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all
her chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Minister Michaele Anne
Byers, reflecting fondly upon her impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living
the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, we express our sympathy and offer our condolences to
the family of Minister Byers.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
