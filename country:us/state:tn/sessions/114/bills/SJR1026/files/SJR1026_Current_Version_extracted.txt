SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1026
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Tom Sturdivant of
Nashville for decades of devoted service to the
citizens of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
and business leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Tom Sturdivant of Nashville, a consummate
professional whose service to his community and this State has improved the well-being of
countless individuals and organizations; and
WHEREAS, a charismatic and visionary founder with more than fifty-eight years of
experience leading one of Nashville's most iconic luxury transportation companies, Mr.
Sturdivant has served his customers and fellow citizens with compassion and devotion; and
WHEREAS, in 1967, with only $1,000 in start-up funds and one Fleetwood 75, Tom
Sturdivant founded Sedan on Demand; since that time, he has grown his fleet to include twenty-
eight vehicles, serving those in Middle Tennessee and beyond; and
WHEREAS, over the past six decades, he has trained more than 100 chauffeurs in the
art of refined service and client management and served many elite clients, including Kid Rock,
Vanderbilt University, and participants and attendees at the Super Bowl, Kentucky Derby, and
Indianapolis 500; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Sturdivant is an unwavering advocate for elegance and service
excellence and a hands-on leader with a passion for mentoring chauffeurs and crafting
memorable client experiences; and
WHEREAS, highly regarded in his industry, Mr. Sturdivant is a regular speaker and
seminar leader at the National Limousine Association annual convention; he was honored with

the National Cancer Association Award in 2010 and 2022, served on the Board of Directors of
the Tennessee Hospitality Association from 2001 to 2010, and is a tenured member of the
Davidson County Drug Court Board; and
WHEREAS, Tom Sturdivant graduated from Butler School in London, England, where
his education focused on etiquette and high-level service training; after his many years in

training; and
WHEREAS, Tom Sturdivant epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic
of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, we find it appropriate to acknowledge and applaud Mr. Sturdivant for his
many years of dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Tom Sturdivant for his
distinguished career in the hospitality industry and his many years of public service and
leadership, extending our best wishes for every continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
