HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 723
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to honor Tennessean Jack White upon The
White Stripes being inducted into the Rock &
Roll Hall of Fame.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in paying tribute
to a Tennessean who, as a result of his trailblazing work in returning rock music to the raw
power of its origins, has joined the greatest artists in the music industry as a member of the
Rock & Roll Hall of Fame; and
WHEREAS, The White Stripes, a dynamic duo founded by Jack White and Meg White,
was inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in 2025 by the legendary Iggy Pop; and
WHEREAS, on this auspicious occasion, the Hall praised the White Stripes for
"reinvigorat[ing] rock & roll by returning it to its primal blues roots, proving that a duo with defiant
energy, catchy riffs, melodic savvy and unique style could captivate as much, if not more, than a
large, complex ensemble"; and
WHEREAS, composed of mastermind multi-instrumentalist and vocalist Jack White and
Meg White, a minimalist, yet hypnotic, drummer, The White Stripes emerged from Detroit in
1997 and became one of the leading bands of the early 2000s' indie and garage-rock revival;
and
WHEREAS, the band's raw, simple approach to composing, arranging, and performing
music created a low-fidelity, stripped-down sound that proved greater than the sum of its parts;
and
WHEREAS, inspired by the garage rock of The Stooges and MC5 and blues artists such
as Son House and Blind Willie McTell, The White Stripes have influenced a diverse array of
present-day artists, including Beyoncé, Olivia Rodrigo, and St. Vincent; and

WHEREAS, The White Stripes's first two albums established the band with music critics
and knowledgeable rock fans, but their third album, White Blood Cells, catapulted the duo to a
new level, and they soon became one of the most acclaimed bands of the early 2000s; and
WHEREAS, named one of the top-ten albums of its era, White Blood Cells earned the
band its first gold record, and its signature driving sound, as personified by "Fell in Love with a
Girl," attracted new fans from around the globe; and
WHEREAS, The White Stripes's next album, Elephant, received even greater critical
acclaim and won the 2004 Grammy for Best Alternative Music Album; the platinum-selling
album is generally considered the pinnacle of the band's oeuvre and one of Jack White's
greatest works; and
WHEREAS, included on this triumphant album is the genre-defying anthem "Seven
Nation Army," which won the Grammy for Best Rock Song and is now ubiquitous both in the
media and at sporting events; and
WHEREAS, the Grammy-winning Get Behind Me Satan, a more experimental album
released in 2005, set aside The White Stripes's primitive, propulsive sound in favor of expanded
arrangements, studio overdubbing, country-folk influences, and piano-driven songs, including
"My Doorbell"; and
WHEREAS, The White Stripes concluded their decade of musical excellence with 2007's
Icky Thump, which marked a return to their rock and blues influences and produced their first
top-forty hit with the title song; and
WHEREAS, their sixth album, Icky Thump also won the Grammy for Best Alternative
Music Album, bringing the band's total to eleven Grammy nominations and six Grammy wins;
and

WHEREAS, after a grueling decade-plus of extensive recording and touring, The White
Stripes officially parted ways in 2011, leaving behind a legacy of musical innovation through
simplicity; and
WHEREAS, since the dissolution of the band, Jack White has continued to make
essential music, first as a member of The Raconteurs and then as a solo artist; he has six solo
albums and six additional Grammys to his credit, and his 2025 world tour featured sold-out
shows in the United States, Denmark, Norway, Sweden, South Korea, the United Kingdom, and
eight additional countries; and
WHEREAS, touted as "the coolest, weirdest, and savviest rock star of our time," Jack
White is also a proud Tennessean, and the Nashville store of his Third Man Records label is
one of only three such facilities in the world; and
WHEREAS, Jack White is most deserving of our approbation on the momentous
occasion of his induction into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Tennessean Jack White upon The White
Stripes being inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame, recognize his many contributions to
popular music, and wish him all the best in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
