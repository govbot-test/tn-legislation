SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1189
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Michael Davis, director
of Hardin County Schools, on the occasion of his
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should specially recognize those outstanding
professionals who have devoted their careers to providing the highest-quality education and
learning environments for Tennessee's students; and
WHEREAS, one such exceptional individual is Michael Davis, who, having rendered
nearly thirty years of service as an educator and administrator, will take his much-deserved
retirement from the Hardin County school system in June 2026; and
WHEREAS, born on September 23, 1967, Mr. Davis is the son of William and Nancy
Davis and the brother of Susan Liddon and the late John Davis; he is a lifelong resident of
Hardin County and a 1985 graduate of Hardin County High School; and
WHEREAS, Michael Davis continued his education at Martin Methodist College on a golf
scholarship before earning a bachelor's degree in education from the University of Memphis in
1998; and
WHEREAS, shortly thereafter, Mr. Davis began his career in education at Hardin County
Middle School, where he taught and made an immediate impact on his students while also
serving as the golf coach at Hardin County High School; and
WHEREAS, in August 2001, Michael Davis earned a master's degree from Freed-
Hardeman University, paving the way for a distinguished career in school leadership; he went
on to serve as assistant principal and principal of Hardin County Middle School, where he was
known for his steady leadership, strong relationships, and commitment to student success; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Davis later transitioned to the Hardin County Board of Education Central
Office, serving as a supervisor and continuing to influence teaching and learning across the
district; and
WHEREAS, he was duly named Director of Schools for Hardin County in March 2014, a
role he has served in with dedication, integrity, and vision, working assiduously to ensure that
each student is prepared for success beyond graduation; and
WHEREAS, throughout his tenure as director, Mr. Davis has been a champion for
students, educators, and the entire Hardin County community; his leadership has been defined
by a focus on academic growth, expanding student opportunities, supporting educators, and
strengthening school-community partnerships; and
WHEREAS, known for his approachable nature, servant leadership, and deep-rooted
love for Hardin County, Michael Davis has impacted thousands of students, families, and
educators throughout his distinguished tenure, and his legacy is reflected not only in the
district's progress but also in the countless relationships he has built along the way; and
WHEREAS, his many professional accomplishments aside, Mr. Davis is most grateful for
the love and support he shares with his wife, Tamara Willoughby Davis, a devoted educator
who has served as a curriculum facilitator at Northside Elementary School; together, the couple
share two sons, John Patrick and Willoughby; and
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career in education, Michael Davis has
demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unbridled respect
and admiration of the students, parents, teachers, and board members of Hardin County
Schools; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we extend to Michael Davis, director of Hardin
