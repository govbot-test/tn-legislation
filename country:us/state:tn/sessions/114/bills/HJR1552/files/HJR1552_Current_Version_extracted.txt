HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1552
By Camper
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the 160th anniversary of the
Memphis Massacre of 1866.
WHEREAS, millions of Africans and their descendants were enslaved in the United
States from 1619 through 1865, and the system of slavery and the racial discrimination upon
which it depended became deeply embedded in the nation's social and legal structures; and
WHEREAS, although slavery was abolished by the ratification of the Thirteenth
Amendment to the United States Constitution in 1865 following the Civil War, the period of
Reconstruction that followed was marked by significant unrest and violence, particularly in the
Southern states; and
WHEREAS, during Reconstruction, newly freed African Americans sought to exercise
their rights as citizens, including the rights to work, to assemble, to worship, and to participate in
civic life, often in the face of hostility and resistance; and
WHEREAS, the Memphis Massacre occurred in Memphis from May 1 to May 3, 1866,
during the early Reconstruction era, constituting a tragic and significant chapter in the history of
this State and the nation; and
WHEREAS, during this event, a white mob carried out a brutal and unprovoked attack
on Black residents of Memphis, including formerly enslaved persons and Union veterans,
resulting in the deaths of dozens of African Americans, injuries to many others, and the
destruction of numerous homes, schools, and churches; and
WHEREAS, the scale and nature of the violence, including the targeting of homes,
educational institutions, and places of worship, inflicted lasting harm on the Black community in
Memphis and underscored the vulnerability of newly freed citizens during this period; and

WHEREAS, the violence and devastation of the Memphis Massacre drew national
attention to the urgent need for legal protections for the civil rights of African Americans and
contributed to the broader national dialogue that culminated in the adoption of the Fourteenth
Amendment to the United States Constitution, guaranteeing equal protection under the law; and
WHEREAS, the events in Memphis were among the first large-scale acts of racial
violence during Reconstruction and were followed by similar incidents elsewhere, further
illustrating the challenges faced in securing civil rights and equal protection; and
WHEREAS, despite the magnitude of the violence and the extensive documentation of
the events, those responsible for the Memphis Massacre were not held accountable, and acts of
racial violence continued during Reconstruction and beyond; and
WHEREAS, in the years following Reconstruction, the erosion of federal protections led
to the rise of segregation and disenfranchisement, reinforcing the importance of continued
vigilance in the protection of civil rights; and
WHEREAS, it is important that the history of the Memphis Massacre and the broader
experiences of African Americans during Reconstruction be remembered accurately and not
minimized, so that future generations may better understand the challenges faced in the pursuit
of justice and equality; and
WHEREAS, commemorating such events provides an opportunity for reflection,
education, and a renewed commitment to the principles upon which this State and nation are
founded; and
WHEREAS, reflection upon such events provides an opportunity to reaffirm a shared
commitment to the principles of equal protection, justice, and opportunity for all citizens; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
- 2 - 018263
CONCURRING, that we commemorate the 160th anniversary of the Memphis Massacre of
1866, honor the memory of those who lost their lives, and recognize this event as a solemn and
significant chapter in the history of Tennessee and the United States.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the members of this General Assembly
commemorate this solemn occasion as an opportunity for reflection, greater civic awareness,
and a continued commitment to a more just and unified society grounded in equality and respect
for the dignity of all persons.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 3 - 018263
