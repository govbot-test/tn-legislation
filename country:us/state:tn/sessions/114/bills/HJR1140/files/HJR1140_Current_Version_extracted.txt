HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1140
By Reneau
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate the Siskin Children's
Institute on the occasion of its seventy-fifth
anniversary.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to recognize those
exemplary organizations that are dedicated to improving the quality of life for the people of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, one such organization is the Siskin Children's Institute, which is celebrating
its seventy-fifth anniversary of bringing hope for children and families in the Chattanooga
community; and
WHEREAS, the Siskin Children's Institute has been a cornerstone of support,
empowering children with disabilities and their families through life-changing services for
seventy-five years; and
WHEREAS, in 1950, the Siskin Children's Institute was founded in Chattanooga by
brothers Mose and Garrison Siskin, whose shared commitment to serving people with
disabilities grew from a personal vow to dedicate their lives to helping others in need; and
WHEREAS, the Mose & Garrison Siskin Memorial Foundation was established in 1950
to support services for children with physical disabilities and developmental delays, including the
Siskin Early Learning Center, which provided specialized preschool opportunities for young
children and their families; and
WHEREAS, the mission of the Siskin Children's Institute expanded through the
establishment of Siskin Hospital for Rehabilitation, offering outpatient services such as physical
therapy, speech and language therapy, and other rehabilitative care designed to improve the
lives of individuals with disabilities; and

WHEREAS, in 2009, the Siskin Center for Developmental Pediatrics was established to
provide comprehensive medical evaluations, diagnoses, therapies, and healthcare resources for
children and families navigating complex developmental and healthcare needs; and
WHEREAS, today, the Siskin Children's Institute serves families throughout Tennessee
and Georgia through evidence-based developmental pediatric healthcare services, home and
community-based early intervention programs, and outreach in the field of developmental
medicine; and
WHEREAS, the Institute's Home and Community-Based Early Intervention program
supports children with special needs and their caregivers by providing guidance, training, and
consultation that help families strengthen their child's development and quality of life; and
WHEREAS, through its Family Support Services and partnerships with organizations
such as Family Voices of Tennessee and the Tennessee Disability Coalition, the Institute
provides critical healthcare navigation, family support, and professional training for those caring
for children with special healthcare needs; and
WHEREAS, continuing its commitment to expanding access to care, the Siskin
Children's Institute opened a clinic in Nashville in 2020, broadening the reach of its
developmental pediatric services and early intervention programs; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we specially recognize the Siskin Children's Institute,
commending its seventy-five years of bringing hope and support to children and families in the
Chattanooga community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate the Siskin Children's Institute on the occasion
of the organization's seventy-fifth anniversary and extend our deepest gratitude for its invaluable
contributions to this State.
- 2 - 015650
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
