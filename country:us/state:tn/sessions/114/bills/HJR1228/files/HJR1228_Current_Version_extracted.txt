HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1228
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Bill M. Hall, director of
Scott County Schools, on the occasion of his
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly specially recognize,
particularly on the occasion of their retirement, those outstanding school administrators who
have devoted their careers to providing the highest-quality education for Tennessee's students;
and
WHEREAS, one such exceptional individual is Bill M. Hall, who, after a distinguished
career as an educator and administrator, is taking his much-deserved retirement from the Scott
County school system on June 30, 2026; and
WHEREAS, Bill Hall brought a strong educational leadership background to his role as
director of schools, having spent more than two decades working in public education in Scott
County as a classroom teacher, bus driver, coach, vice principal, and principal; and
WHEREAS, he began his career in education as an elementary classroom teacher and
physical education coach for elementary, middle, and high school students; his love of sports
led him to mentor hundreds of students, in both middle and high school, on the basketball court
and football field; and
WHEREAS, through the spirit of competition and true sportsmanship, Mr. Hall instilled
work ethic and character in his student athletes; even after his formal coaching career came to a
close, it was not uncommon to see him volunteering his time and expertise with Scott County's
middle and high school programs; and
WHEREAS, in 2001, Bill Hall moved into administration, serving for six years as the vice
principal at Scott High School and four years as the school's principal; during his tenure, he

helped guide the school to ten consecutive years of maintaining Adequate Yearly Progress
(AYP) in the areas of reading/language arts, math, and graduation rate; and
WHEREAS, as a high school administrator, he made decisions based not only on the
belief that all children could learn, but on the foundation that all children could enjoy learning;
when he stepped into his current role of director of schools in 2011, he took that guiding
principle with him in hopes of positively impacting all teachers and students in Scott County with

WHEREAS, Mr. Hall is a Scott High School alumnus; he earned his Bachelor of Science
degree from East Tennessee State University and his Master of Science and Education
Specialist degrees from Tennessee Technological University; and
WHEREAS, during Mr. Hall's tenure as director of schools, he and Scott County's
teachers and staff have worked hard to ensure that every student entering a classroom across
the district has been motivated to reach his or her potential, both as a scholar and as a citizen;
and
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career in education, Bill Hall has demonstrated the
utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the respect and admiration of the students,
parents, teachers, and board members of Scott County Schools; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we extend to Bill M. Hall our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling
retirement, applaud his dedication to the youth of Tennessee, and wish him much happiness
and every continued success in his future endeavors.
