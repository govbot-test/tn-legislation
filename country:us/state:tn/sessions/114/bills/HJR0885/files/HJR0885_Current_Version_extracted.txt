HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 885
By Carringer
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Charles Maxwell
"Charlie" Severance of Knox County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Charles Maxwell "Charlie" Severance of Knox County; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Severance was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on September 17, 1939, in Fountain City, Mr. Severance was the son
of Don and Florence Severance; he graduated from Central High School and attended the
University of Tennessee (UT) on a football scholarship; and
WHEREAS, Charlie Severance was later honored on the Central High School Wall of
Fame and inducted into the Greater Knoxville Sports Hall of Fame; he is perhaps best known for
his participation in "The Stop," a legendary play in UT football history; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Severance served for many years on the Fountain City Recreation
Commission; his dedication to his fellow citizens extended to his service as a Tennessee State
Representative to the General Assembly and Assistant Commissioner of State Parks for the
Department of Conservation and Environment; and
WHEREAS, in addition to being an avid UT fan, Mr. Severance was a gifted storyteller,
who was the life of the party and quick with a joke; and
WHEREAS, Charlie Severance was deeply devoted to his family, and he always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his wife of sixty-six
years, Phyllis Bales Severance; his sons, Chuck (Kim) Severance, Marcus (Jamie) Severance,

and Michael (Robin) Severance; grandsons, Connor (Dezirae) Sepesi, Garrett Sepesi, Michael
Christian Severance, Gabriel Severance, Hagan (Katie) Severance, Ben Severance, and Wyatt
Severance; his great-grandchildren, Carter Severance, Collins Severance, and Walker Sepesi;
his brother, Bill (Peggy) Severance; and a host of extended family and dear friends; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his brother,
Lieutenant Colonel Donald Marcus Severance, Jr.; and
WHEREAS, Charlie Severance leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity
in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his
chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Charles Maxwell "Charlie" Severance, reflecting
fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life
with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Severance.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
