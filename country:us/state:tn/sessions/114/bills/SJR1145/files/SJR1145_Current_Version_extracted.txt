SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1145
By Reeves
A RESOLUTION to celebrate Health Information Technology
Week in the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Chapter of the Healthcare Information and Management
Systems Society (HIMSS) is catalyzing actionable change within the U.S. health system and the
region through the application of information and technology; and
WHEREAS, health information technology stakeholders across the State collaborate
toward actionable outcomes that demonstrate the power that information and technology have
to transform health care delivery in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, leveraging health information technology can play a key role in combating
health-related public policy challenges such as the behavioral health crisis; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee is a hub for innovation in health care, with the healthcare
industry contributing $68 billion to the Middle Tennessee economy each year; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we join with the Tennessee Chapter of the
Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society in the celebration of the week of April
27-May 1, 2026, as Health Information Technology Week in the State of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
