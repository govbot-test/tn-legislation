SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1166
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Diane Giddens upon
the occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Diane Giddens is one such estimable individual, who, having rendered
thirty-eight years of sterling service as Chief of Staff for the Williamson County Mayor, will take
her much-deserved retirement this year; and
WHEREAS, a proud native of the Bethesda community, Mrs. Giddens built a successful
career in public service, beginning in 1978 as an administrative secretary with the University of
Tennessee Agricultural Extension Service and then as an administrative assistant for the
Williamson County judge; and
WHEREAS, she has served as Chief of Staff for the Williamson County Mayor since
1988, including under Mayor Rogers Anderson since 2002, defining more than four decades of
dedicated service to Williamson County government; and
WHEREAS, a highly respected member of her community, Diane Giddens is a 2003-
2004 graduate and board member of Leadership Franklin, a founding and honorary board
member of the Williamson County Fair Board (secretary 2016–2021, president 2022–2024), and
she has served as secretary of the Bethesda Recreation Association and the Williamson County
Young Republicans, past board president of Youth Leadership Franklin, and past president and
board member of Waves, Inc.; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Mrs. Giddens received the Nashville
Business Journal Impact Award in 2015 and 2016, the Franklin Tomorrow Anne T. Rutherford

Exemplary Community Volunteer Award in 2016, the ARC of TN Ruth Roberts Memorial Sibling
Award in 2017, the Waves, Inc., Mary Ann Sugg Community Enhancement Award in 2024, and
Franklin Tomorrow Charles M. Sargent Leadership Award in 2026; she was also named to the
Women of Influence list by the Nashville Business Journal in 2010; and
WHEREAS, her many professional accomplishments aside, Diane Giddens is most
grateful for the love and support she shares with her husband of thirty-five years, Don Giddens;
she is the mother of Kelsey (Brent) Young and Heather Giddens and the grandmother of
Maggie Young; and
WHEREAS, throughout her estimable career, Mrs. Giddens has demonstrated the
utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unbridled respect and admiration of
her colleagues and the community alike; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Diane Giddens upon the
occasion of her retirement, extending to her our deep appreciation for more than forty years of
service to the citizens of this State and our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
