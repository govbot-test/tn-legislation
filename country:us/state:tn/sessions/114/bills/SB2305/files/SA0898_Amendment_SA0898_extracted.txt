Senate State and Local Government 1
Amendment No. 1 to SB2305
Briggs
Signature of Sponsor
AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 2305* House Bill No. 2469
by deleting all language after the caption and substituting:
WHEREAS, Tennessee is home to approximately 303,000 veterans and their surviving
spouses; and
WHEREAS, ten percent of these veterans and surviving spouses qualify for Medicaid
reimbursement of their long-term care; and
WHEREAS, twenty percent of these veterans and surviving spouses also qualify for the
aid and assistance benefits from the United States Department of Veterans Affairs, meaning
more than 6,000 veterans and surviving spouses could receive federally subsidized care to
assist them with the activities of daily living and could avoid long-term care subsidized by
Medicaid; and
WHEREAS, the provision of such aid and assistance benefits to 6,000 veterans and
their surviving spouses would save the State of Tennessee more than $56,000 dollars per
veteran per year, for a total State savings of more than $345 million annually; and
WHEREAS, the General Assembly finding these facts as true, hereby adopts the 2026
Aid and Assistance Incentive Act; now, therefore,
BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE:
SECTION 1. This act is known and may be cited as the "2026 Aid and Assistance
Incentive Act."
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 4, Chapter 4, is amended by adding the
following as a new part:
4-4-201.

As used in this part:
(1) "Aid and assistance benefit" means the federal pension benefit
administered by the United States department of veterans affairs for wartime
veterans and their surviving spouses who require assistance with activities of
daily living;
(2) "Division" means the division of TennCare;
(3) "Long-term care assessment" means the financial, medical, and
eligibility evaluation conducted under the supervision of an attorney accredited by
the United States department of veterans affairs, for the purpose of determining
qualification for federal aid and assistance benefits;
(4) "Pension claims assistance provider" or "provider" means a person or
entity that exclusively assists veterans or their surviving spouses with preparing a
long-term care assessment to determine eligibility and prepare a claim for aid
and assistance benefits and who:
(A) Does not engage in the sale of financial products, annuities,
or insurance;
(B) Is compensated for conducting a long-term care assessment
at the time the assessment service is rendered and submitted to the
state;
(C) Has the capacity to accommodate a minimum of three
hundred (300) clients per month;
(D) Possesses a minimum of five (5) years of successful
experience assisting with federal veterans pension claims;
(E) Is able to document at least five thousand (5,000) verifiable
federal veterans pension claim approvals; and

(F) Is accredited by the U.S. department of veterans affairs, or
operates under the direct supervision of a U.S. department of veterans
affairs' accredited attorney or claims agent;
(5) "PIV card" means a personal identity verification credential issued
pursuant to federal standards under Homeland Security Presidential Directive 12
(HSPD-12) that authorizes secure access to federal systems and information;
(6) "PIV card holder" means an individual who has been issued and
maintains an active personal identity verification credential in good standing;
(7) "Qualified provider" means a pension claims assistance provider that
meets all requirements established in this part;
(8) "Surviving spouse" means an individual who:
(A) Was married to the veteran for at least one (1) year prior to
submitting a claim for aid and assistance benefits;
(B) Was married to the veteran at the time of the veteran's death;
and
(C) Has remained unmarried since the veteran's death; and
(9) "Veteran" means a person who:
(A) Served at least ninety (90) days of active duty service in the
armed forces of the United States, with at least one (1) day occurring
during a declared wartime period;
(B) Was discharged or released under conditions other than
dishonorable; and
(C) Is sixty-five (65) years of age or older or unemployable.
4-4-202.
(a) The division shall as soon as practicable, but in all cases, within ninety (90)
days, reimburse a qualified provider for expenses incurred in the preparation of a long-
term care assessment.
- 3 - 016474

(b) A qualified provider submitting claims for reimbursement under this section
shall:
(1) Ensure that any individual with access to a veteran's medical or
financial information undergoes a background check conducted by a reputable
source, including identity verification and a criminal history records check;
(2) Employ a minimum of two (2) individuals who are active PIV card
holders;
(3) Have maintained at least two (2) active PIV card holders continuously
for a minimum of two (2) years prior to applying for qualification under this part;
(4) Prepare, present, and prosecute the aid and assistance benefit
application on behalf of the veteran or surviving spouse free of charge; and
(5) Operate exclusively for the purpose of assisting veterans and
surviving spouses with aid and assistance benefit eligibility and claims.
(c) A qualified provider shall not:
(1) Impose success-based fees, or any other form of compensation
related to a reimbursed assessment;
(2) Assess any fee, directly or indirectly, to a veteran or surviving spouse
for a long-term care assessment, including, but not limited to, any consultation
fees, administrative fees, document preparation fees, or processing fees;
(3) Engage in the business of selling annuities or insurance products to
veterans;
(4) Utilize international call centers or international data centers to
process, store, or transmit a veteran's personal, financial, medical, or benefits
information; or
(5) Use a veteran's personal log-in credentials to directly access the
veteran's medical, financial, or government benefits information.

(d) A violation of this section may result in suspension, potential removal from
the program, and repayment of improperly collected funds as determined by the division.
(e) If a qualified provider falls below the required minimum of two (2) PIV card
holders due to separation or termination of employment, then the provider shall:
(1) Provide written notice to the division within five (5) business days of
falling below the minimum requirement, including an explanation of the
circumstances and the steps being taken to restore compliance; and
(2) Have a grace period of one hundred twenty (120) days to hire and
secure certification for a replacement employee. Failure to restore compliance
within the grace period results in suspension from the program until compliance
is achieved.
(f) The division may establish by rule a maximum reimbursement amount for
each long-term care assessment, not to exceed one thousand five hundred dollars
($1,500) per assessment. The reimbursement rate may be adjusted periodically to
account for inflation or documented increases in the cost of providing assessment
services. Such rules must be promulgated in accordance with the Uniform
Administrative Procedures Act, compiled in chapter 5 of this title.
4-4-203.
(a) The division shall administer the program and review billings submitted by
qualified providers.
(b) The division shall establish standardized billing procedures and
documentation requirements to ensure that assessments reimbursed under this part are
legitimate and complete.
(c) The director of TennCare shall convene an oversight committee composed
of:
(1) The director of TennCare or the director's designee;
(2) A representative from the department of finance and administration;

(3) A representative from the comptroller of the treasury;
(4) One (1) member appointed by the speaker of the senate; and
(5) One (1) member appointed by the speaker of the house of
representatives.
(d) The oversight committee shall review annual expenditures under the
program and ensure audit of submitted billings for compliance by the comptroller of the
treasury.
4-4-204.
(a) Each qualified provider participating in the program shall submit an annual
transparency report to the division on or before March 1 of each year.
(b) The report must include:
(1) The total number of veterans and surviving spouses served;
(2) The total number of claims submitted;
(3) The total number of claims approved;
(4) The average time from assessment to submission; and
(5) The total amount billed to the state under this program.
(c) On or before March 1 of each year, the division shall compile a summary
report and submit it to the speaker of the senate and the speaker of the house of
representatives and submit a copy to the legislative librarian.
SECTION 3. The director of TennCare shall apply for a block grant from the United
States Department of Veterans Affairs for the purposes of funding the reimbursements required
under this part. A reimbursement must not be made until the director certifies to the speakers of
the house of representatives and senate that said grant funds have been received and are
available for expenditure.
SECTION 4. For purposes of promulgating rules and forms, this act takes effect upon
becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it. For all other purposes, this act takes effect July
1, 2026, the public welfare requiring it.
