HOUSE RESOLUTION 192
By Glynn
A RESOLUTION to commend Heavan Raymer-Holt for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this body is pleased to recognize certain persons
who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the legislative
process; and
WHEREAS, Heavan Raymer-Holt is one such individual who has distinguished herself in
her role as a legislative intern for Representative Ronnie Glynn; Ms. Raymer-Holt's work has
resulted in innumerable benefits for the legislators she assisted and the General Assembly as a
whole; and
WHEREAS, born and raised in Nashville by Shetika Raymer and Trent Holt, Heavan
Raymer-Holt is a graduate of Hume-Fogg High School; and
WHEREAS, a recent graduate of the University of Tennessee at Martin, where she was
highly regarded by her peers, Ms. Raymer-Holt achieved academic success and earned a
bachelor's degree in political science and a minor in criminal justice; she participated in
numerous extracurricular activities, including Model UN, where she earned Outstanding
Delegation and Honorable Delegation over three years; and
WHEREAS, during her collegiate career, Heavan Raymer-Holt developed an aspiration
to attend law school and become a prosecutor; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies, Ms. Raymer-Holt brought her many
abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Heavan Raymer-Holt has
surpassed all criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
