SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 943
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to congratulate Douglas E. Kreulen upon his
induction into the Tennessee Aviation Hall of
Fame.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should salute those citizens who, through their
remarkable efforts, have distinguished themselves as community leaders of whom we can all be
proud; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Douglas E. Kreulen of Franklin, who has
been inducted into the Tennessee Aviation Hall of Fame in recognition of his exemplary service,
leadership, and lifelong contributions to the field of aviation; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Kreulen has served with acumen and alacrity as president and chief
executive officer of the Metropolitan Nashville Airport Authority (MNAA) since 2017, in which
role his guidance and vision have led the Nashville International Airport (BNA) and John C.
Tune Airport to unprecedented heights; and
WHEREAS, Doug Kreulen's vision and astute guidance as president and CEO of MNAA
have led BNA through years of expansions and improvements, making it a world-class
destination and providing Tennessee and its citizens with top tier aviation travel opportunities;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Kreulen has been a member of MNAA since 2012; before joining
MNAA, he devoted four years of service as director of operations of the Huntsville-Madison
County Airport Authority; and
WHEREAS, an Accredited Airport Executive, Mr. Kreulen is active in several civic and
industry organizations, including the American Association of Airport Executives Policy Review
Council; the Tennessee Association of Air Carrier Airports, which he serves as president; the

Williamson Inc. Board of Directors; the Nashville Convention and Visitors Corps Board of

WHEREAS, a graduate of both Leadership Huntsville and Leadership Nashville, Mr.
Kreulen earned Master of Science degrees from National War College and Troy University and
a Bachelor of Science degree from Auburn University; and
WHEREAS, prior to his distinguished career in the commercial aviation sector, Doug
Kreulen proudly served his nation as a member of the United States Air Force for twenty-seven
years before taking his much-deserved retirement at the rank of colonel; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Mr. Kreulen was named the 2019
Tennessee Aviation Person of the Year by the Tennessee Aviation Hall of Fame, 2022 Most
Admired CEO by the Nashville Business Journal, and 2024 Airport Director of the Year for
Large Hub Airports by Airport Experience News; and
WHEREAS, he also received the 2022 Award of Excellence and Airport of the Year
award from the Tennessee Aeronautics Commission for the expansion of BNA; the inaugural
Veteran's Leader Award from the Charlie and Hazel Daniels Veterans and Military Family
Center at Middle Tennessee State University; and the 2024 Chairman's Award at the thirty-ninth
annual Airport Minority Advisory Council, a most befitting honor that recognizes his efforts in
advancing opportunities for minorities and women in the aviation and aerospace industries; and
WHEREAS, the success of this State, the strength of our communities, and the overall
vitality of American society depend, in great measure, upon the dedication of people such as
Doug Kreulen, who use their considerable talents and resources to serve others and who
should be specially recognized for their public and community service; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize and congratulate Douglas E. Kreulen
