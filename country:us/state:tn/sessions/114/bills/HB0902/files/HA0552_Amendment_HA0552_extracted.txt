SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 49, Chapter 6, Part 10, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) As used in this section, "substance abuse and violence prevention program"
means a program or series of programs that:
(1) Equips students with knowledge, skills, and resilience for healthy
living and strong decision-making;
(2) Fosters long-term resilience against substance use, mental health
challenges, and preventable disorders by engaging families and communities as
active partners in student development;
(3) Includes a series of lessons that address the risk and protective
factors associated with youth substance use and violence;
(4) Is age and developmentally appropriate for the age and maturity level
of the student receiving the instruction;
(5) Aligns with the coordinated school health program guidelines
developed by the commissioner of education, in consultation with the department
of health, pursuant to § 49-1-1002;
(6) Includes drug prevention-related education that promotes anti-drug
norms and teaches drug-refusal skills and life skills training that foster the
development of personal self-management skills and general social skills; and
(7) Is provided to students throughout the school year.

(b) The department of education shall administer a three-year pilot program to
provide grants to five (5) LEAs to advance the LEA's substance abuse and violence
prevention program to avoid tobacco, alcohol, and illicit drug use by students. The pilot
program begins in the 2026-2027 school year and terminates on July 1, 2029.
(c) To receive a grant through the program, an LEA must:
(1) Have an existing substance abuse and violence prevention program
for students; and
(2) Submit a grant application to the department on a form created by the
department by the application deadline established by the department.
(d) Each LEA that receives a grant pursuant to this section shall:
(1) Instruct students in grades three through twelve (3-12) on avoiding
tobacco, alcohol, and illicit drugs by implementing a substance abuse and
violence prevention program;
(2) Prepare a report on the effectiveness of the LEA's substance abuse
and violence prevention program, including the instruction provided to students
through the program, for the immediately preceding school year; and
(3) Submit the report prepared pursuant to subdivision (d)(2) by June 30,
2027, for the 2026-2027 school year and by June 30 of each subsequent school
year that the pilot program is in effect, to the department of education, the
department of mental health and substance abuse services, the department of
human services, the chairs of the education and health and welfare committees
of the senate, the chair of the committee of the house of representatives having
jurisdiction over education, the chair of the committee of the house of
representatives having jurisdiction over health, and the chair of the committee of
the house of representatives having jurisdiction over insurance.
SECTION 2. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
