SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1079
By Oliver
A RESOLUTION to commemorate National Pencil Day in
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those inventions and innovations that have had a lasting impact on human expression,
education, and creativity; and
WHEREAS, National Pencil Day, observed annually on March 30, provides an
opportunity to celebrate the pencil, a simple yet indispensable writing instrument that has
enabled generations of individuals to record ideas, express creativity, and communicate
knowledge; and
WHEREAS, the pencil traces its origins to the sixteenth century, following the discovery
of graphite in England, and over time evolved from rudimentary writing tools into the widely used
wooden graphite pencil known today; and
WHEREAS, throughout history, advancements in pencil design, including the
development of graphite mixtures and wood casings, have contributed to the widespread
availability and utility of pencils across the world; and
WHEREAS, on March 30, 1858, Hymen Lipman received a patent for attaching an
eraser to the end of a pencil, creating a more efficient and practical writing instrument that
allowed users to easily correct mistakes; and
WHEREAS, pencils have remained an essential tool in education, art, and everyday life,
valued for their versatility, accessibility, and ability to foster learning and creativity; and
WHEREAS, even in an increasingly digital age, the pencil continues to serve as a
symbol of imagination, innovation, and the enduring importance of the written word; now,
therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we join with the good people of this State in
commemorating March 30, 2026, as National Pencil Day and to raise public awareness about
the significance of this simple yet remarkable instrument.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
