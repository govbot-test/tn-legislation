SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 927
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Master Sergeant
Roderick "Roddie" W. Edmonds, United States
Army, Medal of Honor recipient.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pride in honoring those
courageous Tennesseans whose valor and devotion to duty have brought honor to this State
and to the United States of America; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy Tennessean was the late Master Sergeant Roderick
"Roddie" W. Edmonds, United States Army, of Knoxville, who, on March 2, 2026, was
posthumously awarded the Medal of Honor, the highest military decoration awarded by the
United States government; and
WHEREAS, the Medal of Honor is conferred only upon members of the United States
Armed Forces who distinguish themselves through conspicuous gallantry and intrepidity at the
risk of life above and beyond the call of duty; and
WHEREAS, born on August 20, 1919, in South Knoxville, Master Sergeant Edmonds
served with the Regimental Headquarters Company of the 422nd Infantry Regiment, 106th
Infantry Division, United States Army, during World War II; in December 1944, he was captured
during the Battle of the Bulge and held as a prisoner of war at Stalag IX-A in Ziegenhain,
Germany; and
WHEREAS, as the senior noncommissioned officer among approximately 1,200
American prisoners of war, Master Sergeant Edmonds demonstrated extraordinary courage and
leadership when German guards ordered that only Jewish-American prisoners report for roll
call, threatening execution for those who refused; and
WHEREAS, fully aware that such segregation would likely result in persecution or death
for more than 200 Jewish-American soldiers, Master Sergeant Roddie Edmonds directed all

1,200 American prisoners to stand together in formation and refused to identify any Jewish
prisoners, declaring to the German commandant, "We are all Jews here"; and
WHEREAS, when the German commandant placed a pistol against his forehead and
demanded compliance, Master Sergeant Edmonds stood his ground and warned that executing
prisoners of war would constitute a violation of the Geneva Convention and a war crime; the
officer ultimately relented, and no Jewish prisoners were identified; and
WHEREAS, by his fearless leadership, moral courage, and unwavering devotion to his
fellow soldiers, Master Sergeant Roddie Edmonds saved the lives of more than 200 American
servicemen and upheld the highest traditions of military service; and
WHEREAS, Master Sergeant Edmonds passed away on August 8, 1985, having never
sought recognition for his heroic actions; more than eight decades after his act of bravery, he
was posthumously awarded the Medal of Honor in recognition of his extraordinary courage and
selfless leadership; and
WHEREAS, this General Assembly takes great pride in honoring the memory of Master
Sergeant Roddie Edmonds, recognizing the extraordinary courage and character he displayed
while a prisoner of war during World War II; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Master Sergeant Roderick
"Roddie" W. Edmonds and commend his extraordinary heroism and leadership, which saved
the lives of his fellow soldiers and for which he was posthumously awarded the Medal of Honor,
the nation's highest military decoration.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
