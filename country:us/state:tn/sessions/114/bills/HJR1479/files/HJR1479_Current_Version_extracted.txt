HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1479
By Reeves
A RESOLUTION to honor Carnton on the occasion of its 200th
anniversary.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly find it appropriate to honor those
Tennessee landmarks that stand as relics and testaments of the complex history of the
Volunteer State; and
WHEREAS, one such historic landmark is Carnton of Franklin, which marks its
bicentennial this year; and
WHEREAS, founded in 1826, Carnton was a center of nineteenth-century political and
social life; and
WHEREAS, the lives of the McGavock family who called Carnton home and the
enslaved who worked there for decades are instrumental components of its history; and
WHEREAS, Carnton also played a somber role as a site of profound sacrifice during the
Civil War; and
WHEREAS, in marking the bicentennial of this historic Tennessee edifice, citizens of this
State are reminded of the importance of preserving places that allow for a complete and honest
reflection on our shared history; and
WHEREAS, the bicentennial anniversary of Carnton is an opportunity to honor the
resilience and stories of everyone who lived, worked, and was impacted by events on those
grounds during the last 200 years; and
WHEREAS, Carnton is a "witness to history" that continues to tell the story of the
Volunteer State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
