HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 866
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to recognize Greer Tidwell for his honorable and
astute service to the people of Tennessee as
Deputy Commissioner of the Department of
Environment and Conservation.
WHEREAS, it is the privilege of the members of this General Assembly to specially
recognize those estimable public servants who have dedicated themselves to improving the
quality of life for their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Greer Tidwell is one such admirable individual, who evinces the greatest
integrity and probity in all his chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, after serving with distinction as Deputy Commissioner of the Tennessee
Department of Environment and Conservation (TDEC), Mr. Tidwell has taken his well-deserved
retirement this year; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure as Deputy Commissioner of TDEC, Mr. Tidwell provided
leadership to the Bureau of Conservation and advanced the department's mission on behalf of
the people of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, he also served as a leader across a broad conservation portfolio, including
Tennessee State Parks, State Natural Areas, and related divisions responsible for the
stewardship of the State's natural, cultural, and recreational resources; and
WHEREAS, under his leadership, Tennessee experienced significant growth in its public
lands system, including the addition of fourteen new Tennessee State Parks, expanding
opportunities for Tennesseans and visitors to experience the State's natural beauty, outdoor
recreation, and heritage; and

WHEREAS, these additions reflected Mr. Tidwell's conviction that access to nature is
essential to quality of life and to the long-term conservation of Tennessee's natural resources;
and
WHEREAS, Greer Tidwell also supported the protection and advancement of State
Natural Areas, reinforcing Tennessee's commitment to conserving ecologically significant
landscapes and biodiversity for future generations; and
WHEREAS, one of his most meaningful contributions was his leadership in advancing
accessibility across Tennessee's parks and natural areas, supporting the goal of making the
State's public lands among the most accessible in the nation; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Tidwell encouraged this work to be pursued with humility, learning, and
respect for lived experience, strengthening both the department's culture and its impact while
opening the outdoors to individuals who previously faced barriers to access; and
WHEREAS, Greer Tidwell was instrumental in the creation and advancement of the
Office of Outdoor Recreation, helping position Tennessee as a leader in connecting people with
the outdoors while supporting economic development, particularly in rural communities; and
WHEREAS, throughout his service, Mr. Tidwell demonstrated vision, integrity, and a
steadfast commitment to conservation, public service, and the well-being of the State of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, his leadership and dedication have left a lasting and positive impact on the
Tennessee Department of Environment and Conservation and on the citizens who benefit from
Tennessee's parks, natural areas, and outdoor resources, and it is most fitting that he be
honored on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Greer Tidwell for his honorable and astute service to
