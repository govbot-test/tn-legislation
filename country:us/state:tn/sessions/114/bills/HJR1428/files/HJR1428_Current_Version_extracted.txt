HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1428
By Alexander
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Robert "Bob" Batson
upon the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, Robert "Bob" Batson was born on November 7, 1959, and from the earliest
chapters of his life, he has demonstrated a calling toward service, compassion, and the solemn
care of others during their most difficult hours; and
WHEREAS, Bob Batson pursued his education at Jefferson State Community College,
earning an Associate of Applied Science degree in Mortuary Science, a foundation upon which
he would build more than four decades of extraordinary service to the people of Tennessee and
the nation; and
WHEREAS, after ten years as a licensed funeral director and embalmer, including
service as the contract funeral establishment for fatalities at the Fort Campbell, Kentucky,
military base, Bob Batson transitioned into executive leadership, serving as executive director of
the Tennessee Funeral Directors Association (TFDA) from September 1991 to the present, a
tenure spanning thirty-five years of steadfast dedication to the funeral service profession; and
WHEREAS, in his role as executive director, Mr. Batson managed a multifaceted
professional association; he represented the best interests of funeral service professionals
before the General Assembly, educated State legislators on professional legislation, oversaw
association finances, coordinated the annual tradeshow, managed staff, and directed the
association's for-profit subsidiary, Tennessee Funeral Securities, Inc.; and
WHEREAS, Bob Batson's expertise and commitment to public safety led him to serve as
a Mass Fatalities Management Instructor for the Tennessee Emergency Management Agency;
he is also a Certified EMI Train-the-Trainer Instructor, providing specialized training across the
State to handle large-scale recovery efforts; and

WHEREAS, since joining the Disaster Mortuary Operational Response Teams (DMORT)
in 1993, Bob Batson has served on the front lines of our nation's most profound tragedies,
answering the call without hesitation at moments when few others could; and
WHEREAS, after September 11, 2001, Mr. Batson deployed to New York, New York, as
a DMORT Family Assistance Section Leader, serving three tours; he initially coordinated
information for families of passengers and crew aboard American Flight 11 and United Flight
175 and later assisted with the identification of victims from the World Trade Center; and
WHEREAS, Bob Batson subsequently served as a DMORT Family Assistance Section
Leader in Queens, New York, assisting the New York City Medical Examiner following the crash
of American Airlines Flight 587, and as Family Assistance Section Leader on the Mississippi
Gulf Coast following Hurricane Katrina, helping establish family assistance centers for
communities devastated by that catastrophic storm; and
WHEREAS, Bob Batson served as DMORT Morgue Operations Section Leader on the
Louisiana Gulf Coast following both Hurricane Katrina and Hurricane Rita, assisting the New
Orleans Medical Examiner; and
WHEREAS, Bob Batson was deployed to Haiti following the devastating 2010
earthquake, serving as Morgue Operations Section Leader for the repatriation of American
victims and to Puerto Rico following Hurricanes Irma and Maria in the same capacity; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Batson has volunteered his expertise as chairman of the National
Council of Funeral Executives and currently serves as chairman of the Tennessee Council of
Medical Examiners, having been a board member since 2017, shaping policy and standards
that protect the public and honor the deceased; and
WHEREAS, through Bob Batson's commitment to professional development in the
funeral industry, he has been actively involved in continuing education requirements for funeral

directors and embalmer licensees, including helping to pass the first-of-its-kind continuing
education in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Bob Batson has been a stalwart and influential member of his profession;
Tennessee has led the nation by electing multiple presidents to the National Funeral Directors
Association, including a consecutive presidency for two Tennesseans; and
WHEREAS, beyond the weight of his professional accomplishments, Bob Batson is, at
his core, a devoted family man; he shares his life with his wife of forty-five years, Ruby; their two
sons, J.T. and Adam; their daughters-in-law; and four beloved granddaughters, who have been
his greatest source of joy; and
WHEREAS, he is a skilled craftsman, carpenter, electrician, handyman, painter, guitar
player, talented left-handed golfer, dog trainer, soccer official, and kindhearted person who will
have numerous projects to complete in his retirement years; and
WHEREAS, Bob Batson exemplifies the finest tradition of Tennessee public service—
steady, selfless, and defined by a profound sense of duty to those he serves; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Robert "Bob" Batson upon the occasion of his
retirement, extending to him our deep appreciation for thirty-five years of leadership at the
Tennessee Funeral Directors Association and a lifetime of service to families in their most
profound moments of grief and loss.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
