SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1022
By McNally
A RESOLUTION providing for the installation of a bust in the State
Capitol to honor Anne M. Davis, the founding
mother of Great Smoky Mountains National
Park.
WHEREAS, Anne M. Davis is universally recognized as the founding mother of Great
Smoky Mountains National Park (GSMNP); this remarkable lady not only initiated the idea for
the park, she helped generate essential public support for the project and, as a State legislator,
sponsored and passed legislation for the purchase of land to establish the park; and
WHEREAS, Ann May grew up in society in Louisville, Kentucky, where she was active in
philanthropic and church work, and graduated from Bryn Mawr College; in 1902, she married
industrialist Willis Perkins "W.P." Davis; and
WHEREAS, in 1915, the Davises relocated to Knoxville, where Mr. Davis served as
general manager of Knoxville Iron Works Company, and the couple soon became active in
community affairs; and
WHEREAS, described as "a force of nature" and "a rebel," Anne M. Davis first became
involved in conservation and environmental issues through her membership in the Knoxville
Garden Club; and
WHEREAS, the Davises frequently visited the Smokies, and in addition to enjoying the
beauty of the mountains, they recognized the potential of the Smokies in promoting tourism and
business for nearby communities, including Knoxville; and
WHEREAS, Mr. and Mrs. Davis promoted the idea of a Smoky Mountains national park
within the Knoxville business community as an engine for economic growth, and they were
active in founding the Great Smoky Mountains Conservation Association to achieve that
purpose; and

WHEREAS, through the organization, Mr. and Mrs. Davis presented a proposal for the
national park to the U.S. Secretary of the Interior and lobbied the Southern Appalachia National
Park Committee (SANPC) extensively; and
WHEREAS, their hiking trip to Mt. Le Conte so impressed SANPC members that the
Tennessee segment of the Smokies was included as a candidate for a national park; however,
legislation funding the State's purchase of land for the park stalled in the General Assembly in
1924; and
WHEREAS, Anne M. Davis then seized the reins of leadership by seeking an open seat
in the State legislature to achieve this one specific goal; and
WHEREAS, Anne M. Davis was only the third woman ever elected to the Tennessee
General Assembly, her election coming less than five years after the ratification of the 19th
Amendment that granted the right to vote to American women; and
WHEREAS, Anne M. Davis and Knoxville Mayor Ben Morton convinced the Little River
Lumber Company to sell more than 78,000 acres of land to the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, when the funding legislation encountered opposition, Anne M. Davis
organized an inspection trip for the entire legislature, and her persuasive presentation, coupled
with the beauty of the Smokies, effectively swayed critics, who had previously characterized the
area as "stump land"; and
WHEREAS, the House of Representatives passed the legislation on April 9, 1925, and
Anne M. Davis achieved her goal of establishing a national park in the Smoky Mountains; and
WHEREAS, in an appropriate tribute to Mrs. Davis and her important work on the
project, Governor Austin Peay presented her with the ostrich-feathered pen that he used to sign
the legislation into law; and
WHEREAS, Anne M. Davis then distinguished herself as the ultimate citizen-legislator;
having achieved her goal, she did not seek reelection; and
- 2 - 016738
WHEREAS, on September 2, 1940, Great Smoky Mountains National Park was formally
dedicated; Anne M. Davis attended the ceremony, as did President Franklin Roosevelt; and
WHEREAS, Anne M. Davis had moved to Gatlinburg in 1931 following her husband's
untimely passing; she enjoyed living close to her beloved Smoky Mountains and was active in
the community with the Gatlinburg Garden Club and the League of Women Voters; and
WHEREAS, in recognition of the Davises' vital work in founding GSMNP, the National
Park Service named a peak in the park in honor of Mr. Davis and a ridge in honor of Anne M.
Davis; and
WHEREAS, although she passed away in 1957, Anne M. Davis lives on through her
courage, vision, passion, and political expertise in the founding of Great Smoky Mountains
National Park; and
WHEREAS, Anne M. Davis is a most worthy candidate to be honored in the State
Capitol; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that the State Capitol Commission is requested to initiate
the creation and installation of a bust bearing the likeness of the late Anne M. Davis of Knox
County to honor her critical role in founding Great Smoky Mountains National Park.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the bust be installed in the alcove nearest to the
Senate chambers on the second floor of the State Capitol building.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the State Capitol Commission may request the
Tennessee Arts Commission to sponsor a contest, open to all interested artists, to create a bust
to honor Anne M. Davis.
- 3 - 016738
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the State Capitol Commission and the Tennessee
Arts Commission be responsible for selecting the appropriate submission for such contest and
determining the final choice of artist.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that in addition to the approval of the State Capitol
Commission, the installation of the bust is subject to the concurrence of the State Building
Commission.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the creation and installation of the bust are subject to
funds being appropriated therefor by the General Assembly.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the State Capitol Commission and the Tennessee
Arts Commission are authorized to accept private funds and in-kind donations to defray the cost
of the bust and related expenses.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that a certified copy of this resolution be transmitted to
the Tennessee Arts Commission, the State Capitol Commission, and the State Building
Commission.
- 4 - 016738
