HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1441
By Farmer
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Gary Odom Roach of
Sevierville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Gary Odom Roach; and
WHEREAS, Gary Roach was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on December 8, 1949, to Ed and Teresa Roach, Mr. Roach was raised
in Rutledge in a close-knit community that shaped his lifelong values of faith, service, and
fellowship; and
WHEREAS, a 1967 graduate of Rutledge High School, Gary Roach furthered his
education at East Tennessee State University, where he earned a Bachelor of Education
degree in 1971, and at the University of Montevallo, Alabama, where he earned a Master of
Educational Administration degree in 1974; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Roach devoted the majority of his professional life to the field of
education, serving as a teacher in Grainger County; transportation supervisor and assistant
principal in Hamblen County; assistant principal in Jefferson County; and, ultimately, principal of
Sevier County High School, where he earned the distinction as the longest-serving principal in
the school's history, with eighteen years of service; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure as principal, he provided visionary leadership that
contributed to numerous academic and extracurricular achievements, including the
establishment of the school's "Smoky Salutes" Veterans Day program and the Sevier County
High School Alumni Association, and the school's first team state championships in football in
1999 and girls' basketball in 2002; and

WHEREAS, Gary Roach's professional excellence was recognized through numerous
honors, including Tennessee Transportation Supervisor of the Year, TSSAA Regional Principal
of the Year, and Tennessee Secondary Principal of the Year, and the City of Sevierville
proclaimed April 30, 2009, as "Gary Roach Day" in honor of his retirement; and
WHEREAS, in 2023, he was inducted into the Sevier County Education Hall of Fame,
further reflecting the enduring impact of his contributions to education and his community; and
WHEREAS, a man of deep and abiding faith, Mr. Roach was a devoted member of First
Baptist Church Sevierville, where he served as a lifetime deacon and taught Sunday School for
many years, sharing his wisdom and encouragement with generations of congregants; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Gary Roach is survived by his beloved wife of nearly five decades, Darlene
Roach; his daughters, Sally (Whit) Helton, Sandra Roach, Susan (Andy) Steele, and Sarah
(Will) Allen; his grandchildren, Zachary Newsome, John Tate, Erin Tate, Preslee Cubberley,
Hunter Allen, Abigale Allen, and Mason Manning; his sister-in-law, Glenda Roach, and her
children, Willie, Candace, and Julia; his brother-in-law, Quentin Seals, and his daughter, Grant;
and a host of extended family members and friends; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his parents, Ed and Teresa Roach, and his
brother, Dennis Roach; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Roach leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public
life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Gary Odom Roach, reflecting fondly upon his
- 2 - 017970
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Roach.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
