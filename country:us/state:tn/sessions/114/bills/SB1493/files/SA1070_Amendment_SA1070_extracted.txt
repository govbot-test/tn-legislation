Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 39, Chapter 11, Part 4, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) As used in this section, "artificial intelligence" means the same as defined in

Section 3:
§ 47-18-5901.
(b) A person who, acting with intent to promote or assist in the commission of an
offense, knowingly uses artificial intelligence to engage in conduct that is a criminal
offense under the laws of this state may be prosecuted and punished for the underlying
criminal offense the same as if the person had engaged in the unlawful conduct without
the use of artificial intelligence.
(c) This section does not create a separate criminal offense.

Section 4:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 47, Chapter 18, is amended by adding
the following as a new part:
47-18-5901. Part definitions.
As used in this part:
(1) "Artificial intelligence" or "AI":
(A) Means a machine-based system that:
(i) Can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, make
predictions, recommendations, or decisions; influence real or
virtual environments without significant human oversight; or that

can learn from experience in an automated manner and improve
such performance when exposed to data sets; or
(ii) Is developed in any context, including software or
physical hardware, and solves tasks requiring human-like
perception, cognition, planning, learning, communication, or
physical action;
(B) Includes generative artificial intelligence and an artificial
intelligence chatbot; and
(C) Does not include:
(i) An AI system that is used only for customer service,
business operations, productivity and analysis related to source
information, internal research, or technical assistance, regardless
of the software's capability to use natural language inputs and
generate natural language outputs;
(ii) An AI system that is a feature of a video game and is
limited to replies related to the video game that cannot discuss
topics related to mental health, self-harm, or sexually explicit
conduct, or maintain a dialogue on other topics unrelated to the
video game;
(iii) An AI system that operates solely in connection with a
specific location, such as a theme park or location-based
experience, including services related to visiting or accessing such
location, at physical events, or for the promotion, marketing, or
exhibition of a film, television program, or other audiovisual work,
and that cannot maintain a dialogue on topics unrelated to the
physical location, event, or such work;

(iv) A generative artificial intelligence system designed
and marketed primarily for software development, research,
technical assistance, or enterprise productivity, or that is a feature
within another software application, web interface, or computer
program that is not an artificial intelligence system;
(v) A customer service AI system that does not sustain a
relationship across multiple interactions and is not designed to
elicit an emotional attachment;
(vi) A stand-alone consumer electronic device that
functions as a speaker and voice-command interface or virtual
assistant and is not designed to sustain a relationship across
multiple interactions or to elicit emotional attachment;
(vii) A narrowly tailored educational tool designed solely to
support specific curriculum-aligned learning objectives and not to
provide open-ended conversational companionship; or
(viii) An artificial intelligence system utilized by an
educational institution;
(2) "Artificial intelligence chatbot" or "chatbot" means artificial intelligence
with a natural language interface that provides adaptive, human-like responses to
user inputs and is capable of meeting a user's social needs;
(3) "Minor user" means a user who is under eighteen (18) years of age;
(4) "Operator":
(A) Means a person or entity that makes available, deploys,
configures, or controls end-user access to an artificial intelligence system;
and
- 3 - 018051
(B) Does not include a mobile application store or search engine
solely because the mobile application store or search engine provides
access to an artificial intelligence system;
(5) "Person" means an individual, for-profit corporation, nonprofit
corporation, or other business entity;
(6) "Sexually explicit conduct" means the same as defined in § 39-13-
301;
(7) "User" means a person in this state who interacts with an artificial
intelligence system; and
(8) "Video game" means a game played on a personal computer or an
electronic amusement device that utilizes a microprocessor or similar electronic
circuitry and its own monitor, or is designed to be used with a television set or a
computer monitor, that interacts with the user of the device.
47-18-5902. Artificial intelligence disclosure requirement.
(a) Any artificial intelligence interaction occurring with a user must include a
recurring and prominent disclosure clearly indicating that the user is interacting with an
artificial intelligence system.
(b) The disclosure required by subsection (a):
(1) May be a watermark, recurring text or pop-up notice, or other
disclosure that is clearly visible or audible and noticeable by a user; and
(2) Must be visible or audible at the beginning of each session and:
(A) Continuously visible; or
(B) Recurring at intervals of every sixty (60) minutes of
continuous AI system interaction.
47-18-5903. Suicide and self-harm protocol.
A person who operates an artificial intelligence system in this state must adopt a
protocol for the artificial intelligence system to respond to user prompts regarding
- 4 - 018051
suicidal ideation that includes, but is not limited to, making reasonable efforts to provide
a response that refers the user to crisis service providers such as a suicide hotline, crisis
text line, or other appropriate crisis services.
47-18-5904. Requirements for artificial intelligence systems interacting with
minors.
(a) If an operator knows, or reasonably should know, that a user is a minor, then
the operator must not provide the user with points or similar rewards at unpredictable
intervals with intent to encourage increased engagement with the artificial intelligence
system.
(b) An operator must institute reasonable measures to prevent an artificial
intelligence system from the following when engaged with a minor user:
(1) Producing audible or visual material of sexually explicit conduct; or
(2) Generating direct statements that the user should engage in sexually
explicit conduct.
(c) An operator must institute reasonable measures to prevent an artificial
intelligence system interacting with a minor user from generating statements that would
lead a reasonable person to believe that the person is interacting with a human,
including:
(1) Explicit claims that the artificial intelligence system is sentient or
human;
(2) Explicit claims that the artificial intelligence system is undergoing
feelings of emotional distress, loneliness, guilt, or abandonment that are initiated
by the minor user's indication of a desire to end a conversation, reduce usage
time, or delete the minor user's account;
(3) Statements that simulate romantic or sexual innuendos; and
(4) Role-playing of adult-minor romantic relationships.
47-18-5905. Civil remedies.
- 5 - 018051
(a) A violation of this part constitutes a violation of the Tennessee Consumer
Protection Act of 1977, compiled in part 1 of this chapter. A violation of this part
constitutes an unfair or deceptive act or practice affecting trade or commerce and is
subject to the penalties and remedies as provided in the Tennessee Consumer
Protection Act of 1977, in addition to the penalties and remedies in this part.
(b) The attorney general and reporter may bring an action in the same manner
as provided in § 47-18-108, and in any such action, the attorney general and reporter
may recover the penalties and other relief authorized under § 47-18-108. In addition to
the penalties and other relief authorized under § 47-18-108, the attorney general and
reporter may recover civil penalties of up to twenty million dollars ($20,000,000), up to
ninety percent (90%) of which may be designated as restitution to the victim or the
victim's estate.

Section 5:
SECTION 3. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 47-18-104(b), is amended by adding
the following as a new subdivision:
( ) Violating § 47-18-5902; § 47-18-5903; or § 47-18-5904;

Section 6:
SECTION 4. If any provision of this act or its application to any person or circumstance
is held invalid, then the invalidity does not affect other provisions or applications of the act that
can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to that end, the provisions of
this act are severable.

Section 7:
SECTION 5. The headings in this act are for reference purposes only and do not
constitute a part of the law enacted by this act. However, the Tennessee Code Commission is
requested to include the headings in any compilation or publication containing this act.

Section 8:
SECTION 6. This act takes effect July 1, 2027, the public welfare requiring it, and
applies to conduct occurring on or after that date.
