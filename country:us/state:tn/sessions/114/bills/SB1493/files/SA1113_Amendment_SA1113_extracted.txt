Amendment No. 3 to SB1493
Lowe
Signature of Sponsor
by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting instead the following:

Section 2:
SECTION 1.
(a) The Tennessee Artificial Intelligence (AI) Advisory Council shall study:
(1) Safety concerns confronting this state, the citizens and businesses in
this state, and the children of this state resulting from the proliferation of artificial
intelligence systems and programs and from the ubiquity of online access and
content;
(2) Potential solutions for and protections from the concerns identified;
and
(3) Methods of regulating artificial intelligence and online access utilized
by other states.
(b) The AI Advisory Council may request information and feedback from one (1)
or more third parties with specialized knowledge in, or experience with, one (1) or more
of the subject areas identified in subsection (a) to assist the Council in its study.
(c) The AI Advisory Council shall submit a report to the speaker of the senate,
the speaker of the house of representatives, the commerce and labor committee of the
senate, and the committee of the house of representatives with jurisdiction over
commerce by December 31, 2026. The report must include the AI Advisory Council's
recommendations, if any, for legislation or modification of existing rules and policies,
based on the findings of the study conducted pursuant to subsection (a).

Section 3:
SECTION 2. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
