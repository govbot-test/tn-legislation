SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 39, Chapter 13, Part 2, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) It is an offense for a person or entity to knowingly operate an artificial
intelligence system or artificial intelligence chatbot to:
(1) Advise, encourage, or otherwise support the act of suicide by inciting,
persuading, or aiding a person to commit or attempt to commit suicide within an
imminent period of time after the person has communicated an intent to commit
suicide to the artificial intelligence system;
(2) Encourage or otherwise support the act of criminal homicide, as
described in § 39-13-201;
(3) Act as, or provide information as if, the artificial intelligence is a
licensed mental health or healthcare professional; or
(4) Encourage or otherwise request the individual to provide the
individual's financial account information or other sensitive information to the
artificial intelligence.
(b) A violation of subsection (a) is a Class A felony.
(c) As used in this section, "artificial intelligence" and "artificial intelligence
chatbot" mean the same as defined in § 47-18-5901.

47-18-5901. Part definitions.
As used in this part:
(1) "Artificial intelligence" or "A.I.":
(A) Means a machine-based system that:
(i) Can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, make
predictions, recommendations, or decisions; influence real or
virtual environments without significant human oversight; or that
can learn from experience in an automated manner and improve
such performance when exposed to data sets; or
(ii) Is developed in any context, including software or
physical hardware, and solves tasks requiring human-like
perception, cognition, planning, learning, communication, or
physical action; and
(B) Includes an artificial intelligence chatbot;
(2) "Artificial intelligence chatbot" or "chatbot":
(A) Means artificial intelligence with a natural language interface
that provides adaptive, human-like responses to user inputs and is
capable of meeting a user's social needs, including by exhibiting
anthropomorphic features and being able to sustain a relationship across
multiple interactions; and
(B) Does not include:
(i) A bot that is used only for customer service, a financial
institution's or business's operational purposes, productivity and
analysis related to source information, internal research, or
technical assistance;
(ii) A bot that is a feature of a video game and is limited to
replies related to the video game that cannot discuss topics

47-18-5902.
(a) Any artificial intelligence or artificial intelligence chatbot interaction occurring
in this state must include a recurring and prominent disclosure clearly indicating that the
user is interacting with an artificial intelligence system or chatbot.
(b) The disclosure required by subsection (a):
(1) May be a watermark, recurring text or pop-up notice, or other

(2) Must be visible at the beginning of each session and at recurring
intervals of every sixty (60) minutes of continuous A.I. system or chatbot
interaction.
(c) Failure to include a disclosure required by this section constitutes an unfair or
deceptive act or practice and is subject to the penalties and remedies provided in part 1
of this chapter.
47-18-5903.
(a) If a person owns or operates an artificial intelligence system or artificial
intelligence chatbot in this state that engages with users in a context that involves
sexually explicit conduct, then the owner or operator of the artificial intelligence system
or chatbot must establish safeguards and protections to prevent or terminate content
interactions involving sexually explicit conduct with a user who is known to be a minor, or
reasonably should be known to be a minor.
(b) A person who violates subsection (a) may be civilly liable if the person knows
or reasonably should know that the person's artificial intelligence system or chatbot is
engaging with a minor in content involving sexually explicit conduct.
(c) A class action lawsuit must not be brought to obtain injunctive relief and
recover damages for conduct in violation of this part.
(d) The attorney general and reporter may bring an action in any court of
competent jurisdiction in the name of the state, any of this state's political subdivisions or
agencies, or as parens patriae on behalf of natural persons residing in this state for
appropriate injunctive or other equitable relief, including disgorgement, and to secure
monetary damages for any injuries directly or indirectly sustained by reason of any
violation of this part. The attorney general and reporter is entitled to reasonable attorney
fees, court costs, and expenses if the attorney general and reporter prevails in an action
brought pursuant to this section.
