Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 62, Chapter 76, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) As used in this section:
(1) "Biometric data" means data generated by automatic measurements
of an individual's biological characteristics, including, but not limited to, a palm
print or scan, voiceprint, eye retina or iris scan, physical or digital photograph,
video or audio recording, physical or digital fingerprints, or other unique biological
pattern or characteristic, that is used to identify a specific individual;
(2) "Board" means a board, commission, agency, or other governmental
entity created pursuant to this title or title 4 that issues a license, certificate, or
registration to engage in a profession or occupation authorized under this title or
title 4;
(3) "Contractor" means a third-party contractor of a board that conducts
testing for the issuance or renewal of a license, certificate, or registration by the
board, or that facilitates the completion of continuing education requirements by
the holder of such license, certificate, or registration; and
(4) "Qualifying objection" means a religious or sincerely held belief or
objection to the collection of biometric data.
(b) A board or contractor shall provide a reasonable accommodation to an
individual with a qualifying objection from a requirement to provide biometric data as a

completing continuing education requirements for the purpose of obtaining, maintaining,
or renewing a license, certificate, or registration issued under this title.
(c) This section does not prohibit a board or contractor from obtaining:
(1) A physical or digital copy of an individual's fingerprints or government-
issued photo identification card for the purpose of ensuring that the individual is
qualified to take a test or exam or complete continuing education requirements;
or
(2) A physical or digital copy of an individual's fingerprints or other
identifying information as required by another law for the purpose of conducting a
criminal background check.

Section 3:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 63, Chapter 1, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) As used in this section:
(1) "Biometric data" means data generated by automatic measurements
of an individual's biological characteristics, including, but not limited to, a palm
print or scan, voiceprint, eye retina or iris scan, physical or digital photograph,
video or audio recording, physical or digital fingerprints, or other unique biological
pattern or characteristic, that is used to identify a specific individual;
(2) "Board" means the division or a board, commission, agency, or other
governmental entity created pursuant to this title or title 4 that issues a license,
certificate, or registration to engage in a profession or occupation authorized
under this title or title 4;
(3) "Contractor" means a third-party contractor of a board that conducts
testing for the issuance or renewal of a license, certificate, or registration by the
board, or that facilitates the completion of continuing education requirements by
the holder of such license, certificate, or registration; and

(4) "Qualifying objection" means a religious or sincerely held belief or
objection to the collection of biometric data.
(b) A board or contractor shall provide a reasonable accommodation to an
individual with a qualifying objection from a requirement to provide biometric data as a
prerequisite to taking an exam or test for licensure, certification, or registration or
completing continuing education requirements for the purpose of obtaining, maintaining,
or renewing a license, certificate, or registration issued under this title.
(c) This section does not prohibit a board or contractor from obtaining a physical
or digital copy of an individual's fingerprints or government-issued photo identification
card for the purpose of ensuring that the individual is qualified to take a test or exam or
complete continuing education requirements.
(d) This section does not prohibit or limit a board, the division, or a contractor
acting on behalf of a board from obtaining fingerprints or other identifying information as
required by § 63-1-116 or another law for the purpose of conducting a criminal
background check.

Section 4:
SECTION 3. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 49, Chapter 5, Part 1, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a) As used in this section:
(1) "Agency" means the department of education, state board of
education, or other state or local entity that issues a license;
(2) "Biometric data" means data generated by automatic measurements
of an individual's biological characteristics, including, but not limited to, a palm
print or scan, voiceprint, eye retina or iris scan, physical or digital photograph,
video or audio recording, physical or digital fingerprints, or other unique biological
pattern or characteristic, that is used to identify a specific individual;
(3) "Contractor" means a third-party contractor of an agency that
conducts testing for the issuance or renewal of a license by the agency, or that

facilitates the completion of continuing education requirements by the holder of
such license;
(4) "License" means a Tennessee educator license or other temporary
certificate or permit authorizing an individual to teach in grades kindergarten
through twelve (K-12) in this state; and
(5) "Qualifying objection" means a religious or sincerely held belief or
objection to the collection of biometric data.
(b) An agency or contractor shall provide a reasonable accommodation to an
individual with a qualifying objection from a requirement to provide biometric data as a
prerequisite to taking an exam or test for licensure, certification, or registration or
completing continuing education requirements for the purpose of obtaining, maintaining,
or renewing a license, certificate, or registration issued under this title.
(c) This section does not prohibit an agency or contractor from obtaining:
(1) A physical or digital copy of an individual's fingerprints or government-
issued photo identification card for the purpose of ensuring that the individual is
qualified to take a test or exam or complete continuing education requirements;
or
(2) A physical or digital copy of an individual's fingerprints or other
identifying information as required by another law for the purpose of conducting a
criminal background check.

Section 5:
SECTION 4. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it,
and applies to contracts entered into, renewed, or modified on or after the effective date of this
act.
