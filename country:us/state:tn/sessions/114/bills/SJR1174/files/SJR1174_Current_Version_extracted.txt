SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1174
By Watson
A RESOLUTION to recognize and commend Hoppy's North River
Corner Market, home of Chattanooga's Best
Classic Burger.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize
those exemplary local restaurants that, through their unwavering commitment to excellence, are
a credit to this State; and
WHEREAS, one such notable establishment is Hoppy's North River Corner Market in
Chattanooga, one of the best neighborhood markets in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, located at 100 W. Bell Avenue, Hoppy's, a neighborhood market since
1940, has been updated and reimagined over the years; the establishment currently offers more
than 100 varieties of beer and seltzer in addition to breakfast biscuits, burgers, Phillys, and
Reubens; and
WHEREAS, Hoppy's North River Corner Market recently earned the distinction of "Best
Classic Burger" during Chattanooga Burger Week, an accolade it also claimed in 2025; Hoppy's
won the 2026 competition with its Swiss Onion Burger, which features melted Swiss cheese and
slow-cooked caramelized onions; and
WHEREAS, in addition to back-to-back "Best Classic Burger" honors, Hoppy's was
recognized as Business Rate's Best Convenience Store in Chattanooga during its Best of 2026
Awards; and
WHEREAS, Hoppy's North River Corner Market has long been a beloved staple of the
Chattanooga community; and
WHEREAS, it is our privilege to recognize this outstanding establishment on this
momentous occasion; now, therefore,
SJR1174
018313
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize the owners and employees of Hoppy's
North River Corner Market, home of Chattanooga's Best Classic Burger, extending our
congratulations and our best wishes for every continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
