SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1157
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Alberto R. Gonzales for
his distinguished career in public service.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those individuals who, through their professional achievements and public service, have made
significant contributions to the advancement of the law and the betterment of their communities;
and
WHEREAS, Alberto R. Gonzales is one such estimable individual, whose distinguished
career in law and public service has spanned state, national, and academic leadership; and
WHEREAS, after attending the United States Air Force Academy, Mr. Gonzales
received a Bachelor of Arts degree from Rice University and a Juris Doctor degree from Harvard
Law School, establishing the strong academic foundation that would support his distinguished
legal career; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Gonzales has served with distinction as dean of Belmont University
College of Law and presently holds the Doyle Rogers Distinguished Chair of Law, providing
thoughtful leadership and guidance in the education and preparation of future members of the
legal profession; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure at Belmont University, Alberto Gonzales has strengthened
the standing of the College of Law as a respected institution of legal education while fostering a
culture of academic excellence, professional integrity, and public service among its students;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Gonzales has compiled an extensive and distinguished record of public
service, including service as the Attorney General of the United States during the administration

of President George W. Bush, as Counsel to the President, and as a Justice on the Supreme
Court of Texas; and
WHEREAS, Alberto Gonzales's service at the national and state levels also includes
roles as the Texas Secretary of State and General Counsel to the Governor of Texas; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his government service, Mr. Gonzales practiced law in the
private sector as a partner with the Houston-based law firm of Vinson & Elkins and served as a
visiting professor and recruitment consultant at Texas Tech University; and
WHEREAS, in each of these roles, he demonstrated a commitment to the rule of law
and to the careful consideration of complex legal issues at the highest levels of government;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Gonzales has also rendered valuable service through his involvement
with the Uniform Law Commission, contributing to its mission of developing well-drafted,
nonpartisan legislation that promotes clarity, stability, and uniformity in state laws; and
WHEREAS, through his work with the Uniform Law Commission, Alberto Gonzales has
contributed to strengthening and harmonizing the legal frameworks upon which state
governments rely, thereby supporting the effective administration of justice and the consistent
application of law across the United States; and
WHEREAS, throughout his career, Mr. Gonzales has exemplified integrity, leadership,
and dedication to public service, earning the respect and admiration of colleagues, students,
and the broader legal community; and
WHEREAS, Alberto Gonzales has received numerous honors, including recognition as a
Distinguished Alumnus of Rice University, the Harvard Law School Association Award, the
Central Intelligence Agency Director's Award, and the Office of the Secretary of Defense Medal
for Exceptional Public Service; and

WHEREAS, he has also been recognized nationally for his contributions as a leader and
role model, including being named Hispanic American of the Year by HISPANIC magazine and
one of the twenty-five most influential Hispanics in America by TIME Magazine; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we recognize Mr. Gonzales for his many contributions to the
legal profession and for the positive impact he has made on the institutions he has served; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Alberto R. Gonzales for his
distinguished career in public service, his leadership at Belmont University College of Law, and
his contributions to the Uniform Law Commission and extend to him our best wishes for
continued success in all his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
