SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 949
By Watson
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Laura Jane Travis for her
service to the citizens of Rhea County.
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that the members of this General Assembly should
acknowledge and applaud those community leaders who are wholly devoted to enhancing the
quality of life for their fellow citizens and improving and enriching the place they all call home;
and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Laura Jane Travis of Rhea County, who has
distinguished herself as a citizen of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, an active member of the Rhea County Republican Party since 2012, Ms.
Travis, as a member of Strong Women, coordinated a 2016 event at the Rhea County
Courthouse featuring First Lady Crissy Haslam; the event raised $14,000 in funds for Rhea
Historical Society and the United Way of Rhea County, earmarked for the Imagination Library;
and
WHEREAS, Laura Travis created and implemented the Rhea County Restoration Task
Force, also serving as its chairman; this eighteen-month project, begun in 2017, secured
funding to upgrade and renovate portions of the Rhea County Courthouse, in addition to
renovating the entirety of the Rhea Heritage and Scopes Trial Museum; and
WHEREAS, as chairman of the Task Force, she coordinated with the Tennessee State
Museum, the Tennessee State Historian, and various local and elected officials and leaders;
and
WHEREAS, in further testament to her dedication to her community, Ms. Travis also
created a committee composed exclusively of women volunteers to raise awareness and funds
to renovate, decorate, and prepare the 12th Judicial District Children's Advocacy Center; the

come.
