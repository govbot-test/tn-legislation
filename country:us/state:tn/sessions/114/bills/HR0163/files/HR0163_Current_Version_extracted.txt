HOUSE RESOLUTION 163
By White
A RESOLUTION to commemorate April 11, 2026, as Animation
Day in Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should specially recognize those citizens and
organizations that contribute to the cultural, educational, and economic vitality of the State of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, animation is a dynamic and evolving art form that blends visual arts,
storytelling, performance, music, and technology to inform, educate, and inspire audiences of all
ages; and
WHEREAS, animation represents a legitimate and growing career path for Tennessee
students, as it is an industry with real economic impact; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee's creative economy generates billions of dollars in annual
economic impact and supports hundreds of thousands of jobs, with digital media, motion picture
production, and creative services, including animation, playing an increasingly significant role in
that growth; and
WHEREAS, while animation is often associated with entertainment, its influence reaches
much further; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee's colleges, universities, career and technical education
programs, and independent artists are equipping students with animation and digital production
skills that align with the demands of a competitive global marketplace; and
WHEREAS, Animation Day will celebrate the artists, innovators, and educators who are
advancing this important art form, industry, and professional pathway, while encouraging
continued investment in arts and creative workforce development; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that April 11, 2026,
be commemorated as Animation Day in Tennessee to celebrate the art and the profession of
animation and the people across our State who bring stories to life.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
