HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 851
By Bricken
A RESOLUTION to recognize Dr. Gary Arnold Flandro for his
foundational role in the development of NASA's
Voyager program.
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that the members of this General Assembly should
recognize those individuals whose vision and intellect have advanced human knowledge and
brought distinction to this State and nation; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Gary Arnold Flandro, a renowned aerospace engineer and professor
emeritus at the University of Tennessee Space Institute (UTSI), is one such individual, having
made groundbreaking contributions to space exploration and scientific advancement; and
WHEREAS, born on March 30, 1934, in Salt Lake City, Utah, Dr. Flandro earned a
Bachelor of Science degree in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Utah in 1957,
followed by a Master of Science degree and Doctor of Philosophy degree in Aeronautics from
the California Institute of Technology, where his research addressed critical instabilities in rocket
motor design; and
WHEREAS, in 1964, while working as a summer student at the Jet Propulsion
Laboratory, Dr. Gary Flandro discovered a rare planetary alignment among the outer planets, an
occurrence that happens only once every 175 years; and
WHEREAS, this discovery, which became known as the "Grand Tour," made it possible
to design a mission using gravity-assist maneuvers to reach multiple outer planets in a single
flight path, a concept that laid the groundwork for what would become NASA's historic Voyager
program; and
WHEREAS, despite initial skepticism from experts, Dr. Flandro persisted in
demonstrating the feasibility and scientific promise of the mission, ultimately influencing one of
the most successful space exploration initiatives in history; and

WHEREAS, in recognition of this discovery and its impact, Dr. Gary Flandro received the
British Interplanetary Society's M. N. Golovine Award in 1970 and was awarded the NASA
Exceptional Achievement Medal in 1998 for his seminal contributions to multi-outer-planet
mission design; and
WHEREAS, his academic career included serving as the Boling Chair of Excellence in
Space Propulsion at UTSI, where he advanced the study of rocket propulsion and mentored the
next generation of engineers and scientists; and
WHEREAS, he currently serves as Vice President and Chief Engineer of Gloyer-Taylor
Laboratories; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Flandro's professional achievements have also earned him the General
Henry H. H. Arnold Award and fellowship in the American Institute of Aeronautics and
Astronautics; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Gary Flandro's lifetime of scientific innovation and commitment to
education has brought honor to the State of Tennessee and inspired generations of engineers,
students, and space enthusiasts alike; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Dr. Gary Arnold Flandro for his extraordinary
contributions to aerospace engineering and his critical role in the formation of NASA's Voyager
program, extending our gratitude for his lasting legacy in science and higher education and our
best wishes for every continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
