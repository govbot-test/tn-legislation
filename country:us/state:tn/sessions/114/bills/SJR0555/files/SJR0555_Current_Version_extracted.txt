SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 555
By Roberts
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Steve Haley of
Robertson County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Steve Haley; and
WHEREAS, Steve Haley was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Springfield on December 7, 1959, Steve Haley was the son of
Johnny and Rena Wix Haley of Guthrie, Kentucky, where he began his business career at the
age of ten, selling Grit newspapers by bicycle on the streets of Guthrie; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of Todd County Central High School and Austin Peay State
University, Mr. Haley learned the egg business on his grandfather's farm and was employed by
Hudson Bros. Egg Company, which was the beginning of his forty-five-year career in the
grocery business; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Haley later joined RJR/Nabisco and was named Salesman of the Year
for the Nashville Division in 1992; he ended his career with Private Label Marketing, a sales and
merchandising firm for Kroger; and
WHEREAS, seeking to serve his fellow citizens, Steve Haley was elected to represent
District 11 of the Robertson County Commission in 2006; he served his constituents with
acumen and alacrity, attending countless committee meetings; and
WHEREAS, an active part of his community, Mr. Haley was a member of the Robert
Penn Warren Birthplace Committee, coached and refereed YMCA youth soccer for several
years, and acted in several productions of the Robertson County Players; and

WHEREAS, a man of many interests, Steve Haley was a lifelong farmer, a dedicated fan
of Springfield High School, the University of Kentucky, and the Cincinnati Reds, and a published
author, whose column, "The Guthrie I Remember," was published in four regional newspapers
and online, winning an award from the Kentucky Press Association; and
WHEREAS, he was a devout member of Tiny Town Baptist Church of Guthrie, where he
was an ordained deacon, taught Sunday School, and Disciple Bible Study; he was also a
generous supporter of The Gideons International and Fellowship of Christian Athletes; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Haley was also deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Steve Haley is survived by his wife, Elaine Cook Haley; his children,
Timothy (Laura) Haley and Sarah Haley (fiancé Dr. Garrison Johnston); and grandson, Arthur
James Haley; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his sisters, Denise
Haley and Rhonda Haley; his aunt, Joan Wix; and a host of extended family members and dear
friends; and
WHEREAS, Steve Haley leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Steve Haley, reflecting
fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life
with courage and conviction.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Haley.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
