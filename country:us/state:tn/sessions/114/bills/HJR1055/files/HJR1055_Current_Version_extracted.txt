HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1055
By Stinnett
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Tom Greene of
Friendsville.
WHEREAS, it is the privilege of the members of this General Assembly to honor the
memory of Tom Greene of Friendsville; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Greene was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, Tom Greene grew up in Friendsville and graduated from Friendsville High
School; he began his career working at Fort Loudon Electric in 1973 and retired after forty-six
years of devoted service; and
WHEREAS, he began volunteering with the Friendsville Volunteer Fire Department in his
early twenties and served with the department for over fifty years, during which time he acted as
fire chief for more than a decade, eventually retiring in 2020; and
WHEREAS, devoted to serving his fellow citizens and giving back to those most in need,
Mr. Greene completed many construction projects over the years with the Blount County
Community Action Agency; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Greene was exceptionally kind and always willing to help without
expecting anything in return; if someone needed help, he was, without fail, the first person there
to commit himself to assisting; and
WHEREAS, Tom Greene gave so much of his time and money back to the Friendsville
community and to Blount County as a whole; he was truly a pillar of the community; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and

WHEREAS, he is sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his beloved wife of fifty-five
years, Vicki Philips Greene; daughter, Crystal Greene; brother and sister-in-law, Edmond and
Betty Greene; sister-in-law, Randi Potter Greene; nephews, Jonathan (Tabitha) Greene, Jeff
(Heather) Greene, and Edmond (Kelly) Greene, Jr.; and several great- nieces and -nephews;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Greene was preceded in death by his parents, Hugh L. and Mary
Evelyn Bowerman Greene; brother, John L. Greene; and daughter, Jennifer L. Greene; and
WHEREAS, Tom Greene has left behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Tom Greene, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Greene.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
