SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 870
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Officer Michael J.
Douglas of the Metropolitan Nashville Police
Department.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pride in recognizing those
venerable law enforcement officers who are devoted to serving their communities; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Officer Michael J. Douglas of the Metropolitan
Nashville Police Department, who has built a distinguished career marked by professionalism,
leadership, and commitment to his community; and
WHEREAS, a Mounted Patrol Officer currently partnered with Tucker, a four-year-old
Clydesdale-Percheron cross, Officer Michael Douglas demonstrated exceptional dedication
during the January ice storm that left the Mounted Patrol barn without power for nine days; and
WHEREAS, during the first three days of the outage, Officer Douglas remained at the
barn, ensuring the department's eight horses were properly cared for despite the extreme cold
and that they received adequate hay and water to help regulate their body temperatures; and
WHEREAS, his actions during that challenging time exemplified the hard-working and
selfless qualities for which he is known by his peers, and the Mounted Patrol Unit is fortunate to
have Officer Douglas as a team member; and
WHEREAS, a 2007 graduate of the police academy, Officer Douglas has devoted nearly
nineteen years to serving the Nashville community; he has held a variety of assignments and
began in patrol operations before joining the Central Precinct Bikes Unit; and
WHEREAS, Officer Douglas spent ten years with the Motorcycle Unit, where he was
named Officer of the Year in 2017, before transitioning to the Mounted Patrol Unit, in which unit
he has served for the past three years; and

WHEREAS, he has completed 150 hours of Basic Mounted Patrol training and continues
to advance his skills each year through Mounted Patrol In-Service training; he has also pursued
additional professional development outside of the department, attending the Mounted Police
Colloquium in Lexington, Kentucky, and he completed the 2023 Master Horse Program at the
University of Tennessee Institute of Agriculture; and
WHEREAS, born in Cleveland, Ohio, in 1968, Officer Douglas currently resides in
Lebanon with his beloved wife, Leslie Douglas, and their four corgis and five horses; and
WHEREAS, this esteemed law enforcement professional has brought honor to the State
of Tennessee and should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Officer Michael J. Douglas of the
Metropolitan Nashville Police Department on his dedication to both public service and animal
care during the January 2026 ice storm and extend to him our highest approbation for a job well
done.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
