by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:

Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 36-4-132, is amended by adding the
following as a new subsection:
(d)
(1) A guardian ad litem appointed by the court must complete training
appropriate to that role. The training must include, but is not limited to, training in
early childhood, child, and adolescent development provided by a qualified
professional.
(2) Notwithstanding subdivision (d)(1), a court may appoint a person as a
guardian ad litem who has not yet completed the training required by this
subsection (d); provided, that the person must complete the required training
within three (3) years following the date of the person's initial appointment as a
guardian ad litem.

Section 3:
SECTION 2. A person serving as a guardian ad litem appointed pursuant to Tennessee
Code Annotated, Section 36-4-132, prior to July 1, 2026, may continue to be appointed and
serve as a guardian ad litem notwithstanding the training requirements of this act; provided, that
the person must complete the required training before July 1, 2029.

Section 4:
SECTION 3. This act takes effect July 1, 2026, the public welfare requiring it.
