SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 966
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to commend and honor CASA of East
Tennessee on the occasion of its fortieth
anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
worthy organizations that are dedicated to improving the lives of children and families; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy organization is CASA of East Tennessee, which is
celebrating its fortieth anniversary of operation in 2026; and
WHEREAS, CASA's vision is that every abused and neglected child is given the
opportunity to thrive in a safe and loving home; the organization carries out this vision with
trained volunteers who advocate for these children, empowering them to express their concerns
and fears, as well as their hopes and dreams, and to be heard by their families and the court;
and
WHEREAS, since its establishment in Knox County in 1986, CASA has intervened to
represent the best interests of each child it serves; the Knox County Juvenile Court relies on
CASA to provide detailed information regarding every aspect of a child's life in order to aid in
making the best possible decision regarding placement and needed services; and
WHEREAS, the court routinely accepts ninety-seven percent of CASA's
recommendations regarding placement and essential services, and six months after case
closure, ninety-nine percent of children served remain in permanent homes; in 2025, of the 146
children whose cases closed, 142 were successfully reunified with parents, placed with kin, or
adopted; and
WHEREAS, this fiscal year, CASA of East Tennessee has seen a remarkable surge in
community impact, powered by fifty-three active volunteers, up from thirty-nine volunteers the

previous year; these dedicated advocates have already donated 1,870 hours of service,
reaching 172 children, up from 128 the previous year; and
WHEREAS, for four decades, CASA of East Tennessee has worked daily to fulfill its
mission to improve the lives of abused and neglected children through trained volunteers who
advocate for a safe, permanent, loving home; and
WHEREAS, the well-being and safety of our children are of paramount importance to our
future, and it is fitting that we should recognize and honor CASA of East Tennessee and its
volunteers for their extraordinary dedication to helping the children of our State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate CASA of East
Tennessee upon the occasion of its fortieth anniversary and commend the volunteers for their
ongoing commitment to providing outstanding services to Tennessee's children.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
