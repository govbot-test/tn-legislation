HOUSE RESOLUTION 174
By Behn
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Stephanie Ann
Wagner on the celebration of her sixtieth
birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we pay tribute to those citizens who are celebrating special
occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, Stephanie Ann Wagner will celebrate her sixtieth birthday this year, a
milestone that will be commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of life's rich pageant;
and
WHEREAS, Ms. Wagner was born in 1966 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and grew up
in the small town of West Milford, New Jersey; and
WHEREAS, Stephanie Wagner was quite the athlete in her younger years, playing
hockey, tennis, and softball, and eventually being selected "Most Athletic" by her high school;
and
WHEREAS, Ms. Wagner earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Radio and TV from William
Paterson University and dreamed of working in the music industry; and
WHEREAS, she moved to Nashville in 1990 and has made a good life there; she
currently works at the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum and loves showing off her city
to friends and family when they are in town; and
WHEREAS, Stephanie Wagner is devoted to her partner of thirty-six years; they have a
wonderfully diverse group of friends and enjoy Disney cruises and park visits; and
WHEREAS, on this momentous occasion, it is most appropriate that we honor Ms.
Wagner for the love she has shared, the memories she has helped create, and the enduring
example she continues to set for her family; and

WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby
honor and congratulate Stephanie Ann Wagner on the celebration of her sixtieth birthday and
extend to her our best wishes for many happy returns of the day.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
