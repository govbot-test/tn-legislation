HOUSE RESOLUTION 197
By Pearson
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Karmen Winters for her
upcoming college graduation.
WHEREAS, it is both fitting and proper that we recognize the achievements and
aspirations of young leaders whose dedication to service, education, and community uplift and
embody the very best of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Karmen Winters, a proud resident of Memphis, has demonstrated a
steadfast commitment to personal growth, academic achievement, and community engagement;
and
WHEREAS, a graduate of the City University School of Liberal Arts, where she earned
her high school diploma with a strong academic record, Ms. Winters has continued her
educational journey through her studies at Austin Peay State University and LeMoyne-Owen
College, from which she will graduate on Saturday, May 9, 2026, with a Bachelor of Science
degree in Sociology; and
WHEREAS, guided by a passion for service, Ms. Winters aspires to become a social
worker, seeking to uplift individuals and families through compassion, advocacy, and dedicated
care; and
WHEREAS, she has distinguished herself through her strong character and skillset,
including leadership, critical thinking, communication, and active listening—qualities that are
essential to building stronger communities; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Winters has contributed meaningfully to the workforce through various
roles, including internships with J. Adams Financial Services and the Shelby County Assessor
of Property, participation in the Memphis Ambassador Program, and service in the hospitality
industry; and

WHEREAS, her commitment to service extends deeply into the community, as
evidenced by her volunteer work with Le Bonheur Children's Hospital, the Memphis Food Bank,
and Peabody Elementary School, and her support of breast cancer awareness initiatives; and
WHEREAS, she has been actively engaged in student and civic life through
organizations such as the Teenage Improvement Club, Future Business Leaders of America,
cheerleading, and, most recently, as a Social Media Senator in the Student Government
Association; and
WHEREAS, her excellence has been recognized through numerous honors, including
being named Student of the Year, placing in the NAACP Teenager of the Year competition, and
earning distinction as a Kappa Debutante; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Winters represents the promise of a new generation—grounded in
service, driven by purpose, and committed to building a more just and compassionate society;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and
commend Karmen Winters for her dedication to education, her service to the Memphis
community, and her aspiration to improve the lives of others through social work and extend to
her our best wishes for continued success and impact in all her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
