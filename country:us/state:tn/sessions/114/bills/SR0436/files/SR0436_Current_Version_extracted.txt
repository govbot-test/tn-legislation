SENATE RESOLUTION 436
By Harshbarger
A RESOLUTION to honor the life and legacy of Fred Walton of
Kingsport.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Fred Walton of
Kingsport; and
WHEREAS, a legendary, celebrated head coach of the Dobyns-Bennett High School
football team and the first school alumnus to serve in that capacity, Fred Walton inspired
countless athletes and brought great pride to his community; and
WHEREAS, a star athlete during his playing days at Dobyns-Bennett High School, Fred
Walton earned All-American honors at quarterback for Lees-McRae Junior College before going
on to earn his master's degree from East Tennessee State University; and
WHEREAS, Fred Walton began his coaching career in Wise, Virginia, before being
promoted to head coach at Dobyns-Bennett High School in 1972; over the next ten years, his
teams never endured a losing season, and he became the winningest football coach in school
history at the time; and
WHEREAS, Coach Walton was named conference coach of the year five times and his
team won at least a share of four conference titles (1974, 1976, 1977, and 1978), making the
TSSAA playoffs in 1974, 1976, and 1978, in the era when only the top seed from each
conference qualified, and reaching the state semifinals in 1976; and
WHEREAS, after his coaching career, Coach Walton served as the color commentator
for the Dobyns-Bennett football radio broadcasts for twenty-six years, staying close to the
program; and
WHEREAS, a mentor to many both on and off the gridiron, he served in multiple
leadership roles for Kingsport City Schools during his career as an educator, including tenures

at Washington Elementary School, John Sevier Middle School, and later Dobyns-Bennett as the
vocational director; and
WHEREAS, an active part of the Kingsport community, Coach Walton was a charter
member of the Kingsport Boys Club Hall of Fame, a former president of the Dobyns-Bennett
Alumni Association, a member of the Dobyns-Bennett Alumni Hall of Fame, and a charter
member of The Crossings Golf Club; he spent two decades as a member of First Kingsport
Credit Union Board of Directors, including eighteen years as president; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Fred Walton leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this inspirational
leader and iconic coach; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor the memory of Fred
Walton, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and exceptional career. We express
our sympathy and offer our condolences to the family of Coach Walton.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
