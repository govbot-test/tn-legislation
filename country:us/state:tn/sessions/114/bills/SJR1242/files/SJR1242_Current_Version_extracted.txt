SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1242
By Crowe
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of R.L. McKinney of East
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of R.L. McKinney of East Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Mr. McKinney was a highly respected member of his community and a
consummate professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow
citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on November 1, 1953, Mr. McKinney was a skilled craftsman and
devoted fifty-four years of dedicated service as an HVAC-R technician with Johnson City
Schools; and
WHEREAS, R.L. McKinney served his country with honor as a member of the 776th
Army National Guard, earning the respect and admiration of his fellow servicemen and
community alike; and
WHEREAS, a leader in his community, Mr. McKinney was a fifty-year member and past
Master of Watauga Masonic Lodge #662; he was wholly devoted to his faith and attended Doe
River Baptist Church, where he served in several capacities; and
WHEREAS, R.L. McKinney was a blessing to all who were fortunate to know him, and
he will be remembered for his quick wit, passion for travel, insatiable curiosity, and a boundless
kindness that left a lasting mark on everyone who crossed his path; and
WHEREAS, Mr. McKinney was deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, left to cherish his memory are his beloved wife of forty-nine years, Juanita
Culbert McKinney; his sons, Matthew N. McKinney and Andrew P. McKinney; his grandson, C.J.

McKinney; his special nephew, Tim (Christy) McKinney; his brother, Darrell Tolley; his sister,
Shannon (Carlos) Posada; his cousin, Ronnie (Phyllis) Campbell; a host of nieces and
nephews; his cherished bonus family, Sam (Allie) Sanders, Laura Garland, Dave Baker, Eddie
(Diana) Buchanan, and Mike Peters; his special friends, Dr. Bill Neely and Melissa Leake; and
his brothers of Watauga Lodge #622; and
WHEREAS, R.L. McKinney was preceded in death by his parents, Ralph and Myrtle
McKinney; Odell Whitehead and Helen Tolley; his sister, Tina Callahan; his mother-in-law,
Cookie Culbert; and his cousin, Rod Whitehead; and
WHEREAS, warm-hearted and endlessly curious, Mr. McKinney was a unique soul who
had the ability to turn ordinary encounters into stories worth retelling; he found profound joy in
the simple beauty of the mountains and his faithful canine companions, Tagan, Margaret, and
Jesse; and
WHEREAS, R.L. McKinney leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pause to remember the bountiful life of this
exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of R.L. McKinney, reflecting
fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life
with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. McKinney.
