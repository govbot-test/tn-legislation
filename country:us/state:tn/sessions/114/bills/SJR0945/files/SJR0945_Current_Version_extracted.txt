SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 945
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Paula Langley Wilhite of
Bolivar.
WHEREAS, we were greatly saddened to learn of the passing of Paula Langley Wilhite
of Bolivar; and
WHEREAS, Paula Wilhite was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on July 1, 1960, in Lauderdale County, Mrs. Wilhite was the daughter
of Mildred Evangeline Bradford Langley and Curtis Voss Langley; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Wilhite attended schools in Ripley, Bolivar, and Alcoa, and graduated
from Alcoa High School; she earned a degree from Draughons Business College and began her
professional career at Bagwell and Associates; and
WHEREAS, Paula Wilhite devoted thirty-six years of her career to the Bolivar Police
Department, where she served as an administrative assistant to the chief of police and city court
clerk before taking her much-deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, her professional endeavors aside, Mrs. Wilhite was a member of the
Association for the Preservation of Tennessee Antiquities and Daughters of the American
Revolution, organizations that furthered her interest in history and genealogy; and
WHEREAS, she enjoyed reading, gardening, antiquities, the company of her friends,
and family gatherings; she was happiest when surrounded by her family and was known as a
reliable source for stories and knowledge of family trees; and

WHEREAS, Paula Wilhite was deeply devoted to her family and always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; she relished her roles as a doting wife, mother,
and grandmother, and loved showering her grandchildren with surprises; and
WHEREAS, she will be sadly missed but fondly remembered by her first love and
husband of forty-two years, Harold Wilhite; her children, Emma (Austin) Scott and Henry Wilhite;
her grandchildren, Wallace Scott, Emmett Scott, Evangeline Scott, and Hank Wilhite; her
brothers, Curt (Sharon) Langley and Shannon Langley; her brothers- and sisters-in-law, Rick
(Judy) Wilhite, Larry (Teresa) Wilhite, Jerry (Carla) Wilhite, and Frank (Christy) Wilhite; and a
host of nieces and nephews who loved her dearly; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her parents, she was preceded in death by her father- and
mother-in-law, Richard and Sally Wilhite, and her nephew, Richard Wilhite III; and
WHEREAS, Paula Wilhite practiced her faith at Bolivar First Methodist Church, where
she taught Sunday School and was a member of various committees and the Bolivar Methodist
Women's group; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Wilhite lived her life with faith, grace, and courage, and she greatly
impacted the lives of those who were blessed to know her; her life was a testimony of
perseverance, love, and unwavering devotion to God, her family, and her community; and
WHEREAS, Paula Wilhite leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Paula Langley Wilhite,
