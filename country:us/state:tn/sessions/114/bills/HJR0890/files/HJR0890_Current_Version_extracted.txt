HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 890
By Lamberth
A RESOLUTION to confirm the appointment of Dane Scism to the
advisory board for the University of Tennessee,
Knoxville.
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-9-501, established advisory boards
for the University of Tennessee, Knoxville; University of Tennessee at Martin; University of
Tennessee at Chattanooga; and University of Tennessee Health Science Center; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-9-501, provides that the governor
shall appoint five members to each advisory board; and
WHEREAS, Governor Bill Lee has appointed Dane Scism to serve as a member of the
advisory board for the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, for a term that expires on June 30,
2029; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Scism is the chief executive officer and founder of Cellular Sales, which
has grown to include 800 stores across forty-three states with more than 7,000 employees as a
result of his people-first philosophy and commitment to customer satisfaction through
meaningful, in-person interaction; and
WHEREAS, he is also deeply involved in community engagement and philanthropy,
serving on the boards of the Knoxville Chamber of Commerce and the United Way of East
Tennessee executive cabinet; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of the University of Tennessee (UT) with a Bachelor of Applied
Science in Economics, Mr. Scism maintains a strong connection to UT; he supports UT athletics
and academics, contributes to major campus initiatives, guest lectures at the business school,
and has received the UT Distinguished Alumni Award; and

WHEREAS, Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-9-501, provides that each member
appointed by the governor to the advisory board for the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, shall
be confirmed by the general assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby confirm the appointment of Dane Scism to the advisory board
for the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, for a term that expires on June 30, 2029.
