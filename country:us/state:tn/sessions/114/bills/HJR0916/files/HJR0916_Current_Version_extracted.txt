HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 916
By Hicks T
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Kenneth Harlan Green
of Johnson City.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Kenneth Harlan Green; and
WHEREAS, Kenneth Green was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Coach Green had a lifelong love of athletics and took pleasure in coaching
and mentoring the youth of his community; and
WHEREAS, he was also deeply devoted to his family and endeavored to remain true to
family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Kenneth Green is survived by his son, Kent Julian Green; his daughter-in-
law, whom he thought of as a daughter, Dana Laura Mullins Green; and his two
granddaughters, Macie Ellyn Green and Kenley Danielle Green, who is his namesake; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his parents, William Carl Green and Tursie
Lee Green; his wife of sixty-one years, Lynn Ellyn Julian Green; and his two brothers, William
"Carl" Green and James Marlin Green; and
WHEREAS, Coach Green leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
HJR0916
012931
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Kenneth Harlan Green, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Green.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
