SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 29-39-102, is amended by adding
the following as a new subsection:
(o)
(1) Notwithstanding subdivision (a)(2) and subsections (b)-(e), the
amount of noneconomic damages recoverable by a plaintiff must not exceed one
million dollars ($1,000,000) in a health care liability action under chapter 26 of
this title, if the negligence arose directly and proximately from the provision of
obstetric care to a pregnant or postpartum woman.
(2) As used in this subsection (o):
(A) "Obstetric care":
(i) Means medical, surgical, or clinical care provided by a
licensed healthcare provider acting within the scope of obstetric
practice that is directly and proximately related to the
management of a pregnancy, labor, delivery, or postpartum
recovery within the postpartum period; and
(ii) Does not include:
(a) Gynecologic care unrelated to the management
of a pregnancy or delivery, including elective hysterectomy,
sterilization procedures, or other gynecologic surgeries not

(b) Care provided by a healthcare provider acting

(c) Care rendered for a condition or injury not

(B) "Permanent physical injury":
(i) Means an anatomical or physiological impairment to the

(ii) Does not include psychological conditions, psychiatric

permanent;

(ii) Does not include a woman whose pregnancy

(D) "Pregnant woman" means a woman carrying a viable

(3) A plaintiff seeking to recover noneconomic damages under this

(A) The plaintiff was a pregnant or postpartum woman at the time

(B) The negligence arose directly and proximately from the

(C) The plaintiff sustained death or permanent physical injury as a

(A) Displace or modify any procedural requirement, notice
provision, certificate of good faith requirement, or other provision of
chapter 26 of this title, or any other provision of this section other than the
noneconomic damages limitation set forth in subsection (a). If this
subsection (o) conflicts solely with the amount of the noneconomic
damages limitation in subsection (a), then this subsection (o) controls; or
(B) Authorize a cause of action against a licensed physician who
refuses to perform an abortion, as defined in § 39-15-213.
