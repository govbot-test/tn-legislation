SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 571
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to urge the Tennessee Department of
Transportation to establish habitat and no-mow
areas on highway rights-of-way for the monarch
butterfly.
WHEREAS, the General Assembly recognizes that monarch butterflies are a critical part
of the ecosystem in North America, including the Volunteer State, and that they are in grave
danger of extinction due in part to the destruction of their natural habitat in Mexico and the
United States and, in particular, here in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, monarch butterflies winter in Mexico and then journey through the United
States, with some reaching as far as Canada during the summer months before returning to
Mexico; and
WHEREAS, milkweed plants are critical to the survival of monarch butterflies because
that is the only place where they lay their eggs, and milkweed plants are the only source of food
for monarch caterpillars; without milkweed, there would be no monarch butterflies; and
WHEREAS, the construction of roads, shopping centers, subdivisions, and other
development has resulted in the destruction of most growths of milkweed in Tennessee and
elsewhere in the United States; and
WHEREAS, frequent mowing of rights-of-way and the use of herbicides have led to very
little milkweed/monarch butterfly habitat being left in Tennessee and elsewhere in the United
States; and
WHEREAS, in addition to needing milkweed for laying eggs and feeding the larvae, the
species also need wildflowers of all kinds in order to feed adult butterflies; and
WHEREAS, there is a tremendous amount of road and highway rights-of-way in
Tennessee that can be used for planting milkweed and other wildflowers; and

WHEREAS, if significant sections of road and highway rights-of-way in Tennessee were
dedicated to growing milkweed and other wildflowers, it could make a huge difference in the
ability of monarch butterflies to survive; and
WHEREAS, the dedication of rights-of-way to milkweed and wildflowers could result in
significant cost savings in mowing expenses; and
WHEREAS, large plantings of wildflowers along Tennessee highways would give
travelers something to notice and remember and make them want to come back, whether that
be as tourists or residents; and
WHEREAS, other states are already using highway rights-of-way to provide habitat for
monarch butterflies, including the Interstate 35 Monarch Butterfly Highway from Texas to
Minnesota; and
WHEREAS, the route of monarch butterflies via I-40, I-75, and I-81, and other highways
through Tennessee is just as vital to monarch butterflies as the route along I-35; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby urge the Tennessee Department of
Transportation to consider dedicating significant sections of road and highway rights-of-way for
milkweed and other native wildflowers in order to provide habitat for monarch butterflies.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we also urge the Tennessee Department of
Transportation to identify sections of rights-of-way where milkweed or wildflowers are presently
growing and to refrain from mowing those areas.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that a certified copy of this resolution be transmitted to
the Commissioner of Transportation.
