HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1541
By Mitchell
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of songwriting legend Don
Schlitz.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Don Schlitz, a celebrated songwriter who penned some of country music's most
enduring songs; and
WHEREAS, Don Schlitz is best known for chart-topping hits that include Kenny Rogers's
"The Gambler," Randy Travis's "On the Other Hand” and "Forever and Ever, Amen," Mary
Chapin Carpenter's "He Thinks He'll Keep Her," and Keith Whitley's "When You Say Nothing At
All"; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Schlitz's first-ever recorded song was Kenny Rogers's "The Gambler,"
setting an incredible standard that he would meet for the rest of his career; and
WHEREAS, at the tender age of twenty-three, Don Schlitz celebrated a massive
crossover hit with "The Gambler," which won the Grammy for Best Country Song in 1978 and
was named the Country Music Association's Song of the Year in 1979; and
WHEREAS, during his long and distinguished career, Mr. Schlitz achieved twenty-five
No. 1 tracks and fifty Top Ten singles and shaped the careers of Kenny Rogers, Randy Travis,
The Judds, Nitty Gritty Dirt Band, Tanya Tucker, Mary Chapin Carpenter, Keith Whitley, Alison
Krauss, and many more; and
WHEREAS, Don Schlitz was lionized as the ASCAP Country Songwriter of the Year
from 1988 to 1991, and he won three Country Music Association Song of the Year Awards, two
Academy of Country Music Song of the Year awards, and two Grammys across his five
decades in music; and

WHEREAS, he had been inducted into the Nashville Songwriter Hall of Fame,
Songwriters Hall of Fame, and Country Music Hall of Fame, where he was only the sixth
songwriter so honored at the time; and
WHEREAS, when Kenny Rogers inducted Mr. Schlitz into the Songwriters Hall of Fame,
he said, "Don doesn't just write songs, he writes careers"; and
WHEREAS, in 2022, Don Schlitz became the first non-artist songwriter inducted into the
Grand Ole Opry in the show's 100-year history, an honor that speaks volumes about the high
regard in which he was held by the country music industry; and
WHEREAS, the Saturday night performance of the Grand Ole Opry on April 18, 2026,
was dedicated in Mr. Schlitz's honor; and
WHEREAS, a man of great empathy, Mr. Schlitz donated his time and talents to charity
and was a stalwart supporter of Room In The Inn for nearly twenty years; and
WHEREAS, he often sang on Room In The Inn's stage in support of Nashville's
unhoused community, serenaded residents' on their birthday, and encouraged people in its
recovery programs; he also performed at Nashville's homeless memorial; and
WHEREAS, born and raised in Durham, North Carolina, Don Schlitz briefly attended
Duke University before moving to Nashville at the age of twenty; arriving in Music City with a
mere eighty dollars to his name, he set out for Music Row, where his talent was quickly
recognized and fostered by country music greats, including Bob McDill and Bobby Bare; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Schlitz was one of the first performers at the now-iconic Bluebird Café in
Nashville; he and his friends, Thom Schuyler, J. Fred Knobloch, and Paul Overstreet, originated
the Café's songwriter-in-the-round format in 1985; and
WHEREAS, on Tuesday nights, Mr. Schlitz held court at the venue with his "Don For A
Dollar" show, charging a cover of only one dollar; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his wife, Stacey; his daughter, Cory Dixon, and her
husband, Matt Dixon; his son, Pete Schlitz, and his wife, Christian Webb Schlitz; his
- 2 - 018358
grandchildren, Roman, Gia, Isla, and Lilah; his brother, Brad Schlitz; and his sister, Kathy
Hinkley; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his great talent, Don Schlitz was a hard-working artist who
carefully crafted his songs into masterpieces about the human experience, and he became an
icon and inspiration for a new generation of songwriters; and
WHEREAS, we were richer for his presence, and the country music industry will mourn
his loss, as will the citizens of Nashville and the State of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Don Schlitz, one of the greatest songwriters in
country music history, reflecting fondly upon his peerless catalog of music and his indelible
legacy as an artist and human being.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Don Schlitz.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
