SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 935
By Haile
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Anna Armstrong Coile
Durham of Gallatin.
WHEREAS, it was with great sadness that the members of this General Assembly
learned of the passing of Anna Coile Durham, who will be fondly remembered as a lifelong
learner and student of numerous disciplines, a mental health proponent, a skillful Southern
hostess, and a devoted wife, mother, and grandmother; and
WHEREAS, a native of Knoxville, Anna Armstrong Coile Durham was the daughter of
Grace Anna Armstrong Coile and Eugene Leland Coile; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Coile graduated first in her class from the Collegiate School for Girls in
Richmond, Virginia; in an early display of her characteristic determination, she overcame a high
fever to deliver her valedictory address; and
WHEREAS, while attending Vanderbilt University, Ms. Coile met a World War II veteran
named Walter Durham; both parties had a grandfather who had served in the Tennessee
Senate, and their inherited and mutual love of politics became a shared interest upon which
they built their budding relationship; and
WHEREAS, upon her graduation from Vanderbilt, Anna Coile married Walter Durham
and moved to Gallatin, where she spent most of her adult life; and
WHEREAS, although she was soon busy raising their family, Mrs. Durham continued her
educational and intellectual pursuits with vigor; she was an avid reader and a serious student of
philosophy and metaphysics; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Durham was an early proponent of healthy living and fed her family
according to forward-thinking guidelines endorsed by pioneering nutritionists; and

WHEREAS, she later studied various healing practices at the Estuary in Nashville,
thoroughly exploring and sincerely committing herself to the three-tiered model of mind, body,
and spirit healing; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Durham demonstrated her passion for mental health care and
treatment by co-founding the Sumner County Mental Health Services Center; despite some
initial skepticism, the center was enthusiastically welcomed by the community; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Durham served as the center's first board chair and was a decades-
long supporter, taking great pride in her role in making mental health care and treatment more
accessible; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Durham also served as the unofficial editor and proofreader for her
husband, who was a distinguished author of twenty-four books on local history and had served
as Tennessee State Historian; and
WHEREAS, she also supported many other civic and historical organizations and was a
lifelong member of Gallatin's First United Methodist Church; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Durham entertained often with her own style of elegance, charm, and
wit; many of her social gatherings welcomed newcomers to Gallatin or celebrated marriages or
babies, but others were devoted to more serious political or historical discussions; and
WHEREAS, Anna Coile Durham is survived by her children, Anna Durham Windrow
(Thomas Lanham), Robert "Bob" Coile Durham, James "Jim" Franklin (Lynee) Durham, and
Elizabeth Durham Lindsey; her grandchildren, Robert Leland (Dana) Windrow, Samantha
Lindsey (Jesus) Tavera, Stanley Doak Lindsey, and Allison Lynee Durham (Lewis Rogers); and
six great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Anna Coile Durham is deeply mourned by her loving family and her friends
and neighbors in Gallatin, and we wish to pay our respects to a gracious lady who lived the
examined life of the mind while serving others with her heart; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we celebrate the life of Anna Armstrong Coile
Durham, even as we honor her memory and mourn her passing, remembering her life of
purpose and commitment to her community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our condolences to Mrs. Durham's family
and many friends.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
