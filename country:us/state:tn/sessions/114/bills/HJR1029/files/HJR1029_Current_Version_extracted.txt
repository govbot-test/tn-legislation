HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1029
By Camper
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Deborah Manning
Thomas for her many contributions as a gospel
musician.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
recognize those Tennesseans who enrich the lives of their fellow citizens through musical
performance; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Deborah Manning Thomas, one of Tennessee's most
distinguished and beloved gospel vocalists, whose life and career reflect her profound
commitment to faith, artistry, and service; and
WHEREAS, a native of Memphis, Ms. Thomas began singing at the age of six, nurtured
by the rich musical traditions of the Black church; her roots were firmly planted at Golden Leaf
Missionary Baptist Church, where she was guided and inspired by Reverends Cleophus
Robinson and Oris L. Mays; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Thomas continued her musical ministry at Olivet Baptist Church,
singing with the Olivet Celestial Voices under the leadership of Pastor Kenneth Twigg Whalum,
Sr.; and
WHEREAS, while she was a student at Memphis State University, Deborah Thomas
was recognized for her extraordinary talent by Dr. Erma L. Clanton, who cast her in the
production An Evening of Soul, a performance that became a defining moment and led to her
mentorship under legendary musician Isaac Hayes; and
WHEREAS, Deborah Thomas went on to perform in Los Angeles, California, in the
gospel segment of the historic Wattstax concert in 1972, singing Thomas A. Dorsey's timeless
hymn "Precious Lord, Take My Hand"; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Thomas's artistic career spanned not only music but also theater and
film; she appeared as the Quinn family maid in The Firm, starring Tom Cruise, and as a choir
singer in Hustle and Flow; and
WHEREAS, on stage, Ms. Thomas brought legendary gospel figures to life, portraying
Mahalia Jackson for three seasons at the Hattiloo Theatre and earning acclaim for her portrayal
of Sister Rosetta Tharpe in the production of Marie and Rosetta; and
WHEREAS, beyond gospel music, Ms. Thomas has performed at venues across the
country, including NAACP Freedom Fund Banquets, and opened for National Civil Rights
Museum ceremonies; and
WHEREAS, her voice was included on several notable recordings by STAX recording
artists, and she continues to inspire people from all walks of life with her incredible gift; her
recorded works are available on streaming platforms and include the independently released
gospel project Blest Be the Tie (1992), the single "Shout Now" released during the COVID-19
pandemic, and her latest single, "My Trust in You" (2025); and
WHEREAS, Deborah Thomas is currently a devoted member of Christ Missionary
Baptist Church under the leadership of Dr. Gina M. Stewart; she faithfully serves as a member
of the Women of Wisdom ministry, which is committed to encouraging and empowering young
women; and
WHEREAS, in addition to teaching Christian music to church workers across the State,
Ms. Thomas served two terms as president of the music department within the Tennessee
Baptist Missionary and Educational Convention, in which role she made lasting contributions to
arts education and ministry; and
WHEREAS, apart from her fruitful career as an artist, Ms. Thomas retired from her
position with the Office of the Shelby County Criminal Court Clerk following twenty-five years of
astute service; and

WHEREAS, wholly devoted to her family, Deborah Thomas is the proud mother of three
adult children and grandmother of four grandsons; and
WHEREAS, through her voice, service, and unwavering faith, Ms. Thomas has left an
indelible mark on the cultural and spiritual landscape of Tennessee and the global music
industry; and
WHEREAS, her continued contributions to gospel music are unparalleled, and her
steadfast commitment to the genre, as well as her undeniable talent, will continue to inspire
generations with messages of faith, resilience, and praise; and
WHEREAS, Deborah Thomas epitomizes the spirit and commitment to family and
community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we recognize one of Tennessee's most outstanding
citizens at this time; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and commend Deborah Manning Thomas for her many
contributions as a gospel musician and extend to her our best wishes for much continued
success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
