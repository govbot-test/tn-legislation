HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 778
By Freeman
A RESOLUTION to commemorate Eating Disorders Awareness
Week.
WHEREAS, eating disorders are serious and potentially life-threatening mental health
conditions that affect individuals of all ages, genders, and backgrounds; and
WHEREAS, eating disorders are among the psychiatric illnesses with the highest
mortality rates in the United States, second only to opioid use disorder; and
WHEREAS, early awareness, identification, and intervention promote recovery and can
save lives; and
WHEREAS, increasing public understanding and reducing stigma around eating
disorders supports Tennessee families, educators, healthcare providers, and community
organizations as they work to connect individuals with appropriate care and resources; and
WHEREAS, commemorating Eating Disorders Awareness Week helps to highlight the
importance of these efforts and promote outreach to resources such as the National Alliance for
Eating Disorders, Renewed: Eating Disorders Support, Project HEAL, Equip, and other support
organizations; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we join with the National Alliance for Eating Disorders, Renewed: Eating
Disorders Support, Project HEAL, Equip, and other interested parties as they commemorate the
week of February 23-March 1, 2026, as Eating Disorders Awareness Week.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
