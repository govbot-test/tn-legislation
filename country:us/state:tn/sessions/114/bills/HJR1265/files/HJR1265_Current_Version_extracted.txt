HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1265
By Reeves
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the 170th anniversary of the
founding of Thompson's Station.
WHEREAS, we take pleasure in observing special occasions that preserve and
celebrate the history of the municipalities that comprise this great State; and
WHEREAS, one such community is the Town of Thompson's Station, which is
celebrating the 170th anniversary of the founding of this beautiful and scenic community in
Williamson County; and
WHEREAS, in 1856, Thompson's Station officially took root and adopted its name,
heavily influenced by Dr. Elijah Thompson, who donated land for the village center. This
followed the 1855 arrival of the Tennessee & Alabama Railroad, which spurred growth and
transformed the area into a crucial shipping point for local agriculture; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Elijah Thompson was the son of an early settler, John Thompson, and
he led efforts to route the railroad through his property and establish a rail depot nearby; and
WHEREAS, known previously as White House (1833) and Littlebury (1836), Thompson's
Station quickly grew into a trade center for southern Williamson County, with trains taking
livestock and grains, such as German millet, to markets; and
WHEREAS, the citizens of Thompson's Station have added and continue to add
immeasurably to the strength and character of Tennessee; their history and accomplishments
have helped to build the cultural richness for which this State is known; and
WHEREAS, it is most appropriate that we should honor this fine community and its
citizenry as they commemorate this significant milestone in their historic existence; now,
therefore,
HJR1265
017042
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate the citizens of the Town of Thompson's Station
upon the 170th anniversary of their fair village and extend to them our best wishes for continued
success, growth, and prosperity in their outstanding community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
