HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 918
By Stinnett
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Thomas William
Taylor on the celebration of his eightieth
birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we pay tribute to those citizens who are celebrating special
occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, Thomas William Taylor celebrates his eightieth birthday this year, a
milestone that will be commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of life's rich pageant;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor was born on February 21, 1946, in Maryville to the late Frances
Kinton Taylor and J. Wilson Taylor; he graduated from Maryville High School in 1964, earned a
Bachelor of Science degree in Biology from Maryville College in 1970, and received an honorary
Doctor of Public Service from Maryville College in 2021; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor subsequently completed graduate work at the University of
Tennessee Medical Units in Memphis and the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, Department
of Ecology, where he was a graduate teaching assistant and research assistant; and
WHEREAS, from 1971 to 1976, Tom Taylor taught in the Maryville College Department
of Biology while also serving as Camp Director of the U.S. Department of the Interior Youth
Conservation Corps (YCC) in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park during the summers;
under his guidance, the program was the first YCC program to offer enrollment for the deaf and
hearing impaired and also to attain Equal Employment Opportunity goals for all races in youth
and staff; and
WHEREAS, furthermore, during Mr. Taylor's tenure, the Great Smoky Mountains YCC
was the largest residential program of its kind in the nation and was selected to open the first
co-ed residential program; from 1973 to 1976, Mr. Taylor chaired the National Training Cadre of

the YCC and worked with Southern Illinois University to create the national YCC education
program entitled Acclimatization; and
WHEREAS, beginning in 1974, Tom Taylor was, alongside his family members, a
partner in Drake Auto Parts, an automotive jobber started in 1946 that was the second of its
kind in Tennessee to fully computerize in 1980; and
WHEREAS, by the time Mr. Taylor sold Drake Auto Parts in 2004, it was one of the five
largest independent automotive jobbers in Tennessee; for one year after its sale, Mr. Taylor
continued working as sales manager of the business; and
WHEREAS, in 1985, Tom Taylor and his wife, Janet "Nan" Krause Taylor, restored and
converted the former home of the Maryville College president into a 155-seat fine dining
restaurant called the Morningside Inn, which they operated until 1992; and
WHEREAS, from 1996 to 2018, Mr. Taylor served as president and CEO of Commercial
Cutting Equipment, Inc., a commercial lawn equipment dealership in Maryville, and since his
departure from that position, he has worked in equipment sales for the company and for
MowerWorx; he also works as a substitute teacher for Maryville City Schools; and
WHEREAS, devoted to serving his fellow citizens, Mr. Taylor was first elected to the
Maryville City Council in 2001 and served until 2020; during his tenure, he held the position of
vice mayor from 2004 to 2008 and mayor from 2008 to 2020; and
WHEREAS, he is especially passionate about the public library system and served as
chairman of the Blount County Board of Trustees during the building of the current Blount
County Public Library; he was instrumental in creating the Foundation for the Blount County
Public Library and also chaired the Fort Loudoun Regional Library Board and the State of
Tennessee Advisory Council on Libraries (TACL); and
WHEREAS, while serving as chair of the TACL, he revised the minimum standards for
Tennessee libraries, initiated specific standards of excellence for Tennessee libraries,

established the marketing program for State library services, and revised the long-range plan for
Tennessee libraries; he also served as a member and the chair of the Tennessee Library
Association; and
WHEREAS, Tom Taylor served four terms on the Board of the Great Smoky Mountains
Association; during his three years as chair, the Association planned and opened three new
retail outlets, remodeled the Sugarlands Visitors Center, created an independent Board of
Directors for the Tremont Center, and provided the initial funding for Discover Life in America
while still tripling funding to the Great Smoky Mountains National Park; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor serves on several state and local boards, but he is particularly
involved as one of the founders and the current board chair of SMiles, a ride-sharing program
for seniors sponsored by the Blount County Community Action Agency; and
WHEREAS, Tom Taylor has been the board chair of the Maryville Housing Authority
since 2023; in this role, he focuses his attention toward public housing and affordable housing;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor has been active in Boy Scouts since his youth; he is an Eagle
Scout and a Silver Beaver, and he also serves as a Cubmaster, Scoutmaster, and Explorer
Advisor; he served on the Tuckaleechee District Scout Committee and the Local Troop
Committee, and he is a member of the executive board of the Great Smoky Mountains Council,
BSA; and
WHEREAS, Tom Taylor has participated in Shaped Note Old Harp singing for fifty years
and is the moderator for three annual singings in the region; he is also a member of the Chancel
Choir at Broadway United Methodist Church, where he has been a lifelong member and has
also taught Sunday School for more than forty years; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor and his wife raise and show American Kennel Club (AKC)
registered bloodhounds; one of their dogs earned a championship and competed at the

Westminster Kennel Club show at Madison Square Garden, and another earned an AKC Grand
Championship and was inducted into the American Bloodhound Club Hall of Fame; and
WHEREAS, Tom and Nan Taylor are the proud parents of two sons, Robert (Katie) and
Benton (Sarah), and have been blessed with four grandchildren, Frank, Wade, Magnolia, and
Olive; and
WHEREAS, as he celebrates his eightieth birthday, Mr. Taylor, admired for his wisdom,
spirit of generosity, integrity, and enthusiasm for life, still exudes the goodness that is so
characteristically his; and
WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and congratulate Thomas William Taylor on the
celebration of his eightieth birthday and extend to him our best wishes for many happy returns
of the day.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
