Amendment No. 1 to HB1676
Hicks G
Signature of Sponsor
AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 1739 House Bill No. 1676*
by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting instead:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 64-2-101, is amended by deleting the
section and substituting instead:
(a)
(1) There is created the North East Tennessee railroad authority in the
counties of Anderson, Campbell, and Scott.
(2) The North East Tennessee railroad authority must also include the
counties of Claiborne, Union, and Grainger upon all three (3) of those counties
satisfying the requirements of § 64-2-102(b).
(b) This authority is intended to secure economic benefits to the counties that
are members of the North East Tennessee railroad authority, by providing for the
continuation of railroad service in those counties.
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 64-2-102, is amended by designating
the existing language as subsection (a) and adding the following as a new subsection (b):
(b) The North East Tennessee railroad authority must include the counties of
Claiborne, Union, and Grainger, and this part applies to those counties, when:
(1) Claiborne, Union, and Grainger counties are authorized, by a vote of
two-thirds (2/3) of their respective governing bodies, to become members of the
authority;

(2) Evidence of such authorization is proclaimed and countersigned by
the presiding officer of each ratifying county certified by that presiding officer to
the secretary of state; and
(3) The governing bodies of all three (3) counties have indicated their
willingness to appropriate sufficient funds to support the administration of the
authority along with the existing county members of the authority.
SECTION 3. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 64-2-110, is amended by deleting the
section and substituting instead:
All contracts of the authority must be entered into and executed in such manner
as may be prescribed by statutes, regulations, and procedures governing contracting by
county governments; provided, that a contract or acquisition by purchase of equipment,
apparatus, materials, or supplies involving more than the applicable maximum threshold
over which public advertising and sealed competitive bids or proposals are required for
nonemergency, nonproprietary purchases as established pursuant to § 12-3-1212, or for
construction, installation, repair, or improvement of the property or facilities involving
more than such applicable maximum threshold must not be made until after the contract
has been advertised for bids. Purchases of like items must be aggregated for the
purposes of the bid threshold. Advertisement is not required when an emergency arises
and requires immediate delivery of the supplies or performance of the service.
SECTION 4. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
