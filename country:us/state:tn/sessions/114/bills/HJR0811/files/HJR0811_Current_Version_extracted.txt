HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 811
By Camper
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the 100th anniversary of Black
History Month.
WHEREAS, February 2026 marks the 100th anniversary of Black History Month, an
observance that honors the achievements, resilience, and enduring contributions of African
Americans to our national story; and
WHEREAS, Black History Month traces its origins to 1926, when the renowned historian
Dr. Carter G. Woodson and the Association for the Study of Negro Life and History established
Negro History Week; and
WHEREAS, what began as a weeklong celebration grew steadily in recognition and
importance, expanding in 1976 during the nation's Bicentennial into a monthlong observance
that continues to inspire Americans; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee has a long and distinguished tradition of observing Black History
Month and honoring the achievements of African Americans whose courage, leadership, and
cultural contributions have profoundly influenced the history and character of this State; and
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State proudly claims among its native daughters and sons
many whose lives and legacies illuminate the broader struggle for justice and equality, including
the fearless journalist and anti-lynching crusader Ida B. Wells-Barnett; the trailblazing surgeon,
educator, and legislator Dr. Dorothy Lavinia Brown; and the Honorable Lois M. DeBerry, former
Speaker Pro Tempore of the Tennessee House of Representatives, whose leadership and
integrity inspired all who served with her; and
WHEREAS, in 2019, Leader Karen D. Camper achieved a historic milestone as the first
African American and first Black woman to lead a major political party caucus in the Tennessee
General Assembly, embodying the progress envisioned by those who came before her; and

WHEREAS, Tennessee's Historically Black Colleges and Universities, including
Tennessee State University, Fisk University, Meharry Medical College, Lane College, LeMoyne-
Owen College, and Knoxville College, have served as beacons of excellence and opportunity,
educating generations of leaders, scholars, and citizens; and
WHEREAS, the rich legacy of Knoxville's Black history endures through the work of
Knoxville College and the Beck Cultural Exchange Center, which stand as cornerstones of
education and preservation of African-American heritage and education in East Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, the Highlander Folk School (now the Highlander Research and Education
Center) in New Market played a pivotal role as a training ground for Civil Rights leaders,
including Rosa Parks, John Lewis, Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., and Septima Clark; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee native Alex Haley of Henning profoundly shaped the nation's
understanding of African-American history and ancestry through his landmark works Roots: The
Saga of an American Family and The Autobiography of Malcolm X; and
WHEREAS, as the nation marks the 100th anniversary of Black History Month, it is fitting
that we affirm Tennessee's ongoing commitment to teaching accurate and inclusive history and
promoting civic understanding, as we encourage communities across the State to
commemorate the centennial of Black History Month through educational and cultural
programming; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commemorate the 100th anniversary of Black History Month in February
2026 and honor the remarkable legacy of African-American achievement, perseverance, and
leadership that continues to shape our State and our nation.
- 2 - 010154
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we encourage communities, schools, and cultural
institutions across Tennessee to commemorate Black History Month's centennial year through
educational and cultural programming.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that appropriate copies of this resolution be transmitted
to the National Civil Rights Museum in Memphis, the Beck Cultural Exchange Center in
Knoxville, the Highlander Research and Education Center in New Market, the Alex Haley
Museum and Interpretive Center in Henning, and the Association for the Study of African
American Life and History in Washington, D.C., with this final clause omitted from such copies
and upon proper request made to the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately
following the State seal appear without House or Senate designation.
- 3 - 010154
