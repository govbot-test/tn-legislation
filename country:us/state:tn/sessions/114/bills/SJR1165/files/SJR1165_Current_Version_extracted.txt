SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1165
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Rogers C. Anderson
upon his retirement as Mayor of Williamson
County.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should specially recognize those citizens who have
performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their years of public service;
and
WHEREAS, Rogers C. Anderson is one such estimable person, who has served with
distinction as Williamson County Mayor since September 2002 and will retire at the conclusion
of his current term in August 2026; and
WHEREAS, throughout his distinguished tenure, Mayor Anderson has provided solid
leadership and thoughtful guidance for his fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Rogers C. Anderson was born in Hamilton County and is a graduate of the
University of Tennessee, Knoxville, and a veteran of the United States Air Force, having served
his country with honor in Africa and Vietnam; and
WHEREAS, prior to his tenure as county mayor, Rogers C. Anderson was a member of
the Williamson County Commission from 1986 to 2002, including service as chairman for four
years and as vice-chairman for eight years; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Anderson has rendered extensive service through numerous
leadership roles and appointments, including gubernatorial appointments to the Workforce
Development Board and the Tennessee Veterans' Homes Board, and has served as chairman
or president of several regional and statewide organizations, including the Association of County
Mayors, the Greater Nashville Regional Council, the Tennessee County Services Association,
the Regional Transportation Authority, the Metropolitan Planning Organization, and the Middle
Tennessee Mayors' Caucus; and

WHEREAS, he has further contributed to his community and region through service on a
wide range of boards and organizations, including the Tennessee Advisory Commission for
Intergovernmental Relations, the Tennessee Hospital Association, Williamson Health,
Workforce Essentials, Williamson, Inc., and the Franklin Noon Rotary Club, as well as through
his involvement with Leadership Franklin, Franklin Tomorrow, and the Williamson County Fair
Board, which he served as a founding and honorary board member, and as a founding member
of the Grassland Athletic Association; and
WHEREAS, during his many years of public service, Mayor Anderson has been the
recipient of numerous honors recognizing his leadership and commitment to his community,
including the Caroline J. Cross Community Leadership Award, the Silver Good Citizenship
Medal from the Sons of the American Revolution, the County Technical Assistance Services'
County Official of the Year and Mayor of the Year awards, the Tennessee Hospital Association's
Trustee of Distinction Award, the American Planning Association's Friend of Planning Award,
and the Greater Nashville Regional Council's Excellence in Achievement Award; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Anderson evinces his devout religious faith as a member of Berry's
Chapel Church of Christ, where he has served in a number of capacities over the years; and
WHEREAS, Rogers C. Anderson is deeply devoted to his family; with his wife, the late
Anita Conchin Anderson, he is the proud father of five children, Ryan, Travis, Laura, Wes, and
Russ, and the loving grandfather of Olivia, McCabe, Molly, Cole, twins Audrey and Evangeline,
Scott, Raleigh, and Iris; and
WHEREAS, a public-spirited citizen of the highest order, Mayor Anderson has compiled
a singular record of success during his exemplary public service career; and
WHEREAS, his remarkable success as a civic leader is directly attributable to his
reliance on the time-honored values of hard work and common sense and his uncommon ability
to work well with people from all walks of life; and
- 2 - 018232
WHEREAS, a strong advocate for the citizens of Williamson County, Mayor Anderson is
wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service that have earned Tennessee
recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Rogers C. Anderson for his
honorable and astute service to the people of Tennessee as Mayor of Williamson County, salute
his unswerving commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction, and extend
to him our best wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 3 - 018232
