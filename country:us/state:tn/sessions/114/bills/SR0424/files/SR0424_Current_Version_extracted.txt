SENATE RESOLUTION 424
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to honor the Revolutionary War veterans interred
within the Great Smoky Mountains National Park
and surrounding areas.
WHEREAS, as the United States marks its 250th birthday, citizens are called upon to
celebrate not only our country's auspicious founding, but those who placed their lives in peril for
the sake of the freedoms that we enjoy even today; and
WHEREAS, tens of millions of Americans have served in the Armed Forces of the
United States from the American Revolution to present-day conflicts, and many of these
veterans chose to settle in East Tennessee; some of these veteran settlers had their land taken
from them for the establishment of the Great Smoky Mountains National Park; and
WHEREAS, many of these Revolutionary War veterans are buried within the bounds of
the national park, including Henry Bohanan and William Hamby, as well as near the park,
including Fredrick Emert, Spencer Clark, William Cocke, and the wives who supported the war
efforts, including Mary Blount and Martha Dandridge Washington; and
WHEREAS, it is imperative that we not forget the sacrifices these men and women
made for our country, including giving the ultimate sacrifice for our freedom; and
WHEREAS, these veterans were brave and selfless individuals who, when duty called,
willingly put themselves in harm's way to defend the lives and liberty of others; their abiding
patriotism and enduring devotion to the ideals on which the United States is founded continue to
inspire us today; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we honor the memories of these individuals who fought
valiantly so that America could become a sovereign nation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that, as we celebrate the 250th

contributions of our Revolutionary War veterans to the principles of democracy, individual

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
