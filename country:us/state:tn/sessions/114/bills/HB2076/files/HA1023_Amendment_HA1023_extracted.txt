by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:

Section 2:
SECTION 1.
(a) The Tennessee advisory commission on intergovernmental relations
(TACIR), in consultation with nutritional subject matter experts, shall study:
(1) The prevalence of ultra-processed foods (UPFs) and other
substances, including artificial dyes and chemical additives, in food or drinks
served through school nutrition programs; and
(2) The impacts that UPFs and other substances have on student health
using scientific data.
(b) TACIR shall prepare a report that includes:
(1) Identification of UPFs, artificial dyes, and chemical additives provided
to students through school nutrition programs;
(2) A summary of research regarding the link between UPF consumption
through food or drinks served through school nutrition programs and rising rates
of childhood obesity and metabolic dysfunction; and
(3) An analysis of ways to transition school nutrition programs toward
whole-food alternatives.
(c) The report must be submitted to the members of the general assembly, the
governor, and the legislative librarian no later than January 31, 2027.
(d) It is the legislative intent that this study must be conducted from TACIR's
existing resources.
