Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 55, Chapter 10, Part 4, is amended by
adding the following as a new section:
(a)
(1) Notwithstanding another law to the contrary, if a person is convicted
of a violation of § 55-10-401 involving the use of a controlled substance, the
person has no prior convictions for a violation of § 55-10-401, and the court
determines that it is appropriate to order the person to operate only a motor
vehicle equipped with a functioning ignition interlock device under § 55-10-409,
then the court may allow the person to agree to receive naltrexone injections at
the person's own expense for a period of twelve (12) continuous months in lieu of
ordering the person to operate only a motor vehicle equipped with a functioning
ignition interlock device.
(2) If a person agrees to receive naltrexone injections for a period of
twelve (12) continuous months in lieu of operating only a motor vehicle equipped
with a functioning ignition interlock device pursuant to subdivision (a)(1), then the
court must include in the sentencing order reporting requirements related to the
person's receipt of the naltrexone injections and compliance with treatment
recommendations as a condition of the person's probation. A person's failure to
receive naltrexone injections as ordered by the court is a ground for revocation of
the person's probation, and the court shall notify the department of safety of the

person's noncompliance. If the department receives notice from the court that
the person has been noncompliant with the court's order regarding naltrexone
injections, then the department shall revoke the person's driver license and order
the person to operate only a motor vehicle equipped with a functioning ignition
interlock device for a period of twelve (12) continuous months. The conditions of
the ignition interlock usage period under § 55-10-409 and § 55-10-417 apply to
the person.
(b)
(1) Notwithstanding another law to the contrary, if a person is convicted
of a second or subsequent violation of § 55-10-401, each of which involved the
use of a controlled substance, and the court determines that it is appropriate to
order the person to operate only a motor vehicle equipped with a functioning
ignition interlock device under § 55-10-409, then the court shall order the person
to receive naltrexone injections at the person's own expense for a period of
twelve (12) continuous months in addition to ordering the person to operate only
a motor vehicle equipped with a functioning ignition interlock device.
(2) The court's sentencing order must include reporting requirements
related to the person's receipt of the naltrexone injections and compliance with
treatment recommendations as a condition of the person's probation. A person's
failure to receive naltrexone injections as ordered by the court is a ground for
revocation of the person's probation.

Section 3:
SECTION 2. This act takes effect July 1, 2026, the public welfare requiring it, and
applies to offenses committed on or after that date.
