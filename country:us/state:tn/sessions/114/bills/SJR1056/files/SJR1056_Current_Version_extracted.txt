SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1056
By Haile
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Grady Eades upon his
receipt of the 2026 Hillcourt Bronze Medal.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Grady Eades, who is being honored with the
2026 Hillcourt Bronze Medal, which recognizes individuals who have made distinguished
contributions to Scouting history at the local and regional levels; and
WHEREAS, this prestigious award was established in 2024 by The "Green Bar Bill"
Hillcourt Foundation to pay homage to legendary leader William "Green Bar Bill" Hillcourt,
whose writings, training, and dedication to the patrol method shaped generations of Scouts; and
WHEREAS, a lifelong Scouter, Mr. Eades earned the Eagle Scout rank in 1990 and
served on the Boxwell Reservation summer camp staff from 1990 through 1997, which sparked
his enduring passion for the camp and its history; and
WHEREAS, today, he continues his support of Scouting as a volunteer manager of
VirtualBoxwell.org and its social media channels, documenting and sharing the legacy of one of
Scouting's most treasured places; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Eades is also the author of For the Good of the Program: A Century of
Middle Tennessee Scouting at Boxwell 1921-2021 and has shared Boxwell's story through
presentations at local libraries and community venues; and
WHEREAS, a highly esteemed member of his community, Grady Eades serves as a
Professor of History at Volunteer State Community College and gives back directly to youth in
