SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 541
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to recognize the ninetieth anniversary of Delta
Kappa Gamma Society International, Tennessee
Chapter.
WHEREAS, Delta Kappa Gamma Society International was founded at the University of
Texas in Austin, Texas, in 1929 for key women educators; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee State Chapter of Delta Kappa Gamma (DKG) was organized
as Xi State on November 30, 1935, in Nashville as the fourteenth state organization in the
Society; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee State Founders were Ada Hornsby Earnest, Mary Morrow
Frizzell, Julia Green, Ina Yoakley, Mary Hall, Julia M. Harris, Evangeline Hartsook, Dr. Helen
Lacy Shane, Mary Hetherington Mackinley, Elizabeth Oehmig, Nell Parkinson, Dr. Maycie K.
Southall, Amanda Bibb Russell, and E. May Saunders; and
WHEREAS, forty-five women have served as president of the Tennessee State
Organization since 1935, with each leading a biennium; sixteen presidents are still living today;
and
WHEREAS, members past and present have had the honor of being invited into the
organization for their service to any field in education; and
WHEREAS, members of DKG in Tennessee have been key leaders who have positively
impacted the educational experiences of Tennesseans from preschool through the university
level; and
WHEREAS, the current mission of Delta Kappa Gamma Society International is to
promote the professional and personal growth of women educators and excellence in education;
and

WHEREAS, the current Tennessee Organization of Delta Kappa Gamma includes over
1,700 members in sixty chapters across the State; and
WHEREAS, the members of the Tennessee Organization will gather for the 90th Annual
State Convention in May 2026 at The University of the South in Sewanee to celebrate their
granite anniversary; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we congratulate the members of Delta Kappa
Gamma Society International, Tennessee Chapter, on the occasion of its ninetieth anniversary,
commending these key women educators who have impacted education for Tennesseans for
generations as they continue to uphold the Society's mission, vision, and purposes.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
