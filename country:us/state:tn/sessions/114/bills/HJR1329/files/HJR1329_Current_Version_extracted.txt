HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1329
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to recognize Brenda Wynn on the occasion of
her retirement as Davidson County Clerk.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of public service; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable person is Brenda Wynn, who, having admirably served
the citizens of Davidson County as County Clerk since 2012, is taking a much-deserved
retirement this year; and
WHEREAS, a trailblazer, Ms. Wynn was the first woman elected to serve as Davidson
County Clerk and the first African-American woman to win election to a countywide
constitutional office; and
WHEREAS, in her capacity as Davidson County Clerk, Ms. Wynn has overseen the
administration of motor vehicle registrations, business licenses, marriage licenses, notary public
commissions, and related public services for one of the fastest-growing counties in Tennessee;
and
WHEREAS, a longtime Nashvillian, Brenda Wynn has devoted her career to public
service and community engagement, having previously served as Director of Community
Outreach for U.S. Congressman Jim Cooper and as the inaugural Director of the Mayor's Office
of Neighborhoods under Mayor Bill Purcell; and
WHEREAS, prior to her tenure in public service, she served in a number of executive
roles at Meharry Medical College, where she worked to expand outreach and strengthen
community partnerships; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Wynn holds a bachelor's degree and a master's degree from
Tennessee State University, where she has served as an adjunct professor in the Department
of Health Care Administration and Planning; and
WHEREAS, her extensive civic involvement has included leadership roles with
Leadership Nashville, the League of Women Voters of Nashville, Women in Numbers (WIN),
and the YWCA, as well as board service with numerous nonprofits and community
organizations; and
WHEREAS, Brenda Wynn exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout her illustrious tenure as Davidson County Clerk, Brenda Wynn
has distinguished herself as a public-spirited citizen of the highest order, winning the respect
and admiration of her colleagues and the people she has so ably served; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we extend to Brenda Wynn, Davidson County Clerk, our heartfelt wishes
for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
