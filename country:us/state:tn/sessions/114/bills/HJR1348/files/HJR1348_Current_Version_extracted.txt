HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1348
By Dixie
A RESOLUTION to commend Sheeva Chourazghi-Azad for
exemplary service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Sheeva Chourazghi-Azad is one such individual, who has distinguished
herself in her role as a legislative intern for Representative Vincent Dixie; her work has resulted
in innumerable benefits for the legislators she assisted and the General Assembly as a whole;
and
WHEREAS, a student at Belmont University, where she is highly regarded by her peers,
Ms. Chourazghi-Azad has achieved academic success while pursuing a degree in public
advocacy with a minor in political science; and
WHEREAS, during her collegiate career, Ms. Chourazghi-Azad has served as the DEI
Chairwoman of Phi Mu Theta, Executive-Elect of Lambda Pi Eta, Council Leader of Towering
Traditions Orientation, and a Student Apprentice for the Belmont Formation Collaborative; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Chourazghi-Azad is truly committed to her community and advancing
civic engagement; she is passionate about fostering a more just, equitable, and prosperous
society by empowering diverse and growing populations through advocacy, education, and
public service; and
WHEREAS, Sheeva Chourazghi-Azad's global perspective has been shaped by her
academic experiences, including study abroad programs in London, England; Costa Rica; and
New York, New York, as well as her involvement with the University College London Iranian
Society; and

WHEREAS, a leader in her community, she has contributed to youth development
through the Preston-Taylor Club and the Boys & Girls Clubs of Middle Tennessee, where she
designed lesson plans and cultivated inclusive, engaging learning environments for young
students; and
WHEREAS, prior to her time with the legislature, Sheeva Chourazghi-Azad gained
extensive experience through internships with Nashville's civic institutions, including the Historic
Metro Courthouse, where she worked with Judge David Briley in Trial Court, and the First
Circuit Court; she further served the Mayor's office during the "Choose How You Move"
campaign, in which role she supported scheduling operations, optimized organizational
systems, and led a presentation on effective social media engagement; and
WHEREAS, a recipient of the prestigious Ingram Scholarship and multiple awards for
leadership and social impact, Sheeva Chourazghi-Azad has been duly recognized for her
dedication to service and ethical leadership; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies, Sheeva Chourazghi-Azad brought her
many abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Chourazghi-Azad strives to create meaningful and lasting social change
and has applied her experience in collaboration, critical thinking, social media management, and
organizational systems to her time with the legislature; she collaborated with her fellow interns
to launch "Youth Voices Against Violence: A Community Conversation," an initiative that
amplifies youth perspectives on critical social issues; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Ms. Chourazghi-Azad has
surpassed all criteria that define a devoted and conscientious intern; and
- 2 - 017172
WHEREAS, Sheeva Chourazghi-Azad has performed her many duties with
extraordinary insight and rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Sheeva Chourazghi-Azad for outstanding service as
a legislative intern for Representative Vincent Dixie and extend to her our best wishes for every
future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
