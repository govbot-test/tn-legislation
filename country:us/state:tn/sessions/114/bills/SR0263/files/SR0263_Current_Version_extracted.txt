SENATE RESOLUTION 263
By Lamar
A RESOLUTION relative to coverage of doula services and care
under the TennCare program.
WHEREAS, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC),
Tennessee has the nation's highest maternal mortality rate; and
WHEREAS, from 2018-2022, Tennessee's maternal mortality rate was 42.1 per 100,000
births, a statistic that translates to 166 pregnancy-related deaths within that time frame; and
WHEREAS, according to the Tennessee Maternal Mortality Review Board, seventy-six
percent of pregnancy-related deaths occurring between 2020-2022 were preventable; and
WHEREAS, as it currently stands, doula care is not a covered healthcare service in most
states, and clients are forced to pay out of pocket; and
WHEREAS, people who live in rural areas with no access to nearby hospitals with
maternity care would benefit the most from doula care, but they often do not have access to
doulas due to income, geography, and insurance coverage, creating a noticeable health
disparity; and
WHEREAS, pregnant and postpartum people receiving doula care have been found to
have improved health outcomes for themselves and their infants, including higher breastfeeding
initiation rates, fewer low-birthweight babies, and lower rates of cesarean births; and
WHEREAS, doulas offer a unique opportunity for healthcare systems to reduce
workloads for nurses and frontline providers with many simultaneous responsibilities, retain
patients for future pregnancies and other services, and attract new patients by providing a
unique, valuable service; and

WHEREAS, the benefits of doula care can also have a financial impact in helping
families and the State avoid the cost associated with low-birthweight babies, cesarean births,
and other pregnancy-related complications; and
WHEREAS, many Tennessee hospitals and healthcare providers have recognized the
essential nature of doula care; and
WHEREAS, in March 2025, the Doula Services Advisory Committee for the Tennessee
Department of Health identified five existing pilot programs for doula services in the State; and
WHEREAS, at the time of the Committee's report, the Nashville Strong Babies (NSB)
Doula Services Pilot Program served a total of seventy-six families in Davidson County; and
WHEREAS, performance indicators for NSB showed higher breastfeeding initiation and
retention rates, improved postpartum care, lower rates of cesarean births, and the birth of more
full-term, healthy birth-weight infants among participants; and
WHEREAS, NSB participants' response to their experience with doula care and its
impact on their pregnancy and birth outcome was overwhelmingly positive; and
WHEREAS, in evaluating the pilot program, the Metro Nashville Public Health
Department recognized doulas as "a tool to support women through the labor and delivery
journey" as part of a "support network to achieve optimal outcomes"; and
WHEREAS, doulas are both vital childbirth team members and community health
workers, and they are valuable contributors in the ongoing effort to end maternal and infant
health disparities; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that the Division of TennCare is
urged to provide medical assistance coverage and reimbursement for services and care
provided by doulas.
