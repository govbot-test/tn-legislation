HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1422
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Jerome "Hertis" Phillips
of Oneida.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Jerome "Hertis" Phillips of Oneida; and
WHEREAS, Hertis Phillips was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on July 29, 1939, in Scott County, Mr. Phillips was the son of Leamon
and Millie Keeton Phillips; and
WHEREAS, Hertis Phillips served in the U.S. Army and was an ordained minister; over
the years, he pastored at New River Missionary Baptist Church, Tunnel Hill Baptist Church, and
New Haven Missionary Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Phillips worked for Big John's and retired after twenty-eight years of
serving as a supervisor; he was also a driver for ETHRA for many years and served as a county
commissioner for thirteen years; and
WHEREAS, Hertis Phillips was deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Phillips is survived by his wife of forty-six years, Terri Newport Phillips;
his children, Teresa Phillips Terry, Tim (Penny) Phillips, Kaitlyn Phillips (Caleb) King, and Sarah
Phillips (Colton) Gray; his grandchildren, Seth Phillips, Kelli Miller, Andrew Phillips, Jacob
Phillips, Remi Gray, Ruger Gray, and Asher King; his great-grandchildren, Oaklyn Phillips,
Brynlee Miller, and Gracyn Miller; his special caregiver, Elizabeth Phillips; his hospice nurse,
Pam Byrge; and a host of other family members and friends; and

WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his brothers, J.C.
Phillips, Ronnie Phillips, Bill Phillips, and Mike Phillips; his sisters, Alene Phillips McDonald,
Joan Phillips, Lois Phillips Brown, and Veneda Phillips Grimes; his special son-in-law, Scott
Terry; and Tim and Teresa's mother, Charlene; and
WHEREAS, Hertis Phillips leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Jerome "Hertis" Phillips, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Phillips.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
