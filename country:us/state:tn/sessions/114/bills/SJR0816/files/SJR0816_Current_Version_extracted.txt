SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 816
By Stevens
A RESOLUTION to recognize Phil Rogers on the occasion of his
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should specially recognize those outstanding
professionals who devoted their careers to providing the highest-quality education and learning
environments for Tennessee's students; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Phil Rogers, who, having distinguished
himself as a professional of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity throughout his career,
took his well-deserved retirement as the principal of South Gibson High School in June 2025;
and
WHEREAS, a native of West Tennessee, Phil Rogers graduated from Riverside High
School before attending Freed Hardeman University, where he earned a degree in education;
he received his master's degree from the University of Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Rogers began his exemplary career in the classroom as a teacher,
coach, and athletic director at Jackson Christian School; he taught a number of different grade
levels and courses, including physical education, health, science, and driver's education in
addition to coaching baseball, football, and basketball; and
WHEREAS, his next teaching post took him to University School of Jackson, where he
got his first experience in administration, serving as the middle school dean of students, athletic
director, and eventually middle school director; he obtained his administration and supervision
license in 1987; and
WHEREAS, while working at University School of Jackson, Mr. Rogers was hit head-on
by a drunk driver on his way back from a football game, with the resulting injuries and physical
limitations shaping his career choices moving forward; and

WHEREAS, two years after the accident, he accepted a position with Jackson Central
Merry High School, where he worked as a science teacher and helped with administrative roles
for athletics before being named assistant principal and athletic director for the school in 1994;
and
WHEREAS, in 2007, Phil Rogers was offered the position of principal at Gibson County
High School, where he remained for a year before he was named the principal of the brand new
South Gibson High School in January 2009; and
WHEREAS, Principal Phil Rogers quickly established a record of excellence at South
Gibson and he continued to demonstrate the utmost professionalism during his sixteen years
leading the school; and
WHEREAS, Principal Rogers truly served as an inspiration to the faculty and staff at
South Gibson High School, and his friendship and counsel have always been highly valued by
those who are fortunate to know him; and
WHEREAS, his numerous professional achievements aside, Mr. Rogers is most grateful
for the love and support he shares with his family and looks forward to spending more time with
them, attending University of Memphis football games, and going fishing; and
WHEREAS, Phil Rogers is an extremely talented and compassionate administrator who
worked assiduously throughout his career in education to motivate his students, teachers, and
staff to reach their full potential as scholars, professionals, and citizens; and
WHEREAS, Phil Rogers epitomizes the dedicated principal who is wholly committed to
the highest ideals of public service and quality education for all, and he should be specially
recognized on the occasion of his retirement; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Phil Rogers upon his
exceptional service to the students and schools of Tennessee, salute his professional expertise
- 2 - 013148
and personal excellence, and wish him much continued success and happiness during his
retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
