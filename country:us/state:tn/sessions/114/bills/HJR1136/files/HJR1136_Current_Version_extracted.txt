HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1136
By Howell
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Richard Taylor for his
distinguished career in emergency services and
law enforcement.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those dedicated public servants who performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm in
securing the safety of their community; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable individual is Richard Taylor, whose lifelong commitment
to public service has benefited the people of Bradley County, Polk County, and the State of
Tennessee for more than fifty years; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor began his career in public service in 1977 as a reserve officer
with the Cleveland Police Department, demonstrating a sense of duty and responsibility that
would define his career; and
WHEREAS, in 1980, he joined the Bradley County Sheriff's Office, where he served with
distinction for fourteen years and rose to the position of chief deputy, in which capacity he
served from 1990 to 1994; and
WHEREAS, throughout his career, Richard Taylor has been involved in emergency
management and disaster response, serving with the Tennessee Emergency Management
Hazardous Materials Team, beginning in 1981, and playing an important role in hazardous
materials response and training for emergency personnel; and
WHEREAS, in 1982, Mr. Taylor joined the Bradley County Civil Defense Rescue Squad
as law enforcement liaison and remains a lifetime member of that organization; and
WHEREAS, Richard Taylor further demonstrated his dedication to emergency
preparedness and response through his service as a Tennessee Emergency Management
Agency Area Coordinator from 1984 until his retirement in 2014; and

WHEREAS, in addition to these responsibilities, he served as Search and Rescue
Coordinator for Polk County Emergency Management and became widely respected for his
expertise in search and rescue operations and disaster response; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor, a skilled K-9 handler, trained search and rescue dogs that aided
in numerous successful search operations; and
WHEREAS, throughout more than five decades of service, Richard Taylor has mentored
countless men and women in emergency services, teaching search and rescue classes,
incident command courses, and other training programs that have strengthened emergency
response capabilities throughout East Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor's career has been marked by a steadfast commitment to service
above self, responding to natural and man-made disasters across Tennessee and mentoring
the next generation of first responders while continuing to share his knowledge and experience
in the field of emergency services; and
WHEREAS, Richard Taylor exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his years in public service, Mr. Taylor has demonstrated the
utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the respect and admiration of his fellow
officers, emergency responders, and the citizens of this State; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor Richard Taylor for his remarkable career in law enforcement and
emergency management and commend him for his lifelong commitment to protecting the
citizens of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
