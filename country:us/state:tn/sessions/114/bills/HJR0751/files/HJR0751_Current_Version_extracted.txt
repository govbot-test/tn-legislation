HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 751
By Atchley
A RESOLUTION recognizing the City of Gatlinburg as the official
Gateway to the Smokies.
WHEREAS, Gatlinburg borders the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, with multiple
park entrances, including the primary Sugarlands entrance, located within or immediately
adjacent to the city, providing the most direct and most popular access point for visitors; and
WHEREAS, Gatlinburg is widely recognized as the gateway community to the Great
Smoky Mountains National Park, the most visited national park in the United States, attracting
over 13 million visitors annually in recent years, many of whom enter through Gatlinburg; and
WHEREAS, the city serves as a primary basecamp for park exploration, surrounded on
three sides by the mountains and offering immediate access to key trails, visitor centers, and
scenic routes that lead into the heart of the Smokies; and
WHEREAS, Gatlinburg's location along U.S. Route 441 positions it as the busiest and
most tourist-oriented entryway to the park, facilitating the majority of visitor traffic from the
Tennessee side and supporting the park's role as a premier destination for millions each year;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby designate the City of Gatlinburg as the official Gateway to the
Smokies.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
