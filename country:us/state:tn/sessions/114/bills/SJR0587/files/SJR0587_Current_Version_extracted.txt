SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 587
By Gardenhire
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Greggory Joe Ridley,
County Mayor of Bledsoe County, upon his
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those public servants who, in their years of work, have performed with uncommon devotion and
enthusiasm; and
WHEREAS, Greggory Joe Ridley is one such estimable individual, who has served with
distinction for twenty years as Mayor of Bledsoe County; in August of 2026, he will begin a well-
deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, Gregg Ridley has faithfully and honorably served as the County Mayor of
Bledsoe County for twenty years, providing leadership, dedication, and vision to the citizens of
this county; and
WHEREAS, born on April 9, 1962, to parents Marion Talley Ridley and Jo Hughes
Ridley, he is one of six children; and
WHEREAS, on September 21, 1985, Greggory Joe Ridley married Janice Rebecca
"Becky" Deakins; this loving couple has been blessed with two children, William Talley Ridley
and Greggory Matthew Ridley, and four grandchildren within their forty-one years of marital
happiness; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure as County Mayor, Gregg Ridley has demonstrated an
unwavering commitment to fiscal responsibility, conservative values, community growth, and
improving the quality of life for all residents; and
WHEREAS, under his leadership, Bledsoe County has achieved significant
accomplishments, including working alongside the Bledsoe County Board of Commissioners
and State and Federal officials to expand post-high school educational opportunities with the

October 2, 2024, groundbreaking for the construction of a 14,000-square foot Tennessee
College of Applied Technology (TCAT) facility on US Highway 127 Bypass; enriching primary
health care access for citizens with the completion of the $1.56 million, 6,200-square-foot
Erlanger-Bledsoe medical office building in 2022; constructing and then expanding a new local
jail; expanding Bledsoe's potable water infrastructure for overall community well-being and high
quality of life; and constructing a new health department; and
WHEREAS, Bledsoe County Mayor Gregg Ridley has built strong partnerships with
local, state, and federal officials, as well as community organizations, always putting the needs
of Bledsoe County first; and
WHEREAS, his leadership has been marked by integrity, humility, and a deep respect
for the people he serves; and
WHEREAS, upon retirement, Gregg Ridley leaves a legacy of service, growth, and
community pride that will continue to benefit generations of Bledsoe County citizens; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Mayor Greggory Joe Ridley upon the
occasion of his retirement and commend his many years of distinguished service, wishing him
continued success in all his future endeavors and a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
