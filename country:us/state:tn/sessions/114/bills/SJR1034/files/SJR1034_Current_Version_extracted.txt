SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1034
By Hensley
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate the 2026 Mule Day
Court Third Runner-Up, Cayley Jordan Crews.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
young citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as
outstanding Tennesseans of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Cayley Jordan Crews, who was recently
selected as the 2026 Mule Day Court Third Runner-Up; and
WHEREAS, seventeen-year-old Cayley Crews was born and raised in Maury County
and graduated from high school in 2025; currently employed at Buffalo Wild Wings, she is
excited to begin the next chapter of her life, attending cosmetology school in May at TCAT
Hohenwald; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Crews's noteworthy achievement is a tremendous source of pride for
her family, her community, and the citizens of Maury County; and
WHEREAS, the Mule Day Queen and her court play a prominent role in the promotion of
Mule Day, participating in media interviews and serving as hostesses during the festivities; and
WHEREAS, 2026 Mule Day Court Third Runner-Up Cayley Crews epitomizes the spirit
of distinction and the commitment to greatness that are characteristic of true Tennesseans; her
dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of the respect and admiration of this body; and
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly find it appropriate to pause in their
deliberations to acknowledge and applaud this young woman for her remarkable achievement;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
