HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1063
By Helton-Haynes
A RESOLUTION to recognize William "Bill" F. Knowles, Hamilton
County Clerk, on the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of public service; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable person is Bill Knowles, who, having admirably served
the citizens of Hamilton County as County Clerk since 1974, is taking a much-deserved
retirement on August 31, 2026; and
WHEREAS, a native of Chattanooga, Mr. Knowles was born in the St. Elmo community
and is one of six children born to the late Chattanooga Police Detective Claude Knowles and
Rose Knowles; and
WHEREAS, before his career as a county clerk, Bill Knowles received an honorable
discharge from the United States Army Reserve at the rank of Sergeant First Class; in 1978, he
received an honorary Doctor of Laws degree from Trevecca Nazarene University, and he holds
a Master Barber license; and
WHEREAS, first taking office as county clerk in 1974, Mr. Knowles was elected to his
thirteenth term in 2022; he is the longest-serving elected official in Hamilton County history,
serving for a total of fifty-two years; and
WHEREAS, in the early days of his service, Quarterly Court and County Council minutes
were maintained by pen and a stenographer pad; however, Mr. Knowles promptly implemented
audio recordings and led the transition to digital systems that computerized County Commission
minutes and records, and these recordings are provided to local media and made available to
county officials and the public after each meeting; and

WHEREAS, under his leadership, the Clerk's office has prioritized preserving Hamilton
County history through research and digitizing Legislative Body records dating back to the
county's early days; computerizing historical and current marriage records; and enabling library
staff, genealogists, and the public to conduct research online; and
WHEREAS, throughout his tenure, Bill Knowles and a dedicated team of deputy clerks
have continually evaluated and implemented innovations to better serve their community; in
1993, the Clerk contracted with the State and allowed Hamilton County motorists to receive
replacement titles in his office; and
WHEREAS, in 2001, Mr. Knowles worked with the State to allow motorists to renew
registrations by mail and eventually online, making Hamilton County the first county in the State
to offer internet vehicle renewal; in 2004, he helped make it possible for residents to renew or
replace Tennessee driver licenses through the Clerk's office. Each initiative helped reduce long
lines and provide more options for the public in renewing tags, titles, and licenses; and
WHEREAS, with the help of Mr. Knowles and the Hamilton County Clerk's Office, these
innovations paved the way for statewide adoption of tags by mail and internet in all ninety-five
counties; in addition to this progress and the construction of satellite tag and title facilities
funded by the Mayor and County Commission, the Clerk's office was able to help motorists
avoid traffic and congestion around the downtown courthouse; and
WHEREAS, most recently, Bill Knowles partnered with Food City to install self-service
tag renewal kiosks throughout the county, which helped expand convenient renewal options for
Hamilton County residents; and
WHEREAS, another successful initiative under Mr. Knowles's leadership was the
identification and registration of semi-trailer fleets for companies with offices or depots in the
county, an endeavor that helped generate millions of dollars in additional state and local
revenue; and

WHEREAS, a leader in his profession, Bill Knowles has been entrusted with accounting
for millions of dollars in State and county funds, and the Clerk's office consistently receives
positive audit reports; and
WHEREAS, over the years, Mr. Knowles has received many recognitions, including a
citizenship award from the Civitan Club and a Distinguished Alumni award from Chattanooga's
Central High School, and his innovations have been recognized by the National Association of
Counties on numerous occasions; he has been selected as County Clerk of the Year three
times and was voted Outstanding Official of the Year by the County Officials Association; and
WHEREAS, Bill Knowles has also served in other ways that have benefited his
community, including as a member of the Salvation Army Board of Directors, the Public Bible
School Committee, the Advancing the Ministry of the Gospel Board of Directors, and the
Convention and Visitors Bureau Board; he has served as president of the Tennessee County
Officials Association, president of the Association of County Clerks, and founding member of the
County Clerk's Organ Donor Awareness Program; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Knowles's philosophy of government, deep institutional knowledge, and
openness to responsible change have elevated public service in Hamilton County, benefited
taxpayers, and positively impacted not only those within his county but all Tennesseans; and
WHEREAS, he has been married to Marlene Coulter Knowles for seventy years, with
whom he shares three grown children, Reba, Finley, and Alan; eight grandchildren; and five
great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Knowles and his wife are devoted members of Ridgedale Baptist
Church in Chattanooga; and
WHEREAS, Bill Knowles exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and community
that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
- 3 - 014541

WHEREAS, throughout his illustrious tenure as Hamilton County Clerk, Bill Knowles has
distinguished himself as a public-spirited citizen of the highest order, winning the respect and
admiration of his colleagues and the people he has so ably served; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we extend to William "Bill" F. Knowles, Hamilton County Clerk, our
heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future
endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
