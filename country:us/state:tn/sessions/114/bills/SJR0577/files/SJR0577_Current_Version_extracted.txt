SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 577
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Julian Taylor Bolton of
Memphis.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Julian Taylor Bolton of Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Julian Bolton was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on October 28, 1949, in Memphis, Mr. Bolton was the son of Alex and
Millicent Bolton; his family moved to Chicago, Illinois, and after completing high school, he
returned to Memphis to attend Rhodes College, from which he earned his Bachelor of Arts
degree in Communication in 1971; and
WHEREAS, he went on to obtain his Master of Arts degree in Theater from Memphis
State University, now the University of Memphis, and began his career in education with
Memphis City Schools, later becoming a professor at LeMoyne-Owen College; and
WHEREAS, Julian Bolton was elected to the Shelby County Commission in 1982,
serving with distinction for twenty-four years and holding the position of chairman in 1995; he
chaired the Budget Committee and Legislative Committee during his tenure and after his
departure continued his public service as a policy advisor to both the City of Memphis and the
Shelby County Board of Commissioners; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bolton earned his Juris Doctor degree from the University of Memphis
and was admitted to the Tennessee Bar Association in 1992; he served as managing partner of
the Memphis branch of the Cochran Law Firm for four years before founding his own practice,
the Law Office of Julian T. Bolton, and he also served as general counsel for the National

Organization of Black County Officials and special counsel to the Shelby County Board of
Commissioners; and
WHEREAS, deeply committed to community service, Julian Bolton served on numerous
boards, including the Midtown Mental Health Center Board, the National Association of County
Officials, and the National Association of Counties; and
WHEREAS, often duly honored for his good work, Mr. Bolton was recognized with the
Rhodes College Distinguished Alumni Award in 2009 and received the City of Memphis
Exemplary Service Award from Mayor A.C. Wharton in 2006; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bolton was also wholly devoted to his family, and he always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly missed but fondly remembered by his beloved wife of nearly
fifty years, Joyce Walker Bolton; children, Julian Taylor (Monique) Bolton II, Jared Walker
Bolton, and Jasmine Bolton (Junior) West; granddaughters, Olivia Bolton and Alexis Bolton;
siblings, Leah Cornelius, Mattlyn Turks, Kenneth Bolton, Alex Bolton, and Nedra Bolton Bailey;
his stepsibling, Marion Mauldin; and a host of extended family and friends; and
WHEREAS, Julian Bolton was a true public servant who impacted the lives of those who
were blessed to know him, and he leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we should pay tribute to his bountiful life of purpose and
service; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Julian Taylor Bolton,

reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. Bolton.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
