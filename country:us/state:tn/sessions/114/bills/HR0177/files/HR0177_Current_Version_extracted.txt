HOUSE RESOLUTION 177
By Lamberth
A RESOLUTION to commend Noelle Porterfield for exemplary
service as a legislative intern for the Tennessee
Board of Regents.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, we are pleased to recognize certain persons who
have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the legislative
process; and
WHEREAS, Noelle Porterfield is one such individual, who has distinguished herself in
her role as a legislative intern with the Tennessee Board of Regents; Ms. Porterfield's work has
resulted in innumerable benefits for the staff of the Tennessee Board of Regents she assisted
and the operation of the agency as a whole; and
WHEREAS, Noelle Porterfield is a global honors student at Belmont University in
Nashville, where she studies politics and public law; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government, Ms. Porterfield brought her many abilities and singular expertise
to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Noelle Porterfield has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Porterfield has performed her many duties with extraordinary insight
and rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby
commend Noelle Porterfield for outstanding service as a legislative intern for the Tennessee
Board of Regents and extend to her our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
