HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1166
By Capley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Billy Gene and Doris
Jean Skinner Beckham of Clifton.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
honor the memory of those esteemed individuals whose lives of service and dedication left an
indelible mark upon their communities and the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, two such individuals are Billy Gene and Doris Jean Skinner Beckham of
Clifton, who distinguished themselves as public-spirited citizens of the greatest dedication,
ability, and integrity throughout their lives; and
WHEREAS, Bill Beckham was born in 1925 on Indian Creek in Wayne County in the
Three Churches community; he was a lifelong cattle farmer, and from a young age, he was
inspired by and understood the values of hard work and land stewardship; and
WHEREAS, he proudly served his nation as a member of the United States Navy during
World War II, earning the Victory Medal and the Asiatic-Pacific Medal for his meritorious service;
he received an honorable discharge in 1946 and was a longtime member of the American
Legion, honoring and supporting fellow veterans; and
WHEREAS, Bill Beckham devoted much of his life to civic duty, serving as vice mayor of
Clifton from 1972 to 1974; he was twice elected mayor and served from 1974 to 1976 and 1990
to 1998, earning the respect and admiration of his fellow citizens for his steady and practical
leadership and deep-rooted love for his hometown; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Beckham operated the Sinclair gas station in Clifton, where
conversations flowed freely, much like the fuel he supplied; he took his much-deserved
retirement from Production Credit Services in 1983; and

WHEREAS, Doris Jean Skinner was born in 1929 in Henderson; her father worked in the
timber industry, and as a young girl, she lived in several different parts of the state before her
family ultimately settled in Clifton in 1940, planting roots that left a lasting imprint on the town;
and
WHEREAS, in 1940, the Skinner family opened a five-and-dime store, adjacent to the
historic Russ Hotel; in 1947, the store was moved across the street and became a community
staple and gathering place; and
WHEREAS, Doris Skinner graduated from Frank Hughes School and attended
Draughons' Business College before returning home to help care for her mother and operate
the family's beloved storefront; and
WHEREAS, for decades, the store was the heartbeat of downtown Clifton; Doris Skinner
employed countless individuals throughout the years and taught them responsibility, kindness,
and the importance of knowing individuals by name; and
WHEREAS, after the store closed its doors in 1988, Doris Skinner found employment at
Willoughby Drug Store and Dollar General and continued to greet customers with her warm
smile and servant's heart; and
WHEREAS, Bill Beckham and Doris Skinner were united in marriage in 1950 and lived
lives marked by integrity, humility, generosity, and an abiding love for their hometown; they
wholly believed in honoring faith, family, and their country, providing young people with
opportunities, and showing up for others when it mattered the most; and
WHEREAS, Bill and Doris Beckham were devout members of the Presbyterian Church
in Clifton; Mr. Beckham served as chairman of the board for the daycare, investing in the
youngest members of the community and ensuring a nurturing future for them; and

WHEREAS, Bill and Doris Beckham were consistent in their service and paved the way
for others through their legacy of civic duty, sacrifice, leadership, and love that will forever
remain woven into the Clifton community's story; and
WHEREAS, Bill and Doris Beckham represented a generation that built communities not
for recognition but simply because it was the right thing to do; their spirit of adventure,
unwavering work ethic, pride in Clifton, and devotion to their neighbors have left a legacy that
continues to resonate through the community they so ably served; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Billy Gene and Doris Jean Skinner Beckham of
Clifton, reflecting with gratitude upon their lives of service and extending our deepest respect to
their family and all those who continue to cherish their memory.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
