SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 811
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to recognize Duane Grieve on the occasion of
his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
specially recognize those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and
enthusiasm throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Duane Grieve is one such estimable person who, having rendered sterling
service as executive director of the East Tennessee Community Design Center, will take his
much-deserved retirement on February 15, 2026; and
WHEREAS, the East Tennessee Community Design Center is a nonprofit organization
dedicated to enhancing the quality of life in the region by providing professional design and
planning assistance to community groups and organizations; and
WHEREAS, during his distinguished tenure with the East Tennessee Community Design
Center, Duane Grieve strengthened the organization's financial stability and expanded its
presence in the community, taking on more than 1,300 projects over the span of fifty-six years;
and
WHEREAS, his projects as executive director have included redesigning the old Miller's
Building, which became the headquarters of the Knoxville Utilities Board; raising money for a
statue of the Everly Brothers in the park that honors the brothers; working with the Black
community in the Lyons View area to preserve the neighborhood; and ensuring the completion
of the SoKno Rail Trail and Art Walk from Chapman Highway to Ijams Nature Center; and
WHEREAS, prior to his service at the East Tennessee Community Design Center,
Duane Grieve served as vice mayor of Knoxville from 2015 to 2017 and as a member of the

Knoxville City Council from 2009 to 2017, in which positions he earned the respect and
admiration of his peers and community alike; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Grieve, an architect specializing in historic preservation, was formerly
the owner of an architectural practice situated in an 1880s building on Emory Place; and
WHEREAS, a leader in his community and his profession, Mr. Grieve served as a
tenured assistant professor at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, and an adjunct professor
in professional practice; he has been an active member of the American Institute of Architects at
local, state, regional, and national levels; and
WHEREAS, his many professional accomplishments aside, Mr. Grieve is most grateful
for the love and support of his wife, Marsha; and
WHEREAS, his remarkable success as a public servant is directly attributable to his
reliance on the time-honored values of hard work and common sense and his uncommon ability
to work well with people from all walks of life; and
WHEREAS, Duane Grieve is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service
that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby commend Duane Grieve on his
honorable and astute service to the people of Tennessee and extend to him our heartfelt wishes
for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
