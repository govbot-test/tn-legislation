SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 990
By Rose
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Bartlett Panthers
boys' basketball team upon winning the 2026
TSSAA Division I, Class 4A state championship.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly should salute those student athletes
who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as true champions of
whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such exemplary group of young men is the Panthers boys' basketball
team of Bartlett High School in Shelby County, whose members have proven themselves to be
the fiercest of competitors and truly worthy of admiration by winning a TSSAA state
championship for the first time in twenty-five years; and
WHEREAS, there were high expectations for Bartlett this season, as the nationally
ranked Panthers were the overwhelming favorites to win the state title; despite facing this
pressure, the team did not blink but instead excelled at every level of competition to claim the
school's second Gold Ball trophy as Class 4A champions; and
WHEREAS, after winning their district and regional tournaments, Bartlett destroyed
Rossview in the sectional round to punch their ticket for the Big Dance at Middle Tennessee
State University's Murphy Center; and
WHEREAS, the Panthers bested Brentwood 65-56 in the quarterfinals and defeated
Bearden 71-61 in the semifinal round of the state tournament; and
WHEREAS, in the title tilt, Bartlett rolled over Walker Valley 69-47 behind the game-high
15 points, six rebounds, and four blocked shots posted by senior Axton Perry, a Southeast
Missouri State signee, to claim the laurels of a state champion; and

WHEREAS, there was certainly no shortage of dynamic talent on the Panthers'
championship squad; junior Dylan Jones was named the state tournament's Most Valuable
Player after scoring 22 points in the quarterfinals, 22 points in the semifinals, and nine points in
the final match-up; Dylan has been named to the 2025-2026 ALL-USA Southeast Boys'
Basketball Team and is ranked as the No. 49 player in the 2027 class; and
WHEREAS, DJ Okoth, who was honored as Class 4A Mr. Basketball in his sophomore
season, scored eight points in the championship game; DJ is ranked as the No. 15 prospect in
the 2028 class; and
WHEREAS, in addition to winning the state title, Bartlett posted an outstanding 35-4
overall record without suffering a loss to an opponent from Tennessee and was nationally
ranked No. 12 in the USA Today Sports Super 25 rankings; and
WHEREAS, the Panthers' superb performance would not have been possible without the
expert guidance of Head Coach Dion Real and the unfailing support of the players' families,
their fans, and the Bartlett community; and
WHEREAS, the members of the Bartlett High School boys' basketball team epitomize all
that is good in today's student athletes, as they have achieved a premier level of success in
competition while exhibiting the highest degree of character in all aspects of life; and
WHEREAS, the perseverance, determination, work ethic, and talent of this group of
young men not only made possible a championship season but also surely bode well for their
future success; and
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly find it appropriate to acknowledge
and applaud the Bartlett Panthers for their outstanding season on the hardwood; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Bartlett High School
