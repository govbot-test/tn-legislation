SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 879
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to honor Don Lindsey, 2026 Community
Volunteer recipient of the John J. Duncan Sr.
Award for Senior Advocacy.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Don Lindsey of Knoxville, who was recently named
the 2026 Community Volunteer recipient of the John J. Duncan Sr. Award for Senior Advocacy;
and
WHEREAS, the John J. Duncan Sr. Award for Senior Advocacy was created to honor
those who steadfastly work to improve the lives of seniors in the Knoxville community, as the
award's namesake did throughout his career; recipients are professionals and community
members who have been nominated in recognition of their tireless efforts on behalf of seniors;
and
WHEREAS, Don Lindsey has made a significant impact in the Knoxville community over
the years through his work with AAA East Tennessee, the Knox County Health Department, and
numerous other governmental and public service organizations; and
WHEREAS, during his nearly forty-year career with AAA East Tennessee, Don Lindsey
conducted numerous traffic safety, public affairs, and public relations programs in cooperation
with local, state, and national groups; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lindsey was an important member of the AAA team that founded the
groundbreaking Volunteer Assisted Transportation program that has helped thousands of
people during its nearly seventeen years in operation; he continues to serve as a member of its
advisory board; and
SJR0879
014128

WHEREAS, in 2006, he was instrumental in Knoxville being selected as one of the nine
pilot sites for the new CarFit national program that helps seniors achieve greater comfort,
safety, and confidence in driving in their cars, and he has coordinated or assisted with more
than sixty training programs and other events for the program since that time; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lindsey retired from AAA in 2016, and with the backing of his former
employer, he quickly became a vital contributor to the vibrant senior support and public service
community in Knoxville; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lindsey is active with the Knox County Health Department's Senior
Safety Task Force and its fall prevention awareness programs and the Task Force's Stay Active
and Independent for Life program, which has helped thousands of seniors gain greater strength
and improved balance; and
WHEREAS, truly a public servant of the highest order, Mr. Lindsey continues to
volunteer with AARP, the Metro Drug Coalition, the Front Page Foundation, and the Tennessee
Commissioner of Health's Council on Injury Prevention; he is also a past president and board
member of the Optimist Club of Knoxville; and
WHEREAS, prior to his career with AAA, Mr. Lindsey worked ten years as a disc jockey
and news reporter at radio stations WSEV in Sevierville and WBIR in Knoxville; and
WHEREAS, his many professional and personal accomplishments aside, Don Lindsey is
most grateful for the love and companionship he shares with his three children and four
grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lindsey's involvement in senior and other community organizations
speaks volumes about his commitment to making Knoxville a better place for everyone,
particularly those who are most vulnerable; and
- 2 - 014128
WHEREAS, Don Lindsey's lifelong dedication to service is a testament to the impact one
person can have on a community; he has left an indelible mark on Knoxville by acting on his
steadfast belief in the power of giving back; and
WHEREAS, as Knoxville continues to grow and evolve, the foundation that Mr. Lindsey
has helped build will remain a guiding light for future generations; his legacy exemplifies his
deeply rooted desire to make the world a better place; and
WHEREAS, Don Lindsey is most worthy of our approbation on this special occasion;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Don Lindsey, 2026
Community Volunteer recipient of the John J. Duncan Sr. Award for Senior Advocacy, extending
our best wishes for his continued success as he compassionately and devotedly serves his
community and its senior citizens.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 3 - 014128
