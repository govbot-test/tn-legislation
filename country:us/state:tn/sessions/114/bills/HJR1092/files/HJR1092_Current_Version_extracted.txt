HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1092
By Lynn
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Henry Shere Queener,
Jr., of Mt. Juliet.
WHEREAS, it was with great sadness that the members of this General Assembly
learned of the passing of Henry Shere Queener, Jr., who will be fondly remembered as a proud
veteran, a faithful civic leader, and a devoted family man; and
WHEREAS, a native of Jacksboro, Mr. Queener attended military college at The Citadel
and served on active duty in the U.S. military during the Vietnam War; and
WHEREAS, he later earned a Bachelor of Arts from Cumberland University and a
Bachelor of Science from Austin Peay State College, majoring in U.S. history for both degrees;
and
WHEREAS, a lifelong student of military history, Mr. Queener collected a wide variety of
U.S. military artifacts, ranging from the Revolutionary War era to the present; and
WHEREAS, he began his career in insurance with Aetna and spent his entire
professional life in the industry; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Queener met his future wife, Gwynne Lee Kingsley, while she was
working at Charles Elder's Book and Antique Shop in Nashville; and
WHEREAS, the couple was joined in holy matrimony on August 2, 1969, and enjoyed
more than fifty-six years of married life together before his passing; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Queener was a devoted father to his two sons, always encouraging
them to pursue their goals and aspirations; and
WHEREAS, after taking his much-deserved retirement, Mr. Queener was active in civic
affairs, regularly volunteering with American Legion Auxiliary Unit 281; and

WHEREAS, he was a mainstay of the Wilson County Republican Party for many years,
and Mrs. Queener was involved with the Mt. Juliet Republican Women; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Queener was also an active member of First Baptist Church of Mt.
Juliet; and
WHEREAS, Henry Shere Queener, Jr., is survived by his wife, Gwynne Lee Queener;
his sons, Henry Shere (Jennifer) Queener III and Mitchell Asher (Jessica) Queener; and his
grandchildren, Michal Marie Queener, Rose Karen Queener, and Henry Shere Queener IV; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his parents, Henry Shere Queener and Clara
Gibson Queener, and his half-brother, George David Queener; and
WHEREAS, Henry Queener is deeply mourned by his loving family and his friends and
neighbors in Mt. Juliet, and we wish to pay our respects to a gentleman who lived in service to
his fellow human beings; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we celebrate the life of Henry Shere Queener, Jr., even as we honor his
memory and mourn his passing, remembering his life of purpose and commitment.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our condolences to Mr. Queener's family
and many friends.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
