HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1186
By Reneau
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Auto Crimes Unit of
the Chattanooga Police Department upon its
receipt of the Chief's Award.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal concerns to ensure the public safety and welfare, and the Volunteer State is especially
proud to be the home of so many valiant individuals who have performed above and beyond the
call of duty in service of public safety; and
WHEREAS, such individuals comprise the members of the Auto Crimes Unit of the
Chattanooga Police Department, including Investigators Brian McClard, Kevin Orsburn, and
Jamie Willis, and Sergeant Randy Raulston, who were recently honored with the Chattanooga
Police Department's Chief's Award, in recognition of their exemplary service and dedication; and
WHEREAS, in 2025, the Auto Crimes Unit handled more than four hundred cases while
maintaining exceptional investigative integrity and productivity, demonstrating a strong work
ethic, professionalism, and a commitment to supporting fellow officers; and
WHEREAS, most notably, the unit uncovered and dismantled a large-scale organized
auto theft ring involving more than one thousand vehicles—a complex, multi-jurisdictional
investigation that required extensive coordination and persistence, ultimately preventing further
victimization and delivering a significant impact to organized auto crime; and
WHEREAS, the efforts of the Auto Crimes Unit made a lasting impact on the
Chattanooga Police Department and the community it serves; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend the Auto Crimes Unit of the Chattanooga Police
Department upon its receipt of the Chief's Award.
