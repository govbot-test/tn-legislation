AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 312* House Bill No. 1235
by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 37-1-172, is amended by adding the
following as new subsections:
(c) When the department serves as the representative payee on behalf of a child
in the custody or guardianship of the department for any social security benefits under
Title II of the federal Social Security Act, compiled in 42 U.S.C. § 401 et seq.,
supplemental security income benefits under Title XVI of the federal Social Security Act,
compiled in 42 U.S.C. § 1381 et seq., or veteran’s benefits that the child may receive,
the department shall conserve those benefits for the child's future needs in a manner
consistent with federal and state asset resource limits, or use the funds as allowed in
subsection (e).
(d) The department shall exercise its discretion to conserve or use the benefits
specified in subsection (c) in the best interest of the child. The department is authorized
to establish appropriate accounts for the use or conservation of the benefits, including a
plan for achieving self-support, a 529 or 529A plan, an individual development account,
a special needs trust, a bank account, or any other trust account determined not to
interfere with social security or asset limitations for any other benefit program.
(e) If it is in the best interest of the child, then the department may utilize the
benefits, rather than conserving them, in the following circumstances:
(1) For any special needs services that are not otherwise provided by the
department;

(2) For the cost of caring for the child, if the conserved benefit amounts
negatively impact social security or asset limitations; and
(3) For the cost of caring for the child, if the child is placed in a residential
facility. For the purposes of this subdivision (e)(3), a residential facility does not
include a group home or similar placement.
(f) The department shall maintain child-specific records related to the
conservation and use of benefits specified in subsection (c).
(g) The department may use a reasonable portion of interest earnings from the
benefits specified in subsection (c) to offset the reasonable expenses incurred in
meeting the department's obligations to use or conserve benefits pursuant to this
section.
(h) When the child is no longer in the legal custody or guardianship of the
department, the department shall release any conserved benefits pursuant to the
requirements of the funding source, or in the absence of any requirements, release the
conserved benefits to:
(1) The child, if the child is at least eighteen (18) years of age or is
emancipated; or
(2) The child's legal custodian if the child is a minor and not
emancipated.
(i) The department is authorized to promulgate rules to effectuate the purposes
of this section.
SECTION 2. For the purpose of promulgating rules, this act takes effect upon becoming
a law, the public welfare requiring it. All other sections take effect January 1, 2027, and apply to
any benefits accrued on or after that date, the public welfare requiring it.
