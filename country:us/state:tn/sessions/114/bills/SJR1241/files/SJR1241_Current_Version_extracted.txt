SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1241
By Crowe
A RESOLUTION to celebrate the clearing of Jackson Bridge in the
aftermath of Hurricane Helene.
WHEREAS, on September 27, 2024, one of the most catastrophic floods ever to hit this
State devastated several areas across East Tennessee due to Hurricane Helene; and
WHEREAS, on that fateful day, the water rose rapidly across East Tennessee as
torrential rains fell, causing extensive damage, with vast swaths of communities washed away in
the flood waters, and seventeen individuals were confirmed to have lost their lives due to the
rampaging water that reached an unprecedented level; and
WHEREAS, located in Washington County, the Jackson Bridge near Conklin is a vital
pipeline to the community, and after the devastation from Hurricane Helene, it became a major
priority to clear the bridge as soon as possible; and
WHEREAS, just hours after the fooding, area neighbors jumped in to serve, as the
community came together to clear Jackson Bridge, showcasing the best of humanity and
community strength, as the Lord's hand was made known, and proving that even in challenging
times, great things take place; and
WHEREAS, the clearing of Jackson Bridge not only opened a pipeline that allowed for
the rescue of stranded people and the distribution of items necessary for the recovery of the
area but also established bonds of friendship that will last for generations to come; and
WHEREAS, replacing a ford known as "Wears Rock Ford," named after a Mr. Wear who
drowned in the Nolichucky River, Jackson Bridge was first constructed in 1890 and was rebuilt
in 1901 and again in 1940, when portions were washed out by flooding; and
WHEREAS, emptying into the Nolichucky River, the Keplinger Branch has provided
recreation for the community via swimming and fishing for generations and, in early days,

supplied the power to three mills: Ruble Mill, Graham Mill, and a sawmill; portions of Ruble Mill

WHEREAS, the current Jackson Bridge over the river was begun in 1987 and completed

WHEREAS, it has been said that the human race is at its best when circumstances are

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we join in the celebration of the clearing of Jackson
Bridge in Washington County and commend those everyday Tennesseans, who, in a moment of
great peril, came to the aid and rescue of their neighbors and friends without hesitation; it is
these unsung heroes who comprise the very heart of the Volunteer State.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
