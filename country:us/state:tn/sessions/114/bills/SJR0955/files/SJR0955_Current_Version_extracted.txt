SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 955
By Pody
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Debbie Stephens for her
years of dedicated service to the General
Assembly.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should pay
special tribute to those estimable legislative employees whose commitment to excellence and
dedication to public service have brought untold benefits to the citizens of the State of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Debbie Stephens, who has served as a legislative
assistant since 2011, when she brought her singular ability and characteristic grace and charm
to Capitol Hill, and she is beloved by her colleagues and legislators alike; and
WHEREAS, a native of Wilson County, Ms. Stephens is the daughter of Robert and Lois
Kerr; she was born on Edwards Air Force Base in California, where her father was stationed at
the time, and grew up in Watertown, attending school in a three-room schoolhouse in Shop
Springs for the first eight years of her educational career; and
WHEREAS, a 1971 graduate of Lebanon High School, Debbie Stephens received her
diploma from Victory Bible Training Center in Nashville, focusing on Bible ministry, and began
her career as an assistant to the comptroller of Jarman Shoe Company, ultimately retiring from
Genesco, Inc., after nearly twenty-five years; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Stephens first came on board with the General Assembly to serve as a
legislative assistant to then-Representative Mark Pody, moving with him to the Senate after his
election to that body in 2018; in January 2025, she joined Senator Dawn White, chairman of the
Senate Education Committee, as her legislative assistant, in which role she continues to serve
today; and

WHEREAS, Debbie Stephens has been an invaluable asset to the legislators she has
served and to the General Assembly as a whole; as an assistant, she is capable, dependable,
and hardworking, and as a colleague, she is a blessing to all who are fortunate to know her; and
WHEREAS, apart from her service with the State, Ms. Stephens has an array of
passionate pursuits, all of which she excels in; as a hunter, she has bagged a 5X5 elk in
Colorado, and her fishing prowess was evidenced by her forty-pound King Mackerel catch off
the coast of Florida; and
WHEREAS, she is the reigning Ms. Wilson County Senior Tennessee, having won the
talent, interview, and evening gown components of the competition, and she will represent
Wilson County in statewide competition in April 2026 in Crossville; and
WHEREAS, devoted to serving her community, Debbie Stephens is the pianist and
treasurer at Cornerstone Baptist Church, is a member and past president of the Watertown
Chamber of Commerce, and is currently a member of the Lebanon/Wilson Chamber of
Commerce; she was also active in raising public awareness about Jake's Blessing Box, which
serves individuals in need; and
WHEREAS, for sixteen years, she has served on the Board of Directors for Wilson
County Promotions, where she chairs four committees for the Wilson County/Tennessee State
Fair; and
WHEREAS, Debbie Stephens is also a small business owner; she has operated Depot
Junction Café, Inc., in her hometown for thirty-three years, serving all the locals and the
passengers on the Tennessee Central Passenger train that travels fourteen times per year from
Nashville. Her customers have traveled from many bordering states and at least four countries
to dine in her establishment, where she works every Saturday and Sunday, welcoming patrons
with hometown cooking; and

WHEREAS, throughout her many adventures in life, she enjoyed the love of her
husband, James Stephens, whom she married in 1972; the couple enjoyed fifty-two years of
wedded companionship until his passing in November 2024; and
WHEREAS, she has also been blessed with the love of her daughter, Kelly (Chad)
Boles; three grandchildren, Kaitlyn, Davin, and Abby; one great-grandson, Declan Oliver James
Trusty; and fiancé, Tony Watson; and
WHEREAS, Debbie Stephens is an exemplary citizen who epitomizes the ideal of the
consummate professional, dedicated public servant, and reliable friend and neighbor; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Stephens has distinguished herself as a public servant of the highest
order and is clearly deserving of our respect, admiration, and commendation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Debbie Stephens,
extending our deepest appreciation for her meritorious service to the State of Tennessee and
the General Assembly.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
