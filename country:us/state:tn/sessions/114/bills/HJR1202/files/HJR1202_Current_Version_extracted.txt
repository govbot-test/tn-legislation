HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1202
By Reneau
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Andrew Irwin upon his
receipt of the Patrol Officer of the Year Award
from the Chattanooga Police Department.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal concerns to ensure the public safety and welfare, and the Volunteer State is especially
proud to be the home of so many valiant individuals who have performed above and beyond the
call of duty in service of public safety; and
WHEREAS, Andrew Irwin was recently honored with the Chattanooga Police
Department's (CPD's) Patrol Officer of the Year Award in recognition of his consistent
demonstration of the highest standards of professionalism, dedication, and service; and
WHEREAS, Officer Irwin is a member of the SWAT Team and a SWAT school
instructor, and his passion for teaching is evident; he excels in training and mentoring new
officers, translating real-world tactical experience—building clearing, firearms, medical
refreshers—into practical instruction, and emphasizing safety and sound decision-making; and
WHEREAS, he also serves on Peer Support, in which capacity he offers vital emotional
assistance to his fellow officers; and
WHEREAS, Officer Irwin's dedication, initiative, and balance of tactical excellence with
compassionate mentorship help shape officers for the demands of the job and their future
careers, and he is highly deserving of this honor; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Andrew Irwin upon his receipt of the Patrol Officer
of the Year Award from the Chattanooga Police Department.
