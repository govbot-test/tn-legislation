HOUSE RESOLUTION 149
By Garrett
A RESOLUTION to condemn the failure of Nashville Electric
Service during the recent storm event and call
upon the board of directors of Nashville Electric
Service to exercise leadership, including the
replacement of the chief executive officer.
WHEREAS, Nashville Electric Service (NES) is entrusted with the critical responsibility
of providing reliable electric service to the residents, businesses, hospitals, and first responders
of Davidson County and parts of Sumner, Rutherford, Robertson, Williamson, Wilson, and
Cheatham counties; and
WHEREAS, severe storm events are foreseeable and recurring in Middle Tennessee,
and electric utilities have a duty to prepare, respond, and communicate effectively during such
emergencies; and
WHEREAS, during the recent storm impacting Nashville and the surrounding region,
NES experienced widespread outages affecting tens of thousands of customers, including
prolonged outages lasting days in some neighborhoods; and
WHEREAS, numerous constituents reported a lack of timely restoration, inadequate
communication, inaccurate outage estimates, and insufficient transparency from NES
leadership throughout the crisis; and
WHEREAS, these failures compounded the hardship faced by families, seniors,
medically vulnerable residents, and local businesses already dealing with storm damage; and
WHEREAS, confidence in a public utility depends not only on infrastructure but on
competent leadership, accountability, and honest communication; and
WHEREAS, the response by NES leadership has raised serious concerns regarding
preparedness, management effectiveness, and executive decision-making; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we formally
condemn the abysmal response of Nashville Electric Service to the recent storm event and find
that such performance falls short of the standard expected of a critical public utility.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we call upon the Board of Directors of Nashville
Electric Service to conduct a full and transparent review of the storm response, including
operational readiness, communication failures, and leadership performance.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we hereby urge the NES Board of Directors to
exercise its authority to replace the chief executive officer should the board determine that
current leadership is unwilling or unable to restore public confidence and ensure reliable service
going forward.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we call for NES to present a comprehensive
corrective action plan addressing storm preparedness, grid hardening, workforce deployment,
and customer communication, with measurable benchmarks and public reporting.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that a certified copy of this resolution be transmitted to
the Board of Directors of Nashville Electric Service, the Mayor of the Metropolitan Government
of Nashville and Davidson County, and the members of the Metropolitan Council of Nashville
and Davidson County.
