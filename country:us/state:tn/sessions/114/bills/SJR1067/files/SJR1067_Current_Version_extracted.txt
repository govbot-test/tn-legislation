SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1067
By Haile
A RESOLUTION to honor and celebrate the 175th anniversary of
the YMCA.
WHEREAS, the Young Men's Christian Association (YMCA or "the Y") marks its 175th
anniversary this year, having been founded in the United States by Thomas Valentine Sullivan
at the Old South Church in Boston on December 29, 1851; inspired by the YMCA movement in
England, the organization pursued a mission to put Christian principles into practice by building
healthy spirit, mind, and body for all; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA has continually adapted to meet the evolving needs of American
communities, strengthening society by bringing people together, fostering relationships, and
creating a sense of belonging; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA's legacy is inextricably tied to the history and progress of our
nation; the YMCA provided safe housing during the Industrial Revolution; pioneered the first
night school and English as a second language classes; and delivered the first group swim
lessons; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA is widely known as the birthplace of basketball, but it is also the
birthplace of Father's Day, with the first celebration having been held at the Spokane YMCA in
1910; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA established the Youth and Government program in 1936, a
premier civic engagement initiative that continues today; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA has led humanitarian efforts during wartime, including the work
of YMCA leader and President Wilson's General Secretary for the National War Work Council,
John R. Mott, who received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1946 for the undertaking; and

WHEREAS, the YMCA has been the nation's leading nonprofit provider of childcare and
afterschool programs since the 1970s and 1980s to support working families; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA is the leading provider of emergency childcare for front-line
workers and was a significant provider of food during the COVID pandemic; and
WHEREAS, today, 2,600 YMCAs serve more than 10,000 communities nationally in
every state and territory, with more than 300,000 staff and 350,000 volunteers delivering
essential programs to more than seventeen million people annually, including nearly eleven
million adults and 6.5 million children and youth; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee YMCAs currently serve over seventy percent of Tennessee
counties and impact nearly 300,000 youth across the State; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee YMCAs are the state's largest nonprofit provider of childcare
and afterschool programs, caring for more than 192,000 children; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee YMCAs provide more than 21,000 swim lessons to children,
distribute nearly six million meals, and serve as the largest provider of fitness programs for older
adults—reaching more than 116,000 seniors across the State; and
WHEREAS, "the Y" serves people of all ages, backgrounds, and abilities, creating
spaces where all people feel welcome, are treated with respect, and find opportunities to
connect with their neighbors; and
WHEREAS, YMCAs across the nation are working to build stronger, more connected
communities through intentional experiences that help people achieve their goals, foster
connection, and promote well-being in order to build relationships and thrive; and
WHEREAS, in 2026, YMCAs nationwide will host special events that celebrate the
moments, programs, and people that have shaped their story; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF

REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we congratulate and express appreciation to the
YMCA for 175 years of service to the nation and honor the YMCA's role in responding to
community needs, connecting people, fostering support, building relationships, and creating a
sense of belonging.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we commend the hundreds of thousands of staff and
volunteers who create connected communities and advance the YMCA's mission to build
healthy spirits, minds, and bodies for all.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we honor the YMCA's efforts to address social
isolation and loneliness by creating places and spaces that promote achievement, well-being,
and connection and help people build relationships and thrive.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
