SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 887
By McNally
A RESOLUTION to support Tennessee's response to the United
States Department of Energy Request for
Information on Nuclear Lifecycle Innovation
Campuses.
WHEREAS, Tennesseans have always answered America's call; embodying a
"Volunteer Spirit" passed down from generation to generation, we stand ready to answer
President Trump's charge to unleash the domestic nuclear industrial base and secure American
energy dominance; and
WHEREAS, on January 28, 2026, the United States Department of Energy invited states
to host Nuclear Lifecycle Innovation Campuses—integrated hubs integrating the full nuclear
value chain—that could each attract up to $50 billion dollars in private investment, create tens of
thousands of jobs, and begin initial operations as early as 2027; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee opened the Atomic Age with the Manhattan Project in Oak
Ridge and has never left the frontier of nuclear innovation, sustaining more than eighty years of
unbroken service to the nation through Oak Ridge National Laboratory, the Y-12 National
Security Complex, and a nuclear environmental cleanup project recognized as a "remarkable
achievement" by the United States Environmental Protection Agency and positioning the nation
for future nuclear advancements; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee is today the number-one state in the nation for nuclear energy
development, home to more than 230 nuclear companies, the top nuclear engineering programs
in the country, and a workforce that includes various professions, from the research scientist to
the welder—an ecosystem that has attracted more than $8 billion dollars in nuclear investment
from around the world; and

WHEREAS, Tennessee is the location of the nation's first early site permitted for a small
modular reactor (the first SMR for the American grid), which is backed by a $400-million federal
award; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee hosts the first new reactor type licensed by the Nuclear
Regulatory Commission in over fifty years, which is now under construction in Oak Ridge; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee also hosts the first new fuel facilities licensed by the Nuclear
Regulatory Commission in over fifty years, with one facility set to become the first-ever Category
II nuclear fuel facility in the United States; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee has been chosen as the location for the first privately funded
nuclear fuel recycling facility in American history; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee is home to the only commercial uranium enrichment centrifuge
manufacturing plant in the country, one of the largest private uranium enrichment investments in
the nation's history; and
WHEREAS, while Tennessee is a demonstrated leader in accelerating deployment of
commercial nuclear power, the nuclear activities in this State also result in critical national
security and defense achievements. Public and private sector investments and partnerships
have placed Tennessee at the heart of domestic enrichment supply chain development,
strengthening national security and fueling energy dominance; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee is at the vanguard of the development of commercial fusion,
moving forward as an Agreement State with a foundational regulatory framework developed in
collaboration with the industry and financially supported by Governor Bill Lee and the General
Assembly; this framework is both flexible and protective of health, safety, and security, while
also substantially contributing to national and international fusion advancements; and
WHEREAS, Governor Bill Lee and the General Assembly have invested $70 million in
the Tennessee Nuclear Energy Supply Chain Investment Fund to build this nuclear ecosystem,
- 2 - 014167
with an additional $25 million proposed in the 2026 budget, and these State dollars have
catalyzed billons in private capital—proof that Tennessee does not just welcome nuclear energy
but actively builds the conditions for its success; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee is, by any measure, the most prepared State in the Union to
host a Nuclear Lifecycle Innovation Campus; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we urge Governor Bill Lee to submit a
comprehensive response to the Department of Energy Request for Information on Nuclear
Lifecycle Innovation Campuses no later than April 1, 2026, pledge our full support for
Tennessee's candidacy, and declare our intent to take any legislative action necessary to
position Tennessee as the host of this campus.
