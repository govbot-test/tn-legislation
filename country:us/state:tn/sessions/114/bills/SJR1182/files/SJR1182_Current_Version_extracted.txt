SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1182
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Glenn Jacobs on his
exemplary tenure as Knox County Mayor.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those Tennesseans who have performed with uncommon devotion and ability
throughout their careers in public service; and
WHEREAS, one such outstanding person is Glenn Jacobs, who is taking a well-earned
retirement as Mayor of Knox County after a distinguished two-term tenure; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Jacobs was born in Spain while his family was stationed there with
the United States Air Force; raised in Missouri, he earned a bachelor's degree in English
literature from Northeast Missouri State University, where he played both football and basketball
on a full scholarship; and
WHEREAS, prior to his service in public office, Glenn Jacobs enjoyed a successful
career in professional wrestling, achieving national recognition as a WWE Superstar known as
"Kane"; over two decades, he won numerous titles and was inducted into the WWE Hall of
Fame in 2021; and
WHEREAS, elected Mayor of Knox County in 2018 and reelected in 2022, Mayor Jacobs
has worked diligently to enhance the quality of life for the citizens of Knox County, striving to
make the community an exceptional place to live, work, and raise a family; and
WHEREAS, Mayor Glenn Jacobs has supported a number of initiatives designed to
strengthen education, workforce development, and community well-being during his tenure,
including Read City USA; the Knox County Skilled Trades Academy and Regional Training
Center; the Knox County Mayor's TCAT Scholarship; the Beaver Creek Water Trail cleanup;
and Operation Hero's Hill, an initiative aimed at addressing veteran homelessness; and

WHEREAS, through his leadership, he has demonstrated a commitment to innovation,
opportunity, and service to others, earning the respect and appreciation of his fellow citizens;
and
WHEREAS, Glenn Jacobs is grateful for the love and support he shares with his wife of
more than thirty years, Crystal; they are the proud parents of two daughters and are blessed
with two sons-in-law and two grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, a public-spirited citizen of the highest order, Mayor Jacobs has compiled a
singular record of success during his exemplary public service career; and
WHEREAS, his remarkable success as a civic leader is directly attributable to his
reliance on the time-honored values of hard work and common sense and his uncommon ability
to work well with people from all walks of life; and
WHEREAS, a strong advocate for the citizens of Knox County, Mayor Jacobs is wholly
committed to the noble precepts of public service that have earned Tennessee recognition as
the Volunteer State, and he should be specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Glenn Jacobs for his
honorable and astute service to the people of Tennessee as Mayor of Knox County, salute his
unswerving commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction, and extend to
him our best wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
