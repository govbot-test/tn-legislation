SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 572
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Centenary United
Methodist Church upon the dedication of its
historical marker.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should recognize and
celebrate those historic institutions that have played a vital role in the civic, educational, and
spiritual life of this State and its citizens; and
WHEREAS, one such venerable institution is Centenary United Methodist Church, which
dedicated its historical marker on November 20, 2025, at 3:00 p.m., at its current location at 584
East McLemore Avenue in Memphis; and
WHEREAS, the marker, approved by the Tennessee Historical Commission, stands as a
tribute to the sacrifices, struggles, bravery, and resilience of Centenary United Methodist
Church's pastors and congregations and serves as a solemn reminder of the enduring costs of
progress and freedom; and
WHEREAS, Centenary United Methodist Church and its accompanying school were
founded in 1865 by the Methodist Episcopal Church North, originally located on Raburn Street
near downtown Memphis; and
WHEREAS, from the church's earliest days, its pastors and members engaged in civil
action and community leadership, establishing the church as a cornerstone of faith and justice in
the city of Memphis; and
WHEREAS, after the church burned during the Memphis Massacre of 1866, the
congregation reconstructed the building in January 1867, continuing its ministry of hope and
perseverance; and
WHEREAS, Centenary United Methodist Church's affiliated school educated a
generation of students who became some of Memphis's first Black teachers, and its parsonage

housed the first kindergarten for Black children in the city; in addition, the first Black Boy Scout
troop in Memphis was organized at the church; and
WHEREAS, throughout the twentieth century, Centenary United Methodist Church's
leadership and congregation remained at the forefront of the struggle for equality and civil rights;
and
WHEREAS, under the guidance of Dr. H.B. Gibson, pastor and local NAACP president,
the church was instrumental in advancing voting rights during the 1940s; and
WHEREAS, in the 1960s, Centenary United Methodist Church's ministry reached
national significance under Reverend Dr. James M. Lawson, Jr., whose leadership made the
church a vital organizing hub for civil rights efforts in Memphis and beyond; and
WHEREAS, in 1966, Centenary United Methodist Church served as a site for strategic
planning to ensure that James Meredith's March Against Fear, from Memphis to Jackson,
Mississippi, would continue after Mr. Meredith was shot; church member and attorney A.W.
Willis, Jr., served as Mr. Meredith's local counsel; and
WHEREAS, in 1968, as tensions mounted between Black sanitation workers and
Memphis officials, Reverend James Lawson and Centenary's congregation hosted meetings
urging city leaders to provide fair working conditions; when no agreement was reached, the
workers organized their strike meetings at the church, shaping a pivotal chapter in the nation's
Civil Rights Movement; and
WHEREAS, at Reverend Lawson's invitation, Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., came to
Memphis to support the sanitation workers' cause, and strategy sessions were held at
Centenary; and
WHEREAS, following Dr. King's assassination on April 4, 1968, two Centenary United
Methodist Church members—Clara Ester, who attempted to aid Dr. King on the balcony, and
Robert S. Lewis, Jr., who prepared his body for burial—bore direct witness to history; and

WHEREAS, in the years since, Centenary United Methodist Church has remained
steadfast in its calling to serve, teach, and uplift, preserving the memory of those who came
before while fostering a vibrant and compassionate community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Centenary United
Methodist Church upon the dedication of its historical marker, recognizing its 160 years of
extraordinary service, leadership, and witness in the pursuit of faith, equality, and community
progress.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
