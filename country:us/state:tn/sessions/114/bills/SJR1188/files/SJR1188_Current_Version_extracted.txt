SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1188
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Rhett Pratt for his
service to the General Assembly.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
pay tribute to those estimable legislative employees whose commitment to excellence, genuine
decency, and dedication to public service have brought untold benefits to the citizens of the
State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Rhett Pratt is precisely such an individual, having served the Tennessee
General Assembly with distinction, dedication, and an unmistakably jovial disposition for more
than a decade; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Pratt earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science with a
concentration in Public Administration and a master's degree in public policy and administration
from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, an institution to which he has remained devoted
with the kind of loyalty only a true, long-suffering Volunteers fan could sustain; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Pratt began his legislative career as an assistant for Senator Steve
Dickerson in May 2014 and served in that role until 2019; and
WHEREAS, from his role as legislative assistant, Mr. Pratt then joined the Senate State
and Local Government Committee as a research analyst in 2019, reviewing calendars,
preparing analyses, and providing legislative guidance in committee meetings, all while gently
redirecting any lobbyist who sought an audience with him to the more orderly venue of his email
inbox; and
WHEREAS, from 2021 to 2023, Rhett Pratt gained invaluable experience during his time
as a legislative budget analyst within the Office of Legislative Budget, in which position he

conducted a thorough analysis of state budgets in the fields of education, higher education, and
capital projects; and
WHEREAS, from January to October of 2023, he briefly departed the General Assembly
for "the dark side," devoting his time and talents to government relations at the Tennessee
Municipal League, where he advocated for municipal interests in the areas of finance and
property while further cementing his undying love for all things pertaining to the riveting study of
municipal governance; and
WHEREAS, in October of 2023, Mr. Pratt returned to the General Assembly as the
senior legislative advisor for the Senate Republican Caucus Chairman Ken Yager, in which
position he researched key issues, analyzed legislation, and prepared reports for Chairman
Yager and the Caucus; and
WHEREAS, Rhett Pratt has since returned to the dark side, now serving his beloved
Tennessee Municipal League with acumen and alacrity as Senior Director of Government
Relations and Membership Engagement, earning the respect of his colleagues and community
alike; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee Municipal League's gain is, truly, the General Assembly's
loss; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards, Mr. Pratt earned the well-deserved distinction of
being named the 2020 Senate Employee of the Year in recognition of his years of conscientious
and devoted service; and
WHEREAS, his professional accomplishments aside, Mr. Pratt is quick to credit the love
and support of his wife, Jocelyn, as the true measure of his good fortune; and
WHEREAS, a true son of the Senate, his fiscal discipline is perhaps best evidenced by
his measured decision to graduate to a more expensive brand of cigarettes only after securing a
corresponding salary increase; and
- 2 - 018298
WHEREAS, consistent with that same fiscal philosophy, his subsequent decision to quit
smoking is understood to have been informed, in part, by health concerns, though the annual
expenditure is said to have weighed rather more heavily; and
WHEREAS, beyond the briefing books and budget analyses, Rhett is equally well known
for an affable manner, a remarkably steady supply of groan-worthy puns, and a gift for lifting the
mood of any room he walks into; and
WHEREAS, for all that levity, his colleagues have learned to recognize the phrase
"cards on the table" as the unmistakable signal that Mr. Pratt is setting the jokes aside and
getting down to brass tacks; and
WHEREAS, he possesses a near-mythical tolerance for spicy foods, apparently finding
delight in heat levels that would send the rest of us in search of a glass of milk; and
WHEREAS, his devotion to the University of Tennessee Volunteers and the Nashville
Predators is exceeded only, perhaps, by his loyalty to Taco Bell; and
WHEREAS, his attention to detail and coordination extends even to yard work, as he is
known to keep a shirt reserved exclusively for mowing the grass, selected for its precise color
match to his mower; and
WHEREAS, his generosity is perhaps most quietly demonstrated in his consistent and
personal kindness toward those in Nashville who are down on their luck, an instinct he extends
without fanfare or expectation of notice; and
WHEREAS, he possesses a singular and well-documented ability to convert what should
be a fifteen-minute drive into something closer to thirty; and
WHEREAS, through every ebb and flow this profession offers, Rhett has maintained a
genuinely remarkable outlook, one that finds the bright side of nearly any situation and brings
the people around him along for the ride; and
- 3 - 018298
WHEREAS, Rhett is fondly remembered by those around him for his jolly humming, his
enthusiastic renditions of "Purple Rain," his unhurried approach to the posted speed limit, his
gift for routinizing, and his annual stewardship of the office college football pick 'em; and
WHEREAS, regarded by his colleagues as one of the kindest and most decent people
ever to walk the halls of this Capitol, Rhett Pratt has distinguished himself as a public servant of
the highest order and is richly deserving of the respect, admiration, and commendation of this
General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Rhett Pratt for his meritorious service to
the General Assembly and extend to him our heartfelt wishes for every continued success in his
career with the Tennessee Municipal League.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 4 - 018298
