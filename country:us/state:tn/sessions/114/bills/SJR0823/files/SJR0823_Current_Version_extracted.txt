SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 823
By Haile
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Sumner County and
Trousdale County emergency services agencies
and volunteers in Senate District 18 for their
efforts during Winter Storm Fern.
WHEREAS, it is the privilege of this General Assembly to recognize those devoted
Tennesseans who, in the discharge of their duties, commit long hours and often place
themselves in harm's way to ensure the safety and well-being of their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Winter Storm Fern, which began impacting Tennessee on January 24,
2026, brought with it a dangerous mix of snow, sleet, and freezing rain that coated roads, trees,
and power lines in a layer of ice, accompanied by frigid temperatures that lingered for several
days; and
WHEREAS, this historic winter storm caused widespread disruption and danger across
the State of Tennessee, including the closure of schools and offices, impassable roads, and
extensive power outages affecting thousands of residents; and
WHEREAS, in the face of these perilous conditions, line workers and staff at Tri-County
Electric Membership Corporation, Gallatin Department of Electricity, Nashville Electric Service,
and Cumberland Electric Membership Corporation worked around the clock to restore power to
homes and businesses, even as temperatures plummeted into the single digits; and
WHEREAS, emergency services agencies and first responders in Sumner and
Trousdale counties rose to meet the storm's challenges, responding to accidents, checking on
vulnerable residents, and maintaining public safety under extreme conditions; and
WHEREAS, volunteers throughout Senate District 18 likewise stepped forward to assist
their neighbors, providing support, resources, and care during the storm's most dangerous
days; and

WHEREAS, together, these courageous and tireless workers acted with integrity, skill,
and determination, placing the welfare of others above their own comfort and safety; and
WHEREAS, these exemplary professionals and volunteers represent the very best of
public service and community resilience, and they are richly deserving of our gratitude and
recognition; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Sumner County and
Trousdale County emergency services agencies, first responders, line workers, and staff at Tri-
County Electric Membership Corporation, Gallatin Department of Electricity, Nashville Electric
Service, and Cumberland Electric Membership Corporation, as well as volunteers in Senate
District 18, for their efforts during Winter Storm Fern, extending our deepest appreciation to
these dedicated individuals who, in a moment of great peril, came to the aid and rescue of their
fellow citizens.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
