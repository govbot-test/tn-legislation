SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 585
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of James Rufus "JR"
Armstrong of Germantown.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of James Rufus "JR" Armstrong of Germantown; and
WHEREAS, JR Armstrong was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on October 6, 1935, in Memphis, Mr. Armstrong was the son of
Chester Rufus Armstrong and Corryne Wood Armstrong; and
WHEREAS, on January 4, 1953, Mr. Armstrong married his loving wife, Ruth Joy Horne
Armstrong, with whom he enjoyed seventy-three years marriage; and
WHEREAS, JR Armstrong worked for eighteen years at Charles Reed Buick and earned
the title of leading salesman, a role he held for many years; and
WHEREAS, in 1972, Mr. Armstrong moved to Bolivar, where he purchased Bolivar Ford
Motor Company; and
WHEREAS, along with his successful career as a salesman, Mr. Armstrong attended
Jack Knox SS class, was a Jaycee in Memphis, and served as a member of Bolivar Rotary
Club, which honored him as a Paul Harris Fellow; and
WHEREAS, JR Armstrong was deeply devoted to his faith and his family, and he always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, JR Armstrong was an established member of Germantown Baptist Church
and a former member of Bolivar First Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Armstrong is survived by his cherished wife, Ruth Joy Horne Armstrong;
his sons, David Rufus (Jianling) Armstrong and Steven Wayne (Melissa Bolton) Armstrong; his

grandchildren, Michael (Amanda) Armstrong, Benjamin Armstrong, Robyn Mackenzie Mathis,
and Taylor Mathis (Lee) Meek; his great-grandchildren, Marissa Brooke Watson, Wyatt Lee
Meek, and Kate Riley Meek; his nephew, Richard (Laura) Armstrong; and a host of friends and
colleagues; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, JR Armstrong was preceded in death by his
stepmother, Louise Armstrong; his brother, Bobbie Armstrong; his stepbrother, Edwin Johnson;
and his stepsister, Joann Russell; and
WHEREAS, the epitome of excellence, Mr. Armstrong was a faithful soul who impacted
the lives of those who were blessed to know him, and he leaves behind an indelible legacy of
integrity and probity in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and
dedication in all his chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of James Rufus "JR"
Armstrong, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to
living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Armstrong.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
