SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 570
By Massey
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the fifth anniversary of the
passage of the updated Tennessee Silver Alert
law.
WHEREAS, in 2021, the Tennessee Silver Alert law was updated to create greater
collaboration between local enforcement and the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation,
incorporating enhanced cooperation with the media; and
WHEREAS, the updated Silver Alert program has proven to be highly effective, resulting
in over 500 Silver Alerts being issued since 2021; and
WHEREAS, through this collaboration with the General Assembly, administrative
agencies, and community stakeholders, Silver Alert has experienced success in its ongoing
commitment to save lives in the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, approximately 120,000 Tennesseans have a diagnosis of Alzheimer's
disease and related dementias that may cause loss of memory and other cognitive function; and
WHEREAS, as the senior population steadily grows, the aging of the baby-boomer
generation will likely increase the number of Tennesseans with Alzheimer's disease; and
WHEREAS, Silver Alert will continue to result in more Tennesseans being returned
safely to their families after wandering; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commemorate this significant milestone of
achievement and service of the Silver Alert program.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
