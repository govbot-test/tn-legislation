HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1230
By Glynn
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Norman G. "Greg" Lane
for his distinguished military service.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal safety and concerns to ensure our individual and collective freedom, and the Volunteer
State is especially proud to be the home of so many valiant men and women who have
performed above and beyond the call of duty; and
WHEREAS, while the heroic deeds of Tennesseans have been extolled throughout the
history of our State and nation, the distinguished military service of every proud Tennessean
should be illuminated, shared, and properly honored; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Norman G. "Greg" Lane, a gallant veteran of the
United States Army, who served with distinction over the course of a long and honorable military
career; and
WHEREAS, born in Chattanooga in September 1959, Greg Lane matriculated to The
Citadel in August 1977, from which he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in History and
was commissioned in the United States Army as an active-duty infantry officer in May 1981; and
WHEREAS, he served on active duty from 1981 through 1993, with assignments at Fort
Benning, Georgia; Fort Campbell, Kentucky; Camp Page, South Korea; Fort Polk, Louisiana;
and Austin Peay State University Army ROTC; and
WHEREAS, his active duty positions included rifle platoon leader, company executive
officer, company commander on two occasions, garrison S2/S3, battalion and brigade assistant
adjutant, brigade plans officer, and Army ROTC instructor; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Lane continued to serve his nation with distinction in the United States
Army Reserve from August 1993 to September 2005, where his roles included Army ROTC

WHEREAS, during this period of continued service, he furthered his education, earning

WHEREAS, Mr. Lane's commitment to developing future officers is particularly
noteworthy through his long and distinguished service with the United States Army Cadet
Command and the APSU ROTC program, where he served as an assistant professor of military
science, instructor at multiple cadet levels, contractor instructor, and cadre member in roles
including S3 and executive officer; and
WHEREAS, his contributions extended to twelve Army ROTC Advanced Camps
between 2000 and 2013, where he served first as a platoon TAC Officer and later as Camp
Liaison Officer chief, helping shape and evaluate the next generation of Army leaders; and
WHEREAS, since 2014, Greg Lane has served as Cadet Success Coordinator at APSU,
providing critical administrative leadership, coordinating departmental operations, leading
annual staff rides, teaching military history, and serving as the primary liaison between the
university and the Army ROTC department; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure at APSU, Mr. Lane has played a vital role in the
commissioning of 627 cadets into the active Army, Army Reserve, and Army National Guard,
thereby leaving a lasting legacy on the strength and readiness of our nation's armed forces; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his military and instructional service, he has contributed to
academic and leadership development programs, including teaching in APSU's President's
Emerging Leaders Program and serving on the APSU Faculty Senate, while also publishing
numerous professional articles in respected military journals, including SOLDIERS, ARMOR,

ENGINEER, MILITARY INTELLIGENCE, TRANSPORTATION, and the Association of the
United States Army's ARMY magazine; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to accolades, Greg Lane has been recognized with induction
into the United States Army ROTC Hall of Fame in 2019, receipt of the Austin Peay State
University Staff Service Award for the 2018–2019 academic year, and the Ohio Valley
Conference Thurston Banks Award in 2021 for his outstanding contributions to student athletes
who were also Army ROTC cadets; and
WHEREAS, his many professional achievements aside, Mr. Lane is most grateful for the
love and support he shares with his wife, Lucy Lane; and
WHEREAS, throughout his military career, Greg Lane has served the United States with
honor, integrity, and distinction, and it is wholly fitting that he be recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Norman G. "Greg" Lane for his distinguished
military service, express our sincere gratitude for his many years of dedication to the defense of
our nation, and extend to him our best wishes for every future happiness and success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
