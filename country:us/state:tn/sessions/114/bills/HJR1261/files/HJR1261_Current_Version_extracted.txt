HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1261
By Farmer
A RESOLUTION to commend Victoria Marie Grigsby for
exemplary service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Victoria Marie Grigsby is one such individual, who has distinguished herself
in her role as a legislative intern for Representative Andrew Farmer and the House Judiciary
Committee; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Grigsby's work has resulted in innumerable benefits for the legislators
she assisted, including Judiciary Committee Chairman Farmer, Criminal Justice Subcommittee
Chairman Clay Doggett, Children and Family Affairs Subcommittee Chairlady Mary Littleton,
Civil Justice Subcommittee Chairman Lowell Russell, and the General Assembly as a whole;
and
WHEREAS, Ms. Grigsby earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Political Science and
German with a minor in Economics from Middle Tennessee State University (MTSU) in 2025,
where she was an Honors College Buchanan Fellow and a recipient of the 2025 President's
Award; and
WHEREAS, during her collegiate career, Victoria Grigsby served the Tennessee
Intercollegiate State Legislature as a representative, the MTSU Student Government
Association as a senator, and the MTSU American Democracy Project as president; she was
awarded a 2025 Public Policy and International Affairs fellowship to Princeton University, a 2024
Gilman Scholarship from the U.S. Department of State, and a spot in the 2023 U.S./U.K.
Fulbright Summer Institutes program; and

WHEREAS, in her free time, Ms. Grigsby enjoys hiking in Tennessee State parks,
reading history books, and traveling to new places; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies, Victoria Grigsby brought her many
abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Ms. Grigsby has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, she has performed her many duties with extraordinary insight and rare
decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commend Victoria Marie Grigsby for outstanding service as a
legislative intern for Representative Andrew Farmer and the House Judiciary Committee and
extend to her our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
