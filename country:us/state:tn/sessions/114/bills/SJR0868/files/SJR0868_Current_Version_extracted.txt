SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 868
By Seal
A RESOLUTION to congratulate Duncan Oshumu of The King's
Academy on winning the 2025 TSSAA Division
II, Class A Boys' Cross Country Individual
Championship.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to specially honor and
commend those successful high school athletes who, through their magnificent exploits, bring
honor to Tennessee and serve as exceptional ambassadors of this State; and
WHEREAS, one such estimable athlete is Duncan Oshumu of The King's Academy, who
won the 2025 TSSAA Division II, Class A Boys' Individual State Cross Country Championship
held at Shelby Farms Park in Memphis on November 6, 2025; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Oshumu's winning time of 15:48.47 secured the championship and
demonstrated his exceptional endurance, discipline, and competitive spirit; and
WHEREAS, a sophomore at The King's Academy, Duncan Oshumu finished twenty-
seven seconds ahead of the runner-up and improved upon his previous personal best by a full
minute; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Oshumu is only the second individual state champion in school history,
an achievement that stands as a testament to his hard work and determination; and
WHEREAS, such success is due in no small measure to the guidance and
encouragement of his coach, Jeff Ayers; and
WHEREAS, Duncan Oshumu's noteworthy accomplishment is a tremendous source of
pride for his family, his school, and his community; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Oshumu's dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of our respect,
admiration, and commendation; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Duncan Oshumu of The
King's Academy on winning the 2025 TSSAA Division II, Class A Boys' Cross Country Individual
Championship and extend to him our best wishes for much continued success in his future
endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
