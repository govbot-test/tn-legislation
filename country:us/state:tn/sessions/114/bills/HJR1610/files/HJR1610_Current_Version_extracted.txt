HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1610
By Moody
A RESOLUTION to honor Colleen Curtis upon her exemplary
service to Representative Debra Moody and the
Tennessee General Assembly.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those valuable legislative employees
who have performed with unwavering devotion and enthusiasm throughout their tenure with the
General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, one such esteemed individual is Colleen Curtis, the Legislative Assistant to
Representative Debra Moody, current Chair of the House Calendar and Rules Committee and
the former chair of the House Education Committee, who has rendered exemplary service to the
Tennessee General Assembly for more than a decade; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Curtis began her estimable career with the Tennessee legislature in
2013 and has since demonstrated unwavering commitment, dedication, and diligence to the
citizens of Tipton County and House District 81; and
WHEREAS, during her distinguished tenure, Ms. Curtis played a key role in the mentor
program and leveraged her background in media, video, and photography to cultivate valuable
relationships, inviting guests such as Mike Nawrocki of VeggieTales, Felix Cavaliere of The
Rascals, and Rick Vito of Fleetwood Mac to the chambers; and
WHEREAS, a highly respected member of both her community and profession, Colleen
Curtis has taken great pride in attending Human Trafficking Advisory Council meetings with the
Tennessee Bureau of Investigation; she has also served as board president of the Department
of Safety Citizens Academy Alumni, president of the Homeowners Association of Spring Place,
and as a board member and producer for CATV Public TV; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Curtis earned a degree in commercial photography from Nossi College
of Art in 2017 and graduated from the Citizens Academy programs for the Department of Safety,
Metro Nashville Police, and the Department of Correction; and
WHEREAS, additionally, Colleen Curtis served the Tennessee State Guard with
distinction for two years as a Chief Warrant Officer, exemplifying exceptional leadership and
commitment to service; and
WHEREAS, her many professional accomplishments aside, Ms. Curtis is most proud of
her role as a mother to her son, Sean; and
WHEREAS, Colleen Curtis has demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and
integrity, winning the unbridled respect and admiration of her colleagues, the citizens of District
81, and the members of the General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Colleen Curtis for her exemplary service to
Representative Debra Moody and the Tennessee General Assembly and extend to her our best
wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
