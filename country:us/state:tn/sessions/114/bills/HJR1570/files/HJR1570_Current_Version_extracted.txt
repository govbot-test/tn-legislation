HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1570
By Cochran
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Etowah Boy Scout Troop
74 on the occasion of its 100th anniversary.
WHEREAS, the Etowah Boy Scout Troop 74 charter was signed and made official on
August 21, 1926, by Daniel Beard, James Weston, and Calvin Coolidge, with Scout Master
Lloyd Campbell starting this adventure; and
WHEREAS, Boone Powell was the first Eagle Scout from Etowah Boy Scout Troop 74,
which has since averaged about 1.5 Eagle Scouts per year for the past 100 years, as many
Eagle Scout projects have been developed and added to local communities, churches, and civic
groups; and
WHEREAS, Etowah Boy Scout Troop 74, with the help of the City of Etowah, McMinn
County, and others, has taken part in approximately twenty-five high adventure Scouting events
and countless camping, hiking, fishing, canoeing, and Scout Jamboree events, with no Scout
being unable to participate due to finances; and
WHEREAS, over the past 100 years, Etowah Boy Scout Troop 74 has instilled the
values of the Scout Oath and Scout Law—trustworthy, loyal, helpful, friendly, courteous, kind,
obedient, cheerful, thrifty, brave, clean, and reverent—into its members, shaping generations of
responsible citizens and community leaders; and
WHEREAS, the members of this Boy Scout Troop 74 have demonstrated their
commitment to service through countless community projects, aiding area parks, serving those
in need, and upholding the spirit of "cheerful service" to others; and
WHEREAS, this centennial milestone marks a remarkable legacy of dedication by
volunteer leaders, chartered organizations, parents, and Scouts who have built a strong
foundation for the future of Scouting in our community; and

WHEREAS, over the years, Boy Scout Troop 74 has been led by Lloyd Campbell, Honh
Owenbey, John Leinert, Fred Leon, Fred McGee, Spencer Boyd McClary II, Gene Witt, Charles
Thompson, LeRoy Hershey, Randy Colson, Kim Gossett, Dennis Underdown, Roy Johnson,
and Mike Gironda; and
WHEREAS, Etowah Boy Scout Troop 74 has had the privilege to routinely meet at the
Scout Lodge at the corner of 6th Street and Pennsylvania Avenue, which is listed on the National
Register of Historic Places; and
WHEREAS, Etowah Boy Scout Troop 74 has successfully served the youth of the
community and surrounding areas for 100 years with the support of the City of Etowah, McMinn
County, and the State of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Etowah Boy Scout Troop 74 on the occasion of its
100th anniversary, extending our best wishes for a successful community celebration on
Saturday, July 25, 2026, on the Lawn at the Historic L&N Depot in Etowah, and joining with
them as they look forward to the next 100 years of Scouting in the community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
