HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 968
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Troy Wayne Poteete.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Troy Wayne Poteete, whose life of public service and cultural stewardship left an
indelible mark upon the Cherokee Nation; and
WHEREAS, born in 1955, Mr. Poteete dedicated his life to the service of his people and
the preservation of Cherokee history and heritage; and
WHEREAS, Troy Poteete served with distinction as a member of the Cherokee Nation
Tribal Council from 1991 to 1999, helping to shape legislative policy during a pivotal era of
growth for the tribe; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Poteete's steadfast dedication to the Cherokee Nation continued when
he was appointed as a Justice of the Cherokee Nation Supreme Court in 2007, and he further
served as Director of the Arkansas Riverbed Authority; and
WHEREAS, widely recognized for wearing his distinctive traditional turbans in homage
to his ancestors, Troy Poteete honored the enduring traditions and cultural identity of the
Cherokee people; and
WHEREAS, as a founding member and Executive Director of the National Trail of Tears
Association, he worked tirelessly to enhance public knowledge of the Trail of Tears, ensuring
that this chapter of history would never be forgotten and helping to preserve historic sites and
deepen understanding of its lasting impact; and
WHEREAS, Cherokee Nation Principal Chief Chuck Hoskin, Jr., aptly observed, "Troy
Wayne's passing represents a significant loss of institutional knowledge and cultural passion.
Today we take pause and honor his legacy of leadership and life's work."; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
HJR0968
013268
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Troy Wayne Poteete, reflecting with gratitude
upon his life of service and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Poteete.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
