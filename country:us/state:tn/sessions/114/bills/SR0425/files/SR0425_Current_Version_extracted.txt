SENATE RESOLUTION 425
By Briggs
A RESOLUTION to commend John "Jack" Bulmer for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this body is pleased to recognize certain persons
who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the legislative
process; and
WHEREAS, John "Jack" Bulmer is one such individual who has distinguished himself in
his role as a legislative intern for Senator Richard Briggs, M.D.; Jack's work has resulted in
innumerable benefits for Senator Briggs and his staff, the Senate State and Local Government
Committee, and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, where he is highly
regarded by his peers, Jack Bulmer has achieved academic success while pursuing a degree in
Business Administration with a major in Supply Chain Management and a minor in Public Policy
Analysis; this stellar scholar is a perennial fixture on the Dean's List and expects to graduate in
May 2027; and
WHEREAS, he has been active in campus life with the University's divisions of Student
Life and Student Success, the Student Government Association, and Lambda Chi Alpha
Fraternity; and
WHEREAS, Jack Bulmer has honed his political skills as a member of the
Undergraduate Student Senate, also serving that body as Chairman of the Student
Advancement Committee and a member of the Government Affairs Committee; and
WHEREAS, a member of the Epsilon Omicron Chapter of Lambda Chi Alpha, Jack
Bulmer has served his fraternity as Chairman of its Judicial (Standards of Fraternal Excellence)
Committee; and
SR0425
017405
- 1 -
WHEREAS, hailing from a Virginia family of five people and two dogs, Jack has fallen in
love with Tennessee during his tenure in Knoxville; he has become a huge Vols fan, supporting
them on the road and across the region, and has not missed a single home football game during
his three years as a student; and
WHEREAS, a former baseball player himself, Jack Bulmer is an especially ardent
supporter of the Vols baseball team, and he was very excited when the team won its first
College World Series Championship on his birthday; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize himself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further his studies, Jack Bulmer brought his many abilities
and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth
General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Jack Bulmer has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious legislative intern; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bulmer is grateful for being allowed to dive further into policymaking
during his internship, and he is thankful to Dr. Briggs, Rochelle Frazier, and Meredith Lifson for
their teaching him the ropes this session; and
WHEREAS, he is also appreciative of the post-graduate employment possibilities that
have been revealed to him during his internship and feels better prepared for future
opportunities; and
WHEREAS, Jack distinguished himself as a gifted cook and gracious host, who fed his
colleagues in Dr. Briggs's office on a regular basis, a practice that contributed to the infamous
"freshman 15" that is taboo at the General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, Jack Bulmer has performed his many duties with extraordinary insight and
rare decorum; now, therefore,
- 2 - 017405
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we commend John "Jack"
Bulmer for exemplary service as a legislative intern for Senator Richard Briggs, M.D., and
extend to him our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
- 3 - 017405
