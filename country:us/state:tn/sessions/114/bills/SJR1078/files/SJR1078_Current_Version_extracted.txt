SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1078
By Kyle
A RESOLUTION to commend Mariana Perez Pita for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this General Assembly is pleased to recognize
certain persons who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment
of the legislative process; and
WHEREAS, Mariana Perez Pita is one such individual, who has distinguished herself in
her role as a legislative intern for Senator Sara Kyle; her work has resulted in innumerable
benefits for the legislators she assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at the University of Memphis, where she is highly regarded by her
peers, Mariana Perez Pita has achieved academic success while pursuing a Bachelor of Arts
degree in Philosophy with a minor in Legal Thought and Liberal Arts; and
WHEREAS, during her collegiate career, Ms. Pita has served the Undergraduate
Philosophy Circle as a tutor, in which role she actively supports her peers in rigorous
philosophical inquiry; and
WHEREAS, a native of Memphis, Mariana Perez Pita is driven by a deep commitment to
her community; she aspires to practice family law, combining her passion for justice, human
dignity, and advocacy for Tennessee's most vulnerable families; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies, Mariana Perez Pita brought her many
abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Ms. Pita has surpassed all criteria
that define a devoted and conscientious intern; and

WHEREAS, Mariana Perez Pita has performed her many duties with extraordinary

WHEREAS, Ms. Pita epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of a

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH

REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Mariana Perez Pita for her

success.

the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
