HOUSE RESOLUTION 169
By Love
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Dr. Carolyn Baldwin
Tucker for her contributions to the State of
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this legislative body should salute those
citizens who, through their extraordinary efforts, have distinguished themselves as community
leaders of whom we can all be proud; and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Dr. Carolyn Baldwin Tucker, a highly
respected member of her community who has dedicated herself to improving the quality of life
for her fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Carolyn Baldwin Tucker is the youngest of nine children born to George
and Susie Baldwin in Chattanooga; and
WHEREAS, after the passing of her father when she was five years old and her mother
on her eleventh birthday, Dr. Tucker was reared by one of her older sisters, Mrs. Jewell Baldwin
Cousin; she graduated from Howard High School in 1965 at the age of seventeen; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Tucker enrolled at Tennessee A&I State University in the fall of 1965,
pledged to the Alpha Chi Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, in 1966, and
earned her Bachelor of Science degree in Elementary Education from Tennessee State
University (TSU) in 1969; and
WHEREAS, while in college, Dr. Carolyn Tucker met her wonderful, loving, and devoted
husband, Elder Jesse F. Tucker, and the two were married in the fall of 1969; to that union, two
children were born, Attorney Susan Tucker Jones and Evangelist Randall F. Tucker; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Tucker, a lifelong learner, continued her education, earning her master's
degree from TSU in 1971, her Education Specialist Degree from the University of Tennessee,
HR0169
014744

Knoxville, in 1977, and her Ph.D. from George Peabody College of Vanderbilt University in
1982; and
WHEREAS, for thirty years, Dr. Carolyn Tucker was employed by Metro Nashville Public
Schools, where she served five years as a classroom teacher, twenty-two years as a principal,
and three years as an elementary schools director; she also served as interim director of
schools for Murfreesboro City Schools during the 2009-2010 school year; and
WHEREAS, for sixteen years, Dr. Tucker served as a visiting professor at Lipscomb
University and as Lipscomb's Director of Graduate Studies in Education for two years; even in
retirement, she continued to serve our great State by accepting a role as an exemplary educator
for the Tennessee Department of Education; and
WHEREAS, as a continuous influence for women, her family, and her community, by the
grace of God, Dr. Carolyn Tucker was elected to the Metro Council in 1999, becoming the first
African-American woman to win a county-wide race in the history of Nashville and Davidson
County and the first African-American woman to be elected as an At-Large member of the Metro
Council; as a result of this accomplishment, she has been recognized by the City of Nashville
with a street named in her honor; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Tucker, as an educator concerned about the issues affecting families
within the State of Tennessee, served as an expert consultant for the late Senator Avon N.
Williams to sponsor legislation to provide guidance counselors in elementary schools across the
state, the passage and impact of which continues to help students traverse and cope with life,
family, and systemic challenges; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Carolyn Tucker and her husband, who served the State of Tennessee
as a counselor in the Department of Correction and later as an administrator within probation
and parole, observed a need for educating the community regarding violence prevention, and in
response, they offered and presented workshops on "Prevention Now or Correction Later"; and
- 2 - 014744
WHEREAS, an active mother and educator committed to investing in the development of
children, Dr. Tucker was asked by two members of the Tennessee Black Caucus of State
Legislators to design a program for the youth of Tennessee, which would be an active
component of the Annual Legislative Retreat of the Tennessee Black Caucus of State
Legislators; she created the Youth Task Force, which she chaired for fifteen years, providing
opportunities for youth across the state to engage with legislators and decision makers through
panel discussions, essay contests, oratorical contests, and the Youth Luncheon; and
WHEREAS, being aware of the impact of youth violence on our communities, Carolyn
Tucker worked with the Tennessee Black Caucus to conduct the "Stop the Violence Now"
conferences for Tennessee youth; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Tucker is a woman of many talents; she has authored and sung country
music songs and written two inspirational books for the Christian woman; she continues in
service to the Lord, her family, and her community as a Ladies Bible Class facilitator and a
Sunday School teacher at the Church of Christ at Jackson Street; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Carolyn Tucker epitomizes the spirit and commitment to family and
community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this legislative body acknowledge and
applaud Dr. Tucker for her dedication to the State of Tennessee and its citizens; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and
commend Dr. Carolyn Baldwin Tucker for her lifetime of service to her community and the State
of Tennessee and extend to her our best wishes for much continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
