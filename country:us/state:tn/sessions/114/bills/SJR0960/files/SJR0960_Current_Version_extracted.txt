SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 960
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Wyatt Poole and Joseph
Tosh upon winning first place in the Problem
Solving Division at the Tennessee Jobs for
America's Graduates State Career Development
Conference.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those outstanding young Tennesseans who, through their achievements and
dedication, bring honor to their schools, communities, and this State; and
WHEREAS, Wyatt Poole and Joseph Tosh are two such exceptional students, who
recently distinguished themselves through their academic skill and leadership by earning first
place in the Problem Solving Division competition at the Tennessee Jobs for America's
Graduates (JAGTN) State Career Development Conference, held in Murfreesboro on February
25-27, 2026; and
WHEREAS, seniors at Haywood High School, Wyatt Poole and Joseph Tosh previously
secured second place at the West Tennessee Regional Competition, thereby advancing to the
state-level contest; and
WHEREAS, during the competition, participants were presented with a prompt and given
thirty minutes to prepare and deliver a presentation evaluated across multiple criteria, including
content, engagement, confidence, body language, transitions, time management, audience
awareness, originality, overall impression, and professional attire, and the Haywood High
School competitors achieved a minimum nine out of ten points in every category; and
WHEREAS, founded in 1980, Jobs for America's Graduates (JAG) partners with
schools, employers, and communities nationwide to help students overcome barriers to success
by providing a comprehensive curriculum focused on career readiness, leadership development,
and personal growth; and

WHEREAS, the JAG chapter at Haywood High School was reinstated in 2023 under the
leadership of Principal Michelle Tillman and JAG Specialist Eliza Ford, whose guidance and
commitment have helped foster new opportunities for students at the school; and
WHEREAS, Wyatt Poole (Sarah and Josh Poole) and Joseph Tosh (Denise and Scott
Hooser) have brought great pride to their families, their school, and their community through
their impressive accomplishment; and
WHEREAS, by virtue of their First Place finish, these remarkable students have earned
the opportunity to represent Tennessee at the Jobs for America's Graduates National Career
Development Conference in Salt Lake City, Utah, from April 29-May 2, 2026, marking the first
time that Haywood High School's JAG team will compete at the national level; and
WHEREAS, the talent, determination, and leadership demonstrated by Wyatt Poole and
Joseph Tosh are a source of great pride to their families, their school, and their community; and
WHEREAS, this General Assembly finds it appropriate to pause in its deliberations to
congratulate Wyatt Poole and Joseph Tosh on their outstanding accomplishment; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Wyatt Poole and Joseph
Tosh upon winning first place in the Problem Solving Division at the Tennessee Jobs for
America's Graduates State Career Development Conference and extend to them our best
wishes for much continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
