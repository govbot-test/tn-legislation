HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1110
By Capley
A RESOLUTION to honor NASCAR legend Sterling Marlin.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing a
native Tennessean who has distinguished himself as one of the greatest race car drivers of all-
time, namely Sterling Burton Marlin from Maury County; and
WHEREAS, Sterling Marlin is the son of another stock-car racing legend, the late Coo
Coo Marlin, and he proudly followed in his father's footsteps to make his own mark on the track;
and
WHEREAS, during his thirty-three-year and 748-race career (1976-2010), Sterling Marlin
won numerous Cup races and became a legend in his own right; and
WHEREAS, Sterling Marlin's consecutive victories in the Daytona 500 in 1994 and 1995
are his most memorable accomplishments in NASCAR; his back-to-back Daytona 500 wins
placed him in the elite company of Richard Petty and Cale Yarbrough as only the third driver to
achieve this incredible feat; and
WHEREAS, making his NASCAR debut in 1983, he was named Rookie of the Year in
recognition of his great potential; and
WHEREAS, Sterling Marlin won two races each at the renowned Darlington, Talladega,
Charlotte, and Michigan speedways during his career; and
WHEREAS, he also won the UAW-DaimlerChrysler 400 at Las Vegas Motor Speedway
two times and 1996's Firecracker 400 for his third victory at Daytona International Speedway;
and
WHEREAS, after making his debut at Nashville Speedway USA (now Nashville
Fairgrounds Speedway) in 1976, Sterling Marlin was a three-time track champion (1980-1982)
at this historic racecourse; and

WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Sterling Marlin was named
Tennessee Professional Athlete of the Year in 1995 and 1996 and was a nominee for the award
again in 2002; and
WHEREAS, after his retirement from racing, he was deservedly recognized as one of
NASCAR's 75 Greatest Drivers in 2023; he has also been inducted into the Nashville
Fairgrounds Speedway Hall of Fame, Tennessee Sports Hall of Fame, and Daytona Stock Car
Racing Hall of Fame; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Marlin has frequently appeared in national media both during his career
and since his retirement, even once competing against another NASCAR driver and his team on
Family Feud; his most recent appearance was on Dale Earnhardt, Jr.'s podcast; and
WHEREAS, a graduate of Spring Hill High School, Sterling Marlin excelled in basketball
and football there and served as captain of the football team during his senior year while playing
quarterback and linebacker; he accomplished all this while also regularly working on his father's
race car; and
WHEREAS, Sterling Marlin has been and continues to be an outstanding ambassador
for Maury County and the State of Tennessee, and he is most worthy of our approbation; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor NASCAR legend Sterling Marlin, salute his illustrious career in
racing, and extend to him our best wishes for much continued success and happiness.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
