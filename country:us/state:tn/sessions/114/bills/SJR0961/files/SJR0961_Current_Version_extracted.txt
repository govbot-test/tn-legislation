SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 961
By Bailey
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Jeffrey Edward Guth of
Sparta.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Jeffrey Edward Guth of Sparta; and
WHEREAS, Jeff Guth was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on August 28, 1961, in Memphis, Mr. Guth was the son of Dr. James
Edward and Yvonne Clair Dickson Guth; and
WHEREAS, Jeff Guth earned his bachelor's degree from Tennessee Technological
University and began a career in law enforcement in 1986 with the Franklin Police Department;
and
WHEREAS, in 1992, he returned to Sparta to join the Sparta Police Department, where
he ascended to the post of chief of police, serving in such capacity until his retirement in 2019;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Guth evinced his religious faith as an active member of the Sparta First
United Methodist Church; and
WHEREAS, a staunch baseball fan, Mr. Guth proudly served as a Little League coach
for many years; and
WHEREAS, Jeff Guth was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored to
remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his wife of thirty-eight years, Judy Guth; son, James
(Kelcey) Guth; daughter, Stephanie Guth (Stephen) Eubanks; grandchildren, Grant Guth and

Everly Guth; brother, Gerald (Ramona) Guth; niece, Ashlee (Jeff) Nebel; and nephew, Kyle
Guth; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Guth was preceded in death by his parents; and
WHEREAS, Jeff Guth leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public
life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Jeffrey Edward Guth,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Guth.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
