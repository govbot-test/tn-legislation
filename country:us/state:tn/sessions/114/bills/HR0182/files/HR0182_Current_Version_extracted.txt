HOUSE RESOLUTION 182
By Lamberth
A RESOLUTION to commend Priscilla Hanna for exemplary
service as a legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this body is pleased to recognize certain persons
who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the legislative
process; and
WHEREAS, Priscilla Hanna is one such individual, who has distinguished herself in her
role as a legislative intern for Representative William Lamberth; her work has resulted in
innumerable benefits for the legislators she assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at Belmont University, where she is highly regarded by her peers,
Ms. Hanna has achieved academic success while pursuing a Bachelor of Arts degree in Political
Science with a minor in Philosophy; and
WHEREAS, she earned an Associate of Science degree from Volunteer State
Community College in 2025 and graduated magna cum laude; during that time, she served as a
President's Ambassador, representing her school at trustee events, and as a member of the
TRIO Program, participating in leadership and academic development as a first-generation
college student; and
WHEREAS, prior to her time with the General Assembly, Ms. Hanna was a legal intern
for Youssef Law, in which role she communicated with clients; translated contracts, documents,
and client communications; reviewed cases to support attorneys; and conducted legal research;
and
WHEREAS, a leader in her community, Priscilla Hanna has devoted her time and talents
to St. Paul the Hermit Coptic Orthodox Church, where she teaches first-grade Sunday School

classes, plans youth activities, supervises high school students during community service and
trips, and assists with event organization; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies, Priscilla Hanna brought her many
abilities and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred
Fourteenth General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, throughout the 2026 legislative session, Ms. Hanna has surpassed all
criteria that define a devoted and conscientious intern; and
WHEREAS, Priscilla Hanna has performed her many duties with extraordinary insight
and rare decorum; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Hanna epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of a
true Tennessean, and she should be specially recognized and celebrated for her remarkable
accomplishments; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we recognize
and honor Priscilla Hanna for her outstanding service as a legislative intern and extend to her
our best wishes for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
