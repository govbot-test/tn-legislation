HOUSE RESOLUTION 181
By Hill
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Evelyn McQueen Cook
of Mountain City.
WHEREAS, the members of this legislative body were greatly saddened to learn of the
passing of Evelyn McQueen Cook of Mountain City; and
WHEREAS, Evelyn Cook was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, a native of Shady Valley, Mrs. Cook was the beloved daughter of Marcus
McQueen and Myrtle Gentry McQueen and a seventh-generation member of the McQueen
family in Johnson County; and
WHEREAS, Evelyn Cook earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in English from Berea
College and a Master of Science degree in Administration and Supervision from the University
of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, in her professional career, Mrs. Cook taught English at Johnson County
High School for five years; she moved overseas with her husband, who was a superintendent,
and taught English for five years at George C. Marshall High School in Ankara, Turkey; and
WHEREAS, Evelyn Cook worked as part of a team with the director of the North Central
Accrediting Association for Secondary Schools and Colleges to establish a program that would
allow military service personnel to attain a high school diploma instead of a G.E.D.; the program
became known as the Pre-discharge Education Program, and Mrs. Cook first served as
principal and then as deputy director of the European program; and
WHEREAS, a leader in her community, Mrs. Cook was a member and vice president of
the Bristol chapter of Delta Kappa Gamma; charter member of the Mountain City chapter of

Gamma Mu; member and three-time president of the Wednesday Music Club, a local chapter of
the Tennessee Federation of Music Clubs; secretary of the Tennessee Federation of Music
Clubs, which she served for one term; member of the Democratic Women's Committee of
Johnson County; member of the Heritage Hall Board of Directors; chair of the Johnson County
branch of the 1986 Tennessee Homecoming; and co-chair of Johnson County's Tennessee 200
committee; and
WHEREAS, she was a strong advocate for the arts and took a particular interest in
offering opportunities to young people to perform or enjoy drama and music; she was an avid
supporter of the Barter Theatre as a member of the Porterfield Society and the Barter Board of
Trustees and an integral part of the Johnson County Center for the Arts, Long Journey Home,
and the Junior Appalachian Musicians Program; and
WHEREAS, Evelyn Cook was also deeply devoted to her family, and she always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Cook will be sadly missed but fondly remembered by her daughter,
Stephanie L. Cook (Dr. Masaru Okabe); her grandson, Kye L. Okabe; and a host of relatives
and friends; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Cook lived a life of service and was a member of First United
Methodist Church, where she served in several capacities, including as a Lay Leader, Sunday
School teacher, and chair of the Pastor Parish Committee; and
WHEREAS, Evelyn Cook leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pause to remember the bountiful life of this
exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor the
memory of Evelyn McQueen Cook, reflecting fondly upon her stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mrs. Cook.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
