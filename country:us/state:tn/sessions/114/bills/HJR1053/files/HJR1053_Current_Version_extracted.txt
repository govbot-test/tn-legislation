HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1053
By Sparks
A RESOLUTION to honor those individuals and organizations that
are working to address adolescent mental
health, bullying, and the harmful effects of social
media on Tennessee's young people.
WHEREAS, Tennessee faces an urgent mental health crisis, with rising rates of anxiety,
depression, self-harm, and suicide, particularly among adolescent girls; and
WHEREAS, behind every statistic is a real story of pain, including the tragic loss of
Rivers "Riv" Kelleher on September 12, 2025, just days before her fifteenth birthday, which
inspired her family and community to establish the "We Live for Riv" ministry and movement to
support youth facing depression, anxiety, bullying, and other challenges; and
WHEREAS, alongside other mental health advocates, many of whom are members of
this General Assembly, the "We Live for Riv" movement has raised local and national
awareness of youth suicide, bullying, social isolation, and the harmful impacts of social media;
and
WHEREAS, research, including information found in Jonathan Haidt's The Anxious
Generation, shows dramatic increases in adolescent mental health issues since the early
2010s, linked to widespread smartphone and social media use, engagement-driven algorithms,
cyberbullying, body image pressures, reduced face-to-face interaction, and diminished
unstructured play; and
WHEREAS, adolescent girls are disproportionately affected, and factors such as
loneliness, overprotective environments, and excessive screen time contribute to
unprecedented psychological distress that cannot be dismissed as mere "growing pains"; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee has emerged as a leader in addressing Adverse Childhood
Experiences (ACEs) and promoting Positive Childhood Experiences (PCEs)—such as stable

WHEREAS, working in the area of suicide prevention are the Jason Foundation, the

(TSPN), which in coordination with the Tennessee Department of Mental Health and Substance
Abuse Services (TDMHSAS), advances evidence-informed prevention and coordination, while

gardening/horticultural therapy, animal-assisted therapy, gratitude practices, and clinical

Rivers "Riv" Kelleher and the We Live for Riv movement, the Jason Foundation, the American
Foundation for Suicide Prevention, and the Tennessee Suicide Prevention Network, that are
working to address adolescent mental health, bullying, and the harmful effects of social media
on Tennessee's young people.
