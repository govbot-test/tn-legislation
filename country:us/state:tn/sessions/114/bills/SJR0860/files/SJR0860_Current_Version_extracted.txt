SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 860
By Crowe
A RESOLUTION to urge Congress to rename Dogwood Lane on
the federal property of Mountain Home VA the
"Congressman Phil Roe Boulevard."
WHEREAS, Congressman Phil Roe was first elected to represent the 1st Congressional
District of Tennessee in 2008; and
WHEREAS, Congressman Roe served in Congress from January 3, 2009, to January 3,
2021; and
WHEREAS, he served as Chair of the House Committee on Veteran Affairs of the 115th
Congress; and
WHEREAS, he served as Ranking Member of the House Committee on Veteran Affairs
of the 116th Congress; and
WHEREAS, Congressman Phil Roe regularly visited United States Department of
Veteran Affairs (VA) facilities across this nation; and
WHEREAS, a veteran himself, Congressman Roe served as an officer in Korea, seeing
duty as a physician with the 2nd Infantry Division, Medical Battalion; and
WHEREAS, during his six terms in Congress, he sponsored and worked to pass some of
the most transformational pieces of legislation to improve the lives of our veterans ever to have
been signed into law; and
WHEREAS, the Mission Act, the Bluewater Navy Vietnam Veterans Act, the Forever GI
Bill, the VA Accountability and Whistleblower Protection Act, and the Veterans Appeals
Improvement and Modernization Act were products of true bipartisanship and passed under
Congressman Roe's leadership; and
SJR0860

- 1 -
WHEREAS, Congressman Phil Roe has received the American Legion's Distinguished
Service Medal, and he has shown his passion for serving veterans and military families through
his lifelong commitment to their needs; and
WHEREAS, the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs requires authorization from
Congress to name roads or buildings in honor of individuals; and
WHEREAS, because of Congressman Roe's invaluable contributions to not only our
nation's veterans but to the mission of the Department of Veterans Affairs and its VA facilities
across the nation, we find it most appropriate to pursue Congressional action to specially honor
Congressman Phil Roe; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we hereby urge and encourage the members of the
Tennessee Congressional delegation to introduce and work for the passage of legislation to
redesignate Dogwood Lane at Mountain Home VA the "Congressman Phil Roe Boulevard" in
honor of Congressman Roe's superlative leadership and vision during his tenure as Chair and
Ranking Member of the House Committee on Veteran Affairs and his meritorious service to his
constituents of Northeast Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an enrolled copy of this resolution be transmitted to
each member of the Tennessee Congressional delegation.
