HOUSE RESOLUTION 142
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Bill R. Dunlap of Scott
County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Bill R. Dunlap of Scott County; and
WHEREAS, a compassionate man of faith, Bill Dunlap was a highly respected
community leader who poured the best of himself into the lives of everyone around him; he will
be specially remembered for his strong work ethic and quiet generosity; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Dunlap had a steady, unmistakable strength about him, and he carried
himself with a level of class that left an impression on all who knew him; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Dunlap was the founder and owner of Plateau Drugs for more than fifty-
three years; he also was a longtime member of the Scott County Chamber of Commerce and
the Industrial Board; and
WHEREAS, Bill Dunlap found great purpose in going to work each day and serving his
community with care, integrity, and compassion; and
WHEREAS, his efforts positively impacted the lives of countless people in Scott County
and the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Dunlap's favorite pastimes were playing golf and supporting the
Tennessee Volunteers as a longtime season ticket holder; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Dunlap was also deeply devoted to his family, and he took great pride in
caring for them, always remaining true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his loving wife, Susan
Dunlap; sons, Brent (Christy) Dunlap and Blake (Sarah) Dunlap; the mother of his children,
Wanda Dunlap; his stepchildren, Zackwell Huff and Edrie (Travis) Owens; his grandchildren,

Madison (Evan) Roberts, Macy (Danner) Keeton, Myia Dunlap, Mylee Dunlap, Hayden Dunlap,
Kamden Dunlap, Manning Dunlap, Greysen Dunlap, Kalani Owens, Witten Owens, and Nora
Huff; and his great-grandson, Ezra Roberts; and
WHEREAS, he is further survived by his sister-in-law, Pamela Stephens Reed; his
mother-in-law, Donna Shipman; Bonita Shipman; Kanissa Martin; and a host of nieces,
nephews, and cousins; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Dunlap was preceded in death by his mother, Louise Dunlap Reed;
father, Billy James "Jim" Dunlap; stepfather, Boyd "Buzz" Reed; and brother, David Reed; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Dunlap leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor the
memory of Bill R. Dunlap, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart
commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Dunlap.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
