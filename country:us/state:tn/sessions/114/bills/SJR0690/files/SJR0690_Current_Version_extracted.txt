SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 690
By Crowe
A RESOLUTION to honor Dr. Randy Wykoff, Dean of the College
of Public Health at East Tennessee State
University, on the occasion of his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should honor those citizens who have performed
with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their years of industry; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Randy Wykoff is one such estimable person, who has served with
distinction as the founding dean of the College of Public Health at East Tennessee State
University (ETSU); and
WHEREAS, in addition to serving as the founding dean of ETSU's College of Public
Heath, Dr. Wykoff also serves as the co-director of the Center for Rural Health Research at
ETSU; and
WHEREAS, after a distinguished career, Dr. Randy Wykoff recently announced that he
will be taking a much-deserved retirement after the semester ends in May 2026; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Randy Wykoff is a board-certified physician in both pediatrics and
preventive medicine, with additional certification in tropical medicine; he holds four degrees from
Tulane University, including the doctor of medicine, master of public health in tropical medicine,
bachelor of science, and bachelor of arts. He completed a residency in pediatrics at the
University of Virginia and a residency in preventive medicine through the department of tropical
medicine at Tulane; and
WHEREAS, prior to entering academia, Dr. Wykoff served as senior vice president for
International Operations at Project HOPE and enjoyed an illustrious tenure with the federal
government as deputy assistant secretary for health at the U.S. Department of Health and
Human Services; and

WHEREAS, while working for the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, he
was awarded the Surgeon General's Medallion, the highest civilian honor in the U.S. Public
Health Service. He also held positions with the Food and Drug Administration as associate
commissioner for operations and associate commissioner for AIDS and Special Health Issues;
and
WHEREAS, prior to joining the federal government, Dr. Wykoff was responsible for
overseeing all public health activities in a rural six-county area of South Carolina; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Wykoff's research has been published in numerous journals, including
the Journal of the American Medical Association, The New England Journal of Medicine,
Southern Medical Journal, and the Journal of the International Association of Physicians in
AIDS Care; and
WHEREAS, a 2022 inductee in the Tennessee Health Care Hall of Fame, Dr. Randy
Wykoff also co-edited the award-winning book Appalachian Health: Culture, Challenges, and
Capacity, which explores the health issues and challenges facing the people of Appalachia; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Randy Wykoff exemplifies the spirit and allegiance to family and
community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, throughout his estimable career, Dr. Randy Wykoff has demonstrated the
utmost professionalism, ability, and integrity, winning the unbridled respect and admiration of his
colleagues, his students, and the good people of the State of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we extend to Dr. Randy Wykoff our heartfelt wishes
for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
