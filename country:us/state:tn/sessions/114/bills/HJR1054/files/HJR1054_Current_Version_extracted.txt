HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1054
By Clemmons
A RESOLUTION to commend Representative Johnny Shaw for
his honorable and distinguished service to the
people of Tennessee as a member of the House
of Representatives.
WHEREAS, it is most appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should
honor those fellow legislators who have performed their duties with the utmost integrity and
whose efforts during their time in office have been dedicated to providing thoughtful and
exemplary service to their districts and to this great State; and
WHEREAS, Representative Johnny Shaw of Bolivar is one such distinguished public
servant, who has served both his constituency and this legislative body with honor and ability;
and
WHEREAS, during his tenure on Capitol Hill, Representative Shaw has been a highly
influential figure in the political, social, and civic life of Hardeman, Haywood, and Madison
counties, and he has served his constituents in numerous capacities, always working diligently
at his elected duties and expending the necessary effort to understand the opposing sides of
complex issues; and
WHEREAS, Johnny Shaw has distinguished himself as a public-spirited citizen of the
highest order and as an exceptional asset to the good people of the 80th House District, whom
he has represented as a member of the 102nd, 103rd, 104th, 105th, 106th, 107th, 108th, 109th, 110th,
111th, 112th, 113th, and 114th General Assemblies; and
WHEREAS, throughout his service, Representative Shaw has been prominent as a
dedicated and well-informed legislator who always votes his conscience; and
WHEREAS, an active and dynamic participant in the legislative process during his time
in the General Assembly, Representative Shaw has rendered sterling leadership as chair of the

WHEREAS, beyond his legislative duties, Representative Shaw has remained deeply
engaged in civic and charitable life, serving as a founding member of the Shaw Singers; a
member of the Rotary Club of Bolivar, 100 Black Men, the Rural West Tennessee Caucus, the
Gathering of Men missionary organization, the Code Red Ministers Coalition of West
Tennessee, the Hardeman County Chamber of Commerce, and the Tennessee Association of
Broadcasters; a lifetime member of the NAACP; an Alzheimer's Tennessee advocate; and a
Commission on Aging advocate; and
WHEREAS, his community leadership has also included service as chair of the Mayor's
Character First Program in Brownsville, member of the West Tennessee River Basin Authority
Board, board chair of the Western Mental Health Institute, member and vice chair of the Board
of Directors of Community Rehabilitation Agencies of Tennessee, steering committee chair of
TAKE ONE (Offender Support Program), member of the West Tennessee Public Broadcast
Board of Directors, and Hardeman County Commissioner; and
WHEREAS, Representative Shaw has been recognized with numerous honors and
distinctions for his leadership and service, including the West Tennessee Black Business
Association Chairman's Award, Legislator of the Year from the Tennessee Development District
Association, the Jackson Affordable Housing Jo Helen Imani Award, the William Henry Miles
Outstanding Community Service Award, the West Tennessee African American Entrepreneurs
C.A. Rawls Bridge Builder Award, the Big Brothers Big Sisters Appreciation and Service Award,
the Tennessee Association of Broadcasters Distinguished Service Award, the Southeast
Association of Area Agencies on Aging's Impact Award, and Legislator of the Year from the
NBCSL Corporate Roundtable Benjamin Ruffin Award; and
WHEREAS, additional distinctions awarded to Representative Shaw include recognition
as a Media History Maker by The HistoryMakers, Legislator of the Year from the Tennessee
Forestry Association, and Legislator of the Year from the Southwest Tennessee Development
District, in addition to his receipt of the Presidential Award for Meritorious Service from the

Tennessee Education Association and The North Star Award from the Hardeman County
Commission for Black History; and
WHEREAS, he was further honored by the designation of a portion of Highway 64 as the
"Representative Johnny W. Shaw Highway" by the Hardeman County Commission, recognition
as the Legislator of the Year from the Tennessee Veterinary Medical Association and as a
USDA Rural Development Tennessee State Black History Month Honoree, and receipt of the
NFIB Guardian of Small Business Award; and
WHEREAS, born on January 5, 1942, in Laconia, Johnny Shaw attended the American
Baptist Theological Seminary in Nashville, where he studied theology, and pursued a
distinguished career in broadcasting beginning in the 1960s at WBOL-AM in Bolivar, where he
became the station's first African American staff announcer and later served as program
director, assistant manager, and general manager; and
WHEREAS, in 1987, Representative Shaw and his wife, Opal, founded Shaw
Broadcasting Company, LLC, which acquired WBOL-AM and later established WOJG-FM in
Bolivar; he also faithfully served as a minister of Saint John Missionary Baptist Church in
Stanton for many years; and
WHEREAS, throughout his outstanding career as a public servant, Representative
Johnny Shaw has worked tirelessly on behalf of his constituents, bringing to his office an
honorable bearing and the traditional values of hard work and common sense; and
WHEREAS, Representative Shaw is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public
service that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be
specially recognized for his exemplary tenure in the General Assembly; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor Representative Johnny Shaw for his meritorious service
to Tennessee as a member of the House of Representatives, commend him for his countless

to him our best wishes for much continued success and happiness.

the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
