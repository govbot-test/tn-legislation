SENATE RESOLUTION 365
By Gardenhire
A RESOLUTION to honor the 100th anniversary of the Old Church
Building in New Hope, Marion County.
WHEREAS, Marion County was purchased from the Cherokee Indians in 1817 and
named after the Revolutionary War soldier Francis Marion; and
WHEREAS, the New Hope community is located across the Tennessee River from
South Pittsburg, on the east side of Nickajack reservoir in Marion County; and
WHEREAS, in 1925, citizens of New Hope banded together to build an annex for the
Methodist Church. They raised a total of $168.35 from eighty-six individuals, an average of
$1.96 each plus one donation of thirty-two sacks of cement; and
WHEREAS, from March of 1926 until 1968, the building was known as Annex Methodist
Church; the building and land were owned by Bettye and Gerald Thomas, the grandson of J.P.
Hogan, who had constructed the building originally; and
WHEREAS, in 1974, the Antioch community became known as New Hope, which was
the name of a cemetery, and was officially incorporated as a city that same year; and
WHEREAS, in 2013, Tim and Nancy Rorex purchased the old Annex Methodist Church
building and lot. Afterward, they installed plumbing, updated the electricity, replaced the
windows, and added air conditioning and new wood floors but maintained the original walls and
cathedral ceiling; and
WHEREAS, after the renovations, on July 26, 2013, the Rorexes opened Tim and
Nancy's Barber Shop and Style in the historic building; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor the Old Church

Building in New Hope, Marion County, owned by the Rorex family on the occasion of its 100th
anniversary.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
