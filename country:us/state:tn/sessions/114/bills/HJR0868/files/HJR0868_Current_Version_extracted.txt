HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 868
By Jones J
A RESOLUTION to recognize Lunar New Year.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pride in recognizing those
significant cultural observances that enrich our State and uphold cherished traditions; and
WHEREAS, throughout February, countries across the world celebrate and honor the
Lunar New Year, including China, Vietnam, Korea, Indonesia, and many other Asian countries
and communities; and
WHEREAS, Lunar New Year is an important holiday, marking the first new moon of the
year with dates that vary slightly each year due to the moon-based lunar calendar; and
WHEREAS, the 2026 Lunar New Year begins on February 17, 2026, and commences
the fifteen-day Spring Festival, which concludes with the Lantern Festival on the first full moon
of the lunar calendar; and
WHEREAS, each year is associated with one of the twelve animals in the Chinese
zodiac and one of the five elements: wood, fire, earth, metal, and water; and
WHEREAS, the year 2026 is the Year of the Fire Horse, and the horse is a zodiac sign
that represents hard work, bravery, and resilience; and
WHEREAS, in preparation for the New Year, many families participate in the "sweeping
of the grounds," thoroughly cleaning their homes to remove any bad luck that may linger; and
WHEREAS, New Year's Eve and New Year's Day are traditionally reserved for family
celebrations, including religious ceremonies honoring ancestors; and
WHEREAS, the holiday also includes customs such as the giving of red envelopes (lai
see) containing small amounts of money to family members, dances and fireworks, and the
serving of traditional foods such as yuanxiao (sticky rice balls symbolizing family unity), fagao
(prosperity cake), and yusheng (raw fish and vegetable salad); and

WHEREAS, Lunar New Year is a time to gather with family, recognize traditions, honor
ancestors, celebrate the past year, and invite good luck and prosperity for the new year; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we recognize Lunar New Year, acknowledge its rich cultural traditions and
significance, and extend our warmest wishes for a joyful, meaningful, and prosperous new year
to all those who celebrate.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
