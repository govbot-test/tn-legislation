SENATE RESOLUTION 427
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to commend and congratulate Lauren Milam
upon her receipt of the Dennis Washington
Leadership Graduate Scholarship.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we recognize those industrious, talented students of
Tennessee who bring pride and honor to our State; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Lauren Milam, who has the great distinction of being
named a 2026 recipient of the Dennis Washington Leadership Graduate Scholarship; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Milam is one of only five students nationwide to receive this highly
competitive award, which provides up to $250,000 for graduate study; and
WHEREAS, the Horatio Alger Association of Distinguished Americans, Inc., is a
nonprofit organization that honors leaders who have overcome adversity and supports young
people in furthering their education; and
WHEREAS, endowed in 2008 and funded by the Dennis and Phyllis Washington
Foundation in partnership with the Horatio Alger Association, the Dennis Washington
Leadership Graduate Scholarship invests in exceptional individuals with leadership potential
who are striving to make a significant impact in their respective fields; and
WHEREAS, as of 2025, the program has awarded more than $11.8 million in graduate
scholarships to 148 Horatio Alger Alumni Scholars over seventeen years; and
WHEREAS, applicants for the Dennis Washington Leadership Graduate Scholarship
must have a minimum 3.0 GPA; demonstrate leadership, integrity, and perseverance in
overcoming adversity; and show a commitment to entrepreneurship and community service; and
WHEREAS, Lauren Milam was selected from a competitive pool of Horatio Alger Alumni
Scholars based on academic excellence, leadership, and a demonstrated commitment to
overcoming adversity; and

WHEREAS, a Waynesboro native and an alumnus of the University of Tennessee at
Martin, Lauren Milam is pursuing an MD/MBA at East Tennessee State University's Quillen
College of Medicine; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Milam's recent honor is a great source of pride for her family, her
school, and her community; and
WHEREAS, her display of aptitude and ambition surely bodes well for her future
success, and her dedication to excellence is clearly deserving of our respect, admiration, and
commendation; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we commend and congratulate
Lauren Milam on being named a 2026 Dennis Washington Leadership Graduate Scholarship
recipient, extending our best wishes for much continued success in all her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
