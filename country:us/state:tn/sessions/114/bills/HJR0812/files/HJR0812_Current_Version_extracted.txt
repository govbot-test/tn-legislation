HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 812
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Judge Kevin Blair Wilson
of Collegedale.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Kevin Blair Wilson, Collegedale City Judge and attorney; and
WHEREAS, Judge Wilson was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, an esteemed attorney and prominent leader, Kevin Wilson graduated from
Southern Adventist University in 1978 and obtained his J.D. from the University of Tennessee
College of Law in 1981; during his time in law school, he met his wife, Scarlett, and upon
graduating, they opened a law practice together in Chattanooga in 1982; and
WHEREAS, Judge Wilson was a member of the Chattanooga Bar Association,
Tennessee Municipal Judges Conference, Tennessee General Sessions Judges Conference,
and ACA International, the association of credit and collection professionals; and
WHEREAS, in recognition of his sterling legal career, Kevin Wilson was honored by
Legal Aid of East Tennessee with the Bruce C. Bailey Volunteer Lawyers Award for his
assistance to those who were unable to afford legal fees and his passion for helping others, and
he was inducted into the 2019 Fellows Class of the Chattanooga Bar Association; and
WHEREAS, Judge Wilson enjoyed working with young people, and he volunteered to
speak at schools, assisted with mock trial competitions, and participated in Southern Adventist
University's School of Business programs; and
WHEREAS, Judge Kevin Wilson loved helping people and trying to make a positive
difference in the lives of those he encountered; his commitment to public service and his

dedication to improving the lives of his fellow Tennesseans ensured his legacy as a leader who
worked with integrity and compassion; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his passion for serving others, Judge Wilson enjoyed the
camaraderie he shared with other judges, attorneys, and court personnel, and he was a
connoisseur of good food and a skilled baker, who baked cakes and made his special
Christmas Chex Mix for the clerks; and
WHEREAS, his family was his greatest joy, and he took great pride in their
accomplishments while cherishing their happiness, including practicing law with his son, Seth,
and cheering on his daughter, Katy, at sporting events and her PhD defense; and
WHEREAS, Kevin Wilson's dear ones include his wife of forty-four years, Scarlett; his
son, Seth; his daughter, Katy (Chloe); his heart son, Chase (Sara) Reimer; his sister, Candace
(Lennard) Jorgensen; his brother, Kent (Claire) Wilson; and a host of beloved cousins, aunts,
uncles, and countless friends; and
WHEREAS, Judge Wilson leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Judge Kevin Blair Wilson, reflecting fondly upon
his impeccable character, his extraordinary contributions to the people of Tennessee, and his
stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and extend our
condolences to the family of Judge Wilson.
