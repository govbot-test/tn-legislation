HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1476
By Hemmer
A RESOLUTION to honor George Renaudin II on the occasion of
his retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of industry; and
WHEREAS, George Renaudin II is one such estimable person who has served as
President of Insurance at Humana, from which position he will take his much-deserved
retirement this year; and
WHEREAS, as President of Insurance, Mr. Renaudin is responsible for leading strategy,
product development, business operations excellence, clinical quality, sales and distribution,
network and provider experience, and profitability for Humana's more than seven million
Medicare Advantage, Medicare Supplement, Medicaid, and Specialty members, as well as long-
term support services businesses; and
WHEREAS, prior to his career at Humana, Mr. Renaudin served as Senior Vice
President of Administration at Ochsner Health Plan and Executive Director of the Louisiana
Health Care Commission, which he also served as Chief Health Reform Analyst; and
WHEREAS, a highly respected member of the healthcare industry, George Renaudin
served on Louisiana Governor Bobby Jindal's transition team and as president of the Louisiana
Association of Health Plans; and
WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, George Renaudin was named a 2004
40 under 40 member of the Power Generation by the New Orleans City Business magazine;
and

WHEREAS, Mr. Renaudin earned a bachelor's degree from Tulane University and a
master's degree in public policy with an emphasis on Medicare and healthcare policy from
Pennsylvania State University; he is also a Crowe Scholar and cum laude graduate of Loyola
University Law School; and
WHEREAS, his many professional accomplishments aside, George Renaudin is most
grateful for the love and support he shares with his wife and two children; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Renaudin has demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and
integrity throughout his career, winning the unbridled respect and admiration of his community
and a grateful public; and
WHEREAS, George Renaudin is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public
service that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be
specially recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commend George Renaudin II for his honorable and astute service to
the citizens of this State and extend to him our best wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
