SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1087
By Lowe
A RESOLUTION to honor Dewey V. Woody, Jr., on the occasion
of his retirement.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in paying tribute
to a gentleman who has devoted his life to public service in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Dewey V. Woody, Jr., is taking a well-deserved retirement from the State of
Tennessee, having devoted more than four decades to safeguarding the lives and property of
his fellow citizens through service in fire protection, law enforcement, emergency management,
arson investigation, and homeland security; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Woody worked as a deputy with the Bradley County Sheriff's Office
before joining the Cleveland Police Department, where he spent fourteen years in various roles;
and
WHEREAS, demonstrating exceptional skill and dedication, Dewey Woody continued his
public safety work with the Bradley County Sheriff's Office as a reserve deputy and lead fire
investigator from 2000 to 2012; and
WHEREAS, as a member of Bradley County Fire and Rescue Department from 2000 to
2012, he held the position of fire chief, leading ten stations, fifty-seven full-time firefighters, and
ninety volunteers while strengthening countywide fire protection and emergency response
capabilities; and
WHEREAS, Chief Woody transformed the volunteer force into one of the best
professional fire and rescue squads in the State; and
WHEREAS, when Bradley County experienced devastating tornados in 2011, Chief
Woody coordinated a rescue response that established the blueprint for such emergencies, and
his professionalism and efficiency during this crisis became legendary among his peers; and

WHEREAS, Dewey Woody joined Tennessee State Government in 2012, first as
Director of the Bomb and Arson Section of the Department of Commerce and Insurance and
subsequently as Chief Operating Officer and Deputy Director of the Tennessee Emergency
Management Agency, where he helped guide statewide preparedness, coordination, and
emergency response; and
WHEREAS, since 2016, he has served with the Tennessee Department of Safety and
Homeland Security as a Special Agent, bringing to the position a depth of experience matched
only by his steadfast commitment to the mission of protecting Tennesseans; and
WHEREAS, a devoted husband and family man, Dewey Woody is most grateful for the
love and support of his wife, Melissa Alley Woody, and his three daughters; and
WHEREAS, as he enters retirement, Mr. Woody will take with him more than four
decades of accumulated knowledge, skill, and experience, leaving a legacy of excellence that
will be remembered and appreciated by those who have had the privilege to serve alongside
him; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor Dewey V. Woody, Jr., for making Bradley
County and the State of Tennessee safer for their citizens during his forty-plus years of public
service and extend to him our best wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
