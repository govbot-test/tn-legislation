HOUSE RESOLUTION 198
By Pearson
A RESOLUTION to honor Reverend Al Green on the occasion of
his eightieth birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this legislative body should recognize those
remarkable individuals whose lives and legacies have enriched our culture, uplifted our
communities, and inspired generations; and
WHEREAS, Reverend Al Green, an icon of American music and a devoted man of faith,
celebrates his eightieth birthday this year, marking eight decades of extraordinary contributions
to the arts, the church, and the moral imagination of our nation; and
WHEREAS, born on April 13, 1946, in Forrest City, Arkansas, Al Green rose from
humble beginnings to become one of the most influential voices in the history of soul music,
shaping the sound of a generation among the best musicians in the world in Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Reverend Al Green recorded timeless classics such as "Let's Stay
Together," "Tired of Being Alone," and "Love and Happiness", among personal favorites such as
"Rhymes" and "Take Me to The River"; and
WHEREAS, his distinctive voice—simultaneously tender and powerful—has
transcended genre and era, earning him multiple Grammy Awards, induction into the Rock &
Roll Hall of Fame, and recognition as one of the greatest singers of all time; and
WHEREAS, beyond his musical achievements, Reverend Green answered a higher
calling, dedicating his life to ministry as the pastor of Full Gospel Tabernacle Church in
Memphis, where he has faithfully served his congregation and community for decades; and
WHEREAS, in both pulpit and performance, Reverend Al Green has embodied a spirit of
love, resilience, and redemption, using his gifts to bring people together and to remind us of the
enduring power of faith and music to heal and inspire; and

WHEREAS, his legacy is woven deeply into the cultural fabric of Memphis and the
nation, and his influence continues to resonate through artists, congregants, and admirers
across the world; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to Reverend Green on this special
occasion and thank him for his many contributions to his community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and
celebrate Reverend Al Green on the occasion of his eightieth birthday, commending his
extraordinary life of artistry, ministry, and service and extending to him our heartfelt wishes for
continued health, joy, and blessings.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
