SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 700
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to commend Calvary Baptist Church on the
occasion of its 100th anniversary.
WHEREAS, the churches of our nation have been an integral part of our history as a
people and have had an unparalleled impact in forming and maintaining a stable and just
society; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we should recognize those venerable religious
institutions and their congregations who, through their unflagging capacity for love, dedication of
spirit, and faith in God, strive to enrich the lives of people in their communities; and
WHEREAS, one such dynamic community of faith is that of Calvary Baptist Church in
Morristown, whose members will celebrate the church's 100th anniversary this year; and
WHEREAS, Calvary Baptist Church was organized on March 30, 1926, with twenty-
three charter members who established their church across the tracks near Rheatown; the
original members came from First Baptist Morristown, Montvue Baptist Church, and old Second
Baptist Church; and
WHEREAS, Reverend Marshall Hoover was called as Calvary Baptist Church's first
pastor, and he was followed in the pulpit over the next 100 years by a succession of dedicated
pastors, including Reverend John Ogan, Reverend J.C. Davis, Reverend James Lyle, Reverend
T.T. Lewis, Reverend Cecil Mills, Reverend L.W. Vandergriff, Reverend S.D. Rhodes, Reverend
Eugene Moore, Reverend Darrell Seal, Reverend Carl Triplet, Dr. Clyde Martin, Reverend Mike
Henegar, Reverend Jim Fanning, Reverend Sidney Nichols, and Dr. Larry Parrott; and
WHEREAS, in the 1930s, Calvary Baptist Church's membership split, with one group of
parishioners getting the building and the other the church furnishings; Calvary Baptist Church
today still has that original pulpit; and

WHEREAS, Calvary Baptist Church rented an old unpainted store building that had two
stories, the downstairs served as the sanctuary and the upstairs was the Sunday school space
and where the pastor lived; and
WHEREAS, leaving the rented building, Calvary Baptist Church purchased a lot at the
corner of Rosedale and Harrison streets and constructed a tent for worship; concrete blocks
were constructed on site, and the church building was quickly erected and completed by 1939;
and
WHEREAS, in 1952, a Sunday school wing and choir space were added as well as the
front porch and steps; spreading its wings to help launch other congregations, Calvary Baptist
Church assisted in the establishment of Calvue Baptist Church and Morningside Baptist Church;
and
WHEREAS, continuing to grow over the decades, Calvary Baptist Church bought a
parsonage, launched a bus ministry, and dedicated a new building in 1979 to meet the need for
more space; and
WHEREAS, as the church celebrates 100 years of service, it continues to be a light in
the community, and the devout members of the congregation have faithfully striven to carry out
God's work in the world; and
WHEREAS, Calvary Baptist Church is truly one of the great religious institutions and
catalysts for positive spiritual change in our State and nation, and the members of this
exceptional congregation should be appropriately honored for their exemplary service to their
fellow Christians and the good people of Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we join in the celebration of the 100th anniversary of
Calvary Baptist Church, thank the good people of this congregation and their ministers for their
