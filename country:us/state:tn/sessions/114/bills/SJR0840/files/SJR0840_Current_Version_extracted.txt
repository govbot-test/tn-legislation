SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 840
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Frederick Dale "Freddie"
Harvey of Kingston.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Frederick Dale "Freddie" Harvey of Kingston; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Harvey was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional
who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous
capacities; and
WHEREAS, Freddie Harvey was born on May 12, 1934, in the Harvey Hotel in
downtown Kingston; he spent his early life on his family's farm in Grassy Cove near Crossville;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Harvey graduated from Roane County High School and attended
Tennessee Wesleyan University, the University of Tennessee, and Roane State Community
College; a lifelong learner, he was one of the first people in Kingston to learn how to use a
computer; and
WHEREAS, Freddie Harvey worked at all three Oak Ridge plants, Y-12, K-25, and X-10,
and also served in the U.S. Army from 1954 to 1956; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Harvey owned Rare Earth Realty in Kingston and served as a broker
and, later, a landlord; and
WHEREAS, Freddie Harvey loved being on Watts Bar Lake and was the captain of his
houseboat, the "Annabelle"; and
WHEREAS, a man of deep faith, Mr. Harvey was a lifelong member of Kingston United
Methodist Church and especially enjoyed being part of the men's weekly Bible study group; and

WHEREAS, Freddie Harvey was deeply devoted to his family, and he always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his wife of forty-four
years, Annabelle Stegall Harvey; children, Lucy (Richard) Griffey, Jennie (Doug) Ramey, and
Richard (Debbie) Haile; sister, Betty Harvey (Joe) Guinn; grandchildren, Charles Ramey, Anna
(Chris) Spurling, Bethany Haile, Clark Griffey, Jesse Griffey, and Morgan Griffey; great-
granddaughter, Amelia Jane Spurling; special nephews, Duane (Lisa) Davis and Mike Davis;
great-nephew, Carson Davis; and great-nieces, Lauren Davis and Maggie Davis; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Harvey was preceded in death by his parents, Fred and Crystal Harvey,
and his sister, Brenda Harvey Sanderson; and
WHEREAS, Freddie Harvey leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Frederick Dale "Freddie"
Harvey, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living
the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Harvey.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to

without House or Senate designation.
