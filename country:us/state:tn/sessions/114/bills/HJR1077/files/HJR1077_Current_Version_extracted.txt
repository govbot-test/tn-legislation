HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1077
By Russell
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Edyth Ann Worthy on
the celebration of her 100th birthday.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to pay
tribute to those citizens who are celebrating special occasions in their estimable lives; and
WHEREAS, Edyth Ann Worthy celebrates her 100th birthday this year, a milestone
commemorated as yet another precious souvenir of life's rich pageant; and
WHEREAS, born on April 10, 1926, Ms. Worthy has lived a remarkable life, having
experienced firsthand the most eventful and productive years of American history; and
WHEREAS, the youngest of six children born to John Edgar Worthy, Sr., and Lula B.
Logan, Ms. Worthy is a 1943 graduate of Lenoir City High School, where she was voted Most
Popular and Most Athletic and served as the senior class vice president; and
WHEREAS, Edyth Worthy attended Tennessee Technological University, where she
was voted Who's Who, and graduated in 1948; she then earned her master's degree at
Peabody College; and
WHEREAS, early in her professional career, Ms. Worthy worked in Oak Ridge when the
atomic bomb was dropped; and
WHEREAS, Edyth Worthy enjoyed a fruitful and fulfilling tenure as an educator and
coach in Jackson for thirty-five years; and
WHEREAS, throughout her years as an educator, Ms. Worthy was wholly committed to
the growth and development of young people and proud to have influenced hundreds of
students, some of whom still visit and call her today; and

WHEREAS, no stranger to awards and accolades, Ms. Worthy received the
Distinguished Service Award from the Jackson Sports Hall of Fame, a testament to her
character, integrity, and years of astute service; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Worthy is deeply committed to her faith and has been a lifelong
member of Cumberland Presbyterian Church, where she served as an elder for ten years; and
WHEREAS, as she marks her 100th birthday, Edyth Worthy is a respected and active
member of her community, admired for her compassion, strength, gentle wisdom, and radiant
smile, as well as her ability to play four bingo cards simultaneously; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Worthy has been and continues to be an inspiration and a blessing to
all who have the good fortune of knowing her; she is ever supportive, kind, and steadfast in her
faith, and her guidance and dedication to family, church, and community are immeasurable; and
WHEREAS, Edyth Worthy will observe her birthday on Saturday, April 11, 2026,
surrounded by her loved ones, and celebrate a century of love, laughter, faith, family, friendship,
and cherished memories at Heartland Worship Center in Lenoir City; and
WHEREAS, we wish to grasp this golden opportunity to specially recognize one of
Tennessee's most outstanding citizens on this very special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Edyth Ann Worthy on the celebration of her
100th birthday, extending to her our deepest gratitude for a life of exemplary service to her
community and our best wishes for many happy returns of the day.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
