SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 654
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor Melvin Deener on the occasion of his
retirement.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that the members of this General Assembly should honor
those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm throughout their
years of public service; and
WHEREAS, Melvin Deener of Shelby County is one such estimable person, who served
the City of Memphis with distinction for fifty-three years; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Deener recently embarked on a much-deserved retirement from his
position as a bus operator for the Memphis Area Transit Authority (MATA), having garnered a
reputation as the longest-serving bus operator within MATA; and
WHEREAS, a leader in his community and his profession, Mr. Deener began his
estimable career at MATA in February of 1972, and since then, he drove 1.5 million miles and
safely transported more than four million passengers; and
WHEREAS, the success of the State of Tennessee, the strength of our communities,
and the overall vitality of our society depend, in great measure, on the dedication of people such
as Mr. Deener, who use their considerable talents and resources to serve others; and
WHEREAS, Melvin Deener is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service
that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we extend to Melvin Deener our heartfelt wishes for
a happy and fulfilling retirement and every continued success in his future endeavors.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
