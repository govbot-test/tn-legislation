Senate State and Local Government 1
Amendment No. 1 to SB1159
Briggs
Signature of Sponsor
AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 1159 House Bill No. 674*
by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:
SECTION 1.
(a) The Tennessee Department of Correction shall study:
(1) The impact of fostering connections between incarcerated persons
and their families to reduce recidivism, including whether regular visitation from
family members to an incarcerated person lowers the likelihood of that person
reoffending;
(2) The costs to families of incarcerated persons to visit family members
and loved ones who are incarcerated at distant facilities; and
(3) The parameters of programs in other states that authorize a qualified
incarcerated person to be transferred to a facility closer to the person's family,
including the programs' eligibility requirements, costs to the respective states or
local governments, reduction in travel costs to family members of incarcerated
persons, and success in reducing reincarceration and other positive outcomes.
(b) The Department may request information and input from other appropriate
state and local governmental entities, as necessary. Each such entity shall comply with
the Department's request. The study must be conducted from the Department's existing
resources.
(c) By no later than January 31, 2027, the Department shall report its findings to
each member of the general assembly and shall provide a copy of the report to the
legislative librarian. The report may be delivered electronically.
