SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 926
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor The Brown Four of
Memphis.
WHEREAS, Tennessee is proud to be the home of various genres of music that are
essential components of the American musical landscape; from Bristol, the birthplace of country
music, to Memphis, the blues capital of the world, musicians across the State continue to inspire
and influence others with their astounding artistry; and
WHEREAS, The Brown Four of Memphis are a dynamic singing group made up of four
precocious siblings: Daelin Brown, aged fourteen; Daniya Brown, aged thirteen; Deanna
Brown, aged eleven; and Davion Brown, aged ten; and
WHEREAS, Daelin, Daniya, Deanna, and Davion Brown are the children of Pastor David
Brown, Jr., and Olivia Martin Brown; the young singers began singing as The Brown Four in
2019, as they were able to master their harmony and unique, soulful sound; and
WHEREAS, their musical journey began as toddlers playing around their house, church,
and the playgrounds, but they officially began traveling and performing in 2022 after a video of
them from their mother's Facebook page went viral in December 2021; and
WHEREAS, due to their online success, The Brown Four were invited as special guests
on The Jennifer Hudson Show and American Idol in 2025; and
WHEREAS, these talented young individuals love to sing, and the internet and world at
large have shown that they love to experience the musical stylings of the Brown children, who
certainly have bright futures ahead; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we honor the hard work, talent, and promise of The
Brown Four; now, therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor The Brown Four of
Memphis, extending our congratulations for their success thus far and our best wishes for
continued success in the future.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
