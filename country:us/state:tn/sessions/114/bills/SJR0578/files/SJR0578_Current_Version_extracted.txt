SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 578
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to commend UNAPOLOGETIC upon the
organization's tenth anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is the privilege of the members of this General Assembly to recognize
those organizations of this State that seek to promote and revitalize the arts and uplift all voices,
thereby positively impacting the well-being of countless Tennesseans and the communities in
which they live; and
WHEREAS, one such organization is UNAPOLOGETIC of Memphis, a storytelling label
and creative agency that celebrates its tenth anniversary this year; and
WHEREAS, founded in 2015 by IMAKEMADBEATS, UNAPOLOGETIC has built a world
in which artists do not just make things—they embrace weird as being normal and turn
vulnerability into art; and
WHEREAS, through music, visual art, film, performance, and design, UNAPOLOGETIC
showcases voices that challenge, disrupt, and refuse to conform, telling stories that champion
authenticity; and
WHEREAS, the organization's commitment to amplifying homegrown talent has turned
every creative junt into a movement, inspiring a generation to lean into the power of being
different; and
WHEREAS, UNAPOLOGETIC's fearless voice and community impact remind us that
Memphis does not follow trends—"mane, we make them"; and
WHEREAS, as UNAPOLOGETIC celebrates its tenth year, the organization reflects
upon the work, the people, and the city that have helped make its noble endeavors possible;
and

WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we recognize this important organization on this special
occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commend UNAPOLOGETIC upon the
organization's tenth anniversary, extending our best wishes for every continued success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
