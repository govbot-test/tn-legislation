Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 57-4-102(31), is amended by adding
the following as a new subdivision (F):
(F) "Premises," when:
(i) Referring to one (1) or more establishments licensed under this
chapter that are located within or adjacent to a premises for which a license has
been issued under subdivision (18) or (37) in conjunction with an event approved
by a temporary special event zone ordinance in a county having a metropolitan
form of government with a population in excess of five hundred thousand
(500,000), according to the 2020 federal census or any subsequent federal
census;
includes the area described in subdivision (31)(F)(i);
(ii) Consumption of alcoholic beverages and beer on the premises as
defined in this subdivision (31)(F) is authorized:
(a) For events licensed under subdivision (31)(F)(i) and
authorized under a temporary special event zone ordinance; and
(b) For the duration of an event approved by the commission
under subdivision (31)(F)(i). In order for an alcoholic beverage or beer
sold by a licensed establishment to be consumed by customers outside
its regular licensed premises in the larger event premises, the alcoholic

beverage or beer must be in an event-designed to-go cup that is
purchased from the designated event permit holder;
(iii) Notwithstanding this subdivision (31)(F), only the organization named
in the temporary special event zone ordinance and county permit application may
sell an event-designed to-go cup to the licensed establishments described in
subdivision (31)(F)(i);
(iv) For purposes of consumption on the premises as defined under this
subdivision (31)(F), an alcoholic beverage or beer must be in an event-designed
to-go cup that is labeled in a manner that identifies the licensed establishment
that sold the alcoholic beverage or beer at retail to a patron; and
(v) As used in this subdivision (31)(F), "event-designed to-go cup" means
a cup designed by the organization named in the temporary special event zone
ordinance and the county permit application, and sold by an establishment
licensed under this chapter within the premises approved by the commission
pursuant to this subdivision (31)(F);

SECTION 2. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
