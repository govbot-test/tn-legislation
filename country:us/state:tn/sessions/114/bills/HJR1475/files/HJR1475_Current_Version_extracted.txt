HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1475
By Vital
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Deputy Landon
Christopher Faulkner of the Hamilton County
Sheriff's Office.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
own concerns to ensure our collective freedom and personal safety; in providing for the
domestic tranquility of the community as a whole, society recognizes the need for a system of
laws; and
WHEREAS, the enforcement of the law has been entrusted to a small number of
dedicated individuals who daily face the rigors of human conflict in protecting those whom they
so selflessly serve; and
WHEREAS, the people of Tennessee are fortunate to be served by the brave and
dedicated law enforcement officers who put their lives on the line while carrying out their
essential duties; and
WHEREAS, Deputy Landon Christopher Faulkner of the Hamilton County Sheriff's Office
was one such outstanding public servant, and the members of this General Assembly were
greatly saddened to learn of his untimely death in a traffic accident while on duty; and
WHEREAS, born on September 13, 2001, in Knoxville, to Chad Faulkner and the late
Kristal Nicole Faulkner, Landon Faulkner was a devoted son, husband, father, and brother; and
WHEREAS, reflecting a family tradition of public service, Deputy Faulkner was the son
of former Representative Chad Faulkner, who served in the 106th General Assembly
representing District 36, composed of Campbell and Union counties; and
WHEREAS, Deputy Faulkner began his service with the Hamilton County Sheriff's Office
in 2022, following nearly three years of service as a deputy and corrections deputy with the
Grundy County Sheriff's Office; and

WHEREAS, demonstrating an early commitment to law enforcement, he was a graduate
of the Knoxville Police Department's Explorer Cadet Program in 2017; and
WHEREAS, a dedicated public servant and guardian of the community, Deputy Landon
Faulkner served the citizens of Tennessee with professionalism, courage, and a steadfast
commitment to duty; and
WHEREAS, a faithful servant of Christ who was deeply devoted to his family, Landon
Faulkner is survived by his loving wife, Megan; his cherished children, Malachi, Breylee, Mia,
and Lillee; his father and stepmother, Chad and Jeanna Faulkner; his brother and sister-in-law,
Logan and Grace Faulkner; his sister and brother-in-law, Mattie Faulkner Dyer and Draven
Dyer; his sister, Marlei Chandler; and numerous nieces and nephews; and
WHEREAS, he is also survived by members of his extended family, including his aunt,
Kathy (Larry) Drummer; in-laws, Mike (Jeria) Fults and Christie (James) Meeks; brothers- and
sisters-in-law, Logan Fults, Gracie Fults (Colton Summers), Isabella Fults, and Victoria (Hunter)
Pigg; and special cousin, Jeremy Householder; he also shared a special bond of friendship and
brotherhood with Jerry Green, Mason Meeks, and Carter Roberts; and
WHEREAS, Deputy Faulkner was preceded in death by his mother; grandparents,
Johnny Kenneth Faulkner and Yvonne Chadwell Faulkner; and uncle, Jimmy Householder; and
WHEREAS, Landon Faulkner leaves behind an enduring legacy of service, integrity, and
devotion to the protection of others; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly should pause to remember the life
and service of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Deputy Landon Christopher Faulkner, reflecting
fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life
with courage and conviction.
- 2 - 018147
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Deputy Faulkner.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 3 - 018147
