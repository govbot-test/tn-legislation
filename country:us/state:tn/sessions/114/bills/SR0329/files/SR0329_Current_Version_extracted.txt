SENATE RESOLUTION 329
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Alpha Beta Chapter
of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., on the
celebration of the chapter's 100th anniversary.
WHEREAS, it is our pleasure to recognize the Alpha Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta
Sorority, Inc., on the 100th anniversary of its founding; and
WHEREAS, the Alpha Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., was founded on
January 13, 1913; for a full century, this chapter has stood as a pillar of service, scholarship,
social action, and sisterhood, rising with the potential to break barriers, challenge societal
norms, and redefine success in unimaginable ways; and
WHEREAS, the Alpha Beta Chapter was the first Greek-letter organization permitted on
the campus of Fisk University and the first chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.,
established in this State; and
WHEREAS, more than a decade after the founding of the Alpha Beta Chapter, Fisk
University President Fayette Avery McKenzie prohibited most extracurricular student activities,
including Black Greek-letter organizations; and
WHEREAS, during the spring of 1925, student-led protests against autocratic leadership
and repressive policies, including magazine censorship, a dating prohibition, and restricted
conversations between male and female students, successfully forced the president's
resignation and ultimately paved the way for the founding of Black Greek-letter organizations at
Fisk University; and
WHEREAS, led by Lillian Alexander, conversations with National Headquarters and the
Grand Chapter secured permission from Fisk University officials to establish a Delta Sigma
Theta collegiate chapter; and

WHEREAS, the Alpha Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., was officially
chartered on March 13, 1926, at Fisk University under the leadership of charter members Hallie
Walton Smith, Eloise Lowe Clark, Alyce McCarroll, Jeanne Osby Goodwin, and Anna Jewel
Redd, who remained part of the membership intake process for more than a semester; and
WHEREAS, at that time, Dorothy Pelham Beckley was the sorority's national president,
and Anna R. Johnson Julian served as regional director; and
WHEREAS, the Delta Sigma Theta Sorority held its eleventh national convention in
Nashville in December 1931, cohosted by the members of the Alpha Beta Chapter; and
WHEREAS, the Alpha Beta Chapter has distinguished itself as a public service
organization devoted to the highest tenets of community service; its members have hosted
study sessions to assist Fisk University students, cohosted a sexual health fair in partnership
with Music City PrEP Clinic and Nashville CARES, provided free STI testing and materials for
students, and established a scholarship fund to pay in full the balances of the chapter's
members; and
WHEREAS, notable sorors of the Alpha Beta Chapter include Minerva Hatcher Johnson
Hawkins, 1927 Alpha Beta initiate and President of Pi Sigma, who served as Southern Regional
Director from 1941 to 1946 and was renowned throughout the Nashville community as a pioneer
in education; Hortense Golden Canady, 1944 Alpha Beta initiate, who served as national
(international) president from 1983 to 1988, and whose daughter, Alexa Canady, became the
first African-American woman neurosurgeon; Julia Agnes Washington Bond, 1927 initiate of
Alpha Beta, who served as librarian and was the mother of Civil Rights activist Julian Bond;
Katherine Crosby Bowman, 1947 graduate of Fisk University, who wrote Across the River; Kym
Whitley, 1983 Alpha Beta initiate and renowned actress, comedian, and activist; Josie Robinson
Johnson; Carolyn Jordan Booker; and Ramona Hoage Edelin, Ph.D.; and

WHEREAS, additionally, Helen E. Woods was initiated through the Alpha Beta Chapter
and served as Southern Regional Director from 1936 to 1942, and Yolanda R. Moore served as
Southern Regional Representative from 2003 to 2005; and
WHEREAS, current chapter members who are successfully continuing the rich legacy of
the Alpha Beta chapter include Chapter President Dania Rutherford; First Vice President Karli
Lusain; Second Vice President Carline Boston; Treasurer Tamiyah Dubose; Corresponding
Secretary Chloe Nimaga; Recording Secretary Lauryn Fowler; Financial Secretary Jayla
Simmons; Internal Auditor Aries Fitzgerald; and Historian Delaney Farver; and
WHEREAS, for 100 years, Alpha Beta members have championed educational
advancement, civic engagements, cultural awareness, and leadership development,
exemplifying the ideals set forth by its twenty-two founding members; Alpha Beta's story is one
of purpose, pride, and progress, and the next century begins with the same unwavering
commitment to creating a meaningful difference; and
WHEREAS, as the oldest collegiate chapter in the southern region, Alpha Beta was
chartered by five visionary collegiate women who were wholly committed to uplifting their
campus and community and has grown to be a legacy-bearing chapter rooted in excellence and
impact; and
WHEREAS, the Alpha Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., continues to
stand as a pillar of success, and it is fitting that the members of this legislative body honor the
members on the chapter's centennial celebration; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we hereby honor and commend
the members, past and present, of the Alpha Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.,
on the celebration of the chapter's 100th anniversary and extend our best wishes for every
continued success.
