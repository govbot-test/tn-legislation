SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1106
By Bailey
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Bobby Harold Lane of
Flintville.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Bobby Harold Lane of Flintville; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Lane was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on April 4, 1952, Mr. Lane was the son of Elmus Burl and Mildred Lee
Price Lane; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Lane was a pillar of the Putnam County Sheriff's Department for
thirty-six years; he did not just enforce the law, he built relationships with citizens across
Putnam and Jackson counties; and
WHEREAS, a man who defined himself by how well he cared for others, Mr. Lane was
not only known for his high standards and professional excellence but also for his unique gift for
making people feel seen and cared for; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Lane was famous for his smile and laughter, which was contagious,
boisterous, and could be heard across any room; he was equally respected for the firm
convictions he held when pushed; and
WHEREAS, when not in uniform, Mr. Lane found his peace in the stillness of the woods
and enjoyed the quiet patience of hunting; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Lane was also deeply devoted to his family and always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; on May 19, 1972, he married his soulmate,
Carolyn, and together, they built a life centered on faith and family; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Lane attended Smyrna Church of Christ and later Meridianville Church
of Christ, where he was a dedicated member and believer; and
WHEREAS, he is survived by his loving wife of almost fifty-four years, Carolyn; his
daughter, Leslie Ann Lane (Kevin) Kimbrough; his siblings, Sandi Jernigan, Phyllis Moore, and
Tim Lane; his beloved grandchildren, Briana (Tristan) Hope, Trey (Eliza) Kimbrough, Serenity
Kimbrough, Koda Kimbrough, Jake Kimbrough, Iris Kimbrough, and Zeke Kimbrough; his great-
granddaughter, Mattie Kimbrough; and a host of friends and church family; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Mr. Lane was preceded in death by his grandson,
Elisha Kimbrough; and
WHEREAS, Bobby Lane leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Bobby Harold Lane,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Lane.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
