HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1236
By Love
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend the Tennessee State
University Tigers on winning the Ohio Valley
Conference Tournament and earning a berth in
the 2026 NCAA Division I Men's Basketball
Tournament.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly should salute those student athletes
who have distinguished themselves as true champions while proudly representing historically
Black colleges and universities; and
WHEREAS, the Tennessee State University men's basketball team has earned a berth
in the 2026 National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division I Men's Basketball
Tournament and will play in the Midwest Region; and
WHEREAS, 2026 marks Tennessee State's first NCAA Tournament berth since the
1993-1994 season, when the Tigers won their second of back-to-back Ohio Valley Conference
(OVC) Tournament championships thanks to the talent and leadership of two-time OVC Player
of the Year Carlos Rogers; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee State secured a share of the 2025-2026 Ohio Valley
Conference regular-season title with a 15-5 record, thus earning the No. 1 seed in the
conference tournament for the first time in thirty years; and
WHEREAS, the Tigers handily defeated UT Martin and then routed Morehead State to
claim the OVC Tournament title and punch their ticket to the NCAA tournament; and
WHEREAS, Tennessee State is led by OVC Player of the Year Aaron Nkrumah, who
paced the Tigers throughout the season as the team's primary scoring threat, while ranking
among national leaders in steals; and

WHEREAS, he was joined on the All-OVC First Team by teammate Travis Harper II, and
Dante Harris was named to the All-OVC Second Team and OVC All-Newcomer Team, tying the
program's highest single-season total of all-conference honorees; and
WHEREAS, this tremendous trio combined to average 46.8 points per game during the
regular season, helping power Tennessee State to an overall 23-9 record and one of its most
successful campaigns in decades; and
WHEREAS, Nolan Smith has made an incredible impact in his first season as
Tennessee State's head coach and has been recognized nationally for his coaching prowess;
and
WHEREAS, Coach Smith has been named a finalist for the Ben Jobe Award, Joe B. Hall
Award, and Hugh Durham Award; these honors recognize the nation's top coaches at
historically Black colleges and universities, first-year head coaches, and mid-major programs,
respectively; and
WHEREAS, the members of the Tennessee State men's basketball team epitomize all
that is good in today's student athletes, as they have achieved a premier level of success in
competition while exhibiting the highest degree of character in all aspects of life; and
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly find it appropriate to acknowledge
and applaud the Tennessee State University Tigers for their outstanding season on the
hardwood; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate the Tennessee State University Tigers on
winning the Ohio Valley Conference Tournament and earning a berth in the 2026 NCAA
Division I Men's Basketball Tournament and extend to them our best wishes as they represent
the Volunteer State in March Madness.
