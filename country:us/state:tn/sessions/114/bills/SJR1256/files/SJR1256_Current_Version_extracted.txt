SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1256
By Johnson
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of William Kenneth
Jorgensen of Williamson County.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of William Kenneth Jorgensen of Williamson County; and
WHEREAS, Bill Jorgensen was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on March 10, 1964, in Flint, Michigan, Bill Jorgensen was the son of
Kenneth Jorgensen and Nan Mortimer Jorgensen; and
WHEREAS, a public servant of the highest order, Mr. Jorgensen had recently retired
from Williamson County after a distinguished thirty-five-year career, during which he served as a
volunteer firefighter with the Rescue Squad, a telecommunicator, Communications Director, and
ultimately as Public Safety Director; and
WHEREAS, he served with acumen and alacrity, having made a lasting impact on not
only Williamson County but the State of Tennessee and this nation; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jorgensen was an avid amateur radio operator who connected with
people from around the world, and he also enjoyed beekeeping; and
WHEREAS, a gentleman of deep and abiding faith, Bill Jorgensen was deeply devoted
to his family, and he always endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Jorgensen enjoyed the loving companionship of his wife of thirty-three
years, Leigh Jorgensen, and was the proud father of three children, Kenneth (Olivia) Jorgensen,
Leda (Bennett) Carpenter, and Lauren Jorgensen, and three grandchildren, Clint Jorgensen,
Blake Jorgensen, and Billie Grace Carpenter; and

WHEREAS, he will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his mother and his
brother, Jim Jorgensen; and
WHEREAS, Bill Jorgensen leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of William Kenneth
Jorgensen, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to
living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Jorgensen.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
