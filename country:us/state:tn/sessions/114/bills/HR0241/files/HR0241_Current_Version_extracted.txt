HOUSE RESOLUTION 241
By Pearson
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Young, Gifted & Green
and Founder LaTricea Adams for extraordinary
leadership in advancing environmental justice
and public health equity.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this legislative body recognize those
organizations and leaders who have distinguished themselves through a steadfast commitment
to justice, equity, and the well-being of all people; and
WHEREAS, Young, Gifted & Green is a national, award-winning nonprofit environmental
justice organization dedicated to advancing policy change through advocacy, mentorship, and
leadership development, particularly in historically marginalized communities; and
WHEREAS, founded in response to the water crisis in Flint, Michigan, this movement
has grown into a powerful force addressing environmental racism, lead poisoning, and
inequitable access to clean air and water across communities in Tennessee and beyond; and
WHEREAS, through programs such as environmental justice training, youth leadership
councils, and community-based advocacy initiatives, Young, Gifted & Green empowers the next
generation of leaders to engage in civic action and fight for a healthier, more just future; and
WHEREAS, the organization has played a vital role in shaping public policy, including
contributing to the passage of the Childhood Lead Prevention Amendment Act and advancing
community-centered solutions to environmental health crises; and
WHEREAS, at the helm of this transformative work stands LaTricea Adams, an
educator, advocate, and visionary leader whose dedication to environmental justice has
mobilized communities, influenced policy at the highest levels, and inspired a national
movement; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Adams has demonstrated exceptional leadership through her service
as Founder, President, and Chief Executive Officer of Young, Gifted & Green, as well as
through her appointment to national advisory roles addressing environmental justice and public
health; and
WHEREAS, her work reflects an unwavering commitment to protecting vulnerable
communities from environmental harm, ensuring that every family—regardless of zip code—has
access to safe water, clean air, and a healthy environment; and
WHEREAS, the impact of Young, Gifted & Green and LaTricea Adams is felt not only in
policy victories but in the lives of young people, families, and communities empowered to
advocate for themselves and demand justice; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and
commend Young, Gifted & Green and Founder LaTricea Adams for their outstanding
contributions to environmental justice, public health, and community empowerment.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
