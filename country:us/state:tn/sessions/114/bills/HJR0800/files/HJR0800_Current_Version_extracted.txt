HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 800
By Crawford
A RESOLUTION to commemorate Certified Government Financial
Manager Month.
WHEREAS, the Chattanooga, Knoxville, and Nashville Chapters of the Association of
Government Accountants are professional organizations that are associated with and part of the
Association of Government Accountants (AGA), which is a network of around 13,000 members
in eighty chapters in the United States and around the world; and
WHEREAS, approximately 596 members of these three distinguished chapters
represent state, federal, municipal, and private-sector accountants, auditors, and financial
managers in the Volunteer State; and
WHEREAS, AGA Chattanooga, Knoxville, and Nashville Chapter members have
responded to AGA's mission of advancing government accountability as they have continued
their broad educational efforts with an emphasis on high standards of conduct, honor, and
character in the association's Code of Ethics; and
WHEREAS, these members are also making significant advances in both professional
ability and service to the citizens of Tennessee through their mastery of increasingly technical
and complex requirements; and
WHEREAS, the Certified Government Financial Manager (CGFM) program of AGA
provides for the demonstration of professionalism and competency by requiring CGFM
candidates to have an appropriate educational and employment history, to abide by AGA's
Code of Ethics, and to pass three examinations requiring expertise in governmental
environment, governmental financial management and control, and governmental accounting,
financial reporting, and budgeting; and

WHEREAS, the program also requires each CGFM professional to maintain certification
by completing at least eighty hours of continuing professional education in government financial
management topics or related technical subjects every two years; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we recognize and honor the Certified Government
Financial Manager program for its impact on the profession of governmental accounting and
management in Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commemorate Certified Government Financial Manager Month, March
2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
