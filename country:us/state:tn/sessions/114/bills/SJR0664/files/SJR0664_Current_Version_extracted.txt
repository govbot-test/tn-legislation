SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 664
By Oliver
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Nashville Electric
Service line workers and State and Metro
Nashville civil service employees for their brave
and tireless efforts during Winter Storm Fern.
WHEREAS, it is the privilege of the members of this General Assembly to recognize
those devoted Tennesseans who, in the discharge of their duties, commit long hours and often
put themselves in harm's way in order to ensure the well-being of their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Winter Storm Fern first hit Nashville on January 24, 2026, bringing with it
snow, sleet, and freezing rain that coated trees, roads, and power lines in a layer of ice,
accompanied by frigid temperatures that lingered for several days; and
WHEREAS, this historic ice storm resulted in the closure of schools and offices
throughout the Nashville area, and approximately 230,000 Nashville Electric Service (NES)
customers were left without power, even as temperatures plummeted into the single digits; and
WHEREAS, in response to this, the largest single outage in its history, NES doubled its
workforce following day one of the storm; by January 26, 2026, NES had 631 line workers on
the job, bringing more the following morning for a total of 925 responding to the crisis; additional
crews were called in from Florida, Maryland, Kentucky, Indiana, Ohio, Mississippi, and
elsewhere in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, these brave individuals endured perilous circumstances, braving the
extreme cold at all hours to restore power to their fellow Tennesseans; and
WHEREAS, NES line workers are members of the Service Employees International
Union (SEIU) Local 205, a union chartered in Tennessee of diverse members in health care, the
public sector, and property services, as well as members of the International Brotherhood of

Electrical Workers (IBEW), AFL-CIO, and Laborers' International Union of North America
(LiUNA); and
WHEREAS, others who played a critical role in keeping residents of Nashville and
surrounding counties safe during this time are civil service employees of Metro Nashville
government, including Office of Emergency Management and Metro Nashville Public Schools,
and state agencies, including the Tennessee Emergency Management Agency and the
Tennessee Bureau of Investigation; and
WHEREAS, these exemplary professionals are truly dedicated to their jobs and to the
well-being of their fellow Tennesseans, and for their prodigious efforts on behalf of the people of
this State, they should be recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commend and offer our appreciation and most
sincere thanks to the Nashville Electric Service line workers and State and Metro Nashville civil
service employees who worked tirelessly to keep Nashville residents safe during Winter Storm
Fern. We extend our deepest appreciation to these heroic Tennesseans, who, in a moment of
great peril, came to the aid and rescue of their fellow citizens.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
