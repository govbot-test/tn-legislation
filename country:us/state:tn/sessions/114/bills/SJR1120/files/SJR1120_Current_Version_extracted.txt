SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1120
By Lowe
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Charles Richard Riggs.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Charles Richard Riggs; and
WHEREAS, Charles Riggs was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, a lifelong resident of the Dayton area, Mr. Riggs was born on August 13,
1960, and was a 1978 graduate of Rhea County High School; and
WHEREAS, in his professional career, Charles Riggs was recognized for his strong work
ethic, often putting in many hours marked by loyalty and selfless dedication; and
WHEREAS, a gentleman of many talents and interests, Mr. Riggs was known for his
love for animals, politics, cooking, and University of Tennessee sports; he enjoyed attending
various community events and was a staunch supporter of law enforcement; and
WHEREAS, Charles Riggs was deeply devoted to his family; he was proud to have
helped raise several children and always endeavored to remain true to family values of the
highest order; and
WHEREAS, left to cherish his memory are his wife, Trish Maines-Riggs; his sister,
Teresa (Bob) Hulgan; his brother, Dean Riggs; and a host of nieces and nephews; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Riggs was preceded in death by his parents, George and Faye Riggs,
and his brother, Kenneth Riggs; and
WHEREAS, Charles Riggs was active in the Pentecostal faith and attended Four-Square
Church; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Riggs leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public
life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the life of Charles Richard Riggs,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to public service.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. Riggs.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
