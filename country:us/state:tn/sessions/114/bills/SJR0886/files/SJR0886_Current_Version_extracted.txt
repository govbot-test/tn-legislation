SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 886
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Alex Forsbach upon
his receipt of the 2026 National Conservation
Legacy Award from the American Soybean
Association.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in recognizing
those Tennessee farmers who, through their stewardship of the land and commitment to sound
conservation practices, strengthen our agricultural heritage and help ensure a sustainable
future; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Alex Forsbach of Savannah, who was presented with
the 2026 National Conservation Legacy Award by the American Soybean Association (ASA)
during the ASA Awards Celebration at Commodity Classic in San Antonio, Texas; and
WHEREAS, in addition to this national distinction, Mr. Forsbach was previously selected
as the 2026 South Region winner of the Conservation Legacy Award, reflecting his leadership in
conservation among soybean producers throughout the region; and
WHEREAS, the Conservation Legacy Award is a national program designed to
recognize the outstanding environmental achievements of soybean farmers that help produce
more sustainable U.S. soybeans; nominees are evaluated by a national selection committee
composed of soybean farmers, conservationists, agronomists, and natural resource
professionals based on their on-farm legacy of conservation practices and outreach efforts in
their local communities; and
WHEREAS, Alex Forsbach grew up working on his family's farm in Savannah, where he
helped raise corn, soybeans, grain sorghum, and wheat; he has noted that this early experience
helped shape his passion for agriculture and laid the foundation for his own farming journey;
and

WHEREAS, while attending Mississippi State University, Mr. Forsbach returned to the
family farm in mid-2008 to assist with the installation of four center pivots, and, following his
graduation in 2009, he began his own farming operation by renting 163 acres and purchasing a
self-propelled sprayer; and
WHEREAS, today, through his Growing Acres farm, located along the Tennessee River
just below Pickwick Dam, Alex Forsbach produces soybeans, grain sorghum, winter wheat, and
occasionally corn, continuing the agricultural tradition that first inspired him; and
WHEREAS, through his work at Growing Acres, Alex Forsbach has demonstrated that
land stewardship and conservation practices can make a meaningful difference in farm
operations, noting a significant increase in soil organic matter over the past ten years, which he
attributes directly to his use of no-till methods and cover crops; and
WHEREAS, given the ups and downs of today's agricultural economy, Mr. Forsbach has
identified diversification as key to managing the risks associated with innovative farming
practices; together with his wife, Katherine, he opened a seed dealership in 2014, a rural
primary care medical clinic in 2019, and invested in and helped found AgLaunch, which enables
farmers to trial new technologies, gain an equity stake in their companies, and bring grain
sorghum into their crop rotation; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Forsbach has emphasized that conservation plays a central role in
creating and carrying out his farming legacy and has demonstrated conservation in action for his
children and farm visitors while engaging others in thoughtful discussion about modern
agriculture; and
WHEREAS, Alex Forsbach's achievements reflect great credit upon him, his family, and
the Tennessee farming community and serve as a model of how innovation and environmental
responsibility can work hand in hand to secure the future of agriculture; and
- 2 - 014135
WHEREAS, Mr. Forsbach is most worthy of our approbation on this special occasion;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Alex Forsbach upon his
receipt of the 2026 National Conservation Legacy Award from the American Soybean
Association and extend to him our best wishes for much continued success in all his future
endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
