SENATE RESOLUTION 421
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor TCAT Crump students on their
successful participation in the 2026 SkillsUSA
Conference.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this legislative body should recognize and
applaud those outstanding students who bring honor to their schools and communities through
their dedication, skill, and academic achievement; and
WHEREAS, students from the Tennessee College of Applied Technology (TCAT) Crump
demonstrated exceptional skill and professionalism at the 2026 SkillsUSA State Leadership and
Skills Conference, held March 15-18, 2026, in Chattanooga; and
WHEREAS, SkillsUSA is a national nonprofit partnership of students, instructors, and
more than 650 corporations, trade associations, businesses, and labor unions that works to
ensure that America has the skilled workforce it needs to stay competitive; founded in 1965 and
endorsed by the United States Department of Education, the association serves more than
400,000 students and instructors each year in middle schools, high schools, and colleges,
focusing on 130 trade, technical, and skilled-service occupations; and
WHEREAS, a total of twenty-nine students from TCAT Crump competed in nineteen
different contests, representing a wide range of technical and career-focused disciplines; their
hard work and preparation resulted in an outstanding performance, bringing home a total of
fourteen medals; and
WHEREAS, the gold medal winners were Connor Joyce of Henderson, Electrical
Construction Wiring; Cora Franks of Savannah, Nurse Assisting; Jacob Durbin of Savannah,
Welding; Skye Moffitt of Bethel Springs and Ben Cromwell of Savannah, Interactive Application
and Video Game Development; Kylee Nolan, Graphic Imaging Sublimation; and Jeff Fienberg of
Walnut, Mississippi, HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning) and Refrigeration; and

WHEREAS, the silver medal winners were Caleb Collings of Jackson, Plumbing; Patrick
Sherrill of Collinwood, Collision Repair Technology; Baylee Cromwell of Savannah, Practical
Nursing; Evan Vander Heyden of Henderson, Plumbing; Daniel Parra of Savannah, Information
Technology Services; and Kayedance V. Seggern of Savannah, Graphic Imaging Sublimation;
and
WHEREAS, the bronze medal winners were Justin Carnell, Collision Repair Technology;
and Abby Kurowski of Morris Chapel, Medical Math; and
WHEREAS, gold medal winners will advance to represent Tennessee at the SkillsUSA
National Leadership and Skills Conference in Atlanta, Georgia, in June 2026; and
WHEREAS, the success of the TCAT Crump SkillsUSA team is a testament to the hard
work and dedication of these students and the commitment and mentorship of Betsy Hill, Career
Coach and Lead SkillsUSA Advisor; and
WHEREAS, the team's accomplishment not only brings pride to TCAT Crump but also
exemplifies the value of career and technical education in preparing Tennessee's students for
success in the workforce and beyond; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and commend the
members of the TCAT Crump SkillsUSA team for their success during the 2026 SkillsUSA State
Leadership and Skills Conference, applauding their outstanding performance and dedication
and extending to them our best wishes for continued success in all their future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
