SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 564
By Campbell
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the beauty and importance of
Nashville's Highland Rim Forest.
WHEREAS, Nashville's Highland Rim Forest is a vast expanse of woodland that cradles
the city, forming a green crescent that is clearly visible from space; and
WHEREAS, more than simply a beautifully verdant backdrop to the city, the Highland
Rim Forest provides critical stormwater management, improves water and air quality, mitigates
urban heat-island effects, and sustains wildlife habitat and biodiversity; and
WHEREAS, Nashville's Highland Rim Forest is part of a larger physiographic region
known as the Western Highland Rim, which the United States Department of Agriculture has
designated as a Level IV ecoregion within Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, a significant portion of the Western Highland Rim encompasses
approximately 127,000 acres of interconnected forest and wildlife habitat on the western flank of
Nashville and Davidson County, forming a contiguous old-growth forested ecosystem
recognized by state and federal agencies, including the Tennessee Department of Environment
and Conservation, the United States Department of Agriculture, and the United States Fish and
Wildlife Service, as well as regional conservation initiatives such as the Southeast Conservation
Adaptation Strategy; and
WHEREAS, the forested Western Highland Rim lies above the Nashville Basin
ecoregion, and a substantial portion of Nashville's metropolitan population resides downstream,
making the highland forest's ecosystem services essential to Davidson County residents' quality
of life; and
WHEREAS, the ecological significance of the Western Highland Rim forest has been
formally recognized by the United States Fish and Wildlife Service and the State of Tennessee

and is reflected in long-range planning documents adopted by the Metropolitan Government of
Nashville and Davidson County; and
WHEREAS, Nashville's designation as the "World's Largest Urban Forest," with over
100,000 acres of tree canopy within city limits, represents a unique natural asset, distinguishing
Tennessee's capital as a leader in urban environmental stewardship; and
WHEREAS, within the vast urban forest are four stunning parks filled with the natural
beauty of the Highland Rim: Beaman Park, Bells Bend Park, Radnor Lake State Park, and
Warner Parks, which include both Percy and Edwin Warner Parks; these parks provide more
than 4,700 acres of public outdoor recreation area with nearly 100 miles of hiking trails; and
WHEREAS, protective policies and land-use practices that preserve and sustainably
manage Nashville's Highland Rim Forest are vital to safeguarding this beautiful, bountiful
ecological region for the benefit of current and future generations; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we commemorate Nashville's Highland Forest, not
only for its inherent beauty but also for its essential contributions to the environmental health,
quality of life, and economic vitality of Middle Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
