SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 596
By Briggs
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Harold Wayne Sellars of
Farragut.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Harold Wayne Sellars; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Sellars was a highly respected member of his community and a
consummate professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow
citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born November 16, 1942, in Erwin, Wayne Sellars was the youngest of
eight children and was raised with strong values of faith, family, and service that guided him
throughout his life; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Sellars honorably served his country as a member of the United States
Air Force from 1964 to 1968, attaining the rank of Staff Sergeant and completing a tour of duty
at Takhli Royal Thai Air Force Base in Thailand during the Vietnam War; and
WHEREAS, following his military service, Wayne Sellars graduated from East
Tennessee State University with a degree in marketing; he went on to build a successful career
as an investment advisor with Nationwide Retirement Solutions, where he served as Area
Director for the Central and East Tennessee regions; and
WHEREAS, deeply committed to civic engagement and community service, Mr. Sellars
was a member of the Concord-Farragut Republican Club and the Optimist Club, a life member
of Vietnam Veterans of America Chapter 1078, a board member of the Teen Board of Knoxville,
and a faithful member of Concord United Methodist Church; and
WHEREAS, Wayne Sellars married his childhood sweetheart, Jeanne Evans, on June
26, 1965; the couple had met in first grade, and together they shared more than sixty years of
marriage marked by faith, devotion, and enduring love; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Sellars found his greatest joy in time spent with his family; he was
involved with his grandson's activities in baseball and martial arts and in their shared love of
University of Tennessee sports, rarely missing a Big Orange football tailgate; and
WHEREAS, Wayne Sellars was deeply devoted to his family, and he always endeavored
to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, left to cherish his memory are his beloved wife, Jeanne Sellars; daughter,
Tracy Sellars (Kaivon) Kiumarsi; and grandson, Kaicy Sellars Kiumarsi; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his parents, John Henon Sellars and Winnie
Sellars; three sisters; and four brothers; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Sellars leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we pause to remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Harold Wayne Sellars,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Sellars.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
