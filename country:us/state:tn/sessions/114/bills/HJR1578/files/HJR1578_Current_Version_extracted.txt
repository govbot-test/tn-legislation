HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1578
By Harris
A RESOLUTION to commend Indian Clay for her service as a
legislative intern.
WHEREAS, each legislative session, this body is pleased to recognize certain persons
who have committed themselves, through their adroit service, to the betterment of the legislative
process; and
WHEREAS, Indian Clay is one such individual who has distinguished herself in her role
as a legislative intern for Representative Torrey C. Harris; Ms. Clay's work has resulted in
innumerable benefits for the legislators she assisted and the General Assembly as a whole; and
WHEREAS, a student at LeMoyne-Owen College, where she is highly regarded by her
peers, Indian Clay is majoring in criminal justice and has compiled an impressive academic
record as a consistent member of the Dean's List; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Clay has aspirations to go into the legal and policy field after her
graduation in May 2026; and
WHEREAS, eager to familiarize herself with the characteristics and complexities of
Tennessee State government and to further her studies, Indian Clay brought her many abilities
and singular expertise to Capitol Hill for the 2026 session of the One Hundred Fourteenth
General Assembly; and
WHEREAS, through her service, Ms. Clay has proven herself to be an integral part of
the success of the office she served and a valuable contributor to the work of the General
Assembly on behalf of the citizens of House District 91; and
WHEREAS, Indian Clay has performed her many duties with extraordinary insight and
rare decorum; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
