SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 829
By Walley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Wayne Alexander
Maxedon of Selmer.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Wayne Alexander Maxedon of Selmer; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Maxedon was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Wayne Maxedon was born on July 23, 1948, in Oakland, California, to Pitts
Jones Maxedon and Dorothy Marie Counce Maxedon; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Maxedon invested his heart in the next generation as the longtime
basketball coach at Selmer Middle School; beyond teaching the game, he taught discipline,
respect, resilience, and confidence; and
WHEREAS, Wayne Maxedon also spent many seasons working at DDY camp for
children, where he helped create lasting memories; and
WHEREAS, a devoted Tennessee Volunteers fan, Mr. Maxedon rarely missed a football
or basketball game; he also enjoyed golfing and talking sports with his friends, always ready to
relive a great play or memorable season; and
WHEREAS, Wayne Maxedon was a proud member of the PIKE fraternity at the
University of Tennessee at Martin, where he formed lifelong friendships; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Maxedon never met a stranger, and his humor and warmth filled every
room he entered; known as a "cut-up," he was quick with a joke and had a gift for making others
smile; and

WHEREAS, above all else, Mr. Maxedon treasured the love of his family; his daughters
were his greatest pride, and his grandsons were the light of his life. He loved nothing more than
spending time with his grandsons, whether cheering them on from the sidelines or sitting and
sharing stories with them; and
WHEREAS, Wanye Maxedon will be sadly mourned but fondly remembered by his
daughters, Bree (Corey) Barry and Brandice (Trenton) Searcy; grandsons, Jaxon Searcy,
Beckett Barry, and Hudson Searcy; sister, Terrie Kerby; nieces, Tammy (Todd) Moorman, Jill
(Jimmy) Gray, and Julie (Tom) Turner; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his brother-in-law,
Gary Kerby; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Maxedon leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should remember the
bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Wayne Alexander
Maxedon, reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living
the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Mr. Maxedon.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
