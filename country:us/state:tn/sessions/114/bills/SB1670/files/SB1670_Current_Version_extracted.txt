By Lankford
SENATE BILL 1670
By Powers
AN ACT to amend Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 2;
Title 6; Title 8; Title 36; Title 39; Title 40; Title 49;
Title 55; Title 58; Title 62; Title 63 and Title 66,
relative to armed forces.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE:

Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 2-1-104(a)(1), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(1) "Armed forces personnel" means members of the army, navy, air force,
space force, marine corps, coast guard, environmental science services administration,
and public health service of the United States or members of the merchant marine of the
United States and their spouses and dependents;

Section 3:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 6-54-143(b)(1), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(1) The active and reserve components of the army, navy, air force, space force,
marine corps, or coast guard of the United States;

Section 4:
SECTION 3. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 8-18-101(4), is amended by deleting
the subdivision and substituting instead:
(4) Soldiers, sailors, marines, airmen, or guardians in the regular army, navy, air
force, or space force of the United States; and

Section 5:
SECTION 4. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 8-30-307(a), is amended by deleting
the subsection and substituting instead:
(a) When invitations to interview candidates are extended, whether for
appointment or promotion, and the pool of candidates includes any person who has
been honorably discharged from the army, navy, air force, space force, marine corps, or

who performs active federal service in the armed forces of the United States, these
persons must be invited to interview.

deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(A) Active and reserve components of the army, navy, air force, space force,
marine corps, or coast guard of the United States;

Section 9:
SECTION 7. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 39-16-301(e)(1), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(1) Active and reserve components of the army, navy, air force, space force,
marine corps, or coast guard of the United States;

Section 10:
SECTION 8. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 39-17-1302(b)(1), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(1) Was incident to the performance of official duty and pursuant to military
regulations in the army, navy, air force, space force, coast guard, or marine service of
the United States or the Tennessee national guard, or was incident to the performance
of official duty in a governmental law enforcement agency or a penal institution;

Section 11:
SECTION 9. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 39-17-1306(c)(1), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:

employed in the army, air force, space force, navy, coast guard, or marine service of the
United States or any member of the Tennessee national guard in the line of duty and
pursuant to military regulations, or is in the actual discharge of official duties as a guard
employed by a penal institution, or as a bailiff, marshal, or other court officer who has
responsibility for protecting persons or property or providing security;

Section 12:
SECTION 10. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 39-17-1309(e)(1), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(1) Persons employed in the army, air force, space force, navy, coast guard, or
marine service of the United States or any member of the Tennessee national guard
when in discharge of their official duties and acting under orders requiring them to carry
arms or weapons;

Section 13:
SECTION 11. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 39-17-1311(b)(1)(A), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(A) Persons employed in the army, air force, space force, navy, coast guard, or
marine service of the United States or any member of the Tennessee national guard
when in discharge of their official duties and acting under orders requiring them to carry
arms or weapons;

Section 14:
SECTION 12. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 40-16-101, is amended by deleting
the second sentence and substituting instead:
"Members of the armed forces of the United States" includes all officers and
enlisted personnel of the army, navy, air force, space force, marine corps, and
coast guard while on active duty and also includes any and all persons subject to
trial by court-martial under the Uniform Code of Military Justice, whether a
civilian, reservist, or member of any other federal agency.
- 3 - 010561

Section 15:
SECTION 13. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 49-12-301, is amended by deleting
the language:
R. "Uniformed service(s)" means the army, navy, air force, marine corps, coast
guard as well as the commissioned corps of the national oceanic and
atmospheric administration, and public health services.
and substituting instead:
R. "Uniformed service(s)" means the army, navy, air force, space force, marine
corps, coast guard, as well as the commissioned corps of the national oceanic
and atmospheric administration, and public health services.

Section 16:
SECTION 14. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 55-4-120(a)(1)(C), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(C) An owner or operator who operates in this state shall not be entitled to
reciprocity for a period in excess of thirty (30) days; provided, that any personnel of the
army, navy, marine corps, air force, or space force stationed in this state, whose motor
vehicle is properly registered in that person's state of domicile, will not be required to
reregister the vehicle in this state.

Section 17:
SECTION 15. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 58-1-102(2), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(2) "Active military service of the United States" means full-time duty in the army,
navy (including marine corps), air force, space force, or coast guard of the United States;

Section 18:
SECTION 16. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 58-1-217(b), is amended by
deleting the subsection and substituting instead:
(b) Enlistment in the regular army, air force, space force, navy, marine corps, or
coast guard of the United States shall be deemed a resignation by the person so
enlisted of all commissions held in the military forces of this state.
- 4 - 010561

Section 19:
SECTION 17. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 62-3-108(2), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(2) Commissioned medical or surgical officers of the United States army, navy,
air force, space force, or marine hospital service;

Section 20:
SECTION 18. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 62-76-106(a)(1), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(1) "Honorably discharged veteran" means any person who has been honorably
discharged from the army, navy, air force, space force, marine corps, or coast guard, or
any person who has been honorably discharged from a reserve component as defined in
10 U.S.C. § 10101, having performed active federal service in the armed forces of the
United States;

Section 21:
SECTION 19. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-1-110(a), is amended by
deleting the subsection and substituting instead:
(a) Any person licensed to practice a healing art or any branch thereof in this
state who is an officer in the commissioned medical corps of the army, the navy, the air
force, the space force, or the public health service of the United States shall not be
required to register as provided in this chapter.

Section 22:
SECTION 20. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-3-108, is amended by deleting
the section and substituting instead:
This chapter shall not apply to the commissioned physicians and surgeons of the
United States army, navy, air force, space force, or marine hospital service in the actual
performance of their official duties, nor to physicians and surgeons or to osteopathic
physicians and surgeons regularly licensed under the laws of this state, nor to visiting
podiatrists called into consultation in this state from another state where they are duly
qualified under the laws of that state to practice podiatry.
- 5 - 010561

Section 23:
SECTION 21. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-5-109(2), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(2) The practice of dentistry and dental hygiene in the discharge of their official
duties by graduate dentists and by dental hygienists in the United States public health
service, army, navy, air force, space force, coast guard, or veterans administration;

Section 24:
SECTION 22. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-5-130, is amended by deleting
the first and second sentences and substituting instead:
Any person licensed to practice dentistry or dental hygiene in this state who is an
officer in the commissioned dental corps of the army, navy, air force, space force,
or the public health service of the United States shall not be required to register
as required in this chapter. Such person shall file with the board an affidavit on a
form to be furnished by the board, which affidavit shall state the date on which
such person entered the army, navy, air force, space force, or the public health
service of the United States and such other facts as tend to verify such service
as the board shall deem necessary.

Section 25:
SECTION 23. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 63-6-204(a)(3), is amended by
deleting the first sentence and substituting instead:
This chapter shall not apply to surgeons of the United States army, navy, air
force, space force, or marine hospital service regardless of the hospital or
practice site; provided, that the surgeon's practice is part of the surgeon's
authorized military service or training.

Section 26:
SECTION 24. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 66-24-101(a)(20), is amended by
deleting the subdivision and substituting instead:
(20) Discharges of soldiers, sailors, marines, airmen, guardians, and naval and
army officers of the United States. County registers are required, upon application of
- 6 - 010561
lawful holders thereof, to register such discharges without charge to the person named
in the discharge, or to the holder of such discharge. Certified copies of such registered
discharges issued by the register shall be legal evidence of such discharge;

Section 27:
SECTION 25. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
