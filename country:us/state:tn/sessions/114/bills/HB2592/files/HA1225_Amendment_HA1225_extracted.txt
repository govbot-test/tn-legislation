Amendment No. 4 to HB2592
Boyd
Signature of Sponsor
AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 2102* House Bill No. 2592
by deleting § 7-70-102(a) in Section 1 and substituting:
(a)
(1) Notwithstanding another law to the contrary, and except as provided by
subdivision (a)(2) and § 7-70-107:
(A) If an electric utility provides services outside the corporate limits of
the home municipality but within the home county to not less than three thousand
five hundred (3,500) customers and not more than one hundred thirty thousand
(130,000) customers, then the home municipality must expand the utility board to
include one (1) additional voting board member on the utility board for the home
county, appointed by an incorporated city or town in accordance with § 7-70-
103(a)(1), if the home county chooses to appoint a person pursuant to § 7-70-
103(a)(1); and
(B) If an electric utility provides services outside the corporate limits of
the home municipality but within the home county to more than one hundred
thirty thousand (130,000) customers, then the home municipality must expand
the utility board to include one (1) more additional voting board member on the
utility board for the home county, appointed by an incorporated city or town in
accordance with § 7-70-103(a)(2), if the home county chooses to appoint such
persons pursuant to § 7-70-103(a)(2).

(A) If a utility board that would require expansion under subdivision
(a)(1)(A) or (B) includes:
(i) One (1) voting board member who is a resident of the home
county and resides outside the corporate limits of the home municipality
as of May 1, 2026, such board member satisfies the requirements of
subdivision (a)(1)(A), regardless of how such board member is appointed;
and
(ii) Not less than two (2) voting board members who are residents
of the home county and reside outside the corporate limits of the home
municipality as of May 1, 2026, such board members satisfy the
requirements of subdivisions (a)(1)(A) and (B), regardless of how such
board members are appointed.
(B) If a voting board member position described under subdivision
(a)(2)(A)(i) or (ii) ceases to exist, then the home municipality must comply with
the requirements of subdivision (a)(1).
