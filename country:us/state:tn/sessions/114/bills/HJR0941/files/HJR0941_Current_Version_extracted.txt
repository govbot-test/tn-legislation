HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 941
By Travis
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Ola Cecil Harris of
Dayton.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Ola Cecil Harris of Dayton; and
WHEREAS, a highly respected member of the Rhea County Sheriff's Department, Ola
Harris was an exemplary public servant and consummate professional who worked assiduously
to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Harris was born on May 5, 1926, and was a lifelong resident of Dayton,
where he made countless contributions to his community; and
WHEREAS, Ola Harris served the Rhea County Sheriff's Office as deputy in the 1950s
before being elected as sheriff and serving for three consecutive terms between 1968 and 1974,
during which tenure he gained notoriety for busting numerous illegal moonshine operations in
his county; he later served as chief deputy under Sheriff Paul Smith; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Harris possessed a strong passion for trucking and worked for Harrison
Trucking for sixteen years; he later continued his trucking career with Pero Farms, where he
took significant pride in his work as well as the peppers grown on his land; and
WHEREAS, Ola Harris was also deeply devoted to his faith and was a member of The
Grove Church; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Harris leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in public
life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all his chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
remember the bountiful life of this exceptional public servant and human being; now, therefore,
HJR0941
013117
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Ola Cecil Harris, reflecting fondly upon his
impeccable character, his extraordinary contributions to the people of Tennessee, and his
stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. Harris.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
