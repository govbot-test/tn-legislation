HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1102
By Pearson
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend Joe Calhoun, Operations
Manager of the Ernest Withers Collection
Museum & Gallery.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to recognize those
exemplary citizens whose professional achievements and civic contributions have enriched the
cultural and historical fabric of the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Joe Calhoun serves with distinction as Operations Manager of the Ernest
Withers Collection Museum & Gallery in Memphis, an institution dedicated to preserving and
sharing one of the most significant photographic archives documenting the Civil Rights
Movement and the cultural history of the American South; and
WHEREAS, the Ernest Withers Collection Museum & Gallery houses the extraordinary
photographic work of the late Dr. Ernest C. Withers, whose camera documented pivotal
moments in American history, including the Civil Rights Movement, the Memphis sanitation
workers' strike, the life of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., and the vibrant cultural life of Memphis;
and
WHEREAS, through his leadership and commitment, Joe Calhoun has played a vital role
in the daily operations, programming, and stewardship of this historic institution, ensuring that
the powerful stories captured through the lens of Ernest Withers remain accessible to students,
scholars, residents, and visitors from around the world; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Calhoun's dedication to historic preservation and public education helps
ensure that future generations may learn from the struggles, triumphs, and cultural contributions
that have shaped the city of Memphis and the nation; and

WHEREAS, through his professionalism, passion for history, and unwavering
commitment to community engagement, Joe Calhoun exemplifies the very best of public service
and cultural stewardship; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly recognize Mr. Calhoun for his
meaningful contributions to preserving the historical narrative of Memphis and honoring the
legacy of one of Tennessee's most important chroniclers of history; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Joe Calhoun, Operations Manager of the Ernest
Withers Collection Museum & Gallery, for his outstanding service, dedication to historic
preservation, and commitment to sharing the rich cultural legacy of Memphis.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
