HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 966
By Hill
A RESOLUTION to commemorate March 18, 2026, as Dutch Day
in Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly hold immense esteem for this
State's rich cultural diversity and the vibrant contributions made by the Dutch community; and
WHEREAS, the Dutch community has played a significant role in the cultural, social, and
economic development of our great State; and
WHEREAS, Dutch Day at the state capitol serves as an opportunity to celebrate the
heritage, traditions, and contributions of the Dutch in Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, this event fosters cultural understanding and unity among all Tennesseans,
promoting respect and appreciation for the diverse backgrounds that enrich our State's fabric;
and
WHEREAS, the celebration of Dutch Day encourages community engagement,
education, and the sharing of cultural practices that enhance the lives of all citizens; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we join in celebrating this day and the contributions of the
Dutch community to our state; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we join with citizens throughout the State in commemorating Dutch Day in
Tennessee on March 18, 2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
