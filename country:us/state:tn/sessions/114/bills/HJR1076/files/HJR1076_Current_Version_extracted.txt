HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1076
By Russell
A RESOLUTION to commend and congratulate Major Stacey
Heatherly on the occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those estimable public servants who have performed with uncommon devotion and
enthusiasm throughout their careers in law enforcement; and
WHEREAS, Major Stacey Heatherly is one such admirable individual, who evinces the
greatest integrity and probity in all her chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, Major Heatherly has estimably served as East Bureau Major of Field
Operations with the Tennessee Highway Patrol, from which position she is taking her well-
deserved retirement; and
WHEREAS, the daughter of Burgin and Jo Wood, Major Heatherly previously served as
Campbell County E-911 Coordinator and Director from 1993 to 1998; and
WHEREAS, Major Stacey Heatherly has brought honor to her office of East Bureau
Major, a most fitting station that recognized her devotion to the safety and preservation of her
community; and
WHEREAS, Major Heatherly exemplifies the values of hard work, compassion, and
devotion to community that are characteristic of a true Tennessean and diligent officer of the
law; and
WHEREAS, throughout her many worthy endeavors, Major Stacey Heatherly has
enjoyed the love and support of her husband, Trooper Michael Heatherly, who has served since
2008; he is retired from the Army National Guard as a Staff Sergeant with the 278th and 1175th
regiments, having served for twenty years, including two deployments in Iraq; and

WHEREAS, she has also been blessed with the love of her children, Sarah (Robert)
Massengill, John (Cheryl) Heatherly, Jr., Meagan Heatherly, and Joshua Heatherly, as well as
her grandchildren, Jonathon Carroll, Taylor Massengill, Kinsley Carroll, Macy Massengill, Mac
Henry Massengill, and Arabella Heatherly, and her great-granddaughter, Stetson Massengill;
and
WHEREAS, Major Heatherly has demonstrated the utmost professionalism, ability, and
integrity, winning the respect and admiration of her colleagues and the citizens of this State; and
WHEREAS, it is our privilege to honor her life of service on this noteworthy occasion;
now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we commend Major Stacey Heatherly for her honorable and astute service
to the people of Tennessee as East Bureau Major of Field Operations with the Tennessee
Highway Patrol, salute her unswerving commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction, and extend to her our heartfelt wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement and every
continued success in her future endeavors.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
