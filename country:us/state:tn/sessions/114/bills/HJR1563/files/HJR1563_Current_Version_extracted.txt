HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1563
By Carringer
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend M. Kevin Knowles upon
his retirement as Director of East Tennessee
State Veterans Cemeteries.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those citizens who have performed with uncommon devotion and enthusiasm
throughout their years of public service; and
WHEREAS, M. Kevin Knowles is one such estimable person, who is taking a well-
earned retirement as Director of the East Tennessee State Veterans Cemeteries after years of
dedicated service to the State of Tennessee and its veterans; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Knowles has more than four decades of experience in cemetery and
funeral service, demonstrating exceptional leadership, professionalism, and a steadfast
commitment to the families and communities he has served; and
WHEREAS, since 2016, he has served as Director of East Tennessee State Veterans
Cemeteries in Knoxville, overseeing the daily operations of multiple facilities, supervising staff,
and ensuring the dignified interment of those who have honorably served in the United States
Armed Forces; and
WHEREAS, in this role, Kevin Knowles has directed the administration of more than one
thousand pre-registration applications and hundreds of interments annually, working in
conjunction with the United States Department of Veterans Affairs National Cemetery
Administration to ensure compliance with national standards; and
WHEREAS, under his leadership, the East Tennessee State Veterans Cemeteries
received the National Cemetery Administration's Operational Excellence Award in 2023, a
prestigious honor awarded to only a select few cemeteries nationwide in recognition of
outstanding performance; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Knowles has held numerous leadership positions in the funeral and
cemetery profession, including roles with Service Corporation International and Stewart
Enterprises, as well as service as a senior licensed funeral director and embalmer; and
WHEREAS, Kevin Knowles has also demonstrated a commitment to professional
development and public service through his licensure and certifications, including his work as a
certified disaster casualty responder and his participation in leadership programs such as the
Tennessee Government Management Institute and LEAD Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his professional accomplishments, he has been an active
member of his community, serving as a volunteer with the Knoxville Area Rescue Ministry and
as a deacon at New Providence Presbyterian Church; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Knowles is most grateful for the love and support he shares with his
wife, Lauryi, their three children and three granddaughters; and
WHEREAS, a public-spirited citizen of the highest order, Kevin Knowles has compiled a
singular record of success during his exemplary career; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Knowles is wholly committed to the noble precepts of public service that
have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and he should be specially
recognized; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend M. Kevin Knowles for his honorable and astute
service to the people of Tennessee, particularly the veterans of this State, and extend to him our
best wishes for a happy and fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
