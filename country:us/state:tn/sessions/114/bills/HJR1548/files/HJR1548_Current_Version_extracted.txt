HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1548
By Pearson
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend CodeCrew of Memphis
for its transformative work in advancing
computer science education and expanding
opportunity for young people and communities
across the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, CodeCrew, founded in 2015 in Memphis and still going strong in 2026, has
emerged as a leading nonprofit organization dedicated to equipping students and adults with
critical computer science and technology skills; and
WHEREAS, CodeCrew has taught more than 15,000 students to code through
innovative and accessible programming, including in-school electives, after-school and weekend
programs, summer camps, internships, and workforce development initiatives; and
WHEREAS, the organization's mission is rooted in equity and empowerment, with a
strong focus on reaching those historically underrepresented in the technology sector, including
Black and Latino youth and young women, ensuring broader access to opportunity in a rapidly
evolving digital economy; and
WHEREAS, CodeCrew provides hands-on instruction in mobile and web app
development, game design, robotics, drone programming, and artificial intelligence, preparing
participants not merely to consume technology, but to create and lead in the field of innovation;
and
WHEREAS, through its Code School, CodeCrew has extended its impact to adults,
offering intensive training programs that equip participants with real-world software development
skills and pathways to meaningful employment, thereby strengthening families and communities
across Tennessee; and

WHEREAS, CodeCrew's work is building a more resilient workforce, fostering creativity,
critical thinking, and economic mobility, and ensuring that Tennessee remains competitive in the
21st-century economy; and
WHEREAS, the organization stands as a beacon of innovation, opportunity, and justice,
demonstrating what is possible when communities invest in the brilliance and potential of their
young people; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that we specially recognize the leadership, staff, educators,
mentors, and students of CodeCrew for their unwavering commitment to building a more just,
innovative, and inclusive future; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend CodeCrew for its outstanding contributions to
education, workforce development, and community empowerment, and celebrate its continuing
impact on the lives of thousands of Tennesseans.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
