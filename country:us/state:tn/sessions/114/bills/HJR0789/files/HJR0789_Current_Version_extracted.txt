HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 789
By Todd
A RESOLUTION to recognize Arthur D. Johnson, Jr., for his
honorable and astute service to the people of
Madison County and the State of Tennessee.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that the members of this General Assembly should specially
recognize those estimable public servants who have dedicated themselves to improving the
quality of life for their fellow citizens; and
WHEREAS, Arthur D. Johnson, Jr., is one such estimable person, who has
demonstrated the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity as a public servant in Madison
County for sixty-five years; and
WHEREAS, Commissioner Arthur D. Johnson, Jr., was born on July 19, 1937, in
Malesus and grew up in the same home where his father was born; he attended Malesus
grammar school and graduated from Malesus High School in 1955; and
WHEREAS, Commissioner Johnson is a third-generation, lifelong farmer in Madison
County, growing cotton, corn, soybeans, and wheat. He married Sarah Maddox in 1967 and
raised his two children, Arthur "Chip" D. Johnson III and Jill Johnson in Madison County; and
WHEREAS, he joined the Tennessee Army National Guard, 30th Division, 230th Signal
Company in 1955 and served until 1963, earning the rank of Sergeant E-5; he earned the
Marksmanship Badge Carbine Expert, signifying the highest level of skill using a carbine rifle;
and
WHEREAS, Commissioner Arthur Johnson took an interest in public service at a young
age and ran for the Madison County Quarterly Court in 1960; he began his representation of the
Malesus and South Jackson area as a member of the Madison County Quarterly Court, now the
Madison County Board of Commissioners, on September 1, 1960; and

WHEREAS, the longest-serving county commissioner in the history of the State of
Tennessee, Commissioner Johnson has now served Madison County for over sixty-five years;
and
WHEREAS, during his tenure as a member of the Madison County Quarterly Court and
the Madison County Board of Commissioners, Commissioner Johnson has served on almost
every committee, including a tenure as chair of the Financial Management Committee; and
WHEREAS, an active part of his community, he has served as Deacon and Sunday
School Director at Malesus Baptist Church, director and vice president of the Madison County
Farm Bureau, vice president of the Malesus Men's Civic Club, and co-founder of the Madison
South Jaycees Club with his brother-in-law, Joe Nip McKnight; and
WHEREAS, Commissioner Johnson has served as district and state director for the
Jaycees, been a member of the Madison County Co-Op board of directors, and recognized as
the Young Farmer of the Year by the Junior Chamber of Commerce; and
WHEREAS, Arthur Johnson has proven himself to be wholly committed to the noble
precepts of public service that have earned Tennessee recognition as the Volunteer State, and
he should be specially recognized for his many years of honorable and astute service to the
citizens of Madison County; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor and commend Arthur D. Johnson, Jr., on his sixty-five
years of dedicated public service to the people of Madison County and the State of Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
