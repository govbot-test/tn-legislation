HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1090
By Atchley
A RESOLUTION to recognize and honor Clinical Director of Safe
Harbor Child Advocacy Center Rose Gainey on
the occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that the members of this General Assembly should
acknowledge and applaud those community leaders who are wholly devoted to enhancing the
quality of life for their fellow citizens and improving and enriching the place they all call home;
and
WHEREAS, one such noteworthy person is Rose Gainey, LPC, of Seymour, who has
distinguished herself as a citizen of the greatest dedication, ability, and integrity; and
WHEREAS, after nearly fifteen years as Clinical Director of Safe Harbor Child Advocacy
Center, Rose Gainey is taking her much-deserved retirement, bringing to a close an
extraordinary tenure of guiding Safe Harbor's therapeutic care with unwavering compassion,
profound skill, and inspiring leadership; and
WHEREAS, born on August 16, 1959, in Indianapolis, Indiana, Mrs. Gainey was raised
in Indianapolis and in Northern Kentucky alongside her five siblings; after graduating from Ben
Davis High School in 1977, she earned her degree in business administration from Indiana
University-Indianapolis School of Business; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Gainey's journey with Safe Harbor began in 2011, when she came on
board as a therapy intern and first demonstrated her unique ability to connect with and nurture
young people; she graduated from Johnson University in 2012 with a Master of Arts degree in
Marriage and Family Therapy and obtained her LPC/MHSP and LMFT licensure in the State of
Tennessee; and

WHEREAS, she was hired by Safe Harbor Child Advocacy Center as a therapist on May
7, 2012, and since that time, she has served children throughout Sevier, Cocke, Grainger, and
Jefferson counties with the utmost care and compassion; and
WHEREAS, Mrs. Gainey's exceptional dedication, skill, and wisdom soon propelled her
to the role of Clinical Director of the Therapy Program, in which capacity she provides expert
guidance and supervision and has grown her team of therapists from one to five members,
ensuring her service area has access to dedicated child and family advocates; and
WHEREAS, Rose Gainey and her team provide trauma-focused therapy services that
help ensure that child and adolescent survivors of abuse are not defined by their trauma, but
empowered to find hope, strength, and true healing; and
WHEREAS, during her time with Safe Harbor, Mrs. Gainey has conducted or facilitated
over 23,000 therapy sessions, positively impacting the lives of countless young people and their
families; and
WHEREAS, her legacy is one of loyalty, kindness, humility and compassion, and her
indelible contributions to Safe Harbor and the region she served will continue to inspire others
for years to come; and
WHEREAS, throughout her many worthy endeavors, Mrs. Gainey has enjoyed the love
and support of her husband of over forty years, Wes Gainey; their children, Megan, Kristen, and
Matt; and grandchildren, Olivia Rose, James Ezekiel, and Nora Rose; and
WHEREAS, Rose Gainey epitomizes the spirit and commitment that are characteristic of a true
Tennessean; and
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly find it appropriate to acknowledge
and applaud Mrs. Gainey for her compassionate service and dedication to the children of the
State of Tennessee; now, therefore,
- 2 - 015046
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we recognize and honor Clinical Director of Safe Harbor Child Advocacy
Center Rose Gainey and extend to her our best wishes for a happy, fulfilling retirement.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
- 3 - 015046
