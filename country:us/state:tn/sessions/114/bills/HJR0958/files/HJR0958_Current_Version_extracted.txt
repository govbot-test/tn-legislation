HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 958
By Salinas
A RESOLUTION to honor Kobee Minor of the New England
Patriots.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to recognize a
University of Memphis alumnus who reached the acme of professional football as a member of
the New England Patriots; and
WHEREAS, a highly skilled athlete, Kobee Minor played for the University of Memphis
for one season, thrilling Tiger fans with his success on the gridiron as a tenacious cornerback;
and
WHEREAS, in his lone season for the Memphis Tigers, Mr. Minor played in 11 games
and registered 38 tackles, 2 sacks, 6 passes defensed, and 2 forced fumbles; his strip sack of
Middle Tennessee State University quarterback Nicholas Vattiato helped lead to the way to a
24-7 win; and
WHEREAS, in the 2025 NFL Draft, Kobee Minor was drafted by the New England
Patriots in the seventh round as the 257th overall pick; he was released on August 26, 2025, but
was immediately signed to the practice squad; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Minor was elevated to the active roster for four games during the
regular season, seeing action on special teams units in games against the Miami Dolphins, the
Pittsburgh Steelers, and the New York Jets; and
WHEREAS, the 2025 New England Patriots posted an excellent 14-3 regular season
record, winning the AFC East for the first time since 2019, and then won three playoff games to
reach Super Bowl LX; and
WHEREAS, Kobee Minor has reached the very pinnacle of his profession, and he is
most worthy of our approbation; now, therefore,
HJR0958
013180
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate University of Memphis standout Kobee Minor,
now a member of the New England Patriots, upon his team reaching Super Bowl LX and extend
to him our best wishes for much continued success on the gridiron and in life.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
