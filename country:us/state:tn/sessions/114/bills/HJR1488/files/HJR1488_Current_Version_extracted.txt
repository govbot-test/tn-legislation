HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1488
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Billie "Bill" Gray Speck of
Lafayette.
WHEREAS, it was with great sadness that the members of this General Assembly
learned of the passing of Billie "Bill" Gray Speck, who will be fondly remembered as a devoted
public servant, an extraordinary role model, and a beloved family man; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Speck was a humble, generous man who easily connected with people,
and his commitment to Macon County and his fellow citizens knew no bounds; and
WHEREAS, a native of Livingston, Mr. Speck moved to Lafayette in 1960 to take charge
of WEEN Radio, which he owned and operated for forty-one years; and
WHEREAS, he used the station's prominence in the community to assist people in need,
often hosting on-air benefits; and
WHEREAS, in June 2021, he was inducted into the Sports Hall Fame for his work in
broadcasting local sports on WEEN for many years; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Speck gave back to his beloved Macon County by coaching local sports
teams and serving in many other civic and charitable capacities too numerous to enumerate;
and
WHEREAS, a building at the local fairgrounds was named in his honor to commemorate
his thirty years of dedicated service to the Macon County Fair; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Speck had served as the Grand Marshal of the Lafayette Christmas
Parade, an honor that reflects the high regard in which he was held by his friends and
neighbors; and
WHEREAS, he was honored as the first recipient of the Will Hall Sullivan Award given by
North Central Telephone Company; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Speck was an active member of Lafayette First Baptist Church, where
he had been a deacon since 1960; and
WHEREAS, Bill Speck was "the rock of his family," and he loved his children and
grandchildren dearly; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Speck is survived by his daughter, Stacye Collins, and her husband,
Tim; granddaughter, Brooke Oglesby, and her husband, John; grandson, Trenton Collins, and
his companion, Jenna Meador; great-grandson, Tatum Gray Oglesby; brother, Grady Speck,
and his wife, Doris; brothers-in-law, Jim Ray and his wife, Jane, and Robert Ray and his wife,
Carol; and several nieces and nephews; and
WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his wife, Elizabeth "Liz" Ray Speck; son,
Steve Speck; parents, Joe May Speck and Janie Ledbetter Speck; sister, Ann Norris; brothers,
Othel, Estes, Odis, and Willie Pierce Speck; and sister-in-law, Doris Milligan, and her husband,
Richard; and
WHEREAS, through kindness and compassion, Bill Speck earned the admiration of
everyone who was fortunate to know him, and we wish to pay our respects to this remarkable
gentleman; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Billie "Bill" Gray Speck of Lafayette, fondly
remembering his life of purpose and commitment to Macon County.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we extend our condolences to Mr. Speck's family
and many friends.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
