HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1315
By Reneau
A RESOLUTION to honor and commend our Tennessee veterans.
WHEREAS, our nation was conceived by individuals who were willing to sacrifice their
personal safety and concerns to ensure our individual and collective freedom; and
WHEREAS, the Volunteer State is especially proud to be the home of so many valiant
men and women who have, throughout the years, entered military service to defend this great
nation; and
WHEREAS, veterans represent a distinct and honored segment of our population—
individuals who have voluntarily stepped forward to serve our country. Their status is not
incidental or assigned; it is earned through sacrifice, service, and commitment; and
WHEREAS, the State of Tennessee knows that veterans are a uniquely deserving group
of persons whose service has directly contributed to the protection of our freedoms and way of
life; and
WHEREAS, veteran status is earned, not an inherent characteristic, and acknowledged
accordingly in state policy discussions as veterans deserve our respect, recognition, and fair
consideration in public life, including employment opportunities; and
WHEREAS, veterans represent a very small portion of the population—approximately 1
percent—who raise their hand to serve, often at great personal cost. Their service often
includes sacrifice of time with family, career opportunities, personal safety, and, in some cases,
long-term health; and
WHEREAS, military service is not borne by the individual alone but by the entire family
and especially spouses of service members, including active-duty spouses, who make ongoing
sacrifices through frequent relocations, career disruption, and the demands of military life; and

WHEREAS, we should pay special homage to veteran spouses, particularly those Gold
Star spouses who have lost a service member in the line of duty, whose sacrifice is permanent
and enduring, and spouses of veterans who have a 100 percent service-connected disability,
many of whom serve as caregivers and continue to bear the weight of that service long after it
ends; and
WHEREAS, the causes of peace and freedom have been upheld by these valiant
individuals among us, no longer in uniform, and we should all be inspired to dedicate ourselves
to the ideals that our fellow citizens served and sacrificed to protect; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we hereby honor our Tennessee veterans for their service, extending our
gratitude for the contributions they made to further freedom and the future of the United States
of America, for they are deserving of the full support of all citizens.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
