SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1114
By Southerland
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Youth Builders of
Greeneville upon the occasion of its eightieth
anniversary.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to recognize those
exemplary organizations that are dedicated to improving the quality of life for the people of
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, one such organization is Youth Builders of Greeneville, which recently
celebrated its eightieth anniversary of uplifting children and families throughout the Greeneville
community; and
WHEREAS, established in 1946 to build a new accredited high school in Greeneville,
Youth Builders has remained steadfast in its mission to promote the well-being of children and
youth at home, in school, in places of worship, and across the community, by delivering vital
services both independently and in collaboration with other organizations; and
WHEREAS, since its inception, Youth Builders has grown into a volunteer-powered
organization that supports local children and youth through a wide array of programs and
services, including scholarships for local graduates and child safety initiatives; and
WHEREAS, each year, Youth Builders awards three college scholarships to graduating
seniors, furthering its commitment to educational opportunities and the success of the overall
community; and
WHEREAS, Youth Builders plans to partner with CANUP (Coalition Addressing the
Needs of Unhoused People), a local nonprofit serving individuals experiencing homelessness;
this collaboration will help at-risk youth participate in activities such as soccer camp, ballet,
music, and other art enrichment activities they might not regularly have access to; and

WHEREAS, another noteworthy initiative is KidPrint ID, a child safety program that offers
families of all preschoolers in the city and county valuable identification information free of
charge; in the past decade, the program has provided thousands of families and individuals with
identification cards; and
WHEREAS, Youth Builders of Greeneville has been a cornerstone of support for eight
decades, empowering children and their families through transformative services; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate that we specially recognize Youth Builders of Greeneville,
commending the organization's eighty years of bringing hope and support to children and
families in the Greeneville community; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Youth Builders of
Greeneville on the occasion of the organization's eightieth anniversary, extend our deepest
gratitude for its invaluable contributions to the Greeneville community, and offer our best wishes
for every future success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
