HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1030
By Bricken
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Tullahoma High
School students on winning three national
awards at the FBLA National Leadership
Conference.
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should pay tribute to those students who, through their
natural talents and unflagging commitment to academic excellence, have distinguished
themselves in the classroom and beyond; and
WHEREAS, three students from Tullahoma High School recently won national awards at
the Future Business Leaders of America (FBLA) National Leadership Conference in Anaheim,
California, an extraordinarily rare accomplishment for any school, as winning even one national
award is a remarkable achievement; and
WHEREAS, FBLA is a nonprofit educational organization whose first chapter was
established in Johnson City in 1942; the organization has since grown to encompass more than
250,000 members and advisors nationwide; and
WHEREAS, FBLA inspires and prepares students to become community-minded
business leaders in a global society through relevant career preparation and leadership
experiences; and
WHEREAS, Tullahoma's Edward Henry and Jonah Vallejos were recognized as national
winners in the LifeSmarts Challenge, while their teammate, Thomas Cunningham, earned the
title of national winner in the Electronic Career Portfolio event; and
WHEREAS, these young men's achievements reflect months of rigorous preparation,
academic commitment, and leadership development; and
WHEREAS, these students first competed at the State Leadership Conference, at which
they placed first in Tennessee by besting fields of twenty or more participants; advancing to the

WHEREAS, Edward Henry and Jonah Vallejos finished in a five-way tie for fourth place,

WHEREAS, Thomas Cunningham has completed all levels of the FBLA Business

FBLA National Leadership Conference; and
WHEREAS, the Tullahoma High School FBLA national winners' display of talent and
dedication surely bodes well for their future success; and
WHEREAS, their achievements are a tremendous source of pride for their families and
their school and have brought positive attention to their community from across the nation; and
WHEREAS, these young men have represented Tullahoma, Coffee County, and the
State of Tennessee with distinction, and they are clearly deserving of our respect, admiration,
and commendation on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Edward Henry and Jonah Vallejos of
Tullahoma High School on winning a national award in the LifeSmarts Challenge and Thomas
