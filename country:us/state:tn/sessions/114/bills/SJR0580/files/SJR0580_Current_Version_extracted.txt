SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 580
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to designate July 2026 as "Uterine Fibroid
Awareness Month" in Tennessee.
WHEREAS, uterine fibroids, clinically known as uterine leiomyoma, are benign tumors in
the uterus and the single most common indication for hysterectomy; and
WHEREAS, uterine fibroids grow on or in the uterus and cause heavy menstrual
bleeding, debilitating pain, painful intercourse, fetal mortality, and sometimes infertility in
women; and
WHEREAS, consequently, uterine fibroids are the leading cause of loss of reproductive
potential, and nearly one-half of all hysterectomies are performed to address uterine fibroids;
and
WHEREAS, as many as three out of four women will have uterine fibroids during their
lives, and up to eighty percent of women will be affected by uterine fibroids by age fifty; and
WHEREAS, uterine fibroids were estimated to cost the United States $5.9 billion to
$34.4 billion annually, with an estimated annual lost work cost of $1.6 billion to $17.2 billion
through absenteeism and short-term disability in women ages twenty-five to fifty-four; and
WHEREAS, public awareness and education campaigns on uterine fibroid prevention,
screening, and symptoms are held during the month of July each year; and
WHEREAS, the recognition of July 2026 as "Uterine Fibroid Awareness Month" in
Tennessee would highlight conversation around the disorder and bring awareness to the issue;
and
WHEREAS, public awareness empowers and informs residents of this State about
uterine fibroids and encourages earlier diagnosis, more research, and better treatment options
and, eventually, will lead to a cure for uterine fibroids; now, therefore,
SJR0580
010650
- 1 -
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we designate July 2026 as "Uterine Fibroid
Awareness Month" in the State of Tennessee.
