SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 1186
By Yager
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Howard Galyon of
Harriman.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Howard Galyon of Harriman; and
WHEREAS, Howard Galyon was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Galyon was born on July 26, 1930; he served in the U.S. Army Airforce
during World War II and was a longtime employee of Mead Corporation, until it closed; and
WHEREAS, Howard Galyon especially enjoyed woodworking and giving away what he
made; and
WHEREAS, a man of deep faith, Mr. Galyon was a member of the Baptist church and a
firm believer in Christ; and
WHEREAS, Howard Galyon was devoted to his family and always endeavored to remain
true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Galyon is survived by his daughter, Carolyn "Tikey" (Steve) Vought;
son, Larry Eugene (Dale) Galyon; grandchildren, Alicia (Ross) Whittenbarger, Clint (Heather)
Vought, Amy Barnes, and Matthew (Leah) Galyon; eight great-grandchildren; and one great-
great-grandchild, Levi; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by his siblings; his wife
of seventy-three years, Mae Humphreys Galyon; and his son, Mike Galyon; and

WHEREAS, Howard Galyon leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
his public life, compassion and loyalty in his private life, and diligence and dedication in all his
chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Howard Galyon, reflecting
fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the examined life
with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. Galyon.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
