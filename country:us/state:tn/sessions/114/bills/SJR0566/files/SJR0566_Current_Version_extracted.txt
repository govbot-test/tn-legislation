SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 566
By Crowe
A RESOLUTION to honor and congratulate Cristy Dunn upon
receiving the Arts Leadership Award from the
Tennessee Arts Commission.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to commend those
exemplary citizens who, through their abiding commitment to excellence, have made significant
contributions to the arts and brought pride and acclaim to the State of Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Mountain City native Cristy Dunn, a professional
artist, teacher, and community leader who has been named a recipient of the Arts Leadership
Award from the Tennessee Arts Commission as part of the 2025 Tennessee Governor's Arts
Awards; and
WHEREAS, established by the Tennessee Arts Commission in 1971, the Governor's
Arts Awards recognize extraordinary artistic and cultural contributions and achievements by
individuals or organizations with State, regional, and national impact; the awards are the State's
highest honor in the arts; and
WHEREAS, the Executive Director of the Johnson County Center for the Arts, Ms. Dunn
has emerged as a steadfast rural arts leader, elevating regional music and history, fostering
community pride, and enhancing her community as a destination for public art and cultural
tourism; and
WHEREAS, Cristy Dunn's deep connection to her Appalachian heritage is evident in
every mural, class, and cultural initiative she leads, including the Mountain City's Mural Mile, a
walking tour featuring seventeen murals depicting songs, artists, and themes tied to Johnson
County's rich musical legacy; and
WHEREAS, in 2015, she co-founded the Long Journey Home Musical Heritage Festival,
an annual celebration of Appalachian arts and music, and in 2016 helped launch and now

presides over the Johnson County Junior Appalachian Musicians program, nurturing the next
generation's connection to traditional music; and
WHEREAS, in 2017, Ms. Dunn, together with a handful of dedicated citizens, founded
the Johnson County Center for the Arts; as its executive director, she has transformed the
center into a thriving regional cultural hub that supports over one hundred local artists, offers
classes, and serves as an inclusive gathering place; and
WHEREAS, after rapid growth, Cristy Dunn led the recent charge to expand the Center
for the Arts into a larger and more central Main Street location, while retaining the original
building as a frame shop, pottery studio, and gathering space; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Dunn's quiet but effective leadership is matched by her hands-on
commitment, from curating exhibits to installing floors, demonstrating her unwavering
dedication; and
WHEREAS, she also lends her service to the wider arts community as a board member
of Tennesseans for the Arts and is a nationally recognized oil painter whose work has been
exhibited from Blowing Rock, North Carolina, to New York City's Salmagundi Club and is even
archived on the moon as part of the Lunar Codex; and
WHEREAS, for her many contributions to the arts, Cristy Dunn is wholly deserving of
this prestigious honor and special recognition; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor and congratulate Cristy Dunn upon
receiving the Arts Leadership Award from the Tennessee Arts Commission as part of the 2025
Tennessee Governor's Arts Awards, commend her extraordinary contributions to the arts, and
extend to her our best wishes for every continued success in her future endeavors.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
