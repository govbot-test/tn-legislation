WHEREAS, the General Assembly finds and declares that:
(1) Surface transportation corridors throughout the State are increasingly limited by
existing development, right-of-way limitations, topographical and environmental constraints, and
urban density, making conventional surface expansion difficult, costly, and disruptive;
(2) Sustained population growth and economic development in this State continue to
generate increasing demand for expanded transportation capacity and more safe and efficient
transportation options for both residents and visitors;
(3) The development of subterranean transportation infrastructure offers a highly
effective and efficient means of significantly expanding transportation capacity while minimizing
surface disruption, preserving existing land uses, reducing environmental impacts at grade
level, and avoiding widespread acquisition of additional surface rights-of-way;
(4) Uniform, clear, and predictable state-level standards, procedures, and regulatory
oversight for the design, construction, safety, inspection, and operation of subterranean tunnel
transportation systems are essential to ensure the highest levels of public safety, structural
integrity, and engineering and design consistency across all such projects in this State;
(5) The establishment of predictable, efficient, and transparent approval pathways,
permitting processes, and regulatory frameworks at the state level are critical to reduce
uncertainty, shorten project timelines, and mitigate unnecessary duplication between state and
local jurisdictions; and

SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 65, is amended by adding the following
as a new chapter:
65-13-101. Short title.
This act is known and may be cited as the "Subterranean Transportation
Infrastructure Coordination Act."
65-13-102. Creation of Subterranean Transportation Infrastructure Coordination
Authority.
There is created the subterranean transportation infrastructure coordination
authority upon the later of:
(1) Thirty (30) days after an operator contracts with this state for a lease
interest in real property owned by this state for the purpose of constructing a
transit project; or
(2) July 31, 2026.
65-13-103. Chapter definitions.
As used in this chapter:
(1) "Authority" means the subterranean transportation infrastructure
coordination authority created by § 65-13-102;
(2) "Board" means the board of directors of the authority;
(3) "Local governmental entity" means an incorporated city or town,
metropolitan government, county, or other political subdivision of this state, and
includes a board, commission, council, agency, committee, utility, building or
- 2 - 013891
codes authority, development or other district, and any other instrumentality
thereof created by a local governmental entity;
(4) "Operator" means a public or private entity proposing to design,
construct, own, operate, or maintain, or that is a party to an agreement for the
design, construction, operation, or maintenance of a transit project;
(5) "Surface construction" means construction occurring above ground
and outside of the points designated pursuant to § 65-13-108(b) for entry into
and exit from a transit project; and
(6) "Transit project" or "project" means a subterranean tunnel or system
of subterranean tunnels designed primarily for the transportation of passengers
or goods using electric, autonomous, or low-emission vehicles, or other emerging
technology for vehicles and transportation systems, where the tunnels are
constructed at depths sufficient to avoid interference with surface uses.

65-13-108. Description of transit project -- Designation of transit project entrance
and exit.
(a)
(1) For each transit project, the board shall cause to be produced a legal

(A) The metes and bounds of the surface boundary under which

(B) A three-dimensional description of the subterranean

(C) Surface and subsurface easements and rights-of-ways;
(D) Each point for public or vehicular entry into and exit from the

(F) Additional descriptive information as deemed necessary by

65-13-109. Board authority to contract with operator. Operator contracts with
local governmental entities.
(a) The board may execute a contract, lease, or other agreement with an

The board is exempt from the requirements of title 4, chapter 55, and the requirements

(b) The operator may contract with a local governmental entity for the purposes
of facilitating the construction, operation, or maintenance of a transit project, including
the purchase or lease of an interest in property.
65-13-110. Board authority to streamline permitting and regulatory process for
subterranean transit project.
(a) The board shall prescribe by rule a streamlined, state-level application,
approval, permitting, inspection, and regulatory process for the design, construction,
operation, and maintenance of the subterranean portion of a transit project located
between the points of entry and exit designated pursuant to § 65-13-108(b). The rules
must establish:
(1) Uniform building, engineering, construction, safety, inspection,
permitting, land use, ventilation, and related standards and codes;
- 11 - 013891
(2) A single, consolidated state application and approval process that
preempts conflicting or duplicative local permitting and inspection requirements
for subterranean elements of a proposed or approved transit project;
(3) Defined timelines for review and approval of applications,
submissions, appeals, and other administrative matters; and
(4) A process for appeals submitted to the authority. The process must
provide an affected local governmental entity with an opportunity to respond.
(b) The board may coordinate with appropriate state and local governmental
entities to ensure comprehensive oversight of the subterranean portion of a transit
project to avoid undue delay.

brought pursuant to § 65-13-104(a)(5).
(b) This chapter does not affect, limit, or otherwise supersede the application of
the criminal code of this state or another state law establishing a crime.

65-13-114. Jurisdiction of three-judge panel - Criminal laws unaffected.
(a) A three-judge panel created pursuant to title 20, chapter 18 has exclusive
jurisdiction over:
(1) A civil action challenging a determination made by or an action of the
authority or an operator in accordance with this chapter, or otherwise brought
against the authority or an operator; and
(2) A civil action brought by the authority or an operator to enforce any
rights, duties, or powers of the authority or operator under this chapter, or
brought pursuant to § 65-13-104(a)(5).
(b) This chapter does not affect, limit, or otherwise supersede the application of
the criminal code of this state or another state law establishing a crime.
65-13-115. Emergency response authority within transit project.
(a) Notwithstanding another law to the contrary, an operator has exclusive
authority for initial emergency response, emergency medical services, fire suppression,
- 20 - 013891

life safety, and security operations between the points designated pursuant to § 65-13-
108(b) for entry into and exit from a transit project.
(b) Subsection (a) applies to all subterranean portions of the transit project and
to all stations, entrances, exits, and related facilities located between the points
designated pursuant to § 65-13-108(b) for entry into and exit from the transit project.
(c) A local governmental entity shall not assert concurrent jurisdiction over
emergency response activities located between the points designated pursuant to § 65-
13-108(b) for entry into and exit from the transit project unless requested by the

(d) This section does not:
(1) Limit the authority of the state fire marshal under § 65-13-111(c) with

of occupancy; or
(2) Prohibit coordination between an operator and a local governmental
entity for mutual aid, interoperability, or incident support.
(e) The operator shall adopt written emergency response and life safety
protocols consistent with applicable state and federal law.

this chapter controls.
