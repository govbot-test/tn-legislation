AMEND S e n ate Bill No. 2543* House Bill No. 2368
by deleting all language after the enacting clause and substituting:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Title 39, Chapter 13, Part 9, is amended by
adding the following new section:
(a) A person commits the offense of unauthorized operation of an unmanned
aircraft who operates an unmanned aircraft within four hundred feet (400') of or above a
ticketed entertainment event.
(b) A violation of this section is a Class A misdemeanor.
(c) Subsection (a) does not apply when a person who operates an unmanned
aircraft:
(1) Has obtained consent by any person with legal authority over a
ticketed entertainment event;
(2) Is authorized by federal regulations to operate an unmanned aircraft
and is operating the unmanned aircraft in a lawful manner and consistent with
federal regulations;
(3) Is an employee of the property where the ticketed entertainment
event is held and is conducting official business;
(4) Is an employee of a water, sewer, electrical, telephone, cable, or
other regulated utility service provider and is conducting official business;
(5) Is a law enforcement officer engaged in the actual discharge of official
duties;

(6) Is a member or employee of any branch of the United States armed
forces or the military forces of this state engaged in the actual discharge of
official duties;
(7) Is an emergency management officer of this state or of any city, town,
municipality, metropolitan government, or county of this state engaged in the
actual discharge of official duties; or
(8) Is an owner of private property who is operating an unmanned aircraft
above such property; provided, that the operation is:
(A) Consistent with federal regulations;
(B) Not for the purpose of interfering in any way with a ticketed
entertainment event; and
(C) Not to surveille or capture images of security procedures for
unlawful purposes.
(d) As used in this section, "ticketed entertainment event" means any music,
sporting, or performing arts event held in a location with gated entries or barriers that
prevent access to the general public and in which a ticket that has been issued by an
owner, operator, or lessee of such property is required for attendance at such event.
SECTION 2. This act takes effect July 1, 2026, the public welfare requiring it, and
applies to acts occurring on or after that date.
