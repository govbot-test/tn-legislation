SENATE RESOLUTION 294
By Gardenhire
A RESOLUTION to commemorate the Legacy: Lifted By God and
Led By Love and the retirement of Robert
Gardenhire.
WHEREAS, it is proper that the members of this legislative body recognize those
citizens that sacrifice themselves to honor God and family; and
WHEREAS, Robert Gardenhire became a member of the Schrader Lane Church of
Christ in 1974 and in 1984, after thirty-one years, was appointed Assistant Minister to Dr. David
Jones III (Senior Minister); and
WHEREAS, during his forty-one-year tenure, he taught Bible classes, conducted
personal Bible studies, provided ministerial counseling and premarital counseling, and
conducted numerous weddings and funerals; and
WHEREAS, during his forty-one-year tenure, Robert Gardenhire traveled to North
Carolina and Nigeria, West Africa on several missionary journeys. He also preached and
evangelized holding Gospel meetings in numerous churches in the United States, conducting
sessions at state lectureships and other local, state, and national gatherings; and
WHEREAS, during his ten-year tenure as Senior Minister, he ordained eleven formal
leaders of the congregations as elders and deacons. As Senior Minister, he mentored,
encouraged, taught, and trained young men to preach and teach in various capacities and
provided them opportunities to practice their craft in serving the congregation in various
capacities and settings; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure, he authored a book entitled Essentials of Project Funding:
A Practical Guide For Managing the Process, and he conducted debt-retirement workshops at
several Churches of Christ across the country as well as at other nonprofit organizations; and

WHEREAS, Robert Gardenhire continues to serve in various capacities as the Senior
Minister Emeritus, where he continues to be Lifted by God and Led by Love in ALL that he does
for the Glory of God; and
WHEREAS, Robert Gardenhire was born on January 13, 1952, to Charles W. and
Kirkalene Savage Gardenhire in Putnam County, humble beginnings, where he was taught a
love for God and mankind and the value of hard work and service to all people; and
WHEREAS, Robert Gardenhire graduated from Tennessee Technological University
with a Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration; and
WHEREAS, in 1977, Robert Gardenhire married Vanessa Thompson Gardenhire. They
have two daughters, Angela (Eric) and Andrea (Dwayne); one adult son, Auston; and one
grandson, Lan (parents Angela and Eric); and
WHEREAS, in his professional career, Robert Gardenhire was employed with
Bridgestone/Firestone from June 1974 until June 2015 (forty-one years). He held positions from
Plant Transportation Analyst to General Manager of Transportation to Director of Logistics and
resident of Fleet Operations from 2012 until 2015, when he retired; and
WHEREAS, Robert Gardenhire has excelled in civic and nonprofit affiliations and
boards; he has served on the Davidson County Port Authority, St. Thomas Hospital Board of
Ethics, and the Tennessee Technological University College of Business Advisory Board; and
WHEREAS, he currently serves on these prestigious boards: the Tennessee
Technological University College of Business Foundation, Schrader Acres Senior Living Center
Executive Board of Directors, and the United Way of Greater Nashville Board of Directors; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, that we honor and declare February
21, 2026, as "Robert Gardenhire Day" for his service to his God, Family, and Community.
- 2 - 013093
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy.
