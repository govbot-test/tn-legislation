HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1343
By Atchley
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Jere Loveday on the
occasion of a certain bridge in Pigeon Forge
being named in his honor.
WHEREAS, the late Jere Loveday, a highly regarded resident of Pigeon Forge, was a
farmer who worked his land near Middle Creek for decades; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Loveday collaborated with State and local officials in the planning of the
Jake Thomas Road connector and State Route 449 through his property; and
WHEREAS, this General Assembly has passed legislation to designate the bridge on
Jake Thomas Road that spans Middle Creek nearest the intersection of such road with State
Route 449 in the City of Pigeon Forge as the "Loveday Family Bridge" in honor of Mr. Loveday's
life of purpose and commitment to the greater good; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that this General Assembly should commemorate the life of this
exceptional gentleman on this special occasion; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that on the occasion of a bridge on Jake Thomas Road in the City of Pigeon
Forge being named in his and his family's honor, we honor the memory of Jere Loveday,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his many contributions to his community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
HJR1343
