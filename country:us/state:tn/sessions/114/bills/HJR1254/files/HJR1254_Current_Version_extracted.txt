HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1254
By Keisling
A RESOLUTION to honor Will Taylor for his leadership in recovery
advocacy and his service to the people of
Tennessee.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly are pleased to honor those citizens
whose dedication, leadership, and personal example bring credit to the State of Tennessee and
strengthen the well-being of its communities; and
WHEREAS, Will Taylor has earned the respect of colleagues, community leaders, and
recovery advocates across the State for his steadfast commitment to supporting individuals and
families affected by mental health and substance use disorders; and
WHEREAS, drawing upon both professional experience and powerful lived experience,
Mr. Taylor has devoted his career to helping others find hope, stability, and renewed purpose
through the promise of long-term recovery; and
WHEREAS, through his work with the Tennessee Department of Mental Health and
Substance Abuse Services' Faith Based Initiative and his service in the nonprofit treatment field,
Mr. Taylor has contributed to important initiatives that strengthen behavioral health systems and
encourage collaboration among State agencies, treatment providers, faith communities, and
local organizations; and
WHEREAS, he has demonstrated leadership in the development and operation of
addiction treatment programs at both outpatient and residential levels of care, helping ensure
that individuals seeking help have access to compassionate and effective recovery services;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor has also been instrumental in fostering recovery-focused
community engagement efforts that unite public institutions, faith leaders, nonprofit

organizations, and individuals with lived experience to build stronger recovery support networks
throughout the Volunteer State; and
WHEREAS, through these efforts, Mr. Taylor has emerged as one of a growing group of
leaders helping shape Tennessee's modern recovery ecosystem—one grounded in
collaboration, community engagement, and long-term recovery as essential components of
public health and community well-being; and
WHEREAS, his work contributes to the continued advancement of Tennessee's
nationally recognized efforts to expand recovery supports and strengthen community-based
responses to mental health and substance use challenges; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor's influence extends beyond program development through his
work with Recovery Is The New High, an initiative dedicated to breaking the stigma associated
with addiction and mental health disorders and promoting recovery through advocacy,
education, and community engagement; and
WHEREAS, the phrase "Recovery Is The New High," coined in 2013 by Allen Burnette,
has become a powerful message of hope that encourages individuals and communities to
recognize the life-changing fulfillment that accompanies long-term recovery; and
WHEREAS, the mission of Recovery Is The New High is to provide education,
advocacy, and consultation that foster the development of recovery-oriented practices within
communities while encouraging collaboration among those dedicated to helping individuals and
families rebuild their lives; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor openly acknowledges that his own life has been transformed
through long-term recovery and often credits his faith in God, the encouragement of those who
invested in him along his journey, and the strength of community as the foundation for his
commitment to serving others; and

WHEREAS, through his leadership, compassion, and dedication to the well-being of
others, Will Taylor has become a source of encouragement to many individuals pursuing
recovery and a valued partner to organizations working to build stronger and healthier
communities across Tennessee; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Will Taylor for his leadership in recovery
advocacy and his contributions to strengthening communities throughout this State.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sincere appreciation for Mr. Taylor's
efforts to promote hope, expand awareness, and encourage collaboration in addressing mental
health and substance use challenges in Tennessee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
