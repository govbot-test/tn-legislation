HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1569
By Cochran
A RESOLUTION to honor the Alexander Keith Chapter of the DAR
on the occasion of its 100th anniversary.
WHEREAS, the National Society of the Daughters of the American Revolution (NSDAR)
was founded in 1890 to promote historic preservation, education, and patriotism and has since
grown into one of the world's largest nonprofit, non-political volunteer women's service
organizations; and
WHEREAS, the Alexander Keith Chapter of the DAR, based in Athens, has faithfully
served the local community, the State of Tennessee, and the nation for one hundred years,
contributing to the organization's legacy of "God, Home, and Country"; and
WHEREAS, the Alexander Keith Chapter has worked tirelessly to promote American
history, honor our veterans, foster civic duty among young citizens through educational
initiatives, and protect historical landmarks; and
WHEREAS, on the occasion of the chapter's 100th anniversary, the chapter members
reflect on a century of grassroots efforts, fostering true patriotism and dedicating themselves to
the preservation of American independence and freedom; and
WHEREAS, this milestone anniversary is an appropriate time to honor the women of the
Alexander Keith Chapter—past and present—for their dedicated volunteerism and unwavering
commitment to strengthening our communities and commemorating our nation's heritage; now,
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we join with the members of the Alexander Keith Chapter of the DAR as
