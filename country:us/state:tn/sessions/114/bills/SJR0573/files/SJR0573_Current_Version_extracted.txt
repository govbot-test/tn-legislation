SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 573
By Akbari
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Crossett Franklin, Sr., of
Memphis.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Crossett Franklin, Sr., of Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Crossett Franklin, Sr., was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for his fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born in Memphis on December 28, 1950, Crossett Franklin was the son of
Roosevelt and Rosa Lee Franklin and a 1969 graduate of Hamilton High School; one of his
greatest joys was agriculture, and from a young age, he cherished working the land and
celebrating special moments in his community; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Franklin earned a bachelor's degree in political science and a master's
degree in public administration from Memphis State University, presently known as the
University of Memphis; and
WHEREAS, Crossett Franklin served the State of Tennessee with acumen and alacrity
as a business accounts and tax auditor, taking his much-deserved retirement following thirty-five
years of devoted service; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his work with the State, Mr. Franklin was a realtor and an
investor for more than four decades, retiring from Lawrence Johnson Realtors, Inc., in 2018;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Franklin was also deeply devoted to his family, and he always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and

WHEREAS, he will be sadly missed but fondly remembered by his children, Carsett
Franklin Webber, Lashunda Franklin (Eddie) Gatewood, and Crossett Franklin, Jr.; his
grandchildren, Sierra Franklin, Jada Franklin, Keelon Webber, Kaitlyn Webber, Madyson
Gatewood, Ethan Gatewood, Crossett Franklin III, Lailah Franklin, and Sebastian Franklin; his
siblings, Rosa Knox, Patricia Franklin (Maurice) Norman, Maxine Campbell, Dr. Joyce (Horace)
Freshwater, and Brenda (Lloyd) Patterson; his sisters-in-law, Diane Stewart and Theresa Fair;
his aunt, Fannie Stark; his uncle, Walter Hill; and a host of cousins, nieces, and nephews; and
WHEREAS, in addition to his parents, Mr. Franklin was preceded in death by his
beloved wife, Delores "Too Sweet" Fair; his son, Darryl Franklin; his grandson, Eddie Alexander
Gatewood; and his brothers, Joe Thomas and Larry Franklin; and
WHEREAS, Crossett Franklin accepted Christ at an early age and attended Breath of
Life Christian Center; and
WHEREAS, the epitome of love and faith, Mr. Franklin was a wise soul who impacted
the lives of those who were blessed to know him, and he leaves behind an indelible legacy of
integrity and probity in public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and
dedication in all his chosen endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting that the members of this General Assembly should pause to
pay tribute to his bountiful life of purpose and service; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTEENTH
GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE HOUSE OF
REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Crossett Franklin, Sr.,
reflecting fondly upon his impeccable character and his stalwart commitment to living the
examined life with courage and conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family and friends of Mr. Franklin.
