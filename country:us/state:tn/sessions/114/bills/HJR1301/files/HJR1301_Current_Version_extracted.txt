HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 1301
By Haston
A RESOLUTION to honor the memory of Gayle Duncan Gilmer of
Franklin.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly were greatly saddened to learn of
the passing of Gayle Duncan Gilmer of Franklin; and
WHEREAS, Gayle Duncan Gilmer was an exemplary public servant and consummate
professional who worked assiduously to improve the quality of life for her fellow citizens in
numerous capacities; and
WHEREAS, born on July 6, 1943, in Lobelville, Ms. Gilmer was the daughter of Homer
and Mabel Long Duncan; and
WHEREAS, in 1961, Gayle Gilmer graduated as valedictorian from Lobelville High
School; as a lifelong learner, she emphasized the importance of education to her family and
others who were fortunate to know her; and
WHEREAS, on April 3, 1964, Ms. Gilmer married Jacky Wayne Gilmer, with whom she
spent fifty-two years, most of which were spent living on the family farm in Gilmer Hollow, near
Lobelville; and
WHEREAS, after her children were grown, Gayle Gilmer returned to her studies and
earned a bachelor's and a master's degree from the University of Tennessee at Martin; she
furthered her graduate education at Vanderbilt University; and
WHEREAS, greatly admired by her community and colleagues, Ms. Gilmer enjoyed a
meaningful career in education as a special education teacher at Perry County High School;
and
WHEREAS, a devoted Christian, Gayle Gilmer was baptized at Lobelville Church of
Christ, where she was an active member for many years; she later became an active member in
the Crooked Creek congregation and at Brentwood Hills Church of Christ; and

WHEREAS, in addition to serving the school system and church congregations, Ms.
Gilmer was a community volunteer who served on hospital and library boards; and
WHEREAS, Gayle Gilmer was deeply devoted to her family, and she always
endeavored to remain true to family values of the highest order; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Gilmer is survived by her son, Scott (Amber) Gilmer; daughter, Shelby
(Michael) Elswick, M.D.; grandchildren, Adaline Gilmer, Hope (Connor) Manuel, Hunter (Emily)
Olds, Sydney Olds, Skylar Olds, Lawson Olds, Brady Elswick, Brooks Elswick, Noah Elswick,
and Amber (Travis) Dales; great-grandchildren, Sage Manuel and Bentley Dales; and several
other family members and friends; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her parents, she was preceded in death by her husband; her
brother, Van Duncan; and her grandparents, William T. and Mary Lora Brown Duncan and
Henry Hugh and Hattie Coble Long, Jr.; and
WHEREAS, Gayle Gilmer leaves behind an indelible legacy of integrity and probity in
public life, compassion and loyalty in private life, and diligence and dedication in all her chosen
endeavors; and
WHEREAS, it is fitting that we should remember the bountiful life of this exceptional
public servant and human being; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor the memory of Gayle Duncan Gilmer, reflecting fondly upon her
impeccable character and her stalwart commitment to living the examined life with courage and
conviction.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we express our sympathy and offer our condolences
to the family of Ms. Gilmer.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
