Section 2:
SECTION 1. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 67-5-1301(a), is amended by
deleting subdivision (11) and substituting instead:
(11) Motor bus and/or truck companies, which do not include towing companies,
and which are:
(A) Companies that operate commercial motor vehicles exclusively
authorized for hire holding United States department of transportation
registrations issued by this state through the performance and registration
information systems management (PRISM) or the federal motor carrier safety
administration (FMCSA), including those owner operators who operate under the
motor bus and/or truck company's motor carrier authority, and that:
(i) Are domiciled in this state;
(ii) Own or lease real or personal property located in this state; or
(iii) Meet subdivisions (a)(11)(A)(i) and (ii); or
(B) Subsurface passenger transit companies that:
(i) Operate vehicles primarily through subsurface tunnels or
below-ground conduit tunnel systems;
(ii) Own and operate the subsurface tunnels or below-ground
conduit tunnel systems that are used for passenger transit;

(iii) Own, use, or lease the vehicles that are used for transporting
passengers through the subsurface tunnels or below-ground conduit
tunnel systems; and
(iv) Are engaged in the business of transporting passengers
through subsurface tunnels or below-ground conduit tunnel systems;

Section 3:
SECTION 2. Tennessee Code Annotated, Section 67-5-1308, is amended by
designating the existing language as new subsection (a) and adding the following as a new
subsection (b):
(b) In addition to the information required by subsection (a), the schedules and
statements submitted by subsurface passenger transit companies must include:
(1) A listing of all tangible personal property that the company owns,
uses, or leases in the operation of its business;
(2) A description, including the location and the length in miles, of all
conduit tunnels and stations owned by the company;
(3) The total direct and indirect costs associated with making the
company operational; and
(4) Any other information that the comptroller of the treasury may
reasonably require.

Section 4:
SECTION 3. This act takes effect upon becoming a law, the public welfare requiring it.
