HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 721
By Moon
A RESOLUTION to recognize Master Sergeant Rachel J. Wilson
on the occasion of her retirement from the
United States Air Force.
WHEREAS, the members of this General Assembly take great pleasure in honoring
those members of the United States military who, throughout their distinguished careers, have
served with honor, bravery, and an unswerving commitment to duty; and
WHEREAS, one such individual is Master Sergeant Rachel J. Wilson of the United
States Air Force, who is taking her much-deserved retirement from active military service after
proudly defending our nation; and
WHEREAS, Master Sergeant Wilson has most recently served as a 3D072, Cyber
Systems Operations Craftsman/Systems Administrator Supervisor, assigned to the 119th
Cyberspace Operations Squadron, McGhee Tyson Air National Guard Base Louisville,
Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, she enlisted in the U.S. Army Reserve on June 3, 2003, as a Private, E-1,
and attended basic training at Fort Jackson, where she graduated on the Commandant's List;
after advanced training at Fort Leonard Wood, she served with pride and distinction as a convoy
escort gunner during operation Iraqi Freedom in 2005; and
WHEREAS, after being promoted to Private, E-2, on June 7, 2024, she rose through the
ranks, serving as Private First Class, E-3; Specialist, E-4; Army Sergeant, E-5; Army Staff
Sergeant, E-6; Air Force Staff Sergeant, E-5; Air Force Technical Sergeant, E-6; and Air Force
Master Sergeant, E-7; and
WHEREAS, in 2013, Master Sergeant Wilson voluntarily reduced in rank to join the
Tennessee Air National Guard; she returned to McGhee Tyson, where she was assigned to

Combat Operations Division in the Cyber Reporting Cell, and during this time, she provided key
analytical support to the Air Force Cyber mission set; and
WHEREAS, in 2016, Master Sergeant Rachel Wilson was reassigned to Network
Defense, where she served as a Cyber Systems Operations specialist, and she distinguished
herself through outstanding service while deployed to the 624th Operations Center, Kelly Annex
San Antonio, Texas, achieving a 98 percent on the 624th Mission Qualification Evaluation and
receiving her Mission Ready status in record time; and
WHEREAS, immediately earning the trust and respect of her active-duty counterparts,
she played a direct role in the realignment of Air Force Information Network Enterprise Change
Management operations, working with her team to maintain the highest standards of Air Force
Cyber Security while also serving the individual mission requirements of Air Force units
worldwide; and
WHEREAS, in 2017, Master Sergeant Wilson was selected for retraining into the 1B4X1
career field, Cyber Warfare Operations Apprentice, with duties and responsibilities including
monitoring, detecting, and destroying adversarial cyber threats within the Air Force Information
Network; coordinating with Air Force tactical units and external agencies for all mission tasking
execution; and supporting network maintenance and enhancing mission capabilities for all Air
Force units through effective implementation of the Enterprise change management process;
and
WHEREAS, Master Sergeant Rachel Wilson rendered sterling service in all her military
assignments, which included the 465th Army Transportation Company, Pennsylvania (2003-
2005); the 465th Army Transportation Company, Camp Anaconda, Iraq (2005-2006); the 2nd BN
310th Training Regiment, Fort Devens, Massachusetts (2006-2009); the Army Regional Training
Center – East, Fort Dix, New Jersey (2009-2013); and, beginning in 2013, the 119th Cyberspace
Operations Squadron, McGhee Tyson Air National Guard Base, Tennessee; and
- 2 - 010830
WHEREAS, her professional military education includes being a Cyber Systems
Operations Specialist Distinguished Graduate, Keesler Air Force Base, Mississippi; completing
Airman Leadership School; taking a Cyber Warfare Operations Course, Keesler Air Force Base,
Mississippi; and completing Non-Commissioned Officer Academy; and
WHEREAS, Master Sergeant Wilson was honored with a number of awards and
decorations, including the Air Force Commendation Medal, Army Commendation Medal with
oak leaf cluster, Air Force Achievement Medal, Army Achievement Medal with four oak leaf
clusters, and Combat Readiness Medal; and
WHEREAS, throughout her distinguished career, Master Sergeant Rachel Wilson has
served these United States with honor and distinction, and it is wholly fitting that we recognize
her on the occasion of her retirement from military service; now, therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE HUNDRED
FOURTEENTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF TENNESSEE, THE SENATE
CONCURRING, that we honor and commend Master Sergeant Rachel J. Wilson upon her
retirement from the United States Air Force, as we extend our gratitude for her many years of
selfless service and our best wishes for her every future happiness and success.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that an appropriate copy of this resolution be prepared
for presentation with this final clause omitted from such copy and upon proper request made to
the appropriate clerk, the language appearing immediately following the State seal appear
without House or Senate designation.
